# Discovery, Brady & Evidence Timeline (2025–2026)

This timeline follows **one thread only: what happened to the evidence.** Not who did it — what was collected, sealed, produced, protected, destroyed, or never handed over, in the order it happened. The docket in *State of Utah v. Tyler Alexander Robinson* (Case No. 251403576) is the spine; citizen-investigator claims and press reporting fill the gaps. [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) is **charged, not convicted**, and nothing below is a finding that any official acted unlawfully.

## The Timeline

### September 2025 — collection, and the first losses

* **Sept 10, 2025** — A black SUV transports [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) from the UVU courtyard; the vehicle later becomes the subject of the [SUV destruction claim](/CoverUp/SUV_Destroyed), which alleges it was cut up and melted down rather than preserved as a scene vehicle.

* **Sept 10, 2025** — An officer's **body-worn camera** is described as ending while he is still on the roof, roughly 27 minutes and 35 seconds into the recording — the gap examined on the [Bagley bodycam page](/CoverUp/Bagley_Bodycam_Gap) and in the [GRAMA no-footage response](/court/mirandize/bodycam-grama-no-footage).

* **Sept 10–11, 2025** — Witness phone videos from the scene are alleged to have been **remotely wiped**, and separate claims describe federal agents asking a witness to delete footage — see [videos deleted remotely](/CoverUp/Videos_Deleted_Remotely) and the [FBI-asked-delete-video page](/CoverUp/FBI_Asked_Delete_Video).

* **Sept 11, 2025** — A rifle is recovered from the wooded area north of campus, per the [gun recovery location page](/Locations/Wooded_Area_Gun_Recovery); the sequence in which it was found is itself disputed on the [gun discovery sequence page](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Gun_Discovery_Sequence).

* **Sept 11, 2025** — A news organization is later reported to have requested the **video of Robinson turning himself in**; Washington County declined and subsequently stated the file had been **deleted under a 30-day retention policy** — the account attributed to attorney Baron Coleman on the [missing surrender discovery page](/Suspicious/Defense_Attorneys/missing-surrender-discovery) and the [Baron Coleman timeline](./baron-coleman-timeline).

* **Sept 14, 2025** — The courtyard where the shooting occurred is **paved and altered**, documented on the [paving timeline](/Tent/Paving_Timeline_Sept_14) and the [crime-scene paving page](/FBI/Crime_Scene_Paving). Officials describe restoration of a campus space; critics call it the destruction of a scene.

* **Sept 16, 2025** — The **Information** and the **probable-cause affidavit** are filed, along with a bail order, an acknowledgment of firearm restriction, a proposed **pretrial protective order**, the **State's notice of intent to seek the death penalty**, and the signed protective order. The affidavit's contents are summarized on the [probable-cause page](/court/mirandize/probable-cause-affidavit).

* **Sept 17, 2025** — The **ATF laboratory report** on Exhibit 6A — the deformed bullet jacket fragment recovered at autopsy — is dated. Its conclusion is **inconclusive**: the fragment could neither be identified as nor excluded from the recovered rifle. See [ATF inconclusive ballistics](/Proof_Not_Tyler/ATF_Inconclusive_Ballistics) and [court ballistics](/court/ballistics-atf-cbla). It stays under seal for months.

* **Sept 22, 2025** — Initial appearance, plus a **Protection Order Re: Pre-Trial and Trial Publicity** — the first speech restriction in the case, and the ancestor of the later gag-order fight tracked on [court gag orders](/Censorship/Court_Gag_Orders).

* **Sept 24, 2025** — A **standing decorum order** and an order to transport are entered.

* **Sept 25–27, 2025** — Rule 8 declarations and **pro hac vice** admissions bring Michael N. Burt and Richard G. Novak onto the [defense team](/court/defense-team) alongside [Kathryn Nester](/People/kathryn-nester).

* **Sept 30, 2025** — The defense files its formal **Request for Discovery** — the starting gun for everything that follows.

### October 2025 — preservation, protection, and the first sealed hearing

* **Oct 6–8, 2025** — A **request for the audio recording** is filed, the video/audio record is produced, and the defense files a **Motion to Preserve Evidence** — an explicit request that physical items not be consumed, altered, or destroyed.

* **Oct 13, 2025** — The parties file a **stipulated Motion for a Discovery Protective Order**, agreeing that material produced would be restricted from public release.

* **Oct 15, 2025** — The court enters the **Discovery Protective Order**. From this point most of what changes hands is invisible to the public — the structural fact behind the [evidence sealing 2026](/Legal/Evidence-Sealing-2026) page.

* **Oct 20, 2025** — The court **grants** the Motion to Preserve Evidence.

* **Oct 22, 2025** — The State moves to **set aside** the October 20 preservation order and files a response opposing the preservation motion — the first open clash over whether items would be preserved intact or subjected to consumptive testing. The defense's parallel complaint that it was refused observer access to testing is tracked on [denied access to testing](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/denied-access-to-testing).

* **Oct 24, 2025** — A **sealed hearing** is held. Its transcript becomes its own two-month dispute, catalogued on [closed hearings and missing transcripts](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/closed-hearings-no-transcripts).

* **Oct 27, 2025** — The State files a **Notice of Open File Policy and Invitation to Inspect Physical Evidence** — the prosecution's answer to the withholding narrative, and a document the defense's later filings say did not solve the raw-data problem.

* **Oct 28, 2025** — *The New York Times* publishes reporting on FBI files in the case, the first major press attention to what the federal side is holding — see [FBI discovery delays](/FBI/Discovery_Delays).

* **Oct 30, 2025** — Media organizations file a **Request for Notice of Motions to Close Judicial Proceedings and Records**, the opening move of the press intervention.

### November–December 2025 — sealing versus the press

* **Nov 3, 2025** — A **stipulated motion regarding the order to preserve evidence** is filed, together with a stipulation to **classify the October 24 transcript and audio as private**.

* **Nov 4, 2025** — The court enters a stipulated order **setting aside the October 20 preservation order** and **granting in part** the motion to preserve — a partial preservation regime whose exact scope has never been made public.

* **Nov 5, 2025** — Media intervenors file a **Motion for Limited Intervention** seeking notice of any motion to close, seal or reclassify.

* **Nov 6, 2025** — The State moves to **amend or clarify** the pre-trial publicity order, later opposed on **Nov 20** and briefed through **Nov 28**.

* **Nov 13, 2025** — The court orders the parties to **serve all closure and reclassification motions on the media**, and separately orders the transcript of the October 24 hearing.

* **Nov 17, 2025** — The October 24 transcript is filed as **Protected — not publicly available**, and a second transcript is listed as not posted for purchase.

* **Dec 4, 2025** — The parties file a **Joint Status Report Concerning the Redaction** of the October 24 sealed-hearing transcript — a negotiation over which lines the public may read.

* **Dec 8–9, 2025** — Media intervenors file a memorandum **opposing** classification of the October 24 transcript as private; the defense replies. The [publicity-order breadth page](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/publicity-order-breadth) tracks the arguments.

* **Dec 16, 2025** — Judge **[Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf)** issues a broad **gag order on his own motion**, without a request from either party, reaching witnesses, future counsel, staff and unrelated attorneys in the same firms. Commentators call it unprecedented in breadth; the court's stated purpose is protecting the jury pool. See [gag orders and sealing](/court/gag-orders-sealing).

### January–June 2026 — the production fight

* **Jan 21, 2026** — A **FedEx discovery list** compiled outside the court process arrives at defense counsel, and is emailed the same day — the citizen-submission channel described on the [discovery and Brady disputes page](/court/discovery-brady-disputes) and drafted in the private [Discovery workspace](/laws/Discovery).

* **March 2026** — Digital-forensics warrant material — device search history, communications, location data — is described in research notes as **sealed until this month**, per [evidence sealing 2026](/Legal/Evidence-Sealing-2026).

* **March 2026** — The defense cites the **inconclusive ATF report** and moves to delay the preliminary hearing for further forensic review; the report's unsealing is widely amplified online, as tracked on the [trial and autopsy report page](/Medical/trial_and_autopsy_report).

* **March 14, 2026** — A Utah judge **rejects** an effort to keep certain records sealed and **allows cameras** at a Robinson hearing — a rare opening in a case otherwise moving toward closure.

* **March 27, 2026** — An addendum to a defense filing states that the **Utah State Bureau of Investigation has had 28 different law enforcement agencies produce evidence** in the case — a custody chain wide enough that no single agency can account for the whole record.

* **April 2026** — The defense seeks a **six-month continuance**, stating the **ATF and FBI have not produced files they have held since September 2025**, and asks for time to retain independent forensic biologists, genetic-systems engineers and statisticians. Victim-side counsel **opposes** the continuance and objects to some discovery requests — the clash documented on [victim lawyers oppose continuance](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/victim-lawyers-oppose-continuance).

* **April 2026 — in open court** — The defense states that **FBI and ATF have still not produced the raw DNA analysis data files**, even though September **summary reports** cite them. That is the case's clearest **Brady** framing: the files are known to exist because the summaries reference them. See [FBI discovery withholding](/Suspicious/FBI/discovery-withholding).

* **April 2026** — Citizen investigators allege the State **"drowned" the defense in roughly 600,000 files** while the specific contested items remained hard to isolate — the volume-versus-access argument on [discovery document dump](/CoverUp/Discovery_Document_Dump) and [discovery dump and continuance](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/discovery-dump-continuance).

* **2026** — The State is reported to have moved the bullet fragment to **Virtual Comparison Microscopy** while the defense was **denied observer presence or videotaping** — a consumptive-testing objection tracked on [FBI ballistics and forensics](/FBI/Ballistics_Forensics).

* **June 2026** — Judge Graf finds Deputy Utah County Attorney **[Christopher Ballard](/People/christopher-d-ballard)** in **civil contempt** for public statements describing "ample evidence" of guilt notwithstanding the inconclusive ballistics; prosecutors are ordered to pay defense fees tied to the motion. Ballard is a living person; the finding was **civil contempt over publicity**, not a finding of evidence tampering.

* **June 30, 2026** — Attorney and radio host **Baron Coleman** publicly asserts a multi-agency cover-up and says Robinson was in custody well before 6:30 PM on September 11 — an attributed claim, examined on the [Baron Coleman timeline](./baron-coleman-timeline).

### July 2026 — the preliminary hearing, and what still is not in the file

* **July 2026** — The **preliminary hearing** opens; surveillance video, DNA and officer testimony are presented across [Day 1](/court/Preliminary_Hearing/Day_1) through [Day 4](/court/Preliminary_Hearing/Day_4).

* **July 2026** — Agent **David Hull** walks the court through UVU surveillance video, chain of custody and the rooftop search, per [Day 2](/court/Preliminary_Hearing/Day_2). Researchers note the rooftop clothing-change portion drew no public follow-up.

* **July 2026** — An officer testifies that his **body camera battery died** at the moment he was on the roof, and that a red and black **screwdriver** he found there was photographed into evidence — but the screwdriver and DNA statements are said to be **absent from the charging documents and the affidavit**. See [Bagley bodycam gap](/CoverUp/Bagley_Bodycam_Gap).

* **July 2026** — Extraction disputes surface: the State is reported to **disclaim** certain device extractions, against the [Cellebrite extraction contradiction](/CoverUp/Cellebrite_Extraction_Contradiction) and the [state-disclaims-extractions page](/Suspicious/Trial_Legal/state-disclaims-extractions).

* **Still unproduced, as of the latest entries on this site** — UVU's **high-definition camera masters** ([UVU HD footage withheld](/CoverUp/UVU_HD_Footage_Withheld)), the **untrimmed rooftop video** ([rooftop video trimmed](/CoverUp/Rooftop_Video_Trimmed)), the **surrender video** ([sheriff video withheld](/CoverUp/Sheriff_Video_Withheld)), **hospital footage** and associated NDAs ([hospital footage and NDAs](/CoverUp/Hospital_Footage_And_NDAs)), the **full autopsy** ([sealed autopsy](/CoverUp/Autopsy_Sealed_Examiner)), and the **UVU GRAMA records** ([UVU GRAMA records](/CoverUp/UVU_GRAMA_Records)).

## Conflicts and Disputed Points

| Point | Version A | Version B | Who asserts what |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Is discovery being withheld?** | Federal agencies have held DNA and ballistics files since September 2025 without producing raw data | The State filed an **open file policy** and invited inspection of physical evidence on Oct 27, 2025 | Version A: defense statements in open court, April 2026. Version B: the docket entry itself |
| **The 600,000 files** | A deliberate burial of contested items in volume | The ordinary size of a multi-agency capital production spanning 28 agencies | Citizen investigators assert the first; the 28-agency addendum supports the second reading |
| **The surrender video** | Refused, then deleted, though pleadings suggest footage still exists in law-enforcement hands | Ordinary 30-day retention expiry with no request timely enough to preserve it | Baron Coleman asserts the first; Washington County's stated position is the second |
| **The SUV** | Chopped up and melted to destroy a scene vehicle | Ordinary disposal of a private vehicle never designated as evidence | Citizen compilations assert the first; no court finding exists either way |
| **Preservation scope** | The Oct 20 order protected the physical evidence | It was set aside on Nov 4 and replaced with a **partial** grant whose scope is not public | Both are docket facts; what the partial order actually covers has never been published |
| **The gag order** | An unprecedented, self-initiated speech restriction on thousands of potential witnesses | A routine high-profile-case measure protecting the jury pool | Critics assert the first; the court's own stated rationale is the second |
| **Ballard contempt** | Evidence of a State that talks publicly while raw lab files lag | A narrow publicity violation unrelated to production | Citizen investigators assert the first; the order itself is civil contempt over statements |

## Open Questions

1. What exactly does the **November 4, 2025 partial preservation order** cover, and what did it leave unprotected?
2. Do the **raw DNA instrument files** referenced in the September 2025 summary reports still exist in FBI and ATF custody, and when were they first requested?
3. What is in the **redacted portions** of the October 24, 2025 sealed-hearing transcript?
4. Did the **surrender video** ever exist in a second copy, and do the pleadings that reference it identify who holds it?
5. Was the **SUV** ever designated as evidence before it was disposed of, and by whom was that decision made?
6. Which of the **28 agencies** produced which items, and is there a single master index tying custody to each exhibit?
7. Why do the **screwdriver and DNA statements** appear in hearing testimony but not in the charging documents or the affidavit?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* The unredacted October 24, 2025 sealed-hearing transcript, the full scope of the November 4 partial preservation order, the raw FBI and ATF DNA instrument files and examiner notes behind the September 2025 summaries, the complete 600,000-file production index tied to each of the 28 producing agencies, the sealed digital-forensics warrants and their authorizing affidavits, the Washington County surrender video and its retention and deletion logs, the disposal record for the transport SUV, and the withheld UVU high-definition camera masters are things that the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
