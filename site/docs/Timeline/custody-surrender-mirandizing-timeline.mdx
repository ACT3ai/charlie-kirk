# Custody, Surrender &amp; Mirandizing Timeline (September 11–12)

This is the narrowest and most consequential window in the case: roughly thirty hours in
which [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) went from unnamed to booked, and in which
**four different documents record four different moments as the start of custody**. If the
6:25 PM Miranda timestamp is correct, the evening press conference and the 7:57 PM
[Discord messages](/Tyler_Robinson/Discord_Messages) both happened after he was already sitting
in a sheriff's interview room. Robinson
is **charged, not convicted**, and no court has found that any official named below did
anything wrong. All times Mountain Time.

## September 11 — Daytime: Still a Manhunt

* **6:00 AM Sept 11** — President [Donald Trump](/People/donald-trump) says on Fox &amp;
  Friends, "with a high degree of certainty," that the suspected killer is in custody — hours
  before any publicly acknowledged surrender
  ([press conferences and statements](./press-conferences-statements-timeline)).

* **7:15 AM Sept 11** — A gray Dodge Challenger is captured on surveillance at the
  [Cedar City Maverik](/Locations/Cedar_City_Maverik), about 215 miles south of Orem, on the
  route toward Washington County; the driver pays by card and departs in under three minutes
  ([vehicle movements](./rental-cars-vehicle-movements-timeline)).

* **9:58 AM Sept 11** — The FBI's Salt Lake City field office releases the first two
  person-of-interest photos ([SLC Field Office](/FBI/SLC_Field_Office)).

* **10:44 AM Sept 11** — A **$100,000 reward** is announced
  ([suspect manhunt](/Law_Enforcement/suspect-manhunt)).

* **Daytime Sept 11** — Per the probable-cause narrative, Robinson's father contacts a family
  friend, retired Washington County deputy **[Mike Mitchell](/People/mike-mitchell)**, after Robinson allegedly
  confessed to him; other accounts name retired detective
  [Scott Messinger](/People/scott-messinger) as the intermediary, a naming conflict this site
  does not resolve ([Tyler Robinson's family](/Tyler_Robinson/Family)).

* **Afternoon Sept 11 (disputed)** — Investigators including
  [Baron Coleman](./baron-coleman-timeline) have reported hearing that Robinson may have
  turned himself in "very early in the afternoon, not late in the evening" — possibly as
  early as 3:00 PM. This is **unverified hearsay**, but it is the reason the missing
  surrender video matters ([missing surrender footage](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/missing-surrender-footage)).

* **~2:48–3:00 PM Sept 11 (arithmetic inference only)** — Citizen analysts, including posts
  amplified by **[@VLuvMully](/Influencers/x/vluvmully)**, argue that if the Miranda utterance sits at continuous-recording
  mark **03:36:53**, the interview room may have been occupied since roughly **2:48–3:00 PM**
  (about 3.5 hours of tape before the rights reading). That is reverse math from Bates video
  marks, **not** a court finding that custody began then
  ([custody vs Discord confession](./custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline)).

* **6:17 PM Sept 11** — The FBI releases additional person-of-interest photos — eight minutes
  before the Miranda video timestamp ([enhanced suspect photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos)).

## September 11 — 6:25 PM: The Miranda Timestamp

* **6:25 PM Sept 11** — A defense motion (Bates **003996-R2**) describes a **video of the
  encounter** whose timestamp reads 6:25 p.m., in which an officer says: "Before we begin,
  I'm just gonna read you your rights... You have the right to remain silent." Robinson
  acknowledges each right. Commentary (e.g. **[@SteveCameronPr1](/Influencers/x/stevecameronpr1)**, April 2026) treats the
  State as not having publicly disputed that clock time even while the **calendar date**
  remains contested ([Mirandizing](/Timeline/Tyler_Robinson/Mirandizing)).

* **6:25 PM Sept 11 (invocation)** — He then invokes counsel: *"I'd like to speak with my
  legal counsel. I'm hoping for a fella named [Doug Terry](/People/doug-terry). Told he's a
  pretty good attorney here. Think my folks are trying to get a hold of him."*
  ([defense team](/court/defense-team)).

* **6:26 PM Sept 11** — *"I think his office is closed for the night."* Coleman's argument
  turns on this phrase: a Thursday-evening office closes **for the night**; a Friday-evening
  office closes **for the weekend**. The wording, he argues, dates the video to Sept 11
  ([Baron Coleman's early-turn-in argument](/court/mirandize/baron-coleman-early-turn-in)).

* **6:26 PM Sept 11** — *"Until I've had the opportunity to speak with someone, I would like
  to remain silent."* Everything the State later attributes to Robinson as statement evidence
  has to be reconciled with this invocation
  ([Miranda defense filing](/Suspicious/Defense_Attorneys/miranda-defense-filing)).

* **Bates R1 vs R2 (researcher claim)** — Analysts note that Bates **003996-R1** appears to
  excerpt the same source video near the **~6m50s** mark while **R2** lands at **3h36m53s**,
  arguing the suffixes mark revised production of one continuous WCSO recording rather than
  multi-day merges. Attribution only; discovery Bates protocol is the claimed constraint
  ([Miranda defense filing](/Suspicious/Defense_Attorneys/miranda-defense-filing)).

* **6:25 PM Sept 11 or 12 (disputed date)** — The document as filed carries a **time but no
  date**. Washington County Deputy Attorney **Courtney Sinagra** told *The American Prospect*
  (April 2026) that "evening of arrest" language points to **Sept 12**, because formal arrest
  was recorded after transport to Utah County and Robinson was never "arrested" in Washington
  County. Critics answer with "closed for the night," Doug Terry's local geography, and the
  continuous-recording math
  ([investigative hold counterpoint](/court/mirandize/investigative-hold-counterpoint)).

* **~6:30 PM Sept 11 (inference)** — Coleman notes that a 10:00 PM arrest by an officer
  driving 3.25–4 hours from Utah County requires a departure around 6:30 PM — within minutes
  of the Miranda timestamp, and roughly **90 minutes before** the tip that supposedly
  triggered everything ([arrest time discrepancy](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/arrest-time-discrepancy)).

* **~6:50 PM Sept 11 (researcher claim)** — Posts including **[@ImBreckWorsham](/Influencers/x/imbreckworsham)** argue that by
  about 6:50 PM belongings — including a phone — would already have been booked into evidence
  under ordinary WCSO intake practice, so a handset-origin Discord post at 7:57 PM would be
  impossible under an early-custody reading. Counter: Discord can be reached from any device;
  property-receipt times remain unreleased
  ([custody vs Discord confession](./custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline)).

## September 11 — Evening: The Official Sequence

* **~7:00 PM Sept 11 (family-confirmation claim)** — A widely circulated **[@DiligentDenizen](/Influencers/x/diligent-denizen-2)**
  clip (May 2026) attributes to a [family source](/Tyler_Robinson/Family) an arrival around **~7 PM**, with the phone
  taken immediately and station hold until after midnight. That clock conflicts with Brooksby's
  "just before 9 p.m." and the PCA's **2226 hours** — three arrival stories, not one.

* **7:57 PM Sept 11** — Discord messages allegedly posted under *zealous_moneky_55095* /
  *zealous_monkey_55095* (spelling varies in secondary accounts) say "It was me at UVU
  yesterday" and "Im surrendering through a sheriff friend in a few moments" — roughly
  90 minutes after the Miranda timestamp, and about **five minutes before** the Brooksby tip
  call that officially starts the surrender sequence
  ([Discord custody timeline](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Discord_Custody_Timeline),
  [custody vs Discord confession](./custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline)).

* **7:58–8:00 PM Sept 11** — The Utah County press conference releases "enhanced" stairwell
  photos and rooftop video, with Governor [Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox), Lt. Gov.
  [Deidre Henderson](/People/deidre-henderson), FBI Director [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel), Deputy Director
  [Dan Bongino](/People/dan-bongino), Sheriff [Mike Smith](/People/mike-smith) and SAC
  [Robert Bohls](/People/robert-bohls) present — a podium that presented the suspect as still
  at large ([Mirandized Sept 11 image](/court/mirandize/mirandized-sept-11-image)).

* **8:02 PM Sept 11** — Sheriff [Nate Brooksby](/People/nate-brooksby) says he received a
  call from a retired-deputy friend: *"I know who Charlie Kirk's shooter is... he's in
  Washington County now and we're working on trying to get him to come in voluntarily,"*
  adding that the caller mentioned suicidal ideations and a possible drive to a remote area.
  Per Brooksby as reported by *The American Prospect* / ABC, that tip call lasted about
  **1 minute 41 seconds**
  ([Miranda vs Brooksby account](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/miranda-vs-brooksby-account)).

* **8:04 PM Sept 11** — The probable-cause statement records Sheriff Smith receiving
  Brooksby's call at **2004 hours** — while, critics note, Smith was standing at the Utah
  County press conference. Prospect reporting of Brooksby's account puts the Brooksby→Smith
  relay at about **40 seconds**
  ([Utah County Sheriff](/Law_Enforcement/utah-county-sheriff)).

* **8:30 PM Sept 11 (per court testimony)** — Per [@BasedSamParker](/Influencers/x/based-sam-parker)'s summary of Officer
  [Brian Davis](/People/brian-davis)'s testimony, Davis was ordered to fly to St. George at
  8:30 PM — before the 9:00 PM surrender in the official account, and with State Bureau of
  Investigation personnel already on the ground
  ([Preliminary Hearing](/court/Preliminary_Hearing/overview)).

* **~9:00 PM Sept 11 (official / media version)** — AP, CBS, KSL and Brooksby's Sept 17
  press conference say Robinson arrived shortly before **9 p.m.**, driven with **parents and a
  retired detective / family friend**, "truly fearful of being shot," and received by
  plainclothes detectives with water and no WCSO interrogation.
  [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens)–amplified
  commentary stresses Brooksby's own framing that Robinson **set peaceful terms** (including
  no SWAT at the parents' house), **walked in**, asked for a lawyer, and — per relatives
  quoted in investigation narratives — **never confessed** to killing Kirk on that night
  ([Tyler Robinson's surrender](/Tyler_Robinson/Surrender)).

* **10:00 PM Sept 11 (booking-sheet version)** — The **Inmate Booking Sheet and PC
  Statement** records **Arrest Date 09/11/2025 10:00 PM**, arresting officer **DAVIS**,
  arrest agency "Other," arrest location "Outside Ut Cnty." Early public circulation of that
  sheet (including Crowder-linked posts) made the 10:00 PM DAVIS line one of the first
  primary documents civilian researchers could screenshot
  ([arrest, booking and affidavit timeline](./arrest-booking-affidavit-timeline)).

* **10:26 PM Sept 11 (government-document version)** — The probable-cause narrative states
  Robinson "arrived at the Washington County Sheriff's Office accompanied by his parents at
  approximately **2226 hours**," wearing a dark hat, maroon T-shirt, jeans and white/gray
  Converse-style shoes — clothing the document notes was **not** consistent with the
  surveillance clothing. The 2226 arrival line also conflicts with Brooksby's "just before
  9 p.m." framing ([Washington County Sheriff's Office](/Locations/Washington_County_Sheriff)).

* **Evening Sept 11 (conditions)** — Accounts agree he was placed **uncuffed on a couch with
  his parents**, given water, and **not questioned** by Washington County personnel, who said
  their role was only to facilitate a peaceful surrender
  ([Tyler Robinson's surrender](/Tyler_Robinson/Surrender)).

* **Evening Sept 11 (documentation)** — [Sergeant Elsholz](/People/sergeant-elsholz) of the
  Utah State Bureau of Investigation provided a photo of Robinson's Utah driver license,
  which Agent Davis had taken from his person
  ([rifle and evidence chain of custody](/Law_Enforcement/rifle-chain-of-custody)).

## September 12 — Transport and Booking

* **~Midnight Sept 11–12** — Davis testified he did not arrive until **near midnight**,
  which sits uneasily beside his own name on the 10:00 PM arrest line
  ([the "did not surrender" argument](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/robinson-did-not-surrender)).

* **~12:30 AM–2:00 AM Sept 12** — After roughly 2.5 hours at the sheriff's office, custody
  transfers to federal and state investigators for the 250-mile, 3.5–4 hour drive north
  ([Utah County jail custody](/court/utah-county-jail-custody)).

* **1:58 AM Sept 12** — The booking record logs **Date In 09/12/2025 01:58**, under
  **Booking #460956** ([Utah County Jail](/Locations/Utah_County_Jail)).

* **4:00 AM Sept 12** — The affidavit lists the **formal arrest time as 09/12/2025 04:00**.
  Critics argue this reflects when the *Affidavit of Probable Cause* was ready for judicial
  review rather than when custody began, since there was no arrest warrant to execute
  ([probable cause affidavit](/court/mirandize/probable-cause-affidavit)).

* **7:18 AM Sept 12** — Probable Cause entered by **UTBDAVIS — Brian Davis**, a sworn officer
  with the Utah Attorney General's office (UT018015A); Probable Cause ID **189904**; Booking
  Agency ORI UT0250000; OTN 70090584 ([case overview](/court/case-overview)).

* **7:00–7:30 AM Sept 12** — Director Patel describes a **"33-hour manhunt."** From 12:23 PM
  Sept 10 that lands at **9:23 PM Sept 11** — three hours after the Miranda timestamp, and
  roughly 30 hours by the defense filing's clock
  ([Kash Patel manhunt timeline](./kash-patel-manhunt-timeline),
  [Kash Patel briefings](/FBI/Kash_Patel_Briefings)).

* **Charges at booking** — Aggravated Murder (76-5-202), Obstruction of Justice (76-8-306),
  and Felony Discharge of a Firearm Causing Serious Bodily Injury (76-11-210); held without
  bail ([Fourth District Court, Provo](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo)).

## Afterward — The Records That Vanished

* **Sept 17, 2025** — Brooksby holds a press conference re-affirming the 33-hour manhunt and
  restating the 8:02 PM and 8:04 PM calls, including the "truly fearful of being shot" and
  voluntary-walk-in framing
  ([official statements](/Legal/Official-Statements)).

* **Late 2025 – 2026 (GRAMA path)** — Reporting (*The American Prospect*, KOMO/Scripps)
  describes a multi-step [GRAMA public-records](/CoverUp/UVU_GRAMA_Records) path: early
  requests answered as wrong entrance or wrong camera, then lobby CCTV of the surrender
  declared **gone** under a **30-day retention** policy; in a GRAMA appeal the county also
  states on the record that **no officer had a bodycam rolling**. That is a chain of
  non-production, not proof of intentional destruction
  ([GRAMA and bodycam, no footage](/court/mirandize/bodycam-grama-no-footage),
  [sheriff video withheld](/CoverUp/Sheriff_Video_Withheld)).

* **July 2026 prelim (process fact)** — Media and hearing summaries state the defense did
  **not** cross-examine Agent **Brian Davis** on the surrender, transport, Discord, and
  physical-evidence package he sponsored. Pro-guilt commentators treat that as undercutting
  timeline challenges; it remains a **procedural observation**, not proof of guilt or of
  clock accuracy ([Preliminary Hearing](/court/Preliminary_Hearing/overview)).

* **March 27, 2026** — Sheriff Brooksby resigns without a public Kirk-case explanation; UVU
  President [Astrid S. Tuminez](/People/astrid-s-tuminez) also departed in the same period.
  *The American Prospect* reports an anonymous letter to the county commission alleging
  interference in an **unrelated** deputy criminal-investigation-records matter — an
  attributed allegation about resignation context, **not** a finding tying Brooksby to
  falsifying the surrender clocks
  ([Brooksby resignation](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/brooksby-resignation)).

* **2026 (litigation)** — Defense filings continue to press on whether surrender footage
  exists in law-enforcement hands despite the retention claim
  ([discovery and Brady disputes](/court/discovery-brady-disputes),
  [discovery timeline](./discovery-brady-evidence-timeline)).

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| When custody began | **6:25 PM Sept 11** (Miranda video timestamp, defense filing) | **10:00 PM Sept 11** (booking sheet) | **4:00 AM Sept 12** (affidavit formal arrest time) |
| Arrival at the sheriff's office | **~9:00 PM**, per Brooksby / AP / CBS | **10:26 PM / 2226 hrs**, per the probable-cause narrative | **~7:00 PM** family-confirmation claim ([@DiligentDenizen](/Influencers/x/diligent-denizen-2)); unverified **~2:48–6:00 PM** reverse-math / early-arrival claims |
| Date of the 6:25 Miranda | **Sept 11** ("closed for the night," continuous tape math) | **Sept 12** (Sinagra to *Prospect*: evening of formal arrest after Utah County transport) | Filing carries **time, no date** |
| Who arrested him | Booking sheet: **Davis at 10:00 PM** | Davis's testimony: arrived **near midnight** | — |

## Open Questions

1. What date does the metadata on the Bates 003996-R2 video carry, and why was the date
   stripped from the filed excerpt? Do R1 (~6m50s) and R2 (3h36m53s) share one continuous
   source file under discovery Bates rules?
2. If Officer Davis is the named arresting officer at 10:00 PM in Hurricane, what time did he
   leave Utah County, and who ordered the departure?
3. Why was the Utah County press conference held at 7:58 PM if the suspect was already in a
   Washington County interview room?
4. Who posted the 7:57 PM Discord messages, and from which device and IP address? Does a
   **property receipt** show when Robinson's phone was taken?
5. Does any surviving copy of the Washington County lobby CCTV or bodycam footage exist in
   state or federal hands, and what exact timestamps does the KOMO/Scripps GRAMA file show?
6. Why does the county's own record note that Robinson's clothing did **not** match the
   surveillance clothing, and where is the clothing from Sept 10?
7. Which arrival clock is correct — **~7 PM** (family claim), **~9 PM** (Brooksby), or
   **2226** (PCA) — and who drove the vehicle (parents, retired detective, both)?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* The complete unredacted Bates 003996-R2 Miranda video with intact metadata, the Washington
  County lobby CCTV and every bodycam file from the evening of September 11, the dispatch and
  travel logs showing when Officer Brian Davis left Utah County, the Discord account access
  and IP logs behind the 7:57 PM messages, and the internal communications between the Utah
  County and Washington County sheriffs' offices that night are things that the
  [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out
  that aren't out yet.
