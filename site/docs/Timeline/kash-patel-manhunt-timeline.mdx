# Kash Patel Announcements & Manhunt Narrative Timeline

This timeline follows one actor across roughly **50 hours** — from the shot at 12:23 PM MDT on September 10, 2025 through the "33-hour manhunt" framing on the morning of September 12 and the media appearances that followed. It tracks what the FBI Director said publicly, when, and how each statement lined up against what the rest of the case file shows was happening at that moment. Kash Patel is a living public official; nothing here asserts he committed a crime, and the criticism recorded below is criticism of **public statements and release decisions**.

## Why the announcement sequence is its own timeline

Most disputes in this case concern physical evidence. This one concerns **information release** — what the public was told, in what order, and whether the order was coherent. Researchers press three points: a suspect was announced and then quietly un-announced, suspect photos went out after the charged man was arguably already in an interview room, and a manhunt duration was stated that does not divide cleanly into any documented custody time. Each is traced below against the [FBI's official narrative](/FBI/Official_Narrative) and the [Kash Patel briefings](/FBI/Kash_Patel_Briefings) page.

## The Timeline

### September 10 — the day of the shot

* **12:23 PM MDT** — **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** is shot at Utah Valley University; the minute-level reconstruction sits on the [shooting minute-by-minute](./shooting-minute-by-minute) page and the disputed shot time on [12:23 vs 12:27](./12-23-shooting-time).

* **Early afternoon** — Federal involvement escalates quickly. Researchers later allege local investigators were told to stand back, an allegation collected at [locals told not to investigate](/Suspicious/FBI/locals-told-not-to-investigate); the FBI has not confirmed that framing.

* **Afternoon — first detention** — **[Zachariah Qureshi](/distraction_people/Zachariah_Qureshi)**, present at the event, is detained. Commentary asserts the FBI publicly signalled a suspect was in custody and that he was **released roughly 90 minutes later**, reportedly held briefly on a separate obstruction matter. He has not been charged in connection with the shooting; see the [FBI's handling of Qureshi](/FBI/Zachariah_Qureshi) and his father **[Ahmed Qureshi](/People/ahmed-qureshi)**.

* **Afternoon — further detentions** — **[George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn)**, an older man detained at the scene, was stated by the FBI not to be the shooter and was charged on unrelated counts. A **[pellet gun man](/distraction_people/Pellet_Gun_Man)** is a third figure in the same reversal cluster; all are gathered on the [distraction timeline](/distraction_people/Distraction_Timeline).

* **Afternoon (criticism)** — A widely circulated monologue mocked the whiplash — *"Oh, we got the guy. Oh, we don't have the guy. Oh, we have a person of interest"* — and asked whether premature announcements paused the search. The question researchers put is whether an announced-then-retracted suspect created a **90-minute window** in which the real search effectively stood down. There is no official answer to it.

* **Evening** — The rifle is reported recovered in a wooded area. How the search was redirected to it is disputed at [rifle found after federal redirect](/Suspicious/FBI/rifle-found-after-federal-redirect) and [gun drop and search sequence](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/gun-drop-and-search-sequence), with the location itself covered at [wooded area gun recovery](/Locations/Wooded_Area_Gun_Recovery).

### September 11 — releases, the press conference, the surrender

* **First photo set (morning/midday)** — An initial set of suspect images circulates. Researchers argue the figure in the morning images and the figure in the afternoon images **do not share the same build** — a comparison laid out on [Enhanced Suspect Photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos) and the [Black Clothing Photo](/FBI/Black_Clothing_Photo).

* **6:17 PM MDT / 7:17 PM CT (per Baron Coleman)** — The **second set of stairwell photos** is released. [Baron Coleman](/Influencers/podcasts-baron) argues **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)** — who is **charged, not convicted** — was already in a custodial interview at that moment, making a public BOLO unnecessary. That argument is developed at [enhanced photos released after custody](/Suspicious/FBI/enhanced-photos-after-custody) and in full on the [custody vs Discord confession conflict](./custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline).

* **6:25 PM (disputed date)** — A defense filing quoting a Washington County video exhibit records a Miranda warning at this clock time with **no calendar date**. Researchers place it on September 11; the official framing implies September 12. See [Mirandized September 11 (image evidence)](/court/mirandize/mirandized-sept-11-image).

* **7:55 PM** — Per an AP-derived timeline, the FBI states it is seeking a **"person of interest"** and releases the hat, sunglasses, long-sleeve black shirt and backpack series, plus **video of a figure racing across the roof and walking off campus**.

* **7:55 PM onward (criticism)** — The roof video draws sustained criticism for **image quality**. Commentators describe the running figure as so small and low-resolution that a parent could not credibly identify their own child from it — which matters because the official account has the father recognizing his son from released material. The complaint is developed on [Tyler on surveillance cameras](/cameras/Tyler_On_Cameras) and the [roof jumping timeline](./roof-jumping-timeline), and the "why publish low-resolution when higher-resolution exists" question at [selective 4K release](/Suspicious/FBI/selective-4k-release).

* **7:57 PM** — The **Discord messages attributed to Robinson** appear — two minutes after the BOLO and, on the citizen reading, an hour and a half after custody began.

* **7:58–8:00 PM** — A press conference convenes in Utah County. Reported attendees include Governor **[Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox)**, Lt. Gov. **[Deidre Henderson](/People/deidre-henderson)**, Director Kash Patel, Deputy Director **[Dan Bongino](/People/dan-bongino)**, Utah County Sheriff **[Mike Smith](/People/mike-smith)**, and SLC SAC **[Robert Bohls](/People/robert-bohls)**, appointed to the [Salt Lake City field office](/FBI/SLC_Field_Office) weeks earlier.

* **At the press conference (noted)** — Local Utah press remarked that Patel was **present but did not speak**. Researchers treat the silence as notable; there is no established explanation, and a mundane one — a state-led podium — is equally available. Coverage is compared at [FBI press conference media](/Media/fbi-press-conference-media) and in [September 10 breaking news](/Media/september-10-breaking-news).

* **8:02 / 8:04 PM** — Sheriff **[Nate Brooksby](/People/nate-brooksby)** reportedly receives, then relays, word of a voluntary surrender — the calls examined at [Miranda vs Brooksby's account](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/miranda-vs-brooksby-account).

* **10:00 PM** — The Utah County booking sheet records the arrest with **[Brian Davis](/People/brian-davis)** as arresting officer; the competing 4:00 AM affidavit time sits on the [arrest and booking affidavit timeline](./arrest-booking-affidavit-timeline).

### September 12 — the "33 hours"

* **~6:00 AM** — President **[Donald Trump](/People/donald-trump)** announces publicly that a suspect is in hand.

* **7:00–7:30 AM** — Patel characterizes the operation as a **"33-hour manhunt."** Measured from 12:23 PM September 10, 33 hours lands at **9:23 PM September 11**. Measured to the disputed 6:25 PM exhibit timestamp it is roughly **30 hours**; measured to the 4:00 AM affidavit, closer to **40**. No documented custody moment produces exactly 33.

* **Same morning** — Patel also stated Robinson was **arrested at 10 p.m. local time Thursday night**, which matches the booking sheet but not the affidavit — an inconsistency inside the official set, not a citizen inference. See [Utah County jail custody](/court/utah-county-jail-custody).

### September 15–17 — the evidence statements

* **Sept 15** — On Fox News, Patel states DNA on the **towel** around the recovered rifle *and* on a **screwdriver on the rooftop** matched Robinson. CNN had reported on September 11 that the screwdriver was found near the rifle, not on the roof — a physical item relocated between two public accounts.

* **Sept 16** — The indictment and the affidavit of probable cause **omit the screwdriver entirely**. It is the one item publicly described as tying Robinson to the roof that appears in neither charging document, a gap tracked alongside [ballistics and forensics](/FBI/Ballistics_Forensics) and the [ATF fragment inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive) finding.

* **Sept 15 (the "enhanced" statement)** — In the same appearance Patel described the stairwell images as **"enhanced."** Critics argue an enhanced image is by definition not an unaltered representation and should face an authentication challenge; the counterpoint is that contrast and sharpening enhancement is routine, disclosed forensic practice. Both readings are on [Enhanced Suspect Photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos).

* **Sept 17** — Brooksby holds a press conference reaffirming the 33-hour figure and the 8:02 and 8:04 PM calls; the statement set is compiled on [press conferences and statements](./press-conferences-statements-timeline).

### After September — the foreign-leads dispute

* **~Sept 16 (secondhand claim)** — Russell Brand said two people he described as cabinet-meeting sources told him Patel, **[Tulsi Gabbard](/US_Intelligence/tulsi-gabbard)** and the Vice President said *"we're not looking at foreign involvement"* and *"we're not looking at any accomplices domestically."* These are **secondhand claims by a commentator**, not confirmed by any official named; see [Russell Brand cabinet claims](/Israel/Russell_Brand_Cabinet_Claims).

* **Later reporting** — A Daily Mail account reported Patel "snuffed out" National Counterterrorism Center efforts under **[Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent)** to examine foreign involvement. Kent has said publicly he was prevented from pursuing foreign leads. The dispute is set out at [Patel shut down the Kent probe](/Suspicious/FBI/patel-shut-down-kent-probe) and [Kent NCTC probe halted](/Suspicious/US_Intel/kent-nctc-probe-halted), with the underlying leads at [FBI Foreign Leads](/FBI/Foreign_Leads).

* **Ongoing** — Defense filings and commentary allege discovery is being withheld, tracked at [discovery withholding](/Suspicious/FBI/discovery-withholding) and [FBI discovery delays](/FBI/Discovery_Delays).

* **Later (tone criticism)** — Patel's memorial phrasing, *"Rest now brother. We have the watch, and I'll see you in Valhalla,"* drew criticism for applying Norse imagery to a devout Christian. That critique is catalogued at [Buckley Carlson on the Valhalla remark](/Videos/buckley-carlson-kash-patel-valhalla) and is a criticism of wording, nothing more.

## Conflicts and Disputed Points

| Point | One account | The competing account |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manhunt duration | **33 hours**, per Patel on Sept 12 | ~**30 hours** if custody began 6:25 PM Sept 11; ~**40** if the 4:00 AM affidavit governs |
| Arrest time | **10 p.m. Thursday**, per Patel and the booking sheet | **4:00 AM Sept 12**, per the affidavit of probable cause |
| Why release photos at 6:17 and 7:55 PM Sept 11 | The suspect was still at large and public help was needed | Coleman argues the charged man was already in an interview room, so the release built public identification rather than sought it |
| The first detentions | Fast-moving investigative practice — detain, clear, release | Researchers ask whether announcing a suspect prematurely halted the search for roughly 90 minutes |
| The roof video quality | Best footage available, released fast | Critics say higher-resolution campus footage existed and a father could not have identified anyone from what was published |
| The screwdriver | Found near the rifle (CNN, Sept 11) | Found on the rooftop with matching DNA (Patel, Sept 15) — and absent from the Sept 16 charging documents entirely |
| Foreign leads | No public FBI statement that leads were closed | Kent says he was stopped; Brand's sources allege a cabinet-level instruction. Both remain contested |

## Open Questions

1. What is the exact release timestamp, from FBI systems rather than screenshots, for each set of suspect photos on September 10 and 11?
2. What internal record fixes the "33 hours" figure — which start and end moments were used to compute it?
3. On what basis was Zachariah Qureshi detained, and what written record documents the decision to release him roughly 90 minutes later?
4. Did the announcement of a detained suspect pause any active search element, and would dispatch audio show it?
5. Why was low-resolution roof video published when higher-resolution campus surveillance is understood to exist?
6. Was the screwdriver recovered near the rifle or on the rooftop, and why does it appear in neither the indictment nor the affidavit?
7. What instruction, if any, was given regarding foreign-involvement leads, and does a written directive exist?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

The FBI's internal release logs and timestamps for every suspect photo and the roof video, the original unenhanced source imagery with its metadata, the detention and release paperwork for every person briefly held on September 10, the internal document behind the "33-hour manhunt" figure, the chain-of-custody record for the screwdriver, and any written directive concerning the National Counterterrorism Center's foreign-involvement inquiry are things the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
