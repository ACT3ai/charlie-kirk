# Kash Patel Announcements & Manhunt Narrative Timeline

This timeline follows one actor across roughly **50 hours** — from the shot at 12:23 PM MDT on September 10, 2025 through the "33-hour manhunt" framing on the morning of September 12 and the media appearances that followed. It tracks what the FBI Director said publicly, when, and how each statement lined up against what the rest of the case file shows was happening at that moment. [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel) is a living public official; nothing here asserts he committed a crime, and the criticism recorded below is criticism of **public statements and release decisions**.

## Why the announcement sequence is its own timeline

Most disputes in this case concern physical evidence. This one concerns **information release** — what the public was told, in what order, and whether the order was coherent. Researchers press three points: a suspect was announced and then quietly un-announced, suspect photos went out after the charged man was arguably already in an interview room, and a manhunt duration was stated that does not divide cleanly into any documented custody time. Each is traced below against the [FBI's official narrative](/FBI/Official_Narrative) and the [Kash Patel briefings](/FBI/Kash_Patel_Briefings) page.

## The Timeline

### September 10 — the day of the shot

* **12:23 PM MDT** — **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** is shot at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview); the minute-level reconstruction sits on the [shooting minute-by-minute](./shooting-minute-by-minute) page and the disputed shot time on [12:23 vs 12:27](./12-23-shooting-time).

* **~12:39 PM (reported — ~16 minutes)** — Per later **Patel** remarks as carried by **BBC**, the **first federal agents** reached the scene about **16 minutes** after the shot.

* **Early afternoon** — Federal involvement escalates quickly. Researchers later allege local investigators were told to stand back, an allegation collected at [locals told not to investigate](/Suspicious/FBI/locals-told-not-to-investigate); the FBI has not confirmed that framing.

* **Afternoon — first detention** — **[Zachariah Qureshi](/distraction_people/Zachariah_Qureshi)**, present at the event, is detained. Commentary asserts the FBI publicly signalled a suspect was in custody and that he was **released roughly 90 minutes later**, reportedly held briefly on a separate obstruction matter. He has not been charged in connection with the shooting; see the [FBI's handling of Qureshi](/FBI/Zachariah_Qureshi) and his father **[Ahmed Qureshi](/People/ahmed-qureshi)**.

* **4:21 p.m. MT (documented public — Patel tweet)** — FBI Director **Kash Patel** posted that the **"subject"** of the shooting that killed Charlie Kirk was **"now in custody."** An **NPR** manhunt timeline cites the Director's X post as the clock for this announcement. [Wikipedia](/Media/Wikipedia-Coverage) and contemporaneous press note the same reverse-and-release cycle; the misidentification pattern is tracked at [suspect manhunt and misidentification](/Law_Enforcement/suspect-manhunt).

* **Afternoon — further detentions** — **[George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn)**, an older man detained at the scene, was stated by the FBI not to be the shooter and was charged on unrelated counts. A **[pellet gun man](/distraction_people/Pellet_Gun_Man)** is a third figure in the same reversal cluster; all are gathered on the [distraction timeline](/distraction_people/Distraction_Timeline) and on [this site's distraction events timeline](./distraction-events-timeline). Utah DPS Commissioner **[Beau Mason](/distraction_people/Beau_Mason)** later said **two people** were taken into custody and released Wednesday; one charged **obstruction** by [UVU police](/Law_Enforcement/uvu-campus-police); **neither** tied to the shooting.

* **~5:40 p.m. (citizen reconstruction; verify)** — Case-file researchers (e.g. [@TheCKFiles1](/Influencers/x/theckfiles1)) place **rifle recovery** around this time — **inside** the window between the custody tweet and the release tweet. Cross-check against official recovery logs before treating 5:40 as fixed. How the search was redirected is disputed at [rifle found after federal redirect](/Suspicious/FBI/rifle-found-after-federal-redirect) and [gun drop and search sequence](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/gun-drop-and-search-sequence); location: [wooded area gun recovery](/Locations/Wooded_Area_Gun_Recovery).

* **5:59 p.m. MT (documented public — Patel tweet)** — Patel posted that the **"subject in custody has been released after an interrogation by law enforcement."** The ~**98-minute** public "we have him / we don't" cycle (4:21 → 5:59) is the documentary core of the **90-minute pause** question: did announcing a subject halt active search elements? There is **no official answer**.

* **Afternoon/evening (criticism)** — A widely circulated monologue mocked the whiplash — *"Oh, we got the guy. Oh, we don't have the guy. Oh, we have a person of interest"* — and asked whether premature announcements paused the search. NYT-linked contemporaneous coverage also described the mid-manhunt **reversal** of the "apprehended" framing.

### September 11 — releases, the press conference, the surrender

* **10:00 a.m. local (Patel official remarks, Sept 12)** — In the FBI's published text of Patel's **September 12 Orem remarks**, Patel says: at his direction, the FBI released the **first set of FBI photos** of the suspect at **10 a.m. local on 9/11**. Researchers argue the figure in morning images and the figure in later images **do not share the same build** — [Enhanced Suspect Photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos), [Black Clothing Photo](/FBI/Black_Clothing_Photo).

* **10:45 a.m. local (Patel official remarks)** — FBI **$100,000 reward** released (same official sequence).

* **~5:30 p.m. (Patel official remarks)** — Patel and Deputy Director **[Dan Bongino](/People/dan-bongino)** **"arrived on scene."**

* **6:17 PM MDT / 7:17 PM CT (per Baron Coleman)** — The **second set of stairwell photos** is released on citizen clocks. [Baron Coleman](/Influencers/podcasts-baron) argues **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)** — who is **charged, not convicted** — was already in a custodial interview at that moment, making a public BOLO unnecessary. That argument is developed at [enhanced photos released after custody](/Suspicious/FBI/enhanced-photos-after-custody) and in full on the [custody vs Discord confession conflict](./custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline). Reconcile carefully with the official **"10 a.m. first set / ~8 p.m. video + new images"** sequence below — multi-drop timeline, not a single release.

* **6:25 PM (disputed date)** — A defense filing quoting a Washington County video exhibit records a Miranda warning at this clock time with **no calendar date**. Researchers place it on September 11; the official framing implies September 12. See [Mirandized September 11 (image evidence)](/court/mirandize/mirandized-sept-11-image).

* **7:55 PM** — Per an AP-derived timeline, the FBI states it is seeking a **"person of interest"** and releases the hat, sunglasses, long-sleeve black shirt and [backpack](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Backpack_Rifle_Impossibility) series, plus **video of a figure racing across the roof and walking off campus** from the [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center).

* **7:55 PM onward (criticism)** — The roof video draws sustained criticism for **image quality**. Commentators describe the running figure as so small and low-resolution that a parent could not credibly identify their own child from it — which matters because the official account has the father recognizing his son from released material. The complaint is developed on [Tyler on surveillance cameras](/cameras/Tyler_On_Cameras) and the [roof jumping timeline](./roof-jumping-timeline), and the "why publish low-resolution when higher-resolution exists" question at [selective 4K release](/Suspicious/FBI/selective-4k-release). The dispute remained live into 2026 citizen commentary.

* **7:57 PM** — The **[Discord messages attributed to Robinson](/Tyler_Robinson/Discord_Messages)** appear — two minutes after the BOLO and, on the citizen reading, an hour and a half after custody began. How those messages entered the record is argued at [Discord evidence in court](/court/discord-evidence-court) and [confession fabrication questions](/CoverUp/Confession_Fabrication).

* **~8:00 p.m. (Patel official remarks + press conference)** — Governor-led press conference in Utah County; Patel states that **at his direction** the FBI released a **"never-before-seen video"** of the suspect and **new images**. Reported attendees include Governor **[Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox)**, Lt. Gov. **[Deidre Henderson](/People/deidre-henderson)**, Director Kash Patel, Deputy Director Bongino, Utah County Sheriff **[Mike Smith](/People/mike-smith)**, and SLC SAC **[Robert Bohls](/People/robert-bohls)**, appointed to the [Salt Lake City field office](/FBI/SLC_Field_Office) weeks earlier.

* **At the press conference (noted)** — Local Utah press remarked that Patel was **present but did not speak** at the podium moment. Researchers treat the silence as notable; there is no established explanation, and a mundane one — a state-led podium — is equally available. Coverage is compared at [FBI press conference media](/Media/fbi-press-conference-media) and in [September 10 breaking news](/Media/september-10-breaking-news).

* **8:02 / 8:04 PM** — Washington County Sheriff **[Nate Brooksby](/People/nate-brooksby)** reportedly receives, then relays, word of a voluntary surrender — the calls examined at [Miranda vs Brooksby's account](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/miranda-vs-brooksby-account) and, after his March 2026 departure, on [Brooksby's resignation](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/brooksby-resignation).

* **10:00 PM local (Patel official remarks + booking sheet)** — Patel states the suspect was taken into custody at **10 p.m.** "last night"; the Utah County booking sheet records the arrest with **[Brian Davis](/People/brian-davis)** as arresting officer. The competing **4:00 AM Sept 12 affidavit** time sits on the [arrest and booking affidavit timeline](./arrest-booking-affidavit-timeline).

### September 12 — the "33 hours"

* **~6:00 AM** — President **[Donald Trump](/People/donald-trump)** announces publicly that a suspect is in hand.

* **Morning — Patel official Orem remarks (fbi.gov text)** — Patel: **"In less than 36 hours, 33 to be precise"** / "In 33 hours, we have made historic progress for Charlie," restating the 10:00 / 10:45 / 5:30 / 8 p.m. / 10 p.m. sequence above. Measured from 12:23 PM September 10, 33 hours lands at **9:23 PM September 11**. Measured to the disputed 6:25 PM exhibit timestamp it is roughly **30 hours**; measured to the 4:00 AM affidavit, closer to **40**. No documented custody moment produces exactly 33.

* **Same morning — process criticism (attributed, not a charge)** — **BBC**, Wikipedia-style summaries, and viral commentary stress that **parents / people who knew him** facilitated the turn-in after a confession to family — undercutting a pure "FBI ran him down in a classic manhunt" reading even if federal coordination was real. Satirical posts mocked credit-claiming (*"suspect was dropped off at our door"*; *"FBI didn't apprehend anyone"*). These are **criticisms of public framing**, not findings that Patel committed a crime.

* **Same morning** — Patel also stated Robinson was **arrested at 10 p.m. local time Thursday night**, which matches the booking sheet but not the affidavit — an inconsistency inside the official set, not a citizen inference. See [Utah County jail custody](/court/utah-county-jail-custody).

### September 15–17 — the evidence statements

* **Sept 15** — On [Fox News](/Media/fox-news-coverage), Patel states DNA on the **[towel](/Suspicious/Five_Minutes/rifle-towel-on-roof)** around the recovered [Mauser .30-06](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle) *and* on a **screwdriver on the rooftop** matched Robinson. CNN had reported on September 11 that the screwdriver was found near the rifle, not on the roof — a physical item relocated between two public accounts.

* **Sept 16** — The indictment and the [affidavit of probable cause](/court/mirandize/probable-cause-affidavit) **omit the screwdriver entirely**. It is the one item publicly described as tying Robinson to the roof that appears in neither charging document, a gap tracked alongside [ballistics and forensics](/FBI/Ballistics_Forensics) and the [ATF fragment inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive) finding.

* **Sept 15 (the "enhanced" statement)** — In the same appearance Patel described the stairwell images as **"enhanced."** Critics argue an enhanced image is by definition not an unaltered representation and should face an authentication challenge; the counterpoint is that contrast and sharpening enhancement is routine, disclosed forensic practice. Both readings are on [Enhanced Suspect Photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos).

* **Sept 17** — Brooksby holds a press conference reaffirming the 33-hour figure and the 8:02 and 8:04 PM calls; the statement set is compiled on [press conferences and statements](./press-conferences-statements-timeline).

### After September — the foreign-leads dispute

* **~Sept 16 (secondhand claim)** — Russell Brand said two people he described as cabinet-meeting sources told him Patel, **[Tulsi Gabbard](/US_Intelligence/tulsi-gabbard)** and the Vice President said *"we're not looking at foreign involvement"* and *"we're not looking at any accomplices domestically."* These are **secondhand claims by a commentator**, not confirmed by any official named; see [Russell Brand cabinet claims](/Israel/Russell_Brand_Cabinet_Claims).

* **Later reporting** — A Daily Mail account reported Patel "snuffed out" National Counterterrorism Center efforts under **[Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent)** to examine foreign involvement. Kent has said publicly he was prevented from pursuing foreign leads. The dispute is set out at [Patel shut down the Kent probe](/Suspicious/FBI/patel-shut-down-kent-probe) and [Kent NCTC probe halted](/Suspicious/US_Intel/kent-nctc-probe-halted), with the underlying leads at [FBI Foreign Leads](/FBI/Foreign_Leads).

* **Ongoing** — Defense filings and commentary allege discovery is being withheld, tracked at [discovery withholding](/Suspicious/FBI/discovery-withholding) and [FBI discovery delays](/FBI/Discovery_Delays).

* **Later (tone criticism)** — Patel's memorial phrasing, *"Rest now brother. We have the watch, and I'll see you in [Valhalla](/Locations/Valhalla),"* drew criticism for applying Norse imagery to a devout Christian. That critique is catalogued at [Buckley Carlson on the Valhalla remark](/Videos/buckley-carlson-kash-patel-valhalla) and is a criticism of wording, nothing more.

## Conflicts and Disputed Points

| Point | One account | The competing account |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manhunt duration | **33 hours**, per Patel official Sept 12 remarks | ~**30 hours** if custody began 6:25 PM Sept 11; ~**40** if the 4:00 AM affidavit governs; arithmetic depends on start/end points |
| Who "caught" him | "Historic progress" / manhunt-apprehension framing | Family-facilitated surrender / "dropped off at our door" public mockery (BBC + viral commentary) |
| Arrest time | **10 p.m. Thursday**, per Patel and the booking sheet | **4:00 AM Sept 12**, per the affidavit of probable cause |
| Photo / video release order | Official: **10:00 a.m.** first photos; **10:45** reward; **~8 p.m.** video + new images "at my direction" | Citizen clocks also emphasize **6:17 p.m.** stairwell drop and **7:55 p.m.** BOLO/roof package |
| Why release photos at 6:17 and 7:55 PM Sept 11 | The suspect was still at large and public help was needed | Coleman argues the charged man was already in an interview room, so the release built public identification rather than sought it |
| The first detentions | Fast-moving investigative practice — detain, clear, release | Patel **4:21 p.m.** "in custody" → **5:59 p.m.** "released" (~98 min); did that pause the search? |
| Rifle recovery vs false custody | Evening wooded-area recovery (general) | Citizen reconstruction ~**5:40 p.m.** **inside** 4:21–5:59 window — needs official-log corroboration |
| The roof video quality | Best footage available, released fast | Critics say higher-resolution campus footage existed and a father could not have identified anyone from what was published |
| The screwdriver | Found near the rifle (CNN, Sept 11) | Found on the rooftop with matching DNA (Patel, Sept 15) — and absent from the Sept 16 charging documents entirely |
| Foreign leads | No public FBI statement that leads were closed | Kent says he was stopped; Brand's sources allege a cabinet-level instruction. Both remain contested |

## Open Questions

1. What is the exact release timestamp, from FBI systems rather than screenshots, for each set of suspect photos and video on September 10 and 11 — reconciling official **10:00 a.m. / ~8 p.m.** with citizen **6:17 p.m. / 7:55 p.m.** clocks?
2. What internal record fixes the "33 hours" figure — which start and end moments were used to compute it?
3. On what basis was Zachariah Qureshi (and any second detainee) held, and what written record documents the decision to release after Patel's **4:21 p.m. → 5:59 p.m.** public cycle?
4. Did the announcement of a detained subject pause any active search element, and would dispatch audio show it — including whether ~**5:40 p.m.** rifle recovery sits inside that window?
5. Why was low-resolution roof video published when higher-resolution campus surveillance is understood to exist?
6. Was the screwdriver recovered near the rifle or on the rooftop, and why does it appear in neither the indictment nor the affidavit?
7. What instruction, if any, was given regarding foreign-involvement leads, and does a written directive exist?
8. What contemporaneous logs support the **16-minute** federal-arrival claim and the **$100,000** reward release at **10:45 a.m.** Sept 11?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

The FBI's internal release logs and timestamps for every suspect photo and the roof video, the original unenhanced source imagery with its metadata, the detention and release paperwork for every person briefly held on September 10, the internal document behind the "33-hour manhunt" figure, the chain-of-custody record for the screwdriver, and any written directive concerning the National Counterterrorism Center's foreign-involvement inquiry are things the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
