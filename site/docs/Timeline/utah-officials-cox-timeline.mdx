# Spencer Cox, Utah Officials & Governor Timeline

This is the **state-government layer** of the case: who Utah's governor appointed and when, what state officials said at which press conference, who decided the charges, and which Utah statutes and records policies shaped what the public can see. Federal actors are on the [FBI involvement timeline](./fbi-involvement-timeline); this page stays with the state.

**Important framing:** [Governor Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox), [Utah County Attorney Jeffrey Gray](/People/jeffrey-s-gray), DPS Commissioner Beau Mason, and [Phil Lyman](/distraction_people/Phil_Lyman) are all living people. Nothing on this page asserts that any of them committed a crime. Where citizen researchers have made accusations, they are labeled as claims and the counter-explanation is given.

## 2025 — Appointments and Statutes Before the Event

* **January 9, 2025** — Utah **SB0082**, the Autopsy Photo Amendments bill, was introduced in the legislature; it criminalizes knowingly sharing autopsy photographs from a medical examiner's record as a class B misdemeanor and tightens release rules for examiner records — see [Utah Autopsy Law SB0082](/Medical/Autopsy/autopsy_law_sb0082).

* **May 2, 2025** — Governor Cox appointed **Judge Tony F. Graf Jr.** to Utah's Fourth District Court; Graf had previously served as a Deputy Utah County Attorney prosecuting felony cases. He would later preside over the defendant's first appearance — [Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf).

* **May 7, 2025** — SB0082 took effect. Researchers note that its drafting attorney was the subject of unusual search interest from Israeli IP ranges in the same week, an inference drawn from search-trend data rather than from any record — [Utah Autopsy Law Changes](/Medical/utah_autopsy_law_changes).

* **July 1, 2025** — **[Dr. Deirdre Amaro](/People/deirdre-amaro)** began work full-time as Utah's chief medical examiner, two months before the shooting; the examiner's office is profiled on [Medical Examiner](/Medical/medical_examiner). A competing online claim that a different individual was installed as state examiner in September 2025 has **no supporting record** — see [Angela Hammond](/People/angela-hammond).

* **June–July 2025 (date disputed)** — **[Beau Mason](/distraction_people/Beau_Mason)** became Commissioner of the Utah Department of Public Safety by appointment of Governor Cox. The master file carries two dates for this — June 2025 in one entry and **July 17, 2025** in another, replacing Jess Anderson. See [Utah DPS — Beau Mason](/Law_Enforcement/utah-dps-beau-mason).

* **Summer 2025 (context)** — Kirk and Cox had a documented public political conflict, with Kirk having publicly called for Cox's expulsion from the Republican Party; the friction is relevant context for later commentary and nothing more, and is discussed on [Cox Foreign Meetings (Claims)](/Planes/Cox-Foreign-Meetings).

## September 10, 2025 — Day of the Shooting

* **12:23 PM MDT** — Kirk was shot in the [UVU](/UVU/overview) courtyard west of the [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center); the state's initial response ran through [UVU campus police](/Law_Enforcement/uvu-campus-police), the [Utah County Sheriff's Office](/Law_Enforcement/utah-county-sheriff), and DPS.

* **Afternoon** — The Utah **State Bureau of Investigation**, a DPS component, took a lead investigative role that it retained; a later filing states SBI has had **28 different law enforcement agencies** produce evidence in the case.

* **Afternoon** — Cox issued public statements condemning the killing and calling for calm; the sequence of official statements is tracked on [Press Conferences & Statements](./press-conferences-statements-timeline).

* **Evening** — Kirk was pronounced deceased at [Timpanogos Regional Hospital](/Locations/Timpanogos_Regional_Hospital) — the transport decision is disputed at [was a nearer trauma center bypassed?](/Suspicious/Medical/trauma-center-bypassed) — placing the death under the state medical examiner's jurisdiction and therefore under the records rules changed in May — [Autopsy Report Not Public](/Medical/autopsy_report_not_public).

## September 11–12, 2025 — Press Conferences and the Arrest

* **September 11, evening** — A press conference in Utah County presented enhanced suspect photos and rooftop video. Attendees are listed in the master file as Governor Cox, Lt. Governor [Deidre Henderson](/People/deidre-henderson), FBI Director [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel) — whose briefings are tracked at [Kash Patel press briefings](/FBI/Kash_Patel_Briefings) — Deputy Director [Dan Bongino](/People/dan-bongino), Sheriff **[Mike Smith](/People/mike-smith)**, and FBI Special Agent in Charge **[Robert Bohls](/People/robert-bohls)**.

* **September 11, evening (disputed)** — Citizen researchers argue that officials on that stage already knew the defendant was in custody, because a defense exhibit timestamps a Miranda advisement at **6:25 PM** that evening. Officials have not acknowledged this, and the official position places formal arrest early on September 12; both accounts are laid out on the [arrest and affidavit timeline](./arrest-booking-affidavit-timeline). Whether anyone on the stage knew is unestablished.

* **September 12, morning** — Cox announced the arrest publicly and, in remarks quoted by Utah outlets, **read the engravings** reported on the recovered casings, including "Notices, bulges, OwO what's this?", "Hey fascist! CATCH!", and the "Bella ciao" line. This is one of the few instances where a state official put physical-evidence detail directly into the public record — see [Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle). Cox also used a **~33-hour manhunt** framing and remarked that he had hoped the perpetrator would not be "one of us" (Utah) — rhetoric citizen analysts (e.g. **@MJTruthUltra**) clock-check against competing custody times on the [Kash Patel manhunt timeline](./kash-patel-manhunt-timeline). Earlier state messaging also warned about "tremendous" disinformation and urged people to put phones down ([Press Conferences & Statements](./press-conferences-statements-timeline)).

* **September 12** — DPS Commissioner Mason described the recovered weapon publicly as a "high powered bolt action rifle" and released enhanced photographs of the suspect, which became their own dispute on [Enhanced Suspect Photos](/FBI/Enhanced_Suspect_Photos).

## September 13–16, 2025 — The Crime Scene and the Charges

* **Friday evening, ~September 12, 2025 (Judicial Watch / GRAMA sequencing)** — Citizen analysis of **Judicial Watch** university clean-up documents and GRAMA materials, amplified by **@BasedSamParker** and **@TomFitton**, describes UVU grounds staff being told by **local police** late Friday (~8 PM) that **sod cut-out would not be needed** and a **hose-down** might suffice ([GRAMA records](/CoverUp/UVU_GRAMA_Records), [pre-shooting warnings in the same records](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/uvu-grama-warnings)) — but work waited because the **FBI was still processing** the scene. Treat as a **document-based chronology claim** about facilities response, not a finding that any named official destroyed evidence.

* **Saturday, September 13, 2025 (claimed sequencing)** — The same GRAMA/Judicial Watch readings say planning for **deeper excavation** (citizen summaries cite roughly **~10 inches** of topsoil) and hardscape replacement advanced **before** contractor **[Daniel Merrell](/Tent/Daniel_Merrell_Hardscape_Utah)**'s Sunday "paver emergency." The company itself is profiled at [Hardscape Specialties LLC](/Tent/Hardscape_Specialties) and, as a corporate entity, at [Hardscape Specialties LLC — UVU tent paving](/TPUSA/LLCs/hardscape-specialties-llc). Sam Parker and others stress that Merrell was the **install crew**, not the origin of the dig decision — important when mapping who set the schedule ([scene-changes paving timeline](./scene-changes-paving-timeline), [Dirt Removed](/CoverUp/Dirt_Removed)).

* **~September 14, 2025 (a Sunday)** — Excavation and paving work continued in the tent and courtyard area, roughly four days after the shooting; the work and the soil removal are documented on [Crime Scene Paving](/FBI/Crime_Scene_Paving), [Pavers — Dan Merrell's Account](/UVU/Pavers), [the September 14 paving timeline](/Tent/Paving_Timeline_Sept_14), [site alteration evidence](/Tent/Site_Alteration_Evidence), and Judicial Watch university document releases recirculated July 2026.

* **The "by Monday" attribution** — The contractor, **Daniel Merrell** of Hardscape Utah, has said publicly that an on-site coordinator told him: *"This is above our pay grade. The governor and the FBI said they want this done by Monday."* This is a **contractor recounting what a coordinator told him** — second-hand attribution, not a document, not a statement by the governor's office, and not confirmed by any state record. It is reported here because it is on the record from a named witness, and it is the single most-cited basis for citizen claims that the state ordered the scene destroyed. Full treatment on [Daniel Merrell — Hardscape Utah](/Tent/Daniel_Merrell_Hardscape_Utah) and [Hardscape Specialties LLC](/UVU/Hardscape-Specialties).

* **The paving-order claim** — Some commentators state flatly that Cox "ordered the crime scene torn up and paved over." **No document establishing such an order has been produced.** The claim traces back to the second-hand quote above (and has mutated in social posts from second-hand coordinator language into a direct gubernatorial order). It should be read as an **allegation, not a finding** — see [Site Changes and Crime-Scene Handling](/After/Site_Changes_And_Crime_Scene_Handling).

* **Counter-explanation (attributed)** — Defenders, including commentary associated with **@Liz_Wheeler** and on-ground sources, argue investigators **released** the area after multi-day processing and that paving addressed **biohazard cleanup and campus reopening**, not evidence destruction. [Liz Wheeler](/People/liz-wheeler) is a living person and asserts no wrongdoing by anyone here. Both the urgency narrative and the restoration narrative remain disputed.

* **September 16, 2025** — The **Utah County Attorney's Office** under Jeffrey Gray filed charges: aggravated murder, obstruction of justice, and felony discharge of a firearm causing serious bodily injury, with notice of intent to seek the death penalty. The office is profiled on [Prosecution Team](/court/prosecution-team).

* **September 16, 2025** — First appearance by video before Judge Graf, held without bail, at the [Fourth District Court in Provo](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo).

## Late 2025 — 2026: Conflicts, Records, and Commentary

* **Prosecutor conflict motion** — The defense moved for a special prosecutor, arguing that Chief Deputy County Attorney **[Chad Grunander](/People/chad-e-grunander)** had an inherent conflict because his 18-year-old daughter was present at the rally, approximately 85 feet from Kirk, and texted him in real time. County Attorney Gray responded that no conflict exists and declined a formal screening; the defense argued he lacks authority to self-assess — [Prosecutor Conflict](/CoverUp/Prosecutor_Conflict).

* **Testimony correction** — During the hearing Gray testified that he was at a **budget meeting in Layton, Davis County** on the relevant date, not Ogden as he had initially stated at a press conference. Both are his own statements; the correction is on the record.

* **GRAMA records** — UVU and state agencies have denied or delayed multiple Government Records Access and Management Act requests for campus footage and incident records, tracked on [UVU GRAMA Records](/CoverUp/UVU_GRAMA_Records) and [UVU HD Footage Withheld](/CoverUp/UVU_HD_Footage_Withheld). At the **governor's office** layer, at least one 2026 requester (**@walkingwormfood**) reported a **~$615** fee and **denied public-interest waiver** for law-enforcement memos provided to Cox before televised motive remarks — a concrete process complaint, not proof of concealment. Separate social posts allege structural GRAMA-appeal changes involving legislative allies of the governor; treat those as **unverified political process claims** pending primary verification.

* **Autopsy records posture** — Utah uses a state medical examiner rather than county coroners, and examiner reports are withheld during an active capital case as standard practice. Whether that standard practice plus the newly effective SB0082 amounts to an ordinary legal posture or an unusually tight seal is the argument on [Utah Autopsy Law](/CoverUp/Utah_Autopsy_Law) and [Autopsy Sealed by Examiner](/CoverUp/Autopsy_Sealed_Examiner).

* **Phil Lyman commentary** — The former state legislator has posted repeatedly about the case. Citizen researchers note that his vehicle is claimed to have been parked near the defendant's reported 11:49 AM walking route, that he was inside the Losee Center, and that he was among the first on camera to state a direction for the shot. Those are **claims by online researchers**, he has not been accused by any authority of any offense, and the ordinary explanation — a local political figure attending a campus event and answering reporters — has not been ruled out. See [Phil Lyman](/distraction_people/Phil_Lyman).

* **Sheriff's office turnover** — The Washington County sheriff's later departure from office is tracked on [Brooksby Resignation](/Suspicious/Law_Enforcement/brooksby-resignation); no official connection to this case has been established.

* **July 2026 — UVU leadership** — Cox publicly congratulated **Jon Anderson** as incoming UVU president during the post-**Astrid S. Tuminez** transition (**@GovCox**). Recorded here as state–campus institutional continuity, **not** as evidence of involvement in the shooting ([UVU institutional response](/UVU/UVU-Institutional-Response)).

## Conflicts and Disputed Points

| Point in dispute | Version A | Version B |
| --- | --- | --- |
| DPS commissioner appointment date | **June 2025** | **July 17, 2025**, replacing Jess Anderson |
| Who set the "by Monday" scene deadline | A coordinator told the contractor it came from **"the governor and the FBI"**; JW/GRAMA readings describe Fri hose-only → Sat deep excavate → Sun Merrell install | **No document, order, or official confirmation** has been produced; defenders cite biohazard cleanup and campus reopening after multi-day processing |
| Whether officials knew of custody on Sept 11 | Defense exhibit timestamps Miranda at **6:25 PM** that evening | Official position: formal arrest **04:00 on Sept 12** |
| State medical examiner in September 2025 | **Dr. Deirdre Amaro**, in the role since July 1, 2025 | An online claim names a different, newly installed examiner — **unsupported by records** |
| Prosecutor conflict | Defense: a family member at the scene creates structural bias | County Attorney: no conflict exists, no screening needed |
| Records posture | Standard sealing during an active capital homicide case | An unusually tight seal, tightened further by a law effective four months earlier |

## Open Questions

1. Does any written state directive exist setting a "by Monday" completion date for work in the tent and courtyard area, and if so who signed it?
2. Which state officials were briefed on the defendant's status before the evening of September 11, and at what times?
3. Why did the state release casing-engraving detail publicly while withholding the medical examiner's findings?
4. What is the complete GRAMA denial log for this case across UVU, DPS, and the county, with the exemption cited for each denial?
5. Was any conflict-of-interest screening ever performed inside the county attorney's office, and is there a written record of the decision not to screen?
6. What did DPS and SBI know about federal aircraft activity in Utah Valley airspace on September 10, and when — see the [N1098L timeline](./n1098l-plane-activity-timeline)?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

The unreleased state directives and internal communications about the crime-scene restoration schedule, the governor's office and DPS communications logs for September 10–12, 2025, the complete GRAMA denial log with the exemption cited for each request, the medical examiner's full report and the internal record of how SB0082 was applied to it, and the county attorney's written conflict-screening decision are things the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
