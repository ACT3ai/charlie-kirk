# Kash Patel Press Briefings

FBI Director **[Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel)** led the highest-profile public briefings after the
September 10, 2025 killing of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview).
Commentators on X and in independent media have focused on what Patel said — and
what he did not say — as a window into how the federal investigation was framed
for the public. This page documents those reported statements and the questions
they raised. Nothing here asserts that Patel or any other official committed a
crime or knew of the assassination in advance.

## Why the Briefings Matter

The FBI sits at the center of evidence collection and the public narrative in a
federal homicide. When the Director himself briefs the press, every word choice,
every piece of footage released, and every number emphasized becomes part of the
official record citizen investigators must reconcile with primary documents. The
briefings are therefore a first-priority thread — not because rhetoric proves
cover-up, but because federal leadership set the terms on which millions of
people understood the case. Patel's framing is also entangled with a separate
on-record dispute: former NCTC director [Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent)
says his center's foreign-nexus review was ordered to stop once a suspect was
in custody, and reporting ties that reported shutdown to Patel's FBI.

## The "Valhalla" Remark

At a post-incident press conference, Patel reportedly said something to the
effect of *"I'll see you in Valhalla, Charlie."* Commentators including
[Buckley Carlson](/Videos/buckley-carlson-kash-patel-valhalla) noted that Kirk was
a devout Christian and that Valhalla is a Norse/pagan afterlife concept rejected
by orthodox Christianity. Critics asked why a pagan reference was emphasized at a
briefing for a Christian victim, and why no official has since been compelled to
explain the remark on the record.

**Source:** [@FurkanGozukara on X](https://x.com/FurkanGozukara/status/2046380775579209982),
April 21, 2026 (video clip of Buckley Carlson commentary).

## The "33 Hours" Emphasis

Patel reportedly emphasized that it took **33 hours** to bring [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)
to justice, repeating the figure several times. Citizen investigators have
contrasted that public framing with court-document timelines suggesting Robinson
was [Mirandized earlier on September 11](/CoverUp/Custody_Confession_Timeline)
than the "33-hour manhunt" narrative implies. Counting 33 hours from the
Wednesday 12:23 PM shooting lands near **9:23 PM Utah time on September 11** —
roughly three hours *after* the reported 6:25 PM Miranda time. Commentators
including Buckley Carlson also flag the repeated emphasis on the number 33 as
odd "signaling"; the research record characterizes it as "Masonic 33" messaging.
These are interpretive claims, not findings of misconduct.

## Contested Custody Timeline (As Reported)

The single most-cited reason the briefings draw scrutiny is that key public
milestones reportedly occurred *after* Robinson was already in custody. From the
research record (court-document and Baron Coleman timeline):

| Time (Sep 11, MDT) | Reported event |
|--------------------|----------------|
| 6:25 PM | Court documents reportedly show Robinson **read his Miranda rights** at the Washington County Sheriff's office |
| 7:57 PM | The Discord "confession" messages ("turning myself in in a few moments") reportedly appear — ~90 min after the Miranda time |
| 7:58 PM | Utah County **press conference** releases "enhanced" stairwell photos and roof-jump video |
| 8:02–8:04 PM | Sheriff Brooksby reportedly says Robinson wants to turn himself in and calls Sheriff Smith — who is standing at the press conference |
| 10:00 PM | Officer Brian Davis reportedly "arrests" Robinson at the Washington County Sheriff's office |
| Sep 12, ~7 AM | Patel announces a **"33-hour manhunt"** |

If the 6:25 PM Miranda time holds, the "manhunt" framing and the enhanced-photo
release both post-date custody. The research record lists roughly a dozen state
and federal officials — including Patel, Bongino, and SAC Bohls — who it says
have never publicly acknowledged the earlier custody time. Treat every timestamp
as a lead to verify against primary filings.

## Selective Low-Quality Video Release

Commentators noted that the FBI released a very low-quality video of a figure
allegedly jumping from a roof, while higher-fidelity campus surveillance
reportedly existed. Buckley Carlson, in the same clip, asked why investigators
released "1973 quality VHS tape video" when the campus had modern cameras. See
[Evidence & Footage Preservation](./Evidence_Footage) and
[Enhanced Suspect Photos](./Enhanced_Suspect_Photos) for the stewardship questions
that follow from selective release.

## September 11 Utah County Press Conference

A press conference on the evening of September 11, 2025 reportedly included
**Utah Governor [Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox)**, Lieutenant Governor Henderson, **FBI Director Kash
Patel**, Deputy FBI Director **[Dan Bongino](/People/dan-bongino)**, Utah County Sheriff [Mike Smith](/People/mike-smith), and
FBI SAC **[Robert Bohls](/People/robert-bohls)** (see [Salt Lake City Field Office](./SLC_Field_Office)).
That event coincided with the release of "enhanced" stairwell suspect photos and
roof-jump footage — timing that Baron Coleman and others have questioned because
Robinson was reportedly already in custody. See [Enhanced Suspect Photos](./Enhanced_Suspect_Photos).

## Open Questions

- What was the stated rationale for the Valhalla remark, and is there an official transcript?
- How was the "33 hours" figure calculated relative to documented custody times?
- Which camera systems produced the released roof video, and what higher-fidelity
  sources were withheld and under what legal authority?
- Who authorized the September 11 press conference photo and video releases?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* Patel's briefing transcripts, the custody-timeline records behind the 33-hour claim, and the chain-of-custody log for every video clip released at those press conferences are things that the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X (Attributed)

**[@ProjectConstitu](/Influencers/podcasts-project-constitution)** (January 2026) alleged via an unnamed "whistleblower" that **Kash Patel** and **Dan Bongino** prioritized social-media strategy after the shooting—**unverified**.

**[@JustTheTweets17](/Influencers/x/justthetweets17)**-class threads tie Patel to weekend **crime-scene paving** demands with Governor Cox. No public order document is produced here; treat as attributed allegation.

Official-side recaps note the FBI **$100,000 reward**, tip lines, and rapid messaging. Citizen investigators contrast those optics with **33-hour manhunt** framing versus reported earlier Miranda times (see custody table above). Patel's words are **public framing**, not proof of criminal intent.
