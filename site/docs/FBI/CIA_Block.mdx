# CIA–FBI Conflict

Former special-operations figure **Mike Glover** reported on the Mike Ritland
podcast that a high-level **CIA** contact told him CIA personnel were **blocked
by the FBI** from participating in or advancing aspects of the Charlie Kirk
investigation — allegedly turned around at the airport — and that the matter was
relayed to the Oval Office without corrective action. This page documents Glover's
reported account. We do not assert the block occurred or that any official acted
improperly.

## Why Inter-Agency Conflict Matters

Domestic homicides with national-security overlays often involve FBI primacy. If a
parallel CIA inquiry was shut down at the airport, that suggests the FBI controlled
not only local police but also sister intelligence agencies — tightening the
circle of who could investigate [foreign-nexus leads](./Foreign_Leads).

## Glover's Reported Claims

From the research record:

- A high-level CIA official directly told Glover that CIA agents were being
  **blocked by the FBI**.
- Agents were reportedly **physically turned around at the airport**.
- The block was allegedly **relayed to the Oval Office** and nothing was done.
- Glover characterized the FBI's official narrative as containing **lies and
  inconsistencies** — his opinion, not a court finding.

Glover is a public commentator and entrepreneur in the tactical training space;
treat the account as attributed testimony pending corroboration from agency records.

## Relationship to Other Block Claims

This allegation parallels:
