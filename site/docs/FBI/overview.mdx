The FBI section covers federal involvement and awareness in the Charlie Kirk
case — both before and after September 10, 2025 — and the questions that would
compel transparency about how the federal investigation was conducted. It is
significant because the FBI sits at the center of evidence collection, witness
coordination, and the public narrative, so any gap or inconsistency in the
federal record shapes everything downstream. Nothing in this section asserts as
fact that any FBI agent committed a crime; the pages document reported
allegations and open questions, not established conclusions.

The substance breaks into a few clear lines of inquiry. A reported leadership
transition at the Salt Lake City field office roughly one month before the
incident raises timing questions worth charting against the timeline. Reported
directives that local officers limit engagement with witnesses, if real, would
cut against best practices for preserving testimony. Questions about selective
footage release and chain of custody ask whether evidence was preserved and
indexed under formal legal instruments. And the aviation dimension asks whether
flight and telemetry data has been preserved for independent review. Each of
these is a lead to be corroborated or refuted, not a finding.

Readers should begin with the [FBI Investigation](./FBI_Investigation) memo,
which frames the full set of questions and the standard of rigor applied to
each. From there, the [Salt Lake City Field Office](./SLC_Field_Office),
[Witnesses & Local Coordination](./Witness_Coordination), and
[Evidence & Footage Preservation](./Evidence_Footage) pages each take one thread
in depth. The central question this section is trying to answer is simple to
state and hard to settle: did the federal investigation preserve and disclose
the evidence the public is entitled to see?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* FBI Form 302 witness interview reports and the Salt Lake City field office leadership-change records and chain-of-custody logs for the rifle and footage are things that the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
