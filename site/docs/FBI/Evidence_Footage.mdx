# Evidence & Footage Preservation

A recurring question in the FBI inquiry is media and data stewardship: the
existence of higher-fidelity recordings from institutional cameras or event
production systems, versus what has been publicly released piecemeal. Commentary
alleges the FBI **cropped videos**, released low-quality roof footage while
withholding 4K campus archives, and asked witnesses to delete phone video. See
[Kash Patel Briefings](./Kash_Patel_Briefings) and
[Enhanced Suspect Photos](./Enhanced_Suspect_Photos). Assertions that footage
release was constrained "to protect the investigation," followed by selective
release of some materials, should be evaluated for consistency.

## The Core Question

If the FBI or partnering agencies requested withholding, is there a formal
request letter, a preservation order, or a documented chain-of-custody note? If
not, who made the decision and under what authority? A standardized log of these
documents reduces speculation by making the documentary record explicit, rather
than leaving release decisions to inference.

## Evidence-Preservation Checklist

- What formal requests or legal instruments — subpoenas, letters, preservation
  orders — were issued regarding event footage, production archives, and nearby
  surveillance systems?
- Have all camera angles from the venue and from ingress and egress routes been
  preserved and indexed?
- Is there an unbroken chain of custody for each item, with dates and signatories?
- Where footage was released selectively, what was the stated rationale and who
  authorized the timing?

## Hospital and Interior Surveillance

Reported claims hold that the FBI seized [hospital interior footage](./Hospital_Footage_Seizure)
during the emergency response. Hospital and venue stewardship should be logged in
the same preservation checklist as campus cameras.

## Destroyed Physical Evidence: The Transport SUV

Footage is not the only stewardship concern. The research record alleges the
**SUV used to transport Kirk to the hospital was chopped into pieces, melted
down, and quickly disposed of** — removing a vehicle that would have carried
blood evidence, fragments, and interior camera or telematics data. If accurate,
destruction of a transport vehicle within a homicide investigation is a
chain-of-custody event the FBI record should account for: who authorized
disposal, when, and whether the vehicle was forensically processed first. This
is a reported claim requiring salvage/title records and disposal documentation
to confirm. See [SUV to Hospital](/Medical/overview).

## Witness Phone Footage

Beyond institutional cameras, the record alleges the FBI asked eyewitnesses to
**delete their own phone video** — including witness **Ryne Simmons**, who says
he submitted a close-up **4K/60fps** clip to help investigators and was then
called "a few times" at the federal level to confirm he had deleted it (he says
he kept a copy). Multiple witnesses separately report footage that was "edited or
deleted before they even got home." The full attributed accounts live on
[Witnesses & Local Coordination](./Witness_Coordination) and
[FBI Asked Delete Video](/CoverUp/FBI_Asked_Delete_Video).

## Digital Archive Pressure

The research record notes an FBI subpoena to identify the operator of
**Archive.is** / Archive.ph amid commentary about evidence pages disappearing from
the web. See reporting from [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com),
[404 Media](https://www.404media.co), and [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com)
on the criminal probe into the archive site's ownership.

## Aviation and Telemetry Data

The same preservation discipline applies to aviation data. What FAA, ADS-B,
radar, or military telemetry relevant to the time window has been preserved, and
has an independent expert reviewed it under chain of custody? Discrepancies
between public flight narratives and raw telemetry, if they exist, may be
sufficient to warrant escalation or compulsory process. See
[N1098L Spy Plane](../Planes/N1098L/overview) for the aircraft-specific record.

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* Any FBI letter ordering footage withheld and the unbroken chain-of-custody records for venue cameras and the raw FAA/ADS-B telemetry hidden behind public flight narratives are things that the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) may result in powerful truths coming out that aren't out yet.
