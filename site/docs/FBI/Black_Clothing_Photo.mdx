# Black Clothing Photo Discrepancy

Multiple eyewitnesses describe a man in **all black** — long greasy hair, trench
coat, mask, cargo pants, backpack — who does not match [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)'s known
appearance. Construction worker **[Dylan Hope](/People/dylan-hope)** and police dispatch reportedly
described this figure. A further claim holds that the photo shown to on-site
workers by **sheriffs did not match** the image the **FBI released publicly**.
This page documents that reported discrepancy. We do not assert the FBI concealed
a second shooter or that any official acted illegally.

## Why the Photo Mismatch Matters

If local officers showed witnesses one image and the FBI published another, the
public manhunt operated on a different visual target than the people closest to
the shooter path. That is either a communications failure or, as commentators
allege, evidence of narrative management. The question requires the actual image
sets — not characterizations.

See the full eyewitness record on [Black Clothing Suspect](/Suspects/Black_Clothing_Suspect).

## Reported Eyewitness Account

From the research record (Project Constitution / Dylan Hope coverage). Hope is
reported as a **26-year-old electrician** working at a property next to the
university:

> "He had black sunglasses. He had a black C-mask. He had a black trench coat,
> black cargo pants, and he was wearing a small backpack. And he also had long,
> greasy black hair."

- Dispatch and construction workers described a suspect in **all black** unlike Robinson.
- Hope went through the construction site path the shooter allegedly used.
- Sheriffs reportedly showed the crew a photo on Wednesday; the excavator operator
  said it **matched** at the time.
- Hope reportedly said the photo the officers showed the crew on **Wednesday** did
  **not** seem to look like the one the **F.B.I. released to the public on
  Thursday**.

**Source video cited:** Project Constitution — FBI Ordered Local Police Not to
Interview Witnesses (post id 1971655771524911105 in the video archive).

## FBI Public Narrative

The FBI's public narrative centered on Robinson and the [enhanced stairwell photos](./Enhanced_Suspect_Photos).
Commentators ask why the all-black-clad witness thread was not pursued with equal
visibility if dispatch and construction workers flagged it in real time.
