# Media Timeline Compilation

Multiple timelines exist for the Charlie Kirk case — **law-enforcement summaries**, **newsroom live blogs**, **visual reconstructions**, and **citizen-investigator chronologies**. They agree on the broad arc (UVU event → shooting → manhunt → Robinson charged) but diverge on **minute-level timestamps**, **suspect capture details**, and **which post-death events belong in "the story."** This page compiles the major published timelines so readers can see **where media agree and where they don't**.

Primary chronological docs on this site: [Timeline Overview](/Timeline/overview) and [September 8–13](/Timeline/Sept_8_to_13).

**Analytical note:** A **four-minute** shooting-time spread (12:23 vs. 12:27) appears across outlets and summaries. That is small in absolute terms but large for synchronized reconstruction — citizen investigators treat it as a reason to compare **primary footage timecodes** against published tickers ([Videos](/Videos/overview)).

### Aircraft and ISR entries (citizen-investigator timeline)

Alternative timelines add ADS-B entries the same day — e.g., **[SU-BTT](/Planes/SU-BTT)** departures and **[N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview)** passes — which **mainstream breaking-news tickers typically omitted** in week one ([Planes overview](/Planes/overview)). Inclusion or exclusion here is a **source-class difference**, not by itself proof of cover-up.

## 2026 — Courtroom Timeline as Media Story

By 2026, national desks increasingly treated **procedure** as the news:

- **Camera access** rulings and delayed preliminary hearings ([Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/us/judge-allows-cameras-charlie-kirk-assassination-case-delays-preliminary-hearing))
- **ATF ballistics unsealing** and defense emphasis on inconclusive results ([PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/did-a-bullet-analysis-clear-charlie-kirks-suspected-killer-what-an-inconclusive-result-means))
- **June 2026 contempt** against a prosecutor over media statements ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/7b3e65b0c7b12a06828e4f8f0337cb29))

Tyler Robinson remains **accused, not convicted**; courtroom timelines describe **motions and rulings**, not final guilt.

## Why Timelines Diverge

Common reasons compiled timelines disagree:

1. **Live-blog correction cycles** — early timestamps updated without always updating explainers.
2. **Different evidence bases** — CCTV vs. stage audio vs. ADS-B vs. anonymous tips.
3. **Editorial scope** — memorial politics omitted from crime tickers; aircraft omitted from human-interest pieces.
4. **Sealed materials** — public timelines cannot include discovery readers have not seen ([Legal: Evidence Sealing](/Legal/Evidence-Sealing-2026)).
