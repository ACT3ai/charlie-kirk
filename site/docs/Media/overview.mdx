Media coverage is where most of the public first encountered this case, and how it was framed early on still drives what most people believe happened. Mainstream outlets supplied the factual backbone — the UVU shooting, Charlie's death, the law-enforcement response, and the charging of Tyler Robinson — alongside legal-process updates, workplace-retaliation stories, and memorial politics including the posthumous Presidential Medal of Freedom. Independent journalists, podcasters, and citizen investigators pushed further into ballistics disputes, flight paths, leaked-text motive theories, and allegations that footage was deleted or discussions were gag-ordered.

The substance of this section spans both what was published and what may have been kept from publication. [Media Articles](./Media_Articles) is the curated index tracing claims back to wire services, major papers, and broadcast pieces. [Media Censorship](./Censorship) collects gag-order reporting, platform moderation claims, and witness delete-video allegations. [Media Response](./Media_Response) tracks the press arc from September 10 live blogs through 2026 contempt coverage. [Leaked Kirk Texts](./Leaked-Kirk-Texts), [Mainstream vs Alternative Media](./Mainstream-vs-Alternative-Media), and [Presidential Medal of Freedom](./Presidential-Medal-of-Freedom) cover topics that do not fit neatly into a single outlet listing.

Start with [Media Articles](./Media_Articles) if you want a fast map of who reported what, then [Mainstream vs Alternative Media](./Mainstream-vs-Alternative-Media) to see which questions each ecosystem emphasizes. Move to [Media Censorship](./Censorship) and [Leaked Kirk Texts](./Leaked-Kirk-Texts) when evaluating whether the public record may be incomplete. Because verification standards vary widely, the goal here is not to rate outlets but to point toward primary footage, court filings, and official releases wherever possible.
