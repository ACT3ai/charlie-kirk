# Tyler Robinson Trial Press

Once **State of Utah v. Tyler Robinson** (Case **251403576**) moved past the manhunt, press value
shifted from **who shot Charlie Kirk** to **what the state can prove** and **what stays sealed**.
Robinson is **charged, not convicted**.

## 2025 — entering the docket

* **NYT** — first court appearance coverage ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/11/us/charlie-kirk-assassination-suspect-hearing.html)).
* **WaPo** — interactive suspect digital-life profile ([WaPo](https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/interactive/2025/tyler-robinson-charlie-kirk-shooting/)).
* **Fox 13 / local** — gag-order atmosphere, lawyers silent ([Utah Local Press](./utah-local-press)).

## December 2025 — gag order as press story

Judge **Tony Graf** issued a broad **gag order on the court's own motion** per reporting — restricting
parties, counsel, and witnesses. Press could still report **procedure**, but **witness interviews**
dried up ([Media Censorship](./Censorship), [court gag](/court/gag-orders-sealing)).

## March 10, 2026 — seal fight

Defense moved to **seal** filings discussing a **4-page ATF report** — **INCONCLUSIVE** on the
autopsy jacket vs seized rifle. A **news coalition** filed opposing memoranda:

**Deseret News, Salt Lake Tribune, AP, NYT, Fox News, CBS**, and others (per research file).

Media argument: if defense calls material **exculpatory**, sealing from the public is hard to justify.
Partial **unsealing** followed ([Fox ATF story](https://www.foxnews.com/us/tyler-robinson-judge-unseals-atf-report-assassination-charlie-kirk)).
