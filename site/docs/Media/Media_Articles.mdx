# Media Articles

A running list of news and reference articles covering the Charlie Kirk case, grouped by outlet. Each title links to the original article on the publisher's site, followed by a short note on what makes that piece distinctive. Inclusion here is for reference and does not imply endorsement of any outlet's framing or conclusions.

### Reference / Encyclopedic

- **[Assassination of Charlie Kirk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Charlie_Kirk)** — Wikipedia. Comprehensive, neutral encyclopedic overview with timeline, exact location details (coordinates, 142-yard sniper shot), suspect background, weapon specifics, and the 10 counts including aggravated murder. Synthesizes verified facts into one reference source with maps and structured data, where most news pieces focus on breaking updates rather than a full factual compilation.
- **[Assassination of Charlie Kirk | Death, Manhunt, Tyler Robinson](https://www.britannica.com/event/Assassination-of-Charlie-Kirk)** — Britannica. Concise factual summary of the event, suspect arrest, political context, and immediate reactions. Structured encyclopedia-style entry with key questions answered and historical framing; less sensational than daily news, emphasizing verified sequence over speculation.
- **[Utah Valley Shooting Updates](https://www.fbi.gov/news/press-releases/utah-valley-shooting-updates)** — FBI. Official law-enforcement press releases and updates on the investigation, direct from the agency.

### Wire Services

- **[Conservative activist Charlie Kirk assassinated at Utah university](https://apnews.com/article/charlie-kirk-conservative-activist-shot-546165a8151104e0938a5e085be1e8bd)** — AP News. Reports the shooting as a "political assassination" from a rooftop, the initial manhunt, and the governor's statements. Straightforward wire-service factual account with official quotes that emphasizes political context early on.
- **[Judge holds prosecutors in Charlie Kirk murder case in contempt for comments about the defendant](https://apnews.com/article/7b3e65b0c7b12a06828e4f8f0337cb29)** — AP News. Wire coverage of the June 2026 contempt finding against prosecutors over public comments about the defendant.
- **[The Charlie Kirk purge: How 600 Americans were punished in a pro-Trump crackdown](https://www.reuters.com/investigations/charlie-kirk-purge-how-600-americans-were-punished-pro-trump-crackdown-2025-11-19/)** — Reuters. Investigates the post-assassination wave of 600+ firings, suspensions, and investigations over social media comments, detailing tactics like Libs of TikTok shaming and political pressure from Trump allies. Distinctive for its in-depth focus on the scale of retaliation and free-speech ironies, going beyond event coverage into societal backlash.
- **[Utah judge finds prosecutor in contempt of court in Kirk case](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/utah-judge-finds-prosecutor-contempt-court-kirk-case-2026-06-26/)** — Reuters. Wire account of the Utah judge's contempt ruling, framed around the courtroom and legal-process angle.
- **[Rubio to host summit on political violence on July 15](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/rubio-host-summit-political-violence-july-15-2026-06-26/)** — Reuters. Reports a planned government summit on political violence, connecting the case to a broader policy response.

Distinctive for its visual and spatial reconstruction and data-driven timeline, unusual in text-only reports.
- **['A Lot of Blood': Witnesses Describe the Charlie Kirk Shooting](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/10/us/charlie-kirk-shooting-witnesses-utah.html)** — Eyewitness accounts of the chaotic scene and immediate reactions; raw personal testimonies giving a vivid on-the-ground perspective.
- **[Inside The Times's Reporting and Judgment Calls on Charlie Kirk's Assassination](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/20/us/charlie-kirk-shooting-news-coverage.html)** — A meta piece on the paper's editorial decisions, verification challenges, and graphic-content handling. Behind-the-scenes look at the journalism process and self-reflective on media ethics during a breaking tragedy.
