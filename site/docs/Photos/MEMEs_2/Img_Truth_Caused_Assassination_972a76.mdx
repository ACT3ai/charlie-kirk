## What This Image Shows

A meme built from a single line of type and five portraits. The header reads: "YOU DON'T GET ASSASSINATED FOR LYING." Below it, three smaller portraits are stacked on the left — Malcolm X, Martin Luther King Jr. and Robert F. Kennedy — and two larger ones sit on the right, [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) above John F. Kennedy.

The argument is by placement. Setting Kirk alongside four figures whose deaths are widely understood as political killings asserts, without saying so, that his death belongs in the same category and for the same reason. It is a claim about motive dressed as a claim about company.

As evidence it establishes nothing about **September 10, 2025**, and the premise in the header is not true as stated — people are killed for many reasons, and the reverse implication that anyone assassinated must have been telling the truth does not follow. What the meme does record is how quickly Kirk's death was absorbed into an existing frame of American political assassinations, which is a real feature of how this case has been understood.
