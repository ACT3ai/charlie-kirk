## About This Cluster

Among the most-repeated claims in this case is that [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) told people close to him, shortly before **September 10, 2025**, that he believed he was in danger. If a warning of that kind was really given and can be sourced, it reframes everything downstream — it turns an unforeseeable event into one somebody saw coming. This single-image cluster holds the graphic that carried that claim to a mass audience.

The image is a composite thumbnail: two close-up portraits flanking a smartphone screen showing a blue message bubble reading "They are going to KILL me," with the caption "2 days before assassination" and the label "His Friend" identifying the source as an unnamed acquaintance. A speech-bubble pointer visually attributes the message to the man on the left. The graphic is presentation, not documentation — it does not show the full conversation, the phone, the date stamp, or who either person is, and the friend is not named. The underlying account has been repeated by several commentators, but this site treats it as a reported second-hand claim rather than a verified message, and no inference should be drawn from it about anyone's prior knowledge of what would happen.

The sourced treatment of what Kirk is reported to have said, and to whom, is at [Reported Quotes and Warnings](/Charlie/Reported_Quotes_and_Warnings), with the surrounding weeks covered at [Pre-Event Timeline](/Charlie/Pre_Event_Timeline) and [Before](/Timeline/overview). The companion donor-quote meme is at [2 Kill Me](/Photos/MEMEs_2/2_Kill_Me/overview); the wider motive argument is at [Motive](/Motive/overview).
