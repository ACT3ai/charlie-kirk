## What This Image Shows

A composite thumbnail built for maximum impact: two close-up portraits flanking a central image of a smartphone screen, with a speech-bubble pointer drawn from the man on the left to the message.

The on-screen text reads "They are going to KILL me," with the phone status bar showing 9:41 and two captions added by the maker: "2 days before assassination" and, beneath one portrait, "His Friend."

Everything load-bearing here is asserted rather than shown. The friend is not named. The message has no visible sender, recipient, date stamp or surrounding conversation. The screen is a graphic reconstruction rather than an actual screenshot of a phone, so nothing about it can be authenticated from the image.

That does not make the underlying account false — several commentators have repeated a report that [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) expressed fear for his life shortly before **September 10, 2025**. It makes it a second-hand claim awaiting its source. This site records it as such and draws no inference from it about anyone's prior knowledge of what would happen.
