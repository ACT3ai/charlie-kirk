## What This Image Shows

A top-down **Google Earth** capture of a suburban residential block that backs onto a multi-lane road and a set of commercial or institutional parking lots. A thick pink circle has been drawn by hand around one property on the left of the frame - a house with a dark gabled roof, bare dirt yard areas and a light blue-roofed outbuilding beside it.

The only on-screen text is the **Google Earth** attribution in the corner. Everything else about the image is the annotation: someone wanted a specific address looked at, and circled it without writing why on the face of the graphic. Images of this kind circulate constantly in open-source investigation threads, and they are only as good as the sourcing that travels with them, which in this case did not survive the repost chain.

The site publishes it as part of the record of what was being circulated, not as an indication that anyone at that address is connected to anything. No occupant is named here and no claim is made about any resident. Address-level threads that do have documentation behind them are handled on [Locations](/Locations/overview), and the campus-adjacent geography is covered by [Maps and Site Geometry](/Photos/Maps/overview) and [Maps](/maps/overview).
