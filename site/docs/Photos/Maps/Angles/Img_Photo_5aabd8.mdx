## What This Image Shows

An aerial photograph of the manicured lawn area bordered by concrete walkways and retaining walls, overlaid with bright white measurement lines, distance figures and labels, plus a medical inset in the corner.

The map side marks a rectangle labelled **KIRK** near the **Hall of Flags**, with distances of **12** feet and **50** feet noted on the grass and additional reference figures around the edge. The inset shows skull and pelvis outlines from front, back and right profile, with the **PARIETAL** and **SQUAMOUS TEMPORAL** regions labelled and impact points marked in red, along with **RIGHT**, **LEFT**, **RADIUS** and **FRONT** orientation labels. Together the two halves attempt to connect a position on the ground to a specific point of impact on the body.

The anatomical labels here are the analyst's proposition about where the wound was, and they should not be read as the medical examiner's findings, which the site tracks separately and which have their own disputes about who signed off on the report. Treat the whole diagram as an argument to be checked against the record when the record is released.
