## What This Image Shows

A composite analytical diagram built on a 3D aerial render of the concrete structure beside the courtyard, with its flat reddish-brown roof divided by pillars, and the grass area below where line markings are visible.

The overlay carries compass bearings and angle marks - **210** degrees, **240**, **250** and **300** are legible, along with a percentage figure and the label **Hall of Flags**. A red seated-figure icon is boxed in the lawn to mark where **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** was positioned, and a picture-in-picture inset with anatomical graphics sits alongside. The whole graphic is one researcher's attempt to reconcile a firing bearing with the wound path, published on social media with watermarks intact.

It is a reconstruction, not a forensic report. The bearings are measured off a photogrammetry render whose roof heights are software approximations, and the anatomical inset is a generic diagram rather than the autopsy record. What it does usefully is state a testable proposition: if the shot came from the marked bearing, the wound path should have a particular orientation.
