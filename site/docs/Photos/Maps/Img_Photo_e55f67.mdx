## What This Image Shows

A news graphic built on a satellite view of the campus, with coloured circular markers, connecting lines and a printed key running beneath the map.

The key reads **Suspect's arrival**, **Rooftop access**, **Rifle found**, **Sniper's perch**, **Charlie Kirk**, **Bullet's path** and **Suspect's movements**. The caption below states that this is an approximation of the suspected movements of **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)**, described as [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s alleged assassin, based on court documents, video from the scene and conversations with law-enforcement sources, credited to Google Maps and Fox News Digital. The word approximation is the publisher's own, and the site treats every element on this map as an allegation from the prosecution's account rather than as established fact; Tyler Robinson has not been convicted and the charges are contested.

The value of the graphic is that it is the only one in this cluster that draws a **bullet path** as a line. That single stroke is the claim every ballistic and acoustic argument on this site pushes against, from the wound trajectory work to the sound-arrival analysis.
