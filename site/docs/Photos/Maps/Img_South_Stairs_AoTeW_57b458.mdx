## What This Image Shows

A composite reference plate split down the middle. On the left is a ground-level photograph of the modern concrete and glass academic building looking out over the terraced grass amphitheater. On the right is a 2D overhead map of the same place, with the pin labels **UVU courtyard**, **UCCU - UVU Branch**, **Hall of Flags**, **Office of Admissions**, **Accessibility Services** and **UVU Campus Tours** visible. Yellow arrows and orange boxes are drawn on both halves to tie one structural feature on the building to its position on the map. Small interface text credits a Street View style capture by **Luke Atticus Kennard**, dated Apr 2017 and marked eight years ago.

The feature being linked is a stair run on the south side. Stairs matter in this case because the movement account requires a person to get from a roof to ground level and away, and because separate footage of a figure on stairs became one of the most argued-over pieces of imagery in the whole investigation. Analysts have contested whether the figure on the stairs matches the person charged, pointing to differences in backpack and clothing; those are researcher arguments, not findings.

The honest limitation is the date. A 2017 capture may not show later construction or landscaping changes. For the stairs argument see [Proof It Was Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview), and for the geography see [Courtyard](/UVU/Courtyward), [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center) and [UVU Maps](/UVU/maps).
