## What This Image Shows

A vertical close-up of a white thermal adhesive label stuck at an angle to a brown paper bag. The label is partly stained on its left side by a light brown translucent spill that has soaked through and wrinkled the paper. The bag itself carries a printed white diagonal grid pattern and is heavily creased. Two white twisted-paper handles are visible at the top, one lying horizontally across the bag. The background is a smooth matte dark surface, slightly out of focus. A small grey oval reading Crop sits in the lower-left corner, an artifact of a mobile image-editing interface.

The reason this frame exists is prosaic and useful: order receipts carry names, order numbers, item counts, and often timestamps. Researchers photograph them because a legible receipt inside the vehicle would fix a time and a vendor, which is the sort of hard anchor that timeline reconstruction needs and mostly lacks.

The Crop artifact is worth noting. It shows the image was edited on a phone before publication, which matters when assessing provenance across this photograph set.
