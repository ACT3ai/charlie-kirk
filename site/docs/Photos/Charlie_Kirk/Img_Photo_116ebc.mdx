## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution cropped frame of a man with his head tilted back and to his left, exposing the neck and jawline, eyes closed or heavily squinted. He wears a white or light grey long-sleeve henley with FREEDOM arched across the chest in bold black capitals. A small black lavalier microphone is clipped to the collar on the viewer right side of his neck. Two hand-drawn red arrows are superimposed: one from the mid-right of the frame pointing down and left toward the right side of the neck and collar, the second from the lower left pointing up and right toward the center placket of the shirt. The backdrop is a white step-and-repeat with a red circular yrefy brush logo above his head, YREFY.COM below it in bold capitals, partial NING and NT USA lettering at left, and a partial dark circular logo containing THE at right.

The two arrows encode the two competing claims in one image. One points at the neck, where the wound is placed. The other points at the shirt placket, where proponents of the stage-origin argument say a device sat. Presenting them together is how the argument is made visually: that the device and the injury are in line with each other.

Alignment in a two-dimensional frame is not proof of anything. The arrows are the annotator addition, the underlying frame is heavily pixelated, and the official finding remains a rifle shot.
