## What This Image Shows

A vertical iMessage screenshot on a light background. The contact at the top is named Charlie with a circular avatar of a young man in a dark suit jacket and white collared shirt; a blue back arrow and a badge reading 36 sit at the upper left. Incoming messages appear in grey bubbles on the left and outgoing replies in blue on the right. A thin green border and a sliver of dark out-of-focus background run down the far left edge, indicating the capture was taken from a screen displaying something alongside the conversation.

The exchange, read from the capture: Yes you do. kayneeeeee. You are going to be the Iron Lady of America. I see it so frekaing clearly. i might be Moses tho. I might not see this whole thing through lmao. The reply reads: If that is the case, be my David! Then: Dude you do not even realize your power yet. if I tell you the true prophecy I know in my gut its really sad. But I hope its wrong.

This is one of the two exchanges researchers cite as showing that [Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) privately expected not to survive. The reading is contested even among people sympathetic to it — the register is casual, ends with lmao, and the Moses framing is scriptural rather than literal. A companion capture in this cluster highlights the be my David reply in red marker. Both are unverified screenshots with the counterparty unnamed.
