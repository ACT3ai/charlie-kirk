## What This Image Shows

A vertical screenshot of the donor-loss thread on the dark doodle wallpaper, running longer than the other captures in this cluster. A blue CK avatar marks the first and third [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) bubbles. The sequence: Just lost another huge Jewish donor. $2 million a year because we won not cancel [Tucker](/People/tucker-carlson). I am thinking of inviting [Candace](/People/candace-owens), at 3:49 PM, with donor highlighted in orange from an active search. A timestamp reading 23:02 appears beside the counterparty Ugghhh at 3:50 PM. Then at 3:54 PM: Jewish donors play into all the stereotypes. I cannot and will not be bullied like this, and Leaving me no choice but to leave the pro Israel cause. The counterparty then replies at length: Please do not invite Candace. That might feel good short term but it is not good long term, in my opinion. Like all groups, you are going to get wide variety of opinions. That nasty free will thing that God.

This is the fullest published version and therefore the most useful of the set, because it carries the counterparty response rather than only the attributed messages. The reply is a moderating one — cautioning against the invitation and arguing that any group contains a range of opinions — which changes the character of the exchange from a declaration into a disagreement.

The 23:02 marker beside a 3:50 PM bubble is an inconsistency worth noting rather than explaining away; it may be a device clock, a notification overlay, or an artifact of how the capture was assembled. These screenshots are unverified. The counterparty name is blacked out in every published version, no forensic authentication has been performed, and neither the messages nor the search highlight has been tested in any proceeding. They are recorded here as published, with attribution, and nothing in them establishes wrongdoing by any person.
