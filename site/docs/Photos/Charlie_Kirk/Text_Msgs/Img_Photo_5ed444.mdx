## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of the donor-loss thread over the doodle-pattern dark wallpaper. A blue circular avatar with the white initials CK sits to the left of the first message. The bubble is headed [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) in light blue and reads: Just lost another huge Jewish donor. $2 million a year because we won not cancel [Tucker](/People/tucker-carlson). I am thinking of inviting [Candace](/People/candace-owens), timestamped 3:49 PM. The word donor is highlighted in a translucent orange-yellow box from an active in-app search. A thin vertical sliver of another interface, showing a hint of green and a blurred image, runs down the far left edge.

This is a fourth capture of the same exchange, and the set of near-identical versions is itself informative. All of them show the same search highlight on donor, the same timestamps, and in several cases the same green edge sliver. That pattern is consistent with the messages having been surfaced by someone searching a chat archive for the word donor and then captured repeatedly, rather than with several independent leaks.

Provenance is the unresolved question. No source has published the underlying device, no forensic authentication exists, and the counterparty remains unnamed. These screenshots are unverified. The counterparty name is blacked out in every published version, no forensic authentication has been performed, and neither the messages nor the search highlight has been tested in any proceeding. They are recorded here as published, with attribution, and nothing in them establishes wrongdoing by any person.
