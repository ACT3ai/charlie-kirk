## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a social media post showing a scene under the outdoor canopy. The front valance is dark blue with white capitals reading OVE ME WRONG, the beginning cut off by the frame edge, and TOUR printed in red on the lighter blue canopy top. At center a young male figure is held upright, his white t-shirt pulled or rolled to his chest, exposing the torso; his chest, shirt, and extended right arm are heavily coated in a viscous red liquid. A man with medium-length brown hair in a black t-shirt and khaki trousers supports him from behind with both arms around the chest. A third person in a blue long-sleeved layer leans in from the left, and a fourth is hunched at the far edge. In the foreground a table is draped in dark blue with a lightning-bolt graphic. The interface language on the screenshot is French.

This frame belongs to the annotated aftermath set. The canopy branding places it under the tour canopy at the event, and the same scene appears in other frames in the After cluster with circles and arrows drawn onto the clothing.

The image is described here clinically and no person is identified. The visible torso and the red material are the features the annotators in this set consistently direct attention to, in the context of the dispute over wound location. For that dispute see [Cause of Death](/Cause_of_Death/overview) and [Shot in Chest Reversal](/Witnesses/Shot_in_Chest_Reversal); for the canopy and table layout see [The Tent](/Tent/overview).
