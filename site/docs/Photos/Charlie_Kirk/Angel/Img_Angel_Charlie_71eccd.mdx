## What This Image Shows

A daytime photograph of the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus with a large translucent graphic composited over the center of the frame: a barefoot young man with white wings, rising vertically. The underlying photograph shows a two-storey concrete building running across the middle distance, its upper walkway enclosed by black metal railing above a long row of glass windows. Several windows carry printed university banners — a climbing wall image, a green panel reading A PLACE FOR YOU, a student with a pen, a green UVU panel, a group of smiling students, another A PLACE FOR YOU, and a student holding a laptop. Beneath the walkway an open-air bypass holds a white pickup, a dark pickup, and a small orange compact excavator. In the foreground, terraced green lawn steps separated by thick light-grey concrete retaining walls curve down toward a plaza, forming an amphitheatre seating area at the right. In the center middle ground, a work crew is laying stone pavers on a freshly prepped dirt and gravel bed.

The overlay is memorial imagery made and circulated by supporters after [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). It is recorded here because it is part of how the event was received publicly.

The underlying photograph is separately useful. Site work around the event area — landscaping, hardscape, and concrete under and around the tent — is an active question in the investigation, and this frame documents paver installation, an excavator, and the state of the terraced lawn. Nothing in the image establishes when the work was done or by whom, and no connection between the work and the shooting is asserted here.
