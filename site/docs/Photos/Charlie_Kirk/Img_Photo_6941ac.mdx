## What This Image Shows

An eye-level interior view of the rear passenger area. The dark leather captain chair at right is heavily coated with dried reddish-brown smears and splatters, concentrated along the left edge of the cushion, the lower-middle backrest, and the top and side of the armrest; the seat has perforated centre panels and contrast white stitching. The back of the front passenger seat is at left, with the rear console below it carrying a cup holder and chrome-rimmed climate dials. The door panel behind shows a wood-grain accent and a chrome handle assembly. Through the clear window, the side of a white vehicle with partial lettering is visible. On the black carpet, three plastic water bottles lie on their sides, one a Fiji bottle with its pink flower label, alongside a small food wrapper.

This is among the most direct records of the vehicle condition in the whole set. It is described here clinically: dried reddish-brown material distributed across seat, backrest, and armrest surfaces. Commentators describe it as blood; no published record identifies it, and no analysis has been released.

The white vehicle visible through the window and the wider surroundings indicate the photographs were taken somewhere other than the scene, which bears on the chain-of-custody questions raised about this set.
