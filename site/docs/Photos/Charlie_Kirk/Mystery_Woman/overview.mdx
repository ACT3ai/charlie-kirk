## About This Cluster

This cluster holds the single screenshot behind one of the smaller but more pointed questions in the case: who actually signed off on **[Charlie Kirk's](/People/charlie-kirk)** autopsy report. The investigation notes record a private message claiming the report was signed by "a young woman that he — the medical examiner — had never met before," and that detail matters because an unsigned autopsy report is treated as a draft, meaning changes remain possible until the signature lands. Utah uses a state medical examiner rather than a county coroner, so the chain of custody on that document is short and traceable, which is exactly why researchers have pressed on it.

The image is a dark-mode messaging screenshot — three bubbles containing the account, a shocked-face reaction, and a short follow-up. It is a secondhand personal message, not a court filing or an official record, and this site treats it as such: the claim is reported with attribution and no person is identified or accused here. Nothing in the message establishes that anyone acted improperly, and ordinary explanations exist for a delegated or countersigned document. The open question researchers keep asking is narrower and answerable: who signed, in what role, and on what date.
