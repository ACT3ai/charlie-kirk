## What This Image Shows

A clean digital screenshot of a messaging app in dark mode against a solid black background. Three dark grey rounded bubbles are visible: a large one at the top containing five lines of white sans-serif text, a small nearly circular one beneath it holding a single yellow astonished-face emoji, and a medium one at the bottom with a single line of text. The capture is sharp and native rather than a photograph of a screen.

The account it relays, as recorded in the investigation notes, is that [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) autopsy was signed off by a young woman whom the medical examiner had never met before. The reason researchers treat that as significant is procedural: an autopsy report is a draft until it is signed, and changes remain possible up to that point, so who signed and in what capacity is a question with a concrete answer.

This is a private secondhand message, not a court filing or an official record, and it is recorded here with attribution and nothing more. No person is named or identified, nothing in it establishes that anyone acted improperly, and delegated or countersigned documents are ordinary in medical examiner offices. The open question is narrow and answerable: who signed, in what role, and on what date. See [The Medical Examiner](/Medical/medical_examiner), [Autopsy Report Not Public](/Medical/autopsy_report_not_public), and [Autopsy Sealed and the Examiner](/CoverUp/Autopsy_Sealed_Examiner).
