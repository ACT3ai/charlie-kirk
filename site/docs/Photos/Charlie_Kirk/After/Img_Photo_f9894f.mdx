## What This Image Shows

A photograph taken of a television or monitor displaying a news broadcast, with the characteristic rainbow moire pattern from photographing a pixel grid. The red ticker across the bottom reads BREAKING: [CHARLIE KIRK](/People/charlie-kirk) SHOT ON [UVU](/UVU/overview) CAMPUS. The broadcast image shows the abandoned setup on the grass: a light grey folding table set diagonally, its thin white metal legs forming triangular patterns, carrying a small round black portable fan, an open binder or stack of white papers catching the light, and a black flat object resembling a closed notebook, folder, or tablet case. A heavy royal blue drape hangs in vertical folds at left, the side wall of the tent. Loose white papers and debris are scattered on the lawn beneath and to the right of the table, with a dark shape at the far right of the background.

This is among the earliest broadcast records of the scene, before it was controlled, and it is the reason the frame is preserved. What was left on the table — the papers, the binder, the flat black case, the fan — is a recurring question in the investigation, because researchers have compared early frames against later ones to argue about what was removed.

Nothing here is interpreted. The image is a record of the table as broadcast. See [The Tent](/Tent/overview), [AV Team Mic Setup](/Mic/AV_Team_Mic_Setup), and [Timeline](/Timeline/overview).
