## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution, heavily pixelated vertical frame from the aftermath. A dark blue banner hangs across the top of the canopy showing the visible fragment VE ME WRONG, from the tour slogan PROVE ME WRONG. Beneath it, in shadow, two people are engaged in a physical struggle: one bent forward at close to ninety degrees facing left, wearing white shorts patterned with red and blue shapes, with a hand-drawn red circle around them; a second person in a dark shirt leans over them, gripping or holding them. Indistinct figures stand behind in the dark. In the foreground a table is draped in navy blue carrying a large vertical lightning-bolt graphic filled with stars and stripes, with small white text along the bottom edge.

The red circle is the annotator direction. Frames from this angle are collected because they show activity behind the merchandise table in the seconds after the shot, an area that is otherwise poorly covered in the available footage. The annotations in this set consistently mark clothing rather than faces.

At this pixelation, individual identification is not possible and none is attempted. No person is identified and no conduct is attributed to anyone.
