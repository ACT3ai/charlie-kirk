## What This Image Shows

A composite graphic. The yellow headline text reads UTAH CHANGED ITS AUTOPSY LAW IN 2025, CRIMINALIZING THE SHARING OF AUTOPSY PHOTOS. THE DRAFTING LAWYER, GREG GUNN, SHOWS UP IN UNIQUE GOOGLE SEARCHES FROM ISRAELI IP ADDRESSES IN 2024, A FEW MONTHS BEFORE THE BILL WAS INTRODUCED, AND 2025, THE WEEK THE BILL WENT INTO EFFECT. Beneath it is a Google Trends screenshot comparing three search terms, filtered to Israel over the range 2/1/24 to 10/1/25, all categories, web search. Two peaks are marked on the interest-over-time line, labeled June 2024 and May 2025. The handles @BasedSamParker and @SamParkerSenate are overlaid.

The underlying claim is a correlation claim advanced by researcher [Sam Parker](/Influencers/x/based-sam-parker): that search interest in the bill drafting attorney, filtered to one country, spiked shortly before the bill was introduced and again in the week it took effect. The site records the claim as the poster makes it.

The inference does not follow on its own. Google Trends reports relative, normalized interest rather than absolute search counts, small numbers in a small region produce large-looking spikes, and the tool does not identify who searched or why. Nothing here establishes that any person or entity took any action, and no wrongdoing by any named individual is alleged or implied. The written treatment, including the same caution, is on [Utah Autopsy Law Changes](/Medical/utah_autopsy_law_changes) and [Israel Google Searches — Medical People](/Medical/israel_google_searches_medical_people).
