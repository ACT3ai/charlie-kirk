## About This Cluster

These nine media items are the clips behind the wound-location question. Within roughly an hour of the shooting at Utah Valley University on **September 10, 2025**, on-camera witnesses on live television were telling anchors that **Charlie Kirk** had been hit "in the heart." One tells a broadcast anchor it was "one shot, right directly to the heart." Another, speaking to **The National Desk**, says "it was like a direct shot to the heart — right where his heart is, it started going red." A third clip, shared by **@VigilantFox**, carries the contrasting account: "I heard just one pop... his head kind of fell back and blood was just gushing out of his neck." Every item here is pinned to IPFS so the clip survives even if the original post is removed.

Two of the clips add complications that the investigation follows separately. In one, **Glenn Beck** tells **Megyn Kelly** on air that a source in a position to know said Kirk still "has a pulse" — early confusion that later reporting did not bear out. In another, the same on-camera witness who used the heart framing also says the man taken into custody, **George Zinn**, was "the wrong guy" and that he knew him; the public record is that Zinn was not the shooter and faced unrelated charges, and nothing here asserts he committed any crime. The remaining clips are interpretation rather than testimony: a slow-motion frame analysis whose poster argues the round "ricocheted off of his vest and through his neck," a speaker advancing a heart-shot-plus-body-armor fragmentation theory, a reposted frame-analysis clip from an account that separately alleged a witness was "planted" on air, and a widely shared post reporting that **Erika Kirk** had asked Charlie to wear a vest before the event. The "planted" and fragmentation framings are the posters' allegations and are recorded as opinions, not findings.

Read the clips in order — the two first-hour witness accounts, then the neck-wound contrast account, then the interpretive material — and note how the confident heart framing arrives before any footage was public and largely disappears afterward.
