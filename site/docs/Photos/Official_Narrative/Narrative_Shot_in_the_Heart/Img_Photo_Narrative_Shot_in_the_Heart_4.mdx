## What This Image Shows

A television witness clip reshared on X by [@Maxwell1995Vjjo](https://x.com/Maxwell1995Vjjo/status/1966235480732873149) on **September 11, 2025** — the day after the shooting — with the poster's framing question attached: why did "every eyewitness" describe a heart or chest wound. In the clip the witness says, "It was one shot, right, directly to the heart," and also states that the man detained, **George Zinn**, was "the wrong guy."

This is the item where the thread stops being a collection of individual accounts and becomes an argument about the pattern. By September 11 enough early interviews had accumulated that a poster could point at the uniformity itself rather than at any single statement, and that framing is what carried the claim across X.

The framing overstates its own evidence, which is worth saying directly. Not every eyewitness said heart — the neck-wound account in this same cluster is from the same afternoon — so "every" is the poster's rhetoric rather than a count. And uniformity among television interviewees has an ordinary explanation: broadcasts run in sequence, later interviewees have often heard earlier ones, and a phrase that gets repeated on air spreads through the people waiting to speak. Mr. Zinn is a living person, was not the shooter per the public record, and nothing here asserts he committed any crime. Full catalog: [Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart).
