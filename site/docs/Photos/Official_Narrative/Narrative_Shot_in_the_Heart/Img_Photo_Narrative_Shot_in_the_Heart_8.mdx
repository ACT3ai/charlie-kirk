## What This Image Shows

A slow-motion clip shared on X by [@aducarrabh](https://x.com/aducarrabh/status/1965884012565766172) on **September 10, 2025**. The poster's caption reads: "Something whizzes by [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) a split second before impact. The bullet also appears to have, indeed, ricocheted off of his vest and through his neck. Watch in slow motion."

Two separate claims are packed into that caption, and they should be separated. The first is an observation about the footage — that a moving object is visible in frame immediately before impact. The second is an interpretation of what caused the injury: a round deflecting off body armor into the neck. The first can in principle be checked by anyone who watches the clip; the second cannot be established from video at all.

The ricochet reading is the mechanical version of the heart-shot reconciliation and is the poster's interpretation, not a verified finding. Whether body armor was worn has not been established here, and no ballistic report supports the deflection claim. The "something whizzes by" observation connects to separate threads on this site examining what else may have been in the air or on his person at that moment.
