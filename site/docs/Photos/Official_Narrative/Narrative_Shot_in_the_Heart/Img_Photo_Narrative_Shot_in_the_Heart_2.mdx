## What This Image Shows

An on-scene eyewitness interview, shared on X by [@TND (The National Desk)](https://x.com/TND/status/1965890646746513821) on **September 10, 2025** — the day of the shooting. The witness describes what he saw at the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) event: "It was like a direct shot to the heart... right where his heart is, it started going red."

That phrasing is the heart of the research question this cluster documents. The account is specific about location — not the general chest, but the heart — and specific about what made it visible, a spreading red patch. It was given within hours of the event and before any footage had circulated widely.

The contrast comes from the footage that did circulate later, in which the visible bleeding is at the neck and posters noted no bullet hole in the shirt front and no chest blood. Later reporting settled on a neck wound. Researchers ask why so many of the earliest on-camera speakers converged on the same wrong location, and the ordinary answers are strong ones: chaotic scenes produce confident mistaken descriptions, people repeat what they have just heard others say, and a witness at distance sees movement rather than anatomy. Nothing here accuses this witness of anything. Full catalog: [Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart).
