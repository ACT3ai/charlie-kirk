## What This Image Shows

A tightly framed close-up in low light. A pale hand and wrist, heavily smeared with dark red blood, hangs limp with the fingers slightly curled. Above it a tattooed forearm is partly in shadow. A person in a teal-blue shirt is cropped at frame left with a dark grid-patterned object tucked at the waistband, and another dark-clothed figure is cropped at frame right. The background is nearly black with faint metallic glints. A hand-drawn white oval has been added over the hand and the arm above it.

The white oval is an annotation by whoever circulated the frame, directing attention to the blood on the hand rather than to the person it belongs to. Frames like this are used in the investigation to argue about the volume, location, and timing of blood in the minutes after the shot — questions that bear directly on the dispute over where the wound was and what caused it. Nothing in the frame identifies anyone.

The image is presented clinically and without interpretation.
