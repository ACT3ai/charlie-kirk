## What This Image Shows

A clean studio product photograph on a seamless white background. The subject is a Sennheiser BA 2015 rechargeable pack: two orange cylindrical Ni-MH cells held in a molded matte black plastic housing. The housing is embossed SENNHEISER, BA 2015, and MADE IN GERMANY, with a raised plus sign marking polarity, and CE, recycling, and crossed-out bin symbols molded on the opposite side. A white paper label in a recessed channel reads 0159, and the cells themselves are printed Ni-MH Battery and HHR150AA.

Battery packs are central to several of the arguments in this cluster. One line of proponent analysis holds that a modified battery was fitted inside a body-worn transmitter; another reads a circular warning glyph in the charred debris as matching a battery-pack label. Reference photographs of standard, unmodified packs are what those comparisons are measured against, which is why this frame is filed here rather than treated as scene material.

Nothing about this photograph connects it to any specific device used on September 10, 2025 — it is catalog imagery. The comparison claims themselves are set out on [Burnt Object in the SUV — RODE Transmitter Claim](/Mic/burnt-object-rode-transmitter), and the shipping hardware is documented on [RODE Wireless PRO Mic](/Mic/rode-wireless-mic).
