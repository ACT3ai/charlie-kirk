## What This Image Shows

A high-angle photograph shot down into the rear footwell of a large vehicle from between the front seats. A heavily crumpled tan paper bag with a faint white grid print lies on its side, with dark red semi-congealed fluid smeared and splattered across its upper folds. To the right, a cluster of roughly six to eight bright yellow individually wrapped hard candies sits on the dark carpet, with a single matching candy isolated further down near a seat rail. A dark grey vinyl bench seat, a molded plastic side panel with a vertical vent, and a seatbelt anchor are visible behind.

This is one frame from the set of vehicle-interior photographs that researchers attribute to [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens). The scattered candies are a recurring reference point across several of these images because their distribution is used to argue about how much movement occurred inside the cabin. The dark fluid on the bag is described by commentators as blood; it has not been independently identified, and no laboratory result for any of this material has been published.
