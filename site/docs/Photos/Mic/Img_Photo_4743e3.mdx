## What This Image Shows

A vertical phone screenshot of a Yandex search results page, timestamped 6:53 with the battery at 95 percent. The video player at the top shows a pair of hands holding two black RODE Wireless GO transmitters, both with their integrated clips facing the camera and the RODE logo clearly printed on them. The caption below reads rodemic MagClip Go Magnetic Clip for Rode Wire, with a metadata line reading 1K, 5 Aug 2024, Shelly Shops Online.

This is research capture rather than scene evidence — someone looking up how these transmitters mount to clothing. It matters because the mounting question sits underneath several competing claims about September 10, 2025: whether the device was clipped at the collar, worn at the belt, or attached with a magnetic mount changes both what would have been visible on camera and where any fragments would have gone.

The MagClip Go accessory shown here is a magnetic mount sold for exactly this class of transmitter, and its dimensions appear in the forensic-style measurement panel elsewhere in this cluster. For the hardware details and the competing placement claims, see [RODE Wireless PRO Mic](/Mic/rode-wireless-mic), [AV Team Mic Setup](/Mic/AV_Team_Mic_Setup), and [Microphone — The Exploding Mic Theory](/Mic/overview).
