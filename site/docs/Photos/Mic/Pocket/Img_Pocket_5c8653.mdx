## What This Image Shows

A close-up of a small square black device clipped vertically to the breast pocket of a plaid button-up shirt. The front face is glossy black, printed RODE in white with a gold metallic circle beneath it and WIRELESS GO II in smaller white type below that. A lit LCD shows two horizontal green level bars numbered 1 and 2, alongside battery icons and signal-strength indicators. The body is matte black plastic with rounded corners, roughly four and a half centimetres square. The shirt is a soft flannel-textured tartan of dark forest green, olive, charcoal, white and light grey, its pocket flap dark-stitched with a buttonhole visible near the top. A light blue or grey long sleeve is partly visible at the far left, indicating another person standing alongside.

This is the identification frame for the pocket thread. The device is unambiguous here — a RODE Wireless GO II receiver, powered on, with two audio channels showing signal. A receiver, not a transmitter: it is the half of the system that takes in audio from a body-worn unit, which is what a person handling audio for an event would carry.

That distinction is worth holding onto, because the exploding-mic argument concerns the transmitter worn under the shirt, not the receiver. The claim recorded in the investigation notes about a square object present in a pocket before the event and absent afterward relates to this same plaid shirt.
