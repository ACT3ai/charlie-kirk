## About This Cluster

This sub-cluster holds open questions orbiting the audio equipment at UVU on **September 10, 2025** — including a variant of the exploding-device theory that points not at the lapel mic but at the **in-ear monitor (IEM)**. Proponents of that variant argue a tiny low-flash charge in the earpiece would make the ear canal the path of least resistance toward the throat and neck, and point to the IEM wire appearing to recoil violently in the footage. It sits alongside a question about the final audience questioner, **[Hunter Kozak](/People/hunter-kozak)**, whom one circulated post ties to a rival campus tour.

The four images run from speculative graphics to plain hardware: an "IEM DETONATION" illustration of shrapnel paths through a rendered skull, a mock-operation collage labeled "Operation ZION WHISPERS," a macro product photo of a transparent in-ear monitor showing its internal metal components, and a screenshot of an X reply to **[Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens)** about the last questioner. None of these graphics are forensic findings — they are theory illustrations built by researchers to test whether an earpiece-origin blast could explain the wound path, the shirt movement, and the amplified bang heard through the venue speakers. The official account remains a rifle shot, and no earpiece device has been recovered or examined publicly.

Compare this variant against the main lapel-mic case in [Microphone — The Exploding Mic Theory](/Mic/overview) and the competing anchored-charge argument in [The Exploding Pendant](/Photos/Mic/Mic_Exploding_Pendant/overview). The hardware baseline — what the RØDE rig actually looks like intact — is in [Pocket](/Photos/Mic/Pocket/overview) and on the [RØDE Wireless PRO page](/Mic/rode-wireless-mic).
