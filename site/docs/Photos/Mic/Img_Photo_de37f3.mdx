## What This Image Shows

An infographic titled Reaction Timeline with Shaped Charge Events, built around a horizontal timeline that runs from 0.1 microseconds on a blue left half to 50 milliseconds on an orange right half, with a clock icon at the transition. Five illustrated panels sit along it. The labeled stages, read directly from the graphic, are: shaped charge detonates at 0.1 microseconds; pressure wave impacts brain from 0.1 to 100 microseconds, annotated decorticate posture; rear of shirt moves from hemispheric pressure wave at 0.5 to 2 milliseconds; black box deformation at 1 to 6 milliseconds; visible debris at 2 to 10 milliseconds; and sound perceived at 15 meters at approximately 44 milliseconds.

This is one of the core proponent exhibits in the exploding-mic thread, because it is an attempt to make the theory falsifiable. Each stage predicts something that should be visible or audible at a stated time, and the ordering is the argument: that shirt movement precedes visible blood, that debris appears before the sound reaches the crowd, and that the posture seen in the video is a neurological response to a pressure wave rather than to a projectile.

It is a model, not a measurement. Nothing in it is derived from case evidence; the numbers are the author calculated expectations for a hypothetical charge. The official finding remains a rifle shot. The frame-by-frame work this timeline is meant to explain is on [Mic Explosion Video Analysis](/Mic/mic-explosion-video-analysis), the audio side is on [Canon XA55 Audio](/Mic/Canon_XA55_Audio), and the skeptical treatment is on [Demo of Shape Charge](/Mic/Demo_of_shape_charge).
