## What This Image Shows

A top-down close-up of a light grey mottled floor surface with several small dark irregular shards scattered across it, and a dark straight edge running vertically near the lower left corner. A rectangular inset is placed at the center of the frame, showing a darker, smoother surface carrying a much higher concentration of debris: sharp angular glossy black shards from specks up to roughly a quarter of an inch, mixed with tiny reflective silver or white flecks and a few orange-brown particles.

This is a companion frame to the tinted-glass image in this cluster, and it is used the same way — as the evidentiary basis for the claim that small brittle fragments were scattered inside the vehicle. The mixture visible in the inset, brittle black shards together with metallic flecks, is what proponents point to when arguing the debris is device wreckage rather than window glass.

The reasoning is theirs, and it has not been tested. No chain of custody has been published for these frames, no material has been identified by any laboratory, and the inset is an enlargement whose apparent detail is limited by the source resolution. The full set is documented on [SUV Transporting Charlie](/Mic/suv-transporting-charlie), and the identification-claim problems are set out on [Burnt Object in the SUV — RODE Transmitter Claim](/Mic/burnt-object-rode-transmitter).
