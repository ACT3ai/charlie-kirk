## What This Image Shows

A tight vertical crop of the rear tailgate of a dark GMC Yukon Denali. A horizontal chrome trim bar runs across the upper tailgate with the chrome DENALI badge to its right, a recessed license plate alcove at center holding a standard Utah Life Elevated plate with red-orange rock arches, a small backup camera housing above it, a second chrome bar carrying the tailgate release below, chrome YUKON lettering at lower left, and the ribbed black bumper step pad at the bottom. The glossy paint reflects vertical structures and another vehicle behind the camera.

Identification photographs of the vehicle exterior matter because they are how researchers try to tie the interior frames to a specific vehicle and then to a specific history. Elsewhere in this partition there is a matching exterior shot of a black Yukon Denali standing in what appears to be a storage or auction lot, which is the basis for claims about where the vehicle went afterward.

The license plate characters are legible in the source image; they are not reproduced here. Claims that the transport vehicle was later disposed of, destroyed, or sold at auction are recorded on [Cover Up](/CoverUp/overview) and referenced on [SUV Transporting Charlie](/Mic/suv-transporting-charlie), and remain unverified public commentary rather than confirmed record.
