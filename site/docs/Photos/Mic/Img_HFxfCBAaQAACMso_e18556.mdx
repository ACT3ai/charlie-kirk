## What This Image Shows

A vertical close-up of two hands holding a small matte black plastic plate with rounded corners. The inner surface carries two parallel molded ridges and a small circular hole or notch near one end, consistent with a slide-on or clip-on mounting mechanism. One hand pinches the edge between thumb and index finger; the other is further back with a metallic ring on the ring finger. The background is a dark textured surface, well out of focus.

This is a demonstration frame from the same accessory research as the search-results screenshot in this cluster. The plate is the mounting hardware that attaches a body-worn transmitter to a garment, and how the device was mounted is a live question in the case: a clip through the collar, a belt mount under the shirt, and a magnetic mount all leave different traces and imply different positions relative to the neck.

Nothing here connects the specific object shown to the event. It is reference material for the placement argument. The competing placement claims are documented on [RODE Wireless PRO Mic](/Mic/rode-wireless-mic), [AV Team Mic Setup](/Mic/AV_Team_Mic_Setup), and [Burnt Object in the SUV — RODE Transmitter Claim](/Mic/burnt-object-rode-transmitter), which asserts a belt-mounted position on the left side.
