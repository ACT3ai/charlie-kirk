## What This Image Shows

A desktop screen capture taken during a live commentary session. An image viewer fills the screen with a crowd photograph enlarged to 285 percent; the status bar reports a source resolution of 1012 by 695 and a file size of 1.4 MB, and the system clock reads 5:08 PM on 11/8/2025. A small webcam inset in the upper right shows a bald man speaking into a microphone. The photograph itself shows a young man in a plain white crewneck shot from behind, surrounded by animated onlookers, one in a white cap with a gold 47 logo, another raising a phone to take a picture.

Captures like this document the research process rather than the event: a commentator walking an audience through a still frame, zooming past the point where the source resolution carries real detail. The 285 percent zoom on a roughly one-megapixel image is worth noting on its own, because enlargement past a source resolution is where pixel artifacts start to be read as objects.

Preserved here as a record of how a specific claim was presented and at what magnification.
