## What This Image Shows

A wide daytime photograph of the event. Hundreds of attendees are packed onto a grassy slope rising from left to right under a clear blue sky, many in red caps and light t-shirts, some with hands raised or phones held up. Behind them, a concrete structure with a rooftop railing is also crowded, and a multi-storey light-stucco building stands to the upper right. In the middle ground a clearing separates the crowd from the foreground, where a man in a white t-shirt and dark pants walks left carrying a red object, a man in a black polo faces the crowd, and another in a white long-sleeve shirt looks toward them. Metal barricades run along the edge of the slope, and two large black PA speakers stand on tall stands. A green circular inset in the lower right highlights a man wearing a white cap reading 1776. The frame carries a source credit reading @PHIL_LYMAN and an AP mark, with the tour slogan PROVE visible on the setup.

The green circle is the annotator direction, marking one attendee in a crowd of hundreds. The source credit is itself notable: Phil Lyman is a figure who appears elsewhere in this investigation, and photographs credited to him are among the wide-angle records of the crowd available from that day.

Being visible in a crowd photograph is not evidence of anything. This individual is not identified here and no conduct is attributed to him or to anyone else in the frame. The image is preserved as a wide positional record of the crowd, the barricade line, and the speaker placement.
