## What This Image Shows

A six-panel exhibit laid out as a legal forensic document. The header reads RODE Wireless PRO TX — Pre-Event Close-Up CLAHE Analysis, with a case line reading State of Utah v. Tyler Robinson and a defense forensic analysis credit. The left column pairs a color close-up labeled Close-Up 1 angled — a small matte black rectangular transmitter clipped near the collarbone of a bright white synthetic shirt — with a greyscale CLAHE-enhanced version of the same frame in which the fabric folds and the device surface texture are sharply pronounced. The middle column repeats the pairing from a more frontal angle labeled Close-Up 2 front, showing the neck, jawline and collar with a thin gold chain disappearing under the shirt. The right column carries manufacturer dimension diagrams: RX 46.4 mm, TX 45.3 mm, profile 44 mm, 18.5 mm, and MagClip 35.1 by 19.7 mm, with top, bottom and profile views.

CLAHE is contrast limited adaptive histogram equalization, a standard image-enhancement technique that raises local contrast. The exhibit uses it to argue that the object on the shirt can be measured against the published dimensions of a specific product and matched or excluded. Its framing as a defense analysis in the Tyler Robinson prosecution is itself part of the claim; nothing indicates it was filed with any court, and it should be read as an advocacy exhibit, not a court record.

This is the most methodologically explicit image in the cluster, and it is the right one to compare the softer close-ups against.
