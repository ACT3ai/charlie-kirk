## What This Image Shows

An annotated image labelled Symbol from label on modified battery from transmitter. A battery-pack label is shown at left with legible text including MK1SL, 1200mAh, 4Wh, a Sino-brand logo, and a row of standard warning and compliance symbols. A red line connects one circular symbol on that label to a circled small upright fragment within the burnt mass.

The claim is that a fragment sticking up from the charred material carries visible writing and a circular symbol matching the warning glyph from a modified battery pack used inside the transmitter. What would be needed to sustain that is a legible glyph at known scale matching a specific label. What the image offers is a small upright fragment circled in a heat-damaged mass.

This is one of four images published by @ProjectConstitu on X on July 16, 2026, arguing that the burnt object left in the back of the SUV is not only melted shirt fabric and tissue but contains parts of the RODE wireless transmitter. The host page sets out the specific problems with the identification in detail: the circled regions are blurred past the point of component recognition, and the key evidence is a phone screenshot of a video frame of the original photograph, with each generation adding compression artifacts that can produce shapes reading as structure.

The claim is preserved as the poster made it, with attribution, and is not adopted here. No laboratory has examined this object and no official identification of it exists.
