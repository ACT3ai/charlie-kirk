## What This Image Shows

An annotated image labelled Female Audio Jack? Red arrows point to a circled dark region on the left side of the charred object, and a product photo of a RODE Wireless ME TX transmitter sits alongside for comparison. The annotation is posed as a question rather than an assertion, and the question mark in the label is the poster own hedge.

What is being claimed is that a melted female audio jack — the same type found on the top of the transmitter said to have been worn under the shirt — is visible in the burnt mass. A jack is a specific geometry: a barrel, a ring, and a sleeve. What the circled region actually resolves to at this magnification is a dark shape.

This is one of four images published by [@ProjectConstitu](/Influencers/podcasts-project-constitution) on X on July 16, 2026, arguing that the burnt object left in the back of the SUV is not only melted shirt fabric and tissue but contains parts of the RODE wireless transmitter. The host page sets out the specific problems with the identification in detail: the circled regions are blurred past the point of component recognition, and the key evidence is a phone screenshot of a video frame of the original photograph, with each generation adding compression artifacts that can produce shapes reading as structure.

The claim is preserved as the poster made it, with attribution, and is not adopted here. No laboratory has examined this object and no official identification of it exists.
