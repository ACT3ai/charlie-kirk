## What This Image Shows

A split-screen graphic divided by a hard black line. On the left, a fair-haired man in a plain white t-shirt is photographed at night with tears on both cheeks, eyes red-rimmed, mouth open mid-speech, a small mole visible on his left cheek beside the nose. A blurred blue screen and a hint of red and white stripes glow behind him. On the right, a brown-haired man in a suit stands in front of a brick wall and wooden panelling, composed and solemn.

The captions are the whole argument. The left panel is labelled with a garbled version of [Charlie Kirk's](/People/charlie-kirk) name; the right reads **"Robbie Parker — FATHER OF SHOOTING VICTIM."** Setting the two frames side by side, with those labels, is how the "same person" claim was propagated.

That claim is unsubstantiated. It rests entirely on the two men looking alike in low-resolution frames captured thirteen years apart, and no record, document or authority supports it. Robbie Parker's identity and the death of his daughter are matters of public record; the man on the left has never been shown to be anyone other than who he says he is. Neither has been charged with or accused of a crime in any court. This site reproduces the graphic to show what was circulated, not to endorse it.
