## What This Image Shows

A dark-mode news-aggregator feed with four items, all posted within hours of each other on the day of the shooting. A **Threads** item reads: "BREAKING NEWS Update: FOX RENO 11 Misidentified Charlie Kirk suspected shooter. Another suspected shooter has been confirmed as Zachariah Ahmed Qureshi but reportedly has been released and the shooter remains at large." A **Pravda** item reads: "The FBI detainee for Kirk's murder is Zakaria Ahmed Qureshi, 25 years old." A **JFeed** item reads: "Reports: Suspect in Charlie Kirk Murder is Zachariah Ahmed Qureshi — Unverified reports claim identity of killer." A second Pravda item names "Zachary Ahmed Qureshi from Salt Lake City, Utah" and adds employment details.

Read the four together and the picture is of a name spreading across outlets in different countries, in four different spellings, inside a few hours — with one of those same items already reporting a prior misidentification by a television station.

**The man named here was released without charge and was not the shooter.** That is the central fact of this image, and it is why the screenshot is preserved: it documents how fast an incorrect identification travelled while a search was still under way. The investigation's open question is whether the public naming and the ninety-minute detention affected that search.
