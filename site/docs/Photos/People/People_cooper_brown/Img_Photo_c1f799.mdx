## What This Image Shows

A photograph published in a **[Fort Huachuca](/US_Intelligence/Fort_Huachuca/overview) Morale, Welfare and Recreation** social-media post, showing three young men in matching **Lawley Automotive Group** shirts at a base community event.

Morale, Welfare and Recreation posts are routine public-affairs content — community events, sponsors, local businesses — and a dealership-sponsored team appearing at one is entirely ordinary. Fort Huachuca is the U.S. Army Intelligence Center in Sierra Vista, Arizona, which is why the base name draws attention in this investigation; it recurs across several unrelated strands of the file.

The image is filed here because researchers have compared a face in it to a young man photographed at the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) event on September 10, 2025, and asked whether they are the same person. That comparison rests on resemblance and is unresolved. Appearing in a public event photograph at an Army post proves nothing, and **no wrongdoing by anyone pictured is alleged**. See [Cooper Brown](/People/cooper-brown), [Jeb Jacobi](/People/jeb-jacobi) and [U.S. Intelligence](/US_Intelligence/overview).
