## What This Image Shows

A plain screenshot of newspaper body text on a white background, with a translucent pink highlight laid over part of it. The passage reads: **"Dylan Hope, 26, an electrician working at a property next to the university, told The Telegraph that a SWAT team of 30 officers descended on the site at around 2pm on Wednesday and searched the wooded area with dogs before scouring the home."**

Three specifics make this useful: a named source with an occupation, a stated force size, and a time — around 2 p.m. on the Wednesday, roughly ninety minutes after the shooting. Attributed press reporting of that kind is a firmer class of evidence than most of what surrounds it in this cluster, because it can be checked against the outlet's own archive.

The highlight is a reader's emphasis, not the newspaper's. What the passage establishes is that a substantial search happened at a property adjacent to the campus that afternoon; it does not establish what was being looked for or what was found.
