## What This Image Shows

A composite on a black background holding five white-bordered video screenshots from the outdoor event, with the words **"Black shirts," "Crowd"** and **"Control"** set in large white type between them. The frames show a dense crowd on tiered seating under a clear sky: a man face-down on a concrete step with his head shielded by a hand in a red sleeve while a woman in beige leans protectively over him; a man in a black t-shirt and shorts standing on an elevated structure, pointing upward and shouting; barriers, cheering, and people gesturing.

The three words are the argument. The maker is asserting that people in black shirts were directing or containing crowd movement in the moments around the shooting, which connects to the wider question in this investigation of who was managing the venue and under whose direction.

Wearing a black shirt is not a uniform and not an accusation, and none of the people visible in these frames is identified. Read the composite as a hypothesis about crowd behaviour that needs the underlying full-length video to test.
