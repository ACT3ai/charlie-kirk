## What This Image Shows

A two-panel graphic. The large left panel is a documentary photograph of the moments after the shooting at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) on [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025): attendees moving fast across a concrete plaza in bright daylight, a young woman carrying a coral umbrella, another with her hand over her mouth holding a phone, and a man walking away from camera in a royal blue shirt printed **AMERICANCOMEBACKTOUR.COM** — the tour branding for the event itself. The narrow right panel is a vertical indoor portrait of a female U.S. Army soldier standing in a hallway with a sombre expression.

The pairing is the claim. Setting a crowd photograph beside a service portrait is the standard format of the identification graphics that run through this whole cluster: the maker is asserting that a person in the left frame is the person in the right frame.

Nothing in the image supports that assertion beyond the juxtaposition itself, and the soldier is not named on the graphic. Being photographed in a fleeing crowd, or serving in the Army, proves nothing. Read this as an unverified comparison. The left panel is genuinely useful as a document of the plaza in the first minutes — for that thread see [UVU](/UVU/overview), [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) and [Timeline](/Timeline/overview).
