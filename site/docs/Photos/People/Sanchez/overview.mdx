## About This Cluster

This cluster belongs to the military-photograph identification thread that also runs through the Conor Dority and Scott Wilson folders. Researchers have been pulling publicly posted U.S. Army competition photographs and comparing the faces in them against faces visible in crowd footage from [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). The premise is unproven and the method is weak, but the source images themselves are public and worth preserving. No soldier pictured has been accused of anything, and appearing in an Army publicity photograph is not evidence of any connection to September 10, 2025.

Two of the three images come from the same event. One is a portrait of a young soldier in Operational Camouflage Pattern and black-framed glasses, flanked by other soldiers against a red backdrop, with a name tape reading "SANCHEZ" and a rank patch consistent with private second class. The other is the event's own graphic banner: a metallic shield reading "GEN RAYMOND T. ODIERNO BEST REDLEG," dated 2026, with crossed cannon barrels and "FORT SILL" at the tip. The third image is a low-resolution crowd frame from an outdoor event — a bearded man in a grey graphic t-shirt and two-tone cap in the foreground, other spectators looking toward something out of frame.

The comparison being attempted is between that crowd frame and the portrait, and nothing in this cluster completes it. Fort Sill is an artillery post and has no established connection to this case; the Army thread that does recur in the file involves [Fort Huachuca](/Planes/Airport-Fort-Huachuca), which is a different installation entirely. Read the three images as source material for an open question.
