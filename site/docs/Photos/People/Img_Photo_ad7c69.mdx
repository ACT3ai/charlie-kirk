## What This Image Shows

A wide, slightly soft daylight frame of the event site at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). In the foreground a man in a grey flannel shirt and a white cap walks past the camera with a phone pressed to his ear; a woman in a dark navy shirt sits on a concrete step facing away, a light blue backpack beside her and a black backpack on the ground. The midground is the tiered grass amphitheatre containing the white event tent, security fencing and scattered debris, and beyond it a crowd stands near the low-rise campus buildings under a partly cloudy sky.

This is one of the more valuable documentary frames in the People cluster because it shows the physical arrangement of the venue — the tent, the terraced seating, the fence lines and the buildings — rather than a person. Those elements are exactly what the geometry arguments in this investigation turn on: sightlines, distances and where barriers stood.

The abandoned backpacks and the man on the phone place it after the event rather than during it. Nothing in the frame identifies anyone or establishes wrongdoing by anybody visible. For the site geometry see [UVU](/UVU/overview) and [Tent](/Tent/overview); for the surrounding accounts see [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview).
