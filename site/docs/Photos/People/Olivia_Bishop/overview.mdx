## About This Cluster

Olivia Bishop is described in the investigation file as an Orem Police Department records specialist, and she entered the public record because researchers asked whether a woman had any contact with [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). That question came from a claim about Ring-camera footage, and it has never been substantiated. She has not been charged with anything, no court has found that she did anything improper, and this cluster exists only because her name and employer became part of the open-source discussion.

The three images document the paper trail rather than any alleged event. One is a screenshot of an Orem Police Department Facebook post about the retirement of three records service specialists, with a comment from an account under her name boxed in yellow. Another is a LinkedIn profile screenshot with the employer field, Orem Police Department, circled by hand. The third is a video still of a young woman with a lavalier microphone clipped to her shirt, speaking outdoors on a residential street — the kind of frame taken from an on-camera interview. Taken together they establish an employment link and a public statement; they establish nothing about the case itself.

Treat the employment record as verified and everything attached to it as an open question. The claim tying a records specialist to the accused is a single online assertion with no supporting document in this file, and it should be read that way. For her written profile see [Olivia Bishop](/People/olivia-bishop), for the wider policing picture see [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview), and for the accused's own timeline see [Tyler Robinson](/Tyler_Robinson/overview).
