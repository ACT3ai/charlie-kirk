## What This Image Shows

A two-panel composite. The **left panel** is an outdoor daylight frame: a man and a woman run hand in hand from right to left past a large wall of light reddish-brown and tan brick, both with tense, anxious expressions and mouths slightly open. He wears a light grey t-shirt with a rectangular sunset-silhouette print and dark grey athletic shorts; she wears a black t-shirt printed with a mountain lake scene and the word **"LAKE"**, medium-wash jeans ripped at the left knee, and white low-top sneakers. Hard shadows fall across the brick, indicating strong direct sun.

The **right panel** is a completely different scene: two military officers in different uniforms shaking hands indoors in front of a white projection screen.

Setting the two frames side by side is the entire claim, and the composite offers nothing to support it — no names, no circles, no captions tying a face in one to a face in the other. **This site does not assert that the people in the left panel are the people in the right panel, or that anyone pictured did anything wrong.** The left frame is genuinely useful as a document of civilians fleeing the scene; the pairing is not.
