## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post on X by the verified account **Eric Daugherty (@EricLDaugh)**, timestamped seven minutes old. The text reads, in full: **"BREAKING: Subject in custody in the killing of Charlie Kirk reported as Zachariah Ahmed Qureshi."** Below it is the embedded identification portrait filed separately in this cluster — a young man against a plain blue background, staring directly into the lens.

Note the construction of the sentence. The poster writes "reported as," which is a hedge, but the word "BREAKING" and the attached face do the opposite work. A named individual and a photograph reached a large verified audience within minutes, sourced to other reports rather than to any official confirmation.

**The man named and pictured was released without charge and was not the shooter.** He is a living person who was publicly misidentified during a fast-moving story, and nothing here alleges any wrongdoing by him. The reason the screenshot is kept is the mechanism it captures: how a name and a face spread before anything was verified.
