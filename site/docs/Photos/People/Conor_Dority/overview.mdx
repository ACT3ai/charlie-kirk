## About This Cluster

Conor Dority — spelled Connor Dority in the site profile — is named in compiled online research as an EMT who was on the plaza at Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025. According to that research, he and a partner were in full gear with a trauma kit and asked to be let through to render aid. The medical-response timeline is one of the most heavily contested parts of this investigation, and every account of who was allowed near Charlie Kirk and when is treated here as a reported claim. No wrongdoing is alleged against him; the accounts describe him trying to help.

The two images do different jobs. One is a citizen-research collage built on a black background, headed "Conor Dority" with the subtitles "EMT" and "Vascular access technician," pairing a green-circled face in a campus crowd frame with a side profile of a first responder in uniform and a shot of a public simulation exercise. The other is a stack of three U.S. Army artillery team portraits taken in front of a red "Best Redleg" competition backdrop, labelled for the 3rd Infantry Division, the 82nd Airborne Division and the 11th Airborne Division. The second image belongs to a separate military-identification thread that also runs through the [Sanchez](/Photos/People/Sanchez/overview) and [Scott Wilson](/Photos/People/Scott_Wilson/overview) clusters.

The collage is an argument, not a finding — a researcher's attempt to prove a face in a crowd is a specific named EMT, using green circles and arrows. Read it as such. For the written profile see [Connor Dority](/People/connor-dority), and for the wider question of how the medical response was managed that afternoon see [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) and [People](/People/overview).
