## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of somebody's macOS desktop with several windows stacked. The front window is Preview in markup mode, showing a cropped photograph of a woman in sunglasses and a striped shirt standing near a man in a plaid shirt outdoors. Behind and below it sits a larger photograph of eight U.S. Army soldiers posed in front of a **"Best Redleg"** competition banner, and behind that another partly hidden window showing a crowd.

This does not bear on the case. It documents the method — a picture of the comparison being made, caught mid-process, with the markup toolbar active and the crop still open. Somebody was cutting a face out of a crowd photograph to hold against an Army team portrait.

That is worth preserving precisely because it makes the technique legible. The "Best Redleg" photographs recur across this partition, in [Conor Dority](/Photos/People/Conor_Dority/overview) and [Sanchez](/Photos/People/Sanchez/overview), and the comparisons built from them remain unverified. No soldier in the team photograph is named or accused of anything. See [Scott Wilson](/Photos/People/Scott_Wilson/overview) for the rest of this thread.
