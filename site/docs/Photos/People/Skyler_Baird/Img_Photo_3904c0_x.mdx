## What This Image Shows

A two-by-two collage of mobile Google Trends captures divided by thick orange borders and watermarked **@BasedSamParker @SamParkerSenate**. All four panels query the same term, "skyler Baird," across the same window — 3/1/25 to 9/9/25, ending the day before the shooting.

The results differ by region. **Tel Aviv District** peaks at 100 on May 24, 2025. **District of Columbia** shows 72 on March 3, 2025 and a peak of 100 on July 31, 2025. **Haifa District** peaks at 100 on July 3, 2025. **Utah** returns the message "Hmm, your search doesn't have enough data to show here."

The poster's implication is that the name was being searched abroad and in Washington before it meant anything locally. The methodological objection has to be stated alongside it: Google Trends normalises within each region, so a peak of 100 can rest on a very small number of searches, and sparse data for an uncommon name is expected rather than anomalous. Presented as the poster's claim, not as a finding. See the [cluster overview](/Photos/People/Skyler_Baird/overview), [Google Searches](/GoogleSearches/overview) and [Israel](/Israel/overview).
