## About This Cluster

Skyler Baird gave televised eyewitness interviews after the shooting at Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025, and a section of online commentary subsequently questioned how he came to be positioned as he was and how he was later admitted to Charlie Kirk's memorial. He has not been charged with anything, no court has found he did anything wrong, and he has never been shown to be anyone other than who he says he is. This cluster preserves the images that circulated during that discussion so they can be examined rather than repeated.

The set breaks into three parts. First, the broadcast record: a split-screen news frame in which the lower chyron reads "SKYLER BAIRD / WITNESS" opposite an anchor captioned "JOSH BRESLOW," plus several outdoor selfie-style frames of the same man in a white shirt and striped tie with a wireless earbud, and one stadium selfie taken among a seated crowd with Arizona Cardinals signage behind him. Second, a Facebook screenshot in which an account under his name comments, in the poster's words, "Sadly I was there when Charlie was shot. I have to wonder how high up the idea came to install the pavers." Third — and this is the part that requires the most care — a group of PBS and TikTok screenshots about **Robbie Parker**, the father of a Sandy Hook victim, including his 2025 "Think Tank with Steve Adubato" interview about his book on the abuse he suffered from conspiracy claims, and one side-by-side graphic captioned with both names.

That side-by-side is the entire basis of the "same person" claim circulating online, and it rests on visual resemblance and nothing else. Robbie Parker's identity, his daughter's death and the harassment campaign against him are documented in public record and in litigation, and the assertion that he is someone else has never been substantiated. This site does not adopt it. Read the broadcast frames as what they are — a witness who spoke to cameras — and see [Skylar Baird](/People/skylar-baird) for the written profile, [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) for the other firsthand accounts, and [Media](/Media/overview) for how those accounts were broadcast.
