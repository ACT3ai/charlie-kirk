## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Sam Parker**, handle @BasedSamParker, posted 49 minutes before capture, with an embedded video showing a Google Trends interface at 5:30 and bold headline text.

The post reads: "CHARLIE KIRK BOMBSHELL: israeli and Washington DC IP addresses caught conducting UNIQUE Google searches for Timpanogas Regional Hospital, Utah Medical Examiner Deirdre Amaro and various TRH surgeons during June/July 2025! H/t: Baron Coleman, YouTube." The video headline repeats the claim, with the Trends panel showing a query for "timpanogos regional hospital" and an "Interest over time" chart.

Every element named here is tracked separately in this archive on documentary grounds: the hospital chosen was not the closest to campus, the medical examiner paperwork has been questioned, and the surgical team is part of the medical record dispute. The search-trend evidence is the weakest link in the chain, since Trends reports normalized regional interest and identifies no searcher, and interest can follow news rather than precede it. The named professionals are public officials and clinicians and nothing here indicates wrongdoing by any of them.
