## What This Image Shows

An annotated frame of the outdoor event. **Charlie Kirk** is seated at frame right speaking into a handheld microphone, with the densely packed crowd filling the left and centre. Digital overlays have been added: a dark picture-in-picture panel at far left showing firearms, and a red line drawn toward the speaker's position.

The only extracted text is a repeated numeral 47, which appears in the overlays.

Overlay work of this kind is the standard visual grammar of the trajectory argument: a line from a claimed origin to the target, plus a reference image of the weapon type under discussion. The line is drawn by hand on a two-dimensional frame, which cannot establish an angle in three dimensions; the independent ballistics and acoustic analyses in this archive attack the same question with better tools.
