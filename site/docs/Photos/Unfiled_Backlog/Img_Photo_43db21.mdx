## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as 302Girl, handle @302Girl19977, posted seventeen hours before capture. The attached collage has three panels, a tall one at left and two stacked at right, all showing people at an outdoor political event with attention drawn to a man holding a long-lens camera.

The post reads: "Please please please look at this. I have the video but can't send. Guy with long lens camera. On the video they do not flinch when the shot is fired and back away."

Flinch analysis is a recurring method in this investigation, because involuntary startle responses are difficult to suppress and their timing across a crowd can indicate the direction a sound came from. It is also easy to over-read from still frames, since a photographer with an eye to a viewfinder and ear protection behaves differently from a bystander, and no identification is offered here. The crowd-reaction material is collected in [The Shot and the Shooter Position](/Photos/Shot_Position/overview) and [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview).
