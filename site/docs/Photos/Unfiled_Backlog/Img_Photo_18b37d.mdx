## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as Tommy, handle @tjphager, dated **Nov 1**, with a composite of eight small panels showing maps, flight tracking data and a quoted comment.

The post reads: "Here's documentation of N1098L's other 'missed approach' practice descent over Provo Airport on morning of 9/10/2025. Reminding you N1098L ISN'T ISR/HADES NOR equipped to deploy drones. Wallow in your ERRORS." The panels are labelled "Provo Airport 09/10/2025 9:17AM MDT," "N1098L 1st," "N1098L 2nd," "N1098L 3rd," "N1098L 4th" and "lowest recorded altitude," with a quoted comment reading in part: "The most important thing was this plane's location near the campus and connection to the HADES program... I admitted it and did a video about it."

This is a correction from inside the research community, and a valuable one: it distinguishes what the flight data supports, repeated descents over Provo Airport that morning, from what it does not, an intelligence-surveillance role or drone capability for that airframe. This archive tracks **N1098L** and the **HADES** material as separate threads for exactly this reason. A practice approach is a routine flight operation.
