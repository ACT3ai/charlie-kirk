## What This Image Shows

A vertical screenshot of an X thread. The opening post is from an account shown as **Stellar Man**, handle @stellarman22, timestamped 5:47 PM, **Sep 10, 2025**, with 974.9K views. Two replies follow.

The post reads: "A private jet under the tail number N888KG left Provo airport an hour after the shooting. Suspiciously 30 mins into the flight it drops off of ADS-B Illegally and reappears headed back to Provo approx. an hour later. Why did this plane turn off its radar? Where is the shooter?" A reply from an account shown as I Aver reads: "Flight path leads past Page, AZ to super-remote 'Tuba City Airport'. Perfect for your quick landing and hit team transfer needs." A second reply pushes back: "It is apparently somewhat common for ADS-B transponders to loose reception in that area due to terrain, per @Grok. And the public records on the plane don't seem to turn up anything relevant."

The thread is valuable because it contains its own rebuttal. **N888KG** is one of the aircraft this archive tracks, and terrain-induced ADS-B gaps are a real and common phenomenon in that region, which is exactly the kind of ordinary explanation any flight-gap claim has to clear first.
