## What This Image Shows

A flat digital infographic titled **CHAIN OF EVIL**, built from solid color blocks arranged in columns and rows on a white background. The credit line reads "AUTHOR: Twitter: @HolonCitizen."

The boxes and connectors map a proposed influence chain. Reading across, they include "Country X's Foreign Intel Service," "Country X's Money-into-Lobbying," "Foreign / Domestic Money," "Blackmail," "Lobbying," "Sellout Congress Members," "Evil Laws," "President / Administration," "FBI Cover-up (Controlled)," "CIA Cover-up (Controlled)," "Captured MSM News Leadership Groups," "Propaganda MSM News," "Sellout Influencers," "Controlled Church Leaders," and on the other side "Good Church Leaders," "NOT Sellout Influencers," "Christians" and "Citizens." Named boxes include "Trump / Butler PA," "MTG," "Tucker Carlson," "Candace Owens," "Charlie Kirk" and "Jeffrey Epstein," with arrows labelled "Take over TPUSA," "Control Church Leaders," "Assassination or risk of Assassination," "Intel methods" and "Loyalty to Foreign Country."

This is an argument diagram, not evidence. It sets out one researcher's hypothesis about how pressure would travel if the investigation's suspicions were correct, and every box in it is a proposition to be tested rather than a finding about any named person.
