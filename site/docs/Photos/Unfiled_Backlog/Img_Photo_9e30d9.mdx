## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Jon Bray**, handle @jonaaronbray, posted sixteen hours before capture. Two video panels sit side by side beneath the text, both paused on split-screen technical comparisons of crowd footage; the left carries a "Watch again" overlay and the right is paused at 0:06.

The post reads: "BREAKING FORENSIC BOMBSHELL: I hacked phone camera glitches to uncover hidden truths in the UVU incident videos using high quality mov files recorded that day. Rolling shutter 'jello' effect via CMOS sensor scanning? Turned it into a super-precise impulse detector. Analyzed"

The underlying technique is real: most phone sensors expose an image line by line rather than all at once, so a sharp physical shock during a frame leaves a characteristic skew that can be timed. Applied carefully it can put a pressure wave or a physical impulse on a timeline more precisely than audio alone. Whether this particular analysis was done carefully is not something the screenshot can show, and the conclusions remain the analyst's.
