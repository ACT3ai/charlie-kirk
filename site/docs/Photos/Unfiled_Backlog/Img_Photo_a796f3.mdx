## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a repost. The outer post is from an account shown as Trisha Hope, handle @JustTheTweets17, posted thirteen hours before capture; the nested post is from an account shown as **Jon Bray**, handle @jonaaronbray, dated **Sep 22**. Below sits a paused low-resolution video frame of a man in a white t-shirt printed FREEDOM, seated at the branded table with Yrefy lettering behind him.

The outer comment reads: "This sure looks like it came from the the mic clip." The nested post reads: "Step 4: The Hypothesis-Acoustic Trigger and PETN. Here's my theory: a 2-3g PETN charge (effectiveness 1.66) in the 350mAh battery detonated, mimicking a .30-06's 3,000 ft-lbs. But the rifle shot? It was real, an acoustic trigger timed perfectly to mask the explosion."

This is the most specific published version of the exploding-microphone hypothesis: a named explosive, a stated charge mass, a specific battery capacity, and a mechanism for synchronizing with a real shot. This archive tracks **PETN**, the **RODE Wireless PRO** transmitter and the burnt object reported in the vehicle as separate threads. The hypothesis is explicitly labelled as such by its author and is not established; the ballistics presentation held elsewhere in this cluster argues against it. See [The Mic](/Mic/overview), [Cause of Death](/Cause_of_Death/overview) and [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview).
