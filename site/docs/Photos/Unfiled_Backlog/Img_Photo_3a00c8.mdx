## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an interactive relationship-mapping web application. The left canvas shows a circular arrangement of colored node markers joined by thin connector lines; the right panel holds a dossier card and a scrollable people database.

The open dossier card reads: "INVESTIGATION DOSSIER. Dan Flood. 9/10 - SECURITY. TPUSA Security Director. ACTIVE," with a note fragment reading "countdown coordination observed. Stage manager during event. Multiple watch/phone checks," and connections listed to **Charlie Kirk** and **Brian Harpole**. The database below lists names with role labels and status flags, among them "Security Lead - Missing," "Security Guard - Missing," "Chief of Staff - Active," "Tyler Robinson - Official Shooter - Incarcerated," "Organizer - Active," "Photographer - Active," "Attendee - Active," "Wife, CEO and Chair of the Board - Active," "Guard/Tech - Active," "Q and A Questioner - Active," "Person of Interest - Active" and "Mentors - Active," several carrying a numeric score out of ten.

Every label, score and status on this screen is the tool builder's working classification, not an established fact, and the observational notes are one researcher's reading of video. Being in a database of this kind is not an accusation. Except for the person charged, none of the living people listed has been charged in connection with the shooting, and checking a watch or managing a stage is ordinary event work.
