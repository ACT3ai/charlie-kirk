## What This Image Shows

A vertical mobile capture of an X thread from an account shown as **Adam**, handle @bayonerd09, dated **Dec 5, 2025** and later. The thread carries multiple spreadsheet screenshots with rows color-coded in red, green, blue and yellow.

The opening post reads: "I went in and included the last 6 flights I hadn't had time for. Here is the data regarding the Egyptian planes @RealCandaceO claimes were following around first Charlie Kirk, then @MrsErikaKirk, then @tpusafaith ... 40 of the 67 flights didn't exist." A follow-up reads: "Not 'didn't have data', not 'oh we messed up the date', not 'oh we really meant this flight'. Confirmed with 3 services the flights didn't happen. 33 of the 67 flights the plane was on another continent from Mrs. Kirk." Another adds: "On the entire spreadsheet, the one she said she 'totally confirmed' 14 total flights even actually happened. 14 of 67... Even when the flights 'happened' the flights are sometimes over a full week period of time....."

This is the strongest published rebuttal to the Egyptian aircraft tailing claim, and it belongs in the archive precisely because it cuts against the thread's own leading hypothesis. The analysis is one researcher's, checked against three commercial data services by his account and not independently verified here.
