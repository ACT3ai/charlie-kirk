## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a spreadsheet with four visible columns against light grey gridlines, some cells filled in peach-orange and bright yellow.

The column headers read "Hour #," "Minutes" and "Video #." The rows step through 0, 15, 30 and 45 minutes across successive hours, mapping each interval to a consecutive video number from 1 upward, with the first two rows labelled "Spin Up."

This is infrastructure rather than evidence: an index that lets an analyst jump straight to the segment covering a given minute of a long continuous recording. Building one is the first practical step in any serious review of hours of footage, and this archive's frame-level arguments, on the roofline object, the crowd reaction and the shirt movement, all depend on being able to cite an exact timecode.
