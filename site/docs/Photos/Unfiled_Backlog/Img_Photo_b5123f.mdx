## What This Image Shows

This is a mobile screenshot of a thread on X, captured in the platform's dark mode. Two posts are stacked: an original from an account shown as Benjamin Domenech, dated **15 Oct 23**, and a reply from an account shown as Clint Russell on the same date. Engagement counts, verification badges and timestamps are all visible, which is how the screenshot was circulated as a dated record rather than a paraphrase.

The on-screen text is legible. The first post reads: "If Charlie Kirk remains the head of TPUSA, the right has an anti-Semite problem that will follow them into the coming elections." The reply, marked "Replying to @bdomenech," reads: "Do you have a better answer as to why there was no real military presence for 7 hours into the attack? Can you understand why reasonable people would find that bizarre without it being a somehow hateful worldview?"

The exchange is filed here because it dates the public pressure campaign over **Charlie Kirk's** posture on Israel to nearly two years before the **September 10, 2025** shooting at **Utah Valley University**. Researchers have pointed to that long-running friction, and to the donor pressure reported in Kirk's final weeks, when arguing about motive. Nothing in the screenshot connects either poster to the shooting. Read it alongside [Israel](/Israel/overview), [Motive](/Motive/overview) and [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview).
