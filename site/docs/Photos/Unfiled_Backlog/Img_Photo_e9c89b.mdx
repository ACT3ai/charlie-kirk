## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Stew Peters**, handle @realstewpeters, posted 21 hours before capture, quoting a post from an account shown as **AF Post**. The composite below shows a cropped view of the suspended account's profile alongside the suspension notice.

The outer post reads: "What is 'inauthentic behavior'?" The quoted post reads: "X user Antunes was suspended for 'inauthentic behaviors' on the platform." The suspension notice reads: "Your account, Antunes, was reported and has been suspended for violating the X Rules. Specifically, for: Violating our rules against inauthentic behaviors. You may not use our services to engage in inauthentic activity that undermines the integrity of X." The profile fragment shows a follower count in the tens of thousands.

The question in the outer post is the useful one. "Inauthentic behavior" is a category broad enough to cover coordinated bot networks and, on the same wording, an account that a reporting campaign targets successfully. Suspensions of accounts covering this investigation are documented separately here. See [Censorship](/Censorship/overview) and [Media](/Media/overview).
