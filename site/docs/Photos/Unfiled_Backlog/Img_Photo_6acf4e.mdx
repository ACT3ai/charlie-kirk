## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Beau Hightower**, handle @beauhightowerdn, posted seven hours before capture, quoting an aviation trade announcement.

The post reads: "Egypt and Duncan have been working together since 1999. Based out of Lincoln with Major operations in Provo and Scottsdale." The quoted announcement reads: "Duncan Aviation announces the company has entered into a five-year direct contract agreement with the Egyptian Air Force (EAF) for aircraft maintenance services. Duncan Aviation has been managing the maintenance and repair of the EAF fleet of Falcon 20 aircraft since 1999," and quotes a company manager saying the choice to "fly over closer maintenance companies" reveals "a lot about our relationship with them."

This is a documentary find rather than a speculation. It establishes a long-standing, publicly announced commercial relationship between an American maintenance provider with **Provo** operations and the Egyptian Air Force, which is directly relevant to the [Egyptian aircraft](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada) thread this archive tracks. A maintenance contract is an ordinary commercial arrangement and implies nothing about the events of [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025).
