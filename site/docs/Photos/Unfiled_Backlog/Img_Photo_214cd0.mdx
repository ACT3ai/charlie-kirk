## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as National Conservative, handle @NatCon2022, posted eight hours before capture. The attached press photograph, credited to a wire agency, shows an older man in a blue shirt being held and escorted by several men including uniformed police officers, in a crowd with tour branding visible behind.

The post reads: "The original person of interest, George Zinn, 71, has allegedly confessed that he pretended to have shot Charlie Kirk to help the real killer escape. He is being held without bond and charged with felony obstruction."

The **George Zinn** episode is documented in this archive as one of the central distraction questions: a man detained at the venue after drawing attention to himself, later facing an obstruction charge. A charge is an allegation, not a conviction, the reported admission is described as alleged in the post itself, and the inference that he was helping anyone escape is the framing rather than a finding. He has not been charged in connection with the shooting. See [Distraction People](/distraction_people/overview), [Suspects](/Suspects/overview) and [Court](/court/overview).
