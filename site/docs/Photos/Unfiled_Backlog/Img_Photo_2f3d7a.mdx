## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Zeb Boykin**, handle @ZebBoykin, posted 47 minutes before capture. Below it is a heavily zoomed, low-resolution video still of the crowd at the outdoor venue, with a red circle drawn around the back of the head of a man in a white t-shirt.

The post reads: "Ok. Here. Proof the bullet didn't stop wherever they claim it did. This is direct and localized expansion from the round. No, it changes nothing. It aids in explaining Charlie's movements."

This goes directly to the disputed wound description. If a round did not stop where the official account places it, the trajectory, the angle and the recovered evidence all have to be reconsidered, which is the same conclusion the independent ballistics presentation held elsewhere in this cluster reaches by a different route. The claim rests on reading a heavily compressed still, which is the weakest kind of visual evidence, and the poster's own caveat that it "changes nothing" is preserved.
