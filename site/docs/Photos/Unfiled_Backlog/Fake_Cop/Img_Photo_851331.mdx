## What This Image Shows

A three-panel composite of video stills showing a man in a black button-down shirt and a white **MAKE AMERICA GREAT AGAIN** cap standing on a grass lawn. Red arrows are overlaid on each panel in sequence: first at a gold star-shaped object pinned to his shirt pocket, then at his hand moving toward it, and finally at his hand holding the removed object near his waist.

The only readable on-screen text is the cap lettering.

The sequence is what makes this frame worth more than a single still: it shows an object present, then handled, then off, in three steps rather than asserting the change. Whether the object is a law enforcement badge is the posters' reading, made at low resolution. The man has not been publicly identified, no agency has claimed him, and wearing or removing an item of clothing or insignia is not wrongdoing. Compare with the two-frame version in [Fake Policeman](/Photos/Unfiled_Backlog/Fake_Policeman/overview), and see [Suspects](/Suspects/overview) and [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview).
