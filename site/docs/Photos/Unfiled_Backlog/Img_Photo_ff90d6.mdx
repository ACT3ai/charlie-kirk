## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Sulaiman Ahmed**, handle @ShaykhSulaiman, posted three hours before capture. The attached composite pairs a photograph of a New South Wales driver licence displayed on a phone screen with two Google Trends charts headed "Interest over time" and "Interest by subregion."

The post reads: "BREAKING: One of the Bondi shooters name searches spiked on Israeli and Australian google searches HOURS before the shooting on Bondi Beach..."

This screenshot is not about the **September 10, 2025** shooting. It is filed in the backlog because the method it uses, reading Google Trends spikes as a signal of advance knowledge, is the same method applied to this case elsewhere in the archive. That method has real limits: Trends data is normalized and coarse, it does not identify who searched, and spikes can follow early reporting rather than precede an event. See [Google Searches](/GoogleSearches/overview) for how the same technique is applied and argued here.
