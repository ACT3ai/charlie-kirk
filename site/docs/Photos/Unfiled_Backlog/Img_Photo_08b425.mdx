## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post analytics panel overlaying a blurred platform interface. It reports metrics for a post published by an account shown as **GoingStrategic** on **Oct 11**.

The post text reads: "1/ Cover Up in Charlie Kirk assassination. Cover up caught," tagging several other researcher accounts, with an attached graphic labelled "Cover Up in Charlie Kirk Assassination" comparing "Before" and "After" media layouts. The analytics show impressions of 94, engagements of 12, detail expands of 5, and a profile visits row.

The numbers are the point. A thread claiming to document a cover-up reached fewer than a hundred impressions, while other posts held in this cluster on the same subject record hundreds of thousands or millions. That distribution gap is itself part of the censorship and amplification question this archive tracks, though low reach also has ordinary explanations including account size.
