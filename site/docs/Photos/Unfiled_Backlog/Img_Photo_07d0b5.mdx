## What This Image Shows

A screenshot showing a composite of nested captures on the left and a live thread on the right. The outer post is from an account shown as **MJTruthUltra**, timestamped 12:30 PM, **9/14/25**.

The nested capture shows a post from an account shown as @TallyHallAlbum, timestamped 2:47 AM, **Sep 3, 2025**, with 169 views, reading: "itd be funny if someone like charlie kirk got shot on september 10th LMAO." A second nested panel reads "Donald Trump. December 14th." The account page shows "Something went wrong. Try again" and a notice reading "This Post was deleted by the Post author." The outer post tags federal accounts and reads "Y'all may want to take a look at @tallyhallAlbum." Replies below read: "needs to track down whoever had this @TallyHallAlbum account. They knew a day ahead. And have called out a date for Trump," and "Don't forget to let @USSecretService know of their upcoming threat in December."

This is one of the most-circulated foreknowledge claims in the case, and it is also the kind most easily fabricated: a screenshot of a deleted post from an anonymous account, unverifiable after deletion. Nothing here establishes that anyone knew anything in advance, and the replies' conclusion that "they knew" is the repliers' inference. The archive treats it as a claim to be checked, not a finding.
