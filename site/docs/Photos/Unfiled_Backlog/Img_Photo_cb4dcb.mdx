## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as RealRobert, handle @Real_RobN, posted an hour before capture. The embedded video player is frozen on a television election graphic reading CALIFORNIA RECALL, SHOULD GOV. NEWSOM BE RECALLED, with totals of 4,530,002 at 67.1 percent and 2,225,915 at 32.9 percent.

The post reads: "September 14, 2021 Gavin Newscum recall election live on CNN. 351,709 votes magically disappears from the YES total. 2,225,915 - 1,874,206 = 351,709. This is called a 'glitch' - a phenomenon that, mysteriously, favors Democrats exclusively by default. By the way, if you were around in 2020 - you'd know about the glitch too."

This is election material rather than case material. Live broadcast tallies routinely revise as counties report and correct, which is the ordinary explanation for a moving number on air; the interpretation offered is the poster's. See [Vote](/Vote/overview).
