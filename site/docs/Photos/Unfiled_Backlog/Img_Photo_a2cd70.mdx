## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as YourLeastFavoriteGuy, handle @BeneathTheBrain, posted nineteen hours before capture, with a three-image collage: a portrait of a smiling man, a website search error page, and a mobile page showing a company co-founder listing.

The post reads: "Charlie Kirk's security detail, Echelon Front, has wiped their records of the 'commence firing' arm signal man," and adds: "Weird. They disappeared." The error page reads: "you are looking for using the form below. If that still doesn't provide the results you are looking for, you can always start over from the home page." A leadership panel reads "LEIF BABIN, CO-FOUNDER and PRESIDENT."

The characterization of an arm movement as a firing signal is the poster's reading of video and is not established by anything shown. A page returning a search error is not proof of deletion; sites are restructured routinely. What the frame does record is a claim that a security firm's public staff listings changed after the event, which is checkable against archived copies. The people named are business figures shown on their own company's public pages, and nothing here indicates wrongdoing.
