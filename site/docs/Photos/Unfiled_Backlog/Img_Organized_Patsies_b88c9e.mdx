## What This Image Shows

A researcher-made collage on a white background, built from red and blue text blocks around four photographic inserts of men. The layout numbers the inserts and labels them.

The on-screen text reads: "Organized Murder," then "#1 Tayler + Distraction Patsy #1 + Distraction Patsy #2 means organized sophistication," then "NOT A Lone Wolf Murder." The individual inserts are labelled "#1," "#2," "#3," "Pellet Gun Man," "Distraction Patsy #1" and "Distraction Patsy #2."

This is an argument graphic rather than evidence. Its claim is structural: that the presence of multiple unexplained figures acting at the same time is harder to reconcile with a single-actor account than with a coordinated one. The people in the inserts are not publicly identified, the labels are the maker's own, and nothing here establishes that any of them did anything wrong. The underlying figures are tracked in [Distraction People](/distraction_people/overview), [Suspects](/Suspects/overview) and [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview).
