## What This Image Shows

A composite screenshot dated **September 10, 2025**. The upper portion is a user comment; the lower portion is a business registry lookup record.

The comment reads: "The weird part about this is that it shows that plane is registered to Derrick Porter Maxfield... upon looking up that name... this is what you find," followed by a partial university web address. The registry record reads: "Company Name: N888KG LLC. Entity Type: UTAH FOREIGN LLC. File Number: 12947959-0161. Filing State: Utah (UT). Domestic State: Delaware (DE). Filing Status: Active. Filing Date: July 20, 2022. Company Age: 3 Years, 2 Months. Registered Agent: Derek Porter Maxfield, 4101 N Thanksgiving Way Ste 420, Lehi, UT 84043. Principal Address: same. Expiration Date: July 31, 2023. Products/Services: 5324 - Commercial and Industrial Machinery and Equipment Rental and Leasing."

**N888KG** is the aircraft that left Provo about an hour after the shooting and dropped off tracking mid-flight, discussed elsewhere in this cluster along with the terrain-based counter-explanation. This record is the ownership side of that thread: a Delaware LLC registered in Utah under an equipment leasing classification, which is a routine structure for aircraft ownership. Holding an aircraft through an LLC is ordinary and lawful, and nothing here indicates wrongdoing by anyone.
