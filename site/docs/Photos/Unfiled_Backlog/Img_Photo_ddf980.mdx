## What This Image Shows

A broadcast graphic showing a long block of black text in a white card with a drop shadow, over a light textured background carrying a repeated serif watermark. A pill-shaped badge at the lower right shows a recording dot and a viewer count of 876K.

The message reads: "All the people that trusted you, Jews and Christians. Including me! Why would you platform anti-Semites like Candace and Tucker? There are many Evangelical Christian Zionists who would never give these people the opportunity to spew their hatred. You say people are accusing you of being an antisemite because you disagree with the Israeli government. Well what is it exactly that you disagree with? You threatened Jews and Christians with your 30 mn followers? Are you kidding?! Be very careful, Charlie. You are in violation of the 9th Commandment, by bearing false witness against your brother, Judah. When was the last time you read your Bible, Charlie."

This is the fuller version of the message held elsewhere in this cluster, read out to a live audience of nearly a million. The line "Be very careful, Charlie" is the one researchers have fixed on, in a message otherwise framed in religious terms. It is a rebuke from a viewer, not a threat by any established account.
