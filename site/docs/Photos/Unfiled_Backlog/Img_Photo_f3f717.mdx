## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as 1984, handle @TheOfficial1984, posted seven hours before capture, with an attached black-and-white graphic headed "Executive Summary."

The post reads: "TLDR version.. THE @ADL IS SPYING ON YOU. ISRAELI INTELLIGENCE IS SPYING ON YOU. THEY ARE MANIPULATING YOUR FEED, AND YOUR MIND, USING BOTS, AND AI BEHOLDEN TO A HOSTILE FOREIGN GOVERNMENT THAT WANTS TO KILL YOU. X IS THE WILD WEST." The attached summary is far more careful: "The query references the ADL's partnerships with tech firms like Cyabra and CHEQ - analyzed in prior reports - as potential privacy risks, viewed through the lens of ADL's documented history of surveillance and infiltration activities. This analysis confirms that ADL's past operations, spanning decades, involved extensive spying on activists, civil rights groups, and critics of Israel, often in collaboration with law enforcement and informants. These actions, exposed in scandals like the 1993 FBI investigation, raise legitimate concerns about data handling in modern partnerships."

The gap between the caption and the summary is the whole story of this genre. The summary refers to a documented historical episode and frames present-day partnerships as raising concerns; the caption converts that into a claim about a hostile government wanting to kill the reader. Only the first is supportable. See [Censorship](/Censorship/overview) and [Technology and Surveillance](/technology_surveillance/overview).
