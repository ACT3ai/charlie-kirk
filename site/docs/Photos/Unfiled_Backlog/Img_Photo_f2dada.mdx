## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as @amuse, posted four hours before capture, with an article-style graphic containing a headline, a red and blue map of New Jersey and a photograph of celebrating politicians under falling confetti. A quoted post from an account shown as Michelle Maxwell displays election data tables.

The post reads: "ELECTION INTEGRITY: New Jersey's population grew 351K. Voter rolls grew 554K (mostly all Democrat). Democrats won big. Conservatives call it what it looks like, mass registration fraud." The article panel repeats the figures and states that "Republicans are calling for audits, arguing that voter growth without population growth is proof of institutional fraud."

This is election material rather than case material. Registration totals and population change are measured differently and over different bases, so a gap between them is not by itself evidence of fraud; the framing is the poster's.
