## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as **Diligent Denizen**, handle @DiligentDenizen, posted an hour before capture, with two side-by-side video frames showing the crowd in front of a multi-story building, red annotations marking a spot on the roofline. Both frames carry a source timestamp of 1:09:45.

The post reads: "8 minutes before Charlie Kirk was shot an object or person on the roofline was seen to his right. (9:04, 10:42) on the 1-hour 10-minute video. 30 seconds after Charlie Kirk is shot (19:55) the object is GONE from the roof line. A witness stated he looked LEFT at the shot." The annotations are labelled "Object there" and "Object."

The method here is sound in shape: cite a single continuous source video, give the exact timecodes, and show the same pixel region before and after. Whether the object is a person, equipment or a compression artifact is not resolvable at this resolution, and the direction the witness looked cuts against the side the object is on, which the poster notes rather than hides.
