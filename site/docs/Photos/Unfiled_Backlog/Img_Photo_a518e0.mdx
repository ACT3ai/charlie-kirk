## What This Image Shows

A light-mode screenshot of a post from an account shown as Tinkerbell Trolling, handle @NOT2Bsilenced, posted an hour before capture, with a link preview card for an NPR article.

The post reads: "Find the people that were NOT on the manifest leaving that morning - thst had come in before and you have your crew. They will be Mossad though. Egypt has no benefit from shutting down Charlie's voice - but ISRAEL did... and Mossad hires terrorists, pretending to be CIA...the list is endless. They're ruthless." The preview card reads: "Foreign Policy: Israeli Spies Claim To Be American. According to U.S. intelligence officials, Israelis, posed as CIA officers to recruit members of the..."

The linked article is real published reporting about a historical intelligence practice; the application of it to this case is the poster's speculation and is not supported by anything in the frame. The manifest idea is, in principle, a checkable method, comparing arrival and departure records for the same aircraft, and it bears on the Egyptian aircraft thread this archive tracks.
