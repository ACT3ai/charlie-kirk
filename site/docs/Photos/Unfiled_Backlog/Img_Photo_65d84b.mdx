## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post from an account shown as RealRobert, handle @Real_RobN, posted three hours before capture, with an embedded clip of a middle-aged man in glasses speaking to camera.

The post reads: "November 8, 2022 - Arizona Gubernatorial Election. Poll Official: Witnessed firsthand. At 11:30 a.m., a technician arrived to check the voting machines. Only then did the tabulators begin to experience issues. Between 11:30 a.m. and 12:00 p.m.: 10% of ballots were failing. Between 12:00 and 12:30 p.m.: 20% of ballots were failing. Between 12:30 and 1:00 p.m.: 4 out of 7 ballots were failing." It quotes the official: "I will never forget the look on these affected voters' faces. They were in disbelief that our voting system had failed them en masse on the most important Election Day of the 2022 cycle." It closes with allegations against two named state officials.

This is election material rather than case material. The tabulator problems in that election were real and were litigated; the claim that named officials rigged the result was rejected by the courts and is not adopted here. See [Vote](/Vote/overview).
