## What This Image Shows

A smartphone screenshot of a Facebook post dated **May 1, 2019** from the page **Iris Relief**. The photograph shows a man in his late forties or fifties in a dark suit and patterned tie standing at a wooden podium in front of a white step-and-repeat backdrop tiled with the **IRVING POLICE** logo.

The post text reads that [**Rick Cutler**](/People/rick-cutler) just retired from twenty-nine years of service with Irving PD, that he will be moving to Guatemala serving as Director of Training and Logistics for Iris Relief, and that he will be training and equipping first responders, described as the body of Christ, in prevention, sustainability and preparedness, giving hope, saving lives and rescuing the one. It closes with congratulations.

This is the source document behind the career summary that circulates in the research threads about him: long-service municipal police officer, then faith-based international first-responder training. It is a public announcement of a retirement and a humanitarian posting, and it establishes background only. Nothing in it bears on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), and the site alleges no wrongdoing.
