## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution wide shot of the event stage, wrapped in blue fabric panels with white banners reading **THE AMERICAN COMEBACK** on the front faces, a crowd of supporters gathered in front of it in hard sunlight. The speaker's face has been blurred out by whoever processed the image, and another presenter stands off to the side.

The legible fragments in the frame are the banner text and partial letters from the surrounding signage. The blur is not part of the original footage; it was added downstream, and it is a reminder of how much of the public image record for this event has passed through several hands before reaching a researcher.

This frame belongs to the plaid-shirt set because the man being tracked is in it, positioned behind the front row. Wide shots like this establish where a person stood relative to the stage and the barricade line, which is the only thing a frame at this resolution can honestly support. Nothing about the position shown implies anything about conduct.
