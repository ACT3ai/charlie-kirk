## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a social-media memorial post. The photograph at the top shows a young serviceman in United States Marine Corps desert MARPAT camouflage sitting on dusty ground, leaning back against a rough mud-brick wall.

The post beneath is dated **11-Jun-2025** and attributed to **Blake Harruff**. It reads that fifteen years ago it seems like yesterday, that not a day goes by that we do not think about you and the price you paid, and closes with love you Brum. It carries 58 reactions. On its face it is a personal act of remembrance for a fallen comrade, posted by whoever holds that account.

It is filed here because it is one of the documents researchers used to attach a name and a service background to the plaid-shirt figure in the event footage. That is its only investigative function, and the site includes it with the note that a memorial post says nothing whatsoever about the events of [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) and implies no conduct by anyone.
