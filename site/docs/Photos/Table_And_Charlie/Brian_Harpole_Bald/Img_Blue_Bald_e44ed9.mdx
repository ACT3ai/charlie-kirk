## What This Image Shows

A wide frame taken from within the crowd, looking in at the tent. The host stands near the table in the centre-right of the frame with one arm outstretched, while spectators and crew watch, photograph and record on phones. A weathered blue vinyl banner reading **WRONG PROVE ME** with the lightning-bolt device spans the top of the frame.

The automated description generated for this file names a different commentator as the host; the banner, the table layout and the surrounding frames place it in the same **Prove Me Wrong** setup covered throughout this section, and the site does not adopt that automated identification.

What the frame documents is the crowd-side view: how close attendees stood, how many were filming, and how open the table was to people approaching it. That openness is the practical background to the security questions the site raises about screening and perimeter control.
