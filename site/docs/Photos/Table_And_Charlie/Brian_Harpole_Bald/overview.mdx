## About This Cluster

This sub-cluster tracks a man with short grey hair in a blue button-down shirt, wearing a beige acoustic-tube earpiece of the type used by protective details, who is visible in multiple frames inside the barricade line at the **Prove Me Wrong** tent. Researchers working the public footage attached the name **Brian Harpole** to that figure. Harpole is a real, named, living person who has spoken publicly about the event, and the site records what the images show without asserting that he did anything improper or knew anything in advance.

The frames are mostly wide event shots: [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) seated on the stage in the white FREEDOM T-shirt with the blue **PROVE ME WRONG** banner and its stars-and-stripes lightning bolt stretched overhead, the crowd pressed against grey-covered metal barricades, a camera operator on a tripod ringed in red by the poster, and Kirk standing and pointing while holding a cap. One image is a four-panel grid collage compiled by a researcher, showing the same grey-haired man in aviator sunglasses with a thick black cross-body strap in several settings including a grass lawn at a [**TURNING POINT USA**](/TPUSA/overview) backdrop and a firearms training environment. The circles and crops are the compilers' emphasis, not findings. Investigation notes also record that a civil suit Harpole filed against [**Candace Owens**](/People/candace-owens) in the Middle District of Tennessee saw the district's judges recuse themselves, with an outside judge brought in; that is a reported procedural fact about litigation, unrelated to any allegation against him here.
