## What This Image Shows

An outdoor frame taken over the heads of the crowd, several of them in red and white caps. In the centre a man in a blue button-down and sunglasses stands facing the camera; behind him a man in a white polo and white baseball cap is speaking into a microphone.

The legible banner fragments read **ERICAN** and **INE**, parts of the American Comeback signage. The framing is the useful part: the man in blue is oriented outward, toward the crowd, while the person with the microphone faces the other way. That is the standard posture split between someone working a crowd line and someone addressing it.

The site records the orientation as an observation, not as proof of role, and asserts nothing about the individual. Positions established from frames like this feed the composite picture of who was inside the barricade at what time.
