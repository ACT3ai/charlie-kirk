## What This Image Shows

A low-angle vertical frame taken from behind a man kneeling on the grass with his back to the camera, in a white polo, dark jeans and trail-running shoes. Beyond him, security personnel and crew are in frantic motion around the low stage platform, and one figure appears to be tumbling or vaulting over a barrier. Behind them the crowd watches from the grassy hillside behind metal barricades in hard sunlight.

There is no readable on-screen text.

This is the clearest public frame of a person actually crossing the barrier line, which is the physical act that named the table jumpers question. What it does not show is who, or why, or what they did next. A person going over a barrier toward someone who has just been shot is as consistent with rushing to help as with anything else, and the site draws no inference. Nobody in the frame is identified and no conduct is alleged.
