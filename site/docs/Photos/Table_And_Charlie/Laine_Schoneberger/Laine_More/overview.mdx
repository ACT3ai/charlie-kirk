## About This Cluster

This page holds two additional crowd frames kept with the [Laine Schoneberger](/Photos/Table_And_Charlie/Laine_Schoneberger/overview) comparison set. Both show [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) in the white FREEDOM T-shirt working the front of the crowd, one as he leans forward to catch a red baseball cap frozen in mid-air and one as he points forward with the cap suspended beside him, surrounded by raised phones.

Their value here is not the subject but the background. Because both are high-resolution press-quality frames rather than compressed video grabs, the faces of the people standing immediately behind and beside Kirk are legible in a way that most of the Table and Charlie material is not. That makes them the reference plates researchers use when trying to match a low-resolution figure from a video still to a person actually standing at the table line. The frames also record the physical geometry of the cap-throwing exchange, which is one of the routine object movements across the table that the [Table Jumpers](/Mic/Table_Jumpers_Device) thread has to account for before treating any single hand movement as unusual.

Go back to the parent set at [Laine Schoneberger](/Photos/Table_And_Charlie/Laine_Schoneberger/overview) for the comparison images, and to [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) for the biography. For the surrounding event and who was inside the barricade, see [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview) and [UVU](/UVU/overview).
