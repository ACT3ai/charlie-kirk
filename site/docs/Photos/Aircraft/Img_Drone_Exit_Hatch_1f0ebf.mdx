## What This Image Shows

A composite graphic. The upper left carries The War Zone headline "Army Wants To Test Air-Launched Drones From A Global Express Business Jet" with the standfirst about trials tallying "with previous comments about launching the drones from the" [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) jet, bylined **THOMAS NEWDICK**. The rest of the frame is a large photograph of a military-modified Bombardier Global Express in flight against an overcast sky, with a red circle drawn around a fairing on the underside of the rear fuselage.

The circle is the argument. Whoever made the graphic is pointing at a ventral modification and asking whether that is where drones would exit. The filename this image was filed under reads it as a drone exit hatch, which is the poster's interpretation rather than an identification from any published diagram.

What can be said carefully: aircraft in this program carry belly-mounted sensor fairings for the [surveillance mission](/Planes/ISR-Operations), and a fairing visible in a photograph does not establish a launch tube. **[@tjphager](/Influencers/x/tjphager)** has argued specifically that the airframe was never built out to launch drones, which is the countervailing view worth holding while looking at this image.

See [N1098L Air-Launched Drones](/Drones/N1098L_Air_Launched_Drones), the operator research in [Nwq](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview), and [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview).
