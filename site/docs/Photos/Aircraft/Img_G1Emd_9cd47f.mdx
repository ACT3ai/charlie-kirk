## What This Image Shows

A mobile screenshot of a **Perplexity** search answer. The query reads: "What does this aircraft do: US Army's Bombardier Global 6500 from Leidos, N1098L / AXLE10."

The answer states that the aircraft "is a next-generation airborne platform used primarily for intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) missions as part of the High Accuracy Detection and Exploitation System ([HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES)) program," and that "its core function is to enhance deep sensing capabilities, enabling extended-range detection, surveillance, and exploitation of threats at high altitudes and over long dur..." Three numbered source citations are attached.

The substance here is uncontroversial and matches published defense reporting: HADES is a real Army program, the Global 6500 is its airframe, and the mission is long-range sensing. What the answer does not do -- and cannot -- is say anything about what this particular aircraft was doing over Utah on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). Answers of this kind are useful for establishing the platform's role and useless for establishing intent.

Note also the callsign spelling: this screenshot reads **AXLE10**, while the Flightradar24 captures elsewhere in this cluster read **AXEL10**. That discrepancy runs through the investigation's notes and is worth resolving before either spelling is cited.
