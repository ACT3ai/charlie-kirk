## What This Image Shows

The flight-tracker detail panel that anchors the whole **SU-BTT** timeline.

It reads: **SUBTT**, type **FA7X**, operator **Egypt Government**; **PVU PROVO**, "Departed N/A ago"; **Dassault Falcon 7X**; **ILG WILMINGTON**, "Arriving in 03:42"; **BAROMETRIC ALT. 0 ft**, **GROUND SPEED 0 kts**, **REG. SU-BTT**. The playback timeline is set to **Wed, Sep 10, 2025**, with the scrubber at **13:08 UTC**.

Every element of the claim is legible in a single frame -- the registration, the Egyptian government operator, [Provo](/Locations/Provo_Airport) as origin, Wilmington as destination, and the date -- which is why this capture is reproduced so widely. The zero readings for altitude and groundspeed mean the aircraft was still on the ground when this moment was captured.

One detail is routinely lost in retelling: 13:08 UTC is **7:08 AM** local time in Utah, more than five hours before the 12:23 PM shooting. The aircraft was preparing to leave that morning, not fleeing afterward, and honest accounts of the timeline should say so while still asking why it was there at all.
