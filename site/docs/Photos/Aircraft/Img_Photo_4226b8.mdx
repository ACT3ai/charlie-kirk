## What This Image Shows

A typed multi-year itinerary compiled from flight records, organized by year on a pale background.

Sample entries: "**2022** November 13th: Cairo to Paris to Wichita, KS to Wilmington to Cairo. December 20th: Cairo to Paris to St. Louis, MO to Bangor, ME to Cairo. **2023** February 25th: Cairo to Paris to St. Louis, MO to Wilmington to Cairo. April 14th: Cairo to Paris to Wichita, KS to Wilmington to Giza to Cairo. **2024** February 9th: Cairo to Paris to Omaha Nebraska. March 29th: Cairo to Paris to Wichita, Kansas. April 23rd: Paris to Williston, ND to Provo..."

This is the multi-year pattern argument in its most compact form. The same shape repeats: out of Cairo, through Paris, into a mid-continent United States airport -- Wichita, Omaha, Lincoln, St. Louis, Minot, Williston, Provo -- then Wilmington, then home. Researchers read the recurrence as a route structure rather than as unrelated trips.

What the itinerary supports is that these routings happened repeatedly over four years. What it does not supply is purpose, passengers, or any tested claim that the stops correlate with TPUSA event cities -- that correlation has been asserted by **Candace Owens** and others and has not been independently verified here.
