## What This Image Shows

A mobile browser capture of an IRS filing on **ProPublica**'s nonprofit explorer. The document is **Schedule G (Form 990), Supplemental Information Regarding Fundraising or Gaming Activities**, filed by **Turning Point USA Inc**, with Object ID 202501359349304810 and a submission date of **2025-05-15**. Visible column headings include name of grantee, IRS code, purpose of grant, amount of cash grant, manner of disbursement, description, and method of appraisal.

This is a public tax filing, and it is filed in the aircraft folder because the Wilmington thread and the organizational-finance thread were being researched together. Schedule G covers fundraising and gaming activity, and the grant columns visible here are the standard reporting fields for a large nonprofit.

Nothing in a Form 990 filing establishes wrongdoing -- these are routine annual disclosures required of every tax-exempt organization. Questions about the organization's finances have been raised publicly, including reporting that [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) had ordered an internal audit committee shortly before his death, and those questions are covered with attribution elsewhere on this site.
