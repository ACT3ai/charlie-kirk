## What This Image Shows

A crop of the same **Defense Post** article, framed on the illustration. A matte light-grey aircraft based on a Bombardier Global 6500 is shown banking above a dense cloud layer with snow-capped peaks below. The headline above reads "SNC Accepts Second Bombardier Jet for US Army [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) Spy Plane Program," bylined Rojoef Manuel, September 10, 2025, and the caption below reads "**Rendering of the future HADES ISR aircraft based on the Global 6500 business jet. Photo: US Army/Bombardier Defense.**"

This is the image at the heart of a persistent misunderstanding. The grey, purposeful-looking aircraft that illustrated much of the online spy-plane discussion is a **digital rendering of a planned configuration**, and the article's own caption says so.

The aircraft that flew low over the Utah Valley on September 10, 2025 was a plain white Global 6500, as its tracking-site photographs show. Anyone forming an impression from the rendering is forming it from artwork commissioned to depict a future capability.

See the explicit comparison in [Nwq](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview), the [cluster overview](/Photos/Aircraft/Articles/overview), and [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview).
