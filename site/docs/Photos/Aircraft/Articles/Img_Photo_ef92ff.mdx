## What This Image Shows

A capture of a **Defense Post** article bylined **Rojoef Manuel** and dated **September 10, 2025, 8:09 am**, filed under Air, Americas.

The headline reads "**SNC Accepts Second Bombardier Jet for US Army [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) Spy Plane Program**." The body states: "**Sierra Nevada Corporation (SNC)** has received the second Bombardier Global 6500 business jet for conversion into the US Army's **High Accuracy Detection and Exploitation System (HADES)** aircraft. HADES is being developed to give the force long-range platforms with a higher speed and service ceiling tha[n]..." The illustration is captioned "Rendering of the future HADES ISR aircraft based on the Global 6500 business jet. Photo: US Army/Bombardier Defense."

The publication date is the coincidence that made this article circulate: it went out on the morning of the [shooting](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). That is a coincidence of news scheduling, not evidence -- defence-trade outlets publish daily and the piece concerns an aircraft acceptance in Nevada.

Two substantive points survive. The second airframe was still being **converted** as of that date, which bears on what was and was not operational. And the illustration is explicitly labelled a rendering, the exact confusion the [Nwq](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview) comparison graphic sets out to correct.
