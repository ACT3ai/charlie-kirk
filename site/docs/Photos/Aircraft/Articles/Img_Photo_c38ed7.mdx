## What This Image Shows

The tightest crop of the article in this cluster: the headline, the image, and the caption, with everything else stripped away.

The headline reads "**SNC Accepts Second Bombardier Jet for US Army [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) Spy Plane Program**." The image shows a sleek white twin-engine Global 6500 banking above cloud with mountain peaks breaking through. The caption reads "**Rendering of the future HADES ISR aircraft based on the Global 6500 business jet. Photo: US Army/Bombardier Defense.**"

Stripped to its essentials, the article says two things: a second airframe has been accepted for conversion, and the picture is a rendering of what the finished aircraft is intended to look like. The phrase "spy plane" in the headline is the outlet's shorthand for an [intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance platform](/Planes/ISR-Operations), not a claim about any particular flight.

Crops like this are how a document travels: shared without the byline, the date or the caveats, a headline becomes an assertion. Keeping the caption in frame is what stops that happening here.
