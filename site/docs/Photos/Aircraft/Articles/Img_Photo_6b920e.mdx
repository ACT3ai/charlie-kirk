## What This Image Shows

A second capture of the same **Defense Post** article, framed slightly differently -- navigation bar, the photograph of the white Global 6500 on the tarmac, headline "**US Army [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) Program Receives First Global 6500 Jet From Bombardier**," byline **CHRISTINE CASIMIRO, NOVEMBER 27, 2024**, and the social sharing row.

Taking two captures of one article is ordinary research practice: one framing preserves the page furniture and date, the other the image and headline. Together they make the source hard to dispute later.

The photograph itself is the useful part of this crop. It shows the aircraft type in plain white livery with no visible sensor fairings or antenna farms -- which is what a Global 6500 looks like before mission fit-out, and what [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview) looks like in tracking-site photographs. That visual point is made explicitly in the rendering-versus-reality graphic in the [Nwq](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview) sub-cluster.
