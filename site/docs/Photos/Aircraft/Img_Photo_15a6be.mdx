## What This Image Shows

A validation sheet auditing the circulating flight claims, each entry numbered with a date, a route and a status marked with a tick or a cross.

Sample entries: "**41 - 9/10/25 - SUBTT.** Route: Cairo - Paris - Minot - Provo. Status: **Correct**." "**42 - 9/18/25 - SU-BND.** Route: Paris - Lincoln - Wilmington - Cairo. Status: **Correct**." "**60 - 1/12/25 - SUBTT.** Route: Paris - Wichita - Cairo. Status: **Incorrect - repeat of #31**." "**61 - 2/8/25 - SUBTT.** Route: Paris - Omaha - Cairo. Status: **Incorrect - in Cairo**." "**62 - 3/15/25 - SUBTT.** Route: Wichita - Wilmington - Cairo. Status: **Incorrect - duplicate of #33 except misnotated**." "**64 - 4/19/25 - SU-BND.** Route: Provo - Paris - SLC - Provo. Status: **Incorrect - in Cairo**."

This is the most methodologically honest document in the entire aircraft cluster, and it deserves attention out of proportion to its plainness. Somebody took the widely shared list of flights and checked each one against the records, finding duplicates, misnotations, and entries where the aircraft was demonstrably in Cairo on the claimed date. The count of trips in circulation is therefore inflated.

It cuts both ways: the [September 10](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) SU-BTT routing and the September 18 SU-BND routing are both marked Correct. The pattern survives the audit; the exaggerated version of it does not.
