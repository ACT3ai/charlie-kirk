## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a business-registry page for **N888KG LLC**. The record states the company "is an Utah Foreign LLC filed on July 20, 2022," that its "filing status is listed as Active," and gives File Number 12947959-0161. It lists a registered agent and two contacts, with a principal address on **N Thanksgiving Way in Lehi, Utah**.

The aircraft this entity is named for is the Bombardier Challenger 300 that researchers report leaving [Provo](/Locations/Provo_Airport) about an hour after the [shooting](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) and dropping off ADS-B mid-flight. This record is why the ownership question has any traction at all: the tail number is held by a single-purpose limited liability company rather than by a named individual or a charter operator, which is an ordinary and entirely lawful arrangement for private aircraft but also one that puts a layer between the tail number and whoever was flying.

The individuals named in this filing are private people. Appearing in a state business registry is a matter of routine public record and implies nothing whatever about the events of September 10, 2025, and nothing on this site should be read as suggesting otherwise. What remains unanswered is a narrow factual question: who was aboard that flight, and no public record answers it.
