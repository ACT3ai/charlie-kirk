## What This Image Shows

A Flightradar24 playback for **N59906**. The map shows a complex looping track over Utah Lake and the surrounding valley, with Lehi, American Fork, Lindon, Orem, Provo, Springville, Mapleton, Spanish Fork, Salem, Payson and Santaquin all labelled and a clock reading **19:59 UTC**.

The panel below reads: "Playback of flight N59906," FROM **Provo (PVU)**, TO **Provo (PVU)**, ACTUAL FLIGHT TIME **2:39**, TIME 5:35 PM UTC, **BAROMETRIC ALTITUDE 19,000 FT**, **GROUND SPEED 212 KTS**, VERTICAL SPEED 0 FPM.

This is the second aircraft over the Utah Valley on **September 10, 2025**, and its profile is quite different from the Global 6500's. A two-hour-thirty-nine-minute flight departing and returning to the same field, holding a steady 19,000 feet at 212 knots, flying a repeating loop -- that is a textbook aerial survey pattern, and it is exactly what a Piper PA-31 equipped for mapping and imaging would fly.

The unresolved questions are not about the profile but about the tasking: who commissioned it, and whether imagery from the four passes over campus exists. Neither **MARC Inc.** nor any agency has answered publicly.
