## What This Image Shows

An aircraft registration record for **N59906**, shown as white text on black.

It reads: "Aircraft N59906 is currently in a registration status of '**In Question**,' meaning its registration may not be suitable for operation until resolved. The listed owner name is **REGISTRATION PENDING**, with the address 101 Highpointe Ct Ste C, Brandon, Mississippi. An application was submitted on **July 31, 2025**, by applicant **ALPHA WHISKEY 2 LLC**." The specification block gives Fixed Wing Multi-Engine, **PIPER PA-31-350**, serial **31-7552027**, year manufactured **1974**, airworthiness date December 24, 1974, Lycoming TIO-540 engines, Mode S code **A7C14D**.

This is the documentary basis for **John Cullen**'s claim that the aircraft "isn't supposed to be airborne, at all" because its registration is "In Question." The record confirms the status and the pending-transfer application dated six weeks before the shooting.

A registration in transition is common and not by itself improper -- ownership changes routinely leave a period where FAA records show a pending application. But an aircraft flying survey patterns over a campus while its registration is unresolved is a legitimate question, and the client for the flight has never been identified.
