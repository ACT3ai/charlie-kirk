## What This Image Shows

A FlightAware capture for **N1098L** on **WED 10-SEP-2025**. The route header reads **BIF FORT BLISS / EL PASO, TX** departing **07:48AM MDT**, to **KALISPELL, MT (GPI)** arriving **10:38AM MDT**, with "2h 50m total travel time." A map shows the track running north across Utah and Montana, and a performance graph plots altitude and speed below.

Someone has annotated the image with the line "Lowest recorded altitude of 4700 ft ASL @ 9:16 MDT." That timestamp falls inside the window investigators point to for the first low pass over the Utah Valley, and 4,700 feet above sea level is only a few hundred feet above the valley floor, which sits near 4,500 feet.

That altitude annotation is where the interpretive work begins. Read as feet above sea level, a Global 6500 at 4,700 feet over [Provo](/Locations/Provo_Airport) is extraordinarily low for a transit flight. Read carelessly as feet above ground, it would be unremarkable. The distinction is exactly the kind of detail that separates a documented anomaly from a misread chart, and it is worth checking against the raw track log rather than the annotation.
