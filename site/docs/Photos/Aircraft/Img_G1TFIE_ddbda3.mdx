## What This Image Shows

A four-panel composite of the aircraft-for-sale listing that circulates through this cluster. The breadcrumb reads "AIRCRAFT FOR SALE &gt; BOMBARDIER &gt; GLOBAL 6500 &gt; 2023." The listing shows "Call for price," "Aircraft Location: Wilmington, Delaware," "Photos (5)," and seller **AeroAmerica Group**, Sales Team, Manassas, Virginia, with tabs for Operating Costs and financing. One panel is captioned "FILE PHOTO / SAMPLE OF SPECIAL MISSION INTERIOR" and shows a cabin rendering with console positions rather than passenger seating.

The special-mission interior caption is what makes this more than a sales page. It documents that Global 6500 airframes are marketed in a mission-configured layout -- operator consoles instead of a corporate cabin -- which is the configuration the surveillance-aircraft argument depends on.

Two geographic details recur elsewhere in the investigation: **Manassas, Virginia**, which is also the address published for **LASAI Aviation II LLC**, the operator associated with N1098L, and **Wilmington, Delaware**, which is where the Egyptian jet SU-BTT is reported to have flown on September 10. Both are noted here as recurrences rather than as established connections.
