## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a flight-tracker aircraft record shared in a post, with a hand-drawn green circle over one of the fields. The record lists AIRCRAFT **Bombardier Challenger 300**, SERIAL NUMBER 20402, AGE 12 years, AIRLINE Private owner, OPERATOR N/A, COUNTRY OF REGISTRATION United States, TYPE CODE CL30, MODE S **AC3C75**. Below the specification block the page reads "No flights found." The accompanying post text reads: "And they are a ghost as well."

The "ghost" framing means the aircraft has no public flight history on that service. That condition has several ordinary causes worth stating plainly: an owner can enroll in the FAA's Limiting Aircraft Data Displayed programme or its successor, blocking the tail number from public trackers, and the same suppression appears on **N888KG**, whose record elsewhere in this cluster notes it "is not available for public tracking per request." Aircraft that have not flown recently also return empty histories.

So the absence of data is real and documented, but it is a privacy setting as often as it is anything else. It is preserved here because blocked tail numbers are one of the recurring obstacles in this thread -- researchers cannot verify what they cannot see.

See [N888KG](/Planes/N888KG) for the clearest example, and [Sept 10 Flight Timeline](/Planes/Sept10-Flight-Timeline) for what public data does show.
