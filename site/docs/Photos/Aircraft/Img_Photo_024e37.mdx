## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an aviation trade press release, dated **THU, 10/29/2015**, headlined "**Duncan Aviation Signs a Five-Year Contract With the Egyptian Air Force**." Two passages have been circled in red by hand.

The text reads: "Duncan Aviation announces the company has entered into a five-year direct contract agreement with the Egyptian Air Force (EAF) for aircraft maintenance services. Duncan Aviation has been managing the maintenance and repair of the EAF fleet of Falcon 20 aircraft **since 1999**. Duncan Aviation's Manager of Government &amp; Special Programs Dave Shipperbottom says the fact that the EAF has chosen to fly over closer maintenance companies and bring..."

This is one of the most load-bearing documents in the Egyptian aircraft thread, and it points in a direction the loudest versions of the theory tend to skip past. **Duncan Aviation** is named in the investigation's notes as the Provo facility where the Egyptian crews were handled and where rental cars were issued. This release establishes a long-standing, publicly announced commercial maintenance relationship between that company and the Egyptian Air Force going back to 1999 -- which is an ordinary, documented reason for Egyptian government aircraft to visit Duncan facilities in the United States.

That does not answer every question about the September 4 to September 10 Provo stay, but it is the most obvious innocent explanation on the record. See [Egyptian Crew Hotel](/Planes/Egyptian-Crew-Hotel), [Egyptian Armada](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada) and [SU-BTT Israel/Egyptian Jet](/Planes/SU-BTT).
