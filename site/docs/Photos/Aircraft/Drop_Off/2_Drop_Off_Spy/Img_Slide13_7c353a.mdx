## What This Image Shows

One cut of a thirteen-slide deck built on a single annotated aerial photograph of the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus.

The red banner across the bottom reads: "Plane designed for Drone drop-off (US Army's Bombardier Global 6500). 3 hours before @ 203 feet altitude, over water, 116 knot stall speed. Returned 23 minutes after Assassination, dropped to 203' altititude @ 116 knots (pick up?). Flight N1098L. Data from Gov flight public website (AXLE10). Take-off: [Biggs Army Airfield](/Planes/Airport-Biggs-Army-Airfield). Spy Program: [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) (High, Accuracy, Detection &amp; Exploitation, System)." The misspelling of "altititude" and the question mark after "pick up?" are in the original.

This cut functions as the analytical slide, with the red boxes and arrows pointing out specific ground locations and the telemetry summarised below. It is a clear piece of visual argument, and its clarity is also its risk -- a well-designed graphic can make an inference feel like an observation.
