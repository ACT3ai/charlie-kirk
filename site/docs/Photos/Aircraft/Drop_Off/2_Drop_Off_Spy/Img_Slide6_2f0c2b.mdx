## What This Image Shows

One cut of a thirteen-slide deck built on a single annotated aerial photograph of the Utah Valley University campus.

The red banner across the bottom reads: "Plane designed for Drone drop-off (US Army's Bombardier Global 6500). 3 hours before @ 203 feet altitude, over water, 116 knot stall speed. Returned 23 minutes after Assassination, dropped to 203' altititude @ 116 knots (pick up?). Flight N1098L. Data from Gov flight public website (AXLE10). Take-off: Biggs Army Airfield. Spy Program: HADES (High, Accuracy, Detection &amp; Exploitation, System)." The misspelling of "altititude" and the question mark after "pick up?" are in the original.

A landscape cut with the red text boxes at top and the full banner at the bottom. Note the callsign in the banner reads AXLE10, while the Flightradar24 captures in this investigation read AXEL10. That one-character discrepancy runs through the material and should be resolved before the deck is cited.
