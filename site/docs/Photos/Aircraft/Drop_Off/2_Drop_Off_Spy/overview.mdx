## About This Cluster

This is the annotated version of the drop-off argument: one aerial photograph of the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus, marked up and re-cut into a numbered slide deck so the geography and the flight data can be read in a single frame. The red callouts label two points — "Charlies Tent" on the blue canopy in the central plaza, and "Drone Drop Off" on the open water past the campus edge — putting the distance between them in front of the reader instead of describing it. The deck was built for sharing, which is why thirteen near-identical crops of the same image sit in this cluster.

The red banner text is identical on every slide and reads: "Plane designed for Drone drop-off (US Army's Bombardier Global 6500). 3 hours before @ 203 feet altitude, over water, 116 knot stall speed. Returned 23 minutes after Assassination, dropped to 203' altititude @ 116 knots (pick up?). Flight N1098L. Data from Gov flight public website (AXLE10). Take-off: Biggs Army Airfield. Spy Program: [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) (High, Accuracy, Detection &amp; Exploitation, System)." The typo in "altititude" and the question mark after "pick up?" are in the original. Two claims in that block are checkable against public tracking data — the altitude and speed of the two low passes, and the departure from [**Biggs Army Airfield**](/Planes/Airport-Biggs-Army-Airfield) in El Paso — while the inference that drones were released and recovered is exactly that, an inference. The claimed callsign is rendered as AXLE10 here and as AXEL10 elsewhere in the investigation's notes, a small discrepancy worth resolving before the deck is cited.

Because the slides repeat, reading one is enough; the rest are alternate crops. Go back up to [Drop Off](/Photos/Aircraft/Drop_Off/overview) for the clean, unannotated aerial. Then weigh the claim against [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview), [N1098L Air-Launched Drones](/Drones/N1098L_Air_Launched_Drones), and [Sept 10 Flight Timeline](/Planes/Sept10-Flight-Timeline). The removed source article behind the HADES drone-launch reporting is documented in [Cover Up](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/overview).
