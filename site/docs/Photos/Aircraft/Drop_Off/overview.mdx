## About This Cluster

The "drop off" thread is the claim that [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview) did not simply overfly [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) but descended twice to release and then recover small drones over open water. Public ADS-B data is the evidence base: researchers report the aircraft coming down to roughly 203 feet at about 116 knots — close to stall speed for a Global 6500 — hours before the [12:23 PM shooting](/Timeline/12-23-shooting-time), then repeating the same profile about twenty-three minutes afterward, roughly a mile from campus with Utah Lake below. The base geography is what this cluster illustrates, because the argument only makes sense once you can see how close the water sits to the tent.

The image on this page is the unannotated version: a high aerial photograph looking across the UVU campus, low modern academic buildings and walkways in the midground, a reflecting pond with fountains, and Utah Lake with the white **Provo City Center**-style temple silhouette on the horizon. Presented without overlays, it lets a reader judge the distances for themselves before the annotated slides in [2 Drop Off Spy Plane](/Photos/Aircraft/Drop_Off/2_Drop_Off_Spy/overview) tell them what to look at. It is worth holding the counter-argument in mind while looking: the operator has been reported as describing low passes of this kind as drone retrieval training, and researcher **@tjphager** has argued the airframe was never built out to launch drones at all. Neither the flight's purpose nor its tasking authority has been confirmed by any official source.

Open [2 Drop Off Spy Plane](/Photos/Aircraft/Drop_Off/2_Drop_Off_Spy/overview) for the annotated versions of this same view. Then read [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview) for the flight profile, [N1098L Air-Launched Drones](/Drones/N1098L_Air_Launched_Drones) for the launch claim, and [Military Drones On Video](/Drones/Military_Drones_On_Video) for what witnesses and cameras reportedly captured overhead. The departure airfield is covered at [Biggs Army Airfield](/Planes/Airport-Biggs-Army-Airfield).
