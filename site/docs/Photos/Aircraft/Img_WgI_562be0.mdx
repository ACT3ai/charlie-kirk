## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an aviation-news article from **Aeronaut Media**, in the site's Aviation news section, headlined "U.S. Army to Test Launching Drones from Aircraft," bylined **Julia Alexandrova** and dated **11/01/2025**.

The body reads: "The U.S. Army is considering testing drone launches from a specialized version of the Bombardier Global 6500, designed for special missions. This initiative aims to enhance the capabilities of the service's High Accuracy Detection and Exploitation System (HADES), which is based on the Global 6500 platform. The first HADES aircraft was delivered in Nov..."

This capture matters for a specific reason. The primary source most researchers cited, The War Zone, later stopped resolving at the URL they had linked -- the subject of the [Cover Up](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/overview) sub-cluster. This article is a second, independent outlet reporting the same program, which means the underlying claim about the Army's plans does not rest on a single removed page. Note the wording again: the Army is reported to be *considering testing* a capability. That is the ceiling of what the reporting supports.
