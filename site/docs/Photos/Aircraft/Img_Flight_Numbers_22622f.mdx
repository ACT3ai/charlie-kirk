## What This Image Shows

A Flightradar24 mobile capture headed AIRCRAFT **N1098L**, YEAR 2025, listing the aircraft's recent legs under the callsign **AXEL10**.

Reading down the visible rows: on **Sep 10**, Salinas (SNS) to El Paso (BIF); Lake Havasu City (HII) to Salinas (SNS); El Paso (BIF) to Lake Havasu City (HII); and **Kalispell (FCA) to El Paso (BIF)** with ACTUAL DEPARTURE 11:27 AM, SCHEDULED DEPARTURE 11:27 AM, SCHEDULED ARRIVAL 02:31 PM, FLIGHT TIME 03:04, status Landed. Below that, on **Sep 09**, legs run El Paso (BIF) to Kalispell (FCA), Kalispell to El Paso, El Paso (ELP) to Kalispell, and Boise (BOI) to El Paso.

This is the day laid out as a schedule rather than as a map, and it is useful for two reasons. It confirms the callsign-to-registration pairing that every other capture depends on, and it shows the aircraft flying a busy multi-leg pattern across the southwest on September 9 and 10 -- context that any claim about a single Utah orbit has to sit inside. The Kalispell to El Paso leg with an 11:27 AM departure is the one investigators have paired with the earlier morning repositioning flight.
