## What This Image Shows

A close crop of the article itself, without the surrounding social-media post. The headline reads "Army Wants To Test Air-Launched Drones From A Global Express Business Jet," with the standfirst "The planned trials tally with previous comments about launching the drones from the new Global 6500-based HADES surveillance jet." The byline reads **THOMAS NEWDICK / PUBLISHED JAN 9, 2025**. Below it is a photograph of a matte-grey twin-engine business jet climbing.

The January 2025 date matters. This was published eight months before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), in the ordinary course of defense-trade reporting and with no reference to any assassination -- which is exactly what makes it useful as a source. It documents the capability discussion independently of the case.

It is also the article at the center of the removal claim. The [Cover Up](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/overview) sub-cluster holds the 404 page the URL later returned, and the [Versions](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/Cover_Versions/overview) slides pair the two states side by side. Read the headline carefully: it describes planned trials of a capability, which is a long way from establishing that drones were launched over Utah.
