## What This Image Shows

A flight log headed "**Flight history for aircraft - SU-BGM, Gulfstream IV, Egypt Government**," with two photographs of the white jet credited to Jetphotos and a **[@WeAre_TheElite](/Influencers/x/weare-theelite)** watermark.

The rows read: **16 Apr 2025** Goose Bay (YYR) to **Provo (PVU)**, flight time **5:06**, landed **9:16 PM**; **16 Apr 2025** Paris (LBG) to Goose Bay, flight time **4:55**; **10 Apr 2025** **Provo (PVU) to Provo (PVU)**, flight time **0:55**, landed **8:10 PM**. Scheduled and actual departure and arrival times are given alongside each.

The fifth and final aircraft in the group. Two entries stand out: a long trans-Atlantic ferry via Goose Bay arriving at [Provo](/Locations/Provo_Airport) on 16 April, and a **55-minute local flight departing and returning to Provo** on 10 April -- the same local-circuit pattern that [**SU-BND**](/Planes/SU-BND) flew in June and September.

A short local flight from a field where the aircraft is parked is what a maintenance test or a crew currency sortie looks like, particularly at a facility with a long-standing Egyptian Air Force maintenance contract. That is the ordinary reading, and it has neither been confirmed nor ruled out.
