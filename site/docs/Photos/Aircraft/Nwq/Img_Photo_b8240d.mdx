## What This Image Shows

A mobile screenshot of a page about **Leidos**, the contractor behind the operator of [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview).

The text lists four divisions: "**Defense &amp; Intelligence**: Cybersecurity, signals intelligence, and mission support. **Civil**: IT infrastructure, energy engineering, and transportation security. **Health**: Healthcare data management and medical enterprise optimization. **Advanced Solutions**: Research, space, and emerging technologies." It continues: "The company has faced scrutiny, including a 2025 termination of a $2.4 billion DHS cybersecurity contract for convenience, a Chinese export ban on dual-use goods, and ongoing DOJ investigations into potential foreign bribery and antitrust issues related to 2021-2022 contracts."

The first division listed -- signals intelligence and mission support -- is the relevant one, and it establishes that the aircraft's operator sits inside a company whose core business is exactly the kind of work the flight profile suggests.

The scrutiny paragraph needs care. A contract terminated "for convenience" is a routine government cancellation carrying no finding against the contractor, and reported investigations are not conclusions. None of it relates to the events of **September 10, 2025**, and none of it is stated here as established wrongdoing.
