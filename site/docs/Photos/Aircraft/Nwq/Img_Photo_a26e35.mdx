## What This Image Shows

A **FlightAware** mobile record for **N808LP**, watermarked "10/04/2025 @tjphager."

It reads: N808LP, flight identifier **BBA48**, "LANDED OVER 9 MONTHS AGO," "Operating as **Bombardier 48**," **ICT WICHITA, KS** to **HGR HAGERSTOWN, MD**, **WED 04-DEC-2024 02:38PM CST** to **WED 04-DEC-2024 05:41PM EST**, "2h 3m total travel time." A route map traces the leg across the midwest.

This documents the HADES prototype's own movements, and they look nothing like the Utah profile. A single point-to-point leg from the Bombardier Defense facility in Wichita to Hagerstown, flown under a manufacturer callsign, is a delivery or modification positioning flight.

The contrast is the argument. The genuine prototype flies factory-to-facility legs at ordinary altitudes; the aircraft over the Utah Valley on September 10 flew low, slow orbits. Whether that difference favours the spy-plane theory or undercuts it depends on which aircraft you think was configured for what -- which is precisely the unresolved question at the centre of this thread.
