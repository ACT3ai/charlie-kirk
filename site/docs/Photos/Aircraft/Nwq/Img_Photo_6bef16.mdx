## What This Image Shows

A group photograph inside a large hangar: a crowd of people posed in front of a white Bombardier business jet, with rows of empty black chairs in the foreground and a large screen displaying an aerial view of an aircraft at the right.

The framing text reads: "**Tail Number N808LP.** A group shot in front of the **first [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) prototype aircraft** at the Bombardier Defense facility in **Wichita, Kansas**, today. Bombardier. **November 25, 2024**."

This is the public rollout of the first HADES airframe, and it is useful for two reasons. It fixes a date -- late November 2024 -- for when the first prototype was presented, which is nine and a half months before September 10, 2025. And it shows the aircraft in the same plain white livery as [N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview), undercutting the mental image of a grey, sensor-covered machine that circulated with the drone-launch claim.

The scale of the gathering also says something plain: this is an openly announced defence programme with a public rollout, photographed and captioned by the manufacturer, not a covert asset.
