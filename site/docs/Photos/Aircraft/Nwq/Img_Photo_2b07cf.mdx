## What This Image Shows

The continuation of the same AI answer, scrolled down.

Under "Aviation Expertise" it states that "Leidos' broader Civil Division integrates aviation systems, modernizes infrastructure, and supports air navigation for global providers. **LASAI handles specific flight operations, such as surveys, reconnaissance, and test flights**, often using aircraft like the Beechcraft BE350 or prototypes (e.g., the ARTEMIS plane for advanced aerial missions)." Under "Government Contracts": "LASAI supports Leidos' contracts with entities like the **U.S. Army Geospatial Center** and the **Nevada Test and Training Range (NTTR)**, involving airborne surveys and defense-related flights." It also mentions weather briefings and flight planning via the 1-800-WX-BRIEF service.

This is the substantive answer to what the operator does. Surveys, reconnaissance and test flights for the Army Geospatial Center is precisely the profile that would produce unusual altitudes and speeds over a specific area -- which is to say, it makes the September 10 flight profile less anomalous, not more.

The **ARTEMIS** reference is worth noting: that is the airborne reconnaissance prototype programme that preceded HADES, tying the operator directly to the surveillance-aircraft lineage.
