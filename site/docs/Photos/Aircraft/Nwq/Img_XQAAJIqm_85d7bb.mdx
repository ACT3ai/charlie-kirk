## What This Image Shows

A two-panel comparison graphic built to correct a specific error.

The top panel shows a dark grey military-style jet with overlaid text reading "**24001 is NOT real. This is a rendering of a FUTURE ME-11B aircraft. A rendering of a fully equipped [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) based on the Global 6500 airframe.**" The bottom panel shows a plain white business jet labelled "**Bombardier Global 6500 BD-700-1A10. N1098L. This is a REAL plane.**"

The point is precise and worth taking seriously. Much of the online discussion illustrated the spy-plane claim with an image of a grey, sensor-laden military aircraft -- but that image is an **artist's rendering of a planned configuration**, not a photograph of anything that flew over Utah. The aircraft actually tracked on September 10, 2025 is the unpainted white business jet ([N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview)) in the lower panel.

That distinction changes how the story reads. A menacing grey aircraft in a graphic implies capabilities the real airframe may not have, and the number **24001** in the corner of the rendering is the tail marking on the artwork rather than a registration.

This is the strongest single item in the counter-case. See the [cluster overview](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview), [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview), and the article captures in [Articles](/Photos/Aircraft/Articles/overview).
