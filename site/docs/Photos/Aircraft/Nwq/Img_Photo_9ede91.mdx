## What This Image Shows

A mobile aircraft-registration page for tail number **N808LP**, watermarked repeatedly with **@tjphager**.

The summary block reads: **2023 BD-700-1A10**, Fixed wing multi engine, 23 seats, 2 engines, owner listed as a Foreign Corporation, **Airworthiness Class Experimental/Normal**, **Serial Number 60116**, engines Rolls-Royce BR700-710D5-21. A small thumbnail caption reads: "A group shot in front of the **first HADES prototype aircraft** at the Bombardier Defense facility in **Wichita, Kansas**, today. Bombardier."

This is the comparison aircraft. If you want to know what an actual HADES prototype looks like on paper, this is it -- and the fields that matter are the **Experimental/Normal** airworthiness class and serial **60116**. A mission-modified prototype carries an experimental certificate; **N1098L**, serial **60098**, does not, which is the technical core of the argument that N1098L was not the configured HADES aircraft.

That is the counter-case, and it is a serious one. It does not explain the low, slow passes over the Utah Valley, but it does undercut the claim that the aircraft was purpose-built to launch drones.
