## What This Image Shows

A Flightradar24 mobile list headed AIRCRAFT **SU-BTT**, Year 2025, covering the spring.

Entries include **May 02** Medina (MED) to Cairo and Cairo to Medina; **Apr 26** Jeddah (JED) to Cairo and Cairo to Jeddah; **Apr 08** flights logged under callsign **EGY239** between Cairo and El Arish (AAC); **Apr 05** Cairo local; and further Cairo legs on **Mar 18** and **Feb 23**.

Two things are worth pulling out. First, the aircraft's ordinary work is regional government transport around Egypt, Saudi Arabia and the Sinai -- the trans-Atlantic runs are the exception, not the pattern. Second, the callsign shifts: some legs fly as SUBTT, others as **EGY239**, which is the kind of detail that makes flight-log compilation error-prone and helps explain the duplicate and misnotated entries flagged in the audit sheet elsewhere in this cluster.

El Arish, in North Sinai near the Gaza border, appears in the April entries. It is noted here as a routing fact rather than as a connection to anything in this case.
