## What This Image Shows

A Flightradar24 mobile record for **N888KG**. A photograph of the Challenger 300 in flight, credited **KnightHammer Aviation**, sits above the specification block: AIRCRAFT Bombardier Challenger 300, SERIAL NUMBER **20402**, AGE 12 years, AIRLINE Private owner, OPERATOR N/A, COUNTRY OF REGISTRATION United States, TYPE CODE CL30, MODE S **AC3C75**. At the bottom, where the flight list should be: "**No flights found.**"

The empty history is not an anomaly, and it is worth being precise about why. The same tail number carries the FAA's data-limiting flag, and the block notice preserved on [Photo 47](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_c79196) states outright that the aircraft "is not available for public tracking per request from the owner/operator." Mainstream trackers honour that request; **ADS-B Exchange**, which does not, shows the September 10 track in detail.

So this screenshot documents an information gap rather than an absence of flights. That distinction is the crux of the whole N888KG thread: researchers describe the aircraft as a ghost, while the underlying cause is a routine privacy election available to any US registrant.
