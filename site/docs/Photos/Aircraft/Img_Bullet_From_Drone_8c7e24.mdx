## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution frame taken from video, with the subject's face blurred, overlaid with orange arrows and three labels: **Inlet**, **Outlet**, and **Bullet shadow**. A player scrubber at the bottom reads 00:01 / 00:07, indicating this is a still pulled from a seven-second clip.

The annotations set out an argument about wound geometry: that a mark on one side is an entry, a mark on the other is an exit, and a shadow visible between them indicates the path. Because the file is stored under the spy-plane folder, the poster's implied inference is a downward trajectory from an airborne source rather than from the roof position in the official account.

This needs stating plainly. Reading wound geometry off a compressed, low-resolution video frame is not reliable, and the labels here are one person's interpretation of pixels, not a forensic finding. The [medical examiner's](/Medical/medical_examiner) record and the ballistics evidence in the [court proceedings](/court/overview) are the authoritative material on trajectory. Independent commentators have raised trajectory questions in public, and those questions remain open, but this image does not settle them.
