## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a research application headed **INVESTIGATION DOSSIER** with a **PEOPLE DATABASE** below it. Each row carries a name, a descriptive role, a status word such as Active or Missing, and a numeric score rendered as a fraction out of ten. Roles visible include Security Lead, Security Guard, Chief of Staff, Security Director, Organizer, Founder, Co-host, Photographer, Attendee, Board Member and Student Speaker.

This is an investigator's private tracking tool, and the fields should be read as such. The scores appear to record how strongly a researcher rated a lead, not any finding about a person; "Missing" appears to mean the person has not given a public account rather than anything about their whereabouts; and the role labels are organizational descriptions drawn from public reporting. No entry in a list like this establishes that anyone did anything wrong.

One row is worth flagging for accuracy: the accused in this case has been charged and the proceedings are ongoing, so any label in a research tool that reads as a finding of guilt is the tool's shorthand and not a legal fact. The site's maintained profiles, with status fields and attribution, are in [People](/People/overview) and the court record is at [Court and Legal Proceedings](/Photos/Court_Legal/overview).
