## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture. Search term **Elena Vasquez**, region **Israel**, window **Past 90 days**, All categories, Web Search. The Interest over time graph runs from July 23 through October 9 and shows a flat baseline broken by a single labelled peak at **Aug 23, 2025**.

The name is one of three in a crew list that circulated for **N1098L** on September 10, 2025 -- rendered in that list as Sgt. (Ret.) Elena Vasquez, Mission Engineer/Observer. The reason someone ran it through Trends filtered to Israel is the theory that a foreign service would look up names before or after an operation.

That inference does not hold up on this data, and the reasons are worth stating. Google Trends reports a normalized index, not a count, so a spike to 100 from a near-zero baseline can represent a handful of searches. Elena Vasquez is an extremely common name. And the crew list itself traces back to an AI chat exchange in which the assistant described the details as fabricated -- preserved on [Photo 26](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_95fe57).

Nothing here indicates that any person by this name exists in connection with the flight, was aboard, or did anything at all. See the [cluster overview](/Photos/Aircraft/N1098L_NCSS_CIA/overview) and [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview).
