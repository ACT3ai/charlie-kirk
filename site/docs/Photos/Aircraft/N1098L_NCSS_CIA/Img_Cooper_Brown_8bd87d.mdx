## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture. Search term **Cooper Brown**, region **Washington DC (Hagerstown)**, window **Past 12 months**, spanning **Oct 27, 2024** to **Sep 28, 2025**. The tooltip reads "**Jul 13 - 19, 2025. Cooper Brown. 46**," and unlike most charts in this sub-cluster the line fluctuates rather than sitting flat at zero.

The fluctuation is the useful detail. A term with continuous background interest means the name has ordinary public traffic -- people with that name doing unremarkable things -- which makes any single week's value meaningless as a signal about anything.

It also undercuts the method used across this whole sub-cluster. Charts that sit flat and spike once look dramatic but reflect near-zero volume; charts that fluctuate show real volume but no anomaly. Neither shape supports an inference about who searched a name or why.

The name comes from a disputed crew list for **N1098L**, and it also belongs to a person appearing elsewhere in the investigation's material in an event role.
