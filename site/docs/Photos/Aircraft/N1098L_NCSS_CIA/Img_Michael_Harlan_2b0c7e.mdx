## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture, near-identical to others in this sub-cluster. Search term **Michael Harlan**, region **Washington DC (Hagerstown)**, window **Past 12 months**, with the tooltip reading "**Aug 24 - 30, 2025. Michael Harlan. 100**" and two isolated spikes on a flat line.

The repetition is itself informative. Six captures of the same term, taken at different moments with slightly different tooltips visible, suggest a researcher returning to the query repeatedly rather than a set of independent findings. Screenshots of the same query are one observation, not six.

What the observation amounts to is modest: a low-volume search term registered a one-week bump in one American metropolitan market in late August 2025. That is compatible with almost any explanation, including entirely unrelated local news about somebody sharing the name.
