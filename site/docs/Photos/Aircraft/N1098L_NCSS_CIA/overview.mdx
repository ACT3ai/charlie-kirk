## About This Cluster

Nobody has produced a verified crew list for **N1098L** on **September 10, 2025**. Flight-tracking services publish route, altitude and timing but withhold personnel, and the operator, **LASAI Aviation II LLC**, holds Department of Defense work that keeps such details out of public reach. Into that vacuum came two competing name lists circulating online — one naming Michael R. Harlan, J. "Jax" Donovan and Elena Vasquez, the other naming Cooper Brown, Michael Reynolds and Sarah Kline — with neither list carrying documentation. This cluster is the record of one attempt to test those names indirectly.

Every image here is a **Google Trends** screenshot for one of the disputed names, filtered by region and time window. Several are run against **Israel** over the past twelve months, others against the **Washington DC (Hagerstown)** market over ninety days. The graphs mostly show a flat line at zero interrupted by a single sharp spike to an index value of 100 — for example "Michael Harlan" peaking the week of August 24-30, 2025 and again in early December 2024; "Sarah Klein" spiking on September 4, 2025; "Mark Harlan" on September 9, 2025; "cooper brown" the week of August 17-23, 2025; "Michael Reynolds" the week of September 14-20, 2025; and "Rusty Needs" on August 15, 2025. Researchers have read these isolated spikes as a sign that somebody in a particular region was looking up the names around relevant dates. That reading is contested and should be treated with care: Google Trends normalizes to a relative index, so a spike from near-zero volume can represent a very small absolute number of searches, common names attract unrelated traffic, and none of this establishes that any named person was aboard the aircraft or did anything at all.

The aircraft analysis these names attach to is on [N1098L Spy Plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview), with the operator and airframe documents in [Nwq](/Photos/Aircraft/Nwq/overview). For what the flight data itself shows, see [Sept 10 Flight Timeline](/Planes/Sept10-Flight-Timeline) and [ISR Operations](/Planes/ISR-Operations). Treat this sub-cluster as an open research thread rather than a finding — the honest summary is that the crew of N1098L remains unidentified, and a FOIA request to the FAA is the route by which that could change.
