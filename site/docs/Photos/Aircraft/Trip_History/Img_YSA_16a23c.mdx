## What This Image Shows

Audit rows numbered from 25 upward, covering **4/19/2024** through **9/13/2025** and then an earlier block from **11/25/2022**, with colour-coded status cells and written correction notes.

One note is worth quoting in full because it shows the method working: against a claimed route of "PROVO to PARIS to SALT LAKE CITY to PROVO," the auditor writes "**No the plane didn't fly from Provo to Paris, then back to Salt lake. Route was on 4/18 Cairo-Paris.. 4/19 paris to Goose Bay. Goose Bay to SLC.. SLC to Provo. Then sat there until 12 July.**" A second note against "CAIRO to PARIS to WILLISTON to PROVO" reads "Acc 4/22-23."

That first correction matters. The claimed route was geographically impossible as stated, and the actual routing -- Cairo, Paris, Goose Bay, Salt Lake City, Provo, then a long stay -- is a normal trans-Atlantic ferry. A sequence that looked like erratic shuttling turns out to be one ordinary crossing badly transcribed.

It also shows that some claims are corrected rather than deleted: the aircraft really did reach Provo, just not the way the sheet said.
