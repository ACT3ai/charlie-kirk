## What This Image Shows

A close crop of two audit rows, index numbers **43** and **44**, highlighted in red.

Row 43, dated **11/25/2022**, claims **SU-BTT** flew "PARIS to WILMINGTON to CAIRO." The note reads: "**In Cairo. Closest flight was 11/20/22 when the plane flew from...**" Row 44, dated **12/5/2022**, claims "PARIS to CHICAGO to CAIRO," with the note "**Still in Cairo, flew back into Cairo on 20 Nov, Flew back out to Par[is]...**"

Zoomed in like this, the correction method is fully visible. The auditor does not simply mark a claim wrong -- they give the nearest real flight and the date, so the reader can see how the error probably arose. In both cases the aircraft's last actual movement was a return to Cairo on 20 November 2022, and the claimed onward trips did not happen.

This level of specificity is what separates a genuine check from a rebuttal. It also explains why the audit's headline figure -- sixty percent of claims placing the aircraft on the wrong continent -- is credible rather than rhetorical.
