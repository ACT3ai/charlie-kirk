## What This Image Shows

A vertical capture of a four-section specification brochure for a **2023 Bombardier Global 6500**, overlaid with blue diagonal watermarks and a red banner reading "**Identical specs for BOTH N1098L + N2100L**."

The visible fields: ENGINES Manufacturer / Model **Rolls-Royce Pearl BR700-710D5-21**, enrolled in the Rolls-Royce **CorporateCare** Program; **Serial Number 60098**, **Registration N1098L**; right engine 985 hours, 759 cycles since new; left engine 985 hours, 759 cycles; APU **Allied Signal RE-220 (GX)** on the **Honeywell MSP Gold** Program, 335 hours and 614 cycles since new; source **aeroamericagroup.com**.

The red banner is the claim: **N1098L** and **N2100L** are matched airframes with identical specifications. That is a substantive point for the aircraft thread, because N2100L is the sister ship in the same operator's fleet, and a matched pair is how a contractor runs a two-aircraft mission programme.

The hours figures are also worth reading -- 985 hours since new on a 2023 delivery is low utilisation, consistent with an aircraft not in regular charter service.
