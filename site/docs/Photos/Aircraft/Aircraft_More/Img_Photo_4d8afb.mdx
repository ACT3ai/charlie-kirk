## What This Image Shows

A close photograph of a screen showing the Flightradar24 detail panel for **SU-BND**.

The fields read: **SUBND**, Private owner, type **GLF4**, **PVU PROVO**, MDT (UTC-06:00); AIRCRAFT TYPE (GLF4) **Gulfstream 4SP**; COUNTRY OF REG. **Egypt**; REGISTRATION **SU-BND**; SERIAL NUMBER (MSN) **1332**; age **27 years**; BAROMETRIC ALT. **0 ft**, VERTICAL SPEED 0 fpm, TRACK 180 degrees. A photograph of the twin-engine jet in flight sits above, and the panel offers Recent SU-BND flights, speed and altitude graphs, 3D view and route.

Two details are worth noting. The operator field reads "**Private owner**" rather than a government entity, in contrast to [**SU-BTT**](/Planes/SU-BTT), which trackers list as Egypt Government -- a discrepancy in how the two aircraft are classified that has not been explained. And the aircraft is 27 years old, an ageing Gulfstream IV rather than a modern jet.

The zero altitude and zero vertical speed place it on the ground at [**Provo**](/Locations/Provo_Airport) when the panel was captured, during the period researchers report it stationed there.
