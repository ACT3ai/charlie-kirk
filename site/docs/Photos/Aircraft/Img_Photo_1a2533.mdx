## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an X post sharing an article from **The War Zone** (TWZ). The post text reads: "Maybe this is a coincidence but just last night [@I_Am_JohnCullen](/Influencers/x/john-cullen) and I were discussing the possibility of air launched drones at the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) assassination and this Bombardier [HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES) ISR jet was the exact platform we suspect they came from."

The embedded article is headlined "Army Wants To Test Air-Launched Drones From A Global Express Business Jet," filed under AIR / AIRBORNE SENSORS, with the standfirst "The planned trials tally with previous comments about launching the drones from the new Global 6500-based HADES surveillance jet."

This matters because it is the published, third-party basis for the drone-launch claim -- not a researcher's assertion but a defense-trade report describing a program the Army itself was discussing. The link in this capture is the URL that later stopped resolving, which is the subject of the [Cover Up](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/overview) sub-cluster and its [Versions](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/Cover_Versions/overview) slides. Note carefully what the article does and does not say: it reports planned trials of a capability, not that any such launch occurred over Utah.
