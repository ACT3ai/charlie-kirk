## What This Image Shows

An **ADS-B Exchange** capture for **N59906**. The sidebar reads Hex **A7C14D**, Reg. N59906, United States, Type **PA31**, **1974 PIPER PA-31-300/310/325/350/425**, operator **MARC INC**, type description L2P. Telemetry shows groundspeed 11 kt, on ground, barometric altitude 4,450 ft, position **40.217, -111.721**, signal source TIS-B. The map traces flight paths over the Utah Valley near UVU, and the capture has been annotated with a timeline and the heading "Apparent Aerial Surveillance Contractor Flights over..."

The position given is the Provo airport area, so this frame catches the aircraft on the ground rather than mid-pattern. What the annotated track shows is the reported sequence: four passes over the campus between roughly 11:01 AM and 11:35 AM local time on **September 10, 2025**, at around 15,000 feet above ground level, ending about 48 minutes before the shooting.

**MARC Inc.** is a contractor supplying survey and mapping aircraft to government and commercial clients, and a PA-31 flying a north-south grid at that altitude is the standard profile for aerial photography or LiDAR work -- possibly for the nearby interstate corridor. The unanswered question is narrow and specific: who commissioned the flight, and does imagery from it exist. Neither MARC Inc. nor any agency has answered publicly.
