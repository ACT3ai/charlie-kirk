## What This Image Shows

A third **ADS-B Exchange** capture of **N888KG**, this one weighted toward the map. The topographic view traces the flight path over Utah and into northern Arizona; the sidebar repeats the identity block -- Hex AC3C75, LADD, CL30, 2013 Bombardier BD-100 Challenger 300, N888KG LLC -- with groundspeed **266 kt**, barometric altitude **8,425 ft** descending, vertical rate **-1,856 ft/min**, track 173.7 degrees, position **37.182, -111.503**.

The telemetry matches [Photo 39](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_a7f60f) exactly, so these are two crops of the same moment -- one framed on the data panel, one on the route. Preserving both is deliberate: the panel proves the numbers, the map proves where they were taken.

What the combined picture supports is narrow and worth stating precisely. A privately held Challenger 300, blocked from mainstream trackers, was descending toward the Page area of northern Arizona on the evening of **September 10, 2025**, some hours after departing the Provo area. Where it went next, and who was aboard, is not in any public record.
