## What This Image Shows

A capture from the State of Delaware's Division of Corporations portal, headed "Entity Details" with the standard disclaimer "THIS IS NOT A STATEMENT OF GOOD STANDING." The record reads: File Number **6901748**, Incorporation Date / Formation Date **7/7/2022**, Entity Name **N888KG, LLC**, Entity Kind Limited Liability Company, Entity Type General, Residency Domestic, State DELAWARE. Registered agent is **APEX RESIDENT AGENT SERVICES LLC**, 2701 Centerville Rd, **Wilmington**, New Castle County, DE 19808.

Paired with the Utah filing on [Photo 38](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_84067b), this shows the standard structure for private aircraft ownership: a Delaware limited liability company formed in July 2022, registered in Utah as a foreign LLC thirteen days later, holding a single asset. Nothing about that arrangement is unusual or improper -- it is how a large share of US-registered business jets are held.

Wilmington recurs across the aircraft thread, appearing both as this registered-agent city and as the reported destination for the Egyptian jet **SU-BTT** on [September 10](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). It is worth noting that Wilmington is also one of the most common corporate-registration addresses in the country, which makes the recurrence far weaker as a signal than it first appears.
