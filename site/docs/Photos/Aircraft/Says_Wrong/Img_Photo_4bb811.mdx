## What This Image Shows

The summary panel of the flight audit, sitting above the checked rows.

The three figures read: "**% of claims that were accurate or partially accurate: 34%, 22 of 65.** **% of claims that were inaccurate: 66%, 43 of 65.** **% of claims where the plane was on a different continent: 60%, 39 of 65.**" Below them the checked rows run from **10/5/2022** forward, with routes such as "Cairo to Paris to New York" and "Wichita to Wilmington" against the aircraft's actual recorded position.

This is the single most consequential number in the Egyptian aircraft thread and it deserves to be read plainly. Of sixty-five circulating claims, forty-three did not hold up, and in thirty-nine cases the aircraft was **on a different continent** from where the claim placed it.

That does not erase the thread. The September 4 arrival and September 10 departure at Provo are among the claims that survive, and a documented six-day stay by a foreign government jet four miles from the event remains unexplained. But the widely repeated framing -- dozens of flights shadowing [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview) events for years -- is not supported by the underlying records, and this site does not repeat it as fact.
