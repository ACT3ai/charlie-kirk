## About This Cluster

Whether drones were in the air over the UVU courtyard on **September 10, 2025** is one of the sharpest factual disputes in the case. TPUSA and UVU initially said no drones were flying; researchers responded with video frames showing small objects overhead, and **Frank Turek** has said publicly that TPUSA had a drone up and that he and Charlie Kirk reviewed its footage on the way to the event. This cluster is the small set of drone-related stills filed under the spy-plane thread, which is where the two questions meet: whether the aircraft overhead was a hobby drone, a production drone, or something launched from elsewhere.

Two of the images are frames from crowd video with a yellow circle and a magnified inset drawn around a small dark object high in the sky above the plaza — one carries a burned-in timecode reading 12:24:00:05 and a "CROWDSOURCE THE TRUTH" watermark, placing it about a minute after the 12:23 PM shot. The object is small, blurred, and unresolved; researchers have offered it as evidence of an aircraft overhead, while critics have argued it could be a bird, a lens artifact, or a distant balloon. The third image is unrelated to UVU: a text screenshot recounting the reported 2010 State Department episode in which then-Secretary **Hillary Clinton** allegedly asked "Can't we just drone this guy?" about Julian Assange, filed here as background on how drone strikes enter political discussion rather than as evidence about this case.
