## About This Cluster

All three images in this cluster are diagrams drawn by one person to illustrate one hypothesis. On **July 15, 2026**, the X account **[@wtshesaid72](/Influencers/x/wtshesaid72)** — self-described as belonging to an "artist, educator, inventor, and physics researcher" — published what it calls the **B-Field Resonant Cascade (BFRC)** hypothesis: the claim that [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) died on **September 10, 2025** not from a rifle round and not from an explosive in his microphone, but from a pulsed electromagnetic discharge routed through the corded mic he was holding. The post reached roughly 11,300 impressions and carried an animated video plus these three diagrams. This cluster exists so that readers can inspect the actual drawings the argument is built on instead of taking the summary on faith.

The hypothesis as posted runs like this: the venue was a prepared "resonant site" with a bowl-shaped courtyard, a raised platform with a deliberate air gap, and an underground high-voltage cavity tapped from existing infrastructure; when Kirk touched the mic with both hands he completed a conductive path through body tissue, a metal necklace, and the mic cord; a generator fired a rapidly changing magnetic field, and Faraday induction with Lorentz forces and magnetic pinch produced the chest and neck compression, the necklace movement, the acoustic pop, and the tissue ejection that observers read as a close-range gunshot. The named physics is real physics used in roughly its conventional sense, but naming a mechanism correctly is not the same as showing it operated. There is no field-strength figure, no current estimate, no generator specification, no calculation, no photograph of any such equipment at the venue, no procurement record, no witness, and no engineer on record endorsing it. Its load-bearing empirical claim — that the internal injuries were inconsistent with ballistic trauma — cannot be checked while the autopsy remains sealed. The four claimed site preparations are reinterpretations of ordinary venue features: outdoor venues have service vaults, stages are raised and hollow, and campus courtyards are enclosed. The site rates this **SPECULATIVE**.
