## What This Image Shows

This is the site-geometry cross-section, the first of three diagrams the author published with the hypothesis. It renders the venue in section: a bowl-shaped courtyard, a raised platform with what the author calls a deliberate air gap beneath it, and an underground high-voltage cavity said to be tapped from existing infrastructure, with a pulsed electromagnetic field generator connected and ready.

The drawing is where the argument does most of its work, because it converts ordinary venue features into deliberate preparations. Outdoor venues have service vaults and electrical runs. Stages are raised and hollow. Campus courtyards are enclosed by the buildings around them. Nothing in the diagram identifies an anomalous feature discovered at the site; it reinterprets normal ones.
