## About This Cluster

These images capture how the shooting was reported and framed, rather than the event itself. They are screenshots of published coverage. One is a full desktop capture of a **Times of Israel** article dated September 11, 2025, the day after the shooting, reporting that US authorities had "good video footage" of a suspect. The other is a social media post on X sharing a snippet of an opinion piece, "The Assassination of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)," attributed to Ron Unz at The Unz Review and dated September 15, 2025. Together they sample two ends of the coverage spectrum: a wire-style news report and a long-form dissenting commentary.

Media framing matters to this investigation because the earliest headlines set the terms the public accepted, sometimes before basic facts were confirmed. Researchers here track how outlets described the suspect, the timeline, and the official account, and where later reporting diverged from the initial story. Nothing on these screenshots is treated as settled fact; each is a record of what a given outlet or poster claimed, reported with attribution. Open any image to read the full write-up, then use the previous and next links to move through the cluster. For the written analysis of coverage patterns, see the Media section.
