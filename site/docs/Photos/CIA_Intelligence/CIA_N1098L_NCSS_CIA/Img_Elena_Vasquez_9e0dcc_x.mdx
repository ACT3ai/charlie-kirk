## What This Image Shows

This **Google Trends** screenshot charts the search term **Elena Vasquez** filtered to the region of **Israel** over a past-12-months timeline extending into late 2025. It shows a single extremely sharp spike reaching a value of 100 in **early November 2024**, with the rest of the line completely flat at zero.

The name is one of seven the research deck says belonged to profiles connected with UVU's Center for National Security Studies, and which it claims were removed from public web pages in late September 2025, after the **September 10, 2025** assassination of **Charlie Kirk** at Utah Valley University. The deck describes this name as a national security policy figure and, on other slides, as a claimed mission engineer or avionics specialist aboard the September 10 flight.

Google Trends normalizes every chart to a 0-100 scale, so a spike built from a handful of searches is drawn exactly the same as one built from thousands. The tool reports no searcher identity, no IP address, and no reason for a search. Common names collect unrelated traffic from unrelated people. A country or metro filter tells you where queries originated, not who made them or what they were looking for.

The timing here cuts against the argument rather than for it. A November 2024 peak sits ten months before the assassination, and the chart shows no elevated interest at all in the days surrounding September 10, 2025.

No person named in this cluster has been charged with or accused of any offense, and no agency has confirmed a link between any of these names and any intelligence service. The chart is one researcher's exhibit, not a finding.
