## What This Image Shows

This **Google Trends** screenshot charts the search term **Michael Harlan** filtered to the **Washington DC (Hagerstown MD)** market over a 12-month period spanning late 2024 to late 2025. The baseline is flat at zero and is broken by two extremely sharp, isolated spikes that reach the maximum value of 100.

The deck's annotated version labels the two peaks at **December 1-7, 2024** and **August 24-30, 2025**. This is the name the deck attaches to its unsourced crew claim, listing "Michael Harlan - Pilot in Command" and, on one slide, adding "Raytheon." No manifest, employer statement, or regulatory filing supports that assignment, and the slide itself marks it with a question mark.

Google Trends normalizes every chart to a 0-100 scale, so a spike built from a handful of searches is drawn exactly the same as one built from thousands. The tool reports no searcher identity, no IP address, and no reason for a search. Common names collect unrelated traffic from unrelated people. A country or metro filter tells you where queries originated, not who made them or what they were looking for.

No person named in this cluster has been charged with or accused of any offense, and no agency has confirmed a link between any of these names and any intelligence service. The chart is one researcher's exhibit, not a finding.
