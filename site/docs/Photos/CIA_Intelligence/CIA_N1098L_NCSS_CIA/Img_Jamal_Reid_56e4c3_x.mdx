## What This Image Shows

This **Google Trends** screenshot charts the search term **Jamal Reid** filtered to **Israel** over a 12-month timeline. The line shows one extremely sharp spike that peaks at the maximum value of 100 and drops immediately back to zero, with no sustained interest before or after.

The name is one of seven the research deck says belonged to profiles connected with UVU's Center for National Security Studies, and which it claims were removed from public web pages in late September 2025, after the **[September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025)** assassination of **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). This is the foreign-filtered companion to the 90-day domestic chart elsewhere in this sub-cluster.

Google Trends normalizes every chart to a 0-100 scale, so a spike built from a handful of searches is drawn exactly the same as one built from thousands. The tool reports no searcher identity, no IP address, and no reason for a search. Common names collect unrelated traffic from unrelated people. A country or metro filter tells you where queries originated, not who made them or what they were looking for.

A 12-month window compresses the horizontal axis heavily, so a spike that looks precise here may cover several days. Anyone checking this claim should pull the underlying date range directly rather than reading it off the graph.

No person named in this cluster has been charged with or accused of any offense, and no agency has confirmed a link between any of these names and any intelligence service. The chart is one researcher's exhibit, not a finding.
