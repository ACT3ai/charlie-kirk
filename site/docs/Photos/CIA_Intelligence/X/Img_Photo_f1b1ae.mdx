## What This Image Shows

This is a labeled graphic whose entire content is its captions. They read: "32 CAVITY PHASED ARRAY RADAR ELECTRONIC ASSAULT WEAPON," "PHASED ARRAY VEHICLE RADAR SCANNER," "SENSYS - 2.4 GHz WIFI MAC VEHICLE PASSENGER TRACKER," and "STEROSCOPIC NIR IONIZING RADIATION BRAIN SCANNER."

The technique is labeling rather than demonstrating. Each caption takes a real category of equipment — phased-array radar, Wi-Fi MAC address detection, near-infrared imaging — and renames it as a weapon. Phased-array radar is standard detection technology. MAC address collection over Wi-Fi is a well-documented and widely deployed tracking practice, and is genuinely a privacy concern, but it is not an assault system. Near-infrared is non-ionizing, so the phrase "NIR ionizing radiation" is a contradiction in its own caption.
