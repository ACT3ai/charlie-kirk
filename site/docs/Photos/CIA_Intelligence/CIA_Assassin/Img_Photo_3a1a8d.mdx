## What This Image Shows

This is a scan of a declassified CIA document, degraded and partly illegible, marked as an attachment and stamped **ARTICHOKE**. It dates to **January 22, 1954** and is a feasibility evaluation, not an operation.

The legible text states the problem: whether a subject could be "induced to perform the act of attempted assassination" "involuntarily under the influence of ARTICHOKE." It notes the subject had worked for the government but "has since terminated," that he "would offer no further cooperation," and that "access to the SUBJECT would be extremely limited, probably limited to a single social meeting." It then proposes the method: "Because the SUBJECT is a heavy drinker, it was proposed that the individual could be drugged through the surreptitious medium of an alcoholic cocktail at a social party," with ARTICHOKE techniques applied and the subject "induced to perform the act of attempted assassination at some later date," after which he would "be taken into custody."

This document is genuine, declassified, and available from the CIA's own reading room, which is exactly why it carries weight in this investigation: it establishes that the agency formally studied involuntary-assassin scenarios. It establishes nothing about 1954 operations, and nothing whatever about 2025.
