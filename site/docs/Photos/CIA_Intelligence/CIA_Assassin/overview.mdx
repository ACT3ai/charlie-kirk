## About This Cluster

This small cluster covers the "CIA assassin" thread — the argument, made by independent researchers, that the CIA's own declassified paperwork shows the agency once studied whether a person could be made to attempt an assassination involuntarily. The anchor document is a **January 22, 1954** Project ARTICHOKE memo, republished in the CIA reading room, that evaluates drugging a subject through "a single uncontrolled social encounter" and then using hypnosis and drugs to induce an assassination attempt against a foreign politician "or, if necessary, an American official." Researchers raise it in the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) case to argue that a programmed or handled shooter is documented capability, not invented fiction.

The two images here circulate as photo evidence for that argument. They matter to the case only as historical context: nothing in them names **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)**, [UVU](/UVU/overview), or [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), and no evidence has established that any such program touched this case. The investigation logs them because the patsy framework — the claim that Robinson was radicalized, steered, or set up — leans on this documented history, and readers should see the primary-source material the claim rests on rather than a paraphrase.

Read both images here, then continue to the [Artichoke Project](/Photos/CIA_Intelligence/Artichoke_Project/overview) and [Project T6224](/Photos/CIA_Intelligence/Project_T6224/overview) sub-clusters for the related document sets. The site's analysis of this thread lives at [Project Artichoke Memo](/Gov_Mind_Control/project-artichoke-memo) and [MKUltra Program](/Gov_Mind_Control/mkultra-program) under [Gov Mind Control](/Gov_Mind_Control/overview).
