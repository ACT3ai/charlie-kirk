## What This Image Shows

The slide's left column is the deck's CIA thread. It reads "CIA is suspected linked to NSCC (or UVU Center for National Security Studies)" and states that seven profiles connected to that campus body "appear to have profiles removed" on **September 28**, roughly two weeks after the assassination. The seven names listed are [**Mark Harlan**](/People/mark-harlan), [**Dr. Elena Vasquez**](/People/elena-vasquez), **Jamal Reid**, [**Rusty Needs**](/People/rusty-needs), [**Sarah Klein**](/People/sarah-klein), **T Ramirez**, and **L Patel**.

This slide carries all three annotated threads at once. Thread 2 lists the names the author says were searched from Israel and Washington, D.C. before the assassination. Thread 4 adds a crew claim: "One internet researcher said these were on the Sept 10th N1098L Flight," beginning with "Michael Harlan - Pilot in Command. Raytheon." That crew assignment is sourced on the slide itself to an unnamed internet researcher, with a question mark beside the letter code.

The deck states its own limit in writing: "This doesn't mean these people did the assassination." That caveat should travel with every reproduction of this slide. A removed or updated web profile proves nothing, universities revise staff pages constantly, and none of these individuals has been charged with or accused of any offense by any authority. The crew claim in particular has no manifest, no company statement, and no regulatory filing behind it on this slide. Flight crew identities are not published in public flight-tracking data.
