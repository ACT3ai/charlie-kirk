## What This Image Shows

A plain five-line note, white text on black, functioning as a key. It reads: "Dan flood : maroon shirt / Rick cutler : brown shirt / Frank turek white shirt / Brian harpole: blue shit / Justin : not sure what color shirt." The fourth line contains an obvious typing error for "shirt," and the last line records the writer's uncertainty rather than papering over it.

Unglamorous as it is, this is one of the more practically useful items in the cluster. Video from **September 10, 2025** shows people around the tent whose faces are often turned away or motion-blurred; clothing colour is frequently the only stable identifier across frames. A key of this kind is what lets a reviewer follow the same individual from one camera angle to another.

It is a citizen's working key, not an official roster, and it should be verified against clear photographs before being relied on. All the people named are living private individuals; recording what colour shirt someone wore carries no implication of any kind about their conduct.
