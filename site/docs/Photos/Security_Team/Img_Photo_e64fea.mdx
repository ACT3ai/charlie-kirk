## What This Image Shows

A second screenshot from the same notes document as the position list earlier in this cluster, white text on black, written informally. This entry is headed as the writer's "2nd" point and sets out a speculative theory that a small concealed weapon was used from close range by someone standing near the stage.

This site does not reproduce that accusation. The note names a living private citizen who has not been charged with, accused by any authority of, or found by any court to have committed any offence, and the theory rests on the writer's own reading of a few video frames plus his interpretation of what a person's hands were doing. The writer himself sets out the main objection in the text — that others say the person was around twenty feet away and that such a weapon is a short-range device — and then simply disagrees, without evidence, that the distance was that great. He also refers to remarks by **Candace Owens** about the wound as partial support.

What the note is genuinely useful for is as a specimen of how this kind of theory is constructed: a gesture is noticed, a mechanism is proposed to fit it, and objections are answered by assertion rather than by measurement. Nothing in it has been corroborated, and no part of it should be read as a finding.

For the close-range theories that have actually been examined with sourcing see [Close Range Theories](/Killer/Close_Range_Theories) and [Mic](/Mic/overview); for the positions of people around the stage see [Researcher Note: Who Stood Where at the Tent](/Photos/Security_Team/Img_Photo_0f5582).
