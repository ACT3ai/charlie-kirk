## What This Image Shows

A mobile browser screenshot of a company website. The address bar reads "ctgdfw.com." A large circular black-and-white logo sits at the top, and under the heading "About Us" are five small circular portraits of men in tactical or outdoor gear arranged in two rows.

The visible body text reads: "Consolidated Training Group (CTG) derives its curriculum from the knowledge of our members. This knowledge is based upon a culmination of over one hundred and fifty years of real life..." — the sentence is cut off by the screen edge. The domain suffix dfw indicates the Dallas-Fort Worth area of Texas, which is the same region in which **Rockhouse Integrity Group LLC** is registered.

The page was saved because the private-security world around this case is small and regional, and instructor rosters are one of the few public records of who has trained or worked with whom. That is a mapping exercise, not an accusation: appearing on a training company's staff page is entirely ordinary, no individual is named on the capture, and nothing here connects this company or any person on its page to the events of **September 10, 2025**.
