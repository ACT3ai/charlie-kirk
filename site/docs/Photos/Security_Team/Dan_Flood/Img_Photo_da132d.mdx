## What This Image Shows

A portrait phone screenshot of a Facebook comment thread in a white overlay panel, position marker "8 of 30."

The exchange is short and domestic. An account named Lynne Flood Fisher asks, "Interesting, explain please," and the account named **Bob Flood** replies: "US Army Military Intelligence Branch (mine) (Dagger, Compass, Rose of Secrecy, rays of the Sun)." A further comment from **Guy Hood** reads "MI all the way." Timestamps show the exchange as about a year old at capture.

The parenthetical "(mine)" is why a researcher kept this frame: it is a first-person statement by the account holder that he served in Army Military Intelligence, offered casually to a relative rather than for public consumption. That is the strongest form this kind of open-source claim takes, and it is still only a personal assertion on social media.

What it is not is a link to this case. Nothing in the thread mentions [**TPUSA**](/TPUSA/overview), [**Utah Valley University**](/UVU/overview), or any event of [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), and the people in it are private individuals against whom no allegation is made.
