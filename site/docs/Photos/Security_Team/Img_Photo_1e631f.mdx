## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post on X by Stew Peters ([@realstewpeters](/Influencers/podcasts-stew-peters)), with a four-panel image grid attached showing chat-interface text, professional headshots, and photographs of [**Benjamin Netanyahu**](/People/benjamin-netanyahu) on an airport tarmac.

The post text states that "Grok confirms that Dan Flood, head of security for Charlie Kirk, has ties to Mossad and personally did security for Netanyahu," and invites readers to compare two photographs and judge whether they show the same man. The attached chat panel shows a conversational AI reply agreeing that they are, and it continues into personal and ethnic invective about the man in the photographs; that language is derogatory and is not reproduced here.

Two things need saying plainly. Output from a chatbot is not confirmation of anything — a language model asked to compare two photographs will produce a confident-sounding answer whether or not it has any basis, and it is not a forensic tool. And the surrounding invective marks the post as advocacy rather than investigation. **Dan Flood** is a living private citizen; this site does not adopt the identification, does not assert any link between him and any foreign service, and makes no allegation of wrongdoing against him.

The sourced material on Flood is at [Dan Flood](/Security_Team/dan-flood); the separate photo-comparison claim about a different individual is filed with attribution at [lsraeI connection](/Photos/Security_Team/lsraeI_connection/overview).
