## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post on X from the account Crowdsource The Truth, @JG_CSTT, with an annotated photograph of the event attached — the blue canopy valance reading PROVE ME WRONG is visible above the crowd. A bright yellow platform notice sits at the bottom stating that a warning was placed on the post because it might contain sensitive content, with an appeal link.

The post reads: at 500 feet per second an object moves approximately 2.08 feet in one two-hundred-and-fortieth of a second — could it possibly be a coincidence that three equidistant insects flew in a straight line toward the base of Charlie's skull just before an acute disturbance pinched and pulled his shirt collar forward.

It is framed as a question, and the wording carries its own answer: the poster is proposing that three evenly spaced specks in consecutive frames are one object moving too fast for the frame rate rather than three separate things. Frame-rate artefacts of exactly that kind are also produced by dust, insects, and compression, and the still is far too compressed to distinguish them. The collar disturbance the post refers to is itself a documented and contested observation, treated at [Mic Explosion Video Analysis](/Mic/mic-explosion-video-analysis), [Exploding Pendant and Shaped Charge](/Mic/exploding-pendant-shaped-charge), and [Alternative Kill Mechanisms](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Alternative_Kill_Mechanisms).
