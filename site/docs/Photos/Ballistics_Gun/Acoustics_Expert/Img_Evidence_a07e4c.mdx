## What This Image Shows

A livestream frame from the same **Project Constitution** discussion, with **Gideon** and **Tai (PenPub)** in the sidebar. The main window holds an aerial satellite view of the plaza, with a location marker reading **Hall of Flags** and a faint building label reading Level 1. Digital measurement lines and red handwritten annotations are drawn across it, including an angle written as 8.4 degrees.

That figure is the substance. Angles in this range are what the whole trajectory dispute is fought over: a shot from the rooftop in the official account arrives at a shallow downward angle over the distance involved, and the presenters are measuring the geometry on the map to test what that angle implies for where the sound and the impact originated.

An angle measured on an aerial map is a horizontal bearing, and converting it into a firing solution requires the elevations as well — which is what the CAD models elsewhere in this sub-cluster are for.
