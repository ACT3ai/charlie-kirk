## What This Image Shows

A livestream frame showing the **SOLIDWORKS** workspace with a 3D model of blocky architectural forms and a set of stairs. The assembly tree lists a Stairs component and a series of extruded solids, and the status bar reads Fully Defined, Editing Assembly, in millimetre-gram-second units. The sidebar again shows **Project Constitution** with **Gideon** and **Tai (PenPub)**.

Fully Defined is a meaningful label in this software: it means every dimension and constraint in the sketch has been fixed, so the geometry is not free to move. In other words the modelled stairs and structures have committed dimensions, which is what allows the sightline work in the other frames to produce numbers.

The stairs matter independently. The stairwell frames released by the bureau are among the most contested images in the case, and the physical dimensions of that structure bear on arguments about the height, build, and movement of the figure in them.
