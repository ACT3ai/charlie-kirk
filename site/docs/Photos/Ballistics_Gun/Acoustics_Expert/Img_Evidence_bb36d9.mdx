## What This Image Shows

A screen capture from a live-streamed technical discussion. The left sidebar carries the **Project Constitution** logo and the label Charlie Kirk Investigative Journalist, above two webcam feeds identified as **Gideon** and **Tai (PenPub)**, described on screen as the author of a work titled planned. The main window shows an image file named Camera Pointing 1.PNG open in a viewer at high zoom: a 3D wireframe of a structure with lines projecting outward from it.

The file name states the purpose. Before any acoustic or trajectory conclusion can be drawn from the surviving videos, you have to know where each camera was and which way it was pointing, because a recording only constrains the scene along its own sight line. This wireframe is that groundwork.

The stream this comes from is the source referenced in the investigation notes as an acoustics engineer walking through a three-dimensional model of the venue. The conclusion the presenters reach — that the sound direction points to the south, above the balcony level rather than to the rooftop in the official account — is theirs, and it is disputed. The competing readings are at [Acoustic Two-Event Split](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Acoustic_Two_Event_Split), [Shooting Locations](/Suspects/Shooting_Locations), and [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center).
