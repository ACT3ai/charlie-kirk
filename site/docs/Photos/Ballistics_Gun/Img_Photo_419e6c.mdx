## What This Image Shows

A top-down night photograph of a bolt-action rifle with a black synthetic stock, a silver-grey barrel and receiver, and a mounted telescopic scope, lying inside a long brown cardboard box on patchy grass. The scene is lit by a harsh circular flashlight beam against the surrounding darkness. No text or branding is legible anywhere in the frame.

The circumstances the image implies — a rifle in an open box, outdoors, on grass, photographed at night under a torch — are the circumstances of a recovery or a documentation stop rather than a laboratory. Whether this specific photograph shows the weapon in this case has not been established on the public record, and it carries no exhibit label, timestamp, or case marking.

That caution is not academic here. Researchers have traced at least one widely shared photograph of a scoped rifle on cardboard to stock photography rather than to the case, and images have circulated as the recovered weapon that are not. The documented account of the rifle and its recovery is at [Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle), [Gun Discovery Sequence](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Gun_Discovery_Sequence), and [Rifle Found After Feds](/CoverUp/Rifle_Found_After_Feds).
