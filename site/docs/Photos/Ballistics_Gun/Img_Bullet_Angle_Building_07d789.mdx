## What This Image Shows

A cropped daylight exterior photograph of the campus buildings with a dense crowd gathered at the lower right. Three bright red vector arrows have been drawn onto the image, each originating at a different level of the building on the left and angling down toward the crowd.

The three arrows are three hypotheses, not three shots. Each one starts from a different height — a rooftop, an upper floor, a lower vantage — and the graphic exists to show how differently the descending angle behaves depending on which one you pick. A shot from the roof arrives shallow at that distance; a shot from a lower opening arrives closer to level.

That difference is the entire argument. The state's account places the shot on the rooftop of the [**Losee Center**](/UVU/Losee_Center), and the objection researchers raise is that the wound path described in medical summaries fits a much flatter trajectory than a rooftop at that range produces. Other analysts have proposed positions inside buildings or at ground level instead. The competing positions are set out at [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center), [Shooting Locations](/Suspects/Shooting_Locations), and [Alternative Calibers and Weapons](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons).
