## What This Image Shows

A reconstruction graphic from the same independent analyst as the other numbered impact-location sheets in this cluster. Three-dimensional skull and neck models sit alongside a compass wheel marked at thirty-degree intervals from 0 through 330, with red lines and glowing red highlights tracing a path. The header reads IMPACT LOCATION 120 degrees with the subheading FACIAL RADIUS, and a photographic inset at the upper right is captioned EXIT WOUND EJECTION. The watermark reads @MrDiscordeous, Justin Ruh, 2025.

The method is to treat the head as a sphere and express the impact point as a bearing on it, then convert that bearing into a direction of fire once the head's orientation at that instant is known. The 120-degree figure on this sheet and the 157-degree figure on the companion sheets are different measurements of the same reconstruction taken on different radii — facial and top — not competing conclusions.

The inset is where the argument gets contestable: identifying ejection direction from compressed public video is an interpretation, and the analyst labels it as such. Read this sheet alongside the [157-degree top radius sheet](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Photo_dc2701) and the [aerial impact map](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Photo_1fe5c0_x). The mainstream and alternative readings of the wound are at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle) and [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch).
