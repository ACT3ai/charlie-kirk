## What This Image Shows

A close-up photograph of a matte black, pocket-sized folding device resting on weathered wooden planks. Closed, it is a flat rectangle of interlocking panels with flush-mounted Torx screws, a hex bolt, and a sliding latch. No branding or text is visible on the exposed surfaces. The form matches a folding single-shot pistol of the LifeCard type.

The reason a photograph like this sits in a ballistics cluster is the shape. Folded, the object reads as a card case, a multi-tool, or a phone, and the point being made by whoever collected it is that a firearm need not look like one — which is relevant to any argument that the fatal injury came from close range rather than from a rooftop at distance.

That argument is a hypothesis, not a finding, and nothing on the public record places any such device at the event. It is recorded here as part of the alternative-mechanism research thread and should be read that way. The companion photograph showing a similar device unfolded is [in this cluster](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Gun_Cell_8f76da).
