## What This Image Shows

A muzzle-energy comparison chart, pinned on IPFS and credited to [@HolonCitizen](/Influencers/x/holoncitizen). It sets the **.30-06** against the **9mm**: a 180-grain .30-06 at 2,700 feet per second yielding roughly 2,914 foot-pounds, about 3,950 joules, against a 115-grain 9mm at 1,180 feet per second yielding roughly 356 foot-pounds, about 482 joules.

That is roughly an eightfold gap, and it is the quantitative core of the whole energy argument. The claim made from it on [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch) is that a round carrying that much energy does not stop until it has dissipated through tissue and bone, and that a cervical-spine strike would be expected to produce catastrophic channel damage and pronounced exit disruption rather than a restrained neck injury.

The underlying figures are standard published ballistics and are not in dispute. What is disputed is the inference — whether the observed injury is in fact inconsistent with them, which depends on bullet construction, what the round struck, and an autopsy record that has not been released. See [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch), [ATF Fragment Inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive), and [Autopsy Report Not Public](/Medical/autopsy_report_not_public).
