## What This Image Shows

A size comparison of three cartridges — a 9mm handgun round, a 5.56 NATO rifle round, and a **.30-06** — shown side by side so the difference in scale is immediate. The graphic is pinned on IPFS and is credited on the investigation page to @HolonCitizen, posted April 17, 2026.

It is used on [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch) to establish the premise of that page before the numbers arrive: the round in the official account is not a handgun round and is larger than the standard-issue rifle round used by most modern military units.

The argument built on that premise is that a projectile of this size and energy behaves very differently on impact from what the described injury suggests. Commentators including Dr. **Chris Martenson** have argued a genuine .30-06 strike at that range would have produced far more violent movement and far more catastrophic tissue damage than the footage shows. Those are commentator inferences rather than autopsy findings, and the counter-arguments are recorded alongside them at [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch), [Alternative Calibers and Weapons](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons), and [Alternative Kill Mechanisms](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Alternative_Kill_Mechanisms).
