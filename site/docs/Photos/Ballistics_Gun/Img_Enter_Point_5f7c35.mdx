## What This Image Shows

A heavily compressed screenshot from a vertical social-video app, showing a close-up of a neck, ear, and shoulder area. A hand-drawn red circle marks a small dark pixelated spot on the skin, and bold text at the lower right reads FROM THIS, with the word THIS boxed in red.

The caption tells you this frame is one half of a before-and-after comparison — the video it came from cut between this mark and something else. What the circled pixels are cannot be determined from the image: at this compression a dark cluster of a few pixels is produced by a shadow, a mole, a fold of skin, a microphone clip, or codec noise as readily as by an injury.

It is filed with the entry-point material because that is what the poster was arguing about, and the entry point is the measurement the whole ballistics dispute turns on. The substantive treatment is at [Ballistics and Wound Analysis](/Medical/ballistics_and_wound_analysis) and [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch).
