## What This Image Shows

A four-panel composite laid out in a two-by-two grid outlined in bright green, dated to a September 2025 screenshot. One panel shows a public speaker holding a black wireless handheld microphone, another is a close-up zoom of the neck and jaw, and a fourth is a medical illustration of the trachea, thyroid gland, and the major blood vessels of the neck. Hand-drawn red annotations and a green dashed line connect the panels. A watermark at the bottom of one panel reads Samson absolutely alongside a YouTube play icon.

The composite is arguing that something visible on the neck in the footage corresponds to a specific anatomical structure in the illustration. That is the whole claim, and it is the claim of the person who made the graphic. Video compression, motion blur, shadow, and jaw position all produce apparent features on skin at this magnification, and no medical authority has confirmed what the zoomed crop shows.

It is filed here because the entry-point question is the hinge of the ballistics dispute: where the wound actually is determines which firing positions are geometrically possible. The competing readings are collected at [Ballistics and Wound Analysis](/Medical/ballistics_and_wound_analysis), [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch), and [Alternative Kill Mechanisms](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Alternative_Kill_Mechanisms).
