## What This Image Shows

A forensic investigator in a black polo reading **DPS FORENSICS** bends over in a dry grassy field beside a large fallen log, holding a clear zip-top collection bag containing a reddish-orange object. Beside her is a yellow tent marker printed with the number **17**.

Seventeen is the highest marker number visible across this sub-cluster, which puts a floor under the scale of the search: at least seventeen separately catalogued points along the corridor between the campus and the recovery area. That is a substantial collection haul, and it belongs to a Utah [**Department of Public Safety**](/Law_Enforcement/utah-dps-beau-mason) examiner rather than to the federal team.

None of the seventeen has been publicly itemised. The defense has raised the handling of physical exhibits repeatedly in this case, from the condition of the recovered bullet fragment to the volume and timing of discovery material, and an unpublished inventory of a seventeen-point scene sits squarely in that dispute.
