## What This Image Shows

An overhead satellite view of the plaza between the campus buildings, overlaid with coloured arcs, a thick green direction arrow, marker pins, and thin sight lines carrying distance labels.

The readable annotations are 75 yards from stage right, 78 yards, a purple pin labelled Bartholomew, a note reading stage right camera has Frank, and a pin for the Hall of Flags. In other words the map fixes where individual recordings were made and how far each was from the stage — the necessary groundwork for any acoustic timing argument, because a sound arriving at two microphones a known distance apart carries information about where it started.

This is the measurement layer beneath the acoustic claims elsewhere in the cluster. The Bartholomew recording named on this map is the same audio file analysed in the waveform screenshots here, which is why the pin matters more than it looks.
