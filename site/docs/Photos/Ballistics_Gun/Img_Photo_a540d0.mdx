## What This Image Shows

The full-screen version of a post on X from an account posting as Freemason, [@STARFORCEHH](/Influencers/x/starforcehh), with the attached medical illustration of the skull and cervical spine marked in red and green beneath the text. The companion composite showing the same post with its replies is [elsewhere in this cluster](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Photo_23d662).

The post reads that new medical data states the bullet entered Charlie's neck at C2, obliterated C3 through C7, and lodged in T1; that he was leaning slightly forward when struck, slightly to the left of his Adam's apple; and that with a gradual ten-degree downward angle it should have entered around C7 or T1. It argues that given the height of the roof and the range [Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) was at, entry at C2 is mathematically impossible, that C2 is nowhere near the wound in the neck, and closes with the sarcastic alternative that the bullet hit T1, bounced up to C2, and came back down to T1 — before concluding that the official story has become nothing more than a tall tale.

The argument is geometric, and it can only be tested against the actual autopsy measurements, which are not public. That is the recurring problem: researchers are reasoning from summarized medical descriptions relayed second-hand. The structured version of the dispute is at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle) and [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch); the sealing of the underlying record is at [Autopsy Report Not Public](/Medical/autopsy_report_not_public).
