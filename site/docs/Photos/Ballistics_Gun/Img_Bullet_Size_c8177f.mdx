## What This Image Shows

A top-down photograph of five cartridges laid out side by side in descending order of size on a closed black spiral notebook, on a granite counter. Each has been labelled in the image: 30-06, 556, 357, 45, and 9mm.

The photograph exists to make one point visually that is otherwise buried in numbers. A **.30-06** cartridge is far larger than the handgun rounds most people picture when they hear the word bullet, and the energy figures follow the size — roughly 2,914 foot-pounds, about 3,950 joules, for a 180-grain .30-06 at 2,700 feet per second, against roughly 356 foot-pounds, about 482 joules, for a 115-grain 9mm.

The argument built on that gap is that a round carrying eight times the energy of a 9mm is expected to pass through a neck and keep going rather than deposit fragments and stop. Analysts who accept the official account respond that soft-point construction, bone strike, and fragmentation change that picture substantially. Both are laid out at [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch) and [Alternative Calibers and Weapons](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons), with the recovered fragment record at [ATF Fragment Inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive).
