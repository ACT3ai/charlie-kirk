## What This Image Shows

A three-dimensional anatomical render of the skull, jaw, and cervical spine in side profile, with each vertebra labeled C1 through C7. Four red starburst markers sit along the spine, a green dashed vertical line and a red dashed diagonal line cross the image, and a solid red circle marks one point.

The starbursts belong to the illustration's original clinical purpose — they are the generic pain or irritation markers used in chiropractic and physical-therapy diagrams. The green and red lines are the annotations added afterwards, and they are what makes this a working file rather than a stock image: someone has drawn a vertical reference and a candidate angle across a standard anatomy plate.

This image is the base layer that several of the other diagrams in this cluster build on, which is why it is worth looking at before them. Once you know which lines came with the illustration and which were drawn on top, the derived graphics are much easier to read. The trajectory arguments these diagrams support are set out at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle) and [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch); the underlying medical record, still largely sealed, is discussed at [Autopsy Report Not Public](/Medical/autopsy_report_not_public).
