## What This Image Shows

A three-view infographic built on three-dimensional renders of a head and shoulders against black — front view, right profile, and left profile. Red circles mark the claimed entry wound, yellow circles the claimed exit, a yellow dotted line joins the two on the right profile, and green arrows indicate direction. The graphic is credited across all three panels to @I_am_JohnCullen, and a dreamstime watermark shows the base renders are stock models.

The diagram is a researcher's reconstruction, not a medical document. Its value is that it commits to specific locations and a specific direction — most of the material in this cluster gestures at the problem, while this one states a testable position and can be checked against the medical record whenever that record is released.

John Cullen is one of the independent analysts who has published a detailed alternative reading of the wound. The competing reconstructions are collected at [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch), [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle), and [Ballistics and Wound Analysis](/Medical/ballistics_and_wound_analysis), with the alternative-mechanism arguments at [Alternative Kill Mechanisms](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Alternative_Kill_Mechanisms).
