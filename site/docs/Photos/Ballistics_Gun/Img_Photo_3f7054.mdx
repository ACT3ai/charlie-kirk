## What This Image Shows

A phone screenshot of a paused video, timestamped 11:38 on the device and showing 00:02 of a 10-second clip. The frame shows a seated man under the white canopy in front of the event banner — the readable branding is yrefy, YREFY.COM, PRIVATE STUDENT LOAN RELIEF, and TURNING POINT USA — with the word FREEDOM partly visible on his shirt. Green motion-tracking vector lines cover the frame and a multi-coloured heat-signature overlay sits on his knee. An overlay label reads Conv: 0.0.

This is the same analysis pass as the companion frame in this cluster, applied to the tent footage. The coloured overlay is a false-colour rendering of pixel change, not thermal imaging — an ordinary phone camera records no heat data. Where the colour concentrates is where the encoder registered the most change between frames, which is affected by movement, lighting, and compression as much as by anything in the scene.

The frames matter mainly for what they show incidentally: the stage set-up, the banner, the canopy, and the seating positions in the seconds before the shooting. That layout is documented at [AV Team Mic Setup](/Mic/AV_Team_Mic_Setup) and [Tent](/UVU/Tent), and the surviving footage inventory is at [TPUSA Production Footage](/cameras/TPUSA_Production_Footage).
