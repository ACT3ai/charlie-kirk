## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a post on X from **[Blake Bednarz](/People/blake-bednarz)**, dated 1:35 PM on October 18, 2025. Three numbered panels are attached, each showing the same moment at the event — the white canopy printed THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR and PROVE ME WRONG, the merchandise table with white 47 caps, a PA speaker on a stand, and the crowd behind metal barricades. Hand-drawn circles and green highlights trace an object across the three frames.

The post argues that frame 3 defeats the opposing claim: that in frame 3 the shirt is visibly disturbed and the object appears where it has travelled furthest, while frames 1 and 2 show little or no change in posture with the object having moved in both. The conclusion drawn is that because the shirt disturbance occurs in frame 3, the object was recorded in the correct order rather than out of sequence, and that the counter-argument talks the reader in circles.

The underlying dispute is whether an object visible across consecutive frames is a real moving thing and, if so, whether it precedes or follows the disturbance to the shirt — which bears directly on what caused the injury. This is one researcher's frame-order argument against another's, recorded with attribution and not adopted.
