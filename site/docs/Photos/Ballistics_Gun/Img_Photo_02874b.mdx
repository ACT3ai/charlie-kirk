## What This Image Shows

The photograph looks straight down into the footwell of a large vehicle, an SUV or minivan by the layout. A torn, crumpled light-brown paper bag sits on the grey carpet beside a grey leather bench seat, heavily stained with a dark reddish-brown substance. Several twisted yellow candy wrappers lie scattered around it, and the black metal seat mounting brackets are visible at the edge of frame. A circular watermark in the upper right reads We the People and, partly obscured, PROJECT CONSTITUTION.

The image is circulated by researchers examining the vehicle used to move [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) from the courtyard toward the hospital on **September 10, 2025**. What the staining is has not been established on the public record, and the photograph carries no chain-of-custody information, no timestamp, and no independent confirmation of which vehicle it shows. It should be read as a circulated photograph, not as a forensic finding.

The vehicle matters to this cluster because so much of the physical exhibit trail runs through it. The SUV was reportedly cut up and disposed of afterwards, which removed any possibility of independent examination — see [SUV Destroyed](/CoverUp/SUV_Destroyed). The transport itself, including the burnt object photographed inside, is covered at [SUV Transporting Charlie](/Mic/suv-transporting-charlie) and [Burnt Object and RODE Transmitter](/Mic/burnt-object-rode-transmitter), and the hospital run is at [Charlie Trip to Hospital](/Medical/charlie_trip_to_hospital).
