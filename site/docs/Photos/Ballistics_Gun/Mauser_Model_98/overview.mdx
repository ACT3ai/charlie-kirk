## About This Cluster

These reference images document the weapon class named in the official narrative: a **Mauser Model 98** bolt-action rifle chambered in **.30-06 Springfield**, said to have belonged to [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)'s grandfather. The cluster shows the rifle type disassembled into its components, in studio profile views, and as a scoped sporting rifle — the visual baseline for judging every claim about what the alleged weapon could and could not do.

The details matter to the case in specific ways. A Model 98 breaks down into a barreled action of roughly 28-32 inches and a stock of similar length, and requires only a screwdriver — which frames the questions about how a rifle was carried onto campus and what could fit in a 17-inch backpack. Researchers have also questioned the "grandfather's World War I rifle" framing: commentators note the pictured type is a commercial Mauser 98, not a military K98, and that a widely circulated photo of a black scoped rifle on cardboard appears to trace to stock imagery rather than the evidence rifle. These are reported observations, and the identification of the actual rifle in evidence remains a matter for the court.
