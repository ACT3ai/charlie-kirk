## About This Cluster

These reference images document the weapon class named in the official narrative: a **Mauser Model 98** bolt-action rifle chambered in **.30-06 Springfield**, said to have belonged to [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)'s grandfather. The cluster shows the rifle type disassembled into its components, in studio profile views, and as a scoped sporting rifle — the visual baseline for judging every claim about what the alleged weapon could and could not do.

The details matter to the case in specific ways. A Model 98 breaks down into a barreled action of roughly 28-32 inches and a stock of similar length, and requires only a screwdriver — which frames the questions about how a rifle was carried onto campus and what could fit in a 17-inch backpack. Researchers have also questioned the "grandfather's World War I rifle" framing: commentators note the pictured type is a commercial Mauser 98, not a military K98, and that a widely circulated photo of a black scoped rifle on cardboard appears to trace to stock imagery rather than the recovered rifle. These are reported observations, and the identification of the actual rifle held by the court remains a matter for the court.

Look at all three frames, then read the dimensions and disassembly analysis at [Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle) and the fingerprint reporting at [Rifle Fingerprints Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Rifle_Fingerprints_Mismatch). The energy questions that follow from the .30-06 chambering live in the peer cluster [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Gun_Thirty_06_Energy/overview).
