## What This Image Shows

A flat-lay reference photograph of a German **Karabiner 98k** bolt-action rifle taken fully apart on a wooden floor: wooden stock and handguard, barrel and receiver, bolt body, sleeve, firing pin and spring, trigger guard and magazine assembly, band springs and screws. Laid out alongside are a leather sling, an SG 84/98 bayonet with scabbard, an RG34 cleaning tin with pull-through and brushes, and five 7.92x57mm Mauser cartridges in a stripper clip.

The photograph is a reference exhibit, not case material, and it does two jobs. It shows how far a Mauser-pattern rifle breaks down — into a barrelled action of roughly 28 to 32 inches and a stock of roughly 30 to 34 inches — which is the measurement at the centre of the argument about whether a rifle could have been carried in the backpack seen in the released footage, whose interior researchers put at about 17 inches.

It also marks a distinction that runs through this cluster. The wartime Kar98k shown here is chambered in 7.92x57mm, while the rifle in the official account is a **.30-06**, and researchers have argued the recovered weapon is not a Kar98 at all but a commercially produced Mauser 98. See [Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle), [Backpack Rifle Impossibility](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Backpack_Rifle_Impossibility), and [Rifle Fingerprints Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Rifle_Fingerprints_Mismatch).
