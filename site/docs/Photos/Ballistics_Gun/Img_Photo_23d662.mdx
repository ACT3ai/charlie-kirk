## What This Image Shows

A composite screenshot of a thread on X, showing the same post twice — once in a dark interface with the attached anatomical diagram enlarged, and once in a light interface with the replies visible. The diagram carries the C1 through C7 labels and a drawn bullet path.

The post, from an account posting as Freemason [@STARFORCEHH](/Influencers/x/starforcehh), reads that new medical data states the bullet entered [Charlie](/People/charlie-kirk)'s neck at C2, obliterated C3 through C7 and then lodged in T1; that Charlie was leaning slightly forward when struck, slightly to the left of his Adam's apple; and that with a gradual ten-degree downward angle it should have entered around C7 or T1. It concludes that given the height of the roof and the range [Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) was at, it is mathematically impossible for the bullet to enter at C2, adding that C2 is nowhere close to the wound in his neck. A visible reply from a nurse account argues the opposite — that the bullet hit the edge of body armor and ricocheted upward — and tells the poster to stop.

The screenshot is kept because it captures both sides of the argument in one frame, including the pushback. Neither position is settled: the full autopsy is not public, so the entry level, the lean, and the exact range are all being inferred from partial descriptions. The structured version of this dispute is at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle) and [Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch), with the firing position itself examined at [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center).
