## What This Image Shows

A daylight press photograph of three investigators working a dirt clearing in brushy, semi-wooded ground. One crouches and points at a specific spot while holding a clear plastic collection bag; the other two lean in to look. Agency lettering is partly legible on two of their shirts, and a piece of orange flagging ribbon is tied to a branch in the background.

The flagging ribbon is the detail worth noticing: it marks a point already identified, which means this frame shows documentation of a located item rather than an open search. The terrain — dry grass, brush, a clearing — matches the description of the wooded area north of the campus where the government's own summary states the **Mauser .30-06** was located wrapped in a towel.

The photograph is press coverage and does not itself establish what was found or when. The recovery sequence is exactly what researchers have questioned: when the area was searched, who found the rifle, and how the timeline of the find sits against the movements attributed to the suspect.
