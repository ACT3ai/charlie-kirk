## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Jaxon Thomas Fox", filtered to **Israel** across the widest window Trends offers, **2004 - present**. The line is flat at zero for nearly two decades and then spikes to the maximum value of 100 around **November 2023**. Red hand-drawn annotations — a circle and a thick arrow — have been added to the image to point at the search-term field. The profile avatar in the corner reads "PROJECT CONSTITUTION", identifying the account that made the capture.

The twenty-year axis is what the annotation is trying to make the viewer notice. Across the entire measured history of Google's index for this bucket, the name registers exactly once, and that once is in late 2023 — well before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). **Jaxson Fox** appears in this investigation in connection with a [court informant order](/court/jaxson-fox-informant-order), which is why the name is being checked at all.

The court thread is at [Jaxson Fox informant order](/court/jaxson-fox-informant-order) and the profile is at [Jaxson Thomas Fox](/distraction_people/Jaxson_Thomas_Fox). The same caution applies as everywhere in this cluster: a spike on a rare name in a low-volume bucket can rest on a handful of queries, and Trends identifies no one. No claim is made here that Mr. Fox did anything wrong.
