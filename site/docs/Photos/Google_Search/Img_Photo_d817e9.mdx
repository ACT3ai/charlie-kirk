## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of Utah's official "Announcement of **JUDICIAL VACANCY**", dated **January 17, 2025**. The text reads: "Applications are now being accepted for the position of district court judge in the [**Fourth District Court**](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo). (Counties include Juab, Millard, Utah and Wasatch). This position results from the retirement of [**Judge Robert C. Lunnen**](/court/judge-robert-lunnen), August 1, 2025. Applications must be submitted online by Tuesday, February 18, 2025."

The rest of the notice is standard: applications through judicialvacancy.utah.gov, the Judicial Nominating Commission's role, the Governor's thirty days to make a selection, minimum age and residency requirements under Article VIII of the Utah Constitution, the retirement formula, and the terms — "The annual salary is $213,900 for a District Court Judge, 20 days paid vacation per year, 11 paid holidays, $25,000 term life insurance policy" with optional additional coverage and a choice of medical and dental plans.

This is the earliest document in the judicial-timeline set, and it is what anchors the sequence: the vacancy was posted in **January 2025**, more than seven months before Judge Lunnen's retirement took effect and nearly eight months before [September 10](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). A vacancy announced that far ahead of a scheduled retirement is entirely routine, and nothing improper is asserted.
