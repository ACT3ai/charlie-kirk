## What This Image Shows

A mobile **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Patrice andrews", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**. The axis runs October 13, 2024 to July 20, 2025 and the tooltip marks the peak at **June 1-7, 2025** with a reading of **100**. An "Interest by subregion" panel sits partly visible below the graph.

The capture belongs to the same family as the other single-name Israel-bucket screenshots in this cluster: a flat twelve-month line broken by one week at the maximum, months before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). Researchers collected a long list of such names — officials, medical staff, security figures, and local political figures — and the argument is cumulative rather than individual. No single panel proves anything; the claim is that too many of them peak inside the same summer.

The obvious objection is selection. If you run enough low-volume names through Trends, a large share will show exactly this shape, because a term with almost no baseline must reach its own maximum on some week. Nothing on this capture identifies who searched or why, and no wrongdoing by anyone named is asserted.
