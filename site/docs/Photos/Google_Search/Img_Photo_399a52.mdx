## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Timpanogos Regional Hospital", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs from October 6, 2024 through September 7, 2025, and the tooltip marks the peak at the week of **August 10-16, 2025** with a reading of **100**.

**Timpanogos Regional Hospital** in Orem is where Charlie Kirk was taken on September 10, 2025 — and it is not the closest hospital to the Utah Valley University campus, which is a separate question this investigation examines. The week marked here falls about four weeks before the event. Researchers pair this capture with the claimed pre-event searches for the hospital's emergency and surgical staff and with the "distance uvu timpanagos" query that appears in the [Searches By Date](/Photos/Google_Search/Searches_By_Date/overview) cluster peaking on September 5, 2025.

Directly against it stands the companion capture in this same cluster in which the identical query returns "not enough data" — the two together being the reason the deletion claim exists and also the reason it cannot be resolved from screenshots alone. Trends suppresses low-volume series as its trailing window moves, which produces the same visible outcome as removal.
