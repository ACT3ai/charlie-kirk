## What This Image Shows

A four-by-four collage of sixteen **Google Trends** panels, all filtered to **Israel**, **Tel Aviv District**, or **Center District**, and all showing the same flat-then-single-spike shape. The tooltips advance chronologically from **July 21, 2025** to **September 8, 2025** — the last of them two days before the shooting.

The sixteen terms and their marked dates are: "Casper brown layout" (Jul 21), "Lisa Thomas" (Jul 28), "utah valley university parking" (Jul 27 - Aug 2), "Jamal reed" (Jul 27 - Aug 2), "Tyler Robinson Charlie kirk" (Aug 3, reading 59 and the only panel with two spikes), "Jeff gray" (Aug 9), "Orem Police Department" (Aug 17-23), "phil lyman rsl" (Aug 17-23), "Dixie Technical College" (Aug 13), "Intermountain Health" (Aug 23), "dr. frank kurek" (Aug 23), "Rick cutler" (Aug 24-30), "Lease center uvu" (Aug 26), "Dan Flood" (Aug 31 - Sep 6), "tyler robinson Latter-day Saints" (Aug 31 - Sep 6), and "Rob hild" (Sep 8).

One panel deserves separate attention. "Tyler Robinson Charlie kirk" pairs the two names in a single query and is dated **August 3, 2025** — before any public connection between them existed. That is the strongest single claim in the grid and also the one most dependent on the screenshot being accurate, since it cannot be reproduced now that the names are permanently linked in the record. Everything else on the grid inherits the standard caution: Trends identifies no searcher, normalizes each panel separately, and a low-volume term always peaks somewhere.
