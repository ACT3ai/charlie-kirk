## What This Image Shows

A four-by-four collage of sixteen **Google Trends** panels, filtered to **Israel**, **Tel Aviv District**, or **Center District**, each flat except for one spike, with tooltips advancing week by week from **December 2024** through **April 2025**. Most use a custom range of 6/1/24 to 11/10/25.

The terms and marked weeks are: "philip lyman tiffany blalock" (Dec 1-7, reading 97), "Mike Mitchell utah" (Dec 8-14), "tyler robinson lds" (Dec 8-14), "tyler boyer" (Feb 2-8), "lance twiggs fbi" (Feb 9-15), "Tyler Robinson caldera engineering" (Feb 16-22), "utah valley university rooftop" (Feb 16-22), "george jia 911" (Mar 9-15), "utah valley university gun" (Mar 16-22), "tyler robinson ksl" (Mar 16-22, reading 64), "lance twiggs transgender" (Mar 23-29), "tyler robinson Latter-day Saints" (Mar 23-29), "caldera engineering" (Apr 6-12), "dr. frank lusk" (Apr 13-19), and a further panel beginning "Cooper".

This grid sits chronologically between the 2024 grid and the May-to-July grid, and it carries the queries a researcher finds hardest to explain away: "**utah valley university rooftop**" and "**utah valley university gun**" in February and March 2025, months before a shooting from a campus rooftop. It also contains the December 8-14, 2024 week that recurs in the [Kathryn Nester](/GoogleSearches/kathryn-nester-searches) claim. Against that, the same structural objection stands as for every grid: sixteen low-volume terms selected because each peaks in a different week will always tile a calendar, and Trends shows no searcher, no volume, and no purpose.
