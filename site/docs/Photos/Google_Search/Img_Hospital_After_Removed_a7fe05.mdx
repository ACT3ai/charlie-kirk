## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** screen for the search term "Timpanogos Regional Hospital", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search. The interest-over-time panel is blank and Google returns: "Hmm, your search doesn't have enough data to show here. Please make sure everything is spelled correctly, or try a more general term."

The filename this was archived under marks it as an "after removed" capture — that is, taken after a version of this query had reportedly returned a visible series. That claim is the reason the null is preserved. Researchers on this thread allege that Trends results for terms in this case have disappeared after the screenshots went viral; **Stew Peters** made the same allegation on July 16, 2026 about [Fort Huachuca](/Planes/Airport-Fort-Huachuca) image-search results, saying every named figure's results "had their results completely wiped" while unnamed people still returned data.

Both readings are available and neither is proven. Trends genuinely does suppress series below a volume threshold, and a term that sat just above the line can fall below it as the trailing window moves — no deletion required, and no named company or individual is alleged here to have removed anything. Nor is any of this an accusation against the people whose names were named on that broadcast: being searched for, or being discussed on a show, is not conduct. **Timpanogos Regional Hospital** is where [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) was taken on [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). A capture of the same term showing a peak the week of August 10-16, 2025 is at [Timpanogos Hospital — Israel Search Peak](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_399a52).
