## What This Image Shows

The third capture in the four-term plate-and-tail-number sequence. The **Google Trends** comparison runs "Utah License Plate Z923DA", "tail number N560tw", "Utah License Plate T092ZS", and an Arizona plate number against **Israel** over the custom range **4/1/25 - 9/10/25**. The tooltip sits on **August 17, 2025**, where the tail-number series reads **97** and the plate series read **0**.

Placed next to its siblings this capture shows the N560TW series holding a high reading across consecutive days — 63 on August 12, 91 on August 16, 97 on August 17 — rather than producing a single isolated needle. A sustained run is a different kind of signal from a one-day spike, and it is harder to attribute to a stray query.

What the capture cannot show is absolute volume. Trends indexes each series against the maximum in the selected range, so "97" means ninety-seven percent of this term's own busiest day inside this bucket, not ninety-seven of anything.
