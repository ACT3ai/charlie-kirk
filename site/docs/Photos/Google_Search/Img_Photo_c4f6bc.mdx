## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** comparison running four terms together — "charlie kirk", "deirdre amaro", "josh hammer", and "tyler robinson" — filtered to **Israel** across the custom range **2/1/24 to 7/31/24**. Date markers on the chart pick out **3/31/24**, **5/10/24**, **5/17/24** and **5/24/24**, with the highest reading falling on **May 17, 2024**. The capture is watermarked with the X handles that circulated it.

The custom window is chosen to end more than a year before September 10, 2025, so every reading on the chart is far pre-event. That is the whole argument: **Deirdre Amaro** is the medical examiner who would later be associated with the case, and **Tyler Robinson** is the man later charged. Neither name was publicly connected to Charlie Kirk in early 2024. A researcher pointing at co-movement between those four terms in May 2024 is asking why they would rise together at all.

The counterweight is that all four are ordinary personal names shared by many people, and a comparison chart normalizes every series against the single highest point across all of them, so low-volume terms can appear to move when the underlying counts are tiny.
