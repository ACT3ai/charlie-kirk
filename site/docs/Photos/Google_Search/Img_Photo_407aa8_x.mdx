## What This Image Shows

A two-part screenshot. The upper half is a KUTV article headlined "New Utah judge takes on accused killer's death penalty case in Charlie Kirk murder", bylined "by Jim Spiewak, KUTV | Wed, October 15th 2025 at 9:48 PM" and updated the following morning. The lead reads: "SALT LAKE CITY (KUTV) - [**Judge Tony Graf Jr.**](/court/judge-tony-graf) has found himself at the center of one of Utah's most closely watched murder cases less than two months after taking the bench."

The lower half repeats the **Utah State Bar** press release of **Friday, May 2, 2025**, announcing that Governor [**Spencer J. Cox**](/People/spencer-cox) appointed **Tony F. Graf** to the [Fourth District Court](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo), "filling a vacancy left by the retirement of [**Judge Robert Lunnen**](/court/judge-robert-lunnen)." The capture is watermarked with the X handles that circulated it.

Pairing the two documents is the argument in miniature: a judge appointed in May and sworn in on August 4 was assigned, roughly six weeks later, to the highest-profile capital case in the state. Nothing about that sequence is improper — vacancies are filled on their own schedule and case assignment follows court rules — and no impropriety is alleged. Researchers cite it as the real-world timeline that the search-interest charts for both judges' names should be measured against.
