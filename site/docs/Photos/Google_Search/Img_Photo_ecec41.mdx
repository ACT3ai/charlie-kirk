## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** comparison running three terms together — "charlie kirk", "deirdre amaro", and "josh hammer" — filtered to **Israel** across the custom range **2/1/24 - 7/31/24**. Tooltips mark readings on **March 21, 2024**, **May 10, 2024** (58), and **May 17, 2024** (100), with the axis spanning February to June 2024. The capture is watermarked with the X handles that circulated it.

This is the three-term version of a comparison that also exists with **tyler robinson** added as a fourth series. The window is more than a year before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). **Deirdre Amaro** is the medical examiner later associated with the case; her name had no public connection to [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) in early 2024, which is why a researcher would find co-movement in that period worth flagging.

The methodological problem is built into the tool. A multi-term comparison scales all series to one shared maximum, so a term with high absolute volume — "charlie kirk" — can flatten the others visually while a low-volume term's apparent movement rests on a handful of queries. Nothing here shows who searched or why, and no wrongdoing by anyone named is asserted. The four-term version is at [Four-Term Israel Comparison, Early 2024](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_c4f6bc).
