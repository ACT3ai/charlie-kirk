## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an April 13, 2025 hoodline.com article headlined "FIVE POTENTIAL NOMINEES VYING FOR FOURTH DISTRICT COURT VACANCY AHEAD OF JUDGE LUNNEN'S RETIREMENT IN UTAH". The article states that [**Judge Robert Lunnen**](/court/judge-robert-lunnen)'s retirement was "set, effective August 1," and that the Fourth District Judicial Nominating Commission had narrowed the field to five candidates.

The five named in the text are **Von Christiansen**, County Attorney of Beaver County; **Joshua Esplin**, Assistant Director and Trial Attorney with the Utah County Public Defenders Association; [**Tony Graf**](/court/judge-tony-graf), then serving dual roles as Deputy County Attorney for Davis County and volunteer judge pro tempore at Salt Lake City Justice Court; **Samuel Pead**, an Assistant United States Attorney; and **Ryan Stack**, a Deputy County Attorney and prosecuting attorney in Summit County. The capture carries a "asedsamParker" watermark from the account that circulated it.

Researchers filed this with the Trends captures because it fixes the public timeline of the judicial vacancy — announced January 2025, shortlisted April, appointed May, retirement effective August 1 — against which the search-interest charts for the two judges' names are being read. This is ordinary public-record material about a routine appointment process, and nothing here suggests otherwise. See [Judge Robert Lunnen](/court/judge-robert-lunnen), [judges](/court/judges), and [judges Graf and Lunnen searches](/GoogleSearches/judges-graf-lunnen-searches).
