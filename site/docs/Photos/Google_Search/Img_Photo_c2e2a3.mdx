## What This Image Shows

This **Google Trends** screen tracks the search term "ZACHARIAH QURESHI" filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**, and — unusually — the **YouTube Search** property rather than Web Search. The line runs flat and then peaks at the maximum index of 100 during the week of **July 13-19, 2025**. The capture carries a "THE FOUNDERS' PRESS" watermark.

The YouTube filter matters. Trends lets a researcher choose which Google surface to measure, and video search is a much smaller pool than web search, so a spike there can rest on a very small number of queries. That cuts both ways: it makes the index easier to move, and it also makes an isolated peak on a specific personal name harder to explain as background noise. **Zachariah Qureshi** appears in this investigation through separate threads involving a Department of Defense contract and National Guard service; the searches themselves establish nothing about him.
