## What This Image Shows

Three **Google Trends** panels stacked in one capture, all filtered to the **District of Columbia** over the **Past 90 days**. Each reads 100 at its peak. The terms and their marked dates are "**brian harpole**" on **August 30, 2025**, "**charlie kirk hasbara**" on **September 2, 2025**, and "**charlie kirk family**" on **September 2, 2025**.

Stacking is the method: three unrelated strings, one region, one window, so the peak dates can be compared without switching charts. All three land in the eleven days before September 10, 2025. **Brian Harpole** is a security figure in this investigation; "hasbara" is the Hebrew term for state public diplomacy and appears in the discussion of Charlie Kirk's shifting position on Israel; "charlie kirk family" is a plain biographical query.

What the capture cannot do is tell you who ran any of these searches, how many people ran them, or why. Google reports a normalized index inside a metro bucket, and in a low-volume bucket a small number of queries reaches 100.
