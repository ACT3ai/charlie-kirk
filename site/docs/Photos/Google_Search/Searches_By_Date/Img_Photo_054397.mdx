## What This Image Shows

Three **Google Trends** panels stacked in one capture, all filtered to the **District of Columbia**, **Past 90 days**, each reading 100 at its peak. The terms and dates are "**Tyler Bowyer**" on **July 31, 2025**, "**rep lyman**" on **July 21, 2025**, and "**kirk schedule**" on **August 5, 2025**.

The third term is the one researchers point at. "kirk schedule" is not a name or a topic — it is an itinerary query, and it peaks in the D.C. bucket on **August 5, 2025**, five weeks before the Utah Valley University event. The other two are people: **Tyler Bowyer** is a Turning Point USA executive, and "rep lyman" refers to Utah legislator **Phil Lyman**, whose name recurs across this whole search thread.

A three-word phrase like "kirk schedule" is ambiguous enough that it could refer to any Kirk, and Trends will not disclose the queries behind an index. The claim here is one of pattern rather than proof, and nothing on this capture identifies a searcher or shows that anyone knew anything in advance.
