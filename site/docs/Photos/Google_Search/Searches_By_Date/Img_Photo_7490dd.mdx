## What This Image Shows

Six **Google Trends** panels in one capture, all filtered to the **District of Columbia**, **Past 90 days**, with axes running July 11 to October 3. The terms are "**UVU Courtyard**" (classified by Google as a Topic), "**Nicholas J. Fuentes**", "**Robert J. Shillman** — American businessman", "**Robert W. Kirk** — Charlie Kirk's father", "**Utah Valley University** — Public university in Orem, Utah", and "**Greg Shaffer**".

Note that Google has resolved several of these to entities rather than raw strings — the subtitles "American businessman", "Charlie Kirk's father", and "Public university in Orem, Utah" are Google's own descriptions. Entity queries measure interest in a specific person or place rather than in a word, which makes them harder to dismiss as ambiguous string matches and is why the researcher captured them in this form.

Grouping is the argument here: campus geography, a media figure, a funder, a family member, and a security name in one D.C. window before September 10, 2025. No peak dates are visible in this capture, so it should be read as a survey rather than as evidence of timing, and nothing in it identifies a searcher or implies anyone did anything wrong.
