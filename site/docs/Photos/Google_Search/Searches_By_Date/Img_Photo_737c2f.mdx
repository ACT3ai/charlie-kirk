## What This Image Shows

Two **Google Trends** panels stacked in one capture, both filtered to the **District of Columbia**, **Past 90 days**, axes running July 11 to October 3. The first term is "**David Yosef**"; the second is "**Dairy Queen Grill & Chill**", which Google has resolved to an entity and labels "Fast food restaurant in **Orem, Utah**".

The Dairy Queen panel is the point. Google is not matching the chain nationally — the entity card identifies one specific franchise location in Orem, the city where [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) sits. A researcher's argument is that D.C.-bucket interest in a single suburban fast-food restaurant two thousand miles away is not ordinary browsing; the restaurant appears elsewhere in this investigation as a reported stop in the movements around [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025).

The honest counterweight is that Google's entity resolution is imperfect and that low-volume metro panels are noisy. This capture also carries no visible tooltip, so no peak date can be read from it. Nothing here identifies a searcher, and no wrongdoing by anyone is asserted.
