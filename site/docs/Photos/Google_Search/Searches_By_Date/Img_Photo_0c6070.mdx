## What This Image Shows

Three **Google Trends** panels stacked in one capture, all filtered to the **District of Columbia**, **Past 90 days**, and all three peaking on the same day — **September 5, 2025** — each at **100**. The terms are "**distance uvu timpanagos**", "**orem ut coroner**", and "**Valhalla Danish House**".

This is the single strongest capture in the date-stacked set, and the reason is the alignment. Three unrelated strings reaching their maximum in the same D.C. bucket on the same day, five days before the event, is a coincidence that has to be either explained or dismissed. The three subjects are also uncomfortably specific: the driving distance from the campus to the hospital, the county coroner, and **Valhalla** — the Danish house associated with the Frantzve family, which is the subject of its own thread in this investigation.

The dismissal is available and should be stated. Trends buckets are coarse, a normalized index reaches 100 on whatever the busiest day happens to be, and three low-volume terms can share a peak day by chance more easily than intuition suggests. The capture identifies no searcher, no query count, and no purpose, and it asserts no wrongdoing by anyone.
