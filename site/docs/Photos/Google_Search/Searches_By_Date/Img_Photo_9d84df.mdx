## What This Image Shows

Five **Google Trends** panels in one capture, all filtered to the **District of Columbia**, **Past 90 days**, axes running July 11 to October 3. The terms are "**matt robinson**", "**september blood moon**", "**Pine View High School** — High school in St. George, Utah", "**David Yosef**", and "**charlie kirk anti-semitic**". One panel also shows an "Interest by metro" section.

Two of these are specific in ways the others are not. **Pine View High School** is resolved by Google as an entity and is the St. George school associated with the man later charged; and "charlie kirk anti-semitic" is a compound opinion query rather than a lookup, which researchers read as someone testing a framing rather than seeking information. "matt robinson" is a family name in the same thread.

"september blood moon" pulls the other way — a lunar eclipse on September 7, 2025 was a widely covered astronomical event, and interest in it needs no explanation at all. Its presence in the grid is a reminder that these captures mix strong and weak terms, and that the reader has to sort them. No peak dates are visible here, nothing identifies a searcher, and no wrongdoing by anyone named is asserted.
