## What This Image Shows

Three **Google Trends** panels, two of them set to **Past 7 days** rather than ninety, which switches the chart to hourly resolution. The quoted term "**derek maxfield**" is filtered to **Washington DC (Hagerstown)** with a tooltip reading "**Sep 7, 2025 at 1:00 PM**" and a value of **36**, and the axis extends to Sep 9 at 5:00 PM. The quoted term "**losee center**" is filtered the same way with a tooltip at "**Sep 8, 2025 at 7:00 PM**" and a value of **45**, the axis running to Sep 12 at 9:00 AM. Between them sits a ninety-day panel for "**intermountain st vincent trauma center**" in the **District of Columbia** bucket, peaking **September 8, 2025** at 100.

The hourly view is the reason this capture matters. Every other panel in this cluster reports weeks or days; a seven-day window gives a timestamp. Researchers argue that a quoted search for the **Losee Center** — the building at the center of the Utah Valley University shooting analysis — timestamped to the evening of September 8, two days before the event, is a different order of specificity from a name search.

The seven-day window is also the most fragile: it expires within a week, cannot be reproduced afterward, and hourly indices in a metro bucket are drawn from very small counts. Nothing here identifies a searcher or establishes intent.
