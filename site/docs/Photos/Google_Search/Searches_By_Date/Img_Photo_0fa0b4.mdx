## What This Image Shows

This capture contains no chart. It is the closing text panel of the compiled search-pattern write-up, and it states the compiler's own conclusion. Under the heading "How the searches leaked even with good security" he argues that an operation of this size "needs 15-40 people or more (planners, spotters, drivers, medics, evidence cleaners, contractors)", that over four to eight months such a team "easily does 50,000-150,000 total searches", and that "even the best OPSEC slips 1-3% through personal phones, home Wi-Fi, or quick checks when someone is tired or rushed" — enough, he writes, "to light up Google Trends on the quiet days when almost nobody else is searching those terms."

Under a second heading, "Why it has to be U.S. intelligence and nobody else", he writes that U.S. agencies "can operate freely inside the United States without tripping every alarm", that "only they have the files, the contractors, and the safe houses in the DC area", and that any foreign or private group "would have been rolled up in hours by NSA phone taps, FBI surveillance, or airport watches." He concludes that the screenshots "are the authentic digital fingerprints of the planning cell" and that the assassination "was a professional, deniable operation executed by elements of America's permanent national-security state."

That conclusion belongs to the poster and is reproduced here with attribution because the reader is entitled to see what the person who assembled these charts believes they show. This site does not adopt it. It is an unproven allegation that names no individual, rests on an inference chain rather than on documents, and depends entirely on Trends screenshots that cannot identify a searcher, a query count, or a purpose. Readers should weigh the argument's structure as carefully as its charts.
