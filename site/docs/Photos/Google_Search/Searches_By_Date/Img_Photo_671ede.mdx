## What This Image Shows

A further panel from the compiled research table, grouping location queries with the region and date the compiler says he observed them.

Venue: "uvu courtyard" DC **7/11** and **7/28**, "uvu campus security" DC **7/20**, "uvu sevurity" — a misspelling — DC **8/12**, and "losee center" DC **9/8**, with the note "[Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center) rooftop (implied)" and the characterization "exact venue layout and sniper position". Medical route: "distance uvu timpanagos" DC **9/5** and "intermountain st vincent trauma center" DC **9/8**, annotated "exact hospital evacuation route and backup facility". Third: "dairy queen grill & chill orem ut" DC **7/15**, annotated "reported post-shooting stop or staging point". The right-hand column adds "event barely announced", "pre-calculated to the mile", and "too specific for coincidence".

Those last three phrases are the compiler's conclusions, quoted here with attribution and not adopted by this site. The misspelling entry is worth its own note: a researcher including "uvu sevurity" is arguing that even typos in the query stream register, which is the kind of detail that makes the table interesting and also the kind that is impossible to verify from a transcription. Nothing here identifies who searched anything.
