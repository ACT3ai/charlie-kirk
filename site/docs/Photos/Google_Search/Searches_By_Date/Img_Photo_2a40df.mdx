## What This Image Shows

A panel from the compiled research table, headed "Search term (location and date observed)", "What it means in the case", and "Why it is impossible for a random person to search this early".

The listed entries are "Timpanogos Regional Hospital" DC **8/1**, "timpanagos" DC **7/21**, "timpanagos hospital lead surgeon" DC **7/23**, "Timpanogos surgeons (Israel July)", "[Integrity Security Solutions](/Security_Team/integrity-security)" DC **7/20** and **8/24**, and "brian harpole" DC **8/20**. The compiler's own annotations read "exact hospital, shorthand, lead surgeon, and trauma team"; "Kirk's event security company and his personal security chief"; "no public link existed; surgeons pre-selected months ahead"; and "recon or neutralization of his protection".

Those annotations are the compiler's characterizations, quoted with attribution and not adopted here — in particular, the phrase "surgeons pre-selected" is his inference and not something a search chart can show, and "recon or neutralization of his protection" is his reading of what a query might have been for, not a claim by this site that anything was done to or by **Brian Harpole**, [Integrity Security Solutions](/Security_Team/integrity-security), or any hospital staff member. Mr. Harpole is a private individual doing a security job; his name appearing in a search-interest table says nothing about his conduct. What the table does capture is the shape of the argument: two clusters of queries, one aimed at where a casualty would be taken and one aimed at who was providing protection, both dated to the weeks before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). The misspelling "timpanagos" recurs across the compilation and is treated by the compiler as a signature. No wrongdoing by any hospital, company, or individual is asserted.
