## What This Image Shows

A panel from a compiled research table rather than a Google Trends screen. The left column lists search terms with the region and date the researcher says he observed them; the right columns give his interpretation. The entries legible here are "robinson high school" DC **8/10**, "Pine View High School (St George Utah)" DC **7/20**, "tyler robinson" Israel **8/14**, "Hunter Kozak" DC **8/5** and **8/22**, "deirdre amaro" DC **9/2** and **9/3**, "deirdre amaro" Israel **8/19**, "orem ut coroner" DC **9/5**, "uvu courtyard" DC **7/11** and **7/28**, and "uvu campus security" DC **7/20**.

The interpretive column carries the researcher's own notes: that Hunter Kozak was the "last student to ask Kirk a question before shots", that Deirdre Amaro is the "Utah medical examiner who signed death certificate", that the campus queries read as "threat assessment or audience mapping", and that the names were "unknown until after the killing".

Those are the compiler's characterizations, presented here with attribution and not adopted. A table of this kind is a secondary artifact: the dates are transcribed by hand and cannot be verified from the table itself, only from the underlying captures. It asserts no wrongdoing by any named person, and Google Trends does not identify who searched anything.
