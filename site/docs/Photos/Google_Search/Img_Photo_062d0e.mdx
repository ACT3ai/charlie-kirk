## What This Image Shows

A mobile capture of **Google Trends** showing the search term "Hunter kozak" filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**, with the window running from roughly July 11 to October 3. The interest-over-time chart is displayed above a partly visible "Interest by subregion" panel.

**Hunter Kozak** is the student who asked [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) the final question at the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) event on [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) — he was mid-exchange when the shot was fired. Because his name was unknown to the public before that moment, any measurable search interest in it beforehand is the kind of anomaly this cluster exists to flag. Whether this particular capture shows pre-event interest depends on where the peak falls inside the ninety-day window, which is easier to read on the companion captures.

Two further captures of the same term are at [Hunter Kozak Israel Search — Mobile Capture](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_baf564) and [Hunter Kozak — Israel Search, August 31](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_09d1f4). His profile is at [Hunter Kozak](/distraction_people/Hunter_Kozak), and the written analysis is at [Israel IP search patterns](/GoogleSearches/israel-ip-search-patterns). Trends never identifies a searcher, and nothing here implies Mr. Kozak did anything wrong.
