## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Russell Kim Kennington", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**, All categories, and the **Image Search** property. The axis runs July 18 to October 4 and the tooltip marks the peak at **July 31, 2025** with a reading of **100**.

Image Search is the narrowest of the three Google surfaces queried for this name in this cluster, and it is the one researchers consider most telling when it moves: someone running a person's full name against image results is usually trying to find out what that person looks like, not what they have said or done. That is the argument. The counterargument is arithmetic — Image Search is a small pool, so the number of queries required to reach an index of 100 is correspondingly small.

**July 31, 2025** is six weeks before [September 10](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). Taken with the News Search peak on August 3 and the Web Search peak in the week of August 17-23, the three captures place all measurable interest in this name inside a five-week band that ends shortly before the event. **Russell Kennington** is covered at [Russell Kennington](/distraction_people/Russell_Kennington); nothing here asserts he did anything wrong.
