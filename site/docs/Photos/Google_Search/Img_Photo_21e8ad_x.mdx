## What This Image Shows

The archived biography text from the **Utah State Courts** page for **Judge Robert C. Lunnen**, captured through the **Wayback Machine** (archive bar: 7 captures, 3 Oct 2023 to 5 Aug 2025). The page reads in full:

Judge Lunnen "was appointed to the [Fourth District Court](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo) in **December 2016** by Governor **Gary R. Herbert**. He serves in Utah, Wasatch, Juab, and Millard counties." He received a Bachelor of Arts from **Brigham Young University** in 1982 and a law degree from **Loyola University, School of Law, New Orleans** in 1985. "Prior to his judicial appointment, Judge Lunnen served as an attorney for the **United States Department of Justice for 21 years**. He was the **Criminal Chief of the United States Attorney's Office, District of Utah** when he retired. During his career with the Department of Justice, he served two details as the **Judicial Attache in Bogota, Colombia** and in **Kabul, Afghanistan**." The page adds that he speaks fluent Spanish, taught judicial advocacy for prosecutors across Central and South America and at the National Advocacy Center in Columbia, South Carolina, and was assigned to the American Fork Department of the Fourth District Court.

The lines researchers highlight are the federal ones — two decades at the Department of Justice, the Criminal Chief role, and overseas judicial attache postings in Bogota and Kabul. A distinguished federal career on the resume of the judge whose retirement created the vacancy is the kind of detail this investigation logs when it maps who sat where; it is a matter of public biography and implies nothing improper about him or about the timing of his retirement.
