## What This Image Shows

A four-by-four collage of sixteen **Google Trends** panels, filtered to **Israel**, **Tel Aviv District**, or **Center District**, each showing a flat baseline broken by one spike, with the tooltips stepping through **2024**. Most panels use a custom range of 6/1/24 to 9/10/25, ending the day before the shooting.

The terms and marked weeks are: "tyler robinson discord" (Jan 7-13), "lance twiggs Latter-day Saints" (Jan 16-22), "tyler robinson reddit" (Jun 23-29), "tyler robinson lpusd" (Jul 7-13), "Shaner Broderick" (Jul 2024), "tyler robinson fbi" (Jul 21-27), "utah valley university buildings" (Aug 4-10), "tyler robinson lds" (Sep 15-21), "lance twiggs fbi" (Sep 22-28), "Ervin Steele cabrera" (Sep 29 - Oct 5), "tyler robinson israel" (Oct 6-12), "Tyler Robinson Lance twiggs" (Oct 27 - Nov 2), "utah valley university cctv" (Nov 10-16), a second "tyler robinson israel" (Nov 10-16), and "gerold oliver" (Nov 17-23).

This is the earliest grid in the sequence and the boldest claim in it. Several panels do not just search a name — they search a name plus a platform or an institution: Robinson with Discord, with Reddit, with the FBI, with the LDS Church, with Israel; Twiggs with the FBI; the university with its buildings and its CCTV. If the screenshots are accurate, those compound queries were being run through 2024, a full year before any public connection existed between these names and Charlie Kirk. The unavoidable caution is that these are among the hardest readings to verify after the fact, that compound low-volume queries produce extremely noisy indices, and that Trends never identifies a searcher or a purpose.
