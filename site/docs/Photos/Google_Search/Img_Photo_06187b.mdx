## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a ChatGPT response summarizing the judicial calendar. The answer states that [**Robert C. Lunnen**](/court/judge-robert-lunnen) of Utah's [Fourth District Court](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo) had a retirement "listed as effective **August 1, 2025**"; that [**Tony F. Graf, Jr.**](/court/judge-tony-graf) was "appointed **May 2, 2025**" with a swearing-in date "reported as **August 4, 2025**"; and that, "given the swearing-in August 4, 2025 and a court appearance on **September 16, 2025**, it's reasonable to assume his active service began in early August 2025." Small source chips beside each line read "Judicial Vacancy", "Utah State Bar", and "WEAR".

This capture is a working note rather than proof. It shows a researcher using an AI assistant to assemble the date sequence quickly, and the assistant's own answer flags that the last step is an inference, not a record. Anything drawn from it needs to be checked against the primary documents, which this cluster also holds.

Those primary documents are the [judicial vacancy announcement](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_d817e9), the [Utah State Bar appointment press release](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_31980a), and the [archived court biography of Judge Lunnen](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_21e8ad_x). See also [Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf) and [Judge Robert Lunnen](/court/judge-robert-lunnen).
