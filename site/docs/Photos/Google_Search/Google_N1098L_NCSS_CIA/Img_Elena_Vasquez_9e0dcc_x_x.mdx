## What This Image Shows

The twelve-month **Google Trends** series for "Elena Vasquez" filtered to **Israel**, captured with the tooltip open: **November 3-9, 2024**, value **100**. The axis runs October 20, 2024 to September 21, 2025 and there is virtually no search activity before or after that single week.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for **N1098L**, the Bombardier Global 6500 that dropped to roughly 203 feet near the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus on the morning of September 10, 2025 and repeated the maneuver about twenty-three minutes after the shooting. Crew names for private ISR flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so the lists circulating on X are unverified and mutually contradictory. Elena Vasquez is named on one list as the mission engineer and avionics specialist.

This is the dated version of the late-2024 spike, and together the three captures of this name in this cluster tell a more complicated story than any one of them alone. On a twelve-month axis the only measurable interest is the week of **November 3-9, 2024**, ten months before the event. On a ninety-day axis the maximum falls on **August 23, 2025**. Both are true — Trends normalizes to the selected range — which is precisely why a single screenshot showing a dramatic peak proves very little without its window.
