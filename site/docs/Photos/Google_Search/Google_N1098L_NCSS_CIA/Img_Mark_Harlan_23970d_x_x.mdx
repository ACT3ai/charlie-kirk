## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Mark Harlan", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs July 23 to October 9 and the tooltip marks a single sharp peak at **September 9, 2025** with a value of **100**.

The date is the reason this capture matters more than its four siblings: **September 9, 2025** is the day before the shooting at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). The name is a variant of the "Michael Harlan" string captured repeatedly in the Washington D.C. bucket in this same cluster, and a related string, "Professor mark harlin", appears inside one of the sixteen-panel grids in the parent cluster dated to the week of June 8-14, 2025. Researchers treat the three spellings as attempts at the same person; that assumption is theirs and is not established.

Everything about this capture that makes it striking also makes it fragile. Spelling variants multiply the chance of catching an unrelated individual, a ninety-day Israel-bucket index reaches 100 on very few queries, and Trends discloses nothing about who ran a search or why. No claim is made here that any person knew anything in advance, and no wrongdoing by anyone is asserted.
