## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Elena Vasquez", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs July 23 to October 9 and the tooltip marks the peak at **August 23, 2025**.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for **N1098L**, the Bombardier Global 6500 that dropped to roughly 203 feet near the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus on the morning of September 10, 2025 and repeated the maneuver about twenty-three minutes after the shooting. Crew names for private ISR flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so the lists circulating on X are unverified and mutually contradictory. One list names **Sgt. (Ret.) Elena Vasquez** as the mission engineer and avionics specialist aboard the September 10 flight.

August 23, 2025 falls about two and a half weeks before the event. Three other captures of this same name exist in this cluster at different windows, and they do not agree with each other about when the interest occurred — a twelve-month view puts the maximum in early November 2024 instead. That divergence is the honest state of the record: Trends rescales to whatever the busiest point in the selected range happens to be, and "Elena Vasquez" is a common name that may not refer to the same person at all.
