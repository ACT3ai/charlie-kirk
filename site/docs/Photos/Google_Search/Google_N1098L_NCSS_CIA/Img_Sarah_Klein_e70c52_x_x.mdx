## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Sarah Klein", filtered to **Washington DC (Hagerstown)**, **Past 90 days**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs July 23 to October 9 and the tooltip marks a single sharp peak at **September 4, 2025** with a reading of **100**, flat at or near zero on either side.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview), the Bombardier Global 6500 that dropped to roughly 203 feet near the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus on the morning of [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) and repeated the maneuver about twenty-three minutes after the shooting. Crew names for private ISR flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so the lists circulating on X are unverified and mutually contradictory. The competing list — the one that names **Cooper Brown** as pilot in command and **Michael Reynolds** as co-pilot — gives **Sarah Kline** as relief and systems operator. Note the spelling: this capture searches "Klein" while the crew list gives "Kline", which is exactly the kind of variance that makes name-based Trends work unreliable.

**September 4, 2025** is six days before the event. That proximity is the claim; the spelling mismatch and the commonness of the name are the counterweights, and a D.C.-market index reaching 100 on a flat baseline can rest on a handful of queries.
