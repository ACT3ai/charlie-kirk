## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Elena Vasquez", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search, with the axis running October 20, 2024 to September 21, 2025. The series shows a single sharp spike in **late October 2024** at the far left of the chart and then flatlines at zero through to late 2025.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for **N1098L**, the Bombardier Global 6500 that dropped to roughly 203 feet near the Utah Valley University campus on the morning of September 10, 2025 and repeated the maneuver about twenty-three minutes after the shooting. Crew names for private ISR flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so the lists circulating on X are unverified and mutually contradictory. One list names Elena Vasquez as the mission engineer aboard the September 10 flight.

This capture pulls in the opposite direction from the ninety-day version of the same query. Read on a twelve-month axis the name registers once, nearly eleven months before the event, and shows nothing at all in the weeks around it. Both captures are preserved because the contradiction is the finding: a Trends index is only meaningful with its window stated, and different windows on the same term can support opposite readings.
