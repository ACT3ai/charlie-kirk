## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Michael Harlan", filtered to **Washington DC (Hagerstown)**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs October 20, 2024 to September 21, 2025. The series is flat at zero except for **two** sharp spikes, both reaching the maximum of 100, and the tooltip is parked on the later one: **August 24-30, 2025**.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for **N1098L**, the Bombardier Global 6500 that dropped to roughly 203 feet near the Utah Valley University campus on the morning of September 10, 2025 and repeated the maneuver about twenty-three minutes after the shooting. Crew names for private ISR flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so the lists circulating on X are unverified and mutually contradictory. One list names **CTP (Ret.) Michael R. Harlan** as pilot in command of the September 10 flight.

Two spikes rather than one is what makes this the most-cited name in the cluster: an isolated peak can be an artifact, a repeated peak on the same rare name in the same bucket is a pattern. The later of the two falls in the last full week of **August 2025**, roughly two weeks before the event; the earlier one is dated in the companion captures to the first week of **December 2024**. Against that, "Michael Harlan" is a common enough name that the series may be catching unrelated people, and Trends discloses no searcher or count.
