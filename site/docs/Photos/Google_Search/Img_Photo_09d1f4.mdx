## What This Image Shows

A desktop **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Hunter Kozak", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs July 18 to October 4 and the tooltip marks **August 31, 2025**.

This is the capture that puts a date on the two mobile screenshots of the same query elsewhere in this cluster. **August 31, 2025** is ten days before the shooting at Utah Valley University — and before that day **Hunter Kozak** was an ordinary student whose name had no public profile. He was mid-question with Charlie Kirk when the shot was fired, which is the only reason anyone knows the name now.

That is the whole of the claim, and its limits are the same as everywhere in this cluster: a Trends reading shows relative interest inside one geographic bucket, not who searched, how many searched, or why, and a name with no baseline can reach the top of its own scale on very few queries. Nothing here suggests Mr. Kozak did anything wrong.
