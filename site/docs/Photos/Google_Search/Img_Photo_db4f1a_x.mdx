## What This Image Shows

Two **Google Trends** comparison panels stacked in one capture, both running "Tony graf" against "Robert lunnen". The upper panel is filtered to **Israel** over **5/1/25 - 10/19/25**; the lower panel is filtered to **Utah** over **1/1/25 - 10/19/25**. Hand-added labels mark peaks at **MAY 15** and **JULY 26** on the Israel panel and at **SEPT 21-27** and **OCT 5-18** on the Utah panel. Both carry the watermark of the X account that circulated them.

Side-by-side geography is the whole argument here. The Utah panel behaves the way an ordinary reader would expect: local interest in local judges rises after the case becomes news in late September and October. The Israel panel is the claim — readings in **May** and **July 2025**, months before [September 10](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), in a bucket with no obvious reason to care about a [Fourth District Court](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo) appointment in Utah County.

The counterweight is that Trends normalizes each panel against its own maximum, so two panels drawn at different scales cannot be compared by eye, and low-volume buckets amplify small query counts. The annotations are the poster's, not Google's. Single-geography versions of the same comparison are at [Israel filter](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_f3a6f5_x), [Utah filter](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_cb859b_x), and [no DC data](/Photos/Google_Search/Img_Photo_686fd2_x).
