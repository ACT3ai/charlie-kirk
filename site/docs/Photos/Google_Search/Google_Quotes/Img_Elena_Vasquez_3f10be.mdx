## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the exact-phrase search term "Elena Vasquez", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs October 27, 2024 to September 28, 2025. The line is flat at zero for the great majority of the timeline, then rises in one sharp triangular spike to **100** and immediately returns to zero. The tooltip reads "**Jun 29 - Jul 5, 2025**".

The name comes from the disputed crew list for [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview); one of the two competing accounts names **Sgt. (Ret.) Elena Vasquez** as the mission engineer and avionics specialist on the [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) flight. Neither list has been confirmed by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service.

Two cross-references matter here. The same week — **June 29 to July 5, 2025** — is where the "Daniel Merrell" capture in the parent cluster peaks, and it sits immediately before the **July 20** anchor date. But this capture also conflicts with the three other captures of this name in the sibling **[N1098L NCSS CIA](/Photos/Google_Search/Google_N1098L_NCSS_CIA/overview)** cluster, which variously place the maximum in **November 2024** and **August 2025**. That spread across four screenshots of one name is the clearest demonstration in this whole cluster of how much a Trends reading depends on the window chosen. Nothing here identifies a searcher, and no wrongdoing by anyone is asserted.
