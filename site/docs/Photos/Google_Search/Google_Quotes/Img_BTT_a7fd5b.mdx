## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "**SU-BTT**", filtered to **Israel** over the custom range **6/11/25 - 9/1/25**, All categories, Web Search. The axis is marked Jun 11, Jul 21, and Aug 30. The line runs flat along the zero baseline, spikes vertically to the maximum of **100**, and drops straight back — and the tooltip fixes the peak precisely: "**Jul 20, 2025**".

This is the strongest single capture in the cluster and arguably in the whole search thread, for two reasons. **SU-BTT** is an Egyptian aircraft registration — a six-character alphanumeric string with essentially no organic search demand anywhere, let alone in one country. And **July 20, 2025** is the exact date the wider investigation treats as its anchor: the week that also carries the AMFEST and Tucker Carlson appearance, a cluster of Timpanogos surgeon queries, and a run of other terms examined across this cluster.

The custom range is also chosen well — it opens June 11 and closes September 1, so the whole chart sits before September 10 by construction and cannot be explained by post-event news interest. The standing limits still apply: Trends reports a normalized index inside one geographic bucket, never a query count, and never who searched or why.
