## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the exact-phrase search term "Michael Harlan", filtered to **Washington DC (Hagerstown)**, **Past 12 months**, All categories, Web Search. The axis runs October 27, 2024 to September 28, 2025. The blue trend line sits flat on the zero baseline for almost the whole year and then rises in one sharp triangular spike to the maximum index of **100** around **mid-February 2025**, before dropping straight back to zero.

The name comes from the disputed crew list for [**N1098L**](/Planes/N1098L/overview), the Bombardier Global 6500 that made two low passes near the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). One of the two competing lists in circulation names **CTP (Ret.) Michael R. Harlan** as pilot in command. Crew names for private intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance flights are not published by the FAA, the operator, or any flight-tracking service, so neither list has been confirmed.

This capture is the exact-phrase counterpart to the five broader captures of the same name in the sibling **[N1098L NCSS CIA](/Photos/Google_Search/Google_N1098L_NCSS_CIA/overview)** cluster, and it does not agree with them: those place the D.C. spikes in **December 2024** and **August 2025**, while this one lands in **February 2025**. Different windows and different query forms produce different maxima, which is exactly why a single Trends screenshot proves very little on its own. Nothing here identifies a searcher or asserts wrongdoing by anyone.
