## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Russell Kim Kennington" filtered to **Israel** over the **Past 5 years**. The axis runs from October 2020 through April 2025, and the tooltip marks a peak in the week of **August 17-23, 2025**.

The five-year window is the point of this particular capture. Where a ninety-day view can make any blip look dramatic, a five-year baseline shows whether a name has ever registered before. Here the line sits at zero across years and then moves once, weeks before [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). Researchers treat that shape — long silence, single move, close to the event — as the pattern worth asking about. It remains an aggregate signal: Trends does not report who searched, how many people searched, or why, and a name with essentially no baseline can be pushed to an index of 100 by a very small number of queries.

**Russell Kennington** appears in this investigation among the figures examined on the [Russell Kennington](/distraction_people/Russell_Kennington) page; nothing on this capture asserts he did anything wrong.
