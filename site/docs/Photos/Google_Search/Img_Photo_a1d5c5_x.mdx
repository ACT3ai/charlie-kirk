## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an MSNBC report on FBI leadership turnover. The article states that "an unprecedented campaign by FBI leaders to force senior bureau officials out of their jobs has disproportionately hit women and people of color, according to public records and an unofficial tally by current and former FBI officials." It reports that "FBI leaders last week forced the resignation of a decorated female Pakistani American counterterrorism agent who was appointed in February to run the Salt Lake City field office," identifying her as **Mehtab Syed**, and says she "is being forced out just six months after being appointed in February, for reasons that are not clear, the sources said."

The report continues that Associate Deputy Director J. William Rivers "told Syed she wasn't a good fit for the office, which covers Utah, Idaho and Montana," and offered her a lower-level position at the FBI facility in Huntsville, Alabama, which she declined in favor of retirement. It notes she did not respond to a request for comment. Every one of those statements is attributed to unnamed current and former officials by the outlet, not established here.

This article sits with the Trends captures because the Salt Lake City field office is the FBI office covering Utah Valley University, and researchers compiled search interest in both the outgoing and incoming special agents in charge. See [Mehtab Syed](/People/mehtab-syed), the [FBI and DPS searches](/GoogleSearches/fbi-dps-law-enforcement-searches) analysis, and the [FBI](/FBI/overview) section.
