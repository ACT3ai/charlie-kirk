## What This Image Shows

A mobile **Google Trends** capture for the search term "Andrew James zenger utah", filtered to **Israel**, **Past 90 days**. The axis runs July 14 to September 30 and the tooltip marks the peak at **August 27, 2025** with a reading of **100**. Below the chart the "Interest by subregion" panel begins to return a "Hmm" message, indicating too little data for a geographic breakdown. The iOS text-selection menu is visible at the top of the screen, showing the capture was taken mid-copy.

The filename this image was archived under identifies the subject as a physician connected to Charlie Kirk's treatment. That places it in the broader medical-search thread, where researchers compiled claimed pre-event interest in **Timpanogos Regional Hospital** and in a number of its emergency and surgical staff — a set of queries in the weeks around **July 20, 2025** that they argue is difficult to explain as coincidence.

The counterweights are unusually strong on this one. The subregion panel failing means the underlying query volume is tiny, the search string is a specific three-part name plus a state, and a peak of 100 in a low-volume bucket can rest on a handful of queries. **August 27, 2025** is two weeks before September 10. No wrongdoing by any physician is asserted, and Trends identifies no searcher.
