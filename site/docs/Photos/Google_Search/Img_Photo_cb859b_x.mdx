## What This Image Shows

A **Google Trends** comparison running "Tony graf" against "Robert lunnen", filtered to **Utah** across the custom range **1/1/25 - 10/19/25**, All categories, Web Search. The tooltip marks the week of **September 21-27, 2025** at a reading of **100**, with the axis running from December 29, 2024 to October 5, 2025. The capture is watermarked with the X handles that circulated it.

This is the control panel for the judge thread, and it behaves exactly as it should. Local interest in two local judges is flat for nine months and then peaks in late September 2025 — after the case became the biggest story in the state and after [**Judge Tony Graf**](/court/judge-tony-graf) was assigned to it. That is news interest following an event, which is what a normal Trends series looks like.

Its value is comparative. Set against the Israel-filtered version of the same query, which the researcher says shows readings in **May** and **July 2025**, the Utah panel establishes what ordinary post-event behavior looks like — and therefore what would make the foreign panel unusual, if the foreign panel is read correctly.
