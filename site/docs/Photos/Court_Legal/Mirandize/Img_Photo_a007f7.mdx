## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a printed filing or transcript page. It records an exchange in which a person identified as **Tyler Robinson** is read his Miranda rights and then invokes his right to counsel and his right to remain silent. Several key passages are picked out in bright translucent light blue.

The exchange this page transcribes is the one quoted in defense filings from exhibit **Bates 003996-R2**: an officer saying "So Tyler, um, before we begin, I'm just gonna read you your rights," and Robinson answering that he would like to speak with legal counsel, naming an attorney he hoped to reach. The reading is logged at **06:25 PM** and the response at **06:26 PM**.

Two details in that exchange are what researchers press on. He asks for a specific named lawyer, and says his family is trying to reach him — language suggesting he had just spoken with his parents. And the reported line that the lawyer's office is "closed for the night" rather than closed for the weekend is the basis for arguing this happened on a Thursday, **September 11**, rather than the Friday. Those are inferences from wording, not proof. Robinson is charged, not convicted.
