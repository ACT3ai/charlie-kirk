## What This Image Shows

A high-resolution image of a single typed page from a court filing, numbered **70**. The page carries a legal argument about the defendant's right to remain silent, followed by a transcribed exchange from a recorded police interview.

The argument on the page is that **Tyler Robinson** invoked both his **Fifth Amendment** right to silence and his **Sixth Amendment** right to counsel during that encounter, and that public statements made afterward by government officials do not match what the recording shows. That is a filing made in court by a party to the case, not a claim posted online.

Why this page sits at the top of the cluster: the encounter it transcribes is the one recorded in discovery exhibit **Bates 003996-R2**, where a rights reading is logged at **06:25 PM** and a request for counsel one minute later. If a defendant invoked counsel and silence at that hour on **September 11, 2025**, the timeline of everything that followed — the public bulletin, the messages, the booking paperwork — has to be re-read against it. Robinson is charged, not convicted. See [Mirandize Evidence](/court/mirandize/mirandize-overview) and [Custody Timeline and Confession](/court/mirandize/custody-timeline-fake-confession).
