## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a Google **AI Overview** panel on a white background, with two small blue Facebook source links embedded in the text.

The panel reads: "Based on the search results, a video shows an encounter involving Tyler Robinson on the evening of September 11, 2025, with a referenced timestamp of 03:36:53 in the document identified as Bates 003996-R2." Its bullets then give: "Context: The document mentions that on the evening of September 11, 2025, a family member of Tyler Robinson contacted the Washington County Sheriff's Office with information that Robinson had confessed to or implied they committed an incident. Encounter: The video at timestamp 03:36:53 shows an in-person encounter with investigators on September 12, 2025. Arrest: The same records indicate Tyler Robinson was arrested for the offense on September 12, 2025, at 4:00 AM."

The same exhibit and the same internal timestamp are placed on two different dates, one bullet apart. That contradiction is the reason this screenshot is filed on its own page. Researchers read it as a sign the public record is genuinely inconsistent about when custody began; the fair counterweight is that an AI overview restates its sources and may simply be reproducing inconsistent reporting rather than inconsistent records. Either way, the underlying question — what date is on the **Bates 003996-R2** video — is exactly what the unedited exhibit and the custody logs would answer. **Tyler Robinson** is charged, not convicted.
