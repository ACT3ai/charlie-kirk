## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of part of a typed filing page: black serif text on white, presenting legal argument and then transcribing a **Miranda** warning. One phrase in the main paragraph is highlighted in translucent light blue. Below it, a timestamp is enclosed in a red rectangle drawn by whoever shared the page.

The red box around the timestamp is the entire point of this crop. Everything in this cluster turns on a number printed in a filing, and this is the version that isolates it so a reader can see the figure the argument rests on rather than take it on trust.

The timestamp in question corresponds to **06:25 PM**, tied to internal video position **03:36:53** in exhibit **Bates 003996-R2**. The document does not carry a date on its face, which is the gap the rest of the argument works to close — through the arresting officer's travel time, through the wording about the lawyer's office, and through the booking sheet entries. That gap is why the date remains contested rather than settled. Robinson is charged, not convicted.
