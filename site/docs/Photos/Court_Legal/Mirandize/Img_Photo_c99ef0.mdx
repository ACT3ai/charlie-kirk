## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a block of informational text on a solid dark grey background, laid out like a search result snippet. It sets out the legal meaning of an **arrest** under Utah state law and cites the statutory section.

This is the definitional page in the cluster, and it is there because the whole dispute is a definitional one. The official record gives a formal arrest on **September 12, 2025**. The filings place a Miranda reading and an invocation of counsel at **6:25 PM on September 11**. If a person is being read their rights and questioned, the question of whether they are legally under arrest at that moment is not rhetorical — it has a statutory answer.

Utah law generally treats an arrest as a restraint of a person's liberty to answer for an offense, which is why a rights reading is such a strong indicator of custody. The counterpoint filed elsewhere in this cluster argues from the other direction: a person may lawfully be held for up to 24 hours on an investigative hold before rights are read, so a delay by itself proves nothing. Both propositions are about law, not about anyone's conduct. Robinson is charged, not convicted.
