## What This Image Shows

The same two search terms, **Tony graf** and **Robert lunnen**, run again with the region set to **District of Columbia** and the range **1/1/25 to 10/19/25**. Instead of a chart, the page returns: "Hmm, your search doesn't have enough data to show here. Please make sure everything is spelled correctly, or try a more general term." The device status bar and the **[@BasedSamParker](/Influencers/x/based-sam-parker)** watermark are visible.

This is a negative result, and the researcher kept it. Interest in these names from the District of Columbia over ten months is below the threshold Trends will even display.

A null result is worth as much as a spike, and including it is the honest thing to do. It also sets the scale for everything else in this set: on terms this obscure, the tool cannot distinguish a few searches from none, which is exactly the sensitivity that makes single spikes elsewhere hard to interpret.
