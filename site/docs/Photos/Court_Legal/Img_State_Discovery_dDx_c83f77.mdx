## What This Image Shows

A scan of a printed court exhibit page labelled **Exhibit E**. The heading reads **List of Law Enforcement Agencies Producing Discovery to Utah State Bureau of Investigation (as of March 26, 2026)**. Below it, a numbered list of agencies runs down two asymmetrical columns of black serif text on white.

This is one page that answers a question the rest of the archive keeps asking: how many separate agencies hold material in this case. The answer is a list long enough to need two columns, still being compiled more than eighteen months after **September 10, 2025**, and routed through the **Utah State Bureau of Investigation** rather than produced directly.

That structure is what makes the discovery fight in this case slow and what makes records requests to any single agency incomplete. Researchers point to it when explaining why a **GRAMA** response from one department can honestly say it holds nothing while material exists elsewhere. The exhibit is an inventory, not an accusation; nothing on it suggests any agency withheld anything.
