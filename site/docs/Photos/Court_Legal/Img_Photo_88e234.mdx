## What This Image Shows

The same **Google Trends** comparison — **Tony graf** against **Robert lunnen**, region **Utah**, range **1/1/25 to 10/19/25** — captured with the tooltip open on a different week: **Oct 12 - 18, 2025**. The **[@BasedSamParker](/Influencers/x/based-sam-parker)** watermark sits near the centre.

That week is when the case assignment was reported. The **KUTV** article filed elsewhere in this cluster, headlined that a new Utah judge had taken the death penalty case, is dated **October 15, 2025** and updated **October 16** — inside this exact window.

So this panel pairs a search peak with a specific, identifiable news event on the same days, which is the cleanest illustration in the set of how ordinary coverage drives these curves. Readers should carry that lesson into the Israel panels rather than away from them: a spike needs a candidate explanation before it needs a theory. No claim of wrongdoing is made against either judge. See [Google Searches](/GoogleSearches/overview) and [Media Coverage](/Media/overview).
