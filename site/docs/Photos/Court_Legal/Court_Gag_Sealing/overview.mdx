## About This Cluster

This cluster documents the secrecy fights inside the Utah case against [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson). The image here is a screenshot of an X post describing a gag order in the case, over a courtroom still frame showing a judge at the bench, an American flag, and an inset booking photo of a young man presented as Robinson. It captures, in one frame, the two forces the cluster is about: a court closing the record, and the public reacting to that closure.

According to reporting, the presiding judge issued a broad gag order in December 2025 on his own motion, without either the prosecution or the defense requesting it, restricting witnesses, attorneys, and media from speaking about the case. Separately, a defense motion referencing an ATF report on a bullet-jacket fragment was sealed, and a coalition of news organizations moved to unseal it, arguing the public has a right to see evidence the defense itself called non-incriminating.

Critics reportedly frame these steps as First Amendment concerns and question the timing; defenders note that sealing and gag orders are routine in high-profile prosecutions. The competing views are reported here rather than resolved. Open the image for its full write-up, and see the written record linked below.
