## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a legislative tracking page showing the **History** table for the bill: three columns of Date, Chamber and Action, listing every recorded step in reverse chronological order.

The table runs from **2025-01-09** through **2025-03-26**. The Senate entries cluster through January and February — readings, committee referrals, floor votes — then the bill moves to the House on **2025-02-19**, passes third reading in the House on **2025-02-26**, returns to the Senate, is signed by the Speaker and the President, goes to enrolling, and on **2025-03-26** the final line reads "Governor Signed in Lieutenant Governor's office for filing."

The full history is here because a completed, unremarkable legislative record is itself the answer to a question. The bill moved on an ordinary schedule through an ordinary process, bipartisan, over ten weeks, months before anyone could have known what would happen at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). That is worth showing plainly. The consequence for this investigation — a narrower public record on the medical side — is real regardless of how the law came to be.
