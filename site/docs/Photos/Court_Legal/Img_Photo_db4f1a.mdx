## What This Image Shows

A composite inside a bright blue border showing two **Google Trends** comparisons of the same pair of search terms, **Tony graf** and **Robert lunnen**. The top panel is filtered to **Israel** over **5/1/25 to 10/19/25**, with markers annotated **MAY 15** and **JULY 26**. The bottom panel is filtered to **Utah** over **1/1/25 to 10/19/25**, with markers annotated **SEPT 21-27** and **OCT 5-18**. Both carry the **@BasedSamParker** handles.

The researcher's argument is in the contrast between the panels. Utah interest spikes in late September and October 2025 — after the assassination, and around the case assignment, which is what ordinary news-driven search looks like. The Israel panel spikes in **May** and **July** 2025 — before the assassination, and around the appointment and the run-up to the swearing-in.

The limits are serious and have to be stated. Google Trends reports relative, normalized interest rather than counts; for names with almost no baseline traffic a very small number of queries can register as a peak. The May window is also when the appointment was announced and covered, which is an ordinary reason for interest anywhere. The chart identifies no searcher and no purpose, and neither judge is accused of anything. See [Google Searches](/GoogleSearches/overview), [Israel](/Israel/overview), and [Judges](/court/judges).
