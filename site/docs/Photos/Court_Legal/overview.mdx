## About This Cluster

This cluster is the paper trail. Almost every image here is a filing, a docket screenshot, a statute page, or a records exhibit from *State of Utah v. Tyler James Robinson*, **Case No. 251403576**, in Utah's Fourth District Court before **Judge Tony F. Graf, Jr.** The prosecution's case is where the official account of **September 10, 2025** is written down, dated, and signed, which makes it the one place where the official account can be checked against itself. That is why researchers photograph filings rather than summarize them: the **Information** filed **September 16, 2025**, the **Order to Hold Without Bail** signed **September 12, 2025**, the stipulated motions over preserving evidence, and the motions to classify hearing transcripts and audio as private all carry dates and docket numbers that either line up with the timeline or do not.

Three threads run through the images. The first is **secrecy** — a stipulated motion asks the court to seal the transcript and audio of the **October 24, 2025** hearing as "private," and a screenshot of a folder holding 98 downloaded Robinson court PDFs shows how much of the record citizen researchers have had to assemble themselves; see [Gag Orders and Sealing](/court/gag-orders-sealing) and [Discovery and Brady Disputes](/court/discovery-brady-disputes). The second is the **judge**. **Judge Robert C. Lunnen** retired effective **August 1, 2025**, and Governor **Spencer Cox** appointed Graf to the vacancy on **May 2, 2025**; Graf was sworn in **August 4, 2025**, weeks before the case landed on his bench. Researcher **@BasedSamParker** posted Google Trends comparisons of "Tony graf" and "Robert lunnen" — including one filtered to **Israel** — arguing the search spikes are worth explaining. Those are search-interest charts, not evidence of anyone's conduct, and no claim of wrongdoing by either judge has been established. The third thread is **statute**: Utah **SB0082, Autopsy Photo Amendments**, signed **March 26, 2025** and effective **May 7, 2025**, made it a class B misdemeanor to share medical-examiner autopsy photographs, which researchers say narrows what the public can ever see of the autopsy record.

Start with the case filings themselves, then the judge-vacancy and Google Trends set, then the SB0082 legislative history. The [Mirandize](/Photos/Court_Legal/Mirandize/overview) sub-cluster is the deepest thread in this area and deserves its own pass — it is the fight over whether **Tyler Robinson** was read his rights at 6:25 PM on **September 11, 2025**, hours before the Discord message the state calls a confession. For the written analysis behind all of it, see [Court and Trial](/court/overview), [Legal](/Legal/overview), [Case Overview](/court/case-overview), [Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf), and [Judge Robert Lunnen](/court/judge-robert-lunnen).
