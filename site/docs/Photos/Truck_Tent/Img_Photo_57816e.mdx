## What This Image Shows

A heavily edited video still, presented as an overlay composition. On the left is a man in a white shirt and cap; on the right, a man in a black shirt and sunglasses standing in front of a blue banner. A bright red line with an arrowhead is drawn diagonally across the frame, running from a camera setup at the left toward the banner at the right, with several lines of yellow text added by the analyst.

The visible annotation includes the phrase **30 frames per second**, which identifies what is being argued: a frame-by-frame timing analysis, where the arrow is a proposed sight line or direction rather than anything visible in the original footage.

Frame-rate arguments run through the whole case, because the interval between the visible impact and the audible report is what several analysts use to argue the shot came from far closer than the roof position in the official account. Those arguments are laid out in [Tent Shooter Theories](/Tent/Tent_Shooter_Theories) and weighed against the official ballistics in [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview). None of them has been confirmed by any forensic authority.
