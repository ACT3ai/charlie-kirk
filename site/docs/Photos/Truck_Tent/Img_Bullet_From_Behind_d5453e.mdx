## What This Image Shows

A heavily pixelated close-up of a person in a white baseball cap, taken from crowd or event footage. A large red digital arrow has been superimposed pointing from right to left toward the person's face. Yellow watermark text for an analysis site runs across the lower left.

The file was saved under a name referring to a shot from behind, which is the argument the arrow belongs to: several researchers contend that the direction of the wound and the reaction of people around **Charlie Kirk** are more consistent with a shot originating behind the stage than with the roof position in the official account. That is a contested reading of low-resolution footage, advanced by private analysts, and no forensic body has endorsed it.

The arrow is drawn on. It marks the direction the annotator wants the viewer to consider and carries no evidentiary weight by itself. Read it against the official ballistic account in [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview), the wound analysis in [Cause of Death](/Cause_of_Death/overview), and the close-range arguments in [Tent Shooter Theories](/Tent/Tent_Shooter_Theories).
