## What This Image Shows

A two-panel composite. The left panel is a blurry, pixelated close-up of a pale grey concrete or tiled surface crossed by thin dark lines, with a small reddish-brown cylindrical object sitting on it. Two bright blue three-dimensional arrows point diagonally down and left, a thick semi-circular white line frames the right of the scene, and hand-lettered captions read **SHOT CAME FROM HERE** and **SHELL CASING**. The right panel is promotional artwork for a **CornerShot** weapon system: a pistol on a hinged frame with a red-dot sight and an integrated video monitor showing a tactical scenario.

The argument, advanced publicly by **@ProjectConstitu** with **@stevendenoon**, is that the small object is an ejected cartridge case that came to rest on the courtyard surface at close range, and that a hinged corner-firing weapon would explain both a concealed firing position behind the canopy and a downward ejection.

Every load-bearing element of that reading is annotation. The object is small, out of focus and identified only by the caption beside it; the arrows are drawn; the weapon panel is a manufacturer image, not a photograph of anything at the scene. No such weapon has been recovered, no forensic examination has been published, and the claim is disputed. The surface it rests on was excavated and paved four days later, so it cannot now be re-examined.
