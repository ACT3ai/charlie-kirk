## What This Image Shows

A mobile browser screenshot of the **probable cause statement and arrest affidavit** in the case, showing two pages of the document against a grey background with the browser address bar still visible at the top. The text sets out the investigation into the shooting and names **Tyler Robinson** as the suspect.

This document is the source of nearly every fact repeated in coverage of the case: the 8:29 a.m. arrival in a gray Dodge Challenger, the 11:49 and 11:50 a.m. neighborhood and campus sightings, the stiff right leg, the stairwell and parking garage frames, the entry to the **Losee Center**, the roof at 12:15, the shot at about 12:23 p.m. on **September 10, 2025**, and the dark item placed on the rooftop before the descent. It is also where the arrest data researchers dispute appears. Attorney **Baron Coleman** has argued from the inmate booking sheet and probable cause statement that the arrest date is recorded as **September 11 at 10 p.m.** by a State Bureau of Investigation officer, which he says cannot be reconciled with an account of a manhunt ending the following day.

A probable cause statement records what an officer swore in order to justify an arrest; it is an allegation, not a finding, and Robinson has pleaded not guilty.
