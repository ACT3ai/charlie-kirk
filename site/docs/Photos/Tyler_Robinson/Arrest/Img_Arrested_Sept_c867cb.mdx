## What This Image Shows

A dark-mode explainer graphic pairing a portrait with a cropped screenshot of **Discord** messages and blocks of text setting out the case for the confession message being what it appears to be. The archived filename records the argument the graphic is answering: an arrest at **6:25 p.m. on September 11**.

The dispute in one paragraph. Attorney **[Baron Coleman](/Influencers/podcasts-baron)** argues from a defense-filed motion referencing a Bates-stamped interview video at a **6:25 p.m.** timestamp that **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)** was already in custody and had asked for a lawyer roughly ninety minutes before the Discord post attributed to his account, and that Washington County protocol is to take a suspect's phone once he is in custody and Mirandized. He points to the transcript line about a lawyer's office being closed for the night rather than the weekend, and to a booking sheet recording an arrest at 10 p.m. on September 11 by an officer from northern Utah. On the other side, an investigative-hold explanation accounts for the paperwork without moving the arrest, and the state's timeline stands.

This graphic argues one side; it is advocacy assembled from crops, and neither position has been ruled on.
