## About This Cluster

The alleged Discord "confession" is one of the most contested items in the entire case. Investigators say **[Tyler Robinson's](/People/tyler-robinson)** roommate showed them Discord messages on a phone screen, and the Utah State Bureau of Investigation later sought Discord's records for usernames including "Craftopia." Discord itself publicly pushed back on the idea that any plot was organized on its platform.

The two images here document that corporate response: a **Headline USA** article by **Ken Silva**, dated **September 13, 2025**, headlined "Gaming Platform Discord Says Tyler Robinson Didn't Plot Kirk's Assassination There," and a highlighted excerpt noting that investigators did find an account linked to him. Researchers add a timing problem — the confession message appeared at 7:57 p.m. on September 11, while court documents cited by attorney [Baron Coleman](/Influencers/podcasts-baron) indicate Robinson had been read his Miranda rights at a sheriff's office at 6:25 p.m., without phone access under standard custody protocol. If both times are right, critics argue, someone else posted the message.

Read both article images here, then see [Confession Discord](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Img_Confession_Discord_c820f0) and [Confession After Custody](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Img_Confession_After_Custody_9ca555) in the parent cluster for the messages themselves.
