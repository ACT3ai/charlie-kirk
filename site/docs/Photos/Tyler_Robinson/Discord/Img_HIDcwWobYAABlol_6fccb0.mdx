## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a news article published **September 13, 2025**, three days after the shooting, headlined that the gaming platform **Discord** says **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)** did not plot **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)**'s assassination there. The subheader quotes the company's statement directly, and the page carries a byline and the Discord logo.

This is a significant and under-weighted item. The platform that held the account attributed to the alleged confession stated publicly, within three days, that its records did not show planning on its service. That does not contradict the existence of a post after the fact, but it does cut against the framing that circulated early, in which Discord was described as the venue where the act was organized. Investigation records separately show the Utah State Bureau of Investigation later sought Discord records by warrant for usernames including Craftopia, Gamin and The Corruption, and a TMZ report noted that Discord removed the account.

A company statement is the company's position, not an audited finding, and platforms speak carefully in the days after an incident.
