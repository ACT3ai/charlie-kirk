## What This Image Shows

This screenshot reproduces the two paragraphs of the **Discord** statement itself, with thin red underlines added by the person circulating it to draw attention to specific phrases.

The statement is the company's account of what its own records show about the account attributed to **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)**, issued in the days after **[September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025)**. Read closely, statements of this kind are usually narrower than the headline they generate — they typically address what was found on the service rather than making claims about anything outside it. The underlining is one reader's judgment about which qualifications matter most, and reading the unmarked paragraphs first is the better approach.

A platform statement is not evidence in the case and has not been tested; the warrant sought later for Discord records suggests investigators were not treating the matter as closed.
