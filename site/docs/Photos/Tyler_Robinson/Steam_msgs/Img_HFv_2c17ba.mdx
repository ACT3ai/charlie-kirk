## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of a paragraph from a court document, setting out argument about what material and which witnesses must be disclosed to the defense in the prosecution of [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson). The final sentence of the paragraph is highlighted in light blue by the person circulating it.

Disclosure is where a pre-trial case is actually fought, and it is where several of the sharpest questions in this one sit. A discovery filing reproduced at [more](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/more/overview) states that the state had provided its discoverable material with the exception of a photograph of a burnt note supplied on January 9, 2026. A separate prosecution filing stated the state did not intend to offer material obtained through forensic extractions of the seized electronics — while the extraction of the roommate's phone was confirmed in open court. Filings like this one are how those tensions become visible.

A highlighted sentence is one reader's emphasis, and a filing states a party's position rather than a finding. Read [Discovery and Brady Disputes](/court/discovery-brady-disputes), [Preliminary Hearing Motions](/court/preliminary-hearing-motions) and [Case Overview](/court/case-overview).
