## What This Image Shows

This is the document at the center of the custody-timing dispute. The page is from a court filing that references discovery material and a Bates-stamped video, and it reproduces a transcribed exchange in which [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson) is read his Miranda rights and then asks to speak with counsel. A single line, **06:25 PM** followed by a running video time, is highlighted in light blue.

Attorney [**Baron Coleman**](/Influencers/podcasts-baron)'s argument runs directly off this page. The filing gives a timestamp but no date, and he argues the date must be [**September 11, 2025**](/After/Sept_11_Surrender_And_Custody), pointing to the transcribed remark that the lawyer's office is closed for the night rather than for the weekend — the 12th was a Friday, the 11th a Thursday — and to the same passage indicating the speaker had just spoken with his parents. He pairs that with the inmate booking sheet recording an arrest at 10 p.m. on September 11 by a State Bureau of Investigation officer who would have needed to leave northern Utah hours earlier. If that reading is right, Robinson was in custody and had invoked counsel roughly ninety minutes before the [Discord message](/Tyler_Robinson/Discord_Messages) attributed to his account was posted.

This is one attorney's reading of a document, contested by an investigative-hold explanation and unresolved by any court. What is not in dispute is that the timestamp is printed on the page.
