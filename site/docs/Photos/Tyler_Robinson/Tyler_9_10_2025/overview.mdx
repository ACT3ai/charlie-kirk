## About This Cluster

This cluster is anchored to a single question about [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025): could one person have covered the ground the surveillance record says he covered, in the time the timestamps allow? The charging timeline has the shot fired at about [**12:23 p.m.**](/Timeline/12-23-shooting-time) from the roof of the [**Losee Center**](/UVU/Losee_Center) at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview), then has the suspect stand, run north, place a dark item on the rooftop, lower himself to the grass and run toward the parking lot. Every second of that sequence is checkable against camera timestamps, which is why researchers keep returning to it.

The image filed here is the composite of surveillance stills circulated by the researcher known as [Gray Hughes](/People/gray-hughes) Investigates, running from 12:23:29 to 12:23:54 and mapping the path from the shooting position to the northeast corner of the Losee Center — twenty-five seconds of elapsed time across that distance. The argument attached to it is that the movement is fast for the route and the obstacles involved, and that the frames deserve independent measurement rather than acceptance. It is a claim from published stills by a citizen investigator, not a finding by an expert witness or a court, and the state has not been asked to answer it on the record. What the composite does establish is that the timestamps exist and can be read.

Open the composite and read each stamp against the position it labels before reading the conclusion drawn from it. Then go to the full written page at [Tyler Robinson on September 10, 2025](/Tyler_Robinson/Tyler_9_10_2025), which sets out the movement analysis in detail. The surrounding route and campus imagery is in [Walking](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Walking/overview) and [On Campus](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/On_Campus/overview), and the wider physical objections are collected at [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview).
