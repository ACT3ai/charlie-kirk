## What This Image Shows

A composite of surveillance camera stills from **9/10/2025**, carrying visible timestamps that run from **12:23:29** to **12:23:54** and mapping the path from the shooting position to the northeast corner of the [**Losee Center**](/UVU/Losee_Center) building — twenty-five seconds of elapsed time.

The charging documents have the shot fired at about 12:23 p.m., the suspect standing and running north to the roof edge, and a dark item placed on the rooftop at 12:24 before he lowers himself to the grass and runs toward the parking lot. This composite covers that exact window second by second, which is what makes it useful: it is one of the few places where the official account can be checked against a clock rather than against a narrative. The researcher who assembled it, posting as [Gray Hughes](/People/gray-hughes) Investigates, argues the movement is fast for the route and the obstacles involved and should be measured independently.

That is a citizen investigator's claim from published stills, not an expert finding, and the state has not been asked to answer it on the record. Read the full analysis at [Tyler Robinson on September 10, 2025](/Tyler_Robinson/Tyler_9_10_2025), then the route imagery at [Walking](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Walking/overview) and the wider objections at [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview).
