## What This Image Shows

A still frame of a printed customer receipt from **Cowboy's Smokehouse**. The legible fields are **Check #211**, **Table 24**, a date of **9/10/25**, an order time of **8:55 PM**, a card payment time of **9:47 PM**, and a total of **$38.51**. The server's name and the card number are blurred in the source video.

Citizen investigators circulating it present the receipt as a possible alibi for [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson) on the night of the assassination at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). The argument, which belongs to the people making it, is that if the document were authenticated and the payment card matched to Robinson, it would be difficult to reconcile with the late-night text-message timeline built from [**Lance Twiggs**](/People/lance-twiggs)' phone, whose first recovered message is timed at 11 p.m. on September 10.

Nothing here is established. The receipt has not been authenticated, the customer has not been identified, and the drive-time reasoning attached to it is the poster's assertion; neither party has adopted it in a filing. It is worth attention because it is checkable — a restaurant's own transaction record and the card processor's data would resolve it quickly.
