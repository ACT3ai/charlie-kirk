## What This Image Shows

A social-media graphic pairs a warm portrait — a young man smiling in a dark teal patterned button-up shirt, blurred red rock formations behind him — with a black caption box across the lower half. The caption states that **Tyler Robinson** has been arrested for the murder of **Charlie Kirk**, and describes him as a 22-year-old white male from Utah raised in a conservative Christian household and introduced to guns at an early age by his parents.

The caption is a good example of how quickly a biography was written in the days after the shooting. Robinson has been charged, not convicted; he has pleaded not guilty, and the case remains pre-trial, so the flat assertion in the caption goes past what the record supports. The biographical detail is also contested at the source: his grandmother is quoted in published reporting saying there is no way he could be violent and no way he did it, and family members have told researchers directly that they do not believe he did this.

The portrait itself is a personal photograph used widely in coverage.
