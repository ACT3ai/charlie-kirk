## About This Cluster

The Robinson family's position is one of the most under-covered facts in the public record of this case. The dominant media account is that his parents recognized him and turned him in; researchers who attended the hearings report that the family told them directly they do not believe he did it, and investigation notes record a relative saying Robinson has told people he did not do it but knows who did and will not say because it would put the family at risk. That is hearsay passed through more than one person and should be treated as such, but it is on the record and it conflicts with the settled story.

The image in this cluster is a vertical stack of three courtroom video screenshots from a hearing, with six color-coded numeric annotations superimposed to mark individual people seated in the gallery and at the tables across the three moments. The annotations are the poster's way of tracking who is present and how positions and expressions change between frames; no one in the gallery is named here, and no conduct is alleged against anyone in the room. What the frames document is attendance and demeanor at a public proceeding, which is exactly the kind of thing citizen researchers use to check reporting about who supports whom.

Open the composite and read the three panels in order before reading the annotations, so the numbering does not drive what you see. Then read [Family](/Tyler_Robinson/Family) for the written account, [Personality](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Personality/overview) for the published relative statements, and [Surrender](/Tyler_Robinson/Surrender) for the turn-in narrative the family disputes. Hearing coverage and filings are in [Court](/court/overview) and [Hearing Witnesses](/court/hearing-witnesses).
