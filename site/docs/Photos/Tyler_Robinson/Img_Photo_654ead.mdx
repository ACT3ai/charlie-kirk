## What This Image Shows

A high-angle camera looks across a full asphalt parking lot at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview), with a green slope, mature trees and campus buildings rising behind it. The frame carries a watermark reading [**BLAKEBEDNARZ**](/People/blake-bednarz) in the corner and a stamp reading PM 05:00 DEC 17 2025 along the bottom, with a small picture-in-picture window at lower right — the signature of a recorded video review of campus footage rather than a raw camera export.

Parking-lot coverage is central to one of the plainest gaps in the official timeline. The charging documents record an arrival at **8:29 a.m.** on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) in a gray Dodge Challenger, but researchers have noted that no departure from that lot is given, and have asked why a timeline assembled with camera and cell data would leave that blank. Frames like this establish what the campus cameras could see and from what angle, which is the prerequisite for judging what should have been captured.

The December 2025 stamp is the date of the review, not the date of the underlying footage, and should not be read as a date of the events. Compare with the campus frames in [On Campus](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/On_Campus/overview) and the route stills in [Walking](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Walking/overview), then read [Cameras](/cameras/overview) and [Tyler Robinson on September 10, 2025](/Tyler_Robinson/Tyler_9_10_2025).
