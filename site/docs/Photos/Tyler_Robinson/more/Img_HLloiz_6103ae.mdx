## What This Image Shows

A page from a legal or investigative document describing a search warrant obtained for the cellphone belonging to [**Lance Twiggs**](/People/lance-twiggs), with the lower paragraph highlighted in light cyan.

This is the warrant that produced the material everyone has quoted. Prosecutors confirmed in open court that a [**Cellebrite**](/court/Cellebrite) extraction was performed on Twiggs' phone, that an agent reviewed it, and that it recovered messages exchanged with [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson) on September 10 and 11, 2025, with the first timed at 11 p.m. on September 10. The peculiarity researchers keep returning to is that a separate prosecution filing said the state did not intend to offer material obtained through forensic extractions of the electronics seized in the case, which sits awkwardly beside a public narrative built substantially on that extraction.

The fair counterweight is that Cellebrite is the industry standard tool, extractions are hash-verified, the defense holds the full device image and could re-process it with a different vendor's tool, and the defense is not alleging fabrication.
