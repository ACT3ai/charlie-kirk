## What This Image Shows

An overhead photograph shows a bolt-action rifle with a mounted telescopic scope lying inside an open, plain light-brown cardboard box set on green grass. A concentrated flashlight beam picks the weapon out of the dark, and the toe of a dark shoe is visible at the upper left of the frame. The rifle has a dark stock with a ribbed rubber recoil pad, a lighter metal barrel and action, a closed bolt handle, and a scope mounted in black rings.

The weapon named in the charging documents is a [**Mauser Model 98**](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle) in .30-06 Springfield, bolt-action, with a scope, reported recovered in a wooded area north of the [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) campus after the shooting. Its dimensions are what make it central to the argument over whether one person could have carried it onto campus unseen: roughly 43 to 49 inches assembled, and still about 28 to 32 inches as a barreled action once the stock is removed, with removal requiring a screwdriver rather than a tool-free takedown.

What this photograph does not establish is where it was taken or by whom, and readers should not assume it is a forensic-response photograph unless a filing says so.
