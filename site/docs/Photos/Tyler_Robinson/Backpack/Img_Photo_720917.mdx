## What This Image Shows

A product-style close-up of a light blue **JanSport** backpack carried mid-stride, with a light blue water bottle in the side pocket, against a banded wall. The brand mark is legible on the pack.

This is a reference photograph rather than proof, and its job is to fix real dimensions in the reader's mind. Investigation notes give the usable internal length of the pack at about **17 inches**. The recovered weapon is a **[Mauser Model 98](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle)** in .30-06 with a mounted scope: roughly 43 to 49 inches assembled, and still about **28 to 32 inches** as a barreled action once the stock is removed — a job that requires a screwdriver, since the rifle is not a tool-free takedown design. Researchers argue from those figures that the rifle could not have travelled inside this pack, and that the state's account therefore needs either another person or a weapon already on site.

The state has adopted neither alternative, no court has ruled on the geometry, and the defense has not publicly staked its case on this argument.
