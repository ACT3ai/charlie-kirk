## What This Image Shows

A single paragraph of analysis arguing that the accepted narrative about [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson) and [**Lance Twiggs**](/People/lance-twiggs) does not hold together on its own terms — that the described sequence of reactions and the timing attached to them are inconsistent.

This is the compressed form of a longer critique that runs through the investigation record. The list of tensions raised there includes a writer worrying about retrieving a rifle so as to leave no trace while producing a long written trail, apologizing for involving the roommate while involving him, saying he hoped to take the secret to his grave shortly before an alleged public confession, and refusing to answer police questions immediately after allegedly confessing to family, a bishop and an online group. Separately, the roommate is reported to have said Robinson never mentioned [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) or politics much, which is a genuine tension with a confession thread.

These are arguments about coherence, not proof of fabrication, and none of them has been adopted by the defense in a public filing or ruled on by a court.
