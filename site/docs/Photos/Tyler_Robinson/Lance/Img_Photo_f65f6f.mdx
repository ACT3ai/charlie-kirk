## What This Image Shows

This analysis screenshot compares how the same alleged confession is worded in different documents. It sets the language of an indictment against the language of search warrants and against the **Discord** post attributed to the account **Zealous Monkey**, noting differences in wording and capitalization, and names **Justin Bowen**, the State Bureau of Investigation investigator who signed affidavits in the case.

Comparing quoted text across filings is legitimate document work, because a quotation that changes between documents is either a transcription artifact or a sign that the documents are drawing on different sources. The specific claim here is that the surrender language and the confession language do not match cleanly across the paperwork.

Whether the differences are meaningful cannot be judged from a cropped screenshot, the author's conclusions are unverified, and no court has been asked to rule on the point.
