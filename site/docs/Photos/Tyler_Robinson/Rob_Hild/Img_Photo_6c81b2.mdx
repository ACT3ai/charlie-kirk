## What This Image Shows

A split-screen graphic on a pale olive background. On the left is the stairwell surveillance frame — a man descending concrete steps in a light blue cap, sunglasses, a dark long-sleeved shirt with a chest graphic and blue jeans. On the right is a low-angle selfie of three men on what appears to be the same staircase, with two of them circled in bright red. A personal name is overlaid in large white text along the bottom.

The underlying activity is legitimate and useful. After the surveillance stills were published, event attendees went to the locations and re-photographed them from the same positions, which fixes camera angles, step geometry and sightlines that cannot be inferred from a cropped frame. The companion image in this cluster carries the original social post, whose caption describes retracing the steps and photographing Converse-brand footprints — footwear being part of the affidavit's own description.

The red circles and the overlaid name are the graphic maker's annotations. Nobody in the selfie has been identified by any investigator, agency or court as connected to the events of [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), being photographed at a public staircase proves nothing, and this site makes no allegation about anyone pictured.
