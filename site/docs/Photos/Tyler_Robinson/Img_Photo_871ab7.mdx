## What This Image Shows

This screenshot captures an X post that quotes and responds to a post from the [**FBI Salt Lake City**](/FBI/SLC_Field_Office) account dated [**September 11**](/After/Sept_11_Surrender_And_Custody), announcing the release of video of the alleged shooter. Embedded in the quoted post is a low-resolution security camera frame showing a distant figure running across a grassy campus area, with a low institutional building and large trees in the shot.

The FBI release is a fixed point in the public timeline. It is the moment the federal government put a suspect image into circulation, and it is what the neighborhood and doorbell footage collected across this cluster was compared against in the hours that followed. It is also the image quality that the entire identification dispute has to work with — a running figure at distance, on consumer-grade video, in which build and gait are more legible than face.

The commentary layered on top by the reposting account is that user's opinion and is not adopted here. Compare this frame against the closer stills in [Walking](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Walking/overview) and [Stairs](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Stairs/overview), and read the identification arguments at [Stairs Guy Identity Gap](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Stairs_Guy_Identity_Gap) and the federal-conduct material at [FBI](/FBI/overview).
