## What This Image Shows

This is the BBC's item-by-item breakdown of the suspect description, headed Shooting suspect's distinctive clothing. A surveillance photograph of the figure descending stairs sits at the center, with pointer lines running out to close-ups and stock images.

The labels read that the cap featured a triangular logo, that the black T-shirt carried a US flag and eagle and was similar to clothing easily available online, that the shirt bore the words **Land of the Free, Home of the Brave**, and that Converse trainers left an imprint in the ground. The image credit reads Images: FBI, eBay — meaning the surveillance frame came from the federal release and the garment comparisons came from a retail listing. That is worth noticing, because the phrase easily available online is doing a quiet job in both directions: it tells a reader the shirt is not rare, which cuts against treating it as a unique identifier.

The underlying description matches the charging affidavit almost word for word, including the Converse-style shoe impression. What a news graphic cannot do is establish who was wearing the clothing.
