## What This Image Shows

This is the clothing-change argument reduced to one graphic. Two frames from the same street camera are placed side by side. The left, labeled **8:07 AM**, shows a person walking left to right in a reddish T-shirt and dark shorts, enclosed in a green automated tracking box. The right, labeled **11:49 AM**, shows a person walking the opposite direction in a dark top, blue jeans and a blue cap.

The state reads this as one person who changed clothes. The 8:07 a.m. stamp is itself notable, since the affidavit-derived timeline that circulates most widely begins with an **8:29 a.m.** campus arrival, so this frame sits earlier than the published start of the sequence. The 11:49 a.m. stamp matches the affidavit's neighborhood sighting on **September 10, 2025**.

The green box is an automated detection drawn by video analytics software rather than a human annotation, which is worth knowing given open questions about what analytics tools were used on this footage and under what authority.
