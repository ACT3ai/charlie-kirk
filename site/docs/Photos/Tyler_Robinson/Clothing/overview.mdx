## About This Cluster

Clothing is the single most contested strand of the case against **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)**, who is charged in the **[September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025)** killing of **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) and has pleaded not guilty. Surveillance stills referenced in the charging affidavit put the rooftop suspect in a black long-sleeve shirt carrying an American flag and eagle graphic, dark jeans, large sunglasses, a cap and a dark backpack, with a shoe impression described by investigators as consistent with Converse or Chuck Taylor sneakers. The same documents place Robinson arriving on campus at 8:29 a.m. in a plain maroon T-shirt, light shorts, light shoes and a black hat with a white logo. The images gathered here are the raw material for the question the state and its critics answer differently: is that one person who changed clothes, or two people?

The state's account is a mid-morning wardrobe change, and news graphics such as the BBC and Sky News breakdowns collected here reproduce the official description item by item. Researchers pressing the other reading point to details inside these same frames — the cap changes color between sequences, the backpack in the stairwell stills reads as full while the light blue JanSport carried from the parking lot reads as roughly half empty, and swapping shorts for jeans should make a pack emptier rather than fuller. They also argue that a **[Mauser Model 98](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle)** in .30-06, roughly 43 to 49 inches assembled and still about 28 to 32 inches as a barreled action, is difficult to reconcile with the pack and the clothing visible in the frames. None of this has been tested in court, and the defense has not publicly adopted every version of the argument; treat each claim as a reported claim rather than a finding.

Start with the news-graphic breakdowns and the annotated stairwell frames, then compare them against the shoe and backpack close-ups filed here. The campus map with the legend for suspect arrival, rooftop access, rifle recovery and bullet path gives the geography those clothing frames sit inside. For the written arguments, read [Stairs Guy](/Tyler_Robinson/Stairs_Guy), [Backpack and Rifle Impossibility](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Backpack_Rifle_Impossibility) and the clothing-change analysis at [Clothing Change](/Tyler_Robinson/clothing-change-agent-david-hull); for more images of the same sequence see [Walking](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Walking/overview), [Stairs](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Stairs/overview) and [Backpack](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Backpack/overview).
