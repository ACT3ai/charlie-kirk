## What This Image Shows

A three-panel composite on a black background. The top half holds two vertical surveillance captures of a man walking a concrete sidewalk, stamped **8:07 AM** and **11:49 AM**; the lower half holds a high-angle crowd frame.

This is the clearest single presentation of the clothing-change question. The 11:49 a.m. stamp matches the neighborhood sighting recorded in the charging documents for [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), immediately before the 11:50 a.m. walk across the grass north of Campus Drive with a stiff right leg. The 8:07 a.m. stamp precedes the 8:29 a.m. campus arrival in the published timeline. Between the two frames the clothing changes, and the state's account is that this is one person who changed; researchers who dispute it argue from the cap, the backpack fill and the build rather than the face, because the resolution does not permit facial comparison.

No identification is asserted here. Compare with the two-panel versions in [Clothing](/Photos/Tyler_Robinson/Clothing/overview), then read [Clothing Change](/Tyler_Robinson/clothing-change-agent-david-hull) and [Stairs Guy Identity Gap](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Stairs_Guy_Identity_Gap).
