## What This Image Shows

A cropped outdoor photograph on a bright sunny day showing two men side by side. The man on the left is cropped from the neck to the upper thighs, wearing a bright white synthetic athletic T-shirt with **FREEDOM** in bold black block capitals across the chest - the shirt **Charlie Kirk** was photographed in throughout the tour. The man on the right, cropped from the waist up, is an older gentleman with a thick white beard, smiling broadly at the camera in a green, grey and white plaid button-up, sunglasses and a cap.

The only on-screen text is **FREEDOM** on the shirt.

The crop is deliberate: the head of the man on the left has been removed, which is how images of this kind are prepared when the point being made is about the other person or about clothing rather than about Kirk. The green plaid shirt here is the same garment that appears in the second image in this sub-cluster with a wireless receiver clipped to its pocket, which is the whole reason both are filed together. A photograph of two people standing next to each other implies nothing about conduct.
