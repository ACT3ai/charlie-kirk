## What This Image Shows

A press-quality frame of **Charlie Kirk** at the front of the crowd in harsh direct sunlight, wearing the white **FREEDOM** T-shirt and leaning forward with his mouth slightly open in concentration, gaze fixed on a red baseball cap frozen in mid-air to his right. The cap has a green under-brim and white embroidery. Behind him the crowd is packed tight, waving, photographing and watching.

The legible text is **FREEDOM** on the shirt and a partial word above it.

The cap exchange is worth stating plainly because it is the ordinary background against which every claim in this section has to be read: objects moved across the table line constantly, in both directions, all afternoon. Caps were thrown up to be signed and thrown back. Any argument that a particular hand movement was unusual has to survive that fact.
