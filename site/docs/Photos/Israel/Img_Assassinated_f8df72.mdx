## What This Image Shows

This meme sets a large photograph of **Charlie Kirk** beside four smaller portraits of assassinated American figures, under the headline "YOU DON'T GET ASSASSINATED FOR LYING."

The argument compressed into that one line is that assassination implies the target was telling a dangerous truth. It is a rhetorical claim, and it does not survive contact with the historical record: people are killed for what others believe about them, for symbolic reasons, out of personal grievance, and by individuals with no coherent motive at all. Nothing about being killed establishes that a person's claims were correct.

The meme names no perpetrator and offers no evidence. It is included in this cluster because it circulated widely as shorthand for the argument the rest of these images make with documents. Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
