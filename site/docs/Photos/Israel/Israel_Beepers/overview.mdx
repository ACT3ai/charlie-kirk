## About This Cluster

This sub-cluster documents the **September 17 and 18, 2024** attack in which thousands of pagers and hundreds of walkie-talkies carried by [**Hezbollah**](/Israel/Hezbollah) members detonated almost simultaneously across Lebanon and Syria. That event is not part of the [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) case. It appears in this investigation for one reason only — as the reference point for a capability argument, the claim that a service able to compromise a manufacturing supply chain and trigger thousands of coordinated explosions in wearable devices should not be assumed incapable of a single killing. Mainstream reporting attributed the operation to **Mossad**, describing small charges of the plastic explosive **PETN** embedded in the devices during manufacture through the supply chain of the Taiwanese maker **Gold Apollo**.

The image kept here is the photograph that circulated worldwide afterward: the charred remains of a detonated pager. It is used across five pages of this site because it is the visual anchor for the whole line of reasoning that connects device-based attacks to the [exploding-microphone theory](/Mic/overview) in the Kirk case — the argument that if a battery compartment can be turned into a weapon, a body-worn transmitter is at least worth examining. That is an argument about what is technically possible. It does not show that any such device was used at Utah Valley University, and no investigating agency has said one was. The official case names a domestic suspect who has been charged and not convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any role in Kirk's death.
