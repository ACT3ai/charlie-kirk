## What This Image Shows

This split-screen composite is one of the most-cited images in the Israel thread. The left half is a dark-mode chat log with messages attributed to [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk): "Just lost another huge Jewish donor. $2 million a year because we won't cancel Tucker" at 3:48 PM, then "I'm thinking of inviting Candace," then at 3:50 and 3:54 PM "Jewish donors play into all the stereotypes. I cannot and will not be bullied like this" and "Leaving me no choice but to leave the pro Israel cause." The other party replies "Ugghhh" and "Please don't invite Candace. That might feel good short term but it's not good long term, in my opinion," adding that "Like all groups, you're going to get wide variety of opinions."

The right half is the corroboration the composite is built to supply: a news excerpt, highlighted, reporting the withdrawal of a $2 million pledge by donor [**Robert J. Shillman**](/People/robert-shillman). The pairing is the argument — that the private complaint and the public reporting describe the same event.

These messages were published by [**Candace Owens**](/People/candace-owens) and were disputed as fabricated before she released them. They are screenshots, and screenshots can be altered; no forensic authentication has been published. What they document, if genuine, is a funding dispute and Kirk's frustration with it.

Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
