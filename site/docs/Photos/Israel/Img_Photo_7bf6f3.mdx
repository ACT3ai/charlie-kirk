## What This Image Shows

This is the original post, captured in dark mode with a hand-drawn red underline added by whoever saved it. Dated **13/08/2025** — August 13, 2025, nearly a month before the assassination — [**Harrison H. Smith**](/People/harrison-smith) wrote: "I'm not gonna name names, but I was told by someone close to [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) that Charlie thinks Israel will kill him if he turns against them."

The chain of custody here is worth stating plainly, because it is three links long and every link is soft. Smith is relaying what an unnamed person close to Kirk told him about what Kirk supposedly thought. Kirk is not quoted. The intermediary is not named. Smith has stood by the post publicly, including on the day of the shooting, and its pre-assassination timestamp is what gives it weight — a fear expressed in advance is different from a theory constructed afterward.

What it records is a reported state of mind. It records nothing about who killed Charlie Kirk. Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
