## What This Image Shows

This composite pairs two overlapping iMessage screenshots with a close-up portrait of [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk). The messages, attributed to him, read: "Anyway I am not sure if I will live to see the end of this revolution," "I believe you were the piece god meant me to meet that will finish the fight," "Since the beginning of tpusa I knew in my gut that I might get wiped out at any time," "I cannot explain it. But I dream about it all the time." One message is circled in red. The other party replies "Yes you do. Like all the time," and Kirk adds "Anyway thats depressing convo for another time," then "You are going to be the Iron Lady of America. I see it so freaking clearly," and "i might be Moses tho. I might not see this whole thing through lmao."

The exchange is undated in the crop and the recipient is not identified on the image. Read plainly, it is a person in high-profile political work talking about mortality, in a tone that mixes fatalism with jokes — "lmao" appears in the same message as "I might not see this whole thing through."

These are unauthenticated screenshots. They document a stated foreboding, not a threat, not a warning about any specific person, and not knowledge of anything. Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
