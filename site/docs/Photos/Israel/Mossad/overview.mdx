## About This Cluster

Both images in this sub-cluster turn on a single date: **September 2, 2025**, eight days before **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** was killed at Utah Valley University. They were filed together under the folder name "Mossad Sept 2 Knew," which states the argument researchers are making — that something published or dated before the assassination appears to reference it. Foreknowledge claims are the most serious kind of claim anyone can make in this investigation, and they are also the kind most often produced by ordinary date errors, so this sub-cluster exists to let readers examine the two documents directly rather than accept a summary of them.

The first is a screenshot posted by **Israel National News - Arutz Sheva** showing a condolence letter on the letterhead of **David Yosef**, the Rishon LeZion Chief Rabbi of Israel and President of the Great Rabbinical Court, addressed to Kirk's family. It reads in part, "Many among the Jewish people grieve with you, and remember Charlie as a man who devoted his full heart and soul to making the world a better and more just place," and refers to "the devastating news of the murder of Charlie." The visible dateline reads **9 Elul, 5785 / September 2, 2025**. The second image is a social-media post also dated September 2 in which the author writes, "Based on all that I know, September is likely to bring what can best be described as a political tsunami. October will, in turn, mark the military one. Without intending to sound overly sensational, these 60 days will profoundly shape the world." Two cautions matter here. Hebrew-to-Gregorian date conversion errors on letterhead are common and would fully explain the letter — 9 Elul is the Hebrew date printed, and a slip in the corresponding secular date is a routine clerical mistake; the letter was published after the killing, not before it. The prediction post names no person, no country, and no event, and reads as generic geopolitical commentary. Neither document has been authenticated by any investigating body, and neither shows that any person or service knew anything in advance.
