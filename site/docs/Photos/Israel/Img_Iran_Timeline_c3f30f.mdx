## What This Image Shows

This graphic is titled "Charlie Kirk Assassination and Iran Regime Change: The Timeline That Raises Eyebrows..." It lays out a chronological sequence in color-coded columns running from **Early 2025** to **January 2026**, shifting from blue to orange across the page, with each column pairing a text block to an illustrative icon.

Its argument is one of sequence: that Kirk was publicly opposing a war with Iran, that he died on September 10, 2025, and that the policy trajectory afterward moved in the direction he had been resisting. Kirk's opposition is documented — he wrote in April 2025 that "a new Middle East war would be a catastrophic mistake" and warned that Iran is "larger than Iraq, Syria, and Afghanistan combined."

The graphic's own title concedes what it is: events that "raise eyebrows." Sequence is not causation, the columns extend into projected future dates, and the graphic carries no citations and no named author. Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
