## What This Image Shows

This is the widely circulated infographic titled **"The Last 60 Days of Charlie Kirk."** It runs left to right from **July 11** to **September 10, 2025**, along a blue-to-red timeline arrow, over a faded portrait of Kirk. Each marker plots a public statement, a reported donor loss, or an Israel-related controversy from his final two months, ending at the date of the assassination.

The individual entries are drawn from public reporting, much of it reproduced elsewhere in this cluster — the Tucker Carlson dispute, the donor withdrawals, the Hamptons meeting, the Iran war commentary. The graphic's problem is not its facts but its form: a timeline arrow implies causation that none of its sources establish, it carries no citations, and its author is anonymous.

A copy of this same graphic is published on the site's [Israel overview](/Israel/overview) and [Timeline](/Timeline/overview) pages, and it also appears in the [Operation Grim Beeper](/Photos/Israel/Israel_Operation_Grim_Beeper/overview) sub-cluster. Nothing here shows that any person or government had advance knowledge of the assassination or any role in it. Prosecutors have charged a domestic suspect who has not been convicted, and no Israeli individual or institution has been shown to have any connection to Charlie Kirk's death.
