## What This Image Shows

A high-angle frame of a crew installing stone pavers beneath the concrete pedestrian bridge, next to the manicured lawn. Workers use shovels, trowels and wheelbarrows to level sand and lay stone. A strip of green grass with a curved concrete border runs across the foreground, with a long blue-handled landscape rake and a white squeegee resting on it. Beyond the work zone, parked utility vehicles, a small excavator and the campus water are visible.

The detail worth noting is the excavator. Laying pavers properly means over-excavating first and building a compacted base, which is what the contractor **Daniel Merrell** described publicly when he explained how the job was done. That means the original ground surface in this area was removed, not covered.

Removal rather than overlay is the crux of the site-alteration argument. Whether the surface was photographed, sampled or logged before it was dug out is not established by any released record.
