## What This Image Shows

A cropped screenshot of a formal records response. The visible text reads that to the extent the request seeks such records, **including correspondence or approvals from UVU Facilities or DFCM, relating to the Orem campus Fountain Courtyard**, responsive records are attached - and that certain information has been **classified as protected under Utah Code Ann. 63G-2-305(11) because their disclosure could reasonably be expected to jeopardize the life or safety of an individual**, with portions redacted or withheld under 63G-2-308(2).

The wording matters line by line. The request was for the approvals behind work in the **Fountain Courtyard** - the courtyard that was excavated and paved four days after the shooting - and it reached both the university's facilities office and **DFCM**, the state's Division of Facilities Construction and Management. Some records were released. Others were withheld under the same exemption code that appears on the redacted September 9 warning communications.

That is the pattern researchers keep pointing at: the approval chain for altering the site exists on paper, was requested, and came back partly redacted under a life-or-safety exemption. Whether that classification is justified is a question for a court or a legislature, not for a photograph.
