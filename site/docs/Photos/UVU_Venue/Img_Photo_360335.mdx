## What This Image Shows

A side-by-side composite. The left panel is a blurry close-up of a pale surface, annotated with red text and blue arrows: **SHOT CAME FROM HERE** and **SHELL CASING**, with a curved white line framing the area. The right panel is promotional artwork for a **CornerShot** weapon system - a handgun mounted on a hinged frame with an integrated camera and monitor screen.

The argument, advanced publicly by **@ProjectConstitu** and **@stevendenoon**, is that a cartridge case visible in event footage was ejected at close range near the rear of the canopy, and that a hinged corner-firing weapon would account for both a concealed firing position and the ejection pattern. It is a hypothesis assembled from annotated stills and product imagery.

Nothing about it has been confirmed. No such weapon has been recovered, no forensic examination has been published, and the object in the left panel is small and out of focus, identified only by the label drawn beside it. This is the same composite catalogued in [MonoPod Camera](/Photos/Truck_Tent/MonoPod_Camera/overview); weigh it against the official account in [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview) and the competing close-range readings in [Tent Shooter Theories](/Tent/Tent_Shooter_Theories).
