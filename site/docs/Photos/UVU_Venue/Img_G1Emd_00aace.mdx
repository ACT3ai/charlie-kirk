## What This Image Shows

A phone screenshot of the Perplexity search assistant. The typed question reads **Does Utah Valley University have some kind of anticipatory defense program**, and the answer states that UVU **does not have a distinct program titled anticipatory defense**, but that its **Center for National Security Studies** co-leads the **Intermountain Intelligence, Industry, and Security Consortium** with Utah State University, with programming around anticipatory intelligence, cybersecurity and security analytics. It notes support from the Deep Technology Talent Initiative.

The screenshot is in the file because the campus itself became a subject of inquiry. Researchers noticed that UVU hosts what is described as the largest national security studies program in the region, and asked whether that had any bearing on the presence of people at the event, on the campus security posture, or on the intelligence-adjacent threads elsewhere in the case.

An AI search answer is not a source; it is a pointer to sources. What it establishes here is only that the programme exists and what it is called. Whether it has any connection to [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025) is an open question and nothing on this page suggests it does.
