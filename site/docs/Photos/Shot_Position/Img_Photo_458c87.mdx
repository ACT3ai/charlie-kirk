## What This Image Shows

A wide, slightly elevated view of the plaza at [**Utah Valley University**](/UVU/overview) on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), with the crowd packed across the lawn and paved areas between the campus buildings. High on the right, on an empty walkway closed off with barricades, an orange circle marks a single figure running. Everything else in the frame is stationary and facing the event; the circled person is the only thing moving across open ground.

The on-screen text is player furniture rather than content: a play control, a timeline reading 19:28 of 1:09:42, an "In this video" label and a closed-caption toggle. That matters for provenance — this is a frame lifted from a long video rather than a standalone photograph, so the circled figure can in principle be tracked forward and backward through the footage instead of being judged on a single instant.

That is precisely what several researchers have done, and it is why this frame sits in this cluster. A frame-timing analysis attributed to [**Blake Bednarz**](/People/blake-bednarz) works from a 12:23:34 timestamp and a measured 220-foot run over the following 15 seconds to argue that the person on the roof was already running seconds before [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) was hit — an argument that, if the measurements hold, would make that runner an implausible shooter. Researchers have also complained that the county released the rooftop-view footage only from a trimmed start point, which limits independent checking. Nothing in this image identifies the circled person, and no claim is made here that they were involved in the shooting.
