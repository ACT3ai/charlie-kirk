## What This Image Shows

A wide, slightly elevated still captured from a video player. Under the white pop-up canopy branded "THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG," figures are seated at the stage table; behind them the crowd is packed onto concrete stairs and held back by metal barricades. Near the top centre of the frame a red circle has been drawn on the concrete overhang.

The player furniture identifies the source file directly. The window title reads "Charlie Kirk Shot - Relevant Angle #3.mp4," and the transport shows a position of 00:25 in a clip of 00:39. So this frame comes from a short angle-analysis clip assembled by a researcher, not from raw broadcast footage, and the circle is that researcher's mark rather than an official annotation.

What the circle contains is not stated on the frame and cannot be resolved at this resolution. Its significance is positional: it sits on the overhang almost directly above and behind the stage, the same band of structure marked in [Object Circled Above the Tent](/Photos/Shot_Position/Img_Charlie_Tent_Door_58638d) and examined in [Gun Port](/Photos/Shot_Position/Gun_Port/overview).
