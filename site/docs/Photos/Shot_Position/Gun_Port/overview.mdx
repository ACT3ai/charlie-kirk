## About This Cluster

The buildings that ring the [**UVU**](/UVU/overview) courtyard are not blank walls. Their concrete faces carry recessed louvres, mechanical exhaust vents, narrow slot windows and service alcoves — any one of which, researchers have argued, could conceal a muzzle from a crowd standing a hundred feet below. This small cluster collects the frames in which posters have circled one of those openings and asked whether it functioned as a firing port on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), an alternative to the rooftop position named in the official account.

What is actually visible in these three images is modest and should be read that way. One frame is a wide, high-angle view of the packed plaza that establishes the sight line; a second is a heavy crop with a green circle drawn over a run of narrow rectangular openings, inside which a small indistinct yellowish object appears; the third circles a square metal vent cap on the concrete wall while onlookers stand on a nearby balcony. No image resolves the object, and none of them carries a timestamp, a source attribution or a scale reference — which is exactly the gap that keeps the question open rather than answering it. Whether these openings are even accessible from inside the building has not been publicly established.

Compare these against the trajectory overlays and the roof-access frames on the parent page, [The Shot and the Shooter Position](/Photos/Shot_Position/overview), and against the wider building views in [Photos](/Photos/Shot_Position/Photos/overview) and the interior shots in [Flag Hall Inside](/Photos/Shot_Position/Flag_Hall_Inside/overview). For the written analysis of alternative firing positions and calibers see [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview) and [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview); for the venue layout see [UVU](/UVU/overview).
