## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution, slightly blurry frame of a group of onlookers standing on a lower open gallery of the building, all facing out to the left. Directly above them, on the next balcony up, a potted plant and a pale wooden railing stand against a reflective glass pane. The facade is a grid of grey and muted beige panels. No annotation, no text.

The filename records why it was kept: this is the level researchers refer to as the teachers lounge balcony, one of the elevated positions repeatedly proposed as an alternative to the roof — including in the acoustic work summarised in the investigation notes, which places the report as coming from the south, above that balcony. The frame documents the balcony's actual construction: an open railing, a plant, glazing behind, and a lower gallery full of people immediately underneath it.

Nothing here shows a person, a weapon or an event. It is architectural and occupancy reference, and the word camo in the filename reflects the collector's shorthand rather than anything visible in the image.
