## What This Image Shows

A three-panel graphic. The top panel is a digital rendering — not a photograph — of a figure in a black hoodie and cap in a shooting position on a rooftop, with a separate inset face of a distressed young man added at the left, both ringed in red. The lower-left panel is a real photograph of Charlie Kirk speaking into a microphone. The lower-right panel is a rendered mugshot-style image of a young man in a green vest. Visible text fragments include "ACK," "TOUR" and a garbled string.

The critical point is stated plainly: two of the three panels are illustrations. The rooftop figure was never photographed; it was generated. The mugshot-style image shows the tell-tale artefacts of synthetic imagery, including distorted lettering. Placed beside one genuine photograph, the renderings acquire a borrowed credibility they have not earned.

This matters beyond one graphic. Synthetic images circulating inside a real investigation contaminate the visual record for everyone, including people trying to work in good faith, and they make it easier to dismiss the evidence that is real. Nothing in this image is evidence of anything, and no person depicted or implied here is accused of anything by this site.
