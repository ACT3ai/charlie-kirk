## What This Image Shows

A four-panel collage with a small extra inset at the bottom centre: Charlie Kirk speaking at the outdoor event, a crowd interaction, a security presence, and an indoor encounter between two men. Red target-style circles have been drawn across several panels. Legible text fragments include "FREEDOM," "AMERICAN COMEBACK" and partial tent branding.

The red circles are the only thing connecting the panels, and they are the maker's marks. No caption states what any circle contains, no panel is dated or sourced, and nobody except Kirk is identified. The indoor encounter in particular is uncontextualised — there is no basis in the frame for saying who those men are, where they are, or when.

Kept as an artefact of circulation rather than as evidence, this graphic shows how quickly the visual grammar of an investigation — circles, insets, arrows — can be applied to material that carries no information at all.
