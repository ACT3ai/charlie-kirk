## What This Image Shows

A four-panel graphic styled to look like a broadcast news card: law enforcement officers, a blurry security-style frame with a red circle drawn on it, a close-up portrait of a young man, and a medium shot of a public figure at an outdoor event.

The headline text reads "NEW FOOTAGE CHALLENGES LONE GUNMAN NARRATIVE," with a sub-line stating that a guard "freezes 42 seconds." Both are the maker's claims. The graphic does not show the footage it refers to, does not say which guard, does not date the 42 seconds, and does not identify anyone in the panels.

The specific number is what makes the claim worth recording. An assertion of exactly 42 seconds is testable against the video record if that record is released, which turns a rhetorical headline into a request: publish the timed footage. Until then it is an unverified claim about an unnamed person, and this site does not suggest that any member of the security detail failed in their duty or acted improperly.
