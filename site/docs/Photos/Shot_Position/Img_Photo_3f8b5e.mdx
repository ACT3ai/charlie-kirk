## What This Image Shows

A tight, eye-level photograph of a glass commercial entry door with dark bronze anodised aluminium framing, secured with a key cylinder. Two white vinyl decals on the left pane read "NO SMOKING INSIDE OR WITHIN 25 FEET." Across the top of the frame, in large black and white letters, someone has added the words "EASY ESCAPE."

The caption is the argument. Whoever made this graphic is pointing at a specific exit and asserting that a person leaving the building at this point could get clear quickly and unseen. Nothing else on the frame supports or dates that claim — the door is not identified by building or elevation, there is no timestamp, and the photograph appears to have been taken after the event rather than on the day.

Read on its own it proves nothing. Read against the rest of this cluster it belongs to the exit-route question that runs parallel to the firing-position question: if the official account of where the shooter was is wrong, then the routes away from the alternative positions matter as much as the positions themselves. Building access and egress at the venue are covered at [Perimeter and Rooftop Failures](/Security_Team/perimeter-rooftop-failures) and [UVU](/UVU/overview).
