## About This Cluster

The **Hall of Flags** is the long interior concourse at [**Utah Valley University**](/UVU/overview) that runs behind the glass curtain wall facing the courtyard where [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) was speaking on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). It matters to this area of the investigation for one reason: it is inside, it is elevated, it looks straight out over the plaza through full-height windows, and it appears by name on the trajectory overlays filed on the parent page. Anyone testing sight lines has to account for what could and could not be seen — or done — from behind that glass.

These three images are ordinary, undated photographs of the space in normal use: rows of international flags hung the length of the ceiling, exposed utility pipes painted in primary colours, long runs of study seating, a student union concourse with information booths along the window wall. None of them was taken on the day and none carries an annotation or a claim. Their function is architectural reference — establishing the ceiling height, the window line, the depth of the corridor and the relationship between the interior floor level and the courtyard outside, so that the angles asserted elsewhere can be checked against the real building rather than a memory of it.

Pair these with the exterior views in [Other](/Photos/Shot_Position/Other/overview) and the annotated diagrams on [The Shot and the Shooter Position](/Photos/Shot_Position/overview), where "Hall of Flags" is labelled directly on the aerial overlays. Broader coverage of the campus and its buildings is at [UVU](/UVU/overview); camera coverage of the same interiors is discussed at [Cameras](/cameras/overview).
