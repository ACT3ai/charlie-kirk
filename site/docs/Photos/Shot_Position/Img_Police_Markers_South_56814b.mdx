## What This Image Shows

An eye-level photograph of part of the concrete facade with a metal extension ladder leaning against it. Two yellow numbered forensic forensic markers are in frame: marker 6 resting on a windowsill behind the ladder, and marker 7 fixed to the concrete wall above the top of the ladder. There is no on-screen text.

This is one of the few frames in the cluster that shows official process rather than citizen analysis. Numbered markers mean investigators identified two separate points of interest on this elevation and recorded them, and the ladder means somebody needed physical access to the upper point. What the markers denote has not been made public: the state has not released the scene diagram, the marker log, or the photographs that would tie each number to a finding.

That gap is the point of keeping the image. It establishes that a documented forensic record of this facade exists, which converts a general complaint about disclosure into a specific, answerable request — for the numbered exhibit schedule covering these two markers.
