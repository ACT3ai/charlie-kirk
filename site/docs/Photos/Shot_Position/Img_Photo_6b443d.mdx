## What This Image Shows

The companion piece to the 157-degree graphic earlier in this cluster, from the same analyst — credited on the frame to **Justin Ruh**, handle [@MrDiscordeous](/Influencers/x/mrdiscordeous), with a creation date of 09/18/25 visible in the header. A 3D aerial map of the concrete building and terraced lawn, again labelled "Hall of Flags," carries the geometric lines; inset panels show a 3D head model with marked impact zones and ground-level photographs of candidate vantage points.

Here the heading reads "IMPACT LOCATION 120" degrees and "FACIAL RADIUS," with a full compass rose stepping through 30, 60, 90, 120, 180, 210, 240, 270, 300 and 330 degrees, and marked bearings at minus 90 and minus 120. The same three-tier ranking appears — "MOST PROBABLE (Trajectory/evasion)," "2ND MOST PROBABLE (Person seen in this location)" and a third "PROBABLE" tier flagged as having no video evidence or conflicting evidence — along with a note on "ELEVATION RISE."

Read the two diagrams together rather than separately. The analyst is testing more than one assumed impact orientation and showing how the candidate positions shift as that assumption changes, which is honest method: the conclusion is explicitly conditional on a wound geometry that has not been publicly released. That the autopsy record remains sealed is precisely why this work is being done from photographs at all.
