## What This Image Shows

A high-angle wide shot of the plaza and the tiered grassy amphitheatre, with a large crowd of mostly college-aged people seated on the grass terraces and concrete ledges, all facing the white event canopy at the left. The canopy carries "THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG"; campus signage reading "A PLACE FOR YOU" is visible.

The uniform orientation of the crowd is the useful datum. Every head faces one way, which fixes the stage bearing precisely — and any angle calculation in this hierarchy is ultimately measured from that bearing. The seated tiers also show the natural rake of the ground, which determines what each row could see over the row in front.

The frame carries no annotation and no timestamp. Like its companions in this sub-cluster it is a clean reference view rather than an argument.
