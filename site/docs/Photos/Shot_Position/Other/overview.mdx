## About This Cluster

Before any argument about angles can be tested, the scene itself has to be established: how the plaza at [**Utah Valley University**](/UVU/overview) was laid out on [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), where the tent stood, how the terraced lawn stepped down toward it, and which building faces looked directly onto the seat [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) occupied. This cluster holds the establishing views that the more argumentative images in the rest of this area depend on — the baseline against which every overlay and circle is drawn.

The three images work as a set. One is a 3D CAD reconstruction of the courtyard with a semi-transparent cone of sight projected from a high point on one of the buildings down to a small figure in the lower right, with hand-drawn numbers and arrows marking architectural vertices — an attempt to model the line rather than assert it. The other two are ordinary wide photographs of the same plaza filled with students, the branded "American Comeback Tour" and "Prove Me Wrong" canopy visible on the lawn, the "A PLACE FOR YOU" campus signage in frame. Their value is that they are uncontested: nothing has been circled, labelled or claimed, so they can be used to check the geometry that the annotated images assert.

Use these as your reference frames, then return to [The Shot and the Shooter Position](/Photos/Shot_Position/overview) for the trajectory diagrams, or step across to [Gun Port](/Photos/Shot_Position/Gun_Port/overview) and [Questionable](/Photos/Shot_Position/Questionable/overview) to see what has been drawn onto the same architecture. The site's written treatment of the venue is at [UVU](/UVU/overview), and the canopy itself is covered at [Tent](/Tent/overview).
