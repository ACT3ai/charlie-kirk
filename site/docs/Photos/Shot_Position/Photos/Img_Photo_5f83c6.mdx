## What This Image Shows

A high-angle walkthrough still taken from an elevated position: the camera looks down the concrete side of the building, past runs of metallic vertical pipework, toward the courtyard with its manicured lawns and terraced steps. An American flag hangs vertically from the facade in the midground. The presenter's inset is in the top right; a fragment of the overlay text and the word "ews" are visible.

This is the most directly relevant frame in the walkthrough, because it is shot from height looking down at the ground where the tent stood. Anyone assessing what an elevated position could see has to account for what is in the way — and this frame shows the pipework, the facade returns and the terrace edges that break up that view.

It also fixes the flag's position on the building, which recurs as a landmark in several other images across this hierarchy and helps orient them relative to one another.
