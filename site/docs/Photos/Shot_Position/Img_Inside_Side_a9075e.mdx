## What This Image Shows

A Street View style interior capture inside the building: a wide corridor with tan carpet, a ceiling of exposed services painted in primary colours, and glass-walled rooms along one side, one of them signed "Innovation Academy." A red circle has been drawn onto the midground left of the frame, marking a reflection or a specific point on a glass pane.

The circle is unlabelled, which limits what can be said about it. Reflections in full-height interior glazing are exactly the kind of feature that produces both genuine finds and false positives — the same pane can show what is behind the camera, what is outside the building, and a smear of light, all at once.

The frame's real value is orientation. Filed under the name Inside Side, it establishes the interior side of the glass wall that the [Flag Hall Inside](/Photos/Shot_Position/Flag_Hall_Inside/overview) images show at greater length, and against which the exterior sight-line diagrams in this cluster are drawn. Knowing the floor level, the depth of the corridor and where the glazing starts is what allows an exterior angle claim to be checked rather than accepted.
