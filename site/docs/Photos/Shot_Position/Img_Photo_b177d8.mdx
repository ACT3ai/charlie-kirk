## What This Image Shows

A high-angle vertical photograph taken from an upper level, looking down on the crowd packed along the pedestrian walkway beside the concrete building. The white event canopy sits centre-right in the mass of people; a pink promotional display stands off to the left. Partial signage is legible on the frame edges but nothing that identifies the photographer or the exact moment.

The photograph is unannotated, and its value is positional. It fixes the relationship between the canopy and the building face directly beside it — the distance, the angle, and the height of the level the camera itself is standing on. Every claim in this cluster about what could be seen from an elevated position is a claim about a geometry this image records without arguing about it.

Note also what it shows about the elevated levels: the person who took this frame was standing on one of them, in the open, in full view of the crowd below. Any account of a concealed elevated position has to sit alongside the fact that these levels were in ordinary public use throughout the event.
