## What This Image Shows

A forensic-style infographic built by an independent analyst. It sets an aerial map of the campus plaza, labelled "Hall of Flags," beside a 3D anatomical model of a skull marking an impact point, plus two annotated photographs of the building facade. The graphic is credited on its face to **Justin Ruh** under the handle @MrDiscordeous, dated 2025.

The legend is the substance of it. Headings read "IMPACT LOCATION 157" degrees and "TOP RADIUS," with a compass rose keyed by "RIGHT," "LEFT," "FRONT" and "REAR" and marked positions at minus 90 and minus 157. Three candidate firing positions are ranked rather than asserted: "MOST PROBABLE (Trajectory/evasion)," "2ND MOST PROBABLE (Person seen in this location)," and a third labelled "PROBABLE (NO VIDEO EVIDENCE OR CONFLICTI..." — the caption is cut off in this capture.

What makes this worth keeping is the method. Ruh is not naming a shooter; he is publishing a reproducible geometric argument that starts from a stated impact orientation and works backward to the positions that could produce it, then grades each by the evidence available. That is checkable work, and it can be wrong in public. The angles depend on an assumed wound orientation that has itself been contested, since the autopsy material remains sealed.
