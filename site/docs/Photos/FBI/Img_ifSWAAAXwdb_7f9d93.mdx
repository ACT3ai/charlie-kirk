## What This Image Shows

The second **New York Times** screenshot in this pair, again on a phone with a yellow highlight and a faint orange underline across part of the text.

The passage reads that [**Kash Patel**](/People/kash-patel) was troubled that [**Joe Kent**](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent) had gone through F.B.I. material related to the case, according to people knowledgeable on the matter, and that Kent's efforts were a topic at a White House meeting that included Patel, Kent, and Kent's direct superior, [**Tulsi Gabbard**](/US_Intelligence/tulsi-gabbard). It states that top Justice Department officials, Vice President [**JD Vance**](/People/jd-vance), and White House chief of staff **Susie Wiles** were also there, that the people interviewed were granted anonymity to speak frankly about internal dynamics in the administration, and that the F.B.I. and the Justice Department generally keep tight control over case material in criminal cases, particularly when preparing for trial, when government documents may have to be turned over to the defense and could be used to poke holes in the prosecution.

The detail that matters is the room. According to the reporting, the question of who could read the case file was escalated to the White House with the Vice President and the chief of staff present. The article frames the bureau's position as ordinary case-material control ahead of trial; researchers reading the same paragraph have asked why an internal access dispute needed that level of attention at all. Both readings are recorded at [White House Halt of the IC](/CoverUp/WhiteHouse_Halt_IC) and [Joe Kent Halt](/CoverUp/Joe_Kent_Halt), with the companion screenshot at [the previous image](/Photos/FBI/Img_Photo_e217e5).
