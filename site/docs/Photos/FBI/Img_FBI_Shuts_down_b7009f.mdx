## What This Image Shows

A mobile screenshot of a **Daily Mail** news article. The headline reads: Kash Patel shuts down Charlie Kirk foreign intelligence probe in explosive feud with Trump's counterterror chief. Below the headline sit the share icons, a count of 408 comments, and a three-photo collage — a family photo including [**Charlie Kirk**](/People/charlie-kirk) with balloons behind him, a portrait of [**Kash Patel**](/People/kash-patel) in a jacket marked FBI, and a smaller inset of a man and a woman outdoors.

The Daily Mail piece covers the same dispute the New York Times reported: an inquiry run out of the [**National Counterterrorism Center**](/US_Intelligence/nctc) by [**Joe Kent**](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent) into whether the killing had any foreign connection, and the bureau's objection to it. The tabloid framing is blunter than the Times version, and the word feud is the paper's characterization rather than an established finding.

The reason this screenshot is kept in full rather than just cited is that the story circulated far more widely in this form than in the original reporting, and the public understanding of the episode was largely shaped by this headline. Two other images in this cluster capture how it spread — the [reaction post quoting the same article](/Photos/FBI/Img_Photo_a45434) and the [Candace Owens post on the Times version](/Photos/FBI/Img_Photo_d6d6c5). The written record is at [Foreign Leads](/FBI/Foreign_Leads) and [Joe Kent Halt](/CoverUp/Joe_Kent_Halt).
