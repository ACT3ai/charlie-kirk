## About This Cluster

These two images document one of the most-asked questions in the investigation: why the tent area at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) was paved over in the days after September 10. Both are screenshots of posts from the account Project Constitution. The first raises the claim that a construction company resurfaced the site so soon after the shooting. The second shows text alleging that UVU partially denied public records requests about the paving, over an image of an official document excerpt citing Utah records law and safety grounds for withholding.

The concern driving the cluster is evidence preservation. According to the reporting collected here, resurfacing work reportedly began within days of the event, and researchers question whether physical evidence on the grass and hardscape could survive that. Records requests filed under Utah's GRAMA law reportedly met partial denials, which fed further questions about transparency.

None of this establishes wrongdoing as fact; officials have their own stated reasons, and an active investigation limits what is released. The images are reported here as the allegations and open questions they represent. Open either image for its full description, and see the written record linked below.
