## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an iPhone text conversation, showing incoming messages in grey bubbles with parts highlighted in yellow. The header shows a Washington, D.C. area-code number with the remaining digits hidden, the date separator reads Yesterday, and a repeating Project Veritas watermark runs across the whole image.

According to the text visible in the screenshot, the sender writes that the recipient handed their text messages about Candace over to Veritas and is being far too brazen, asks how pulling a stunt like that helps someone without a lawyer, and states that the recipient should remember every single day that they are only alive because of the sender and were never extradited because of the sender either, warning that things are going to change if messages are leaked again. A second bubble asks whether Candace is paying for the recipient's security these days and says she really ought to be, adding that now the recipient is on her team the recipient can pass along a message: that things will not end well for either of them if the nonsense keeps up, and to take a good hard look at what happened with Kirk.

This is a circulated screenshot, and it needs to be read as exactly that. The sender is not identified anywhere in the image, the exchange has not been independently authenticated on the public record, and nothing on this page asserts that any identified person sent these messages, made a threat, or committed any offence. [**Candace Owens**](/People/candace-owens) has been one of the most prominent public voices questioning the official account, and the record of pressure directed at people in that position is at [Therapy and Intimidation](/CoverUp/Therapy_Intimidation), [X Candace Engagement Bans](/Censorship/X_Candace_Engagement_Bans), and [Censorship of Investigators](/CoverUp/Censorship_Investigators).
