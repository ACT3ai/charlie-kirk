## What This Image Shows

A vertical photograph of a small patch of green grass in an urban plaza or park, bordered by raw concrete retaining walls with a large flowering bush at one side. A person is lying on the grass. A black backpack lies flat nearby, a green tote bag sits upright near the concrete edge, and several drink cans and other items are scattered around. A cropping icon in the corner shows the frame has been prepared for editing.

The frame is filed with the walk-path material, and the reason is the backpack. The bag carried by the figure in the released stairwell images is one of the most argued-over objects in the case: researchers have contended that the backpack visible on [**Tyler Robinson**](/People/tyler-robinson) earlier in the day differs in type and in fullness from the one in the stairwell frames, and that a rifle of the stated dimensions could not have been carried in it.

Nothing identifies the person or the belongings in this photograph, and no claim is made about either. It is retained as part of the collected imagery from the corridor. The backpack argument itself is at [Backpack Rifle Impossibility](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Backpack_Rifle_Impossibility) and [Stairs Guy Identity Gap](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Stairs_Guy_Identity_Gap).
