## About This Cluster

This is the smallest cluster in the photo archive and the only one that is not about the past. **Fix Laws** is the reform side of the investigation — the argument that the way to get answers about **September 10, 2025** is not to wait for agencies to volunteer them but to compel disclosure by statute. Four federal laws are drafted for that purpose, modeled on the Epstein Files Transparency Act: **FBI and DOJ Disclosure**, **Intelligence Disclosure**, **Mandate the Investigation**, and **Trusted Investigators**. The single image here is the banner graphic that carries that argument across the site.

The graphic is a composite. **Charlie Kirk** appears on the left holding a microphone in front of a campus building; **Candace Owens** appears on the right holding a magnifying glass in a document-filled office. Three labels sit across the top — **NSA**, **CIA**, and **FBI** — the agencies the four laws are written to reach. The design makes the reform case in one frame: the man who was killed, the investigator who kept asking, and the three agencies holding records the public has not seen. Nothing in the graphic asserts that any agency or official committed a crime; the laws are drafted on the premise that disclosure should not depend on anyone's goodwill.
