## What This Image Shows

The same query as the previous image, widened to five years and switched from image search to **Web Search**, still filtered to [**Israel**](/Israel/overview). Two hand-drawn pink ovals circle those two filter menus. The chart is flat across most of the span, with an isolated peak labeled for the week of **August 17 to 23, 2025**.

Stretching the window is what makes the point the poster wanted. Across five years there is essentially no interest in this name from this region until a few weeks before [**September 10, 2025**](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025). That is the shape researchers describe as a pre-event signature, and they have applied the same test to other names in this case.

The same cautions apply as on the 90-day chart, and they are not small. Trends data is normalized and relative, low-volume terms are noisy, and a peak proves nothing about who ran the query or what they wanted. Note also that this chart peaks in mid-August while the image-search chart peaks on **July 31** — different filters, different weeks, and no single clean date.
