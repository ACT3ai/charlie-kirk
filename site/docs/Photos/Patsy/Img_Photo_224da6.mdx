## What This Image Shows

A second screenshot of a published Utah governor's daily schedule, taken from the same official site. The listed items run through budget hearings for the Utah System of Higher Education, the Division of Wildlife Resources, the Department of Veterans and Military Affairs, and the State Board of Education, and close with a 5:30 p.m. speaking engagement at the Kem C. Gardner Policy Institute ten-year commemoration at the University of Utah. Highlighted in yellow: **3:30 p.m. Meeting with Consul General of France**, in the Gold Room, Utah State Capitol. The date is not visible in this crop.

This is the companion to the Egypt schedule screenshot filed beside it, and it exists for the same reason — the France thread that entered the investigation through claims [**Candace Owens**](/People/candace-owens) attributed to a French source. A researcher looked for France in Utah's public calendars and marked what was there.

What was there is a scheduled courtesy meeting with a consular officer, published on a public website, surrounded by routine state budget business. That is what consuls general and governors do. This entry is not evidence of anything, no official is accused of anything, and the highlight is the researcher's. For the underlying claims and the arguments against them, see [French Foreign Legion Claims](/intelligence/French_Foreign_Legion_Claims).
