## What This Image Shows

A watermarked press photograph by **Charles-McClintock Wilson**, distributed by **ZUMA Press Wire**. An older man is physically restrained and escorted by a campus police officer and several plainclothes men in an outdoor plaza. Onlookers watch from the background. Event signage reading **AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR** is partly legible behind them.

The wire caption is why this frame is the anchor of the set. It reads: "[September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025), Orem, Utah, USA: [George Zinn](/Photos/Patsy/Distraction_George_Zinn/overview) is taken into custody by campus security following the fatal shooting of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) during a public event at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview). At the time of the arrest, Zinn was identified as the alleged shooter. He was later released and subsequently arrested on unrelated charges. Law enforcement confirmed that the actual suspect remained at large during this initial response."

That is a professional news agency, writing at the time, stating four things: the date and place, the name it attaches to the man, that he was initially treated as the alleged shooter, and that law enforcement knew the actual suspect was still at large during this response. A wire caption is a contemporaneous report by the agency rather than an official record, and the identification in it is the agency's. Everything else in this cluster is measured against that caption. Anything beyond it — including the reported claim that he told hospital staff he was paid — remains unverified.
