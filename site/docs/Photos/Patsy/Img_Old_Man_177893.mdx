## What This Image Shows

A medium close-up taken outdoors in harsh daylight. The elderly man in the blue button-down shirt and glasses is held from behind by another man. He looks toward the camera with a serious, slightly bewildered expression. Behind them, a crowd of onlookers stands on the tiered concrete steps and grassy areas of the plaza.

The crowd behind him is the detail worth noticing. People are standing and watching rather than fleeing, which places this frame slightly after the initial scramble captured in the ducking photographs — the point at which attention had shifted from the shot to the man being detained.

That shift is exactly what the distraction question is about. According to police, [**George Zinn**](/Photos/Patsy/Distraction_George_Zinn/overview) later said he claimed responsibility in order to draw attention away from the real shooter. Whether he acted alone in that, and why, is unresolved; [**Candace Owens**](/People/candace-owens) reported an unverified claim that he told hospital staff he was paid to be a decoy. Zinn was cleared as the shooter the same day and has not been charged in connection with the killing.
