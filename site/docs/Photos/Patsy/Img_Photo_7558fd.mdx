## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution vertical frame of the crowd in the seconds around the shot. A young man in a black Adidas shirt fills the foreground. Behind him, circled in red by whoever posted the image, several people are bending over or ducking. A concrete structure with a black metal railing rises against a clear blue sky.

The red circle marks a reaction cluster. Frames like this get analyzed for direction — who ducks first, which way people turn — because crowd reaction is one of the few available proxies for where a sound came from when the audio record is contested. The acoustic direction of the shot is disputed throughout this investigation.

What a single still can establish is limited. It has no timestamp, no audio, and no reference frame for the moment of the shot, so it cannot fix a sequence on its own. It is filed here as one of the reaction frames researchers annotate, alongside the frames of **George Zinn** being detained in the same plaza minutes later.
