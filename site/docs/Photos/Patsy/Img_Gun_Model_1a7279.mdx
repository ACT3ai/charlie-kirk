## What This Image Shows

A screenshot of an online search result for an air rifle. The product photograph shows the gun isolated on white: wooden stock, mounted scope, single long barrel. A crudely hand-drawn green arrow points up at the buttstock. The listing text reads "Ruger Yukon Magnum .177 Pellet Break Barrek Air Rifle Wood Stock w ..." — the misspelling of "Barrel" is in the original listing.

This is the identification step. Researchers matched the wood-stocked profile seen in the Provo arrest frames against this model listing and concluded the recovered item was a **.177 caliber pellet air rifle**, not a firearm. The green arrow points at the same buttstock feature the green arrow points at in the arrest frame filed nearby, which is the whole comparison.

The significance claimed is one of scale. An air rifle is not a weapon capable of the shot described in this case, so identifying the recovered item this way is an argument that the Provo arrest could never have been the shooter and that reporting which blurred the two was misleading. It is an identification by visual match from a low-resolution frame, not a confirmed property log.
