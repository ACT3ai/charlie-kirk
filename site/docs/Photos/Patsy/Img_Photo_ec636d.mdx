## What This Image Shows

A close outdoor photograph. The elderly man in glasses and the blue button-down shirt stands with his arms positioned behind his back. Directly behind him is the younger man in the grey baseball cap reading **TOKYO** who appears in several frames in this set. A crowd of onlookers is scattered across the tiered steps of the plaza behind them.

The recurrence of the man in the **TOKYO** cap across multiple frames is the kind of detail researchers track when reconstructing who was standing where in the first minutes. He appears to be a bystander in the immediate crowd; nothing in these frames identifies him or suggests any role beyond proximity, and no claim is made about him here.

The frame's value is comparative. Placed alongside the wider plaza shots and the ZUMA press frame, it helps establish the order of events and the positions of the people around **George Zinn** during a detention that was reversed the same day.
