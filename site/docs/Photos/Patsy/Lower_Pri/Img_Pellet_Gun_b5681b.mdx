## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution vertical frame. A group of officers in dark uniforms is gathered on a daytime sidewalk. One in the centre-left leans forward, taking part in the detention itself. The others stand close and watch. Behind them the street is blurred out, with trees and distant commercial signage.

This is the moment of contact, before the cuffing frames and the cruiser frames in the parent cluster. The number of officers around one man on a sidewalk is what stands out.

That number is the substance of the argument. Whatever the man was carrying, the response drew several officers to a downtown **Provo** sidewalk during the hour after a political assassination five miles away, while the shooter's location was unknown. Researchers call that a distraction; the plainer statement is that it was a diversion of resources whose outcome was never published. Nobody detained here has been charged in connection with the shooting. See [Pellet Gun Man](/distraction_people/Pellet_Gun_Man) and [Distraction Timeline](/distraction_people/Distraction_Timeline).
