## What This Image Shows

A wider, cleaner view of the same detention than most frames in this set. Three men stand on a concrete path in front of a brick building, dappled sunlight coming through a mature tree overhead. The man in the center wears teal scrubs and a white coat with a red lining. The two men flanking him are dressed as security or administrative staff rather than in marked patrol uniforms.

Because the resolution and lighting here are better than in the annotated frames, this is the image to use when checking the clothing claims against the ones drawn on top of other stills. The garment is a lab-style coat with a red-lined hood, worn over medical scrubs — the detail that produced the "fake doctor" shorthand used in posts about this incident.

No uniform, badge, or agency marking is visible on either escort, which is one reason commentators questioned whether this was a police arrest, a campus-security removal, or something else. The public record on that point is contested; see [Russell Kennington](/distraction_people/Russell_Kennington) and [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview).
