## What This Image Shows

The earliest frame in this set. A slightly low-angle wide shot taken from behind a steel crowd barrier at the Utah Valley University event. A man in a two-tone baseball cap — light beige crown, faded dark brim — fills the lower left; his eyes are hidden by the brim. Behind the barrier sits a white stage structure with equipment cases beneath it, then the white canopy tent, then a dense crowd on a bright green grassy slope under a clear sky.

The on-screen text is what makes this the anchor frame. The canopy reads **AMERICAN COMEBACK** and **PROVE ME WRONG**. The burned-in timecode reads **12:23:42:20**. And the cap itself resolves: **MARSEILLE**, **EST. 600 BC**, **FRENCH RIVIERA**.

That cap is the only reason this man is in the archive. **[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** was shot at **12:23:30 PM MDT**, so if the timecode is clock time this frame sits about twelve seconds after the shot. He has never been identified, has not been accused of anything by anyone, and a souvenir cap naming a French city is not evidence of anything at all. He is here because researchers working the France thread searched crowd footage for anything French and found a hat.
