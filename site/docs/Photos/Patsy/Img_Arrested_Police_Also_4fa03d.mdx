## What This Image Shows

A low-resolution vertical frame shot from across the street. A police officer is placing a detained man into the back seat of a dark police cruiser on a city sidewalk. A black lamp post stands in the foreground, cutting across the front of the vehicle. Daylight, concrete sidewalk, buildings and trees behind.

This is the arrest itself, the moment the man reported to have been carrying an air rifle in downtown **Provo, Utah** goes into the car. The addresses circulating with this sequence include the area of **44 S 200 W**, a point on **Freedom Boulevard** about fifty feet south of West Center Street, and the frontage of a restaurant at **201 W Center St**.

The timing is what put this arrest into the investigation. It happened within roughly an hour of the shooting, while the search for the shooter was still open and while a separate man was in custody at the campus itself. Two detentions in two cities inside one hour is what researchers describe as the distraction pattern. That is a pattern claim, not a finding. This man was not charged in connection with the shooting and has never been publicly identified. See [Pellet Gun Man](/distraction_people/Pellet_Gun_Man) and [Timeline](/Timeline/overview).
