## What This Image Shows

A side-by-side composite of two daylight photographs of the same removal. In both, the elderly man is walked along a concrete outdoor path by a police officer with a second person assisting. His trousers have fallen to his ankles as he is led forward. Onlookers and a utility vehicle are visible in the background of the left panel; a modern concrete facility fills the frame behind. The word **POLICE** is legible on uniforms in both panels.

The composite exists because it is the clearest documentation that this was a uniformed police detention rather than an informal removal by bystanders — a point that mattered when accounts of the first minutes at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) began to conflict.

It also documents the condition of the man being led away, which is part of why several witnesses later described him as an unlikely suspect. One on-camera witness said of him, "I don't think he did it... they arrested the wrong guy," and described the person he expected instead. That is a witness opinion, and the [FBI](/FBI/overview) stated Zinn was not the shooter. He has not been charged in connection with the shooting.
