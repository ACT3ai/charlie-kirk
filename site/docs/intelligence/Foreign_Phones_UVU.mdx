# Foreign Phones at UVU (Sept 10, 2025)

The strongest **foreign-presence** data point in open-source threads is analyst **[Tony Seruga](/People/tony-seruga)**'s (@TonySeruga on X) claim that **raw GPS / SIGINT** showed **44 foreign-registered cellphones** in the immediate area when Charlie Kirk was shot at [UVU](/UVU/overview) on **September 10, 2025**:

| National registration (claimed) | Count |
|--------------------------------|-------|
| Israeli | **16** (some posts say **12**) |
| Chinese | **13** |
| Russian | **12** |
| Iranian | **3** |

No NSA, FBI, or court filing **confirming subscriber identities** has been released publicly. [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) and podcast segments amplified an **NSA tip** narrative — see [Israel — NSA Cellphones](/Israel/NSA_Cellphones_Bumblehive) and [U.S. Intelligence — NSA](/US_Intelligence/nsa) for the domestic-collection angle.

## Bumblehive deanonymization (claims)

Seruga tied the UVU count to NSA's **Utah Data Center** in Bluffdale (nicknamed **Bumblehive**), alleging proprietary algorithms can link **burner phones** to prior devices after ~30–60 days ([Charlie_Kirk.txt](https://github.com/) Bumblehive notes). That would let investigators — if they chose — move from **registration country** to **real owners**. Whether that happened is an **open question**.

## Benign explanations (acknowledged in threads)

Even skeptical commentators note:

- UVU hosts **national-security** and policy programs with international visitors
- **Tourism**, **dual citizens**, and **synagogue** traffic exist in the Provo/Orem corridor
- Foreign registration does **not** equal foreign-government operations

We do **not** claim any phone holder committed a crime or knew of the assassination in advance.
