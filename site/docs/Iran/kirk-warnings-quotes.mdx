# Kirk's Iran Warnings: The Record

[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s opposition to a U.S. war or regime change in Iran was **not** a last-minute
position — it was a consistent public stance spanning years, framed around **America First**
non-intervention. This page collects the dated statements attributed to him, and the specific
consequences he warned an Iran war would bring. These are **his own public remarks**, widely
quoted and repeatedly resurfaced after September 10, 2025; a few reach the public through
paraphrase and should be read as reported.

## 2019 — "End the endless wars"

Amid an earlier Iran flashpoint, Kirk posted against the "drums of war" and calls for a ground
war: *"This would be a massive mistake... Another ground war with US troops IS NOT the answer.
[President Trump](/People/donald-trump) rightfully ran on ending endless wars. End the endless, senseless wars. America
first!"*

## April 2025 — "A catastrophic mistake"

In an April 2025 post that resurfaced prominently after his death, Kirk warned of **"warmongers"**
chasing a **"white whale"** — an all-out **regime-change war against Iran** — and called a new
Middle East war a **"catastrophic mistake."** He cited U.S. military stockpiles depleted by
support for Ukraine, Iran's size (larger, he argued, than Iraq, Syria, and Afghanistan combined),
and the risk of calamity. A companion clip has him warning that the **"war drums are beating again
in D.C."** and that sending **"young Americans to die yet again"** so that some **"D.C. has-beens
can feel tough"** would be a mistake.

## June 2025 — "Pathologically insane"

During the June 2025 Israel–Iran fighting (the "12-day war") and the U.S. debate over striking
Iranian nuclear sites, Kirk:

- Ran a poll asking whether the U.S. should **"get involved in Israel's war against Iran"** —
  reportedly **~90% said no.**
- Called a massive U.S. war or regime change **"pathologically insane."**
- Argued: *"Regime change is war... Typically in the Mideast, it's very messy, creates a
  quagmire. And then there is civil war... Iran is massive... mountainous. It makes Afghanistan
  and Iraq look like a cakewalk."*
- Warned of **hundreds of thousands dead**, massive refugee crises, and unintended consequences.
- Favored **internal Iranian change** ("make Iran Western again" by supporting its youth) over
  U.S. military action, and criticized escalation hawks such as **Lindsey Graham.**

One of his final reported urgings — to a Trump counter-terrorism official — was blunt:
*"Stop us from getting into a war with Iran."*

## The specific harms he wanted to prevent

Kirk tied his opposition to concrete predicted outcomes:

- **Quagmire.** Regime change is not a clean leadership swap; it invites prolonged chaos and civil
  war, worse than Iraq or Afghanistan given Iran's terrain, size, and sectarian diversity.
- **Human and refugee costs.** Hundreds of thousands dead; a large refugee crisis pressuring the
  West.
- **Strategic folly.** Depleted stockpiles, mountainous terrain, and escalation risk (Americans
  targeted, the Strait of Hormuz closed).
- **Betrayal of America First.** War diverts from domestic priorities and serves neoconservatives
  and foreign lobbies rather than U.S. interests. He preferred sanctions and organic opposition
  over boots on the ground.
- **Historical parallel.** He compared the regime-change logic to the flawed Iraq justifications —
  "not even the Romans could defeat Persia."

## Why the record matters

The reason this dated record anchors the whole section: it shows the anti-war stance was
**deep, public, and influential** — not a convenient posthumous invention. That is what makes the
motive question on [How the Iran War Raised His Risk](./iran-war-motive) worth asking, and what
makes the alleged [post-death reversal](./post-death-pivot) notable. His warnings gained fresh
traction amid the **2025–2026 U.S.–Iran and Israel–Iran escalations**, when the risks he described
returned to the news during his suspect's hearings.
