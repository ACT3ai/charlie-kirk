## What is reported

- According to a cited account of a defense motion, a chief deputy county attorney's teenage daughter was present at the UVU rally as a witness. The defense argued this could create a bias and asked for a special prosecutor from outside the county.
- Per the same account, Utah County Attorney Jeff Gray responded that no conflict exists; he testified at the hearing and, the account notes, corrected an earlier misstatement about the location of a budget meeting. These are described as ordinary in-court prosecution activities and testimony.
- Gray's name also appears in a commentator's list of search terms said to have been queried before the event; the file presents this only as a list, with no claim of conduct by Gray.
- **Counterpoint:** Responding to a defense motion and testifying at a hearing are standard duties of a prosecuting attorney; the conflict question is a matter for the court to decide. Nothing in the file alleges misconduct proven against Gray, and no charge or court finding exists against him.
