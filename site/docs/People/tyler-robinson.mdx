# Tyler Robinson

## There Is No Way Charlie Kirk Was Killed by a .30-06

The single most-challenged element of the entire case is the weapon. Combat veterans, citizen investigators, and the Kirk family's own courtroom counsel have all argued in public that a **.30-06** round fired from the [Losee Center rooftop](/UVU/Losee_Center) — a cartridge carrying roughly [2,900 ft-lbs of muzzle energy, about eight times a 9mm](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch) — could not have produced what the public saw: a projectile that reportedly [never exited the neck](/Cause_of_Death/overview), no catastrophic [through-and-through wound or exit signature](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch), and a [trajectory from 120 meters east that does not match the reported entry point](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle). The state's own forensics have not closed that gap: the [ATF report dated September 17, 2025 came back **inconclusive**](/Proof_Not_Tyler/ATF_Inconclusive_Ballistics) — examiner Samantha Karner testified she was "unable to say one way or another" whether the jacket fragment recovered at autopsy came from the [Mauser Model 98](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle) tied to Robinson — and [no gunshot-residue test was reportedly performed](/Proof_Not_Tyler/No_GSR_And_Physical_Tests) when he was brought in. The full argument is laid out on [.30-06 Bullet Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie), with the [energy mismatch](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/energy-mismatch), the [alternative calibers and weapons](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons) proposed instead, and the [full ballistics hub](/Gun_Bullet/overview) developed separately.

Everything below is **attributed opinion and analysis**, not adjudicated fact. **Tyler Robinson is charged, not convicted, and is presumed innocent.** The question these speakers raise is about the physics of the cartridge — not about who fired anything.

**Video 4 — what a .30-06 actually does (terminal-ballistics demonstration).** This 26-second clip is the visual form of the same argument: a .30-06 fired into a ballistic-gel head model, shown at normal speed from behind and in extreme slow motion from the side. The gel tears and expands violently. Posted as a rebuttal to the official account, it is offered as the reason commentators say the observed wound and the intact white shirt are irreconcilable with that cartridge. It is a **demonstration on a simulant**, not a reconstruction of the actual shot — the [controlled same-range, same-angle test that both sides say they want](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie) has never been performed publicly.

**Counter-arguments are recorded too.** Prosecution-leaning commentators reply that a spent .30-06 casing was toolmark-matched to the recovered Mauser, that high-power rounds routinely fragment on bone without exiting, and that a downward path into the cervical structures can dump energy without a clean exit. Those rebuttals are collected on [.30-06 Bullet Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie) and [ATF Inconclusive Ballistics](/Proof_Not_Tyler/ATF_Inconclusive_Ballistics). **Tyler Robinson** is the individual charged with the assassination of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) on September 10, 2025, at Utah Valley University. He was 22 years old at the time, residing in St. George, Utah.

## Role in the Investigation

Robinson is the officially accused suspect. The investigation examines both the official narrative — that he acted as a lone gunman — and alternative theories that he may have been a patsy, recruited operative, or that the evidence against him contains significant inconsistencies. His arrest also carried a consequence beyond the courtroom: former NCTC director [Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent) has said on the record that his center's parallel review of possible foreign ties was ordered to stop once Robinson was in custody, before those leads were exhausted.

## Official Narrative (as reported)

According to the charging documents filed by Officer [Brian Davis](/People/brian-davis) of the Utah State Bureau of Investigation:

- Robinson was captured on UVU surveillance cameras arriving on campus in a gray Dodge Challenger at approximately 08:29 on September 10, wearing a maroon t-shirt, light shorts, black hat, and light shoes.
- At approximately 11:50, surveillance captured an individual (described as the suspect) walking across campus toward the Losee Center building.
- At approximately 12:17, the suspect climbed over a wall onto the Losee Center rooftop.
- At approximately 12:22, the suspect assumed a prone shooting position on the rooftop edge facing the courtyard.
- At 12:23, Charlie Kirk was shot. The suspect sprinted north on the rooftop, dropped off the northeast corner, and fled north.
- A Mauser Model 98, .30-06 caliber bolt-action rifle with a scope was recovered nearby, wrapped in a dark towel. Inscriptions were found on the casings.
- Robinson turned himself in at the Washington County Sheriff's Office on the evening of September 11, accompanied by his parents and a retired detective family friend.

## Key Investigative Questions

The investigation raises questions including:

- **Clothing changes**: Robinson was observed in different outfits throughout the day. The suspect on the rooftop wore a long-sleeve black shirt with an American flag/eagle print, dark jeans, and Converse shoes — different from what Robinson wore arriving on campus.
- **Timeline gaps**: Several hours between his campus arrival and the suspect's rooftop appearance lack surveillance documentation.
- **Mirandizing timeline**: Documents indicate Robinson was reportedly Mirandized at 6:25 PM on September 11 — before the officially reported 8:04 PM call from Sheriff Brooksby, raising questions about when authorities actually learned of his identity.
- **Discord messages**: The confession messages attributed to Robinson appeared at 7:57 PM on September 11, over an hour after he was reportedly already Mirandized.
- **Ballistics questions**: Citizen investigators question whether a .30-06 from the rooftop distance matches the observed wound pattern. A **March 2026** defense seal motion cited an **inconclusive ATF** report comparing autopsy bullet jacket fragments to the recovered Mauser 98 — a [media coalition](/Media/tyler-robinson-trial-press) opposed sealing.
- **Jailhouse informant**: [Jaxson Thomas Fox](/distraction_people/Jaxson_Thomas_Fox) appears in court coverage as a jailhouse informant with a transport order — some commentators treat the thread as a distraction from core ballistics evidence (attributed commentary only).
- **Trial personnel**: **[Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf)** (no bail Sept 16, 2025; gag order Dec 16, 2025); lead defense **[Kathryn Nester](/People/kathryn-nester)** with co-counsel [Michael Burt](/People/michael-burt) and [Richard Novak](/People/richard-g-novak); prosecution team [Jeffrey Gray](/People/jeffrey-s-gray), [Chad Grunander](/People/chad-e-grunander), and [Christopher Ballard](/People/christopher-d-ballard) (June 2026 contempt over TMZ/Fox statements per [court press](/Media/tyler-robinson-trial-press)).
