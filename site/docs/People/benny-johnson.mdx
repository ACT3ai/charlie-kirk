# Benny Johnson

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Benny Johnson is a living person. We document what he reportedly said publicly about the case only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Benny Johnson is a commentator who has described himself as close to Charlie Kirk and his team. His relevance to the investigation is a video in which he reportedly stated there is "considerable evidence" of state-actor involvement, while declining to elaborate. Because he frames himself as an insider, his reported remarks are cited within the "state actor" thread of the case — as his own attributed claim, not as an established finding.

His reported statements connect to the [Proof Intel Services](/Proof_Intel_Services/overview) and [Cover Up](/CoverUp/overview) threads and sit near the account, involving [Joe Kent](/People/joe-kent), of a halted foreign-involvement inquiry.

## What is reported

- In a video quote-tweeted by **@CatQuestionsAll** (June 2026), Benny Johnson reportedly said: *"there is some considerable evidence that there were state actors involved here… I don't want to go any further than that. I can't go any further than that."*
- He reportedly prefaced this by saying he is "very, very close to Charlie and his team" and referenced reporting by John Solomon and leaked information cited by Steven Crowder.
- The file notes that, reportedly, the day after this video Johnson met with [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel) and Pam Bondi over death threats — a claim in the file that is not independently verified here.
- **Counterpoint:** Johnson's statement is his own **assertion of belief** ("reason to believe," "some considerable evidence"); he explicitly declined to provide the evidence. It is commentary, not a court finding, and no evidence in the file ties Johnson himself to the event.

## Open Questions

1. What is the "considerable evidence" Johnson referenced, and can any of it be independently examined?
2. What is the basis of his stated closeness to Kirk's team, and what did he learn firsthand versus secondhand?
3. Did the reported follow-up meeting over "death threats" occur, and what was its context?
