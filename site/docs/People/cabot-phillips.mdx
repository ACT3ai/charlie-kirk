# Cabot Phillips

:::caution Legal Disclaimer
Nothing on this page constitutes a finding of wrongdoing, criminal conduct, ethical violation, or participation in any crime by Cabot Phillips or any other living person. Cabot Phillips has not been charged with, accused by any authority of, or found to have committed any offense connected to the death of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk). He has publicly denied the online allegation described below and published footage in support of that denial. This page records an anonymous allegation and his denial side by side, without adjudication, because both are already public.
:::

**Cabot Phillips** is an American conservative journalist and commentator. He is a **senior editor at
The Daily Wire** and a regular voice on the outlet's **Morning Wire** podcast, and he previously served
as **editor-in-chief of Campus Reform**, the campus-news project of the Leadership Institute. He is
based in **Nashville, Tennessee**.

He appears in this investigation for **one reason only**: in January 2026 an anonymous account on X
argued that interior details in a video Phillips had posted resembled interiors at
**[Fort Huachuca](/US_Intelligence/Fort_Huachuca/overview)**, the U.S. Army Intelligence Center in
Arizona. Phillips answered that allegation publicly and directly. He has no established role of any
kind in the events of September 10, 2025.

Phillips is a public commentator by profession — he writes, hosts, and speaks about media bias, free
speech, and campus politics. Nothing in his professional biography connects him to military,
intelligence, or law-enforcement work, and this site is aware of no claim that it does.

## The allegation

On **January 15, 2026**, the X account
**[@AMeadowInquiry](/Influencers/x/ameadowinquiry)** reshared a video Phillips had posted and argued
that three interior features visible in it — the **carpet pattern**, the **dark base trim between
floor and wall**, and a **yellow wall** — matched rooms at Fort Huachuca, and asked what the video
was "supposed to prove."

That is the entire basis of the claim. It is a **visual-similarity argument made by one anonymous
account**, with no metadata, no geolocation, no witness, and no document behind it. Government-contract
carpets, base moldings, and institutional paint colors are repeated across an enormous number of
federal, military, and contractor buildings, which makes interior-finish matching weak identification
evidence in either direction. The full write-up, including the comparison frames and the site's
caveats, is at [Cabot alibi — Fort Huachuca carpet claim](/US_Intelligence/Fort_Huachuca/Cabot_alibi).

A small number of accounts pushed the claim considerably further. Those posts are recorded elsewhere
on this site as **fringe allegations against living people circulating without any court finding**,
and they are **explicitly not adopted** here. This page does not repeat them.

## His response

Phillips answered publicly rather than ignoring the claim. In a post on X he **denied being at Fort
Huachuca** on September 8 or 9, 2025, stated that he was **at home in Nashville, Tennessee**, and
attached supporting media — including a wide-angle security-camera frame timestamped
**"09-09-2025 Tue 09:17:36."** The screenshot of that post is preserved on this site at
[The Public Denial Post With Timestamped Footage](/Photos/People/Cabot_Phillips/Img_Photo_2a6e0d).

A named, on-the-record denial with a timestamp attached is a stronger form of evidence than an
anonymous account's read of a carpet pattern. Readers should weigh the two accordingly.

## What this page does not claim

- It does **not** claim Phillips was at Fort Huachuca.
- It does **not** claim his denial or his footage is false.
- It does **not** claim he has any relationship with the U.S. Army, any intelligence service, any
  defense contractor, or any federal agency.
- It does **not** claim he had any knowledge of, involvement in, or connection to the assassination
  of Charlie Kirk.
- It does **not** treat the claims made about his relatives or associates by anonymous accounts as
  established, and does not reproduce them.

He is on this site because a claim about him circulated widely enough to require a factual record and
because his own denial deserves to sit next to it. That is the whole purpose of this page.

## Open questions

1. Has any source other than the original anonymous account independently supported the location
   claim? As of this writing, none is known.
2. Have the two photographs the poster identified as Fort Huachuca interiors ever been confirmed as
   having been taken at Fort Huachuca, and in which building?
3. Are the carpet, trim, and wall colors in question specific to Fort Huachuca, or common to many
   U.S. institutional interiors?
