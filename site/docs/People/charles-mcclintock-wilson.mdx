## Investigative Value

**Charles McClintock Wilson** is a **ZUMA Press** documentary photojournalist whose still images from the [UVU](/UVU/overview) event are among the most widely cited visual records of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s **final moments** and the **immediate aftermath** of the September 10, 2025 shooting. Unlike TPUSA production cameras — some of which became the focus of SD-card and chain-of-custody disputes — Wilson's independent press work offers a parallel photographic timeline investigators can compare against [Videos](/Videos/overview), [Photos](/Photos/overview), and [eyewitness mobile video](/cameras/Eyewitness_Mobile_Video).

His frames are used in citizen timelines to anchor exact body position, crowd reaction, and post-shot scene geometry. Original full-resolution files with embedded EXIF metadata and sequential timestamps could be decisive for synchronizing photographic evidence with audio anomalies discussed under [Mic — Exploding Mic Theory](/Mic/overview). See also [Andrew Feraci](/People/andrew-feraci), the TPUSA staff photographer positioned feet from Kirk.

- His work sits alongside other visual documentarians at the event, including [Brayden Glascock](/People/brayden-glascock), [Mathew Franke](/People/mathew-franke), and [Moyo Dasaolu](/People/moyo-dasaolu).

## Photographic Documentation Value

Professional press photographers at breaking-news scenes typically shoot in burst sequences with time-stamped metadata. If preserved unedited, Wilson's complete ZUMA assignment folder could:

- Establish millisecond-level ordering between Kirk's final gestures and the audible shot.
- Show vantage points not covered by fixed [cameras](/cameras/overview) or TPUSA production angles.
- Provide independent corroboration for [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) statements about crowd movement after the impact.

The investigation file treats Wilson's images as **documentary evidence**, not proof of any particular shooter theory.
