# Rob O'Neill

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Rob O'Neill is a living person. We document what he reportedly said publicly about the case only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Rob O'Neill is a former U.S. Navy SEAL, publicly known as a member of the team associated with the bin Laden raid. His relevance to the investigation is a Newsmax segment in which he — drawing on his combat and firearms experience — questioned the official account of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s death and said the footage looked to him like an explosion rather than a standard rifle wound. His remarks are cited as an experienced observer's stated opinion, not as forensic proof.

His comments feed the [Mic](/Mic/overview) ("something exploded") and [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview) threads, alongside co-panelist [Blaine Holt](/People/blaine-holt).

## What is reported

- In a Newsmax segment (circulated April 2026 via [@jamiburns45](/Influencers/x/jamiburns45)), Rob O'Neill reportedly said: *"I've never seen a shirt move like that. That looks like an explosion to me."*
- He reportedly questioned why no immediate first aid was given, and raised concerns about SIM cards being removed from cameras and the scene being cleaned up quickly.
- He reportedly cast doubt on the lone-gunman account, calling elements of it "shady" and questioning whether the described suspect, [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson), fit the crime.
- **Counterpoint:** O'Neill himself reportedly framed his remarks as questions and opinion — *"I'm not… saying big lie or a conspiracy"* — and stated he was "asking a lot of questions," not asserting proof. His statements are experienced commentary, not a forensic or court finding.

## Open Questions

### July 2026 Amplification

**[@FurkanGozukara](/Influencers/x/furkangozukara)** (July 6, 2026) and similar accounts recirculated Rob O'Neill commentary framed as confirming a **massive exit wound rather than a simple entry wound** and highlighting security failures. Circulators often overstate O'Neill's words as "destroying" the official narrative; O'Neill's own earlier framing, as reflected in his public commentary, emphasized **questions and experienced opinion**, not a formal forensic report.

### Why Investigators Cite Him

O'Neill's combat/firearms background is used by citizen analysts to argue that lay audiences should take **shirt movement / wound dynamics** seriously when evaluating [Mic](/Mic/overview) explosion theories versus a clean 30-06 rooftop shot. **Counterpoint:** combat anecdote is not a substitute for published autopsy and ballistics under oath.

No allegation is made that O'Neill has any operational role in the events of September 10 — he is cited solely as a **public commentator**.
