> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Robert Bohls is a living person. We document his public official role only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Robert Bohls is described in project notes as the FBI Special Agent in Charge (SAIC) of the Salt Lake City field office, having taken over the office in August 2025. That timing — a change in the field-office lead about a month before the September 10, 2025 shooting — is noted by citizen-researchers as part of a broader pattern of Utah leadership transitions they find worth examining. See the [FBI](/FBI/overview) section.

Per project notes, Bohls led the September 11, 2025 press conference on the shooting and was among the officials in attendance that evening. His prior role is not detailed in the file. Nothing here alleges wrongdoing; these are descriptions of a public official's stated duties.

## Public Statements and Citizen Scrutiny

### September 11, 2025 Press Role

The official **@FBISaltLakeCity** account posted video of **SAC Robert Bohls** updating media on the fatal shooting at UVU, including a digital-media tip line. Contemporary explainers (e.g. **@Shayan86**) summarized authorities' claims of **images/video of a college-age suspect**, recovery of a weapon, and tracking of movements to a **rooftop shooting location**.

### Transition Context

Project research notes Bohls took over the Salt Lake City field office in **August 2025** after [Mehtab Syed](/People/mehtab-syed)'s departure amid broader FBI leadership changes under Director Kash Patel. Citizen investigators treat the **timing of the SAC transition** (weeks before 9/10) as institutional context for who controlled the federal narrative — **not** as proof of personal wrongdoing by Bohls.

### Commentary Critiques

Accounts such as **@TheCKFiles1** have criticized vague phrasing in later remarks (e.g. "at some point" regarding LE briefings) and noted Bohls's earlier **Provo PD** background. Those are **process critiques**, not criminal accusations.
