# Mike Glover

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Mike Glover is a living person. We document his publicly stated account only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Mike Glover is described in the source material as a former Special Operations veteran who, on a podcast appearance, related an account he attributes to a high-level CIA source. According to Glover, that source told him the FBI was blocking CIA personnel from participating in aspects of the investigation. These are Glover's reported statements, attributed to him and not asserted as fact here.

His account is of interest because it touches on claimed inter-agency friction between the [FBI](/FBI/overview) and [US Intelligence](/US_Intelligence/overview). The claim is second-hand (Glover relaying what a source told him) and remains unverified.

## What is reported

* According to the source summary, Glover stated on the "Mike Drop" podcast (Episode 270) that a high-level CIA official personally told him CIA agents were being blocked by the FBI.
* He reportedly said the obstruction was "physical and direct," describing agents being "turned around at an airport."
* He claimed the matter was escalated and "relayed to the Oval Office," but that no corrective action followed.
* **Counterpoint:** This is a second-hand account attributed to an unnamed source; it has not been confirmed by any agency and both podcast speakers reportedly stopped short of naming individuals. Nothing here is established as fact.

## Attributed Secondhand Claim — CIA Access

Public commentary records Mike Glover stating that a **CIA official told him** the FBI blocked CIA personnel from aspects of the Kirk investigation. That is a **double-hearsay public claim** (Glover recounting an unnamed official). If true, it would matter to [Proof Intel Services](/Proof_Intel_Services/overview) and [CIA](/US_Intelligence/cia); it is **not independently verified** here and implies no personal criminality by Glover. Glover's secondhand account parallels a first-hand one: former NCTC director [Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent) has said on the record that his own center's parallel review of foreign-nexus leads was ordered to stop once Tyler Robinson was in custody, before those leads were exhausted.

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X / Podcast Circuit

Glover's relevance is almost entirely **testimonial**: he is cited for saying a CIA contact told him the **FBI blocked CIA agents** from participating in the Kirk investigation. Independent posters amplify this as evidence of **inter-agency turf or cover** ([CIA](/US_Intelligence/cia), [FBI](/FBI/overview), [Proof Intel Services](/Proof_Intel_Services/overview)).

### How to Read the Claim

1. **Speaker:** Mike Glover (public military/special-operations commentator).
2. **Content:** Secondhand statement about CIA exclusion by FBI.
3. **Missing:** Named CIA official, date of conversation, contemporaneous notes, agency confirmation or denial.
4. **Usefulness:** Raises a discovery target (comms between FBI SLC and other IC components) under any serious transparency law — see [Fix Laws](/Fix/overview).

### Counter-Considerations

Agencies routinely limit cross-agency presence for chain-of-custody and classification reasons. Exclusion, if it occurred, could be bureaucratic rather than nefarious. **Neither interpretation is proven here.**
