# Stew Peters

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Stew Peters is a living person. We document what he reportedly said publicly about the case only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Stew Peters is a commentator and broadcaster. His relevance to the investigation is as the source of a widely circulated segment about Charlie Kirk and alleged Israeli financial pressure. He is not connected to the shooting itself; his page documents a claim he made publicly, which remains contested and independently unverified.

The claim sits inside the broader [Israel](/Israel/overview) and [Motive](/Motive/overview) angles of the case, where commentators have argued that donor and foreign-policy pressure preceded the killing.

## What is reported

- According to a Stew Peters segment amplified in a May 2026 X post by **@FurkanGozukara**, Charlie Kirk was reportedly offered a $1 billion bribe from an Israeli source and reportedly rejected it.
- The same segment reportedly claimed the money was accepted by "his organization" (presumed by the poster to be Turning Point USA) after Kirk's death on September 10, 2025.
- These are claims *as reported by Stew Peters via the amplifying post* — they are **not independently verified**, no supporting documentation appears in the investigation file, and no court has made any such finding.
- **Counterpoint:** No evidence in the project file ties Peters to the shooting, and the "$1 billion" figure has not been corroborated by primary sources. It should be treated as commentary, not fact.

## Open Questions

1. Is there any primary-source document (email, contract, testimony) supporting the "$1 billion offer" claim, or is it solely commentary?
2. Who is the original source Peters relied on, and can that source be identified and verified?
3. Has any named organization responded to or denied the claim?
