# Liz Wheeler

Liz Wheeler is a conservative political commentator and podcast host. On this site her name appears not as a subject of suspicion but as a **skeptic** — she is cited for publicly rebutting the flight-overlap analysis that underpins several of the Israel/Egyptian-plane theories surrounding the September 10, 2025 assassination of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk).

## Connection to the Charlie Kirk Case

Wheeler entered the conversation as a critic of the citizen aircraft-tracking claims. According to her commentary circulated on X, the overlap spreadsheets that appear to show Israeli or Egyptian jets "following" Charlie and [Erika Kirk](/People/erika-kirk) rely on loose matching criteria, and tighten dramatically when the parameters are narrowed.

## Reported Claims (Unverified)

- **Methodology challenge.** Wheeler reportedly argued that the overlap tallies count a "match" whenever an Israel/Egyptian jet was within a broad region (tens of miles) and within roughly plus-or-minus three days of a Kirk location — not at the same airport on the same day. In her telling, *"when you narrow the parameters to Egyptian planes landing on the same day in the same location, the overlap dwindles to one — on 9/10 in Utah."*
- **Supporting critiques.** Other reviewers cited alongside Wheeler flagged UTC-versus-local time-zone errors and misattributed locations in the underlying data. These rebuttals are themselves online commentary; this site presents them for balance, not as the final word.
- **Innocent explanation.** Public ADS-B data shows aircraft activity but, per Grok responses cited in the same threads, establishes no proven surveillance or assassination link. The official finding remains a lone shooter.

## Open Questions

1. Do the overlap counts survive a same-day, same-airport standard, or do they collapse as Wheeler describes?
2. Have any of the disputed flight logs been independently audited for time-zone and location errors?
