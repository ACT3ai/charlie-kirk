## Investigative Value

Brian Davis is named in booking and probable-cause paperwork discussed in public commentary and reporting in connection with the arrest and transport of the accused shooter. The paperwork identifies him as the officer (listed as "UTBDAVIS") on the Inmate Booking Sheet, and, in a government document, an "Agent Davis" with the Utah State Bureau of Investigation is described as handling the accused's driver license at the Washington County Sheriff's Office.

Commentators have flagged a location/timeline question around the arrest, which is presented below as an open question, not a conclusion. See [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview) and [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview).

According to @BasedSamParker on X (July 17, 2026): "Recall also that Brian Davis's name is on the booking sheet and probable cause statement as having arrested Tyler at 10 PM, even though in Davis's court testimony, he didn't arrive until near midnight."

The attached image is a screenshot of the Utah County Sheriff **Inmate Booking Sheet and Probable Cause Statement** for Tyler James Robinson. Per the document as shown, the **arrest date is listed as 09/11/2025 at 10:00 PM** with the **arrest officer listed as DAVIS**, arrest agency "Other," and arrest location "Outside Ut Cnty," while the booking entry timestamp reads 09/12/2025 01:58. The commentator's point is that this 10:00 PM booking-sheet arrest time appears, in his reading, to sit in tension with courtroom testimony he attributes to Officer Davis describing a later arrival.

In a companion post in the same thread, the same author asks why Davis was, in the author's account, directed to travel to St. George at 8:30 PM if the accused did not turn himself in until 9:00 PM, and characterizes Davis's testimony about the sequence of the accused turning himself in as shifting between verb tenses. **These are the opinions and questions of an online commentator, expressly framed by the author as his interpretation ("IMO"), not established facts.**

**Counterpoint:** Booking-sheet fields, arrest-officer designations, and testimony phrasing can differ for ordinary procedural and recollection reasons, and apparent time discrepancies in paperwork are common and often innocuous. Nothing in this post establishes that Officer Davis testified falsely or acted improperly, and no court has made any such finding. Officer Davis has not, as far as the record here shows, publicly responded to these specific claims.
