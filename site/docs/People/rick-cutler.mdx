# Rick Cutler

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

**Rick Cutler** is a retired U.S. Army military police officer and Irving, Texas tactical SWAT sergeant who was on Charlie Kirk's **private security detail** at UVU on September 10, 2025. Citizen investigators widely label him the **"arm puller"** based on courtyard footage showing a brown-shirt, sunglasses-wearing figure near Kirk's camera rig before and after the shot. Cutler was also **in the SUV** during emergency transport. Because he sits at the intersection of **stage footage**, **SUV interior theories**, and unverified **Pero wearable-device** speculation, he is among the most discussed — and most mis-accused — security figures in the case. **This page documents attributed claims only; it does not assert Cutler committed any crime or had foreknowledge of the assassination.**

## Background

### Military and Law Enforcement
Cutler served as a U.S. Army **military police officer** for six years (active duty and reserves) before joining the **Irving, Texas Police Department** for approximately **29 years**, retiring in April 2019 as a **tactical sergeant**. He spent **22 of those 29 years** on the SWAT/tactical team, eventually leading it, and also worked undercover on search warrants and narcotics operations.

### Post-Retirement Work
After retiring in 2019, Cutler moved to **Guatemala** for missionary work. According to his biography on the Kabod International website, he focuses on aftercare for human trafficking victims and community feeding ministry. He has also worked with **Operation Underground Railroad (OUR)** — the anti-trafficking organization founded by [Tim Ballard](/People/tim-ballard) — conducting undercover operations. The investigation file notes Cutler **sued Tim Ballard in 2023** after prior collaboration. He has additionally been involved with United Rescue Alliance and disaster relief training.

### UVU Event Role (Per Investigation File)
The master file identifies Cutler as a **private security operator in the SUV** who reportedly **held Brian Harpole's shirt** during high-speed transport while Harpole administered first aid. Pre-shot, the file describes him in a **brown shirt and sunglasses**, associated with a **camera setup mechanical issue on the left side** before the shooting and with **taking down the camera behind Charlie** — actions visible in public footage, not allegations of wrongdoing.

## Connection to September 10, 2025

- Present at the UVU courtyard event where [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) was shot at approximately **12:23 PM**.
- Part of the **five-person SUV team** with [Dan Flood](/People/dan-flood), [Brian Harpole](/People/brian-harpole), [Justin Davis](/People/justin-davis), and [Frank Turek](/People/frank-turek) during transport to Timpanogos Regional Hospital.
- Citizen footage analyses refer to him as the **"arm puller"** based on his positioning near Kirk during the response sequence. See [Rick Cutler analysis video (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tx84ViFZkaA) for publicly available footage cited in research notes.
- According to official-adjacent reporting summarized in the file, he **assisted in transporting Kirk** to the hospital.

## Public Commentary and Context (Attributed Only)

> **Defamation notice:** Rick Cutler is a living person. Extreme claims on X that he "shot," "triggered," or "strangled" Charlie Kirk are **unverified social-media allegations**. **No court has charged Cutler** in connection with the death of Charlie Kirk. This page records what people are saying and what is reported about his background — **not** a finding of guilt.

### Reported Background

Public summaries circulated on X (including synthesised bios shared in July 2026 threads) describe Cutler as:

- A **retired Irving, Texas police officer** (on the order of ~30 years), including substantial **SWAT / tactical** experience
- Prior **U.S. Army military police** background
- Later work in disaster relief and **contracting related to Operation Underground Railroad** activities in Guatemala (as reported in commentary — not independently re-proven here)
- Present at UVU on **September 10, 2025** as a **volunteer / event-specific** security support person rather than a long-term core TPUSA detail member, according to multiple citizen write-ups and statements attributed to head of security **Brian Harpole** (Integrity Security Solutions) on podcast appearances

### What Video Analysts Claim to See

Citizen investigators (e.g. detailed dossiers by **@SelinaAnneXX**, June 2026, and related frame-by-frame threads) allege, among other things:

- A quick **arm / hand motion** under a sleeve at the moment of the hit
- A **wrist/hand band** some interpret as a wearable control device
- That Cutler helped move Kirk to the SUV and was present during transport
- That he had **little or no prior public footprint** at TPUSA events before 9/10

These are **interpretations of open-source video**, not forensic court findings. Official accounts and Harpole-attributed statements describe Cutler as assisting with first aid / stabilization in the vehicle.

### Counter-Claims and Official Context

- **Tyler Robinson** remains the only person publicly charged for the killing in the Utah case as of the reporting reflected here.
- Summaries attributed to Grok and other public explainers emphasize there is **no official ballistic finding linking Cutler to the shot**.
- Security-protocol critics (e.g. **@KeliRabon** frame analyses) raise questions about medical handling by the team generally; those critiques are about **process**, not proven individual murder.

### Open Questions (Not Accusations)

1. What was the exact contractual / volunteer chain that placed Cutler at the UVU barrier that day?
2. Can wristband / hand-motion claims be evaluated with higher-resolution source video and expert biomechanics — without assuming guilt?
3. What statements has Cutler or his counsel made on the record?
