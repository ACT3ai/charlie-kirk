# Ryan Matta

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Ryan Matta is a living person. We document his publicly reported journalism only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Ryan Matta is described in the source material as a journalist who raised the "screwdriver controversy" surrounding the case and who publicly questioned the timing of an officer's body-camera failure on the roof. His work is presented as open questions rather than accusations, and that is how it is summarized here.

According to the file, Matta framed the body-camera issue as a question worth pressing — noting that a camera charged overnight would not obviously die soon into a shift — while explicitly acknowledging innocent explanations are possible. See the [Cover Up](/CoverUp/overview) and [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview) overviews for the surrounding context.

## What is reported

* According to the file, Matta highlighted the "screwdriver" evidence timeline — reported near the rifle, later described as found on the roof with DNA, then omitted from charging documents.
* He questioned why an officer's body camera reportedly died on the roof, framing it as an open question rather than an accusation.
* He raised three questions the file suggests disclosure laws could address: whether the camera was off vs. genuinely failed, who the unnamed second badge-wearing person on the roof was, and why the screwdriver/DNA statements were left out of the charging documents.
* **Counterpoint:** The file explicitly states these are questions, not proven claims, and notes a body camera can die mid-shift for benign reasons. Nothing here establishes intent by any officer.

## Open Questions

1. Was the body camera genuinely failed, or turned off — and what do charging-stand records show?
2. Who was the second badge-wearing individual on the roof, and where is that person's footage?
3. Why were the screwdriver and DNA statements omitted from the charging documents and probable-cause affidavit?

## Status

**Status:** Alive. No crime is alleged against Ryan Matta on this page; his reporting is summarized as questions with attribution and defamation-safe framing.
