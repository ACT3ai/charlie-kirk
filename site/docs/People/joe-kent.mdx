# Joe Kent

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Joe Kent is a living person. We document his reported official role and the commentary surrounding the investigation, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Joe Kent is described in project notes as a Director-level official at the National Counterterrorism Center (NCTC), which reports up through the Office of the Director of National Intelligence to the executive branch. His relevance to the case comes from commentary — attributed to Candace Owens and cited press — reporting that he was reviewing material on the Charlie Kirk shooting. See the [FBI](/FBI/overview) and [US Intelligence](/US_Intelligence/overview) sections for the surrounding dispute.

Whether any such review occurred, and how it related to the FBI's role, is the subject of open questions rather than established fact. Kent has not been accused of wrongdoing; the framing here is reported commentary, not a court finding.

## What is reported

- According to project notes citing Candace Owens's commentary, Joe Kent "was investigating" the case and reportedly "went through FBI material," which commentary claims prompted friction with the FBI. This is an attributed characterization, not a confirmed account.
- Per the same commentary, a news article is described as being "about how Kash Patel is upset that Joe Kent and Tulsi Gabbard are trying to do their jobs" — a paraphrase of commentary, not a verified quotation or finding.
- The org-chart context in the file places Kent at NCTC → ODNI → the President; that is a description of institutional reporting lines, not an allegation.
- **Counterpoint:** Nothing in the file ties Joe Kent to the shooting or to any misconduct. Reported inter-agency friction, if it occurred, is not evidence of wrongdoing by any party.

## Open Questions

1. Did the NCTC in fact review case material, and under what authority?
2. What was the nature of any reported friction between NCTC/ODNI officials and the FBI over the case?
3. Are there primary records (rather than second-hand commentary) that confirm or refute these reports?
