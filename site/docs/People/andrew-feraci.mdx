# Andrew Feraci

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Andrew Feraci is a living person. We document his reported photography and eyewitness role only. We do **not** assert foreknowledge of the attack or evidence tampering.

## Investigative Value

**Andrew Feraci** is a [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview) staff **photographer** who reportedly stood **feet from [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** when the shot occurred at [UVU](/UVU/overview) on September 10, 2025. His still images and interviews — including commentary referenced as **"Charlie Kirk Shooting Analyzed"** — form an insider visual record parallel to independent press work by [Charles McClintock Wilson](/People/charles-mcclintock-wilson) and student videographers such as [Mathew Franke](/People/mathew-franke).

Investigation-file commentary links Feraci and [Andrew Sypher](/People/andrew-sypher) to similar language about blast origin — suggesting either shared briefing or independent perception that the sound came from inside Kirk's shirt. That remains **interpretive commentary**, not established fact. Unreleased RAW files and complete interview recordings are high-priority disclosure items under [Photos](/Photos/overview) and [Videos](/Videos/overview).

According to X.com witness research (Query 6, peopleglyph):

- Feraci **documented the scene** as a working photographer at the courtyard event.
- He gave **interviews** about what he saw and heard.
- He is listed in the videographer/photographer roster alongside [Brayden Glascock](/People/brayden-glascock) and Wilson.
- His vantage complements TPUSA AV footage handled by [Terryl Farnsworth](/People/terryl-farnsworth) — a separate chain of custody discussed under [cameras](/cameras/overview) and [Censorship](/Censorship/overview).

## Photographic Evidence

Staff photographers typically shoot continuous bursts near stage. If preserved, Feraci's complete assignment folder could:

- Show Kirk's orientation milliseconds before impact.
- Capture reactions of AV crew members such as [Phillip Goldsberry Jr.](/People/phillip-goldsberry-jr).
- Be synchronized against [eyewitness mobile video](/cameras/Eyewitness_Mobile_Video) and [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) audio claims.
