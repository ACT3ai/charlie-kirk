# Dan Merrell

:::caution Legal Disclaimer
Nothing on this page is a claim of fact that Daniel Merrell, or any other living person, knew of, planned, participated in, or covered up any crime, or acted illegally, immorally, or unethically. Performing contracted landscaping work is lawful. This page documents Merrell's own on-camera statements and public commentary about them — not findings of fact. Merrell is presumed innocent; commentators circulating his account have themselves stated they are **not** accusing him of anything.
:::

**Dan (Daniel) Merrell** is a Utah landscaping contractor who operates under the name
**[Hardscape Utah](/TPUSA/LLCs/hardscape-specialties-llc)**. He became a public figure in the
[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) case after an on-camera interview — clipped and widely shared
from around May 2026 — in which he described being called to **install pavers over the UVU
courtyard** where Kirk was shot, on **Sunday, September 14, 2025**, four days after the shooting.

## Connection to the Charlie Kirk case

By his own account, Merrell received an unexpected **"paver emergency"** call and assembled a
crew within roughly 90 minutes to lay pavers over the courtyard. He is emphatic that he was the
**second team on the ground** — the soil had already been excavated (he estimates **"about ten
inches"**) by a different, unidentified crew before he arrived. His statements are the most detailed
first-hand source for the disputed rapid re-paving of the scene.

The full breakdown of what he says he was told, and by whom, is on the dedicated topic pages:

- [10 in. of dirt removed](/CoverUp/Dirt_Removed) — the soil-removal claim and how the "six inches"
  and "ten inches" figures reconcile.
- [Daniel Merrell — Hardscape Utah](/Tent/Daniel_Merrell_Hardscape_Utah) — his verbatim quotes,
  the coordinator statements he attributes, and the video.
- [10 Inches of Dirt Removed Before the Pavers](/Tent/Dirt_Removed_Before_Pavers) — the earlier dig
  he says preceded his crew.

## What Merrell says

According to his recorded statements:

- He was called under an **emergency order** to cover the courtyard **by Monday**, and quotes an
  on-site coordinator saying *"This is above our pay grade. The governor and the FBI said they want
  this done by Monday."* These are **Merrell's reports of what he was told**, not confirmed
  statements from Governor [Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox), the FBI, or the coordinators.
- He arrived to find the ground **already dug out** and took photographs of it.
- He explained that **over-excavation is standard practice** for a proper paver base — a routine
  construction detail, not by itself evidence of anything improper.

## The company: Hardscape Utah vs. Hardscape Specialties LLC

Merrell operates as **Hardscape Utah**. Separate social-media research attributes the courtyard
paving to a differently named entity, **[Hardscape Specialties LLC](/TPUSA/LLCs/hardscape-specialties-llc)**,
whose reported owners ([Michael Powell](/People/michael-powell), [Burton Romrell](/People/burton-romrell))
are **not** Merrell. Whether "Hardscape Utah" and "Hardscape Specialties LLC" describe the same job,
two phases of one job, or a confusion of names in the online discussion **remains unresolved**. This
site does not assert that Merrell owns or is affiliated with Hardscape Specialties LLC.

## Backlash

After his story spread, Merrell reportedly faced online backlash, including fake reviews and
accusations. He has said publicly that his intent was simply to help and that he regrets engaging
with online trolls. Research notes also flag that his name and business were reportedly searched
from Israeli IP addresses in summer 2025 — a circumstantial data point raised by commentators that
is explicitly **not** an accusation against Merrell.
