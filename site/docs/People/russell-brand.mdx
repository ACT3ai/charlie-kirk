# Russell Brand

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Russell Brand is a living person. We document what he reportedly said publicly about the case only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Russell Brand is a commentator and podcaster. His relevance to the investigation is a May 2026 video, in conversation with a figure identified as Alex Jones, in which he amplified claims of a cover-up and possible foreign involvement in Charlie Kirk's death. His remarks are cited as commentary he made publicly — including secondhand sourcing he himself described — not as verified fact.

His statements touch the [Israel](/Israel/overview), [Motive](/Motive/overview), and [Cover Up](/CoverUp/overview) threads, where commentators have argued that foreign-policy and donor pressure preceded the killing.

## What is reported

- In a roughly 4:45 video posted to X (@rustyrockets, May 2026), Russell Brand **reportedly claimed** that two sources he described as present at a cabinet meeting told him that, six days after Kirk's death, senior officials said "we're not looking at foreign involvement" and "we're not looking at any accomplices domestically." He framed this as a "bare minimum… cover-up."
- Brand **reportedly asserted** that Kirk's public text messages showed Kirk was lobbying against an Iran war and was under pressure from "the Israel lobby," and he named "the elements running Israel" as, in his words, "the main suspects."
- The transcript is a Whisper auto-transcription with several names spelled phonetically; the file itself flags that these claims "should be independently sourced."
- **Counterpoint:** Brand's central assertions rest on **unnamed sources he described secondhand** and on his own interpretation; none are documented or verified in the file. His naming of "suspects" is stated opinion and commentary, not a court finding, and no evidence connects Brand to the event.
