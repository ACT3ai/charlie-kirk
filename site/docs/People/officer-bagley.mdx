# Officer Bagley

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Officer Bagley is a living person. We document what is reported about his body-camera footage only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Officer Bagley is named in online commentary about the case in connection with body-worn camera footage from the day of the shooting. According to a narration of what is presented as courtroom testimony (circulated in a July 2026 X post), Bagley's body-cam recording reportedly ended while he was still on the roof, before crime-scene tape was up, and he reportedly explained that the battery had died. The same account associates him with the discovery of a red-and-black screwdriver later entered into evidence.

This is an attributed controversy raised by online commentators, not a court finding. It is included because it bears on the [Cover Up](/CoverUp/overview) and [Proof Not Tyler](/Proof_Not_Tyler/overview) questions the investigation raises. Body-worn cameras genuinely running out of battery mid-shift is a common, documented occurrence; nothing here establishes intent.

## What is reported

- According to a narration of purported testimony (X post by @JOKAQARMY1, dated 2026-07-06), Bagley's body-cam footage reportedly ended while he was still on the roof, and he reportedly attributed this to a dead battery.
- The same account states the recording was about 27 minutes 35 seconds long and that he reportedly found a red-and-black screwdriver, a photo of which was admitted into evidence.
- Commentator Ryan Matta reportedly raised, as an open question, whether the camera should have started the day fully charged from an overnight charging stand.
- **Counterpoint (explicitly noted in the source):** A skeptical framing is the posters' opinion. Cameras taken off the charger, used earlier, or with a degraded battery can die mid-shift. Bagley is a living person and has not been shown to have done anything wrong; nothing here establishes intent.

## Open Questions

1. Was the camera genuinely out of battery, and what do the charging-stand and equipment records show?
2. Who was the reported second person with a badge on the roof, and where is their body-cam footage?
3. Why, per commentators, were the screwdriver / DNA statements not included in the charging documents or the affidavit of probable cause?

## Status

**Status:** Unknown. No crime or wrongdoing is alleged against Officer Bagley; this page documents attributed commentary only, under defamation-safe framing.
