## Investigative Value

**Henry Dells** appears on compiled witness rosters from X.com research (Query 6, peopleglyph) as an **early questioner** at the [UVU](/UVU/overview) courtyard event — bookending the Q&A timeline that ends with [Hunter Kozak](/People/hunter-kozak) asking the **final question** before the shot. If both identifications are correct, Dells and Kozak bracket the audience-interaction window investigators use to reconstruct Kirk's last minutes on stage.

Limited public detail exists beyond list placement, which makes Dells a **latent** witness: FBI 302 reports, unreleased stage feeds, or [eyewitness mobile video](/cameras/Eyewitness_Mobile_Video) could establish his exact position, question topic, and post-shot observations. See [Witnesses](/Witnesses/overview) for broader audio-character claims (loud pop vs. rifle crack).

## Background

Dells has a lower public profile than [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview) staff or press photographers documented on pages like [Andrew Feraci](/People/andrew-feraci) and [Charles McClintock Wilson](/People/charles-mcclintock-wilson). That lower visibility may reflect gag-order constraints, limited media outreach, or simply peripheral positioning — not evidence of concealment.

Stage and crowd observations from any early questioner could still help timeline builders synchronize Q&A pacing with security posture and [Videos](/Videos/overview) timestamps.
