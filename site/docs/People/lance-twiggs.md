# Lance Twiggs

:::caution
This page compiles publicly reported information and commentary. **Nothing here constitutes a finding of wrongdoing by Lance Twiggs.** She has not been charged with any crime. All claims are attributed to their sources. Lance Twiggs is presumed innocent of any wrongdoing.
:::

**Lance Twiggs** (also known as "Luna") is a 22-year-old transgender woman and the romantic partner and roommate of Tyler Robinson. She shared a townhouse with Robinson at 3419 S River Road in St. George, Utah, where they lived together for approximately one year before his arrest. The monthly rent was reportedly $1,800.

## Role in the Investigation

Twiggs is a cooperating witness for the prosecution, not charged with any crime. She is the source of key evidence — text messages and Discord messages from Robinson — that form a significant part of the case against him. The authenticity, context, and timeline of these messages are contested by citizen investigators.

## Key Facts (from court documents and news reporting)

### The Handwritten Letter
According to unsealed search warrant affidavits reported by the Deseret News (April 10, 2026), Robinson texted Twiggs on September 10, 2025, telling her to look under her keyboard, where he had left a handwritten letter. That letter reportedly stated: "I had the opportunity to take out Charlie Kirk, and I took it."

### Messages to FBI
On the night of September 11-12, 2025 (approximately 1:00 AM on the 12th), Twiggs provided the FBI with text messages and Discord messages from Robinson. According to the charging documents, messages attributed to Robinson referenced:

- A need to retrieve a rifle from a drop point
- Leaving a rifle in a bush
- Visually watching the area where the rifle was left
- The rifle being wrapped in a towel
- Engraving bullets and having a scope
- Having changed outfits

### FBI Protection
After Robinson's arrest, the FBI provided Twiggs with a four-person security detail for several months. Twiggs eventually left the St. George townhouse and moved to stay with family out of state. According to Fox News reporting, the FBI protection was later withdrawn.

### Upcoming Testimony
Prosecutors have listed Twiggs as a witness for the prosecution at Robinson's preliminary hearing, currently scheduled for May 18-21, 2026, alongside Robinson's parents.

## Contested Elements

Citizen investigators have raised significant questions about the messages attributed to Robinson's communications with Twiggs:

### Contradictions Within the Messages
According to analysis compiled by investigators (including Jack Posobiec and others):

- Robinson allegedly worried about leaving evidence behind — while simultaneously sending a trail of incriminating messages
- He reportedly said he was sorry to involve Lance — while actively involving her
- He allegedly said he wanted to take the secret to his grave — while confessing to Discord contacts
- He reportedly said he wanted to live to old age — while saying he'd "go out on his own terms"
- He allegedly said he didn't want to face the court system — while voluntarily turning himself in

### Version 1.0 vs. Version 2.0
According to investigation analysis, there appear to be two inconsistent sets of messages:

- **Version 1.0** (shared by Twiggs at 1:00 AM on September 12): Messages referencing Robinson calling cops to a location and "going out on his own terms" — consistent with a planned shootout that never happened.
- **Version 2.0** (appearing in the September 16 indictment): Messages referencing Robinson turning himself in willingly — consistent with what actually happened.

Investigators argue that Version 1.0 reflects an original plan (Robinson to be killed in a confrontation), while Version 2.0 was created after Robinson went off-script by turning himself in peacefully.

### Evidence Format
The evidence for these messages consists of photographs of screens — not the original messages, devices, or Discord server data. The photographs were disclosed in stages, months apart. Investigators question why digital forensics on the original devices have not been presented.

## Open Questions

- Have the original Discord messages and devices been forensically authenticated by an independent party?
- Why were the messages disclosed as photographs rather than through direct device forensics?
- How do investigators explain the contradictions between Version 1.0 and Version 2.0 of the messages?
- What was Twiggs's complete timeline on September 10-12?
- Has Twiggs provided any independent testimony beyond sharing Robinson's messages?
- What is the current status of the FBI's relationship with Twiggs?
