# Harrison Smith

> **Disclaimer:** Inclusion on this page does not imply that this individual had knowledge of, participated in, or committed any illegal or immoral act. This page documents their publicly known connection to the events of September 10, 2025 for investigative reference only.

> **Living Person Notice:** Harrison Smith is a living person. We document what he reportedly said publicly about the case only, with attribution. We do **not** assert wrongdoing.

## Investigative Value

Harrison Smith is a host associated with InfoWars. His relevance to the investigation is as one of several commentators who reportedly stated publicly that, in the days before his death, Charlie Kirk privately expressed fear for his life. His account is cited alongside similar reported statements from other associates and feeds into the "foreknowledge" thread of the case.

His reported statements overlap with the [Motive](/Motive/overview) and [Israel](/Israel/overview) angles and with the broader question, examined under [Proof Intel Services](/Proof_Intel_Services/overview), of whether threats preceded the killing.

## What is reported

- According to commentary compiled in the investigation file, Harrison Smith **went public** saying Charlie Kirk told him days before that he feared he would "be taken out."
- His account is listed alongside similar reported statements attributed to other figures such as [Frank Turek](/People/frank-turek), [Andrew Kolvet](/People/andrew-kolvet), and [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens), each said to describe Kirk voicing fear for his life before September 10, 2025.
- Some X posts in the file are cited "via Harrison Smith report" and "via Harrison Smith, August 14 report," indicating his commentary was a relay point for related claims.
- **Counterpoint:** These are **reported recollections**, not verified statements, and at least one prominent figure has publicly disputed the "he said they were going to kill him" framing. No evidence ties Smith to the event; he is described only as recounting a conversation.

## Open Questions

1. Is there a recording, text, or contemporaneous note that corroborates the reported pre-event conversation?
2. How do the various reported accounts (Smith, Turek, Kolvet, Owens) align on dates and wording?
