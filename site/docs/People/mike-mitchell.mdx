## Investigative Value

Mike Mitchell is named in a government document, reported publicly, as a former Washington County Sheriff's Office deputy who, according to that document, received a phone call from the accused shooter's father and relayed the report up the chain, helping to coordinate the accused's turn-in at the Washington County Sheriff's Office. This is described as an intermediary, coordinating role.

## What is reported

- Per a government document reported publicly, Sheriff Brooksby stated he received a call from a former deputy, identified as Mike Mitchell, who had received a call from the accused's father.
- Per the same document, Mitchell reportedly relayed that the accused had confessed to his father, and Mitchell "coordinated with law enforcement" so the accused could turn himself in at the Washington County Sheriff's Office without confrontation.
- **Counterpoint:** Serving as an intermediary to help arrange a peaceful, non-confrontational turn-in is a lawful, cooperative act. Nothing in the source material shows that Mitchell did anything improper; the account is drawn from an official document describing his coordinating role.
