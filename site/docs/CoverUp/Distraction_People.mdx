# The List of Distraction People (Claims)

:::caution Legal Disclaimer
Nothing on this page is a claim of fact that any living person or organization knew of, planned, participated in, or covered up any crime, or acted illegally, immorally, or unethically. We make no claim that anyone named here knew anything beforehand or did anything wrong. This page documents questions and allegations raised in public commentary — not findings of fact. All persons and organizations named are presumed innocent; the allegations referenced are unproven and have not been established in any court.
:::

A recurring theme in citizen-research threads about the September 10, 2025 shooting at Utah Valley University is that more than one person acted as a **distraction** on the day — people who shouted, claimed to be the shooter, carried a decoy weapon, or otherwise pulled limited police and camera attention in several directions at once. Outside of the court-documented facts about George Zinn, **these are unproven allegations**; none of the people below have been charged as the shooter, and several were cleared, released, or never identified. This page is a directory: it gives four sentences on why each figure is discussed and a button through to their full profile.

:::info The roster
The companion **[Possible Distraction People](/distraction_people/overview)** section profiles the individual figures in this thread. The **most-discussed** are listed below, each linking to their own page. For the full working roster and chronology, see the **[Investigation Index](/distraction_people/Investigation_Index)** and the **[Distraction Timeline](/distraction_people/Distraction_Timeline)**.
:::

## The most-discussed figures

### George Zinn — the "Shoot Me" Man

<a href="/distraction_people/George_Zinn" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>George Zinn — Read now ›</a>

He was the first person taken into custody after the shot, and by the Utah County Sheriff's account he shouted "I shot him, now shoot me!" as detectives searched for the real gunman. Police say he admitted he yelled it deliberately to divert officers — the one on-record "distraction" admission in the entire case. He was cleared as the shooter within hours, then separately pleaded guilty to obstruction and to sexual-exploitation-of-a-minor charges. Citizen posters go further, alleging (unproven) that he was "paid to create a distraction," while Zinn himself denied being paid in a jail-call interview — his page lays out both the court record and the disputed claims.

### Zachariah Qureshi — Detained and Released

<a href="/distraction_people/Zachariah_Qureshi" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Zachariah Qureshi — Read now ›</a>

He was reportedly detained near the UVU event and then released roughly 90 minutes later, never charged as the shooter. Some threads describe him as a Heritage Foundation intern within about 25 feet of Kirk and call him an "original" or "intended" patsy before the narrative settled on Tyler Robinson. He sits at the center of the [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel) "suspect in custody" timing debate — Patel publicly announced a suspect, then the person was released with no explanation. Researchers ask whether that premature announcement inadvertently halted the search and opened a window for the real perpetrator to leave; no public evidence ties him to the shooting.

### Russell Kennington — Scene-Tampering Report

<a href="/distraction_people/Russell_Kennington" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Russell Kennington — Read now ›</a>

Circulated booking screenshots described a retired Army combat medic arrested for criminal trespass and felony obstruction for allegedly tampering with the crime scene. A photo showed a sheriff's deputy beside him carrying what some called a bolt-action rifle, fueling disputed "second weapon" questions. But a GRAMA open-records request reportedly came back saying nobody was arrested — directly conflicting with the online "arrest" claims. Posters add that he was released the same day with zero bail and that charges were quietly dropped weeks later; the record here is genuinely contested and unresolved.

### SMBB Break Man — police allegedly held for hours

<a href="/distraction_people/SMBB_Break_Man" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>SMBB Break Man — Read now ›</a>

Scanner audio shared by @ProjectConstitu reportedly caught officers being told to stand by with a man who "stated there's an APB out on him for the shooting at UVU." The location — "SMBB Break" — is read as the Sorenson Molecular Biotechnology Building in Salt Lake City, roughly 43 minutes north of the UVU scene. The claim is that officers were told to stay with him for about two hours and, per the audio, given "clear direction NOT to interview." If accurate, that would pull officers and hours away from the real scene — but the man is unidentified and there is no official confirmation.

### Pellet Gun Man (Provo) — decoy-weapon question

<a href="/distraction_people/Pellet_Gun_Man" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Pellet Gun Man — Read now ›</a>

Scanner-style posts describe a man biking through downtown Provo carrying what observers identified as a break-barrel .177 air rifle — a pellet gun, not a firearm. Sightings were floated near Freedom Boulevard and in front of Joe Vera's Mexican Restaurant. Posters ask whether he was a decoy weapon-carrier meant to generate competing "armed suspect" reports and split police attention miles from UVU. The claim rests on low-context photos and audio, the man is unidentified, and a pellet gun is not the murder weapon.

### Curly Hair / USA-Chant Man — one man, several labels

<a href="/distraction_people/Curly_Hair_Man" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Curly Hair Man — Read now ›</a>

A curly-haired man in the crowd was flagged by poster @RtothepowerofX, who alleged he turned toward the shooter's direction, stood when others ducked, and appeared to react approvingly. The same figure is elsewhere identified as the man who chanted "USA" seconds after the shot — who reportedly said he did it to create a calming distraction, not to celebrate ([USA-Chant Man](/distraction_people/USA_Chant_Man)). Online researchers have tried to attach real names to him — [David Sprague](/distraction_people/David_Sprague), [James Norman Rawlinson](/distraction_people/James_Norman_Rawlinson), and [Matt Taber](/distraction_people/Matt_Taber) — none confirmed, and this site does not republish accusations against private people. Innocent explanations (people freeze or react unpredictably under shock) are at least as plausible; the entry exists only to document that the claim circulates.

### Andrew Piskadlo — South-Door Photographer

<a href="/distraction_people/Andrew_Piskadlo" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Andrew Piskadlo — Read now ›</a>

Andrew Piskadlo is a photographer reportedly positioned at a south-door area at the UVU event. He surfaces in citizen discussion about who held camera positions and sightlines during the shooting. This page accuses him of nothing and presents only his stated public role. Unproven allegations attached to him elsewhere are deliberately excluded.

### Black Clothing Figure — top rooftop suspect (unidentified)

<a href="/distraction_people/Black_Clothing_Figure" style={{display:'inline-block', margin:'0.25rem 0 0.75rem', padding:'0.5rem 1rem', background:'#1a73e8', color:'#fff', borderRadius:'6px', textDecoration:'none', fontSize:'0.95rem', fontWeight:600}}>Black Clothing Figure — Read now ›</a>

An unidentified man in all-black tactical clothing matches a dispatch description, had a face-to-face encounter with a construction crew, was tracked by a canine, and appears in the only pre-shooting rooftop video. He has been reclassified as the top rooftop-shooter suspect and now appears under Suspects on the People index. Unlike most figures on this list, this thread points *toward* the shooting rather than away from it. No living person is named or accused — the figure remains unidentified.

## Counterarguments

The distraction theory has obvious weaknesses, and honest treatment requires stating them:

- **Chaos is normal at mass-casualty scenes.** Panicked bystanders shouting, running, or behaving erratically is well documented at real tragedies and does not require a script.
- **Brief detentions and quick releases are routine** when police sweep a scene and rule people out — not proof of staging.
- **Many claims rely on subjective photo comparison** and "crisis actor" pattern-matching, methods that have repeatedly produced false accusations against innocent bystanders in past tragedies.
- **An admission of obstruction is not an admission of conspiracy.** Zinn attributed his conduct to a personal "reaction," denied being paid, and authorities say his actions were not coordinated with the shooter.

The strongest honest statement is the modest one: early reporting around UVU was genuinely confused, multiple people were briefly detained, one man (Zinn) admitted he deliberately obstructed the scene, and *that confusion itself* is what these threads point to — not a documented roster of paid actors.
