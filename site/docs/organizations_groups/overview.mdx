# Organizations & Groups

## Overview

This hub points to **organizations and informal groups** that appear in open-source discussion of the Charlie Kirk case — as event hosts, security or AV vendors, government agencies, advocacy networks, or subjects of early speculative linkage. **Organizational presence is not guilt.** True institutional facts (e.g., TPUSA organized the UVU event) stay factual; criminal-conspiracy claims about organizations stay **attributed**.

## Key Areas

- [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview) as event organizer and post-event institutional actor
- Private security and AV vendors ([Security Team](/Security_Team/overview))
- Federal and local agencies ([FBI](/FBI/overview), [Law Enforcement](/Law_Enforcement/overview), [government organizations](/government_organizations/overview))
- Israel-adjacent advocacy and donor networks ([Israel](/Israel/overview), [Mossad & TPUSA commentary](/Israel/Mossad_TPUSA/overview))
- Early-scrutinized activist groups and online communities (with careful non-guilt framing)

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X

Commentators argue that understanding **who contracted whom** (security, AV, stage, temporary fencing, paving after the fact) is as important as naming a shooter. Others warn against **guilt by organizational adjacency**. Both instincts matter: map the org chart, demand contracts and radios logs via [Fix laws](/Fix/overview), and refuse to treat a vendor invoice as a murder indictment.
