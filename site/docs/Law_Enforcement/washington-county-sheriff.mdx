# Washington County Sheriff & the Surrender

This page covers the role that **Washington County Sheriff [Nate Brooksby](/People/nate-brooksby)** reportedly played in the surrender of the charged suspect, **[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)**, the day after the **September 10, 2025** shooting.

**Status:** Alive

## The Reported Surrender

According to **@SteveCameronPr1** ([Steve Cameron](/People/steve-cameron), Apr 17 2026), Sheriff **Nate Brooksby** received the tip that led to Robinson turning himself in, and reportedly facilitated or arranged the surrender. Per the same source, Robinson turned himself in the day after the shooting, accompanied by his parents and a family friend.

These details are attributed to that named source and reporting around it. Robinson is the **charged** defendant and has **not** been convicted; references to his alleged role are allegations, not established facts.

## The Surrender Chain, per the Government Document

A government document quoted in the master investigation file lays out the tip-to-surrender sequence in detail. Per that account, on **September 11, 2025**, **Sheriff Smith** of the **Utah County Sheriff's Office** received a phone call at approximately **2004 hours (8:04 PM)** from **Sheriff Brooksby** of the Washington County Sheriff's Office. Brooksby said he had been called by a **former deputy, identified as [Mike Mitchell](/People/mike-mitchell)**, who in turn had been called by **Tyler Robinson's father** — to whom Robinson had reportedly confessed being the UVU shooter. Per the document, Mitchell coordinated with law enforcement so Robinson could turn himself in without a confrontation.

The same document states that Robinson **arrived at the Washington County Sheriff's Office accompanied by his parents at approximately 2226 hours (10:26 PM)**. It notes he was **"not wearing clothing consistent with the clothing observed on surveillance cameras"** — arriving in a dark hat, a **maroon-colored T-shirt**, jeans, and **white/gray Converse-style shoes**, per a photo furnished by **Agent Davis** of the Utah State Bureau of Investigation. Robinson's mother reportedly relayed that he had expressed suicidal ideations. These facts come from a quoted official document in the research file and describe the reported surrender logistics; they are the government's own account, not adjudicated findings.

## A Reported Timeline Dispute

Researchers in the master file flag a conflict between the official surrender narrative and other records. Per the file, an **"Inmate Booking Sheet and PC Statement"** lists an arrest time of **10:00 PM on September 11**, and Robinson was reportedly **Mirandized at 6:25 PM on September 11** — both earlier than the 8:04 PM Brooksby-to-Smith call. The file contrasts this with what it calls the **"fed slop"** database entry of a **4:00 AM September 12** arrest, arguing the later timestamp reflects the time needed to prepare an "Affidavit of Probable Cause" for a judge rather than the moment Robinson actually came into custody. Official spokesperson **Sgt. [Lance Alfred](/People/lance-alfred)** is cited among the sources for the surrender account. This is citizen-investigator reasoning about document timestamps, not a court finding.

## The Later Resignation

Per the same reporting, Brooksby **later resigned**, with no stated reason given publicly. The absence of a stated reason is noted by researchers as an open question, not as evidence of any wrongdoing.

This page asserts nothing improper about Sheriff Brooksby. He is a living public official, and the reported facts here — that he handled the tip and surrender and that he later resigned — are presented as a reported sequence whose significance remains an open question. His resignation is discussed further alongside other departures on the [Official Resignations](./official-resignations) page.

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X

The **March 31, 2026** resignation of **Sheriff Nate Brooksby** became a major X cluster when paired with the reported **ATF bullet/rifle non-match** (**@EvanAKilgore**, **@BowesChay**, **@daviddunn177**). Separately, **Police1**-style reporting amplified by users such as **@brenda11831** quotes Brooksby giving **"full credit"** to retired colleague **Mike Mitchell** for a calm surrender because the family trusted him.

Citizen timeline analysts continue to dispute **when** Robinson was first under control of Washington County personnel, whether **body-worn cameras** captured the surrender (some claim **zero** WCSO surrender footage has been produced), and whether **phone seizure / Miranda** timing affects Discord confession authenticity. Those disputes are developed further in [Proof Not Tyler — Discord Custody Timeline](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Discord_Custody_Timeline).
