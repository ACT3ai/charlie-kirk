# N872RA Provo Arrival

A **Hawker Beechcraft** business jet (reported 7-seat) registered **N872RA** is listed in flight records circulated by independent investigators as one of several private aircraft arriving at **Provo Municipal Airport (PVU)** on September 10, 2025. Its value to the investigation is not a standalone smoking gun — sourcing on this specific tail is thin — but as part of the **Provo arrival cluster**, and because it shares a **Santa Barbara** origin with two other items researchers have flagged: the [N560TW](/Planes/N560TW) jet, and the disputed "tarmac hug" timeline involving a plane said to have landed from Santa Barbara.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N872RA
* **Type:** Hawker Beechcraft business jet (reported 7 seats)
* **Registry / operator / owner:** Not yet publicly identified in the investigation notes
* **Role on the list:** A private aircraft logged arriving at Provo (PVU) on September 10, 2025

## Flight history relevant to the case

As reproduced from flight-record screenshots circulated by independent investigators:

* **September 10, 2025, ~2:19 p.m. MT** — Departed **Santa Barbara, California**.
* **September 10, 2025, ~4:36 p.m. MT** — Arrived **Provo (PVU)**.

This is a late-afternoon arrival, hours after the shooting. No public reporting ties N872RA, its crew, or its passengers to the event. It is logged alongside the other day-of arrivals — N40JD (Scottsdale → Provo, ~2:29 p.m.), N102DZ (Charlie Kirk's plane, into Provo ~3:31 p.m.), and N560TW — so its manifest can be reviewed in context.

## The Santa Barbara connection

Two separate threads in the investigation single out a **Santa Barbara** origin on September 10, which is why N872RA is flagged rather than treated as routine traffic:

* **Shared route with N560TW.** The [N560TW](/Planes/N560TW) Cessna Citation — owned by T.W. Lewis Aviation LLC, tied to a major TPUSA donor — is reported by Candace Owens to have flown Scottsdale → Provo (land 10:06 a.m.) → **Santa Barbara** (depart ~11:37 a.m.) → Scottsdale (~12:44 p.m.) that morning. Researchers ask whether the Santa Barbara leg of N560TW and the later Santa Barbara departure of N872RA are connected, or coincidental.
* **The contested "tarmac hug" timeline.** According to Candace Owens's reporting, TPUSA and Erika Kirk stated she met TPUSA spokesman Andrew Kolvet on the Provo runway when **his plane landed from Santa Barbara** — a plane Owens says did not land in Provo until about **4:40 p.m.** That arrival time and Santa Barbara origin closely match the N872RA record above (Santa Barbara → Provo, ~4:36 p.m.). The investigation notes do not explicitly confirm N872RA is the Kolvet aircraft; the overlap in origin and arrival time is noted as a possible identification to verify, not an established one.

## Theories about the aircraft's role

These are open, unproven interpretations circulated by independent researchers — not conclusions, and not statements of fact about any person:

* **Routine arrival, no connection.** The simplest reading: a private charter that happened to land at Provo that afternoon with no tie to the case. Nothing in public reporting contradicts this.
* **The "tarmac hug" aircraft.** That N872RA may be the Santa Barbara plane referenced in the disputed Kolvet/Erika Kirk runway-meeting timeline. If so, its manifest and exact arrival time bear on the broader question Owens raised about when Erika Kirk reached the hospital. This is a possible identification to confirm, not a finding.
* **Part of a Santa Barbara movement.** That the two flagged Santa Barbara legs on September 10 (N560TW's and N872RA's) reflect coordinated movement of people through that airport. No evidence currently links the two flights beyond the shared airport.

## Open questions

1. Who is the registered owner and operator of N872RA?
2. Who was aboard N872RA on the September 10 arrival into Provo?
3. Is N872RA the Santa Barbara aircraft referenced in the Kolvet / Erika Kirk "tarmac hug" timeline, or a different plane?
4. Is there any connection between this Santa Barbara departure and the Santa Barbara leg flown by N560TW that morning?

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N872RA](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n872ra) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N872RA](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N872RA) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
