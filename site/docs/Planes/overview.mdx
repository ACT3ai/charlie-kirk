## What this area covers

This section catalogs the aircraft activity around UVU and the broader Utah corridor in the days surrounding Charlie Kirk's assassination. The focus is on aircraft whose movements deviate from routine civilian or commercial patterns — military reconnaissance jets, foreign-registered government aircraft, US government VIP transports, and private planes that disabled their transponders mid-flight. Each plane covered here has been independently surfaced in public investigation by Candace Owens, Ian Carroll, Sam Parker, and other researchers tracking the case.

## The substance of the evidence

Several flight patterns recur across the data. A US Army ISR-capable Bombardier Global 6500 (N1098L, callsign AXLE10) made low-altitude passes within a few miles of the UVU campus on the day of the shooting. A fleet of at least five Egyptian Air Force jets (SU-BTT, [SU-BND](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada), [SU-BTU](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada), [SU-BTV](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada), [SU-BGM](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada)) was in and out of Provo Municipal Airport in the days surrounding the event, with SU-BTT's morning departure on September 10 showing tracking inconsistencies suggesting it actually originated at Dugway Proving Ground rather than Provo. A Special Air Mission (SAM 99-0404, a USAF C-37A) made a Fort Huachuca stop on September 8-9 that whistleblower reporting links to a 12-lieutenant-colonel meeting one day before the assassination. A private plane with tail number N888KG departed Utah after the shooting, landed in Wendover, and disabled its transponder. A separate aerial-survey contractor aircraft (Piper N59906, operated by MARC Inc.) is reported to have flown four low-altitude camera-and-LiDAR passes over the campus on the morning of the event. Charlie Kirk's own inbound plane (N102DZ) anchors the day's timeline, while several private jets — N560TW from Scottsdale, plus the Provo arrivals N40JD and N872RA — landed at or routed through Provo around the same window. Independent investigator John Cullen has flagged a further tail (N55906), and the proposed disclosure laws add N582MM, an aircraft reported to be used by TPUSA insiders.

## Where to start

Readers new to the aircraft angle should start with [N1098L Spy Plane](./N1098L/overview) — it has the most detailed public reporting and the clearest geometric link to the kill zone. Readers focused on the foreign-intelligence angle should go directly to [SU-BTT Egyptian Jet](./SU-BTT) and then [Egyptian Plane Armada](./Egyptian-Armada). Readers tracking the US-government command-and-control angle should read [SAM 99-0404 Flight](./SAM-99-0404) for the Fort Huachuca timeline. The remaining pages address transponder anomalies and the ISR program context that frames why these flights matter operationally.
