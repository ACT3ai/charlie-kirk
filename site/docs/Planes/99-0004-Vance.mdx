# 99-0004 Vance Flight

A US Air Force **C-32A** (the military Boeing 757-200 used for executive transport), tail number **99-0004**, was logged on public ADS-B data flying to **Las Vegas on May 27, 2025** under the call sign **SAM912** and reportedly carrying the "AF2" / Air Force Two designation used for the Vice President's aircraft. Investigators tracking aircraft around the case flagged the flight and asked an open question — what business took Vice President JD Vance to Las Vegas that day. This page records the tail number and the question; nothing here alleges wrongdoing by any person.

## Why this aircraft is in the catalog

This jet is **not** placed at UVU or over Orem. It surfaces in the investigation's aviation tracking as a separate VIP-transport data point — a C-32A operating as Air Force Two roughly three and a half months before the September 10, 2025 assassination. It is logged here for completeness alongside the other government and VIP aircraft (the Hegseth-linked [SAM 99-0404](/Planes/SAM-99-0404) C-37A and the [N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview) Army ISR jet) so the full set of flagged tail numbers is preserved in one place.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** 99-0004
* **Type:** US Air Force C-32A (Boeing 757-200 / "B752" in tracker shorthand) VIP transport
* **ICAO hex:** ADFEBA (per ADS-B Exchange)
* **Call sign:** SAM912 (Special Air Mission)
* **Reported designation:** "AF2" — Air Force Two, the call/role used when the Vice President is aboard
* **Date logged:** May 27, 2025
* **Destination flagged:** Las Vegas, NV

Note: tail **99-0004** (C-32A, this page) is a distinct aircraft from tail **99-0404** (a C-37A Gulfstream V), which is covered separately on the [SAM 99-0404](/Planes/SAM-99-0404) page. The two tail numbers differ by a single digit and are easy to confuse.

## Associated identifiers in the same record

The tracking note that surfaced this flight listed several identifiers together in one block. They are recorded here as-found, without asserting any connection between them:

* **N885LS** — a separate tail number appearing in the same data block; no further public detail has been established for it in the case material.
* **SU-BTV** — one of the Egyptian-registered jets covered on the [Egyptian Plane Armada](/Planes/Egyptian-Armada) page.

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — 99-0004](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/99-0004) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — 99-0004](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=99-0004) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
