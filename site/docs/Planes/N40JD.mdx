# N40JD Provo Arrival

A **Beechcraft Premier 1** (6-seat light business jet) registered **N40JD** is listed in flight records circulated by independent investigators as one of several private aircraft arriving at **Provo Municipal Airport (PVU)** on September 10, 2025, the day Charlie Kirk was assassinated at Utah Valley University in nearby Orem. It is on the investigation's aircraft list for one narrow reason: to build a complete picture of who flew into Provo around the event so that no traveler is overlooked. Nothing in the public record ties this aircraft, its crew, or its passengers to the shooting.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N40JD
* **Type:** Beechcraft Premier 1 — light business jet, reported 6 seats
* **Registry / operator / owner:** Not publicly identified in the investigation record; FAA registration would name the registrant.
* **Program / callsign:** None reported. Tracked by its civil registration N40JD.
* **Role on the list:** A private aircraft logged arriving at Provo (PVU) on the afternoon of September 10, 2025.

## Flight history relevant to the case

As reproduced from flight-record screenshots circulated by independent investigators:

* **Departed** Scottsdale, Arizona at **~12:27 p.m. local**.
* **Arrived** into Provo (PVU) at **~2:29 p.m. MDT**.

This is an afternoon arrival, after the shooting (the shot was fired shortly after noon MDT). The same set of records groups N40JD with the other same-day inbound private jets — Charlie Kirk's own aircraft [N102DZ](/Planes/N102DZ), which reached Provo at ~3:31 p.m., and [N872RA](/Planes/N872RA), which arrived from Santa Barbara at ~4:36 p.m. The morning flight that first drew Candace Owens' attention, [N560TW](/Planes/N560TW), is a separate aircraft on the same Provo list. No public reporting establishes a connection among these flights beyond their shared destination and date.

## Theories about the aircraft's role

The flight's public tracking has been **referenced in podcasts and independent investigations** as part of broader arrival/departure pattern analysis around UVU and Provo. Some of those investigator discussions have, **according to commentary circulated on X**, connected the flight to figures such as **Tyler Bowyer**, a Turning Point USA figure.

That association is **reported by commentators and unproven**. There is no evidence in the public record tying Mr. Bowyer, the aircraft, its crew, or its passengers to the event, and no wrongdoing by him is alleged or established. It is noted here only to explain why the flight has drawn discussion. The page treats N40JD as an open item on a manifest-review list — not as a theory of involvement.

The 2:29 p.m. Provo arrival time has also been swept into the broader **Egyptian-plane theory** circulating on X. An unverified screenshot, shared in those threads, claims that a parked Egyptian-registered jet ([SU-BND](/Planes/SU-BND)) sitting on the ground at Provo on September 10 powered its transponder on **only three times that day**, and labels the third of those moments as a **2:29 p.m. Provo landing** — the same minute these records log N40JD touching down. That is the entire connection: a matching clock time on a screenshot, presented by its poster as significant.

This is an **unverified online claim**, not established fact, and it should be read with the same skepticism the rest of the Egyptian-plane material draws. Critics such as **Liz Wheeler** argue these overlap claims rely on loose criteria and that several rest on **UTC versus local time-zone errors** in the underlying logs, which can make unrelated events appear synchronized. Grok and other reviewers note that public ADS-B data shows aircraft activity but establishes **no proven surveillance or assassination link**, and the official finding remains a lone shooter. Nothing about the shared 2:29 p.m. timestamp identifies who was aboard N40JD or ties the flight to any plan.

## Why this flight is scrutinized

N40JD earns its place on the investigation list because, **per some discussions and X OSINT**, its arrival into Provo has been flagged as **mysterious or scrutinized** within the event window — described by some posters as occurring **pre- or around** the September 10 timeframe rather than cleanly after it. The aircraft is **flagged in citizen timelines** that catalog every plane logged at PVU around the assassination.

* Treated as part of a **broader Provo/UVU arrival and departure pattern analysis** circulating on X, where investigators map who flew in and out around the event.
* **Tracked in podcasts and independent investigations** rather than mainstream reporting; public ADS-B history is the underlying data.
* Tied, **per some discussions and unconfirmed**, to **private operators with possible links to TPUSA figures** such as **Tyler Bowyer** — an association that is **alleged, unverified, and not established as fact**. No wrongdoing by Mr. Bowyer is alleged or proven, and nothing connects him, the crew, or the passengers to the shooting.
* **Public details remain limited** — the operator, owner, and occupants are not named in the public record, which is part of why the flight stays an open item.

These points reflect **online commentary and citizen-timeline framing**, not confirmed findings. They are recorded here to explain the scrutiny, not to assert that the flight had any role in the event.

## Open questions

3. What was the stated purpose of the trip, and where did the occupants go after landing at PVU?
4. Is there any documented connection between this flight and any named individual, or is the association purely commentary?

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N40JD](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n40jd) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N40JD](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N40JD) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
