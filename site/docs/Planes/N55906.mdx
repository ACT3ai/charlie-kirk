# N55906 Cullen Flight

Tail number **N55906** is an aircraft that independent investigator **John Cullen** ([@I_am_JohnCullen](/Influencers/x/john-cullen)) has repeatedly flagged as a second flight of interest in the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) case, naming it directly alongside the **[N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview)** HADES surveillance plane. In Cullen's own framing, "HADES N1098L is the elephant in the room. N55906 is the gorilla in the room." Public reporting on N55906 is thin, and it is recorded here as an open lead worth pulling records on — not as an established finding.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N55906
* **Type:** Not confirmed in public reporting
* **Registry / operator / owner:** Not confirmed in public reporting
* **Flagged by:** John Cullen ([@I_am_JohnCullen](/Influencers/x/john-cullen)), who paired it with N1098L using the "elephant in the room" / "gorilla in the room" framing

## Flight history relevant to the case

The public record contains no confirmed route, timestamps, or altitude data for N55906 in connection with September 10, 2025. What exists is Cullen's call to examine the tail number as part of the broader array of aircraft activity scrutinized around **Utah Valley University**. No dated flight log, ADS-B track, or arrival/departure pairing has been published that this page can responsibly cite. That absence is itself the point: investigators are asking for the records precisely because they are not yet public.

## Theories about the aircraft's role

These are open, attributed theories — not conclusions.

* **A paired surveillance asset (Cullen).** John Cullen has publicly listed N55906 as an "interesting flight" worth examining alongside N1098L, the aircraft he describes as a HADES-class surveillance platform. The implication he raises is that more than one aircraft of interest may have been aloft in the relevant window. Cullen has not published a confirmed route or operator for N55906, so this remains a request to investigate rather than a documented finding.
* **A transcription or data-entry artifact.** Because N55906 differs from the better-documented **[N59906](/Planes/N59906)** by a single digit, one open possibility is that some references to "N55906" are a misreading of N59906, the MARC Inc. survey Piper. This page treats the two as distinct (see below), but does not rule out that the lead resolves to N59906 once records are pulled.

## A note on the similar tail number

The investigation also tracks **[N59906](/Planes/N59906)** — the **MARC Inc.** Piper PA-31 Navajo Chieftain reported making four low-altitude passes over the UVU campus. N55906 and N59906 differ by a single digit and should not be assumed to be the same aircraft. The investigation's aircraft list treats them as **two distinct tail numbers**; confirming whether N55906 is a genuinely separate flight or a transcription of N59906 is itself one of the open questions below.

## OSINT tracking on X

N55906 surfaces almost entirely through open-source intelligence (OSINT) work on X, not through conventional reporting. **John Cullen** ([@I_am_JohnCullen](/Influencers/x/john-cullen)) and **similar accounts on X** have flagged the tail number and folded it into the **extensive plane-array entries** the investigation has been compiling — a running list of aircraft scrutinized for **timing and patterns near the event**. Per Cullen and these accounts, N55906 belongs in that array as one more flight worth pulling records on around **September 10, 2025**.

The tail number is **highlighted in deep-dive threads on X**, where it is discussed alongside N1098L and other aircraft of interest. All of this is alleged OSINT analysis: no operator, route, or wrongdoing is established here, and the framing reflects what these accounts have asked investigators to examine rather than any confirmed finding.

## Tracked trip in the array

The investigation's plane array records N55906 as a flagged entry rather than a logged point-to-point flight:

* **Route:** Listed as **"Various"** with the note that it fits **patterns near the event area** — no single confirmed origin or destination is published.
* **Timing:** Tied to **September 10, 2025 context** and the broader event timeframe.

* **Why it is listed:** Per John Cullen and other X accounts, it is alleged to be **part of broader suspicious aerial activity noted on X** in the relevant window. This is an open lead, not a conclusion.

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N55906](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n55906) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N55906](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N55906) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
