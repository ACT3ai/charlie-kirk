# N888KG Wendover Vanish

A private aircraft with tail number **N888KG** is reported to have departed Utah after the September 10, 2025 shooting, landed at **Wendover** (a desert airstrip on the Utah/Nevada border), and disabled its ADS-B transponder mid-flight — with no flight plan filed for the onward leg.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N888KG
* **Type:** Private aircraft (early public reporting did not confirm the model)
* **Reported type:** Independent flight trackers identify the aircraft as a **Bombardier Challenger 300 (BD-100-1A10)**.
* **Reported operator/owner:** **N888KG LLC**, which commentators associate with **Derek Maxfield** and the companies/foundations **Komigo** and the **Saprea** foundation; some commentary notes reported **Israel connections**. These ownership associations are reported by commentators and unproven, and naming the entity is not an accusation against any individual.
* **Departure airport:** Utah (early reporting did not confirm the specific origin; later reporting identifies **Provo Airport (PVU)** — see below)
* **Destination:** Variously reported as **Wendover Airport** (a desert airstrip with limited surveillance infrastructure) in the original framing, and as **Page Municipal Airport (PGA), Arizona** in later flight-record reporting (see below)

## Reported flight pattern

* Departed Utah after the UVU shooting on September 10, 2025.
* Landed at Wendover.
* Roughly **30-46 minutes into the flight (approximately 7:44-7:58 PM MDT)**, the aircraft's ADS-B transponder was disabled, causing it to disappear from public radar.
* No flight plan was filed for the onward leg from Wendover.
* The destination airstrip is described in public reporting as having "cameras off" — minimal surveillance coverage.

## Provo departure and the Page, Arizona routing (later reporting)

This page is titled "Wendover Vanish," but later flight-record reporting describes a different — and more documented — version of the same aircraft's movements. Both are presented here as reported; the "Wendover" framing is attributed to early conspiracy commentary, while the Provo/Page routing comes from subsequent flight-tracking reporting and fact-checks.

According to that later reporting (sometimes called the "Provo Vanish"):

* The aircraft **departed Provo Airport (PVU)** — roughly 12 minutes from UVU — about **one hour after the shooting**, at approximately **1:11–1:20 p.m. MDT on September 10, 2025**.
* It flew **toward Page, Arizona**, and experienced a **mid-flight ADS-B / transponder gap of roughly 30 to 50-plus minutes** while en route, then reappeared.
* It **landed at Page Municipal Airport (PGA) at approximately 1:48 p.m. MDT**.
* It then **returned to Provo, landing at approximately 3:06 p.m. MDT**.

Conspiracy commentary suspects the aircraft may have served as a getaway vehicle for the shooter or related actors. That framing is a **reported allegation, not an established fact**.

## Owner statement and official response

* The owner has stated that the flight was a **prescheduled flight**, and that the **FAA/ATC termination of tracking was standard** behavior for non-towered airports.
* The owner and officials attribute the **tracking gap to poor coverage over a rural area**, not to an illegally disabled transponder.
* **FBI Director Kash Patel addressed the flight publicly**, attributing the ADS-B gap to **rural coverage** rather than an illegal transponder shutoff.
* **Fact-checks report that N888KG is not part of the official investigation** into the assassination.

These statements are included so the owner's stated explanation and the official response are represented alongside the suspicions raised by commentators.

## Why disabling the transponder matters

ADS-B (Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast) has been **FAA-mandated since 2020** for most US aircraft operating in controlled airspace. It broadcasts position, altitude, and aircraft identity in real time. Disabling ADS-B mid-flight without prior special authorization is illegal and can result in fines of $25,000 or more. Authorized exceptions are typically limited to national-security operations or declared in-flight emergencies.

The combination of:

* Departure timing (immediately after a high-profile assassination),
* Choice of a low-surveillance desert airstrip,
* Mid-flight transponder shutoff, and
* No filed onward flight plan,

is the basis for the public framing that N888KG was used to remove personnel from the area without a traceable record.

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N888KG](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n888kg) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N888KG](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N888KG) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
