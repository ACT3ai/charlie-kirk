# N888KG Provo Vanish

**N888KG** is the private jet at the center of one of the most-discussed flight theories on X about the Charlie Kirk assassination. It departed **Provo Airport (PVU)** — about a 10–12 minute drive from UVU — roughly one hour after Charlie Kirk was shot on September 10, 2025, flew south toward Arizona, lost its **ADS-B transponder signal mid-flight for an extended gap**, then reappeared and returned to Provo. Commentators call this the "Provo Vanish" (and, in an earlier framing, the "Wendover Vanish"). The aircraft's owner, the FAA, and FBI Director Kash Patel have all stated the gap was routine and that the jet is **not part of the official investigation**. Both the suspicion and the rebuttals are presented below.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N888KG
* **Type:** **Bombardier Challenger 300 (BD-100-1A10)** — a white-liveried business jet, per independent flight trackers.
* **Registered operator/owner:** **N888KG LLC** (reported registered in Lehi, Utah), with **Derek Maxfield** listed as an associated owner in some public databases. Commentators further associate the entity with the companies/foundations **Komigo** and the **Saprea** foundation, and some note reported **Israel connections**. These ownership associations are reported by commentators, are not independently confirmed, and naming the entity here is not an accusation against any individual.
* **Departure airport:** Provo Airport (PVU), Utah.
* **Reported route:** Provo → toward **Page Municipal Airport (PGA), Arizona** → return to Provo. (An earlier, less-documented version of the story named **Wendover**, a desert airstrip on the Utah/Nevada border; later flight-record reporting points to the Page, AZ routing instead.)

## Flight history relevant to the case

Times are MDT on September 10, 2025 unless noted. Charlie Kirk was shot at approximately **12:23 PM MDT**.

* **September 9, 2025 (the day before):** Per the investigation notes, **"n888kg" spiked in Google Trends searches in Utah** — a search of the tail number reportedly occurring before the shooting. This pre-event search interest is flagged as a possible foreknowledge signal, though it is unverified and could have innocent explanations.
* **~1:11–1:20 PM (about 45–60 minutes after the shooting):** N888KG departed **Provo Airport (PVU)**, heading southeast toward the Arizona/Nevada border at normal cruise speed.
* **~30–46 minutes into the flight:** The aircraft's **ADS-B transponder went dark**, removing it from public radar for an extended gap (reported as roughly 30 to 50-plus minutes).
* **~1:48 PM:** Reported to have **landed at Page Municipal Airport (PGA), Arizona**, after re-acquiring tracking.
* **~3:06 PM:** **Returned to Provo (PVU)** and landed without incident.
* **No public flight plan** was associated with the dark leg, which trackers label a "ghost flight."

Note on times: the master investigation notes contain a conflicting evening timestamp (a "~7:12 PM" departure). That is inconsistent with the 12:23 PM shooting and the relative "45 minutes / one hour after" descriptions, so the early-afternoon times above are used as the consistent account. The discrepancy is noted for transparency.

## Theories about the aircraft's role

These are **open theories advanced by commentators**, framed as reported allegations, not established facts:

* **Getaway aircraft** — that N888KG may have moved the shooter or related personnel out of the area during the transponder blackout. Analysts note that during a 30–50 minute dark window a Challenger 300 could cover roughly 350–400 nautical miles — enough for a drop-off at a remote airstrip such as Page (PGA) or even Tuba City (TBC) before looping back. This is presented as a calculation of what was *possible*, not proof of what occurred.
* **Covert rendezvous / drop-off** — that the dark leg allowed an untraceable meeting or hand-off. The aviation analyst **MonkeyWerx** laid out this version, citing the departure timing, the mid-flight blackout, the lack of a filed plan, and the owner's local Utah ties, while acknowledging the jet's otherwise clean flight history (e.g. prior trips to Cancun in May 2024).
* **Foreign-intelligence link** — that the reported Saprea/Komigo and alleged Israel connections tie the aircraft to the broader foreign-involvement theory of the case. This rests entirely on unconfirmed ownership associations and is the most speculative thread.

## Owner statement and official response

The page presents the rebuttals alongside the suspicions:

* **Owner's explanation:** The owner has stated the flight was a **prescheduled trip**, and that the **termination of ATC/ADS-B tracking was standard** for flights into and out of **non-towered airports** in rural areas — not an intentional, illegal transponder shutoff.
* **Rural-coverage explanation:** The owner and officials attribute the tracking gap to **poor ADS-B ground-station coverage over rural terrain**, where aircraft routinely drop off public trackers, rather than to a deliberately disabled transponder.
* **FBI Director Kash Patel:** Per fact-check reporting, **Patel addressed the flight publicly and attributed the ADS-B gap to rural coverage**, not an illegal transponder shutoff.
* **Not part of the official investigation:** **Fact-checks report that N888KG is not part of the official investigation** into the assassination.

(Readers weighing these explanations may note that elsewhere in this investigation, commentators have challenged other "prescheduled / routine" claims — such as the paving of the crime-scene area. That context is offered for the reader to judge, not as a contradiction of the owner's specific statement here.)

## Why a transponder gap draws scrutiny

ADS-B (Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast) has been **FAA-mandated since 2020** for most US aircraft in controlled airspace; it broadcasts position, altitude, and identity in real time. **Intentionally** disabling ADS-B mid-flight without authorization is illegal and can carry fines of $25,000 or more, with authorized exceptions generally limited to national-security operations or declared emergencies. The key dispute over N888KG is whether the gap was an **intentional shutoff** (the conspiracy reading) or a **routine coverage dropout** over rural terrain near a non-towered field (the owner/FAA/Patel reading). That distinction — not merely the existence of a gap — is what the open questions below try to resolve.

## Open questions

1. Who is the registered operator and beneficial owner of N888KG, and what are the actual corporate ties to Komigo and the Saprea foundation?
2. Who was on board the September 10 flight, both outbound and on the return?
3. Was the transponder gap a deliberate shutoff or a genuine coverage dropout — and is there radar or FAA data that settles it?
4. What was the aircraft's true track during the dark window, and did it land anywhere other than Page (PGA)?
5. Have any FAA enforcement records or inquiries been opened on the ADS-B gap?
6. Why did the tail number "n888kg" reportedly trend in Utah Google searches on September 9, the day *before* the shooting?

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N888KG](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n888kg) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N888KG](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N888KG) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
