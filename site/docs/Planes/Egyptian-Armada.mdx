# Egyptian Plane Armada

Five Egyptian-registered VIP aircraft — **SU-BTT, SU-BND, SU-BTU, SU-BTV, and SU-BGM** — appear in 2025 flight logs operating in and out of **Provo Municipal Airport (PVU)**, about seven miles from Utah Valley University. Independent researchers describe the pattern as a **"five-plane armada"** of Egyptian Air Force Falcons and Gulfstreams routing from Cairo via Paris into Utah, with overlapping presence in the days around Charlie Kirk's September 10, 2025 assassination. This page is the hub for the fleet pattern; each tail has its own page linked below.

The framing comes from **Candace Owens** (who color-coded the lead jets) and X/OSINT researchers including **@ProjectConstitu** (Project Constitution), **Sam Parker**, **Ian Carroll**, **Ryan Matta**, and **John Cullen**. It is contested. The "armada" reading rests on public ADS-B records circulated by amateur trackers; several of the claimed overlaps with the Kirk family have been challenged as inaccurate, and at least one investigator close to the story — John Cullen — has pointed to ordinary maintenance contracts as the explanation. Both the surveillance theory and the innocent explanations are laid out below.

| **[SU-BTT](/Planes/SU-BTT)** | Dassault Falcon 7X | "Yellow plane" | In/near Provo Sept 4–10; departed Utah the morning of Sept 10 (for Wilmington, DE), left US for Cairo Sept 11. The lead "armada" jet. |
| **[SU-BND](/Planes/SU-BND)** | Gulfstream G550 | "Blue plane" | Flew into Provo ~May 23 and reportedly **stayed nearly four months**; transponder ON but did not take off at the key moment; Sept 13 Provo → Goose Bay / Paris / Cairo. |
| **[SU-BTU](/Planes/SU-BTU)** | Dassault Falcon 7X | (none) | Feb 14 Provo → Wilmington; Mar 2 Detroit → Provo; Aug 8 Minot → Provo. May 27 into Provo carrying a passenger researchers name as **"Walid Mahmoud,"** said to depart June 2. Hops tied by researchers to TPUSA Faith-tour stops. |
| **[SU-BTV](/Planes/SU-BTV)** | Dassault Falcon 7X | (none) | Mar 2 and Jul 2 Provo legs; Oct 2 Provo (after the assassination); Oct 29 Cairo local. Provo activity reportedly spikes before September. |
| **[SU-BGM](/Planes/SU-BGM)** | Gulfstream IV | (none) | Apr 10 Provo local (~55-min flight); Apr 16 Goose Bay → Paris → Provo (landed ~6 PM). Described as a "spring swarm" during Kirk's Midwest tour. |

Per-tail routes above are taken from screenshots of raw flight logs posted by Project Constitution and others; they have not been independently verified and are reproduced here as the researchers' claims, not confirmed records.

## Two additional foreign tails

Beyond the five SU-registered jets, the investigation's aircraft list also flags two further foreign-registered tails — **T7ELL** and **EJM36** — grouped with the Egyptian fleet for disclosure purposes. Public reporting on these two is minimal (they appear in source notes simply as "more planes"), and the proposed disclosure laws ask for their full flight records, crew, and passengers alongside the SU- aircraft. They are listed here so the fleet picture is complete; no specific route or passenger detail has been publicly established for either.

## Pattern of presence

* The aircraft are described in public reporting as **VIP Falcons and Gulfstreams from Cairo via Paris**, with multiple stops at Provo Municipal Airport throughout 2025.
* Researchers report that **SU-BTT and SU-BND** have been observed near **TPUSA events for years** before September 10, 2025, with the claimed overlaps spanning roughly **2022 through 2025**.
* Candace Owens's team claims a cumulative **73 documented occurrences near Erika Kirk's travel** and about **23 near Charlie Kirk** across the United States — Omaha, Wichita, Provo, plus domestic and international legs. These tallies are disputed (see fact-checks below) and are reported here as the trackers' allegations, not as established fact.

## Theories about the fleet's role

Framed as open theories, each attributed to the people advancing it:

* **Foreign surveillance / intel-vacuum** — Candace Owens and Project Constitution argue the jets were "Deep State vacuums" allegedly harvesting phones, faces, and plates at TPUSA rallies, not maintenance stops. Owens has cited a Provo hangar she says shut in 2021 and a 2024 French lease as evidence she believes the "maintenance" account is a cover.
* **A possible joint operation** — tied to SU-BTT's Sept 10 routing to Wilmington, DE and unconfirmed "DoD Liaison" contractor claims at Duncan Aviation. See the [SU-BTT page](/Planes/SU-BTT) for that detail, which remains uncorroborated.
* **Counter-drone or drone role** — John Cullen has speculated at points that one of the jets was associated with drones; this sits awkwardly beside his maintenance-contract explanation, and the disagreement shows the researchers themselves do not have a single account.

## Innocent explanations and fact-checks

Balance is essential here. Several facts cut against the "armada" reading, and some come from the same investigative circle:

* **John Cullen's maintenance-contract dismissal.** Cullen has pointed to ordinary **maintenance contracts** as the explanation for the Egyptian jets at Provo — Falcon servicing through **Duncan Aviation**, whose Provo relationship for this kind of work is described as long-standing (reportedly dating to **1999**). Under this reading, a foreign VIP Falcon parked at a full-service MRO field is routine, not operational.
* **Duncan Aviation is a legitimate jet hub.** Duncan runs a full-service maintenance, repair, and overhaul facility at Provo Municipal Airport — airframe, engine, avionics, paint, and interior work. A foreign VIP Falcon or Gulfstream stopping there for service is not inherently suspicious.
* **A skeptic's audit of the overlaps.** At least one reviewer who pulled a year of FlightRadar history reported that **"5 of the 8"** overlaps Owens cited were wrong — claiming the plane had not even left Egypt on some of the cited dates — and disputed the "73 overlaps" framing entirely.
* **Erika Kirk's own account.** According to that audit, **Erika Kirk reportedly said she stayed home in Arizona** on September 10 rather than being in the Nebraska/Delaware locations some overlap claims placed her near. Erika Kirk is a living person; all claims about tracking her movements are allegations by third parties, not established fact, and her stated account is included here in fairness.
* **SU-BND stayed for months.** The "blue plane" reportedly sat in Provo for nearly four months, which fits a long maintenance or storage stay more than a surgical operation.
* **Tracking artifacts.** Transponder gaps and "materializing" tracks can result from terrain shadowing, receiver coverage gaps, and trackers snapping an origin to the nearest public field — mundane data artifacts, not proof of covert flights.

## Why this pattern still draws scrutiny

A single foreign-government aircraft at a US municipal airport is unremarkable, and the maintenance explanation may well account for most of it. What keeps researchers asking questions is the combination they describe — **multiple aircraft from one foreign air force, recurrent visits over months, claimed overlap with TPUSA event geography, and presence in Provo in the days preceding a high-profile assassination**. That combination, if the underlying logs hold up, is the basis for calls to investigate the operators, passengers, and tasking of all five tails. Whether it survives a documentary check is exactly what the open records below would settle.

## Open questions

1. Who in the US authorized recurring access for Egyptian VIP/Air Force jets to a non-international US municipal airport?
2. Who were the passengers on each aircraft during the 2025 visits, and is there any manifest?
3. Do Duncan Aviation's maintenance records account for the full set of Provo stops, as John Cullen suggests?
4. Which of the claimed Erika/Charlie Kirk overlaps are accurate once cross-checked against full flight history — the trackers' tallies or the skeptic's "5 of 8 wrong" audit?
5. What is the operational relationship, if any, between SU-BTT, SU-BND, SU-BTU, SU-BTV, and SU-BGM?

## Track These Aircraft

Each jet in the armada can be researched on both major flight-tracking sites. FlightRadar24 honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list (flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing); ADS-B Exchange is volunteer-fed and unfiltered (it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out).

* SU-BTT — [FlightRadar24](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/su-btt) · [ADS-B Exchange](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=SU-BTT)
* SU-BND — [FlightRadar24](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/su-bnd) · [ADS-B Exchange](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=SU-BND)
* SU-BTU — [FlightRadar24](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/su-btu) · [ADS-B Exchange](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=SU-BTU)
* SU-BTV — [FlightRadar24](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/su-btv) · [ADS-B Exchange](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=SU-BTV)
* SU-BGM — [FlightRadar24](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/su-bgm) · [ADS-B Exchange](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=SU-BGM)

**Tip:** These are foreign (Egyptian) military registrations, which are frequently sparse or absent on FlightRadar24. ADS-B Exchange — fed by volunteer receivers near the actual flight paths — is usually the better source for them.
