# N2100L LASAI Sister Ship

**N2100L** is named in the investigation as a **Bombardier Global 6500** belonging to the **LASAI Aviation** fleet — the same operator and aircraft type as the [N1098L spy plane](/Planes/N1098L/overview). According to the case notes, LASAI's fleet of Global 6500s supports U.S. Army reconnaissance, which is why crew and tasking details for these tail numbers are often classified or otherwise protected. This page exists so the sister ship is catalogued; it has not been independently placed over UVU.

## Why this aircraft is in the catalog

N2100L appears in the investigation as context for understanding [N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview): it is cited as an example of "similar Global 6500s" in the **same LASAI Aviation reconnaissance fleet**. The point researchers draw from it is structural — that N1098L was not a one-off charter but one airframe in a fleet of Army ISR-capable business jets, which helps explain why operator filings and crew manifests for these flights are hard to obtain.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N2100L
* **Type:** Bombardier Global 6500 (G6500)
* **Operator/fleet:** LASAI Aviation (the same operator family behind N1098L's LASAI Aviation II LLC)
* **Reported role:** Supports U.S. Army reconnaissance, per the case notes — the same Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) mission profile associated with the [HADES program](/Planes/ISR-Operations)
* **Significance:** Cited as a fleet sister ship to N1098L, illustrating that LASAI operates multiple ISR-capable Global 6500s

## What is and is not established

* **Established:** N2100L is named as a LASAI Global 6500 in the same reconnaissance-supporting fleet as N1098L.
* **Not established:** There is no public ADS-B track in the case material placing N2100L over Orem, UVU, or the Utah corridor on September 10, 2025. It is documented here as a fleet-mate, not as an aircraft independently observed at the scene.

## Open questions

2. How many Global 6500s does LASAI Aviation operate, and which support Army ISR tasking?
3. Do N2100L and N1098L share crews, contracting authority, or basing?

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N2100L](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n2100l) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N2100L](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N2100L) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
