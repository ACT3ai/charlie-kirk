# N59906 MARC Survey Plane

A **Piper PA-31 Navajo Chieftain** registered **N59906**, operated by aerial-survey contractor **MARC Inc.**, is reported in public OSINT to have flown four mapping-style passes over and around the **Utah Valley University** campus on the morning of **September 10, 2025**, hours before [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) was shot at roughly 12:23 p.m. MDT. Because the aircraft type is configured for high-resolution camera and LiDAR mapping, independent researchers flagged it as a possible aerial-surveillance flight rather than a drone. What it actually photographed — and who tasked it — remains unconfirmed.

This page is about tail **N59906** (MARC Inc. survey aircraft). Do not confuse it with the separately discussed tail **[N55906](/Planes/N55906)**, a different aircraft raised in independent-investigator analysis. The two tail numbers differ by one digit and are easy to transpose.

## Aircraft identification

* **Tail number:** N59906
* **Type:** Piper PA-31 Navajo Chieftain
* **Operator / owner:** MARC Inc. — described in OSINT reporting as North America's largest provider of specialized contract aircraft for airborne GIS, geophysical surveys, mapping, and surveillance projects
* **Base of operation that day:** Provo Municipal Airport (PVU), about 4 miles south of the UVU campus
* **Mission profile:** contract aerial survey / mapping; not a scheduled or military flight

## Flight history relevant to the case

According to OSINT analysis attributed to researcher **@rosenwald_eric** and circulated by independent investigators, on the morning of September 10, 2025 the aircraft made four passes that originated and ended at Provo Airport, flying at roughly **19,000 feet MSL** — about **15,000 feet above ground level** given Provo's elevation. That is a standard high-altitude aerial-mapping band, not a treetop pass; the "low-altitude" label used in some early posts is misleading, and the figures are reproduced here as reported.

| Pass | Time (MDT) | Duration | Note |
|------|-----------|----------|------|
| 1 | 11:01 a.m. | ~5 min | First north-south pass over central campus |
| 2 | 11:09 a.m. | ~4 min | Second pass, offset slightly eastward |
| 3 | 11:18 a.m. | ~5 min | Third pass, reportedly focused on the event area |
| 4 | 11:35 a.m. | ~6 min | Final pass; alleged shooter reportedly first seen on campus surveillance at 11:50 a.m. |

The reporting states that open-source ADS-B flight tracking confirmed this pass pattern. One early post mislabeled the flight date as "Sep 15," but the same post places the passes "hours before" the September 10 attack; the September 10 morning timing is what the pass-by-pass log describes.

N59906 also appears in the broader "anomalous aircraft near UVU / Utah corridor" catalogs that independent investigators have assembled — informal lists cross-referencing aircraft reportedly operating around the campus and the surrounding Utah airspace in the hours and days around the event. Its inclusion there is reported as a flagged data point for follow-up, not as proof of any coordinated activity.

## Survey and surveillance payload

Public reporting describes MARC Inc.'s survey aircraft as equipped for high-resolution data capture. The payloads below are described in general for the operator's fleet; it is not independently confirmed which specific sensors were aboard N59906 on September 10.

* **Cameras:** Vexcel large-format digital, Leica, hyperspectral, and infrared scanners.
* **LiDAR:** Optech Orion / Gemini / 3100 systems for 3D mapping.
* **Storage:** Onboard data systems for multispectral and hyperspectral imaging.

MARC Inc. is reported to serve U.S. federal and state governments, foreign entities, private mapping firms, oil and gas companies, civil-engineering groups, and universities — applications ranging from aerial photography and geophysical surveys to pipeline monitoring and surveillance. Neither MARC Inc. nor the FBI has publicly confirmed the flight's purpose or client, and the airspace over the event reportedly remained unrestricted, which some commenters criticized given the timing.

## Alleged leaked imagery

Public reporting states that on **October 13, 2025**, a TikTok account (**[@universehop00](/Influencers/x/universehop00)**) posted images claimed to originate from the N59906 flight — reportedly one day after the same account's first post on the topic. The account is said to have had no other content, which some commenters read as a sign of a targeted leak from a contractor or insider. The images are described as high-resolution orthomosaic views of the UVU quad and stage area, with metadata said to align with the 11:35 a.m. pass, and one post alleged a figure near a rooftop access point about 45 minutes before the shooting. **These images are unverified.** Their authenticity, source, and metadata have not been independently confirmed, and they should be treated as an unproven claim rather than evidence.

## The Alpha Whiskey / Alexis Wilkins allegation (unproven)

A separate and far more contested claim circulated on X tied N59906's operator to a company linked to **Alexis Wilkins**, a country singer who is publicly reported to be the girlfriend of **[Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel)**, the current FBI Director. This section sets out the claim as it was posted and then the rebuttal. **None of the connection alleged below has been confirmed; it is an unproven allegation amplified on social media and is reproduced here only to document and assess it.**

### What the X accounts alleged

A thread shared by accounts including **[@ProjectConstitu](/Influencers/podcasts-project-constitution)**, **[@AnaEscobarShow](/Influencers/x/anaescobarshow)**, **[@leahfiles](/Influencers/x/leahfiles)**, **[@Mini_111111](/Influencers/x/mini-111111)**, and **@StefaniWilcox8** carried the headline claim, quoted here verbatim:

> "BOMBSHELL: The MARC INC Plane That Was Circling UVU Taking SURVEILLANCE Pictures During The Charlie Kirk Event Is Tied to a Company Where Kash Patel's GIRLFRIEND Alexis Wilkins Is the Registered Agent?"

The posts laid out the following alleged ownership chain:

* A **pending registration** reportedly names **Alpha Whiskey 2 LLC** in **Brandon, Mississippi** in connection with the aircraft's ownership/operation.
* A separately named entity, **Alpha Whiskey Inc** (Tennessee, reportedly active since **2020**), is said to list **Alexis Wilkins** as registered agent/principal, based in **Nashville**.
* The posts note that **"Kash Patel is currently the Director of the FBI,"** and frame the shared "Alpha Whiskey" name plus the timing of the flight as evidence of an intelligence connection — "The intelligence connections are impossible to ignore."

The accounts used these claims to push for legislation forcing disclosure of MARC Inc.'s flight records, who hired the aircraft, the passengers and crew aboard, and any transponder deactivation.

### The rebuttal — what the evidence actually shows

The allegation rests on a **name similarity plus timing**, and the rebuttal to it is prominent and substantive. Responses in the same threads (including from Grok) and from other commenters made the following points:

* **No public evidence** links the two LLCs — or Alexis Wilkins or Kash Patel — to the flight, to MARC Inc., or to the Charlie Kirk event.
* The inference is **speculation drawn from a shared company name and the timing** of the flight, not from any documented relationship.
* **High-resolution LiDAR and aerial-survey work is standard** for the kind of contractor MARC Inc. is, so the aircraft's capabilities are not by themselves anomalous.
* There are **no confirmed shared ownership, officers, communications, financial, or operational links** between the entities or the people named.

Because **Alexis Wilkins and Kash Patel are living people**, no statement here should be read as asserting that either is connected to the aircraft, the flight, or the assassination. They are not. The claim is an allegation made on social media, it is disputed in the same threads where it appeared, and as of now it is **unproven**.

## Theories about the aircraft's role

These are open theories raised by independent investigators, not established facts:

* **Routine survey work.** Provo sits on the Interstate-15 corridor, and survey contractors regularly fly mapping grids for infrastructure, utility, and engineering clients. Under this reading the timing is coincidental, and any imagery — if it exists — would simply document the area, per @rosenwald_eric's own framing that the photos could "debunk conspiracies."
* **Tasked pre-event imaging.** Other commenters argue the four-pass pattern in the two hours before the attack, by a contractor that lists "surveillance" among its services, is suspicious and worth subpoenaing. Under this reading the flight may have captured the stage area, rooftops, and approaches before the shooting.
* **Evidence-capture, intent unknown.** A middle position: the aircraft plausibly recorded high-resolution imagery of the campus that morning regardless of who tasked it, making the existence, custody, and content of that data the central question — whether it helps the [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) case or points elsewhere.

No theory here asserts criminal conduct by any named person or by MARC Inc. The operator's purpose and client remain officially unconfirmed.

## Open questions

1. Who contracted and paid for the N59906 flight on the morning of September 10, 2025?
2. Which specific sensors were aboard N59906 that day, and was imagery delivered to the customer in real time or after the fact?
3. Did the survey passes capture the event area, the stage, and the rooftops before the shooting, and where is that data now?
4. Was this routine infrastructure survey work (e.g., the nearby Interstate-15 corridor) or task-specific imaging tied to the event?
5. Are the alleged leaked TikTok images authentic, and if so, who released them and from what source?
6. Why did airspace over the venue remain unrestricted despite a high-profile public event?

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

* Who contracted MARC Inc.'s Piper N59906, who paid, what sensors were aboard, how any imagery was delivered, and the identity of the customer organization are exactly the kinds of records the proposed [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) are designed to force into the open — facts that are not public today.
* The X accounts amplifying the Alpha Whiskey claim called for the same kind of disclosure: the full **MARC Inc. flight records**, **who hired** the aircraft, the **passengers and crew** aboard, and any **transponder deactivation** during the flight. Forced disclosure would either substantiate or put to rest the corporate-ownership allegations rather than leaving them to circulate unverified on social media.

## Track This Aircraft

Research this aircraft's flight history yourself on both major flight-tracking sites. They differ in one important way:

* [FlightRadar24 — N59906](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/aircraft/n59906) — large commercial tracker that honors the FAA's [LADD](https://www.faa.gov/pilots/ladd) block list, so flights an owner or the government has asked to hide can be missing.
* [ADS-B Exchange — N59906](https://globe.adsbexchange.com/?reg=N59906) — volunteer-fed, unfiltered tracker that does **not** honor LADD, so it often shows blocked aircraft and tracks the filtered sites leave out.
