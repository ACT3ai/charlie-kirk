# DC & Metro Search Patterns (Claims)

**Washington, D.C.** is the second major geography in Kirk-case **Google Trends** posts — often shorthand for
**federal attention** (FBI, DPS liaisons, hospital coordination). Project notes also list **DC-area dates** in
`knowledge/Google_Searches.txt`: **Nov 24, 2024**; **June 28, July 3, July 16, 2025**; **Sept 9–10, 2025**.

**Virginia** appears less often as a named Trends bucket; commentators sometimes fold **Northern Virginia /
Alexandria** into the **DMV federal corridor** when discussing D.C.-origin hospital or doctor search claims (see
[Medical — doctor DC phone call](/Medical/doctor_dc_phone_call)). **No verified Trends export uniquely labels
“Virginia”** for most subjects below — treat Virginia as **context**, not a separate dataset, unless screenshots show it.

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X (Attributed)

Google Trends / search-pattern claims remain among the **most contested** OSINT threads.

**Pro-pattern voices** (Baron Coleman ecosystem; **@ZachCostello_**; **@denton_doyle**) argue pre-event Trends activity on names, hospital, surgeon, rifle-drop locations, and Israel/DC/Alabama geos is irregular enough to investigate.

**Skeptic voices** (**@paramounttactcl**, **@MaxNordau** summarizing Tim Pool) argue Trends is **sampled noise**, unreproducible, and that Google has told media it is not a historical record—especially at single-digit volumes. Joel Webbon-style "Trends prove Israel killed Kirk" claims are treated by skeptics as statistical malpractice.

This page's DC/metro angle appears in threads listing **Washington-area** pre-event interest alongside Israel IP claims—still without log-level WHOIS of any human searcher.

**Rule used on this site:** Trends anomalies are **leads**, not identification of a living searcher, and never proof of foreign-state murder.
