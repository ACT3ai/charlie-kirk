# Trends Deletion & Suppression Claims

If **pre-event search evidence** mattered, **preservation** matters equally. Commentators allege **Google Trends
data disappeared** after investigators published screenshots.

## Precision Marble & Granite LLC

Posts claim:

- **~June 20, 2025** U.S. spike for **Precision Marble & Granite LLC**,
- Firm linked in commentary to **[Tent](/Tent/overview)** paving and **[Matt Robinson](/People/matt-robinson)** (Tyler’s father) — **disputed**,
- Researcher saw data on **mobile**, then **zero on desktop**, then **gone on mobile**; third-party check also empty.

Framed as **live deletion** — alternative: **UI bug**, **term mismatch**, or **insufficient volume** on desktop aggregation.

## Broader scrub narrative

`knowledge/Google_Searches.txt` and CK posts describe **“DEEP STATE panic”** — Israel/DC pre-hit terms **vanishing**
after viral threads ([Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson), [Phil Lyman](/distraction_people/Phil_Lyman), hospital, [Hunter Kozak](/People/hunter-kozak), medical examiner).

**Allegations only** — Google has not confirmed manipulation in this case.

If pre-event search interest was ever a real lead, it sits in the category former NCTC director [Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent) says his team was ordered to stop examining once Tyler Robinson was in custody — before foreign-nexus threads were run to ground.

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X (Attributed)

Google Trends / search-pattern claims remain among the **most contested** OSINT threads.

**Pro-pattern voices** (Baron Coleman ecosystem; **@ZachCostello_**; **@denton_doyle**) argue pre-event Trends activity on names, hospital, surgeon, rifle-drop locations, and Israel/DC/Alabama geos is irregular enough to investigate.

**Skeptic voices** (**@paramounttactcl**, **@MaxNordau** summarizing Tim Pool) argue Trends is **sampled noise**, unreproducible, and that Google has told media it is not a historical record—especially at single-digit volumes. Joel Webbon-style "Trends prove Israel killed Kirk" claims are treated by skeptics as statistical malpractice.

**Trends deletion/suppression** claims allege post-hoc scrubbing of incriminating spikes; hard to falsify without third-party archives (Archive.is, screenshots with timestamps).

**Rule used on this site:** Trends anomalies are **leads**, not identification of a living searcher, and never proof of foreign-state murder.
