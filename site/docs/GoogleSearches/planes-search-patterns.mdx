Aircraft tail numbers are **high-specificity** Trends terms — useful for OSINT if screenshots are archived.

- Featured in mega-post alongside low-altitude Sept 10 flight behavior ([Planes/N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview)).

## Citizen Investigator Claims on X (Attributed)

Google Trends / search-pattern claims remain among the **most contested** OSINT threads.

**Pro-pattern voices** (Baron Coleman ecosystem; **[@ZachCostello_](/Influencers/x/zachcostello)**; **[@denton_doyle](/Influencers/x/denton-doyle)**) argue pre-event Trends activity on names, hospital, surgeon, rifle-drop locations, and Israel/DC/Alabama geos is irregular enough to investigate.

**Skeptic voices** (**[@paramounttactcl](/Influencers/x/paramounttactcl)**, **[@MaxNordau](/Influencers/x/maxnordau)** summarizing Tim Pool) argue Trends is **sampled noise**, unreproducible, and that Google has told media it is not a historical record—especially at single-digit volumes. Joel Webbon-style "Trends prove Israel killed Kirk" claims are treated by skeptics as statistical malpractice.

**N1098L / N888KG** search-pattern claims are paired with ADS-B threads; Trends alone cannot prove aircraft tasking.

**Rule used on this site:** Trends anomalies are **leads**, not identification of a living searcher, and never proof of foreign-state murder.
