## What Is Said

Two voices, both male.

The first is a CNN reporter, setting up the segment: "We met an electrician working near the university who says someone matching that description approached a coworker at this job site."

The second is the electrician himself, and his description is the entire substance of the clip. He lists, in order: "Yeah, black sunglasses. He had a black COVID mask. He had black trench coat, black cargo pants, and he was wearing a small backpack, and he also had long, greasy black hair."

He then reports what the man said, at roughly 0:25 in: "A guy said, someone's been shot." Asked by the reporter whether, looking back, the crew thought that person was the shooter, he answers obliquely — "The guy was already trying to leave" — and then adds the detail that gives the clip its weight: "we were then shown a picture, uh, by the sheriff at the time. And the excavator said that it matched."

That last sentence is the claim the poster is pointing at. The witness is not saying he made an identification. He is saying a colleague operating the excavator, shown a photograph by a sheriff's deputy on scene, said it matched. That is a secondhand identification of an unnamed photograph, relayed on television, by a man describing a stranger he saw in passing during a mass-panic event.
