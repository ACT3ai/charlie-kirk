Two problems should be stated plainly. First, "bow shock" is a real optical phenomenon and it is genuinely photographable — but it requires very specific conditions of resolution, frame rate, lighting and angle, and consumer phone video played back in software slow motion is close to the worst possible medium in which to try to see one. Interpolated frames in software slow motion routinely generate smeared bright artefacts that look exactly like a streak.

Second, the reading is internally ambiguous, and the ambiguity is worth flagging because the video does not resolve it. The captions place the streak near the man's head. The investigation file's own record of this claim describes the same streak as coming out of his hand. Those are not the same claim, and they point in opposite directions: an object passing near a man's head is consistent with him being shot at, while an object leaving his hand is the poster's thesis. A single ambiguous white smear cannot support either reading, and it certainly cannot support both.
