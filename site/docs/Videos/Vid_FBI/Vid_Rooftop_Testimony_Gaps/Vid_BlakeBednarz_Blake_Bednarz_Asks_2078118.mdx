## What Is Said

The passage the clip is built around, transcribed from the audio, is:

> So when he's first spotted on campus, he has different clothing on, and then he changes clothing on the roof. And then changed back into that clothing at some point, so that when he was apprehended, when he was arrested, the clothing matched the clothing he had on before the shooting.

The editing repeats "he changes clothing on the roof" several times in succession, then plays the whole passage again at the close of the clip.

What the account describes, on its own terms, is a three-stage sequence: one set of clothing when first seen on campus, a change on the roof, and a change back before the arrest - producing a match between what was worn at the arrest and what was worn before the shooting. The clip does not argue against it. It simply makes the listener hear the sequence twice, on the assumption that hearing it twice raises the obvious questions: what became of the intermediate clothing, what is the evidentiary basis for each stage, and how the sequence was reconstructed.

A second passage in the clip, from a different speaker at the same briefing, concerns what led to the arrest and whether there was a direct connection between two events in the timeline. The speaker declines to characterise it: "I cannot comment if there is a direct connection or not, but all I can say is that through some process, the family came to know that this had happened."

Bednarz's own voice appears in the middle of the clip, and it is not about evidence. It is about consequences:

> You have to go back to JFK to have seen a video live of something like this happening. I wasn't born until 1975, but I know that things were really dark in the late 60s... But this is our moment. Do we escalate or do we find an off-ramp? And again, it's a choice. Every one of us gets to make that choice.

That passage is worth keeping. It is an appeal against escalation from someone who spent months inside this material, and it sits oddly - and usefully - in the middle of a clip about a discrepancy in an official account.

Nothing in this clip asserts that any person did anything. It reports an official account of a sequence, and asks the viewer to attend to it. The person the account concerns is charged and has not been convicted; nothing on this page treats the account as established.
