## What This Video Is

One minute fourteen seconds of handheld phone video shot from inside a private
residence next to the Utah Valley University campus, looking out across the back
garden at armed tactical officers conducting a search. It was recorded by a
resident, whispering, from behind a patio door.

It circulated on X under the handle HustleBitch with a "BREAKING" framing. That
account is a reposter; the footage is a resident's own capture, and neither the
original filmer nor the exact address is identified here. The residents' first
names are audible on the audio and are not reproduced on this page — they are
private people who were sheltering in their own home.

The clip is filed in this investigation's capture mirror. Timing is not stamped
on the video, but the content places it during the search phase that followed
the shooting on September 10, 2025.

## What Is Said

The whole soundtrack is one resident narrating in a whisper to the people in the
house with her, with a second hushed voice answering. It is the running
commentary of somebody who does not know what is happening:

> There are about 6 armed men in the backyard of a home right next to UVU
> campus. Apparently, they are looking for another suspect — maybe another
> suspect has not been apprehended, and they're actively searching.

> Yes, they're still there. They're huddled together. And they're looking ...
> they're looking for someone. We're all at the windows.

> Oh, my goodness. He's coming right here. Okay. ... Move away from the windows.

Two things need separating here, because the caption the clip circulated under
runs them together.

The residents' belief that officers were "looking for another suspect" is their
own inference, formed at a window while a search went past their house. Nobody
on the video is a police source, and no officer says anything on the audio at
all. A tactical sweep of properties adjoining a campus is what any agency would
do after a shooting with an unlocated shooter — it does not by itself imply a
second person was being sought, and it does not imply anything about whom
investigators had in view at that hour.

What the clip does establish, first-hand, is the character and scale of the
search on the ground: multiple armed officers in tactical dress moving through
private residential property adjoining the campus, close enough to a family home
that the occupants were told to move away from the windows. Very little
first-person footage of that phase exists, which is why the clip is worth
keeping.
