## What The Footage Shows

The Trends interface is on screen for roughly half the running time, and the presenter drives it live rather than showing prepared screenshots — changing the region filter from Israel to the District of Columbia to Utah while talking, so the viewer sees the chart redraw.

That is a real strength of this clip over the others in this section: the method is visible. It is also where its limits become visible. The charts he shows are low-volume queries for obscure names, and at low volume the Trends index is a normalised relative measure, not a count, with sampling behaviour that makes small single-day bumps unstable between runs. None of the exhibits show a query string, an account, or a searcher; a region label reflects the network a request came through, and a VPN or corporate proxy exit resolves to a country without identifying anyone at a keyboard.

What is genuinely useful here, and worth separating from the search charts, is the timeline material the presenter states out loud: Mason's appointment on July 17, 2025 following a resignation, and Kozak's own account that his question was screened in advance by the tour team. Both are checkable against records rather than against a chart.
