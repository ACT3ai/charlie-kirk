## What This Video Is

Twenty minutes twenty-nine seconds, circulated by the account Project Constitution under the headline "ISRAELI IP MYSTERY: Google Searches EXPLODED for Charlie Kirk Assas…". It is the fullest single presentation of the search-trend material anywhere in this corpus, and the shorter clip elsewhere in this section, [carried by the Sam Parker account](/Videos/Vid_Google_Searches/Vid_Search_Trend_Evidence/Vid_Sam_Parker_MORE_CHARLIE_1977027), is an excerpt cut from this same session.

The format alternates between a webcam shot of the presenter — identified in the visual description as "Baron", a middle-aged man with a greying beard, framed chest-up behind a broadcast microphone in a home office with framed photographs, certificates and a gun safe behind him — and screen recordings of his browser. He runs the queries live, changing filters on camera.

## What Is Said

He opens with how he started, which is unusually candid about method: on the phone with a friend on the day, watching the first arrest reported, he simply began typing names into Google Trends and kept going.

He then walks about a dozen exhibits, and states his own rule twice: "I'm just reporting the facts. I'm not drawing conclusions tonight."

**George Zinn.** The first arrest. A blip on September 10 in the District of Columbia, which he explains himself as people looking up a name just reported. Then, weeks earlier, an entry he places around July 20 — and he uses it to set the frame for everything that follows: "keep that July 20 anchor date in your mind."

**Timpanogos Regional Hospital.** DC-geolocated interest on September 10 and 11 he again explains as breaking news; the ones he presses on are earlier — he cites June 28, July 3, July 16, and one on the day before the shooting: "somebody in the District of Columbia says, we should really make sure we know what the Timpanogos Regional Hospital is — the day before he gets shot. Maybe they're looking up an address. Who knows?"

**The medical examiner.** He names Deirdre Amaro as Utah's medical examiner, states his belief that no autopsy was performed — "which I find shocking" — and lists what an autopsy would have established: bullet angle, path, type, organ damage, and whether there were injuries elsewhere. He reports an Israel-geolocated search for her name, and does the control himself: "lest you think I didn't check — go to the District of Columbia, not there. Go to Utah, not there."

**Beau Mason.** Commissioner of the Utah Department of Public Safety, appointed July 17, 2025 after a resignation. DC-geolocated interest around July 16, which he immediately argues against himself: "this one could be explained away… maybe someone says, we need to look at who the new Department of Public Safety head is going to be."

**The aircraft.** He credits John Cullen for the flight-tracking work and describes a Bombardier Global 6500, tail number N1098L, which on the day of the shooting is said to have dropped from cruising altitude to roughly 203 to 204 feet and flown near the campus at close to stall speed, then repeated the manoeuvre on the return leg about 25 minutes afterwards. He reports the tail number itself searched from an Israel-geolocated address on August 8, "a month and two days before."

**The FBI office.** He reports Israel-geolocated searches for the outgoing Salt Lake City special agent in charge on July 9 and August 10, and for his replacement on September 1, with no comparable DC interest.

**Hunter Kozak.** The questioner, searched from an Israel-geolocated address on August 3, with, he says, no pre-event interest from DC or Utah. He notes Kozak's own account that the question had been screened in advance by the tour team. He also makes unverified personal remarks about Kozak's household which are not evidence and are not repeated here.

**Seven surgeons.** The longest and strongest segment. He explains that a Turning Point USA spokesman had relayed a surgeon's account without naming the surgeon, so he pulled a public list of surgeons at the Orem hospital and ran each name. He reports seven of them showing Israel-geolocated searches inside roughly a fortnight in mid-to-late July. He is careful with every one — "I'm sure he's a fine surgeon", "probably a pillar of the community" — and makes no accusation against any of them.

**What he asks for.** His closing is not a conclusion but a request for legal process, and it is the most useful thing in the twenty minutes: "If I'm the Utah State Police, I'm calling Google and saying, I need to know the IP addresses that hit this. I need to know if this belongs to a VPN… Is it the same IP address that looked up all the different surgeons in my county?" He raises the VPN objection himself, repeatedly, rather than waiting for it.

## What The Footage Shows

Roughly half the running time is browser capture: the Google Trends interface with a query typed, a region filter set to Israel, the District of Columbia or Utah, and a line chart redrawing as he switches between them. The other half is the webcam shot of him talking, gesturing, and at one point drinking from a water bottle. A screenshot of a post by John Cullen is shown, carrying Flightradar24 maps for the aircraft.

The visual description records the aircraft registration on the Cullen screenshot as N109BL rather than N1098L; the presenter says "November 1098 Lima" clearly in the audio, so the description is very likely a transcription slip rather than a different aircraft.

What the footage does not show, at any point, is a searcher. Every chart is a normalised relative-interest index over a small number of queries, with no query log, no account, no exact string and no timestamp beyond a day. A region label reflects the network exit a request came from. The presenter knows this and says so; a viewer scrolling past the charts may not.

The pattern claim is nonetheless the strongest form this material takes, because it is not one chart. Seven low-volume name queries clustering in a fortnight is a different kind of observation from a single spike, and it is the kind of thing that either survives subpoena-grade data or dies on it. That is exactly what he asks for.
