## What Is Said

One passage of narration from the person filming, which is the opening half of the audio that runs across this whole family of clips:

> I think they just found the guy, right outside the [...]. The rifle on this dude's dash, and they just handcuffed him. Shit.

The transcription engine renders one word as "carry on," which does not parse and is almost certainly a mis-hearing.

This short cut is useful precisely because it preserves the narrator's reasoning rather than only his conclusion. He is not describing what he sees; he is inferring. "They just found the guy" is a guess about who has been detained, offered within an hour or two of a shooting a few miles away, by someone who has no way to know. "The rifle on this dude's dash" describes something that appears nowhere in any variant of the footage. Only the third clause - that the man has been handcuffed - is something the camera can actually confirm.

According to the investigation's record, the item associated with this detention was reported to be a .177 pellet break-barrel air rifle. On that reading, the narrator's use of the word "rifle" is an honest error at distance, and it is the seed of everything that was later captioned onto this footage.
