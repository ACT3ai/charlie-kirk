## What This Video Is

Twenty-six seconds, published by the account Project Constitution and labelled by them "Raw Video 2 - Under The Walkway In The Tunnel." It carries a "PROTEST CONFIDENTIAL" watermark.

Structurally it is a small piece of published analysis rather than a raw capture. It opens on a Google Maps satellite still with a red dot and an arrow marking the underpass location, then runs about seven seconds of hand-held footage, then repeats the same seven seconds with hand-drawn white arrows added over specific objects. The annotations are the publisher's own, and the second pass is where their argument is.

## What Is Said

The audio is short and it is the most consequential thing in the file. Two voices, overlapping:

> Shoot me. Kill me. ... Kill me. Kill me.

and, from a bystander near the camera:

> We don't know if it's him or not. ... What is that? ... You don't know if it's him or not.

This is a contemporaneous recording of the "shoot me" moment - the incident that put the first person into custody after the shooting and pulled responders and cameras to the underpass. According to the Utah County Sheriff's Office, the man detained shouted words to the effect of "I shot him, now shoot me" while officers were still trying to locate the shooter, and when questioned he stated that he had done it to draw attention away from the actual shooter. Utah's Department of Public Safety commissioner said publicly that he was cleared as the shooter the same day.

The second voice is the part that rarely gets quoted and deserves to be. A bystander, in real time, twice says "we don't know if it's him or not." That is a bystander applying more caution in the first minutes than a good deal of the commentary that followed applied over the next year. It is a useful corrective to hear at the top of this cluster.

The man detained here is a living person whose case has since been resolved in court on separate charges; the account of his motive above is what police and officials have said, and his own reported account is recorded on his profile page. Nothing beyond that is asserted here.
