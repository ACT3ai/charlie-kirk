## What Is Said

The interviewer asks about the day at Utah Valley University. What follows is Zinn's own account, in his own words, and it is reproduced here because his side belongs on the record alongside everything asserted about him.

**On a prior connection to Turning Point USA.** He says he had done volunteer canvassing work associated with the organisation before the event:

> I mean, [I] deployed for him in Phoenix, his headquarters is in Phoenix, and we went out and knocked on doors... to just get votes for Trump... he paid our way, our hotel and everything like that... I really liked his organization.

Asked when, he places it "the year before." He goes on to say he repeatedly called the Phoenix office asking them to bring the tour to Utah: "Bring him to Utah, you know?"

**On the moment itself.** He describes what he says was an involuntary reaction:

> And then some asshole shooting [him] dead, shooting in the head, and I went nuts, okay? I jump up and I go, shoot me, shoot me... it was a terrible reaction... I seriously thought I would have taken the bullets for Charlie that day, you know? Because it was that dramatic.

He adds: "I think I have a medical issue. I have a heart problem and I think I had a stroke or something."

**On the payment allegation - the denial.** The interviewer puts the widely circulated claim to him directly, that he told someone at the hospital he had been paid to cause a scene. Zinn's answer:

> No, for heaven's sake. No... that's just crap that's wrong. This was just the trigger reaction. Excuse the pun. It was just a reaction because I didn't expect to see him shot in front of me like that.

Pressed again - whether someone might have put him up to it and then leveraged the separate charges against him - he repeats the denial:

> No, no, no. I think it was a crowd, but nothing would have happened. It was my own reaction. Nobody... I didn't expect that to happen. I didn't know anything about this was gonna happen.

That is the clearest recorded denial of the payment claim from the person the claim is about. The claim itself traces to unverified secondhand and anonymous accounts and has never been officially corroborated. Anyone repeating it should also carry this.

**On the separate case.** Zinn himself raises it, unprompted, in passing: "I ended up getting arrested for it, and plus that, the child images that they found on my phone." That separate matter was resolved in court by his guilty plea; the detail is recorded on his profile page and is not elaborated here.

**On his stated grievance with the event.** He says the speaker had spent a long stretch criticising his church, and that he had objected: "I thought it was been too long... he said, like, 20 minutes bashing the church, and I said, you know, now, Charlie, we're not here to bash the church." The eleven-minute wide capture elsewhere in this archive does contain an extended theological exchange about the Latter-day Saint tradition immediately before the shot, in which the speaker repeatedly says he is not attacking it. Zinn's recollection of the subject matter is therefore consistent with the recorded session, even though his characterisation of it is his own.

The call ends on the timer, with the interviewer trying to arrange a follow-up before Zinn is moved.
