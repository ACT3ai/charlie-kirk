## What Is Not Established

Everything else. And the gap between the visible fact and the headline attached to it is very wide.

The site's own page on this figure, [Curly Hair Man in the Crowd](/distraction_people/Curly_Hair_Man), sets the standard: the names circulating for him come from anonymous crowd-sourced facial matching and public-records guessing, none has been confirmed by a reliable on-the-record source, and being present in a crowd or reacting to a shooting is not evidence of involvement in any crime. No name appears on this page.

Nor does the footage establish what the man believed was happening. A single loud report in a dense outdoor crowd produces wildly inconsistent responses, and standing up when others duck is one of the documented ones — people routinely fail to identify a gunshot as a gunshot, and mistake it for a firework, a dropped speaker, a vehicle backfire or a stunt. A cheer at an event where cheering has been going on for an hour is not, on its own, a cheer about anything in particular. The red text overlays ask the viewer to read intent into a few seconds of body language filmed off a television screen, and body language at that resolution will not carry it.

What would actually settle it is the original broadcast footage at full quality with a timecode, and the wider unedited shot showing the seconds before and after — neither of which is in this clip.
