## What Is Said

The witness, on camera:

> I think they arrested the wrong guy ... whoever did it was a good shooter, a
> sharp shooter. It was one shot, right, directly to the heart.

He names the detained man as [George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn) and
states his own belief that Mr. Zinn was not the shooter. Those are the witness's
words and his opinion, reported here as such. The public record is that
authorities did not charge Mr. Zinn in connection with the shooting and that he
faced unrelated counts. Mr. Zinn is a living person; nothing on this page
asserts he committed any offence in connection with the events at Utah Valley
University.

The reason the clip was reposted is the phrase "directly to the heart." Several
independent on-camera witnesses used heart or chest language in the first hours,
and later reporting settled on a neck injury. The poster's implied question —
why the early accounts converged on the wrong anatomy — is a real one, and it is
worked through at length on the pages linked below. It also has ordinary
answers that have to be weighed first: witnesses under shock misplace wounds,
"heart" is common lay shorthand for a fatal upper-body hit, and live television
selects hard for the most definite-sounding soundbite on offer. This site treats
the convergence as an open question and does not assert that any witness was
coached, scripted or dishonest.
