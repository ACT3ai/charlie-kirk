## What Is Said

The anchor throws to a witness on the scene. The witness then says, in sequence:

> I turn and look, and they are arresting a guy by the name of George Z[inn].
> Yeah, I know the guy. I don't think he did it. I think they arrested the wrong
> guy. I think whoever did it was a good shooter, a sharp shooter. It was one
> shot, right directly to the heart. And we need to find out who did this. ...
> And I don't think it was George Z[inn]. He was poor, broke ... maybe a perfect
> person to pick and say that did it. But I don't think he did it at all. ...
> Yeah, I saw when they actually put the handcuffs behind his back.

The segment then cuts away — "we're gonna go to Jason Chaffetz" — which is what
confirms this ran inside a live programme.

Three things in that statement are worth separating out.

**The identification of the detained man.** The witness names
[George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn) as the person he saw taken into
custody, and says he knows him personally. This is the witness's account. It is
consistent with the public record in one important respect: authorities did not
charge Mr. Zinn in connection with the shooting, and he faced unrelated counts.
Mr. Zinn is a living person, and nothing on this page asserts that he committed
any offence in connection with the events at Utah Valley University.

**The witness's opinion that the wrong man was detained.** "I don't think he did
it" and "maybe a perfect person to pick" are stated by the witness as his own
belief on live television, minutes after the event, about a man he says he knew.
They are reported here as his opinion. The site does not adopt them, and they
are not findings.

**The wound and shooter description.** "A good shooter, a sharp shooter. It was
one shot, right directly to the heart." This is the same heart framing that
recurs across several independent on-camera interviews from the same window, and
it is why this clip sits in this cluster rather than in the section on the
detained man. The witness gives no basis for the marksmanship assessment; it is
an impression formed on the plaza.

The witness also says publicly that the events warrant investigation. That call
is recorded as his, and this page draws no inference from it about what anyone
did or did not know.
