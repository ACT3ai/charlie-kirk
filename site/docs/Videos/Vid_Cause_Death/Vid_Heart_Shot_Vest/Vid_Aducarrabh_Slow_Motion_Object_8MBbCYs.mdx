## What Is Said

The claim is in the post text rather than in narration. The poster wrote:

> Something whizzes by Charlie Kirk a split second before impact. The bullet also appears to have, indeed, ricocheted off of his vest and through his neck. Watch in slow motion.

That is three separate assertions bundled into one sentence, and they are worth separating:

1. An object passes through frame shortly before the impact.
2. Charlie Kirk was wearing a vest.
3. The round struck that vest and deflected upward into his neck.

Only the first is even potentially a visual observation. The second and third are inferences about what a projectile did inside and against clothing, which no slowed video frame can show. The poster offers no basis for the vest premise; the vest-and-body-armour thread runs through several posts made in the same hours by different accounts, and none of the surviving material in this corpus establishes it.

This page reports the claim. It does not adopt it.
