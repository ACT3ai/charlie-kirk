## What Is Said

Everything here is the unnamed speaker's assertion. It is reported because it is an artefact people circulate. None of it is adopted, and almost none of it is supported by anything else in the record.

On the mechanism, which is the reason the clip is filed here: "Charlie Kirk was assassinated today by a professional ... He double-tapped Charlie right in the heart from 219 yards. That is not an easy shot ... But Charlie was wearing body armor. Why? And that's plate armor. And what happened was the bullets hit, fragmented, and came up through his neck. Killed him."

On the response: "No security at this event. No ambulances at this event. They stuffed him in the back of an SUV. Just six dudes had to pick him up and take him away."

On motive, quoting Kirk at second hand: "why was Charlie wearing body armor? Because Charlie was telling people that he thought Israel was going to kill him ... Charlie was recently on the PBD podcast, and he was talking about how October seventh, that Israel had to have stood down."

On the final question from the audience: "I want to know who asked that question. Because that was very leading, and it sure seemed like it was the trigger point."

On the investigation: "then you got your patsy, and they didn't catch this guy. And now you got Kash Patel out there, still looking like a fool because he has no control over the FBI and is getting fed bad information ... They caught a guy with a pellet gun ... and then some old homeless man."

He closes with a general call for accountability and a great deal of rage.

Several of these deserve an explicit marker. The "double-tap" and "219 yards" figures are stated without source. The body-armour premise is asserted, not shown. The suggestion that the final questioner was a "trigger point" is speculation about a living person and is not supported anywhere; the questioner was interviewed and cleared, and the counterpoints are set out on [his profile](/People/hunter-kozak). This site does not assert that anyone in that audience knew anything in advance or did anything wrong.
