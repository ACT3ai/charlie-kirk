## Why This Footage Sits In The Investigation

The event the caption points at is the destruction of **Accurate Energetic Systems** Building 602 in McEwen, Tennessee on the morning of October 10, 2025 — roughly 23,000 to 24,600 pounds of energetic material detonating at about 7:47 a.m. CDT, registering as a seismic event near magnitude 1.6, felt more than twenty miles away. Public reporting on the disaster records 16 workers killed inside the building and 7 injured, with ATF, FBI, DHS, Tennessee authorities and the U.S. Chemical Safety Board investigating, and later fines and lawsuits over safety conditions. Those details are set out on [AES — the factory explosion](/Mic/aes-explosion-factory).

AES matters to this investigation because researchers have flagged a single Department of Defense contract to that company — described in procurement records as miniaturised anti-personnel demolition charges, valued at about $440,494, with a delivery date of August 25, 2025 — two weeks before September 10, 2025. Those researchers ask whether such a device could relate to the exploding-mic thesis. That is their question and their framing, not a finding of this site; the contract, the delivery date and the flight records they cite are laid out with attribution on [AES — the company](/Mic/AES), [the DoD contract](/Mic/DoD_Contract) and [the Heber City to Nashville flight](/Mic/aes-heber-nashville-flight). Nothing in this six minutes of roadside footage speaks to any of that. It is a worker on a road, and without sound it does not even tell us what he said about the fire.
