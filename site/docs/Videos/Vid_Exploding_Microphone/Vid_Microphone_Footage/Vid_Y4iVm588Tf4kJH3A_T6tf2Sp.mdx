## What The Frames Do Not Establish

Two captions in this stretch need to be handled carefully, because they are accusations and they are the clip's, not this site's.

The edit places the words "These are the Assassins" over unidentified people in the crowd, and circles the back pocket of an unnamed man in a plaid shirt. **This page does not adopt those labels and does not identify anyone they were placed over.** No such identification has been established, none of these figures is named in the clip, and pointing a graphic at a stranger in a crowd is not evidence of anything about that person. A separate caption, "Tyler was the patsy," states the poster's view of the charged defendant; the substance of that argument, which is genuinely contested, sits on [Tyler Robinson](/Killer/tyler-robinson) and [the patsy framework](/Gov_Mind_Control/tyler-robinson-patsy-framework).

More broadly: nearly every frame of the analytic sequences is low-resolution archival footage digitally enlarged, colour-filtered and slowed. The measurement graphics are drawn on top of that magnification, and a line drawn across a blurred region does not become a measurement because it has a number next to it. Manufacturer dimensions for the MagClip are real and checkable; the joule figure derived from applying them to a video frame is not, absent the scale and frame-rate assumptions the clip never shows. The stated venue in the file's automated index is also wrong — the banners for Turning Point USA and Yrefy, the barricades and the covered walkway are the Utah Valley University courtyard in Orem, not a Florida venue.

None of that makes the underlying thesis wrong. It means this clip is a well-organised summary of a case, and the case has to be tested against the pages where the sourcing lives.
