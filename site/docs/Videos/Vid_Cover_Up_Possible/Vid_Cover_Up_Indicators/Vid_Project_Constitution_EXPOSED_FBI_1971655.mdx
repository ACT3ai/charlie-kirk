"EXPOSED: FBI Ordered Local Police Not to INTERVIEW Witnesses In Char[lie Kirk
case]".

The substance is **police radio traffic**, not video of an event. The audio is

## What Is Said

Two exchanges, and they carry different weight.

**The first is the headline line.** A unit states, plainly and without prompting:

> "As per SBI and FBI, we've been given clear direction not to interview."

The surrounding traffic gives it context: a unit is en route, an investigative
team "is gonna be up there to interview", and local officers are confirming they
will hold off - "confirming negative interview until they get there... we were
not gonna interview. We are just arriving now." The reply is "Copy that."

Read strictly, that is a **sequencing instruction** on the radio: local units are
told not to conduct interviews before the state and federal investigators arrive.
Whether that amounts to witness interviews being blocked, or to normal
deconfliction between agencies at a major scene, is exactly the question the site
tracks at
[Witness Interviews Blocked](/CoverUp/Witness_Interviews_Blocked). The clip
establishes that the instruction was given and acknowledged. It does not, on its
own, establish the intent behind it, and the headline's word "ordered... not to
interview witnesses" is the poster's characterisation of a line that says
"not to interview" without specifying whom.

**The second exchange concerns a specific man.** Dispatch relays that someone
called 911, that "there's an APB out on him for the shooting at UVU", and gives a
location - rendered in the transcript as the "Urban Pioneer Cafe at SMVB", which
the investigation's own notes read as the Urban Pioneer Cafe and the Sorenson
Molecular Biotechnology Building. A unit is then told:

> "I was just told that the FBI wants you to stand by there with him and they'll
> - they're on their way to you... and that came from the agent that's leaving
> Provo PD right now."

So the same pattern appears twice in 48 seconds: local officers make contact,
and are instructed to hold position and wait for federal agents rather than to
proceed themselves.

Nobody is named in the audio. This page names nobody either. The man referred to
is not identified, no allegation against him is recorded here, and the presence
of an alert does not establish that any person did anything.

## What The Footage Shows

Very little, and that is the honest characterisation. This is an **audio
artefact with a video wrapper**. The evidential content is entirely in the radio
traffic; there is no scene footage, no document on screen, and nothing visual
that corroborates or dates the audio.

That produces a specific gap a reader should hold onto. The clip does not
establish, from within itself:

* **when** the transmissions occurred, beyond being on the day;
* **which agency's channel** they were pulled from;
* **whether the two exchanges are contiguous** or have been cut together from
  different points in the traffic;
* **who the "him" in the second exchange is**, or what became of him.

The line itself - "as per SBI and FBI, we've been given clear direction not to
interview" - is the kind of statement that is either verifiable or refutable
against the full unedited radio archive and the agencies' own records. That is
why it belongs in a records-disclosure argument rather than in a settled-fact
one. The investigation's position, and this page's, is that the clip is a
**pointer to a record that should be released in full**, not a conclusion.

The detail worth flagging for anyone working the timeline: the second exchange
puts local officers on a stand-by hold with a man at a location that the
investigation's notes place well away from campus, waiting for an agent
travelling from Provo PD. If that reading is right, the effect is local units
being occupied for an extended period. Whether that is significant depends
entirely on records this clip does not contain.
