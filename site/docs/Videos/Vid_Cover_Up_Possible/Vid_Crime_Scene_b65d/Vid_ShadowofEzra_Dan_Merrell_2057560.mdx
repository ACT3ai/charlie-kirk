## What Is Said

Merrell speaks throughout, in a plain and somewhat frustrated register, with one expletive edited out below.

**Why he took the job.** "When I got the call, I thought -- you better believe it, I'm gonna go help. That was all it was: I'm going to go help these guys. He's asking for help. They want to do it right. He framed it in the sense that, hey, we want this to be done right. So that's why I showed up."

**The technical point.** He describes standard practice: "to do pavers correctly, you over-excavate, you get rid of the top... you want to get a good base." He agrees emphatically when the interviewer presses the point. This is the sentence that connects the clip to the wider question about how much material was removed from the courtyard and when, because over-excavation removes exactly the surface layer that would carry physical traces.

**Who he says called him.** He describes an improvised chain of command: "these guys had kind of, you know, there was two guys in charge and a ragtag crew. One of the guys was in charge of a state-owned facility in American Fork. And the other guy was a maintenance groundskeeper on a facility in Provo. And these are the two guys I'm corresponding with."

Neither man is named in the clip, and this page does not name them.

**The line the clip travelled for.** Merrell then relays what he says the second contact told him:

> "The second guy that I hadn't talked to on the phone basically said, hey, look, we're not talking to anybody, we're not saying anything to the media. He says this is above our pay grade. The FBI and the state, the governor and the FBI. That's what they told me. The governor and the FBI said they want this done by Monday and we need to get it done."

That sentence needs reading carefully, because it is easy to compress into something it does not say. It is Merrell reporting what an unnamed state employee told him he had been told. It is third-hand. Merrell does not claim to have spoken to anyone at the FBI or the governor's office, and does not claim to have seen any order.

Neither the Utah governor's office nor the FBI has confirmed issuing such an instruction, and this site does not assert that either did. What the clip establishes is narrower and still substantial: that the contractor who did the work understood it to be urgent, understood a deadline of Monday to be in force, and understood the people who called him to believe the request came from above them.
