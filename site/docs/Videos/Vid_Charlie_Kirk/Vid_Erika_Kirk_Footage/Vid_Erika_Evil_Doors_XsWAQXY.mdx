## What This Video Is

A seventeen minute nine second capture of Erika Kirk's public address, delivered from the studio her husband broadcast from, two days after his death — she says so herself: "Two days ago, my husband, Charlie, went to see the face of his savior." The file is held in the investigation's archive as `Erika_Evil_Doors.mp4`, and the archive's name for it points at the passage researchers keep returning to.

The capture appears to have been taken from a public-broadcaster stream rather than from the primary feed: the final thirty seconds are a public-television membership card with a donation URL and QR code, which is a broadcaster's own end-slate and not part of the address.

A correction to the stored machine description, which the earlier draft of this page carried. The speaker is Erika Kirk, the placard carries her husband's portrait with the line "May Charlie be received into the merciful arms of Jesus, our loving Savior", and the seventeen minutes are her address, not a news segment.

## What Is Said

The address is long and personal and most of it is grief and faith. What follows records the parts that bear on the investigation, without stripping the rest of its character.

**Who she thanks, by name.** She opens with law enforcement, first responders, and "the police who acted bravely to make sure that there were no other victims on that terrible afternoon." She then thanks the Turning Point USA board, "the COO, Justin Streiff", and "my husband's chief of staff, the amazing Mikey McCoy, for all their work in these terrible days, to be the stability for our family." Later she thanks "my husband's dear friend, Vice President Vance, and his phenomenal wife, Usha" — "you guys honored my husband so well bringing him home" — and President Trump: "My husband loved you. And he knew that you loved him, too."

Those names, and the roles she assigns them two days in, are documentary. Several of them are the subject of the investigation's own timeline work.

**The plural.** The passage the file is named for comes about two-thirds through:

> "The evildoers responsible for my husband's assassination have no idea what they have done. They killed Charlie because he preached a message of patriotism, faith, and of God's merciful love."

She uses the plural — evildoers, they — at a point when one man was in custody and the public account was of a lone actor. Researchers have noted the plural. It is worth noting carefully: a widow speaking two days after her husband's murder is not giving testimony about a conspiracy, and "they" is ordinary English for an unknown killer or for the forces a speaker holds responsible in a broader sense. The word choice is recorded here because it is on the tape, not because it establishes anything.

**The continuity announcements.** She commits publicly to the organisation carrying on, in specifics: "Our campus tour this fall will continue. There will be even more tours in the years to come. America Fest here in Phoenix this December will go on... The radio and podcast show that he was so proud of will go on." And the promise: "I'll make Turning Point USA the biggest thing that this nation has ever seen." She does not, in this address, describe herself as taking a role — the question of the succession is worked out on the site's own pages.

**The personal register.** She quotes Ephesians 5:25 and says "my husband laid down his life for me, for our nation, for our children." She admits she does not understand it — "I honestly have no idea what any of this means. I know that God does, but I don't." She describes coming home to their three-year-old daughter and being asked where her father was: "What do you tell a three year old? I said, baby, Daddy loves you so much... he's on a work trip with Jesus." She says she cannot remember the last time she slept.

She closes on calls to action: join a local chapter, start one if there is none, come to the December event, and "if you aren't a member of a church, I beg you to join one."
