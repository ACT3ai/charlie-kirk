## What This Video Is

Thirty-one minutes fifty-eight seconds — the **full source** that the short Bonhoeffer clips circulating elsewhere were cut from. It is a Lance Wallnau broadcast episode. Wallnau opens with a new introduction recorded after September 10, 2025, explaining why he is republishing an old interview, then plays the interview complete, then closes with a sign-off.

The interview itself is archival. Wallnau dates it by circumstance: he and Kirk were together in Dallas for a young women's summit with about 2,500 attendees, and, in his words, Kirk "was just not even married to Erika — she was there, he was introducing her." That, plus Kirk's references to the previous year's event being cancelled by lockdowns and to a forthcoming book with the working title *The College Scam*, places the recording in roughly mid-2021, more than four years before Kirk's death.

Anyone quoting the Bonhoeffer or Dorito lines should quote them from here. The two short reaction videos held on this site — [the MP4 copy](/Videos/Vid_Charlie_Kirk/Vid_Charlie_His_Own/Vid_Did_Know_Charlie_Kirk_p7Xm3eS) and [the YouTube copy](/Videos/Vid_Charlie_Kirk/Vid_Charlie_His_Own/Vid_Did_Know_Charlie_Kirk_SPmC52p) — carry seventy-three seconds of it with a commentator talking over the top.

## What Is Said

**Wallnau's post-September-10 framing (opening minutes).** He describes the Dallas event, says he caught the interview informally — "just two guys constantly being misrepresented in the media, always comparing notes in private about Christian nationalism and the latest articles" — and explains why he is putting it out now: "now you go back in your archives... I got friends of mine going back to texts from Charlie, or pictures they have, and it just makes me realise how precious he is, how precious life is."

**The interview proper.** Wallnau's introduction credits Kirk with mobilising "the next generation of conservatives and Christians." Kirk answers a question about what young audiences respond to:

> "There is a growing desire and a growing appetite for order in the midst of chaos of our entire society. Conservatives must be very clear about what we stand for and why we stand for it — things that are eternal, things that are divine, things that have proven to work. We must be rooted in empiricism and not just abstractions."

**School boards.** Kirk describes turning up unannounced at school board meetings — "I popped up randomly at the Chandler Unified School District a couple nights ago" — and says his point is to get parents involved: "I'm not a guy that complains about something and doesn't try to do about it. I can't stand this kind of spectator political culture."

**Turning Point Faith and Bonhoeffer.** This is the passage the short clips excerpt:

> "At Turning Point Faith, our vision — and it might be foolishly optimistic, but I've been called that before — we want a thousand Dietrich Bonhoeffers. We're not going to say we're going to create them. I want to find them and encourage them. Find and encourage. Because I'm not going to train people. I just want to find and encourage."

Wallnau raises the branding problem — Bonhoeffer "died as a martyr," so a thousand Martin Luthers might sell better. Kirk answers:

> "I like the Bonhoeffer because of the circumstances — the tyranny. And I also like the Bonhoeffer because I never want to make anyone feel as if I'm being anything but honest. It's going to cost us something. God forbid our life. But eventually we're all going to have to go to heaven. So Bonhoeffer is the example of: hey, I'm willing to sacrifice everything I have for God's purpose on earth."

> "I suppose I'd rather die a glorious martyrdom than by getting a Dorito stuck in my throat."

Wallnau replies with the line about dying twice — once in fact, and again the last time your name is spoken — and says Bonhoeffer will be spoken of as long as human beings have breath.

**The line the short cuts leave out.** Later in the same interview, describing what he wants Turning Point Faith to be, Kirk goes further than the excerpted passage does:

> "I'm not here to take credit. I'm not here to build some sort of megaplex Christian Incorporated thing. I just want to play a part in an energy flow towards the good. That's it. And raise up a thousand martyrs. We're all going to be — we're all dead eventually, and then we get to truly live."

**A quoted challenge to the churches.** Kirk cites a preacher he says he wanted to quote in a book and found hard to source, on the argument that "the communists are more willing to sacrifice and pay a price to advance their ideology of death and destruction than Christians are as missionaries to lay down their life."

**The close.** Wallnau and Kirk range across Teddy Roosevelt, the oligarchs, and a historical argument about Lenin's return to Russia in 1917, before Wallnau ends by pointing viewers to TPUSA and, in a line that reads very differently now than when it was recorded, "keep praying for Charlie Kirk. He really, really, really needs it."

## What The Footage Shows

Standard broadcast interview staging: two men at a round table with branded graphics, Wallnau in a suit, Kirk in a white t-shirt, event venue rather than studio. Wallnau's new introduction and outro bracket it. There is nothing forensic here and no footage of any event — it is a talk programme.

**On the foreknowledge framing.** Nothing in these thirty-two minutes indicates that Kirk knew of any plan, threat or event; the recording predates September 10, 2025 by more than four years. What it does document, in his own words and at length, is a theology of costly witness that he held publicly and consistently — martyrdom as a category he admired, invoked as a model, and joked about. That is genuinely load-bearing for understanding both what he was willing to say and how his death was framed afterwards by supporters and critics alike. It is not evidence of anything he was told.
