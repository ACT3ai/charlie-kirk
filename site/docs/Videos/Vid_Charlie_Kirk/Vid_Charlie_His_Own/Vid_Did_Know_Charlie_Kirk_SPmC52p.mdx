## What Is Said

The transcripts of the two copies differ only in the small ways two passes of speech recognition over the same audio always differ. The substance:

**The commentator's opening:** "These comments by Charlie Kirk are astonishing considering what happened, and I'll tell you why at the end. Watch."

**Kirk on the Turning Point Faith vision:**

> "So at Turning Point Faith, our vision — and it might be foolishly optimistic, but I've been called that before — we want a thousand Dietrich Bonhoeffers. We're not going to say we're going to create them. I want to find them and encourage them. That's it. Find and encourage. Find and encourage. Because I'm not going to train people. I just want to find and encourage."

**Wallnau's objection:** that Bonhoeffer "died as a martyr," and that a thousand Martin Luthers would be an easier sell.

**Kirk's reply:**

> "I like the Bonhoeffer example because of the circumstances — the tyranny. And I also like the Bonhoeffer because I never want to make anyone feel I'm being anything but honest. It's going to cost us something. God forbid, our life. But eventually we're all going to have to go to heaven. And so Bonhoeffer is the example of: hey, I'm willing to sacrifice everything I have for God's purpose on earth."

**And the line the edit is built around:** "I suppose I'd rather die a glorious martyrdom than by getting a Dorito stuck in my throat."

**Wallnau closes** with the remark that "you die twice — when you actually die, and the last time somebody mentions your name," and that Bonhoeffer will be mentioned for as long as there are human beings.

**The commentator signs off** by asking viewers whether Kirk will be remembered as a significant political figure or as a Christian martyr, and to leave answers in the comments.
