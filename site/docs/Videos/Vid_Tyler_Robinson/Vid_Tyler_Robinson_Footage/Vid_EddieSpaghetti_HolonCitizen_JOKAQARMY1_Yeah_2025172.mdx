## What This Video Is

Fifty seconds of a law-enforcement press conference, reposted to X by the account EddieSpaghetti in a reply thread with @HolonCitizen and @JOKAQARMY1. The poster's caption frames it as the District Attorney's account of how the suspect crossed campus. The clip is a straight capture of a podium statement: a man in a dark suit, white shirt and striped tie reads from a prepared script into a gooseneck microphone while a uniformed officer in a tan shirt stands slightly behind him to his right. There are no cuts, no graphics and no commentary added by the poster.

## What Is Said

The speaker reads a continuous surveillance narrative. From the opening seconds:

"At approximately 11:51 a.m. the suspect entered campus from the north. He is seen wearing a black shirt with an American flag in the center, a dark baseball cap and large sunglasses."

He then describes the quality of the imagery, which is the part most often left out when this clip is quoted: "Throughout the surveillance, the suspect keeps his head down and rarely raises his head enough to get a clear image of his face."

About halfway in comes the assertion the clip is circulated for:

"As he proceeds across the campus, he is seen walking with an unusual gait. The suspect walks with very little bending in his right leg, consistent with a rifle being hidden in his pants."

The statement closes by tracking the figure to the roof: the unusual gait "continues until the suspect is seen crossing the railing off the open walkway and onto the roof where he leaves the camera's view."

Three things are worth separating out. First, this is a prosecutor's characterization of surveillance imagery, offered publicly before trial, not a finding of fact by a court. Second, the phrase "consistent with" is an inference about what a stiff leg could mean, not a statement that a rifle was visible; nothing in the narration claims a weapon was seen on camera. Third, the same statement concedes that the face is never clearly captured, which is the crux of the identification questions researchers have raised about this footage.

## What The Footage Shows

Almost nothing beyond the podium. This is a single static medium shot for the full fifty seconds. The surveillance stills being described are not shown in this clip; the viewer hears the description and sees only the man describing it. That gap between narrated claim and displayed evidence is the reason the clip travels.
