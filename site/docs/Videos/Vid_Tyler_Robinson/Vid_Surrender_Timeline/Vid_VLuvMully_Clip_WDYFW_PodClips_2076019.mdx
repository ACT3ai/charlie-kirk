## What Is Said

The host opens on a family claim and immediately disclaims it: "I don't know. I haven't been able to fact check this myself, but with a relative of Tyler Robinson that claims essentially all of that to be true, that Tyler Robinson never confessed, that the reason that they, quote unquote, turned him in was because they feared for his life, that it was not because he was guilty."

Coleman takes it up on the same terms, and then turns to the item he considers testable: "I have heard that he turned himself in very early in the afternoon, not late in the evening. And if that turns out to be true, that throws the prosecution's timeline all off."

He spells out why the hour matters: "their entire narrative depends on him turning himself in after 9 o'clock p.m. And if he was in custody before 6 p.m., let's say, maybe as early as 3 p.m., we have a real problem on our hands." He names where that problem would start, referring to the sheriff's public account of a first call at 8:02 p.m. and of not having heard the accused's name before it. The sheriff's own statement of that call is filed beside this clip in this section.

His supporting point concerns records rather than testimony: he says a news organisation requested footage of the surrender, that the county did not provide it, and that it was subsequently said to have been deleted under a retention policy. His objection is that court filings have since suggested such footage does exist and is held by law enforcement.

He states the consequence in the conditional, and the conditional should be preserved: "That video footage is definitive... if he turned himself in between 3 and 6 o'clock." If instead the surrender happened at nine, he says, "then we just go forward with the probable cause hearing in the trial and let's figure out if he's the one who did it or not."

His complaint is that a question this simple remains open: "that is such a definitive way to rule out that he did it alone, that I don't understand why we don't have access to it. What time did he turn himself in?"

He raises a second unresolved item in passing: "I'm also bothered by the fact that we still don't know where Charlie Kirk's shirt was."
