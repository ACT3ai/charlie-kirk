## What Is Said

A short exchange, three voices: the hearing officer, counsel, and a second
respondent.

> [Hearing officer]: We will turn to you, Ms. Cole, and maybe you can address
> that question as well, just as far as body camera footage or other photos,
> because I think that is a distinct request in this appeal. You have up to
> 5 minutes.
>
> [Counsel]: Thank you. Again, the records are maintained by ... Washington
> County. I'm not sure what search was done or whether those exist, but ...
>
> [Hearing officer]: Okay, maybe we can ask Mr. Snow to just address that first.
>
> [Mr. Snow]: Sure, there was a search made for body cam footage and there was
> none.
>
> [Hearing officer]: Okay, thank you very much.

That is the whole clip. It is nine seconds of substance inside thirty-five
seconds of audio, and the nine seconds matter.

**What is established.** On the record, at a formal appeal hearing, a
representative for the county states that a search was made for body camera
footage and that none was found. Not that it exists and is exempt — that it does
not exist.

**Why that is significant.** It puts the body-camera question on a different
footing from the lobby surveillance question. For the lobby video, the county's
stated position is that release could interfere with the investigation and the
prosecution, which presupposes something to release. For body-camera footage,
the stated position is that there is nothing there at all. Those are two
distinct answers to two distinct requests, and they should not be collapsed into
one another.

**What is not established.** Nothing in this exchange explains why no body-camera
footage was found — whether cameras were not activated, whether the encounter
was not one that triggers recording, whether footage was recorded and later
purged under a retention schedule, or whether the search was scoped narrowly.
Counsel for the requester says plainly that she does not know what search was
done. The clip does not answer that, and this page does not assume an answer.
Nobody in this exchange is accused of anything, and none is implied here.

The related question of whether other body-camera recordings in this case exist,
and what became of them, is examined separately on this site; a comparable gap
was reported for an officer's camera on the Losee Center roof.
