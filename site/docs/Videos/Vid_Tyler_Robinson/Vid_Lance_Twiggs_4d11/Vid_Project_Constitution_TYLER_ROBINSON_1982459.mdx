**He sought comment.** Near the end he states plainly: "I have done my best to

## What The Footage Shows

Entirely screen capture of a browser on a Substack page. Within it:

* The post header, with the pseudonymous byline and October 2025 date.
* Screenshots of Discord in dark mode on a mobile device. The primary sender's
  name is **redacted with a heavy black bar**; other usernames are left visible.
* A blurry night photograph of a two-storey apartment complex behind police tape,
  with a sheriff's pickup and its lights on in the foreground.
* An overhead photograph of a folding table covered in tangled wires, tools,
  plastic organisers and a small terrarium.

Three observations about the evidentiary weight of what is on screen:

* **The redaction cuts both ways.** Blacking out the primary sender's name
  protects the individual, and it also removes the only thing on the screenshot
  that would let a reader check whose account it was. What remains is text in a
  Discord-shaped frame.
* **Screenshots are not archives.** The presenter says archives were provided,
  but what is displayed are images. An image of a chat log is trivially
  editable; an exported archive with metadata is not. Nothing shown here is the
  latter.
* **The photographs are the strongest items.** The exterior shot with police tape
  is the sort of thing an actual neighbour or visitor would have and a fabricator
  would find harder to produce. It is what the presenter leans on for
  verification, and that is a reasonable weighting.

Taken together: this is a **single anonymous source, filtered through a
pseudonymous writer, presented as images rather than as verifiable exports**, with
one genuine corroborating detail. It is a lead worth recording and it is not
established fact, and the personal material within it concerns a private person
who has answered nothing.
