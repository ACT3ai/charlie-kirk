## What Is Said

The narration is a single continuous argument about geometry. He begins by asking where the crowd thought the sound came from: "Again, where did a lot of people in the crowd think a gunshot might have come from? The rest is an inventory. He pauses the footage and works across the frame: "we can look under what's going on under here. We can look at that rooftop, make sure there's no people up there. We can look in these offices, make sure there's no people up there. Yep, all clear here, all clear here, no law enforcement here, no law enforcement up here."

The clip ends with a stray fragment of unrelated radio-style chatter — "Got an idea. Okay, got a visual. Thanks, bud. You can move" — which sits oddly against the rest and is not explained on screen.

Note what he is and is not claiming. He is not naming a shooter. He is arguing two negatives: that a position many people converged on could not have seen the target, and that at the moment captured he can see no people and no law enforcement on the rooftops or in the office windows he checks. Both are claims about one frozen frame of one wide shot.
