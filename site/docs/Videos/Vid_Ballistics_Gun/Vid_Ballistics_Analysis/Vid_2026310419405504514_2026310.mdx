## What The Footage Shows

Two shots, both static camera, in sequence.

* **0:00 - 0:05** — Medium shot. The gel bust sits on a white folding table in
  an open field bordered by leafless deciduous trees and dry grass, under heavy
  overcast light. At about 0:01 a round strikes the front of the bust. Gel
  ruptures out of the back of the neck and the head buckles forward onto the

* **0:05 - 0:26** — Extreme slow motion, close-up, side profile of the same
  bust. The round enters at the throat. A large temporary cavity opens along

No people appear on camera. The only objects in frame are the bust, the table
and a plastic water jug in the background of the second shot.

What the footage establishes and what the post claims are not the same thing.
The clip demonstrates one general proposition: a high-energy rifle round
through a neck-sized gel block produces a violent through-and-through with a
large temporary cavity and material ejected out the back. It does not identify
the calibre used in the test, does not state the range, and does not compare
its result against any wound described in the Kirk case. Whether the
demonstration is a fair analogue for the neck injury at UVU is exactly the
question under dispute, and the video does not settle it. Read it as a visual
premise offered in support of an argument, not as evidence about the event.
