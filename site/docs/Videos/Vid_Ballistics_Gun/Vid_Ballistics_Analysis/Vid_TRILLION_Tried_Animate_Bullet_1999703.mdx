## What This Video Is

A six-second animated graphic, posted to X by the account ᵀᴿᴵᴸᴸᴵᴼᴺ under the
caption "I tried to animate the bullet path but even artificial physics
weren't..." — the caption is truncated in the archived filename. It is a meme,
not analysis: a flat 2D anatomical diagram on a white background, animated once
and looped.

There is no speech in the clip. The audio is a gunshot report at about one
second and a cartoon whoosh as the animated round travels. Because there is no
dialogue, there is nothing to transcribe, and the transcription pass over the
file returned a single stray token.

The argument it makes is made entirely by ridicule. The animator has drawn the
trajectory that he says the official account requires, and let it play out at
face value: the round arrives at the front of the neck, fails to penetrate,
stretches the skin outward like a trampoline, rebounds, and then travels a
zig-zag path up through the cervical spine and out the back. The joke is the
path itself.

## What The Footage Shows

A skull and cervical spine in profile, facing right, wearing a bright blue
"AMERICA FIRST" ball cap with a small American flag on the side. A black rifle
barrel enters at frame right, pointed at the neck. A red line marks the front
of the neck and loops behind the spine. At about 0:01 a gold round is fired, the
red neck boundary stretches and snaps back, and the round follows the red
zig-zag up through the vertebrae and off frame left.

Around the diagram sit mock statistics and annotations, which is where the
satire is loaded:

* "Probability &lt; 1:50,000"
* "100psi : breaks skin"
* "60,000psi : 30.06 force"
* "0.002%"
* "Zig-zag bullet &lt; 0%"
* "Trampoline Neck V skin bullet"
* "Galarpy Theory"

The cervical and upper thoracic vertebrae are labelled correctly — C1 (atlas)
through C7, then T1, the first rib and the clavicle — which is what makes the
piece land: the anatomy is drawn straight and only the trajectory is absurd. T1
is the level at which a fragment was reportedly recovered, and the "Galarpy
Theory" label is a nod to the single-bullet arguments of earlier assassination
cases.

Treat this as commentary, not evidence. It shows nothing of the event, contains
no footage from Utah Valley University, and asserts no measurement of its own.
Its only claim is that the path implied by the official account is
improbable — a claim that has to be tested against the actual wound and
fragment material elsewhere on this site, not against a cartoon.
