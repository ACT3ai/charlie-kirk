## What The Footage Shows

A clear ballistic-gelatin bust moulded as a head, neck and shoulders with an
anatomical skull set inside it, on a white folding table in an open field
bordered by leafless trees, under flat overcast light. Two static shots, in
sequence: a medium shot at normal speed in which a round strikes the front of
the bust and gel bursts out of the back of the neck as the head buckles forward;
then an extreme slow-motion side profile in which the round enters at the
throat, opens a large temporary cavity along the track, and blasts gel, water
and gas out the rear of the neck while the head tilts down.

No people appear. Nothing in frame identifies the calibre, the range, or who
fired it.

Separate what the footage demonstrates from what the post asserts. The
demonstration establishes one general proposition — a high-energy rifle round
through a neck-sized gel block produces a violent through-and-through with a
large temporary cavity and material ejected out the back. It does not state its
own test parameters, and it never compares its result to any described feature
of the injury at UVU. Whether it is a fair analogue is precisely the question
in dispute. It belongs in the record as a visual premise, not as a finding.
