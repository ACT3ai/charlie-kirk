## What This Video Is

The format is a two-person video call: the host on one side, a guest introduced as Gideon on the other, with Gideon's desktop shared to the main frame and his webcam in the corner. He drives the whole session, opening files from his own directory as he goes.

What he opens, in order of use: a low-resolution sequence of successive frames from the event video, annotated with coloured arrows inside a Word document; a wider shot of the stage showing the Turning Point USA and Yrefy banners; an AI chatbot window into which he pastes his annotated screenshots; his own 3D CAD model of a head with trajectory vectors; and Google Maps 3D satellite and street-level views of the Utah Valley University campus.

It is worth being clear about what kind of artifact this is. It is not a laboratory report, a peer-reviewed analysis, or an examination of physical evidence. It is one person reasoning out loud over low-resolution screenshots and a model he built, on a live call, with a sympathetic host. ## What Is Said

**The ear-colour argument (opening to about 6:00).** Gideon's starting claim is that Kirk "was shot in the right ear." He steps through successive frames, pointing at a region beside the ear where he says a red colouration appears and deepens: "you see that that area, that red area there is increased. At the ear... now we jump to the next one. You can see it's now even darker." He argues the colour matches blood visible at the neck.

**The AI colour analysis (about 6:00 to 11:00).** Rather than assert it, he tests it live. He uploads the annotated screenshots to a chatbot and reads the output aloud in full. The output is careful and heavily qualified: it describes a "vivid scarlet" region at the neck "consistent with fresh arterial or venous blood egress from a puncture wound," but for the ear region it reports only "a subtle darkening" that "could plausibly indicate early blood spill over from the ear canal," and then states that "low image resolution, dental motion blur and lighting artifact introduce uncertainty," that the observation could stem from "localized inflammation or digital compression noise rather than frank bleeding," and that "high resolution images, forensic enhancement would be required to confirm."

Gideon reads that qualification out and then treats the result as supportive anyway: "well, Grok agrees with you... it's saying it needs a higher resolution angle, but it actually is saying that that's possibly blood. So, I mean, that's just proved it." The gap between what the tool said and what he concludes from it is visible on the recording, and a viewer should note it.

**The marksmanship argument (about 11:00 to 13:00).** He states he was "a competition level marksman" in his youth, shooting a .22 with open sights at 25 and 50 metres, and argues that the ear canal makes a natural aiming point: "if I had to shoot somebody from a rooftop, that's where I would aim." He puts the range at "46 to 50 metres by my calculations."

**The trajectory model (about 13:00 to 16:00).** He describes plotting a vector in his 3D model from the right ear canal to what he takes to be a neck wound, obtaining "a 42.6 degree angle," then arguing that momentum transfer would deflect the round downward so "the actual angle was less than 42.6 degrees." He gives a yaw of "about 9.17 degrees" and an azimuth of "8.4 degrees off his 3 o'clock" relative to a plane through the shoulders. Extending that vector on the campus model, he places the origin at a corner of what he calls the BA building rather than the Losee Center. He is explicit that the model is provisional: "there's one caveat, I am waiting to confirm the exact height of these points."

**The conclusion (about 16:00 to the end).** This is the part that matters and the part that needs the most care in reporting. Gideon's stated conclusion is narrow and he states it narrowly: "we're not proving who shot Charlie Kirk. We just proved that Charlie Kirk could not have been shot by somebody from the Losee building." From that he argues Tyler Robinson could not have fired the shot, and he is careful to separate that from any claim about whether Robinson was present: "I'm not saying that he was or was not on the roof. I'm saying he didn't take the shot."

He and the host then move to advocacy. They argue the charges should be dropped, describe Robinson as "an innocent young man," and raise a due-process complaint — the host says he is "in contact with people that have a super high profile defense attorney out of New York that wants to do the case pro bono" and that "the judge is not allowing it." The host also makes a much stronger statement, that anyone continuing with the prosecution "would have actually all been complicit." That is his rhetoric on a live call, and this site does not adopt it or repeat it as a characterisation of anyone.
