## What This Video Is

A 34 minute 42 second episode of **Redacted**, the independent news programme
hosted by **Clayton Morris**, captured from an X post by the Redacted account.
It is a produced studio interview, not raw footage: Morris broadcasts from a
cabin-styled set, and his two guests appear remotely in a three-way split screen
with lower-third captions reading "LIVE FROM: NEVADA" and "LIVE FROM: FLORIDA".

The guests are **Jesse Beltran**, captioned "TSCM-Certified Investigator", and
**Edward Szall**, captioned "Producer of Died Suddenly". Morris introduces them

Two things should be flagged before the content, because they bear on how the
material is weighed. First, this is a **commercial broadcast**: roughly six
minutes in, the programme breaks for a scripted advertisement for a weight-loss
supplement from The Wellness Company, read by Morris with the promo code

guests close by promoting a forthcoming film, "Died Suddenly 2". The claims in
this interview are being made by people with products and a documentary to sell,

## What Is Said

The core assertion is Beltran's, made in the opening minutes. He says a person
he describes as an **MI6 asset** contacted him after the previous Redacted
appearance. He describes the man as Oxford-educated with a chemistry background,
who "showed us all of the documentation to verify that he has been on
assignments in the Middle East... in Somalia", and who was "the head of
operations".

Beltran's account of what that man told him inverts the usual framing of Havana
Syndrome:

> "What he disclosed is that this phenomenon, the Havana Syndrome, AHI... this
> is a communication system."

He describes it as "a form of synthetic telepathy" that was, according to the
source, preferred over "traditional drop boxes or digital assets" because it was
"a very, very discrete, plausible deniability way of communicating" while under
surveillance. Beltran then states the darker half of the claim, again as the
source's account: "if an agent does not follow orders or falls out of line...
it is a multifaceted system and then becomes a torture device." He says the man
asked to be removed from the programme, went to Parliament, and "currently has a
lawsuit in the UK" about the experience. Szall confirms the framing: "this is on
the court documents. His lawsuit against MI6."

Szall places the claim in a historical line, invoking **Project Artichoke**:
"We go back to Project Artichoke, the 50s and 60s, this was a dream. This was a
mission, this was a goal. Well, it's 2026, and that goal has been realized."

He extends the claim beyond MI6 - "The way it was described to us is that every
Five Eyes nation is not only using this, but actively trying to find new
inventive ways to deploy it" - and raises a hypothetical that is the reason this
clip sits in a mind-control cluster rather than a foreign-affairs one:

> "What if you could convince people of a certain political ideology, or a
> religion, or maybe of a certain task, by talking to them like you're the voice
> of God... It could feel like a distant conversation. A constant voice in your
> head. Maybe some would misidentify it as God or misidentify it as their
> internal conscience."

On mechanism, Beltran describes a **dental implant** the source said had been
fitted earlier - "an amplifier type technology. So you send a radio frequency to
it, and it would amplify what was being transmitted" - and then moves to
nanotechnology, asserting that graphene oxide and silicon in the body act as
"amplifiers, miniature antennas", citing a study he attributes to **Dr. Robert
Young** and a *Scientific Reports* article on terahertz graphene stacked-patch
antennas for 6G. He describes a UK "human subject trial study" he says he
assisted, run through a company he names as Human Consciousness Support with a
CEO he names as Matt Hazen, and reports a "mean average of 74.6 percent"
reduction in detected silicon indicators.

Around 24 minutes in, Morris asks whether the effect is delivered locally or at
a distance. Beltran rejects the handheld-weapon framing outright, calling it "a
psyop and a misdirection", and says that in his assessment "this is satellite
based, integrated with cell towers." His argument is one of logistics: with
"tens of thousands of people globally who are experiencing this", he asks, "what
is the feasibility to have that many individuals out there being paid to aim a
device at these individuals?"

Two further claims in the episode should be recorded precisely because they are
serious and are made without documentary support on camera. Beltran says
**Aaron Alexis**, the man who killed twelve people at the Washington Navy Yard
in 2013 and died at the scene, contacted him beforehand reporting the same
symptoms, and he notes the letters "E-L-F" that were inscribed on Alexis's
shotgun. Szall raises, as a question rather than an assertion, whether the
capability "would probably even bring into question every mass shooter who's
just randomly snapped". Beltran also states that a congressional task force
chaired by Representative Luna of Florida is considering a hearing on
non-consensual experimentation as a follow-up to MKUltra.

Neither guest offers, on camera, a document, a case number, or a name for the
MI6 source. The lawsuit is described but not shown.
