## What Is Said

**Dr. James C. Lin** opens, captioned with his doctorate. He gives the textbook
description of the microwave auditory effect - and it is worth noting that his
statement is the only strictly scientific claim in the clip:

> "In the case of the microwave auditory effect, the source is electromagnetic.
> So if you expose the biological tissue to a pulse of microwave energy, the
> tissue expands and induces a certain vibration. Instead of going through the
> middle ear, the microwave-induced vibration is propagated to the inner ear
> directly."

A narrator then states that **Dr. Nick Begich**, captioned as an author and
speaker, "cites more than 30 patents", over a scrolling list including
US3951134 and US4877027. That second number is the same 1989 patent the site's
[Frey Effect / V2K page](/Gov_Mind_Control/frey-effect-v2k) identifies from an
independent source, so the two lines of material corroborate each other on at
least that point.

At roughly 00:33 a demonstration subject describes a handheld bone-conduction
device being pressed to his face: "I can hear it when you press it to my skin,
but not when you take it off... it's almost like it's right in the centre of
your head." The presenter is careful about the limit of what has just been
shown, and the clip does not cut the caveat:

> "And you can do that, even if you weren't touching my skin?" - "Not with this
> device. But if I were using a microwave carrier, at a distance, we could
> achieve the same thing."

That exchange is the hinge of the whole compilation. A working device has just
demonstrated bone conduction by contact. The leap to action at a distance is
made verbally, not demonstrated.

From about 00:48 the speaker is **Dr. Robert Duncan**, captioned on screen as
"Ex-CIA Whistleblower", speaking at a lectern. He makes a claim about his own
past work:

> "And something that I worked on, that I'm not proud of - it's called the Voice
> of God weapon. There are four different technologies that can pipe voices into
> an individual's head."

He states that scientists "high up within the ranks of this research" have said
the capability was already used operationally, and gives an example: "We used
this to get them to drop their weapons by saying 'drop your weapons, this is
Allah.'" A second speaker, an unnamed woman in a home office, dates that use to
the Gulf War, and Duncan returns to explain why he believes it worked: "hearing
voices which have no direct source, you have to assume that some spiritual
entity."

The clip then closes with **Woody Norris** on the TED stage - "this idea of
being able to put sound anywhere you want to is really starting to catch on" -
and his line about fake troop movements in Iraq and whispering "some biblical
verse" to a target.
