## What This Video Is

A 1 minute 32 second vertical clip, cut for social media and carrying a TikTok
outro card for the account "@journeyofsound". It is a compilation, not original
footage: roughly the first eleven seconds are stock B-roll of Long Range
Acoustic Device (LRAD) hardware in military and police use, and the remaining

The excerpt is played inside a graphic of an old CRT television, with a caption

The narrator then hands off to the archive footage: "I'll play a video of the
CEO Woody Norris describing the voice of God weapon and what it can do when it
first came out."

From roughly 00:18 onward the voice is Norris's own, on the TED stage. He
describes the commercial and military appeal of putting sound in one place and
nowhere else:

> "So again, this idea of being able to put sound anywhere you want to is
> really starting to catch on. It also works for transmitting and communicating
> data. It also works five times better underwater."

He then gives the military application that is the reason this clip sits in the
investigation at all:

> "We've got -- the military had just deployed some of these into Iraq where you
> can put fake troop movements a quarter of a mile away on a hillside. Or you

He closes with hardware numbers, which are the most checkable claims in the
clip: a version rated at "155 decibels" against a stated pain threshold of

## What The Footage Shows

The footage is considerably narrower than the narration around it. Nothing on
screen shows a weapon being used covertly on an unwitting person. What is shown
is hardware and a lecture.

* **00:03 - 00:07** - Five officers in dark uniform posed behind a large white

* **00:07 - 00:11** - Tracking shot of a military truck on a rural road with
  rectangular LRAD panels on the roof.
* **00:11 - 00:18** - Norris on the TED stage holding a flat square emitter
  panel.
* **00:18 - 01:28** - The TED segment, played inside a CRT-television graphic.
* **01:28 - 01:33** - TikTok outro card.

Caption cards do the persuasive work the footage does not: "V.O.G. Voice of God
LRAD TECHNOLOGY being deployed" and "WEAPONS TECHNOLOGY PUSHING VOICES INSIDE
YOUR HEAD". That second card is where the clip overreaches its own source.
LRAD and the directional emitters Norris is selling are **airborne ultrasonic
and acoustic** devices. They put sound in the air at a chosen spot; the ear
still does the hearing. That is a different mechanism from the microwave
auditory effect, in which pulsed radio-frequency energy produces a sensation of
sound with no acoustic wave in the room at all. Norris himself never says the
voice bypasses the ear. He says the sound can be placed where he wants it.

The distinction matters for anyone weighing this footage as evidence, so it is
worth stating plainly: this clip documents a real, commercially sold,
publicly-demonstrated directional-audio capability, and it labels that
capability with a phrase - "pushing voices inside your head" - that belongs to a
different and much more contested claim.
