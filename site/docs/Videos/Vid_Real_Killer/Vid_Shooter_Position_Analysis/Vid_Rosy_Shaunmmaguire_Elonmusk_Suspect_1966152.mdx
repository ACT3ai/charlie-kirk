## What This Video Is

Twenty seconds of handheld phone video, reposted to X by an account posting as Rosy in a reply thread addressed to @shaunmmaguire and @elonmusk. A white banner across the top of the frame carries the poster's claim: "Suspect Spotted on Rooftop Before Charlie Kirk Incident."

It is a bystander capture, not analysis. There is no editing beyond the caption banner, and the digital zoom is done in-camera by whoever was holding the phone.

## What Is Said

Several voices overlap in a short, fragmentary exchange. The audible lines, in order:

"Get right up. Yeah." Then a question that carries the point: "You think you'd already be in place?" And the answer: "You would think that."

Someone asks for confirmation of what they are looking at: "So there's somebody on the roof right there?" A voice replies, with a gesture, that the figure "just ran from, over, where's my finger? from over there, ran in. And I was right there."

That last line is the substance of the whole clip, and it is worth reading carefully. The speaker is not saying he saw a rifle, a shot or an identity. He is saying he watched something move onto the roof from another direction, and that he was standing close by. The exchange before it is bystanders reasoning aloud about whether a person in position beforehand would already have been there, which is speculation by people who have just seen something odd, not testimony about a plan.

None of the speakers is identified, and none is named here.

## What The Footage Shows

The clip opens looking down a concrete walkway toward a modern university building. The operator then zooms hard on the roofline, where a dark, stationary shape is visible. At the resolution and distance involved the shape is a handful of pixels; no clothing, posture or feature can be read from it, and the automated description records only "a dark, pixelated silhouette."

The camera pans right across the building facade, catching signage, then a blurred finger enters the foreground pointing back at the roof, and the shot returns to the dark shape and holds until the clip ends.

The honest reading is that this footage records people pointing at something on a roof and reacting to it, at a moment when they had reason to. It does not establish what the shape was, and it certainly does not identify anyone. The caption asserts a great deal more than the pixels support.
