## What This Video Is

Thirty-two seconds posted by the account Project Constitution under an all-caps "BREAKING: CHARLIE KIRK SHOOTER IDENTIFIED" banner. It is a short vertical social-media edit: courtyard footage, a 3D visualisation of the building complex with arrows and inset photographs, a piece to camera, and a closing clip of a political speech under a "MORE COMING SOON" card.

The format matters to how it should be read. Thirty seconds is not long enough to present evidence for an identification, and this video does not attempt to. It asserts, points and asks the audience for help.

## What Is Said

The narrator opens with an attention hook: "Hey, yo, stop scrolling for 12 seconds."

He then makes the claim the post is built on, framed as a limited but confident finding: "we were not there to figure out who killed Kennedy. We were not there to figure out who killed King, but we did figure out one thing, that this dude right here, look, this guy right here running, you see him running, that is the guy that took out Kirk. That's the guy. That's him right there."

The closing seconds are a clip of a political speech, not the narrator: "If speech is violence, then some are bound to conclude that violence is justified to stop speech. And we're not gonna let that be justified."

The identification claim is the poster's assertion and nothing in the clip supports it. No name is given, no comparison is offered, no chain of reasoning is shown, and the figure being pointed at is a distant person running through a courtyard during an evacuation in which a great many people ran. This site records that the claim was made and does not adopt it. The person in the frame is not identified here and is entitled to the presumption that they were doing what everyone else in that courtyard was doing.

## What The Footage Shows

A rapid sequence. First, a static close-up of people leaning over a railing looking down with concerned expressions. Then a medium-wide shot of an outdoor concrete courtyard with people running and taking cover. Then a 3D computer visualisation of the building complex with overlaid text, icons, red arrows and inset photographs. Then a shot framed with the narrator's face in the foreground pointing at a running figure in the background. Then a low-fidelity black and white clip of a political speech behind a podium.

The overlays carry the argument, and they are worth listing because they are the actual content: "DO YOU KNOW THIS PERSON?"; an appeal for help with an email address; a Google Maps graphic labelled "UVU Courtyard"; labels for two nicknamed figures; "FREE Tyler Robinson & FIND The..."; a line asserting where the shot came from; and a hashtag.

Two of those overlays are in direct tension. One asks the audience to identify a person the narrator has just told them is the shooter. A claim that has been established does not need a crowdsourcing appeal attached to it. The video is best understood as an identification request with a headline that overstates it.
