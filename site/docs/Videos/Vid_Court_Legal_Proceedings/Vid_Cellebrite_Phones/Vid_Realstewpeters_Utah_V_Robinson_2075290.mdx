## What Is Said

The exchange is short enough to give complete. A prosecutor questions a witness on the stand:

> **Q.** "As part of the investigation, was a Cellebrite extraction performed on Mr. Twiggs's phone?"
> **A.** "Yes, it was."
> **Q.** "Did you personally review that extraction?"
> **A.** "Yes, I have."
> **Q.** "And according to that extraction, did Mr. Robinson and Mr. Twiggs exchange text messages on September 10 through 11th, 2025?"
> **A.** "Yes, they did."
> **Q.** "What time was that first text sent, according to the Cellebrite extraction?"
> **A.** "The first text was September 10th at 11 p.m."

Three facts are established on the record by that exchange, and they are worth isolating because they are checkable: a Cellebrite extraction was performed on Lance Twiggs's phone; the witness personally reviewed it; and the earliest message between the two accounts recorded in that extraction is timed at 11 p.m. on September 10, 2025 — roughly eleven hours after the shooting.

That last detail is the one with weight. It is a specific, sworn timestamp, and it can be compared against the other timelines in this investigation, including the disputes over when messages were created and when devices were in whose custody.
