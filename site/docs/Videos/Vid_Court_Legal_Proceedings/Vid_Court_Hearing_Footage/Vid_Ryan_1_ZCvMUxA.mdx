## What Is Said

### The courtroom footage — the part that matters

Counsel identifies himself on the record: "Your Honor, Jeffrey Nyman on behalf of the Kirk family." He then makes a short statement, quoted here in full because it is a primary record and short enough to give whole:

> "I've been very hesitant to speak up at these proceedings. We understand it's the state, the defense, and the judge — you're all doing your job. But I feel as if we need to. I want to be clear as to what the Kirk family's position is on all of this."

> "The Kirk family believes strongly that if the evidence is being admitted in this preliminary hearing, it should be made public for the world to see. No redactions. This court has tools at its disposal to make sure the defendant receives a fair trial. You'll use them if you find that you need to."

> "To not be transparent here, to not be open, to not let the world see what happened, will create doubt and distrust in the judicial system. And that's not what anybody wants. That's not what any of us believe should happen here. And we'd ask the court to consider the position of the family in making its ultimate decision."

Two things are notable about that. It is a **victim's family, through counsel, asking for less sealing rather than more** — which cuts against the usual assumption that sealing protects the bereaved. And it is framed as a concern about institutional trust, not about any theory of the case: the argument is that opacity itself generates doubt.

### The commentary around it

Before the footage, the presenter digresses into an exchange about Iran, reading out a post attacking Laura Loomer over who pushed for strikes, and referring to remarks attributed to Donald Trump about further strikes. That material is unrelated to the hearing and is noted here only so the running order is honest.

After the footage, he offers his own reading of the family's motive, uses derogatory language about TPUSA that this site does not repeat or adopt, and asserts that the parents must be certain the official account is impossible. **That is his speculation about what other people believe, and nothing supports it.** Nyman's statement says nothing about the merits of the case; it asks for publication of evidence.

He closes with a series of questions — where the autopsy is, where the police reports are, why the scene was covered over, and how a rifle round could be stopped in soft tissue of the neck.
