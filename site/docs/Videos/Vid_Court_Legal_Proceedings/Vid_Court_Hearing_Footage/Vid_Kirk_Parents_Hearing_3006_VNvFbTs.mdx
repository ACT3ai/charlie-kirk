## What Is Said

### In the courtroom

Counsel identifies himself: "Your Honor, Jeffrey Nyman on behalf of the Kirk family." His statement is short enough to give in full, and it is the primary value of this file:

> "I've been very hesitant to speak up at these proceedings. We understand it's the state, the defense, and the judge — you're all doing your job. But I feel as if we need to. I want to be clear as to what the Kirk family's position is on all of this."

> "The Kirk family believes strongly that if the evidence is being admitted in this preliminary hearing, it should be made public for the world to see. No redactions. This court has tools at its disposal to make sure the defendant receives a fair trial. You'll use them if you find that you need to."

> "To not be transparent here, to not be open, to not let the world see what happened, will create doubt and distrust in the judicial system. And that's not what anybody wants. That's not what any of us believe should happen here. And we'd ask the court to consider the position of the family in making its ultimate decision."

The substance is unusual and worth stating plainly: a **victim's family, through counsel, asking the court for less sealing rather than more**, and grounding that request in institutional trust rather than in any theory about who did what. Nyman takes no position on the merits. He asks for publication.

### Around it

The commentator introduces the footage as the first time Kirk's parents had been heard at a hearing, and frames their position as wanting the evidence shown to everyone rather than argued behind closed doors.

Afterwards he offers his own reading of what the family must believe, uses derogatory language about TPUSA that this page does not repeat or adopt, and ends on a set of questions: where the autopsy is, where the police reports are, why the scene was covered over, and — the line the clip is usually cited for — how a .30-06 round could be stopped by soft tissue in the neck without passing through.

**Those closing passages are his assertions about other people's beliefs and about the physical evidence, not findings.** Nyman's statement supports none of them. The wound question he raises is genuinely contested and is treated with its sourcing on [entrance or exit](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/entrance-or-exit) and [the wound entrance-exit dispute](/Suspicious/Medical/wound-entrance-exit-dispute); the autopsy question on [the sealed autopsy and the examiner](/CoverUp/Autopsy_Sealed_Examiner).
