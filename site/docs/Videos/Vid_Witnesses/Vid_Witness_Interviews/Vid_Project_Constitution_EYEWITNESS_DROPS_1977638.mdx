## What This Video Is

Thirty-five seconds of a broadcast eyewitness interview, re-cut and re-branded
for social media by Project Constitution. The original is a National News Desk
field interview recorded near Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025: an
older man in a blue patterned shirt and a white cap, microphone clipped on,
speaking to camera beside a busy road with mountains behind him.

The re-cut adds a lower-third headline quoting the witness — "EYEWITNESS: WHERE
HIS HEART IS, IT STARTED GOING RED" — a "UTAH" locator, and a Project
Constitution logo. The file was archived under the reposter's own headline,
which announces a "NUCLEAR CONFIRMATION" that Charlie Kirk was "SHOT
POINT-BLANK."

**That headline is not supported by the clip it is attached to.** The witness
never says point-blank, never says anything about the distance the shot was
fired from, and explicitly says he did not see a shooter at all. Read the
transcript below against the headline; the gap between them is the first thing
worth noticing about this file.

This is the same witness heard in
[the National Desk clip](/Videos/Vid_Cause_Death/Vid_Eyewitness_Accounts_Wound/Vid_TND_Eyewitness_Like_Direct_9uxwgCE)
elsewhere in this archive, and in the opening seconds of
[the MuppetMasher compilation](/Videos/Vid_Cause_Death/Vid_Eyewitness_Accounts_Wound/Vid_MuppetMasher_Compilation_Camera_Witness_2069350).
This cut runs longer than either and contains material they omit.

## What Is Said

One speaker, uninterrupted. Cleaned of transcription noise:

> [Mike], it was a direct shot to the heart. It looked like right where his
> heart is, it started going red. He [fell] back, and went to his side.
>
> And then I looked down at the crowd, and it looked like the crowd, they all
> went to their knees, really fast. I kind of did go to my knees ... but I
> wanted to see, and anyone who was close to the shooter would be trampling all
> over that person, and I saw nothing like that at all. Everyone had [gone] down
> for a few moments. They were just not moving. And [then] people started
> getting up and moving, and the crowd started pushing me. They pushed me so
> that I was basically [at the] stage.

The first paragraph is the heart description that recurs across the earliest
on-camera interviews and that later reporting did not bear out — the injury was
subsequently described as being to the neck. The witness hedges it twice, with
"it looked like" and "direct shot to the heart" as his own reading of red
spreading at the chest.

The second paragraph is the part this longer cut preserves and the others drop,
and it is the more useful of the two. He describes deliberately staying up when
the crowd went down, because he was looking for exactly the thing that would
have been visible if the shot had come from within the crowd:

* He expected that anyone standing near a shooter in the crowd would be visibly
  set upon — "trampling all over that person."
* He says he saw nothing of the kind.
* He describes the crowd going down together, staying still for a few moments,
  and then rising and moving, with the flow pushing him toward the stage.

That is a first-hand negative observation about the immediate area, and negative
observations are worth as much as positive ones when the question is where a
shot came from. It cuts against a crowd-level origin, and it cuts against the
"point-blank" framing of the headline the clip was published under. It says
nothing either way about elevated positions.

He is a witness giving his impression within hours of a killing, under the
ordinary distortions of shock. This site records the account as given, treats
the heart description as an impression rather than a medical finding, and does
not adopt the reposter's headline.
