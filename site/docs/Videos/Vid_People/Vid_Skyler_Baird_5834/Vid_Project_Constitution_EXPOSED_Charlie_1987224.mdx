## What Is Said

**The presenter's list.** The narration opens by naming Baird as someone "singled out as a possible co-conspirator" and stacks up the reasons that has happened: that a witness named Ryan Simmons was reportedly told by the FBI to delete his footage and kept a backup on an external drive; that search interest in Baird's name allegedly spiked from Tel Aviv, Haifa and Washington DC before 10 September and was later no longer visible in Google Trends; that Baird was in close proximity to security before and during the event and sat in the front row; that his Facebook history shows Coast Guard helicopter work in 2018 while he now describes himself as a digital content creator; that he obtained floor access to the memorial; that he gave more than seven media interviews saying much the same thing each time; and, in the presenter's words, "I'm not trying to be cynical, but I don't see any physical tears." The narration also notes that Baird helped bring police to George Zinn, the man who called out that he had done the shooting, and comments: "I guess Skyler just happened to be there to help apprehend the decoy. Coincidence, I suppose."

**Baird's answers.** The interview then works through them.

On why he was there and what he did: "I was a fan of Charlie, I still am." He says he was on a three-way call with friends who could not come and told them, in his own words, "I hope there's not a shooting." He describes standing in the media area because he was dressed for work and "looked somewhat professional," pulling out his camera and standing with the other camera operators — "just kind of like acting the part." He offers that phrase himself, unprompted; readers should notice that he uses it about looking the part of a photographer, and that a presenter could easily quote it to mean something else.

On the family connection to Phil Lyman: he describes the link as a stepfather's ex-wife of thirty years ago having remarried a man of that surname, and says of the researchers, "they're like, ha ha, we caught you, you're related to Phil" — adding that he once said hello at a funeral.

On the crisis-actor accusation directly: he argues the broadcasts were cut. "When news articles happen, they cut pieces out... if there was a moment that I'm like sobbing, not saying anything but just crying, they cut that out." He notes he had no editorial control over what aired.

On George Zinn: "If I'm in on it, and there's some sort of CIA, FBI, Mossad conspiracy that involves George Zinn, would I tell the officer, this isn't really the guy? This is just some crazy guy." The interviewer presses him — that if the goal was a distraction, it would not matter what he said — and he answers, "I guess."

On the search-trends claim, he gives the most concrete rebuttal in the interview. He says he tried to replicate the searches, initially found nothing, then examined the date range and search-type settings used in the screenshots and applied the same filters: "And guess what I find? Every state almost. Every country almost." His point is that the apparent Israel-and-DC signal is an artifact of the filter selection — "if you very specifically only search for District of Columbia and Israel, there's the connection, there it is."

On the phone and the Israeli radio station: he says a radio station from Israel called to interview him about his experience, asked at the end "what do people in your circles think about Israel, and do they think that Israel was involved," and then never followed up. He describes his phone freezing and behaving oddly around the same period and wonders aloud whether the two are connected. He also says a web page he had personally archived was afterwards gone.

On the wound and direction, in his own words: seeing blood on the left side of the neck, "I interpreted that basically a bullet was coming from Charlie's right side, from high up, coming down towards me where I was on the left of Charlie. My interpretation was that the bullet came from south to north." He then concedes the point against himself — "But then I see video footage of a guy laying on a roof, like, okay, maybe my interpretation of that event is wrong."

He states that law enforcement has not contacted him.

On the memorial floor access, he gives a step-by-step account: walking in beside a woman with a media badge who was waved through, reaching a table he assumed was for media and discovering it was a donor table, being told he was not on any list, and then asking a staffer outright for help because he had been at the event where Kirk was killed — after which, he says, a spare donor ticket was passed to him. He is emphatic about what it was not: "It wasn't a media badge. It wasn't the press badge. It wasn't the politician badge. It was just a floor badge where six thousand other TPUSA donors were."

On the photograph with the president, he attributes it to a contact of his mother's and a background check.

The interview ends with the question put flatly — that he would not admit it if he were an agent — and Baird agreeing that the accusation is unanswerable in principle: "It doesn't matter if I say I am or say I'm not."
