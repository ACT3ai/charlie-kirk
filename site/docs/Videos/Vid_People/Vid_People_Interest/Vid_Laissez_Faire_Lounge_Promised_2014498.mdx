## What This Video Is

A 1 minute 3 second vertical montage posted by the account Laissez Faire Lounge,
introduced as the delivery of a chart the poster had promised followers at
Christmas. It is not footage of anything. It is a graphics package: title cards,
social media screenshots, headshots, arrows, mock magazine spreads under the
banner "Inside D.C.", and a final flowchart the video itself labels "this is
called a closed loop".

There is no narration. The only voices in it belong to a broadcast news segment
embedded partway through, running under a low synth drone. Everything the video
argues, it argues through juxtaposition -- two faces, an arrow, a caption -- which
is the form that most needs a reader's guard up, because an arrow asserts a
relationship without stating what the relationship is supposed to prove.

## What Is Said

The only speech is the borrowed news interview, which runs from about 0:09. An
interviewer is questioning two witnesses who were near the stage. She asks the
question that matters: "did either of you actually see what happened? I know you
were only 15 feet away, and there was just this eruption in this chaos. Did
either of you actually see Charlie in that moment?"

The older of the two answers, and the answer is careful rather than dramatic: "I
had looked away right then, but my daughter saw it, and my nieces saw it." The
younger one is then asked directly whether she saw it, and she draws the same
distinction: "I haven't seen him get shot, but I saw it a minute later when they
posted the video online."

Read on its own, that exchange is two witnesses declining to claim more than they
have. Both were within about fifteen feet; both say they did not see the moment
itself. That is a useful data point about how little of the event was directly
observed even at close range, and it is the most substantive thing in the clip.
The montage uses the segment for a different purpose entirely.

## What The Footage Shows

An opening collage captioned "POLITICAL INTRIGUE UNCOVERED" and "THE CONNECTION
WE WEREN'T SUPPOSED TO FIND". Then a graphic pairing a screenshot of one of the
interviewed witnesses with a school-formal photograph of a young relative
standing next to the brother of another person connected to the case, flagged
"SCANDAL EXCLUSIVE" and zoomed into an Instagram post. Then the news segment
itself, split-screened against unrelated street footage. From 0:36 the video
traces arrows between further headshots of private individuals, several of them
young people whose only appearance is a social media photograph. From 0:42 a
sequence of mock magazine pages. At 0:51 a black-and-white flowchart of
relationships. From 0:54 collages of public figures and media producers over a
smoky background, and a closing card reading "ENJOY THE SHOW".

Now the gap between the argument and the frames. What the graphics establish is
that some people in a college town in Utah County know one another and have been
photographed together, which in a community of that size is the expected state of
affairs rather than a discovery. The video presents no wire, no document, no
message and no statement from any of the people it charts. It never says what it
alleges any of them did. It builds an impression out of proximity and leaves the
viewer to supply the accusation, which is precisely why this page will not supply
it. This site does not suggest that any person shown in this montage did anything
wrong, and it does not reproduce the names of the private individuals -- including
several who appear to be very young -- who are charted in it.
