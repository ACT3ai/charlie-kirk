The specific claim, in his words: "Six days after he was killed, I talked to two sources that were in the cabinet meeting who were scared to say it. They asked me to do it. And they said Kash Patel came in with Tulsi Gabbard, Joe Kent, the vice president, the president, a bunch of others, and said, we're not looking at foreign involvement, and shut that down, and we're not looking at any accomplices domestically." He draws his own conclusion from it: "so bare minimum, there is a cover-up of what really happened with Charlie Kirk."

He is careful to describe his own change of position rather than to claim consistency: "day one, people said Israel did it. I said I'm not going to say that day one.
