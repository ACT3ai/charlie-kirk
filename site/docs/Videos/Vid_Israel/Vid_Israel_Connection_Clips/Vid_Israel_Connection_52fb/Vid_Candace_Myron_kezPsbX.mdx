## What This Video Is

A two-minute-forty excerpt from a professionally produced studio podcast interview with Candace Owens. The filename and the content match her appearance on Myron Gaines's programme, the interview that multiple outlets subsequently reported on. The set is a library-style studio: a dark blue shelving unit with books, framed photos and a small British flag; Owens in a grey armchair at frame left in a dark maroon turtleneck; the male host at frame right in a black puffer vest, taking notes on a yellow legal pad. Two Shure SM7B microphones on boom arms. The edit cuts between medium close-ups of Owens and a wide two-shot.

This clip matters more than most in the archive for one reason: it is the primary source for the "they are going to kill me" text claim. Everything the press wrote about that text traces back to Owens saying it out loud, and this is her saying it.

## What Is Said

The host opens by asking who Owens thinks is "the most responsible" and why "they" would want Kirk gone.

Owens answers first with a characterisation of the constraint she says Kirk was under: "I think Charlie was not allowed to be anything but a Christian Zionist." She says he "was changing in the last few weeks," and that when she assembled a timeline she found "some weird stuff" — naming an Egyptian plane that she says began tracking him, and saying "everything began ratcheting up after Tucker Carlson hit the stage."

At roughly 0:50 she turns to what she frames as a pattern of denial after his death, and states her reasoning plainly:

> "Why were they lying about it? There's the thing. Why lie? You have nothing to cover up. That's my common sense here. You only lie because you have something to cover up."

She names two people in that context. She says that if Seth Dillon, "who pressed Charlie in the Hamptons," had simply said his biggest regret was arguing about Israel the last time he saw Kirk, she would have accepted it — "nothing to see here." Instead, she characterises what followed as "a full court press effort to lie about everything." She says of Josh Hammer that "he never blinked on Israel" and that "he was on the text message chain where Charlie said, 48 hours before he died, that he was done with the pro-Israel cause." Those are Owens's characterisations of both men and of their conduct; neither man's account is in this clip, and this page does not adopt her framing of either.

The central claim arrives at about 1:50:

> "I know for a fact, one of them, I'm not going to name, has a text message from Charlie, the day before he died, saying they are going to kill me."

The host reacts audibly. Owens adds: "Now, he was not referring to the trans lobby when he wrote that message." She then argues that a friend interested in the truth would have volunteered it publicly.

She closes with her own history: "Charlie told me years ago that he had, which I shared the messages. He was having a ton of visions and was telling me that Turning Point was going to be the death of him. He knew he was going to die young." She says he also told her she would be the only person to defend him, and finishes, "we're here now. And I'm going to defend him and I'm going to figure out what happened."

Two cautions belong on the record beside all of that. First, no screenshot of the alleged "they are going to kill me" text has been released publicly, the staffer is unnamed, and Turning Point USA — which has confirmed other leaked Kirk texts — has not publicly confirmed this one. Erika Kirk has stated on Glenn Beck's programme that no such messages exist and that she has his phone. The claim therefore stands as Owens's allegation, widely reported, not as an established fact. Second, everything Owens says here about Turning Point USA, Seth Dillon and Josh Hammer is her assertion in her own voice, and the site reports that she said it rather than endorsing any of it.
