## What Is Said

Peterson opens by establishing standing: "My name is Joshua Peterson. I worked for Turning Point USA... and I knew Charlie Kirk personally."

He then makes his central allegation, which he frames explicitly as something he wants "on the record":

> There was a inner circle at Turning Point USA that was constantly working against Charlie and his interests and what he wanted to do, as well as there was a Jewish influence at Turning Point that he was not very fond of, that he wanted to get rid of. He tried doing this. He tried creating a DOGE department. And he got assassinated.

That passage is the reason the clip is filed where it is, and it needs handling with care. Peterson is asserting a state of mind for a man who cannot confirm it, naming no individual, and placing an assassination immediately after a description of internal opposition — a sequencing that invites an inference he does not spell out and does not evidence. Reported as what it is, it is a former employee's characterisation of internal politics. It is not evidence that anyone at that organisation had any part in any crime, and this site does not suggest that they did.

The second half is a financial claim, and it is more specific:

> He had a whole entire branch of Turning Point that he had no idea about, and that was the stock market branch. Now, the thing about this branch is it's been getting millions of dollars poured into it over the past four years now, and that's been seen through their latest tax returns, which were also filed late.

He goes further: "If you look at the staff on that branch specifically, it says zero. It says there's nobody employed in that branch, yet they're pouring five million dollars into funds for employees. Make it make sense." He then offers his own reading of it — "That sounds like money laundering to me. It sounds like there's some pyramid scheme going on behind the scenes" — and closes with what he thinks it means: "I think that he was planning to expose that, and that's ultimately what got him killed."

Those last sentences are Peterson's speculation about a possible motive, stated as his belief. They are his words, not conclusions of this site. No finding by any regulator, auditor or court supports the money-laundering characterisation, and repeating his phrasing here is reporting what he said, not endorsing it. The underlying, checkable part of the claim — what the filings actually show about that fund, its staffing line and its filing dates — is a documents question, and it is pursued separately in the pages linked below rather than settled by a video.

He ends by pre-empting the obvious challenge: "A lot of people are asking, did you really work for Turning Point USA, and yes, I did. I'll tag some photos in the next clip."
