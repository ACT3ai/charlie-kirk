## What Is Said

Identical narration to the final cut: a single voice over prepared slides,
working through four strands - the national-security centre at UVU and the seven
associated profiles that came down about two weeks after the shooting; Google
search clusters originating in Israel in two windows, late 2024 and mid-2025;
the N1098L flight track; and a publicly posted crew list whose names partially
resemble names elsewhere in the material.

Three passages are worth isolating on this page because they are the load-bearing
factual claims and they are the same in both cuts.

The flight profile, given as public transponder data:

> "It then came down to 203 feet off the water and at 116 knots. That's stall
> speed - anything slower, the plane would fall out of the sky. That's uniquely
> slow for this kind of a plane."

The timing of the two low passes relative to the shooting - one about three hours
before, and one at the far end of the loop:

> "25 minutes after the assassination, it came back close to the university, it
> did a very strange dropdown in altitude."

And the narrator's own repeated limitation, which governs how any of it can be
reported:

> "We don't know if they dropped off drones or picked up drones, but this is
> interesting... None of this is implying that any of these people knew about the
> assassination or were anywhere involved."

The presentation ends, as the final cut does, in a set of questions put to the
FBI rather than in conclusions: publish the flight crew, publish whether drones
were over the venue, publish whether the university centre has an agency link.

the adjacent lake, the flight track drawn as a loop with the low passes circled,
and altitude and speed plots.
