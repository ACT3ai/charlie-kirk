## What Is Said

The presentation has four movements.

**One: the university centre.** The narrator raises the Center for National
Security Studies at UVU and asks whether it has any connection to the CIA. He
notes that seven individuals, referred to as A through G, had profiles
associated with it, and that the profiles came down roughly two weeks after
September 10. He does not assert why: "That doesn't imply any linkage to
anything, but that may have something interesting to do with NSCC."

**Two: the search clusters.** He describes Google search activity originating in
Israel for two of those names, occurring in what he identifies as two windows -
November and December 2024, and June through September 2025. His own caveat is
immediate: "that, of course, would not necessarily imply any guilt or any
involvement with them, but that's just an interesting piece of this."

**Three: the aircraft.** This is the substance of the presentation and the part
that rests on public records rather than inference. He identifies the flight as
**N1098L**, states the data came from public transponder records giving
altitude, speed, position and time, and walks the track on screen:

> "The plane flew from far away. And it did a big old loop... that loop brought
> them by the university twice, once three hours before the assassination."

The specific numbers are the point. He describes the aircraft cruising around
**500 knots at 41,000 feet**, then, at a lake roughly a mile or two from campus,
descending to:

> "203 feet off the water and at 116 knots. That's stall speed - anything
> slower, the plane would fall out of the sky. That's uniquely slow for this kind
> of a plane."

He states that this profile matches drone drop-off or recovery, that the loop
brought the aircraft back for a second low, slow pass roughly **25 minutes after
the shooting**, and that the shooting itself occurred at **12:23 Mountain Time**.
He is explicit about the limit: "We don't know if they dropped off drones or
picked up drones, but this is interesting."

He also addresses the counter-argument head on - that the aircraft type is a
surveillance platform and not a drone launcher - and does not claim to refute it
on aviation grounds. His response is to redirect to the search-timing question
instead.

**Four: the crew names.** He discusses a crew list someone posted publicly,
notes that two names on it resemble but do not match names in the other strands,
and treats the resemblance as a reason for verification rather than as a finding:
"Maybe they're brothers, maybe they're just completely different people."

The presentation closes on its own disclaimers, and they should be quoted rather
than paraphrased, because they define what this page can say:

> "None of this is implying that any of these people knew about the assassination
> or were anywhere involved, and it also does not imply that a drone shot any
> bullet."

And on the drones specifically:

> "We don't know if drones came from this plane or not. Maybe the plane didn't
> drop any drones off... This all was probably a coincidence. And so we are
> asking just the FBI to release information about anything involved with this
> flight."

The asks are concrete: publish whether the university centre has an agency
connection; publish who was on the flight; publish whether drones were present
over the venue and whether they were military.

## What The Footage Shows

Screen content only - there is no camera footage of any event in this video.
Over fourteen minutes the screen carries: a connection diagram linking the four
nodes of the thesis; a set of Google Trends charts marked with the two claimed
planning windows; a map with the campus marked as a yellow rectangle and the
nearby lake beside it; the flight track drawn as a loop with the two low passes
circled; and altitude and speed plots.

The distinction that matters for a reader weighing this is between the parts
backed by public records and the parts that are pattern reading:

* **Records-backed.** The aircraft's registration, its track, and its altitude
  and speed at each point are public transponder data. Anyone can pull the same
  track. The 12:23 shooting time is established. Those elements are checkable
  and this presentation reproduces them clearly.
* **Interpretation.** That a 203-foot, 116-knot pass is characteristic of drone
  operations rather than of other low-level activity is the narrator's reading,
  and the presentation itself acknowledges an alternative explanation exists.
* **Inference.** The search-timing clusters and the name similarities are
  circumstantial patterns. The narration says so, in those words, more than once.

Presented honestly, this is a **request for disclosure built on a public flight
track**, not a demonstration that anything was launched. That is how the video
frames itself and how this page records it. No person mentioned in it is
asserted here to have known anything or done anything.
