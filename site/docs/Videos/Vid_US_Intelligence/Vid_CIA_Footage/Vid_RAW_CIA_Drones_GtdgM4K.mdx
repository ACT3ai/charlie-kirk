## What The Footage Shows

A white slide headed with a red banner reading "Charlie Kirk Assassination",
laid out as four boxes joined by arrows.

* **Top left - the university centre.** Headed with the question of whether the
  CIA is linked to the Center for National Security Studies at UVU, carrying that
  centre's logo. It lists seven individuals, labelled A through G, colour-coded
  into two groups.
* **Bottom left - the search activity.** Titled "Israel Google Searches", stating
  that two of the seven names were searched from Israel before September 10.
* **Top right - the event.** The date and time, "Sept 10th, 2025" and "12:23 MT",
  with a note on the flight's altitude behaviour.
* **Bottom right - the aircraft.** Headed with the tail number **N1098L** and the
  **HADES** programme, listing three names given as the flight crew.

Between the boxes, the slide carries its own written caveats. Two are legible in
the graphic and they are the most important text on it:

> "This doesn't mean these people did the assassination..."

> "These 4 may have NO connection to each other."

Those annotations are the author's, not this page's, and they govern how the
slide can be read. **This page names no individual from the diagram, asserts no
connection between the four boxes, and makes no claim that any person listed on
it knew of or took part in anything.** Two of the individuals shown have profiles
on this site - [Elena Vasquez](/People/elena-vasquez) and
[Sarah Klein](/People/sarah-klein) - and those pages, not this one, are where any
question about them belongs.

What the slide is useful for is structural. It makes explicit something that a
fourteen-minute narration leaves implicit: the argument is **four separate
strands set side by side**, and the diagram's own arrows are proposed
relationships, not demonstrated ones. Only one of the four boxes rests on
records anyone can pull independently - the aircraft's registration, track,
altitude and speed are public transponder data, and the 12:23 Mountain Time
shooting is established. The other three boxes are pattern observations and
open questions.

Seen that way the slide is a fair summary of an unresolved line of enquiry and a
poor summary of anything settled, which is presumably why its author wrote the
disclaimers directly onto it. Anyone circulating the image without those
annotations attached would be misrepresenting it.
