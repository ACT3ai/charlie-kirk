## What This Video Is

A 59-second phone capture from an elevated position overlooking the Utah Valley University courtyard on September 10, 2025, taken by someone who was already filming when the shot was fired. It is a single continuous hand-held take with no cuts, no titles and no watermark, held as an anonymous capture in the investigation's archive.

Its value is specific and hard to overstate for a clip this short: it starts before the shot and keeps running. Twelve seconds of an ordinary crowd, then the report, then forty-six seconds of the crowd dispersing, all from a fixed vantage high enough to see the whole plaza at once. Most bystander footage from that day begins after the shot. This one does not.

## What Is Said

The camera operator and at least one person near them are talking, casually at first. The recoverable audio:

> "Charlie Kirk brings a pretty good audience."

Then, after the bang, in sequence:

> "There was some shots." — "Really?" — "There was just some shooting going on." — "Somebody had just shot near... at Utah Valley State." — "One shot."

The transcript renders the name imperfectly at the open and mangles one phrase in the middle, but the shape of the exchange is unambiguous. It is two people watching a crowd, remarking on the turnout, and then working out in real time what they have just heard.

Three things in that short exchange are worth pulling out. The opening line establishes that the operator knew whose event this was and was filming the audience for its size, not because they expected anything. The uncertainty in "there was some shots" followed by "really?" is the sound of people who do not yet know what happened — this is not a capture by someone anticipating an event. And the final phrase, "one shot," is the operator's own contemporaneous count, spoken within a minute, from a position with clear line of sight and no obstruction.

That last point matters because the number of reports is a live question in this investigation. This clip does not settle it. What it provides is one earwitness account, recorded on the spot rather than recalled later, and audio of the event itself on an unedited track that can be examined independently of anyone's recollection.

## What The Footage Shows

* **00:00 - 00:12** — An elevated, slow left-to-right pan across the plaza. A large crowd of young adults packed onto the central grassy lawn and the surrounding concrete walkways. A grey modern building at frame left, a concrete pedestrian overpass with railings at frame right, academic buildings and the Wasatch range on the skyline. A canopy tent stands in the middle of the lawn. Nothing is out of the ordinary.
* **00:12 - 00:13** — A sharp report. The camera jerks slightly. The crowd begins to move.
* **00:13 - 00:39** — The camera pans left and right, following the crowd as it screams and runs for the building entrances and walkways.
* **00:39 - 00:41** — A pan to the concrete walkway at right, showing people fleeing down the stairs.
* **00:41 - 00:59** — Back to the central courtyard, the remaining crowd moving away, while the operator and companion talk through what happened.

Two limits are worth stating. The framing is wide and the vantage is high, so the stage area is small in frame and no detail of the platform, the table or the people around it is resolvable. And the pan is following the crowd rather than searching for a source, so the footage does not cover the rooftops or the surrounding buildings in any useful way.

What it does give, uniquely among the short captures in this cluster, is the direction and shape of the crowd's dispersal from a single fixed viewpoint, on a continuous take — which is what a reconstruction of movement out of the plaza needs.
