## What This Video Is

Sixteen minutes of continuous vertical phone footage from inside the audience at the American Comeback Tour stop at Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025. It is vlog-style social coverage, not journalism: whip pans, shaky hand-held movement, long stretches where the operator's view is blocked by the person in front of them.

Two things about its provenance need saying, because the filename is misleading.

First, the file is named for Ryan Matta, an X account that covers this investigation, but the video is not his. It ends on a standard TikTok end card crediting the creator handle **@caseyjpbryson**. This is a repost of somebody else's TikTok capture, and the archive filename records who it came through rather than who shot it.

Second, and more important: **this footage ends before the shooting.** Sixteen minutes of it are the event running normally. The final pan at 15:23 shows the crowd cheering, and then the TikTok card. Nothing in this file shows the shot, the reaction, or the aftermath, despite its being clustered with the raw shooting footage.

That does not make it useless — the opposite. It is one of the longest continuous looks at the venue, the crowd density, the stage layout and the security posture in the period immediately before the shot, from a position inside the crowd.

## What Is Said

The audio is a Q&A, and the machine transcript of it is badly degraded — the recording is an open-air phone microphone in a cheering crowd, and the engine returns long stretches of word salad. It should not be quoted as a record of anyone's words. What is recoverable is the subject matter, and the subject matter is clear: Kirk is in an extended back-and-forth with a questioner about the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

Fragments that survive intelligibly include the questioner or Kirk saying "the truth is... is more historically accurate," Kirk saying "I love Mormons" and "polite discussion," an exchange about "no more of this LGBT stuff," a reference to "the golden" plates and to Joseph Smith, and Kirk saying "Let me finish, not interrupting" and "there's no evidence for either." The exchange is combative but framed by both sides as a debate.

Nothing in the recoverable audio touches the shooting, security, threats, or any of the investigation's other subjects. Anyone needing the actual wording of this exchange should go to a cleaner source; this transcript cannot carry a quotation.
