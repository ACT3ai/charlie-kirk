## What This Video Is

A 38 second clip, and it is important to be precise about what layer of it you
are watching. It is a **handheld phone recording of a television screen**. The
screen is showing **Fox News** - the "HANNITY" and "BREAKING NEWS" banners are
visible along the bottom, with the chyron "AUTHORITIES SHARE NEW VIDEO OF PERSON
OF INTEREST". Fox is in turn playing **security-camera footage released by
investigators** on September 10, 2025.

So there are three generations between the original camera and this file:
security camera, broadcast, phone-of-a-TV. Resolution, colour and timing are all
degraded accordingly, and nothing about the image quality here should be read as
telling you anything about the quality of the original release.

The clip is held in the investigation's own capture archive with no source post
recorded.

## What Is Said

The audio is a law-enforcement briefing being narrated over the footage on air.
The speaker walks the viewer through the released video and, more usefully,
through what investigators say they recovered from the scene:

> "As he does, he's gonna climb off the edge, and then drop down to the ground.
> As he did that, he left some palm impressions. There's some smudges, some
> places we're looking to collect DNA."

He then describes the footwear evidence:

> "There's a shoe imprint where we believe a suspect is clearly identified as
> wearing Converse tennis shoes. Again, we'll replay that again, so you can get
> another view of that."

And the clothing description, which is the part that was widely circulated that
afternoon:

> "It's important to note his black t-shirt, his black pants, and there appears
> to be some white on the soles of those Converse tennis shoes. Those are all
> identifiable items that we're looking for."

Three separable claims are being made there, and they are worth keeping apart:
that palm impressions and smudges were left and were being collected for DNA;
that a shoe impression consistent with Converse-style soles was found; and that
the person in the video wore a black t-shirt and black trousers. The
Converse-sole detail matters because the investigation's own record notes that
when Tyler Robinson later presented at the Washington County Sheriff's Office he
was described in a government document as wearing white or grey Converse-style
shoes - while also noting he was **not** then wearing clothing consistent with
the surveillance imagery.

What can and cannot be taken from these frames:

* The figure is **unidentifiable**. Dark clothing, distance, a security-camera
  sensor and two generations of re-recording leave no facial detail, no build
  detail beyond gross outline, and nothing that could confirm or exclude any
  individual. The broadcast itself uses the phrase "person of interest", not a
  name, and this page does the same.
* The descent is **short and controlled** rather than a jump - the figure climbs
  down from a lower roof edge before dropping - which is consistent with the
  narrator's account of palm impressions being left on the way down.
* A security-feed timestamp is visible on screen. It is rendered in the

  shot at 12:23:30 p.m. Mountain Time, so this is most likely the leading digit

  put the descent about nineteen seconds after the shot. That reading is
  plausible but it is not established from this copy, and anyone relying on the
  timestamp should go to the original released file rather than to this phone
  capture of a television.
