## What This Video Is

A 37 second vertical smartphone capture taken from the **side** of the tent at
Utah Valley University. No poster and no source URL are recorded; it sits in the
investigation's own capture archive.

It is the most complete short clip in this cluster for one specific reason: it
does not stop at the shot. It carries straight through the security response and
the extraction, ending with the vehicle. Most of the same-moment captures lose
the subject within a second or two. This one follows.

## What Is Said

Very little, and none of it from the stage after the first few seconds.

The clip opens on the tail of the gun-statistics exchange, garbled by distance
and crowd noise but recognisably the same question that closes the Q&A in the
other angles:

> "Let me give you credit. Do you know how many mass shooters there have been in
> America over the last ten years? Counting or not counting gang violence?"

The answer, "Great", is the last ordinary word.

Everything after the report is shouted instruction and reaction near the camera:

> "Go, go, go, go!" - "Get out of - everybody!" - "Stay down."

Those are crowd and security voices, not narration, and there is no commentary
track. Nobody in this clip explains what has happened.

## What The Footage Shows

* **00:00 - 00:05** - Handheld, panning from the crowd to a student at a
  microphone. Metal barricades in the foreground; the crowd is behind them.
* **00:05 - 00:08** - Pan onto the tent. The speaker is seated at the table
  under the "PROVE ME WRONG" canopy, branded "THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR".
* **00:08 - 00:10** - A single loud bang off-camera. Security personnel move
  immediately and take the speaker down to the grass, shielding him.
* **00:10 - 00:19** - Chaotic handheld footage: guards over him on the ground,
  screaming from the crowd, the camera swinging.
* **00:19 - 00:26** - The camera pans across the scattering crowd while security
  clear a path.
* **00:26 - 00:36** - A tracking shot following the security team as they move
  quickly through a concrete underpass and into a **black Chrysler Pacifica
  minivan** in the adjacent parking structure, with bystanders filming the
  departure.

The evidentiary content of this clip sits in the last twenty seconds, and it is
substantial:

* **Response time.** The interval between the report and security making
  physical contact is very short - on the order of a second or two on this
  timeline. Whatever else is argued about the security arrangements, this
  footage documents an immediate close-protection reaction.
* **The route.** It shows the path taken out - across the grass, through the
  concrete underpass, into a vehicle staged in the adjacent structure. That
  route and that vehicle are the beginning of the transport sequence the
  investigation tracks separately, and having it on continuous video from a
  fixed observer is more useful than any still.
* **The vehicle.** A black Chrysler Pacifica minivan, not an ambulance. The
  decision to move by private vehicle rather than wait for EMS is a documented
  fact of the response and is visible here rather than inferred.
* **What it does not show.** The wound is not visible; the roof is not in frame;
  the shooter is not in frame. The camera is at crowd level on the side of the
  tent and stays there.

One further note on reading this clip: the "one report" observation holds here
too. The audio contains a single bang at 00:08, not a sequence, matching the
[north-side capture](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Shooting_Footage/Vid_Raw_Shooting_4e5e/Vid_Full_Video_North_rNPnApF)
and the long
[plaza livestream](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Shooting_Footage/Vid_Raw_Shooting_4e5e/Vid_FqnNxEdIKfPXgAlj_CCtKidN).
Three independent cameras at three vantage points recording one report is a
stronger statement than any one of them alone.
