## What This Video Is

A 39 second clip shot from the **north** side of the Utah Valley University
plaza, looking toward the "PROVE ME WRONG" tent. It carries an **"ISRAEL LIVE
NEWS"** logo in the upper-left corner, so this copy has passed through an outlet
graphic before reaching the archive; the underlying capture is bystander phone
video, not broadcast footage.

Its value is its coverage. In 39 seconds it contains the last exchange of the
Q&A, the shot, and roughly ten seconds of immediate crowd reaction, all in one
unbroken take from a vantage far enough back to show the whole hillside.

## What Is Said

The exchange running as the clip opens is the gun-statistics one, and this
recording catches more of it than most copies do. A questioner and Kirk are
mid-argument about numbers:

> "Do you know how many transgender Americans have been mass shooters over the
> last 10 years?" - "Too many." - "Okay, now five is a lot, right?"

Kirk allows the point - "I'm gonna give you some credit" - and puts the
comparison question:

> "Do you know how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last
> ten years? Counting or not counting gang violence?"

The questioner answers, "Great."

Then the audio changes completely. What follows on the transcript is not
conversation but instruction and reaction, from voices near the camera rather
than from the stage:

> "Down, down, get down." - "What the fuck?" - "Go!"

That is the whole of the speech in the clip. Nobody near this camera says what
has happened; they only react.

The proximity of the last question to the shot is noted here because it recurs
across the witness material - several bystanders in the longer
[plaza livestream](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Shooting_Footage/Vid_Raw_Shooting_4e5e/Vid_FqnNxEdIKfPXgAlj_CCtKidN)
raise it unprompted within minutes. Recording that people found the timing
striking is not the same as asserting it was meaningful, and this page does not
assert that.

## What The Footage Shows

* **00:00 - 00:07** - Medium shot of Kirk and the student questioner under the
  white canopy branded "AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG". The camera
  pans slowly right. Speakers, metal crowd-control barriers and microphones are
  visible around the tent.
* **00:07 - 00:25** - The shot zooms out to a wide, revealing the full scale of
  the crowd seated and standing on the grassy slope facing the stage. The
  exchange continues.
* **00:25 - 00:29** - A single loud report. The crowd reacts instantly - people
  drop flat to the ground or turn and run. The camera swings.
* **00:29 - 00:39** - Violently shaky handheld panning across the slope: people
  lying on the grass, others fleeing, no coherent framing.

Four observations that this angle supports better than the closer clips do:

1. **One report, not several.** The audio in this clip contains a single loud
   bang, not a burst. That is consistent across the other same-moment captures
   in this cluster.
2. **The crowd's reaction is essentially instantaneous and universal.** From a
   wide vantage you can see the whole slope go down at once rather than a
   ripple spreading outward, which is what you would expect from a sharp report
   heard directly rather than a rumour propagating.
3. **The venue's exposure is legible from here.** The wide frame shows how open
   the slope is and how much elevated ground and how many building faces
   overlook it - the physical situation behind the security questions the site
   tracks separately.
4. **It shows nothing of the injury.** At this range and with this camera
   movement, nothing about the wound, the direction, or the stage is resolvable
   after the shot. This clip is evidence about the crowd and the sound, not
   about the target.
