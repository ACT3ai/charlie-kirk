## What This Video Is

A 1 minute 2 second clip posted by the account Alley Files, introduced as
covering the span from the shot to the moment Charlie Kirk was carried out, with
"stay down" in the caption. It is compiled from two sources: a handheld camera
near the stage for the first forty seconds, then a hard cut at about 0:41 into a
static high wide shot looking down over the whole plaza. The second angle is the
reason to watch it. Almost everything that circulates from that afternoon is
close and chaotic; this one pulls back far enough to show the crowd as a system.

## What Is Said

There is barely any speech in it and what there is comes from the stage before
the bang. The transcription recovers a single fragment of the exchange in
progress -- "counting or not counting gang violence" and the reply "great" -- which
places the clip precisely in the last seconds of the question Kirk was taking.
The same fragment appears in several other captures of this moment and is a
useful anchor for lining recordings up against each other.

After that there is no intelligible speech at all. The audio is a sharp report at
about 0:02 followed by screams, gasps and a rising wall of crowd noise. Nobody in
the clip narrates, explains or claims anything.

## What The Footage Shows

The opening frames are the stage under the canopy: the branded banners, a man in
a white T-shirt seated behind a blue-draped table with microphones and laptops on
it, and terraced seating packed with people facing him.

At 0:02 the report lands. The response is immediate and it is worth being precise
about, because it is easy to misread and one automated description of this file
misread it badly. What happens is a protective reaction by staff: a man in a grey
polo and sunglasses comes up onto the platform, another moves in and takes the
seated man down behind the table, and several people drop into cover on the same
side. People on the grass to the left go flat. Others start to run. This site
does not describe that as an assault on the stage, because it is not one; it is
the close protection response, and the same movement is discussed openly as such
in other footage from the day.

From 0:03 to 0:41 the handheld camera swings between the stage and the crowd,
catching people diving for cover, a perimeter forming, and then some of the
audience standing back up.

The cut at 0:41 is the substantive part. From an elevated position -- a balcony or
similar -- the whole plaza is visible at once, with the university building across
the top of the frame and the terraced grass and concrete filling the rest.
Hundreds of people are running outward from the centre in every direction. Some
fall on the grass and on the concrete steps. Some crouch and stay down. Within
roughly twenty seconds the central plaza is essentially empty, with belongings
left where people had been sitting.

What that wide shot gives an investigator is a measurable record of the dispersal
-- direction, speed, which exits filled, how long the whole thing took. What it
does not give is the event itself: no shooter, no rooftop, no trajectory, nothing
about the injury. The caption's promise of "the shot to them carrying CK out"
overshoots what is actually in frame; the carry-out itself is not visible here.
