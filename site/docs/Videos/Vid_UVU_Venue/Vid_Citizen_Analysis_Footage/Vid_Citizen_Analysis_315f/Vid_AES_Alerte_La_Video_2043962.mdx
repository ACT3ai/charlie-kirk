## What This Video Is

A 2 minute 43 second repost by the francophone account AES Alerte, whose caption
describes it as the original UVU footage, unmodified, released in full. That
framing is a claim about provenance, not something the file itself can prove, and
it should be read as the poster's assertion. What can be said is that the clip is
longer than most circulating versions and that it runs continuously up to and

The value here is the ninety seconds before anything happens. Most reposts begin

## What Is Said

The clip opens mid-answer on an unrelated point about a first-century historian,
with Kirk correcting a characterisation -- that the writer in question was a Roman
Jewish scholar rather than a Christian one -- and then closing that questioner out
with a blessing and moving on.

The next questioner steps up and the exchange that follows is the last one. He
introduces his subject: a mass shooting some weeks earlier in which the shooter
was reported to be transgender, and a subsequent statement from the Justice
Department about firearms rights for transgender Americans. He then asks his
first question: "do you know how many transgender Americans have been mass
shooters over the last 10 years?"

Kirk answers, "too many," and when the questioner supplies a figure of five, Kirk
does not dispute it -- "now, five is a lot, right? I'm gonna give you some
credit."

The questioner then asks the follow-up that is cut off: "do you know how many
mass shooters there have been in America over the last 10 years?" and immediately
qualifies it -- "counting or not counting gang violence?" Kirk's reply is a single
word, "great," and that is where the recording stops being a debate.

That fragment -- the qualifier and the one-word answer -- recurs across many
independent captures from the plaza and is the single most reliable
synchronisation point available for lining recordings up against one another.
Anyone comparing angles or audio should anchor on it.

## What The Footage Shows

stage: the branded canopy, the table, Kirk seated with a microphone, a student in
a blue shirt standing at a microphone in front of the platform, and a dense crowd

At about 1:42 the report. Protection staff move onto the platform immediately,

balcony showing the entire plaza, with the university building along the back,

The frames do not show a shooter, a rooftop, a muzzle flash or a trajectory. The
camera is on the stage and then on the crowd. What this recording adds to the
