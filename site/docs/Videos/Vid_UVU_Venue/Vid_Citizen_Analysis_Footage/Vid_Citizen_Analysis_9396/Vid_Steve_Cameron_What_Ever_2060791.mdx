## What This Video Is

Seven minutes thirty-three seconds. It is a studio segment from the **Candace Owens** show, clipped and reposted on X by [Steve Cameron](/People/steve-cameron), whose account posts as [@stevecameronpr1](/Influencers/x/stevecameronpr1). His framing question is in the post text: "What ever became of this story of a Ring camera capturing footage of..."

The artefact is therefore a repost of a broadcast, not original footage of anything. Everything evidentiary inside it is second-hand: a tip Owens says she received, and a clip Owens says she downloaded before it was taken offline. Nothing in the segment is raw doorbell footage in this site's possession.

## What Is Said

Owens speaks throughout. Two other voices appear inside the material she plays.

**The tip.** Owens says a lead came in live on the show, from a woman who "paid money to inform us that there was footage that the FBI was hiding." According to Owens, the tipster wrote: "I have been trying to reach out to you everywhere. The FBI is hiding footage. The doorbell cam footage had more." Owens says the tipster told her the person in the footage "is then seen changing his clothes, and he is with a woman," and then supplied a name — which Owens says belonged to another member of the Lyman family, at which point, in her words, "my Spidey senses started tingling. I'm like, okay, this is a lot of Lymans."

**Owens's own caution.** She states plainly that she would not assert the woman's existence without corroboration: "I didn't want to come out and say that it was a fact... that there was a woman with the shooter, without any proof. We had no proof outside of this person."

**Her framing of Phil Lyman.** Owens is explicit and, on this point, exculpatory. "I want to be very clear and fair to [Phil Lyman](/Influencers/x/phil-lyman)," she says. "He has been forthcoming about the fact that his family owned that property. Like they own everything there. That is not mysterious. He's not hiding that fact. UVU, in many ways, purchased the university from his family — the acreage from his family for that university." Her point is only that family ownership might, in theory, give him a route to ask relatives for footage.

**The recovered clip (about 2:26 in).** Owens plays material she says was posted by a citizen researcher she calls "Shaner," who had been retracing the charged man's route and filming at a relative's house, and which she says has since been wiped from the internet. On it, an older woman — described in the segment as the charged man's aunt, and not named here — is heard saying that the timing did not add up: "If you time how long it took him to get from that little part over to here, was way longer than it should have been. So I wondered if the girl had given him stuff. Like, if there is an accomplice." Someone off camera tells her she is being filmed and she answers, "Sorry, I didn't realise you were filming." Owens replays it and corrects the burned-in captions on screen, noting the automatic subtitles render the line wrongly and that the woman is saying "I wondered if there is an accomplice."

**The questions she puts.** Owens asks who the woman in the footage was, why the public was not told during the 33-hour search, and why doorbell footage was already with TMZ by the morning of September 11 — which she argues means investigators had it too. She says she contacted Phil Lyman to ask whether he would help obtain the raw footage from relatives, and gives her account of his response: that he was angry, said "really not nice things," and asked whether she had something to do with the shooting. That is her characterisation of a private exchange; his own account of it is not in this clip, and this site does not adopt hers.

## What The Footage Shows

Owens at a studio desk with a laptop and microphone, cutting between a wide framing and a closer angled one roughly every twenty to thirty seconds. Twice she goes to a picture-in-picture layout:

- **00:13 - 00:23** — herself on the left, and on the right a residential surveillance view of a paved driveway and street with a green tracking box following a walking figure.
- **02:26 - 02:46 and again shortly after** — herself on the left, and on the right handheld phone footage climbing a dirt path up a dry grassy hillside toward trees and a house, with white automatic subtitles over the conversation described above.

What is verifiable from the frames is narrow: a residential camera view with a tracking overlay, and a hillside walk-and-talk with a relative. The claims about what a longer, unreleased version of the doorbell footage contains rest entirely on testimony — an anonymous tipster's, and a relative's stated impression that the timing seemed too long. A relative wondering aloud whether someone had help is a real, on-camera statement worth recording; it is not the same as footage of a second person, and this page does not treat it as such.
