## What This Video Is

Four minutes twenty seconds, posted to X by [Sam Parker](/Influencers/x/based-sam-parker) under the heading "COMPLETE CHARLIE KIRK EVACUATION TIMELINE," tagging two other investigators. It is a narrated walkthrough: Parker plays event audio, then a recorded first-person account of the seconds after the shot, then pauses on frames and puts a timing question to his audience. It ends with him explicitly asking for help rather than asserting a conclusion.

**Read the whole of this page in the posture the question deserves.** [Blake Neff](/People/blake-neff), [Mikey McCoy](/People/mikey-mccoy), Erika Kirk and Rob McCoy are living people. None has been charged with anything, and nothing here alleges that anyone did anything wrong. People misremember the worst ninety seconds of their lives, and a gap between a recollection and a video frame is the most ordinary thing in any investigation.

## What Is Said

**The event audio (00:00 - 00:15).** The clip opens on the tail of the answer being given from the stage — the automatic transcript garbles it, but the phrase is the one recorded elsewhere in this investigation, about gun deaths "not counting gang violence." Then the crack, and the crowd: "Oh my God. Oh my God." Voices calling "get down."

**Parker's framing question.** Over the frames that follow he asks: "Where's Blake Neff? Allegedly, standing next to Mikey McCoy making phone calls? I don't see him."

**The first-person account (about 00:30 - 02:20).** Parker then plays a recorded account of the seconds after the shot, given in the first person:

> "The shooting occurs. I am next to Mikey. Both of us hear a loud crack. We both turn, we both see Charlie, who has been shot. So I and him both had the reaction of, let's get out of here before we were both shot."

> "I remember even running past the SUV that we came in on and me thinking, should I get in that car — and then thinking, no, that's a really stupid thing to do. And then I kept going. So I was not with Mikey, or I don't remember being with him. I think I was ahead of him as we left."

> "I run for — can't have been more than fifteen or twenty seconds. And then I realise there hasn't been another shot, so the shooter has likely been detained or stopped or something, not in immediate danger. I pause, I look around me, and I see Mikey."

> "It was clear in the moment that he was profoundly freaked out. What I'll always remember is the way his lip was quivering, which I'd never seen before... And then he says, I need to call Erika. And then he takes his phone and he begins calling Erika. And I don't want to disclose how that call unfolded."

The account continues: the speaker says he called his own mother — "mom, there's been a shooting, you're going to see it on the news, I'm okay, pray for me" — and that as he put the phone away, McCoy immediately called his father: "Dad, someone's shot Charlie, you need to call all of your pastor friends. Charlie was hit. We need everyone to pray right now."

**Parker's question (02:20 - 04:20).** Parker then lays out the arithmetic as he understands it. Police place the shot at 12:23:30 p.m. Utah time. At the frame where McCoy is visible alone, past the SUVs, Parker puts the clock at about 12:24 Utah time, 11:24 Arizona time — and says Neff is not with him in that frame. Yet the reported time of the call to Erika Kirk is 11:23 Arizona time, which is 12:23 Utah. "So he's apparently already on the phone with Erika Kirk. Well, where's Blake Neff? How does Blake Neff know who Mikey's calling? He's not anywhere near Mikey right now."

He closes without a claim: "Maybe it's just the fog of war. I don't know, but something's not adding up with this timeline... And I don't know what it means. But what do you think might be going on? Help me out with the timeline."

## What The Footage Shows

Event footage of the seconds after the shot, replayed and paused, intercut with Parker's narration and with the recorded account above. The visual claim rests on a single frame from a single camera in which McCoy is past the SUVs and Neff is not in shot.

That is worth stating precisely, because it is the whole of the visual evidence. **A frame establishes who is in that frame and nothing else.** It does not establish who was ten feet outside it, who was behind the lens, who had been there four seconds earlier or who arrived four seconds later. In a scene where people are running in every direction, "nowhere in frame" is a fact about the frame.

Three further softnesses apply to the arithmetic. "12:23" and "11:23" are rounded minutes — how a person says *around then*, not a timestamp to the second. Phone-log time, livestream overlay time and campus camera time drift relative to one another, and no one has established the offsets needed to compare them. And "witnessed," in ordinary speech, routinely means *was in the same scrum* or *knew he was on the phone*, not *stood beside him and watched him dial*.

What the clip is genuinely good for is that it assembles, in one place and in order, the audio of the moment, a detailed contemporaneous-style account of the next ninety seconds, and the frame the timing question turns on. That is useful primary organisation, and the question it raises is a fair one to ask of a public record. It is not an accusation, and this page does not turn it into one. The full treatment, including what would actually settle it — carrier records obtained lawfully, an established clock offset, and additional camera angles covering 12:23:30 to 12:26 — is on the dedicated page below.
