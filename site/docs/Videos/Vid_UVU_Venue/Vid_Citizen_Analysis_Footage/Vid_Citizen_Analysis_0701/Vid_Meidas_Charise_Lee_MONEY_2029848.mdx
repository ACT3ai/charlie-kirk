## What This Video Is

A 2 minute 49 second selfie monologue, one static take, filmed indoors in a
domestic room. It carries a TikTok watermark and username throughout and ends on
a TikTok end card, so the copy circulating on X under a "MONEY TRAIL" headline is
a repost of a TikTok upload rather than an original X post. There is a low

That matters more than usual here, because the clip's whole persuasive force

## What Is Said

The speaker opens with a claim of original research: he says he has "just spent
the last 48 hours digging through" a February 2026 Epstein ledger and has found
"the exact money trail" connecting a 1.3 billion dollar JPMorgan matter to the
killing of Charlie Kirk. He states directly, "This is not a theory."

From there he lays out a sequence. He says that on September 2, 2025, eight days
before the shooting, Kirk issued an internal memo opening a forensic audit of two
for-profit entities he names as GSM Strategy and Lionrock Ventures, which he
characterises as vehicles TPUSA used to move consulting fees, and that the audit
was aimed at identifying ultimate beneficial owners. "That's when the clock
started ticking," he says.

He then describes a 350,000 dollar "logistics support wire" initiated on
September 8 through a European bank, asserts it funded a ground team in Provo,
and links it to two Egyptian aircraft, saying one of them powered on its
transponder "exactly 17 minutes after the shot was fired." He claims the rental
cars used by that team bill back to Delaware shell companies that were themselves
under audit. He then asserts that a recent unredaction identified individuals he
names, and that two of them designed a security arrangement that replaced Kirk's
regular protection with a third-party team 48 hours before he died. He closes on
his thesis: that Kirk "wasn't killed for his politics" but because the audit was
about to pierce a Delaware shell wall.

The last thirty seconds break the frame entirely. He asks viewers for help
editing and distributing the video, says he is not good at video editing, offers
a religious sign-off and tells the audience to stay safe.

This site reports that the clip contains those assertions. It does not adopt any
of them, and it does not reproduce the names of the living people the speaker
accuses. Several of the load-bearing details do not survive contact with the rest
of the record: the ledger release he cites as his source is dated after the
events he uses it to explain, the corporate names do not match the entities
actually documented in this investigation, and no memo, wire record or unredacted
filing is shown or sourced anywhere in the clip. Treated as evidence it is
unusable. Treated as an artifact it is a good example of a genre that has grown
up around this case: fluent, dated, numbered, and entirely unsupported.
