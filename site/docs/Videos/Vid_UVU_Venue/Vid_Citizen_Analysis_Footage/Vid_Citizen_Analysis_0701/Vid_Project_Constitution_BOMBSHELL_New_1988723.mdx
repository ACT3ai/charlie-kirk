## What This Video Is

A 37 second first-person clip shot on the Utah Valley University campus, posted
by the account Project Constitution. It is an informal walk-and-point interview,
not a sit-down: an unseen interviewer holds an iPhone in the foreground while a

It is a single continuous handheld take with no cuts, shot into low winter sun
with the bare trees, the glass campus buildings and the snow-dusted Wasatch

## What Is Said

The interviewer opens by asking the witness to set aside received assumptions
about position: "Ignore where you think he was." The witness locates himself
against a fixed landmark instead of a description, which is the most useful thing
in the clip. Referring to a small statue in the plaza, he says: "See where like
that little statue... I was like right in front of that."

The interviewer then checks orientation twice, physically demonstrating it, and
the witness confirms both times. "So are you facing him like this?" -- "Yep,
exactly like that." -- "So you're facing, you're facing him like this?" -- "Yes,
sir."

The substance is what happens next. The witness says, "and then so you hear the...
then I heard the thing. I looked directly behind me because I'm like, what the
heck?" The interviewer presses on direction: "Wait, wait, which way did you
look?" The witness answers by showing it -- "I looked, I looked, so I stand
there. I went like that really quick" -- and the interviewer reads it back: "So
it sounded like it was behind you."

He then names the place he thought it came from and why he thought so: "So I
thought maybe it came up from like up on this little terrace right here. There
were tons of people on that terrace. So I was like, who the heck is like lighting
fireworks or something?"

Two things in that are worth holding separately. First, an immediate,
pre-reflection perceptual reaction: he turned away from the stage, toward the
terrace behind him. Second, an interpretation formed in the same second:
fireworks. Neither is a finding about where a shot originated. Acoustic direction
judgment in a hard-surfaced plaza is unreliable, and the witness himself is
describing a guess, not a sighting. What the clip does establish is a specific,
locatable account from a named position that can be checked against the plaza's
layout.
