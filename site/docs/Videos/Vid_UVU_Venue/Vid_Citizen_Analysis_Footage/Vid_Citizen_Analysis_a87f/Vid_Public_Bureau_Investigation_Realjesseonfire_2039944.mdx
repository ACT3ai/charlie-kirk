## What This Video Is

A 37 second handheld capture circulated by the account Public Bureau of Investigation in a thread addressed to @realjesseonfire, @RealCandaceO, @baroncoleman and @BasedSamParker, and saved into the investigation archive under post ID 2039944645028069376. It is a repost of somebody else's phone footage rather than that account's own recording, and no original filmer is credited on it.

What makes it worth a page is its continuity. Most of the widely shared clips of these seconds either start at the sound or cut at it. This one begins in normal proceedings, carries through the sound without a cut, and keeps running while the person holding the phone moves away from the lawn. That is a rare shape for this footage.

## What Is Said

The audio starts on the last Q&A of the event. The speech pass captures the question about how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last ten years, the reply about whether that is counting or not counting gang violence, and then a single word — "Great" — that lands just before everything changes.

After that there is no more debate audio, only the filmer's group. Their words are almost entirely instructions and reactions: "Go, go, go, go", a shouted "Everybody", and then "Stay down." Two exclamations in the last seconds are profanity and are not reproduced here. Nobody in the audio identifies what happened, describes a shooter or gives a direction. That absence is itself information: the people closest to the microphone did not know what they had just heard.

The stored shot log runs:

* **00:02 - 00:08** — the pan continues right to the canopy and the table, holding on the seated speaker answering him.
* **00:08 - 00:10** — a loud pop. Someone in a black shirt takes the seated speaker off his chair and down. Gasps and screams from the crowd.
* **00:10 - 00:26** — fast, disordered handheld: people running in several directions at once, staff scrambling on the grass, attendees dropping behind barriers.
* **00:26 - 00:36** — the filmer runs past a parking sign into a concrete parking structure and ends close on a group huddled beside a dark blue minivan while a guard directs them.

Two details in the automated log deserve care. It records a reserved-parking sign with a licence plate number on it, which is not reproduced here. And it notes an olive cap in frame reading "MARSEILLE EST. 600 BC" — the same cap that the investigation's French Foreign Legion material is built around. That is a genuine cross-reference and worth following, but the presence of a tourist cap in a crowd is not by itself evidence of anything.

The running shot into the parking deck matters for a mundane reason: it documents where part of the crowd went and how fast, which bears directly on the disputes about who was where in the minutes afterward.
