## What This Video Is

A three minute thirty-eight second clip circulated by the account RyanMatta and saved into the investigation archive under post ID 1984014200964796416. It has two parts: a forty-nine second introduction by Matta straight to camera, and then a two and a half minute extract of Candace Owens speaking in a podcast studio. The companion sixty-second version of the same story is filed beside this one in the same cluster.

This is a broadcast claim, not footage of anything. Everything on it is testimony about documents and sources that are not shown.

## What Is Said

**Matta's introduction (0:00 - 0:49).** He sets up the Owens segment — "remember when we told you that Candace Owens is gonna be bringing the heat" — and points at the image on the monitor behind him, which he describes as private Egyptian military contractors. He previews the claim: an aircraft that came to Utah, landed there, was "carrying military contractors or subcontractors", and that those people "didn't make it back on the plane that took off on the 11th." He says the source who gave Owens the information "felt threatened for her life or his life."

**Owens (0:49 - 3:38).** She states the claim herself and is explicit that she is stating it as fact rather than as speculation. Her account, in her own framing:

> "I feel that I have been presented with absolute solid proof from this individual who is in proximity to one of the individuals that was aboard this flight."

She rejects the servicing explanation that she says was offered — "this was not some dropping by in Utah, as they were trying to claim, because the plane needed to be serviced" — and mocks it: "I guess there's just no place from Egypt all the way to Utah that an Egyptian military plane can get a tire changed." She then gives the two assertions the whole item rests on: "that plane was carrying military subcontractors", and "they were dropped off in Provo, okay, and they did not make it back to Cairo." She lays out the movement as she understands it — departure on September 10, a layover in Wilmington, Delaware, and a return to Cairo on the 11th — and says that when the aircraft left, "those people were mysteriously missing from that plane."

She puts her question directly: "What are Egyptian military subcontractors doing in the middle of Provo, Utah on the day that Charlie Kirk was assassinated?"

She then addresses the FBI director by name and invites contradiction: "Kash Patel, please feel free to dispute this. In fact, I almost demand that you dispute it." She says the aircraft was in Utah "on some discrete mission" and that her source was frightened enough to fear for their life but came forward anyway.

She closes on the public nature of the killing — "the mistake that was made here was executing him publicly" — and on what she says followed from it: "more people standing up and doing something brave."

Every factual element above is Owens's, sourced to an individual she does not name and to material she does not show. No manifest, no flight record, no document and no photograph of any named person appears anywhere in the clip. The investigation tracks the underlying aircraft movements separately, and none of the personnel claims here has been independently confirmed. They are logged as a broadcast allegation by a named commentator, which is what they are.

Six static camera setups and no movement in any of them. The stored log: a single close-up of Matta with the monitor behind him (00:00 - 00:49), then Owens alternating between a centred medium close-up and a tighter angle (00:49 - 03:37) with a laptop and a broadcast microphone in frame.
