## What Is Said

**The screenshot is authentic, and he shared it.** The host opens by addressing "a group text chain that has been made known and released by Candace Owens" and says plainly: "that was a text grab, a screen grab that I had shared with people. So it is authentic." He explains why he had not published it himself — "it was a private exchange, and I felt like it didn't necessarily comport with things that were already public. I wanted to not betray my friend's trust in that way" — and then says what he did with it: "I did share it with some people in government because it happened really quick... it took 33 hours for authorities to get their suspect. And in those first moments we wanted no stone unturned. So I shared it with a few people. Don't know where it went from there."

That is the load-bearing part of the segment. A named participant states on camera that a widely circulated screenshot is genuine, that he passed it to unnamed people in government within the first day and a half, and that he does not know how it reached the public.

**The frame he puts around it.** He argues the text is not a revelation: "that text chain is consistent with public frustrations that he voiced many times." He sums up what he says Kirk's actual position was — "it's complicated, and it's nuanced, and it was a wrestle that was going on for months" — and insists "there was nothing hidden about Charlie's views on Israel."

**Blake's account.** The second man says he read a seven-hundred-page history of the Israel-Palestine conflict at Kirk's request: "Charlie said, Blake, get really well versed on this so you can help me whenever it comes up." He then gives his summary of Kirk's position: that he wanted the war to end, did not want America to take Palestinian refugees, did not want American troops on the ground, and was upset by reports of churches damaged and Christians killed, while also holding that "Hamas started the war, Hamas could end the war." He adds that Kirk could see hostility to Israel rising among young conservatives and was troubled by it.

**The Megyn Kelly insert.** From about 4:33 the segment plays Kirk in his own words to Megyn Kelly. The quotes are pointed: "I have text messages, Megyn, calling me an anti-Semite. I am learning biblical Hebrew and writing a book on the Shabbat... I visit Israel and fight for it." And: "my moral character is now being put into question... I am a bad person if I do this." He offers a charitable reading of his critics — "the people that are attacking me are in a hyper paranoid state, because they're at war" — and then withdraws it: "it's not defensible to be dumb... It's offensive to those of us who have been out there defending them, in many instances against critics on our own side."

He's frustrated. Venting." He says Kirk had made clear he would not be pressured into removing Tucker Carlson from the platform, and says of a possible Candace Owens invitation, "I have no idea." His summary: "I think what he was saying in that text is, keep coming at me, bro, and let's see what happens."
