## What Is Said

One voice, close to the phone, unidentified, on-device transcription of the full eleven seconds:

> Bro. It's a gunshot, I think.

That is the whole of the speech. It matters because of what it is: a bystander working out, out loud and in real time, what the noise he just heard was. He is not certain — "I think" — and the sentence arrives while people around him are already running. Recordings that catch the moment of recognition rather than the moment of the event are rare, and they are useful for two separate purposes: fixing the clip's position on the clock relative to the shot, and testing accounts of how the sound registered to people standing in the crowd rather than to a microphone on the stage.

Readers should note the limits. The speaker is not named, the clip is not timestamped, and one person's ear is not an acoustic analysis. What the audio establishes is that at least one person in this crowd read the sound as gunfire and said so immediately.
