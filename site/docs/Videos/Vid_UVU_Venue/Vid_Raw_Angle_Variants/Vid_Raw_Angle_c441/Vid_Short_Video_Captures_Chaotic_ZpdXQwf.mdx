## What This Video Is

An 8 second vertical phone clip filmed by a student standing on an elevated walkway above the Utah Valley University plaza, held in the investigation's capture archive. It is raw: one continuous take, no cuts, no overlay, no editing. Its worth is entirely in what the operator happened to be doing with the camera in those eight seconds, which turns out to be a great deal.

Short as it is, this is one of the more informative bystander angles in the corpus, because it contains a zoom onto the tent at almost exactly the wrong moment for the operator and the right moment for the record.

## What Is Said

The audio opens on the tail of the final exchange at the table, the same phrase heard on every angle from these seconds: "counting or not counting gang violence."

Then the clip becomes reaction. The operator and the friends beside him: "Oh, my God." A name is called - the transcript renders it as "Cody" - followed by "go, run, run" and a shout of "Whoa!" Nobody says what has happened, because nobody knows yet. That is the honest character of the audio: it captures the instant before interpretation, which is exactly what makes it useful and what makes it useless for establishing anything beyond timing.

## What The Footage Shows

Second by second. From 00:00 to about 00:01, a high-angle handheld shot from the walkway looking down on a packed plaza, the white canopy at the centre carrying "THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG," UVU signage and "A PLACE FOR YOU" banners on the building behind, mountains beyond.

From about 00:01 to 00:03 the operator zooms digitally onto the tent. The seated speaker is visible under the canopy. A report sounds, and figures around the table move toward him.

From about 00:03 to 00:05 the camera pans right across the plaza and the upper levels of the building, showing how many people were standing where. From about 00:05 to 00:06 a whip-pan left catches the operator's own face and his friends', alarmed. From about 00:06 to 00:08 the camera swings back and a second report is heard as the crowd breaks and runs, up the stairs and out across the concrete.

Two things to hold carefully. The stored machine description counts two distinct reports in this clip, at roughly 00:02 and roughly 00:07, and the crowd's mass movement follows the second rather than the first. Shot-count and shot-timing claims across this investigation are contested, and an 8-second phone clip with lossy audio is a weak instrument for settling them, so this is a datapoint to be measured properly rather than a finding. The other thing is that the zoom means the tent is framed larger here than in most bystander angles from this distance, which makes the clip useful for who-was-standing-where questions even though the resolution is poor.
