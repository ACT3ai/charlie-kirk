## What This Video Is

Thirty-seven seconds of continuous handheld phone video from inside the crowd at
Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025, running from just before the
report through the crowd's scatter and into the adjacent parking structure.
Filed in the mirror archive as `video/2.mp4` with no poster credit, no source
URL and no upload date.

It carries a transcript identical, word for word, to `video/2.ia.mp4`, which is
catalogued in this same section as
[Crowd Angle — The Last Question, Then the Scatter](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Angle_Variants/Vid_Raw_Angle_0ddd/Vid_Video_Captures_Dramatic_High_UBNwyKh).
The two are the same capture, retrieved by different routes.

**A warning about the stored description of this file, which is the reason this
page is written the way it is.** The automated visual description generated for
`video/2.mp4` characterises the footage, repeatedly, as a *simulated* active
shooter or security drill — "simulated gunshots," "the simulated attack,"
"designed to mimic a real-world emergency response." That characterisation is
false. This is footage from the day Charlie Kirk was killed. It was not a drill
and nothing about it was staged. The description is a machine's guess about
genre, generated without any knowledge of what it was looking at, and it is
recorded here only so that nobody downstream mistakes it for an observation.

The same description also asserts things the companion description of the
identical file does not: that security tackled the speaker to the ground, formed
a barrier with ballistic shields, and bundled him into a minivan in the parking
structure. The other description of the same thirty-seven seconds has the camera
losing the stage entirely after about eleven seconds and ending on bystanders
crouched beside a parked minivan, with no evacuation of the speaker on screen at
all. Those two accounts cannot both be right. Until someone views the file
directly, this page does not assert which is.

## What Is Said

The first eight seconds carry the final exchange of the event:

> [Questioner]: I'm going to give you some credit. Do you know how many
> [mass shooters] there have been in America over the last 10 years?
>
> [Kirk]: Counting or not counting gang violence?

After the report there is no structured speech, only shouted movement
instructions and one reaction, all from people near the camera:

> Go, go, go, go. Everybody — go, go, go, go, go. ... Stay down. ... Holy shit.

Nobody in the audio names a direction, identifies a shooter, or counts reports.
Every characterisation of the sound on this page comes from a machine listener
and should be checked against the waveform before being used for anything.

## What The Footage Shows

Because the two stored descriptions of this file disagree, only what they agree
on is set out here as observed, and the disputed material is marked as such.

Both agree on the opening: a shaky handheld shot across a dense crowd behind
metal barricades toward a raised platform under a white canopy printed "THE
AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG," a man in a white t-shirt seated at
a draped table, a questioner at a microphone in the audience, security staff
around the platform, many red caps in the crowd. Both agree that at about eight
seconds the report sounds and the frame becomes unstable. Both agree that the
camera operator moves with the crowd toward a concrete parking structure and
that the footage ends around a dark Chrysler Pacifica minivan parked under the
deck. Both note a spectator's green "MARSEILLE" cap passing the lens at about
twenty-three seconds.

They disagree on the central question of whether the speaker is visible during
the evacuation and whether he is taken to that minivan. One says he is tackled,
shielded and put into the vehicle; the other says the camera never recovers the
stage and that the people beside the minivan are sheltering bystanders. That
difference matters — the vehicle Kirk was moved in, and by whom, is a documented
point elsewhere in this investigation — which is exactly why it should not be
settled from an automated description.

What can be said without controversy is the same thing the companion angle
offers: this is evacuation-route and crowd-movement material. It shows how
quickly the plaza emptied and which way people went. It shows nothing usable
about the wound, the trajectory or the origin of the report.
