## What This Video Is

Ten minutes and sixteen seconds of continuous, mostly static wide-shot video of
the Turning Point USA event at Utah Valley University on September 10, 2025,
shot from an elevated position in the plaza. It is the longest single-angle
record of the event in this section, and it ends at the last exchange.

The archived filename — `15 (at the very end).mp4` — is the filer's own note.
Whoever put it in the mirror wanted the last minute found again. There is no
poster credit, no source URL and no upload date.

Two questioners work through the queue microphone in these ten minutes. The
first debates theology with [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) for roughly
nine minutes. The second asks a single question about firearms policy, and the
recording ends on Kirk's reply.

A note on the transcript before anything is quoted from it. The audio is
plaza PA reproduced across an open courtyard and captured on a phone at
distance, and the machine transcription of it is poor — words are dropped,
substituted and mangled throughout, especially in the theological section where
proper nouns and scripture references defeat the recogniser. Everything quoted
below has been cleaned for readability, and where a word is reconstructed it is
marked in brackets. Anyone relying on the exact wording should work from the
audio.

## What Is Said

### The theology exchange (roughly 0:00 to 9:00)

A student at the microphone challenges Kirk on Latter-day Saint teaching against
traditional Christian claims. The exchange is adversarial but civil, and both
sides press evidence rather than insult. The student presses on archaeology and
on the physical record — "Where are the golden [plates]?" — and on how many of
the original witnesses to Joseph Smith's account remained followers. Kirk's
line of argument is that the claims should be tested by evidence and that the
resurrection is the one claim Christianity stands or falls on.

Kirk's own summary of the disagreement, near the end of the exchange:

> We just have a different worldview. I believe the Word of God is complete. ...
> And again, I would just ask you, as you continue in your journey of faith,
> show me the ... evidence of why you believe, and ... specifically
> archaeologically. And I think that's an important thing you need to pray
> about.

He then explicitly moves to close the topic, saying he does not want the
audience left with the impression that he is against Latter-day Saints and that
he does not want to spend more of the event's time on it. He allows one last
question on the subject.

Asked for his own evidence, Kirk gives a compressed apologetics answer — the
empty tomb, the reported five hundred witnesses, Paul, the Gospel of Luke, the
women as first witnesses, extra-biblical mentions in Josephus and the Talmud,
and the early creeds embedded in the New Testament. There is a short back and
forth about whether Josephus was a Christian, which the student uses to make the
point that a non-Christian source is the stronger one. They part, and the
transcript records applause and a handshake.

This nine-minute stretch is not about the shooting at all. It is a record of
what Kirk was doing with the last hour of his life: arguing theology with a
student who disagreed with him, in public, without security incident.

### The last question (roughly 9:15 to the end)

A second questioner steps up. He identifies himself, greets Kirk, and sets up
his question from recent news:

> A few weeks ago there was a transgender [mass] shooter. And ever since then,
> about three weeks later, the Trump [DOJ] said that we should start talking
> about revoking gun rights for transgender [people]. ... So, do you know how
> many transgender Americans have been mass shooters over the last 10 years?

Kirk answers: "Too many."

The questioner replies that the figure is five, and then turns the question
around:

> Okay, now, five is a lot, right? I'm going to give you some credit. Do you
> know how many [mass shooters] have been in America over the last 10 years?

Kirk answers:

> Counting or not counting gang violence?

That is where the recording ends, and it is where Charlie Kirk's recorded speech
ends. The shot follows within seconds in the angles that keep rolling — see
[the 15-second crowd capture](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Angle_Variants/Vid_Raw_Angle_0ddd/Vid_Short_Video_Captures_Moment_utQE41v)
and [the 37-second angle](/Videos/Vid_UVU_Venue/Vid_Raw_Angle_Variants/Vid_Raw_Angle_0ddd/Vid_Video_Captures_Dramatic_High_UBNwyKh),
which carry the same audio and continue past it.

The second questioner is the person catalogued on this site as
[Hunter Kozak](/distraction_people/Hunter_Kozak). He is a living person who was
questioned by investigators and was not accused of any involvement. He asked a
policy question and got an answer. Nothing on this page suggests anything else,
and the exchange is recorded here for one reason only: it fixes what was being
said, by whom, in the final seconds.
