shot and runs through the crowd's scatter and into a parking structure. Filed in

## What Is Said

The first eight seconds carry the last exchange of the event, the same one
preserved in the shorter angle. The transcript, cleaned of ASR noise:

> [Questioner]: I'm going to give you some credit. Do you know how many
> [mass shooters] there have been in America over the last 10 years?
>
> [Kirk]: Counting or not counting gang violence?

After that there is no more structured speech, only shouted instruction and
reaction from people close to the camera:

> Go, go, go, go. Everybody — go, go, go, go, go. ... Stay down. ... Holy shit.

The "go, go, go" and "stay down" are being called by people moving the crowd
away from the tent, and the last line is the person filming or someone beside
them. Nobody in the audio identifies a direction, a shooter or a count of
reports.

That last point is worth stating carefully, because the clip is filed against
the multi-report question. The stored visual description characterises the sound
as "a rapid succession" of pops. A machine description is not an acoustic
analysis, and a single supersonic crack with echo off surrounding buildings can
read to an ear and to a phone microphone as more than one event. Anyone using
this clip on the question of how many reports there were should work from the
waveform of the original file, not from the description and not from the
compressed archive copy.

* **0:27 – 0:36**: Inside the concrete garage structure, the camera focuses on a dark blue Chrysler Pacifica minivan. People are huddling against the side of the car. A security guard in a light beige blazer yells commands to "Stay down!" and pushes people low against the vehicle.

Two details in that sequence are worth separating from the panic. The camera
never gets a usable view of the stage after 0:11 — everything from that point is
crowd, ground and pillars, so this angle contributes nothing to the wound or
trajectory questions. What it does contribute is movement: which way the crowd
went, how quickly the plaza emptied toward the parking structure, and where
staff and security ended up. That is timeline and evacuation-route material.

The uniformed and plain-clothes people visible in the panic sequence are doing
crowd protection. Nothing in this clip supports any characterisation of their
conduct beyond that, and none is offered here.
