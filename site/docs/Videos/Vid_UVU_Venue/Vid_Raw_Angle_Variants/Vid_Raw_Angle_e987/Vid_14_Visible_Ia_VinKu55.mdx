## What Is Said

The audio here is fragmentary and, importantly, **does not agree with the twin file**. This pass renders the key line as: "Oh, my God. Did you say it? We watch him go! There's **no** shot fired right now. Panic. Isaac! Isaac! Okay? This would be a bigger target right here? Yes, no, it's okay. Hey, there was a shootout. Where are you? I'm going."

The twin file's pass of the identical audio renders the same passage as: "Oh, my God. It's just... He's been shot. He's been shot. There's a shot fired right now."

That is a straight contradiction — "no shot fired" against "he's been shot" — produced by two machine transcriptions of one recording. It is a transcription artefact, not two witnesses disagreeing, and it is a concrete illustration of why machine transcripts of noisy crowd audio must never be quoted as testimony without checking them against the sound. Neither rendering should be cited as a witness statement.

What is stable across both passes: someone calls out the name **Isaac** repeatedly, trying to locate a companion; someone says a phrase about being "a bigger target right here"; and someone says "Where are you? I'm going."
