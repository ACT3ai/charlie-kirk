## What This Video Is

One hour, nine minutes and forty-three seconds - by a wide margin the longest file in this section and one of the most substantive pieces of citizen documentation of September 10, 2025, anywhere in this archive.

It is a livestream, not a clip. A citizen streamer walks the Utah Valley University campus with a hand-held rig, works the crowd, interviews attendees and counter-protesters, and keeps recording after the shot for roughly forty-five minutes while the campus is evacuated. The file title carried in the archive - "not visible, skip to 19 minutes" - is the note of whoever filed it, telling later viewers that the shot itself is not in frame.

That is the first thing to be clear about. This stream does not show the shooting. The operator is on an upper walkway, some distance away, with the camera pointed at the people he is talking to. What it shows instead is everything around the shooting: the venue before it, the first reactions, and about a dozen eyewitness accounts recorded within minutes, while people still had no idea what had happened.

## What Is Said

This is the reason the page exists. The stream is almost entirely speech.

**Before.** The operator walks the campus, notes the crowd, and crosses between what he calls "the Patriot side" and "the protest side." He spends several minutes talking gear with another streamer - a router in a backpack, a small camera, an SDI recorder - which incidentally establishes how many independent cameras were operating in the plaza that day. He interviews sign-holders on both sides. The tone is entirely ordinary.

**The moment.** Mid-sentence, the audio breaks:

> Holy shit. Holy shit... There's been a shooting. We heard shots. We heard shots. You guys okay?

Then, going into stream-host mode: "We are here live at Utah Valley State University. There's been a shooting at the Charlie Kirk event, the TPUSA event." He immediately hedges: "I don't know if that was shots or if that was fireworks, to be honest." A minute later, after speaking to his own son who was near the front, he corrects himself: "Definitely not fireworks."

**The first reports.** His son messages him. The operator relays it on camera, repeatedly flagging it as unverified: "My son just said Charlie Kirk was shot in the neck. I don't know the accuracy of that... This is unconfirmed." That care with attribution is unusual in this material and is worth crediting. He later reports what he says he heard from staff: "Charlie Kirk got hit is what the security detail just said."

**Eyewitnesses.** Several people give accounts on camera within minutes.

A man who gives his name as Eric says he was about the third level back, roughly fifty feet away: "I heard a shot, which took my attention away from Charlie. And when I look back at him after the echoes, there was blood protruding from his neck or his head... I don't think he's gonna survive this shot, if you want my opinion." Asked whether it sounded close or long range, he says he cannot tell - "it sounded like a higher caliber... it almost sounded like a cherry bomb."

A second witness, from about the third row: "I saw the shot ring out. I looked to my left, and then I looked over to my right. I saw him slump over in his chair forward... he had blood coming from the left side of his neck, down his shirt."

A third, later, describes direction: "I was right in the center, on the top level, and it came out the right side."

A fourth describes his own movement in the seconds before: "maybe 30 seconds before the shot, walking up the stairs all the way to the side of the bridge underpass area because I was looking for the line to ask him a question... that's when we heard the gunshot."

**The roof.** Two separate passages. Early on, an attendee tells the operator: "one of the cops said there might be a guy on the roof... I saw a cop on that roof right over there." Later the operator walks the north side and narrates the search himself: "Actively clearing this building, looking through every storage shed... it's looking like this might have been a sniper outpost up here on this building, possibly. Because that would be the only spot to reach down to the courtyard." That is the operator's own speculation, stated as speculation, while standing under the building.

**Security.** An attendee is emphatic about how she got in: "there was no security. I got tickets, and they didn't even take the tickets to get us in. They just let everybody in there... Walked in with tripods sticking out of my backpack, no problem. Right through the door. There was no security at all. That's what makes me really mad."

**An early detention.** The operator relays: "I heard they had a guy arrested, then the cops just all were rushing back in." Another attendee: "I was up there on the balcony. I saw a guy down there, they cuffed and pulled off." A third says someone "got a picture of the guy that got arrested." These are contemporaneous accounts of a detention at the scene; they do not identify anyone and no one on this page is accused of anything.

**Coincidence noticed in real time.** Two separate bystanders, unprompted, remark on the subject of the final question: "what a coincidence that he was talking about gun violence and then all of a sudden this happens."

## What The Footage Shows

Visually the first twenty-three minutes are a walking tour: elevated concrete walkways, the long queue against the Woodbury Building, the plaza filling, UVU banners reading "Exceptional Accountability" and "Exceptional Results," sign-holders on both sides, and the white "Prove Me Wrong / American Comeback Tour" canopy at the centre. Mountains and clear sky throughout.

After the shot the visual record changes character: running police, an emptying plaza, officers clearing buildings and storage sheds on the north side, people being pushed out of the area, and the operator's own route back to a parking lot.

Two data caveats. First, the automated visual description stored for this file attributes the "Change My Mind" booth to Steven Crowder. The audio and the banners make clear this is the Turning Point USA "American Comeback Tour" event; the attribution appears to be a machine confusion between two similar campus-debate formats, and it should not be relied on. Second, the stored shot-by-shot breakdown stops at about twenty-three minutes out of seventy - it covers only the pre-event walk and omits the entire post-shooting section, which is the part that matters most.
