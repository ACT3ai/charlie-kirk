## What This Video Is

Thirty-seven seconds, shot from an elevated vantage above the plaza - probably an upper walkway, given the angle of view down onto the tent and the lawns. Hand-held, no caption overlay, no watermark, uploader unknown.

Its value is scale. The ground-level captures in this section show a few square metres of grass and a few dozen people. This one, from about thirteen seconds on, opens out to the entire plaza and shows what several thousand people do at once. For reconstructing where the crowd went, which exits filled, and how long the dispersal took, this is the most useful of the short angles.

## What Is Said

The opening line is the shared marker again, from the stage:

> Counting or not counting gang violence. Great.

After the reports the audio is crowd noise with a few close voices calling out over each other.

A caution about the rest of the transcript. The machine transcription of the following twenty seconds is badly degraded - it produces broken fragments including what reads as "good job" and "he shot him" out of overlapping shouting at a distance from the microphone. Those word-level readings are not reliable and should not be quoted as anyone's speech. A transcription engine working over dozens of panicked overlapping voices will produce plausible-looking sentences that nobody said, and this passage is a clear instance. What can be said honestly is that the audio after the reports is a crowd shouting in alarm. Nothing beyond that should be drawn from it, and a page anywhere on this site that quotes a phrase from this passage as a statement by a person is resting on nothing.

## What The Footage Shows

* 00:00 - 00:03. High-angle on the crowd and the tent. The speaker is talking; the scene is calm and dense with people.
* 00:03 - 00:05. Reports. The crowd flinches and ducks as one. The camera swings right; people are diving for cover.
* 00:05 - 00:13. The camera tracks the panic across the grass and pavement, panning left and right.
* 00:13 - 00:28. Cut to a much wider elevated establishing shot of the whole plaza. People run in multiple directions at once. A hand holding a pink can enters the frame - the camera operator's own drink, which is a small but real reminder that this was an ordinary afternoon two minutes earlier.
* 00:28 - 00:37. Back to a medium shot near the stage: security staff directing people, and attendees climbing over the metal crowd-control barricades to get out.

In frame: "The American Comeback" and "Prove Me Wrong" on the tent, "A Place For You" on the building windows behind, metal barricades, and the white pop-up canopy.

The barricade detail in the final passage is worth pausing on. The crowd-control barriers that had been managing the queue became an obstacle to exit at the moment people needed to leave, and this angle shows attendees going over them. That is a venue and crowd-management observation the wider security discussion on this site draws on.
