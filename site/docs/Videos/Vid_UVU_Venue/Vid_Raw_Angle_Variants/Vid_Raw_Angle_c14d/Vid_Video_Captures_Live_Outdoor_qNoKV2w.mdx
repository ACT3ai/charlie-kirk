## What This Video Is

A 19 second handheld clip from a spectator standing slightly above the crowd, held in the investigation's capture archive. One continuous take, no editing, no overlay.

It is the only clip in this cluster that ends before the moment. It records the question, the answer and the crowd's reaction, and then it stops. That makes it useful in a way the panic clips are not: it shows the event as an ordinary event, seconds from the end, with no foreknowledge in the frame and none in the audio.

## What Is Said

Almost all of the audio is the exchange itself, amplified through the public-address system. A questioner at the microphone is partway through when the clip begins. The transcription engine mangles his wording, but the substance is recoverable from the machine visual pass and from the other angles in this cluster: he asks how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last ten years.

The reply comes back immediately: "Counting or not counting gang violence?" A single word of acknowledgement follows, and the crowd reacts loudly - cheering, clapping, some jeering. Nothing else is said before the clip ends.

That exchange is the most-cited audio of the day and it appears on every angle in this cluster, which is what allows the separate cameras to be synchronised against each other. This clip carries it cleanly and at length.

## What The Footage Shows

From 00:00 to about 00:07, a high-angle medium-wide of the crowd packed behind silver metal barricades, with the white and blue canopy at frame-right carrying "THE AMERICAN COMEBACK TOUR" and "PROVE ME WRONG." Under the canopy, one man stands holding a microphone and another is seated. A line of people in black shirts stands between the staging area and the barricades.

From about 00:07 to 00:13 the frame holds and tilts slightly as the question is put. From about 00:13 to 00:16 the reply lands and a spectator's raised fist swings into the right of frame. From about 00:16 to 00:19 the crowd erupts, hands and papers in the air, and the camera pans left across the reaction as the clip ends.
