## What This Video Is

A 45 second handheld clip held in the investigation's capture archive under a filename that flags its own limitation: the table is not visible in it. The operator's attention is on a small group of counter-protesters standing on an elevated concrete walkway, not on the event they are protesting.

That makes it a poor clip for the moment itself and a genuinely useful one for everything around the moment. It is the only angle in this cluster that documents who else was on the walkway, what they were carrying, and where they were standing relative to the plaza.

## What Is Said

The event audio arrives through the public-address system rather than from anyone near the camera. The transcript picks up the answer "too many," then the question about how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last ten years, then the reply "counting or not counting gang violence."

Then the transcript ends mid-word: "What the." Nothing after that is recoverable as speech. The crowd noise takes over completely.

The people the camera is actually pointed at cannot be heard. The machine description notes that the group on the walkway appear to be addressing the crowd below but that their voices are lost under the general noise. So this clip records their presence, not their words.

From about 00:23 to 00:31 it tilts up to open sky and the top edge of the walkway. Two honest notes. The camera never shows the table, so nothing in this clip speaks to what happened there. What the clip is good for: the physical relationship between the elevated walkway and the plaza below, the presence and position of counter-protesters, and a hard venue confirmation on the building signage.
