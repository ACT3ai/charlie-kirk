A 39 second handheld clip held in the investigation's capture archive, one continuous take with no editing. It starts close on the tent and pulls back to a wide of the terraced lawn, which means it captures both the exchange and the crowd's response to it in a single unbroken shot.

Of the short bystander angles in this cluster, this is the most complete: it holds the two final questions, the answers, the report, and roughly twelve seconds of aftermath, without a cut anywhere.

## What Is Said

The clip records the exchange in more detail than any other angle in this cluster, and the sequence matters because both questions came from the same person at the microphone.

First question, from the student: how many transgender Americans have been mass shooters over the last ten years. The seated speaker's answer, quoted from the transcript: "Too many." The crowd reacts. The student presses with a number - "okay, now five is a lot, right?" - and the reply grants him something: "I'm gonna give you some credit."

Second question, immediately after: "Do you know how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last ten years?" Answer: "Counting or not counting gang violence?" Then one word of acknowledgement.

Then the audio changes completely. Voices in the crowd: "Down, get down!" and "What the fuck?" and "Go!" No narration, no explanation, nobody yet describing what happened.

The transcript is the fullest recovery of that final exchange in this cluster. It is worth reading carefully because it establishes that the last question put to the table, and the last answer given, were about mass-shooting statistics.
