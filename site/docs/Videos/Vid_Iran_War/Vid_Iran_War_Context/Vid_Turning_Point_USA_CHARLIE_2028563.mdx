## What Is Said

Kirk delivers a compressed history and then a position.

**The history.** He opens with a question to the audience — "How much do you actually know about Iran?" — and answers it: "Persia was a great power, and then Iran took a turn for the worst." He walks through the sequence: the Shah, then Mohammad Mosaddegh, whom he notes "was democratically elected", and then the intervention. He states the American and British role without hedging it:

> "To be very fair, the United States and Britain got rid of Mosaddegh, and then we reinstalled the Shah. Bad decision. Which then led to the Iranian revolution where radical Islam took over Iran."

That is a sitting movement leader calling a covert Anglo-American regime-change operation a bad decision, on his organisation's own channel.

**The people.** He is warm and specific about Iranians as distinct from the government: "The Persians are wonderful people. Some of my closest friends are Persians. And I love the Persian people. And we should have a heart for the Persian people to try and have a better future."

**The reluctance.** The line that matters most for this cluster:

> "Regime change is very difficult to talk about. I actually don't like talking about regime change. It's very complicated, but understand that there can be brighter days ahead for the Iranian people."

**The close.** He turns it into an argument aimed at Western critics: "it's very easy to kind of sit in your Western armchair and be like, oh, Israel's terrible. Well, you were able to enjoy free speech rights and a lot of rights here in the West that Iran used to have. And if you are serious about actually protecting human rights, you should want to make Iran Western again."

Read as a whole, the clip is doing two things at once, and both are worth noting. It criticises past intervention and states a personal reluctance about regime change. It also argues against people criticising Israel and ends on a line about making Iran Western again. The investigation's interest in Kirk's Iran statements is precisely in that tension, and flattening it in either direction would misrepresent the tape.
