## What Is Said

Carroll builds four linked steps and then, unusually, declines to complete the argument.

**Capability, from a published source.** He holds up *Rise and Kill First* by Ronen Bergman, describing it as an Israeli-written history of Mossad's targeted-killing programmes, and characterises Israel as, in his words, "the world's foremost assassination nation." This is a capability argument. As the site's own page notes, establishing that a service could conduct an operation says nothing about whether it did.

**Kirk's reported friction with donors.** He displays a screenshot of a message chain he says was dismissed as a hoax until it was published, in which Kirk writes about being unable and unwilling to be "bullied like this," and about the consequences of declining to cancel one commentator while considering inviting another. Carroll's reading is that this was sent to major donors rather than exchanged privately with a friend, and that Kirk was reporting real financial pressure.

**Kirk's own public position on an Iran war.** He reads out, at length, a Kirk post he dates to April 3, 2025 — an argument against "an all out regime change war against Iran" on grounds of depleted stockpiles, unfinished reshoring of manufacturing, the deficit, and the scale of the country compared with Iraq, Syria and Afghanistan combined. This is the strongest documentary element in the video because it is Kirk in his own words on the record.

**Kirk's later questions.** Carroll states that Kirk had begun publicly raising questions about October 7 and about Gaza, and had begun expressing frustration at the pressure he was under.
