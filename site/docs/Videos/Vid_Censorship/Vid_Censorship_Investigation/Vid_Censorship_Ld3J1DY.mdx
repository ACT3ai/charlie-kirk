## What Is Said

Governor Spencer Cox speaks throughout. He opens at the podium with a general condemnation: "This is not good for us. It is not good to consume. Social media is a cancer on our society right now." His prescription is personal rather than regulatory at first: "log off, turn off, touch grass, hug a family member, go out and do good in your community."

He then places the moment in historical context, saying the country has "seen an escalation in violence" and comparing it to "the late 60s and early 70s. Certainly we saw these types of high profile political assassinations, another dark time in our history." He introduces a term for the people he holds responsible for amplifying conflict: "the conflict entrepreneurs are taking advantage of us, and we are losing our agency, and we have to take that back."

The exchange that gives the clip its news value comes at about 0:59, when Welker puts the question directly: "you referred to social media as a cancer on Friday. That's an incredibly strong word. Do you believe that social media played a direct role in this assassination?"

His answer is unqualified: "I believe that social media has played a direct role in every single assassination, an assassination attempt that we have seen over the last five, six years. There is no question in my mind that cancer probably isn't a strong enough word." He closes on policy, noting Utah was "first state in the nation a couple years ago to pass comprehensive reform" and adding that "these most powerful companies on Earth are suing us to prevent us from implementing these things."
