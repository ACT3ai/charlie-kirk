## What This Video Is

A 1 minute 25 second vertical TikTok held in the investigation's capture archive. The stored filename shows it reached the archive as a repost by the account Jammles, addressed to @ShadowofEzra, with the framing line that "photos and videos are being deleted." The clip carries the watermark @rynestimetwo and a red banner reading "Breaking they are attacking us even more! They are erasing data off our phones!!"

This is the original source clip. The reaction video filed elsewhere in this cluster embeds this same material behind a commentator's summary. If you want the claim without a layer of interpretation on top of it, this is the one to watch.

## What Is Said

The witness speaks for the whole runtime and makes one claim, repeatedly. He puts it first as a question: "Can someone explain to me how you can literally have stuff on your phone, have a couple posts that you post on TikTok, still have, you know, 20 other videos that you've luckily saved on hard drives that aren't connected to the internet, and then they're all just gone from your phone?"

The detail he keeps insisting on is the location of the loss. Not the platform, the device: "a bunch of my videos are gone, not from TikTok, from my library." He describes device behaviour he could not explain, and is candid about how it sounds: his phone was "turning different colors," lagging as he typed, and he was "watching it type different things that I'm not typing, just a bunch of strange things. I know I sound like a cuckoo when I'm saying this."

He also gives the innocent explanation he first reached for and then discarded: "yesterday I thought it was an overload because of the followers and comments and all this stuff like that, so I had to turn off my notifications." Only afterwards did he conclude something else was happening.

The wider assertion is about coverage rather than about himself: "We haven't seen anyone from this angle. People aren't talking from this angle. People aren't talking and posting their footage. People's footage is getting erased off their phones. People are being silenced. People are being targeted." He names no person, no company and no agency as responsible, and says plainly "I don't know where this comes from or what's going on."

His closing request is archival, and it is the reason this clip matters to a video-evidence hierarchy at all: "please save, save, save. Save them, screen record them, spread them. Do everything you can."

## What The Footage Shows

A single continuous close-up from 00:00 to about 01:20, shot on a phone's front camera at eye level with slight handheld drift, in a residential room with a dark green wall behind him and a framed abstract print at the upper left. He wears a blue fleece pullover and a Seager trucker cap. Auto-captions run at the bottom; the red banner is fixed at the top. At about 01:21 the clip cuts to the standard black TikTok outro card showing his handle.

There is no event footage, no screen recording of a file listing, no device log, and no before-and-after of a camera roll. The claim is entirely testimonial and the frames neither support nor undermine it. What they do establish is that the statement was made on camera under a traceable handle, and that the speaker's stated response was to duplicate rather than to delete.
