## What Is Said

Simmons describes the sequence in order. He submitted the video as a tip and says his motive was straightforward: "I thought I was doing a service, and, you know, to Charlie and helping out." The transcription engine renders the agency name imperfectly at several points, but the escalation he describes is clear: he first spoke to "someone at the local field office, there in my local state, and then it jumped up a few levels. Then I was talking to someone on, like, the national level."

The instruction, as he reports it, came from that national-level contact and was framed as concern for him: "because this was your friend, I think it's best that you erased the video from your phone, 'cause it's gonna give you PTSD." He says a further call followed "to ask if I had erased it," and then another: "I got a call back and was asked to confirm that it was gone."

It is the repetition that he says changed his mind. In his account, being asked more than once to confirm a deletion is what made him feel "it was even right to post these videos." He also says a prior account of his, five years old, "was gone" and had been removed. His response was to distribute rather than delete: "that's when I started sharing, posting, and saving on many different hard drives in many different places." He closes on the cost of it: "It's been crazy. It's been rough."

He does not allege a crime, does not name any individual, and does not claim the file was remotely destroyed. What he reports is a request, repeated, and his refusal to comply.
