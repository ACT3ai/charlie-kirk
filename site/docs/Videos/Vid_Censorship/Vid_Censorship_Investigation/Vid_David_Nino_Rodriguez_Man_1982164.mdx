## What Is Said

Simmons repeats the account almost verbatim from his earlier clip, which is itself a point in its favour: the story does not grow between tellings. His motive first: "I thought I was doing a service and, you know, to Charlie and helping out." Then the escalation: "I got in contact, was talking to someone at the local field office, there in my local state, and then it jumped up a few levels. Then I was talking to someone on, like, the national level."

The instruction as he reports it: "because this was your friend, I think it's best that you erased the video from your phone, 'cause it's just gonna give you PTSD." Then the follow-up calls, which are the part he keeps returning to: the contact "called another time to ask if I had erased it," and later "I got a call back and was asked to confirm it was gone."

He mentions again that a previous account of his was removed, and describes his own countermeasure: "that's when I started sharing, posting, and saving on many different hard drives in many different places." He ends the same way: "It's been crazy. It's been rough."

Nothing here alleges a crime or names an individual. The claim is that a request to delete was made, and repeated, and that he did not comply.
