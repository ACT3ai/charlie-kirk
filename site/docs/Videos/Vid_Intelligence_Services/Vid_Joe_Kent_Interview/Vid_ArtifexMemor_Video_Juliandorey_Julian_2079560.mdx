## What Is Said

**Dorey's question.** He asks Kent to be specific about having said he was "a little bit stonewalled" in trying to look at details of the Kirk case.

**How the process is supposed to work.** Kent explains the standing arrangement:

> "Whenever there's a terrorist attack or an assassination of a prominent figure, National Counterterrorism Center will investigate in parallel with the FBI. In parallel, because we're looking for foreign ties. And once it's established there's no foreign ties, NCTC stops. We're done, because we don't do anything domestically."

**Why the Kirk case qualified.** "He was killed in a very public event. He was an international figure. He had lots of international connections. So we were in the very early phases of looking at potential — and I say potential, because if there was something that I knew definitively, I would say it — international ties."

**The instruction.** This is the core of the clip:

> "We are quickly told, like, hey, stop what you're doing now, because we have Tyler — we have a suspect. We have Tyler Robinson in custody."

**His objection to that reasoning.** Kent's point is that a domestic arrest does not answer the foreign-nexus question:

> "Okay, well, fine. You have a suspect. Great. Do the FBI criminal investigation, do that whole thing. We need to continue to look at all the potential foreign ties, because there was people who clearly had pre-knowledge. There was people posting online who said that Charlie Kirk was going to die that day."

He follows it with an explicit hedge: "So the idea that he simply worked alone — maybe we'll find out that that ends up being the case." **That "pre-knowledge" statement is Kent's characterisation of online posts.** This site does not identify any person as having had advance knowledge of anything, and nothing on this page should be read as doing so.

**The donor thread.** "Charlie had a lot of foreign ties. There's what's been disclosed about the disputes he was having with different donors, you know, pro-Israel donors. So I felt on the international front, in terms of international connections potentially to any foreign individuals, foreign entities or foreign governments, we had more work to do."

**The context he places himself in.** "By the time Charlie was killed, the FBI was already mad at me for Butler. So we were already kind of adversarial in terms of, like, you stop asking questions."

**The careful ending.** Kent does not overclaim, and the restraint is why the clip carries weight:

> "The way any investigation works, you run down a hundred leads and 99.5 of them were total dead ends, right? So a lot of the stuff that I wanted to do, maybe it was going to end up being nonsense. But there was still more work there that I thought needed to be done. So that was my issue with the Kirk case — and the fact that we were pulled off a little bit early on that one."

## What It Does And Does Not Establish

It establishes a first-person account, from a named former director, that the parallel foreign-nexus review was ended early and that the stated reason was a suspect being in custody. That is a specific, falsifiable claim about a process, and it is answerable by records: who issued the instruction, on what date, and under what authority.

It does not establish foreign involvement, and Kent explicitly declines to claim any — "if there was something that I knew definitively, I would say it." The gap he describes is an **investigative** gap, not a finding.
