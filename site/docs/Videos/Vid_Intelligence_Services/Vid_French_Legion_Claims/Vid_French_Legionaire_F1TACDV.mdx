**What the argument actually rests on.** The chain runs: the young man wears a cap; the cap reads "MARSEILLE EST. 600 BC FRENCH RIVIERA"; a Legion regiment trains near Marseille; therefore the wearer is connected to that regiment. Every link after the first is inference, and the middle link is very weak — "MARSEILLE EST. 600 BC FRENCH RIVIERA" is souvenir styling, the kind of cap sold to tourists, and wearing one indicates nothing about military service. The slideshow supplies no name, no service record, no travel record and no second source of any kind. It does not even assert in words that the man is a legionnaire; it places two images side by side and lets the viewer close the gap.

The positional claim — that he stood inside a permission-only zone — is separable from the identity claim and is the part that could in principle be checked, against event credentialing and against the venue's own zone map. The slideshow does not check it; it draws an arrow.
