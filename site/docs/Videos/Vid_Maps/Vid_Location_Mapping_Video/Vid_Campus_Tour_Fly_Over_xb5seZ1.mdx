## What This Video Is

Two minutes and fifteen seconds of drone footage over the Utah Valley University courtyard, flown after the event and edited with labels laid over the picture. A watermark reading "TAYLER USA" sits in the corners. The archive holds it as `Campus_Tour_Fly_Over.mp4` with no posting account recorded. It has not been published to IPFS, so a still frame stands in for the player.

This is a reconstruction, not evidence of the event. Nothing in it was filmed while anything was happening. Its value is spatial: it is the clearest single object in this collection for understanding how the pieces of the site relate to each other — where the stage was, what overlooks it, and how far apart the disputed positions actually are.

Only a few distant, indistinguishable figures appear anywhere in the footage. Rooftop plant, HVAC units, barrier tape or graphic lines and scattered debris are visible. Nobody is identifiable and nobody is named.

The distinction that matters here is between the flight and the labels. The flight is real, verifiable geography: those buildings stand in those relationships, those roofs have those sightlines, that courtyard is that size. Anyone can check it against a map. The labels are the editor's assertions laid on top — including the word "alleged," which the editor uses honestly for the firing position but drops when the yellow line is called the direction "the shooter" ran. The person filmed running has not been established by this footage or any other to be the person who fired, and that identification is exactly what is contested elsewhere on this site. Read the yellow line as "the route the running figure took," which is what the surveillance clips actually support.

With that substitution made, the reconstruction is genuinely useful. It shows in one continuous movement why the roof matters, how far the runner's route is from the claimed perch, and what a camera at each labelled point would have been able to see.
