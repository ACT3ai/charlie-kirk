## What This Video Is

A two-minute-thirty package posted to X by the account FromConvict2Conservative. It has two parts stitched together: an introduction spoken by the poster, and then a piece to camera recorded on location by **Gary Melton of Paramount Tactical**, standing on the roof at Utah Valley University.

The on-location section is the substance. Melton is not commenting on footage — he is physically at the position in question, filming what he can see from it, and describing a survey he says is under way. That makes this one of the few items in this cluster with an original observational component rather than a re-reading of existing material.

The poster's introduction frames the segment before Melton speaks, and the poster returns at the end to put the question to the FBI. Note that the poster's framing — that Melton "has just confirmed what we all already knew" — is his characterisation. Melton's own words are narrower than that, and the difference is worth holding on to.

## What Is Said

**The poster's set-up.** He introduces Melton as "on the ground right now at UVU campus," and says he has "just confirmed... that the camera position directly behind the Losee building would have captured the alleged assassin in the prone position at the time that he took the shot." The transcript renders "Losee" as "low sea" throughout.

**Melton on the roof.** He establishes what he has done: "we are here on location at Utah Valley University, and we're on the roof. We've already walked the entire route. I have that footage coming. We've walked through the tunnel. We've walked up the stairs."

He then describes the position itself, and this is the observational core of the clip: "this little wall is no more than three feet tall. Right over there is where the shot allegedly came from." Note the word "allegedly" — it is his.

He turns the camera to a fixed camera on an adjacent roof edge: "that surveillance camera right there on the edge of that roof. That's where we've already seen footage from, and that, without a doubt, has an entire view of this location. So, if there was a shot taken from there, it's gonna be on that camera. The Utah authorities have it, the FBI has it."

He also says a mapping drone is airborne: "it is mapping this entire campus. We're gonna have a full 3D model of that."

**The demand.** The poster closes by asking why the CCTV has not been released, and then makes a careful correction to his own framing when the answer runs the other way. Asked whether release would "put to rest the theory that the shot came from anywhere else," he answers: "Well, it wouldn't eliminate the theory that the shot came from somewhere else, but it would definitely add a penny to an empty piggy bank towards the statement that Kash Patel made saying that this is the most transparent administration in American history."

His final ask is specific: "release all of the video footage from the camera behind the Losee building... Don't just show us video footage of Tyler Robinson running at the midway point before he jumps off of the building with absolutely no rifle in his hands. At least show us him in the prone position."

Two cautions on that last passage. It assumes as established that the person in the released rooftop footage is Tyler Robinson, and it assumes a shot was taken from that position; neither has been proven in court, and this page does not adopt either assumption. What the passage does capture accurately, and usefully, is the shape of the release complaint: footage of movement has been shown, footage of the act has not.

## What The Footage Shows

The location segment is genuinely evidentiary in a way most commentary clips are not. On camera you can see the roof surface, the low parapet Melton estimates at under three feet, the sightline from that parapet to the plaza below, and — critically — a fixed dome camera mounted at the edge of an adjacent roof with an apparently unobstructed view back toward the position.

That last frame is the whole point of the video. It does not prove what the camera recorded, whether it was operating, or what its field of view actually covered — a camera visible from a position is not the same as a camera trained on it, and Melton's "without a doubt" is his own judgement from the ground rather than a survey result. But it does establish, visually and checkably, that such a camera exists at that location and orientation.

The rest is stated rather than shown: the walked route, the tunnel, the stairs, the drone mapping and the promised 3D model are all described as forthcoming. None of that material appears in this clip.
