## What This Video Is

Sixty-three seconds of a sit-down interview, reposted to X by the journalist @IGraceAshford under a caption reporting that every district and magistrate judge in the Middle District of Tennessee had recused themselves from the civil suit brought against Candace Owens by Brian Harpole. The clip is Owens describing that suit in her own words; the caption is the news item it was attached to.

The set is informal: two brown leather armchairs, framed art, bottles on a shelf, red accent lighting. Owens sits opposite a bald interviewer in a teal shirt. It is a produced podcast-style conversation, not a court proceeding, and nothing in it is testimony.

## What Is Said

Owens explains the threshold legal question the case turns on, and she explains it accurately: whether the plaintiff counts as a public figure or a private person, a distinction that sets a much higher bar for a public figure to clear.

Her characterisation of the opposing argument: "his lawyer wrote this amazing story, which just made entirely no sense, argument, he's not a public figure, which is very important... So they were trying to argue that he's not a public figure."

Her counter is about how the plaintiff entered the story: "obviously he made himself a public figure by going on this podcast and interjecting himself into this Charlie narrative."

She then disputes the causation the claim rests on, which is the substantive part of the clip: "he basically said he was forced to become a public figure because of me... I never covered him on my show once, never spoke his name on my show before he went on your show." She repeats it for emphasis: "you weren't talking about him. Never said one word about him. Or the security apparatus, because I knew them, and my mind just wasn't there at all until he did your interview."

Her account of why she began commenting is a judgement about a broadcast, not an allegation of conduct: "it was so disastrous in my view, and I think also the view of all the people who watched it, according to the comments, there was just something off about it. And I went, whoa, he's answering questions weird, the stuff that he's saying doesn't really make sense."

That last passage is Owens' opinion of how a man came across in a televised interview, offered by one party to an active civil case about her own statements. It is her characterisation and nothing more. The plaintiff's position, as she herself describes it, is the opposite: that he was a private individual pushed into public view. He is entitled to that position, no court has resolved the question at the point this clip was recorded, and this site does not take a side in it.
