## What Is Said

**The host, on the request and the refusal.** He summarises what he says he published previously:

> Apparently they requested — that TPUSA told UVU that they were going to provide their own roof coverage and that they were requesting to fly a drone during the event. That is his characterisation of the exchange, presented alongside a screenshot of the Bowyer post it derives from. Note the two separate items inside it: roof coverage and drone authorisation. They are commonly collapsed together and they are not the same request.

**Turek, on what he saw.** After a passage of raw aftermath audio, the clip cuts to Turek speaking on stage:

> So we drove in. And as we were driving in, Charlie got some video footage from drones above. This is an hour before it was supposed to start, and there were thousands of people there. And I'm looking at the video footage and I go, "Charlie, I don't like this place. There's too many buildings." But Charlie, knowing that the people wanted to interact, and being brave as he was, he just kind of shrugged it off.

That is a named speaker, on a stage, describing his own recollection: aerial footage of the crowd existed, it reached Kirk's phone or screen roughly an hour before the event, and Turek looked at it and voiced a misgiving about the number of buildings overlooking the site.

**Why the two do not simply cancel out.** The investigation's own notes record that this discrepancy has an explanation on the table, and it is a distinction rather than a retraction: TPUSA is recorded as having acknowledged, in correspondence, that a media-production drone flew briefly for B-roll before the event, while maintaining that a security drone was denied. Turek's account, if accurate, is compatible with the media-production drone and incompatible with a flat "no drones flew."

Nobody named here has been found by any court to have said anything untrue, and this page does not assert that anyone did. What can be said is that the public accounts do not line up, that the difference matters — a drone feed of the crowd an hour before the event is a security artifact whether or not it was flown for B-roll — and that the resolution lies in records rather than in commentary.
