## What Is Said

Six seconds of audio, one voice close to the microphone, rendered by on-device transcription as:

> Hey, be safe, right? Apparently, he's been a shooting.

The second sentence is mangled — the engine is plainly wrestling with "apparently there's been a shooting" or similar. What survives the mangling is the shape of the moment: somebody telling somebody else to be careful, and relaying, second-hand and hedged with "apparently," that shots have been fired. It is the same category of material as the bystander line in the running clips elsewhere in this collection — an unnamed person processing a rumour in real time.

That single sentence also dates the clip, at least relatively. Whatever this footage is, it was recorded after the shot, not before it. That matters for the drone claim: a caption asserting the media shows the aircraft "suspected of assassinating" is attached here to footage taken once the event was already over.

## What The Footage Shows

One static, handheld, high-angle wide shot of the plaza, with minor jitter and no pan, tilt or zoom. Hundreds of people, mostly of apparent student age, in casual clothes, standing, talking and looking around. Concrete pillars, metal railings, trash bins, lampposts, and what appear to be golf carts or utility vehicles at a distance. Arid mountains under a clear sky behind. No legible text or branding anywhere in frame. Faces are not resolvable and nobody is identified.

There is no drone. No object in the sky, no annotation, no highlighted dot, nothing airborne at all. Both clips attached to a post promising drone imagery are ground-level crowd views with no aircraft in them.

The honest conclusion is narrow and worth stating: these two files do not support the claim in the caption they were published under.
