## What Is Said

This is the loudest of the drone clips and the audio is genuine aftermath. The speech is fragmented, overlapping, obscene in places, and captured within a minute or two of the shot. Cleaned of expletives, the exchange runs roughly:

A voice urging movement: "Yo, go, go, go, go." Another refusing to leave the area for class: "I'm not trying to go to next class." Then shock — "Oh my God" — and the question that everyone on the tape is asking each other:

> Who got shot?

The answer comes from a young man carrying a skateboard, and he repeats it four or five times to different people in the space of a minute:

> Charlie got shot. Yeah, he's dead. In his throat. He's dead.

And a disbelieving reply: "He's dead? He got shot in the throat. He's dead. No."

Everything about that is second-hand. The speaker is a bystander in a fleeing crowd relaying what he has heard or believes he saw; he is not identified in the file and is not identified here. His account is worth noting for one reason only, and it is a real one: within a minute or two of the event, before any official statement existed, people on that lawn were already saying the wound was to the throat. Where the earliest uninstructed descriptions place the injury is a live question in this investigation, and a raw clip like this is a data point in it — one data point, from one rattled student, and nothing more.
