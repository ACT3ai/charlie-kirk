## What This Video Is

A 32 second clip with **no audio at all** - a single continuous aerial shot,
extracted and reposted by the account **Project Constitution** under the
headline "TPUSA's Tyler Bowyer DOUBLY EXPOSED - Birthday Vid DROPS DRONE
FOOTAGE They 'Couldn't Use' & Shreds FBI Cover Story".

The clip is not new footage of the shooting. According to the poster it is
lifted from **Turning Point USA's own tribute video**, published on October 14
for what would have been Charlie Kirk's 32nd birthday, and the shot in it was
taken by a drone over the Utah Valley University plaza on September 10, 2025 -
before the shooting, while the crowd was still assembling.

Two on-screen details support that provenance. A white canopy in the lower right
of frame carries the words **"AMERICAN COMEBACK"** - the branding of the tour
Kirk was on that day. A circular red, white and blue watermark sits in the
lower left corner. The footage is bright midday sunlight over a modern
glass-and-brick campus with a dry mountain range behind it, consistent with
Orem, Utah.

Because the clip is silent, the "What Is Said" section is omitted. There is no
narration, no ambient audio, and no speech of any kind. Everything this page
records is either visible in frame or is a claim made in the caption around it.

## What The Footage Shows

One unbroken, high-angle aerial take, roughly 31 seconds long, moving slowly:

* The opening framing is tight on a packed paved plaza and lawn, several hundred
  people visible, seen from above.
* The camera tilts up and pans very slightly across the shot.
* By the end of the take the frame has widened to include the surrounding
  multi-storey campus buildings and the mountains beyond.

That is the entire clip. It is a promotional establishing shot - smooth,
stabilised, deliberately composed. Individual faces are not resolvable at this
altitude.

The evidentiary claim being made rests on exactly one property of the footage:
that it exists at all, and that it was taken from the air over a crowd. It shows
no shooter, no rooftop, no weapon, and no incident. The event footage cuts off
well before anything happened.

What the clip is being offered to contradict is a set of statements the
investigation records elsewhere. The master file notes that UVU, TPUSA and
Tyler Bowyer are reported to have said no drones were overhead and that their
security detail had none, and it records the claim that campus police declined
TPUSA's request to fly a drone and to place their own people on the rooftops.
The site's own [UVU Security: No Drones](/Drones/UVU_Security_No_Drones) page
tracks those statements. Project Constitution's argument is that a drone shot
appearing in TPUSA's own memorial video sits awkwardly beside them.

Three cautions belong on this page, because the clip alone does not settle any
of them:

1. **Nothing in the frame timestamps it.** There is no burned-in clock and no
   metadata visible on screen. The claim that this is September 10 and that it
   is pre-shooting comes from the poster and from the tribute video's own
   framing, not from the footage.
2. **A drone being present is not the same as the security detail flying one.**
   The investigation separately records the claim that a Utah drone operator was
   present in Kirk's party that day, a thread the site follows on
   [Butch Hibbs and the Crowd Drones](/Drones/Butch_Hibbs_Crowd_Drones). A
   permitted production drone shooting b-roll for a tour is a different thing
   from an overwatch asset, and neither is the same as an unidentified aircraft.
3. **The characterisation in the headline is the poster's.** This page reports
   that Project Constitution asserts a contradiction between TPUSA's public
   statements and its own video. It does not adopt that assertion, and no
   finding of any kind has been made about any individual named in the headline.

Set against those cautions, the clip's real value is narrow but genuine: it is a
piece of aerial imagery of the venue, from above, on the day, that can be
compared frame-for-frame against parking-lot camera angles and against the other
aerial material in this cluster.
