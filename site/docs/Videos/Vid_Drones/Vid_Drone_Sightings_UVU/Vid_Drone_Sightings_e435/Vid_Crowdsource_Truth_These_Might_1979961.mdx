## What This Video Is

A 21 second clip posted on X by **Crowdsource The Truth**, the channel run by
Jason Goodman, under the caption "These might be the best images of a UAV on
site yet. Doesn't look li[ke]..." - the post text is truncated in the archive.

It is not raw footage. It is a **processed and annotated** version of someone
else's bystander video: the whole clip is played back in heavy slow motion, and
a yellow-bordered circular magnifier has been composited over the sky to
enlarge and track a small dark object as it crosses the frame. A permanent
lower-third overlay reads "CROWDSOURCE THE TRUTH GigaSync".

The 21 seconds are one continuous shot. Because the playback is slowed so far,
the underlying source clip is only a few seconds of real time.

## What The Footage Shows

The source footage is a wide, roughly eye-level handheld shot of the terraced
outdoor plaza at Utah Valley University on a clear day. The **"UVU"** logo is
visible on a building facade at frame right, which is what fixes the location.
Dozens of students are sitting on the grassed terraces, standing, and walking.
Two people are prominent in the foreground: a woman in a floral dress standing
on a ledge at frame left, holding a phone up to record, and a man in a black
shirt and a backwards cap at frame right, facing away from camera.

Inside the yellow magnifier circle near the top of the frame, a **dark,
elongated speck** moves horizontally across the sky, left to right. That speck
is the entire claim.

What can honestly be said about it from this clip:

* There is an object in the sky, it is dark against a clear blue background, and
  it is moving laterally at a rate that required the poster to slow the footage
  down to follow it.
* It is **unresolved**. At this magnification, from consumer phone video, the
  object is a handful of pixels. Nothing about its shape, size, altitude,
  distance, or means of propulsion can be established from these frames. A
  small object close to the camera and a large object far away produce the same
  smear.
* The magnifier is a **digital enlargement of existing pixels**, not additional
  optical detail. Upscaling cannot add information that the sensor did not
  capture, and it will make compression artefacts look like structure.

Those limits are worth stating plainly because they apply to almost every
"object in the sky" clip in this cluster, and because they are what separates
this material from the aerial footage in
[the TPUSA birthday video shot](/Videos/Vid_Drones/Vid_Drone_Sightings_UVU/Vid_Drone_Sightings_e435/Vid_Project_Constitution_TPUSA_S_1978960),
where the drone is not a speck in the frame but the camera itself. The two are
often cited together as though they were the same evidence. They are not: one
shows an unidentified dot, the other shows that an aircraft with a camera was
overhead.

A further caution about method rather than about this poster: the investigation
separately holds a clip in which analyst **Thomas Perera** disputes a
frame-sequence analysis he attributes to Jason Goodman, arguing that the frames
were presented out of order so that an object appeared to travel backwards. See
[Thomas Perera on the frame sequence](/Videos/Vid_Drones/Vid_Drone_Sightings_UVU/Vid_Drone_Sightings_7ea0/Vid_Thomas_Perera_Am_JohnCullen_1977744_2).
That is a disagreement between two researchers about how frames were handled,
recorded here so that a reader weighing this clip knows the analysis coming out
of this channel has been contested by another analyst working the same footage.

The honest summary: this is a genuine bystander recording of the UVU plaza with
a real, unexplained moving object in the sky, annotated by a third party. It
establishes that something was up there and moving. It does not establish what
it was.
