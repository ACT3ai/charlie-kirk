## What The Footage Shows

One continuous vertical handheld shot, roughly two seconds long. The camera pans
slightly right and tilts up. In that time it takes in:

* a multi-storey concrete building at frame left, with people standing along the
  upper balconies;
* a grassy slope to the right of the building, densely packed with onlookers
  standing shoulder to shoulder;
* foreground figures, mostly college-aged, in casual summer clothing, all facing
  the same direction toward something off-frame to the right;
* a clear blue sky with light wispy cloud.

That is everything. The crowd is at full density and every visible person is
oriented toward the same off-screen point, which is consistent with the event
being under way rather than still assembling.

**The filename is a claim the footage does not carry.** Nothing identifiable as
a drone, an aircraft, or any airborne object appears in these frames. The
description generated from the file records a crowd, a building, a hillside and
a sky - no aerial object of any kind. Two seconds at handheld exposure is also
too short to establish motion across the sky even if a dot were present.

This page therefore records the clip honestly as **crowd and venue footage**
rather than as a drone sighting. It has real value in that role. It is a clean,
wide, timestamped-by-context look at how many people were on the south slope and
on the building balconies, which bears directly on sightlines, on how exposed
the venue was, and on how many independent phone cameras were pointed at the
plaza that afternoon. Anyone reconstructing what could and could not be seen
from the south side will want it.

What it should not be cited as is evidence of an aerial platform. If a longer
version of this recording exists that does show an object, the two seconds held
here are not it, and the fuller file would need to be located and described
before the filename's claim could stand.
