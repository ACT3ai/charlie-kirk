The object is small, dark, moving fast, and silhouetted against a glass panel
rather than against sky - which is actually the more informative background,
because the window gives a fixed reference plane and a consistent tone behind
the object.

What the frames support and what they do not:

* They support that a small flying object crossed the window plane at speed,
  close enough to the camera to be resolved at all.
* They do not support any identification. At this magnification, an insect at
  five metres and a small multirotor at forty metres are visually
  indistinguishable. The apparent "wings" could as easily be motion blur across
  the exposure as a physical structure.
* The window itself introduces a further ambiguity that the clip does not
  address: a reflective glass facade can show either an object in front of it or
  the reflection of an object behind the camera. Nothing in the frames settles
  which.

That is not a criticism of the post - the post asks the question rather than
answering it. It is the reason the question stays open. The investigation holds
this clip as an unresolved anomaly, not as a documented small-drone sighting,
and the distinction matters when it is cited alongside claims about
micro-platforms in the
