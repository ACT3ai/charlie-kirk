## What This Video Is

Seventeen minutes. It is a self-published video essay, not footage of the event. The presenter appears on camera in a t-shirt printed with the words "My name is: Irrelevant" and a website address, and he closes the video by repeating that his name is irrelevant. He is anonymous by choice, and this page treats him accordingly.

The format is screen-capture analysis: he plays clips of the UVU event inside a video editing application, pauses, zooms, draws circles and arrows, overlays diagrams and maps, and narrates continuously. The final third departs from the event entirely and becomes a theological argument about Christian Zionism and scriptural interpretation, ending with a call to visit his website.

Before anything else is said about it, the framing has to be set clearly. Everything below is a report of what this video asserts. None of it is adopted by this site. The video makes serious accusations about unnamed private individuals - people who were working as camera operators at a public event - and those accusations are unproven, uncorroborated, and rest on the presenter's own reading of heavily magnified low-resolution frames. No one has been charged with, or found responsible for, anything the video alleges. The people it points at are not named here, and no conduct is attributed to any of them by this page.

## What Is Said

The presenter's argument, as he states it, runs as follows.

**The central claim.** He asserts that a camera visible behind the speaker was "not a real camera" but "a housing for a camera with a firing mechanism inside," and that its telephoto lens contained a suppressor. He says he arrived at this by enlarging the footage in an editing application and believing he could see combustion products emerging from the lens: "you can see the combustion fumes from the bullet coming out of it... it's literally the smoking gun." He later points to specific frame numbers - "213, 214, 215" - as the moment.

**The precedent he offers.** He compares the device to a concealed weapon he says he found online and to a scene in a feature film about the 1972 Munich Olympics: "where do you think Steven Spielberg got the idea from..." That is a film reference offered as an argument by analogy, not evidence.

**The coordination claim.** He asserts that a second person nearby was a signals man with a radio coordinating with a shooter on the roof, that a minivan parked nearby was the exfiltration vehicle, and that a monopod rather than a tripod was chosen to allow mobility and deniability. Each of these is his interpretation of ordinary equipment and ordinary movement.

**The wound argument.** He argues for two wounds from two directions - one from the roof that stayed in the body and one from behind that exited - and describes a supersonic pressure wave lifting the shirt and moving the hair. He explicitly disagrees with an autopsy image he says he found online: "I don't agree with it. I think it's too much damage." He is arguing against the forensic record rather than from it.

**The camera-count claim.** He states that the organisation normally runs four 4K cameras on a livestream and that on this day there were three, and characterises a stated business decision as "a calculated decision." This is one of the few strands in the video that touches a documented question - what was recorded from around the stage and what became of it - and it is a question this site pursues from the records side rather than from frame analysis.

**His own caveat.** Near the end he says: "Now, if I'm wrong in all this, I have one hell of a movie script." That line is the presenter's own acknowledgement that his case is a reconstruction, and it belongs on this page as prominently as any of his claims.

**The closing section.** The last several minutes leave the case entirely for a scriptural argument against Christian Zionist theology, addressed to pastors, and a promotion of his website.

## What The Footage Shows

What is on screen for seventeen minutes is a man talking over a screen recording. The underlying event footage he plays is the same publicly circulated material available elsewhere on this site, put through heavy digital magnification.

That magnification is the crux, and it is where the video's method fails. Enlarging a compressed low-resolution video does not recover detail that was never captured; it produces blocks, ringing and colour artefacts that the eye readily organises into shapes. A dark smudge at that magnification is compatible with combustion products, with lens flare, with sensor noise, with compression artefacting, and with nothing at all. The presenter treats one reading of that smudge as decisive - "if you eliminate the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth" - without establishing that the alternatives were eliminated. They were not.

The on-screen graphics reinforce this: red circles labelling a spectator as a "person of interest," hand-drawn bullet-path lines over an overhead map, a diagram of a concealed weapon, book and film covers used as supporting imagery.

This page records the video because it exists, because it circulated, and because a catalogue of the video evidence that omits the material it disagrees with is not a catalogue. It does not record it because the argument holds.
