## What Is Said

The whole clip is one exchange. Cleaned of transcription noise:

> [Questioner]: Hey, Charlie. ... A few weeks ago there was a transgender [mass]
> shooter, and ever since then, about three weeks later, the Trump [DOJ] said
> that we should start talking about revoking gun rights for transgender
> Americans. ... So, do you know how many transgender Americans have been mass
> shooters over the last 10 years?
>
> [Kirk]: Too many.
>
> [Questioner]: ... Okay? Now, five is a lot, right? I'm going to give you some
> credit. Do you know how many mass shooters there have been in America over
> the last 10 years?
>
> [Kirk]: Counting or not counting gang violence?

The clip ends there. "Counting or not counting gang violence?" is the last
sentence Charlie Kirk is recorded saying.

The questioner is the person catalogued on this site as
[Hunter Kozak](/distraction_people/Hunter_Kozak). He is a living person, he was
questioned by investigators, and he was not accused of any involvement. He asked
a policy question and received an answer. Nothing on this page implies anything
further.

The value of this particular copy is that it captures the exchange from the
opening greeting through to the final reply without a cut in the middle. Shorter
circulating versions start partway into the questioner's setup, which has led to
the framing of the question being misquoted in secondary posts. Anyone citing
