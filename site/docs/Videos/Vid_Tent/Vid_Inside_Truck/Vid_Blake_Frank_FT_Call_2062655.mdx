## What This Video Is

Three minutes fifty-seven seconds of frame analysis, watermarked "BLAKEBEDNARZ."
It is a made piece, not raw footage: two bystander angles of the Utah Valley
University event synchronised into a split screen, played at normal speed, then
replayed slowed and super-slowed with text overlays calling out details.

The title it was filed under states the maker's conclusions as flat negatives —
that [Frank Turek](/People/frank-turek) was not on a video call and was not
watching a drone feed. The video is, in other words, mostly a rebuttal: it takes
claims other people made about a few frames and argues they are wrong. One of
its overlays, though, makes a positive claim of its own about Mr. Turek, and
that one is treated here with the same caution as the claims it is rebutting.

## What Is Said

There is no narration. The audio is the event audio, replayed several times over
— the last exchange of the Q and A, the report, and the immediate reaction:

> Do you know how many mass shooters there have been in America over the last
> 10 years? ... Counting or not counting gang violence?

> Go, go, go, go! ... Everybody! Go, go, go, go. ... Stay down. ... Holy shit.

Everything the video argues is argued in text on screen rather than in speech.
The overlays, in order:

* **"REFLECTION!"** — pointing at a vehicle window in the left-hand angle.
* **"The White Dot is from the Yukon XL's Chrome trim."** — the maker's
  explanation for a bright single-frame dot that others have read as a muzzle
  flash. This is a debunk, and a specific, testable one: it names the object,
  the vehicle and the surface.
* **"Frank is pretending to FaceTime!"** — over a zoom on Mr. Turek holding a
  phone.
* **"WTF did we do face."** — over a close-up of a man's expression in the
  aftermath.
* **"Casual walk. 20 seconds after boom."** — over background footage of a man
  walking.

Two of those five deserve separating from the rest. The white-dot overlay is the
most useful thing in the video, because it offers an ordinary physical
explanation for an artefact that has been cited as evidence of a firing
position, and because a chrome-trim reflection off a known parked vehicle is
something anyone can go and check against the geometry. It also converges with
an independent conclusion reached elsewhere in this archive: a former Marine
Scout Sniper working the same white dot counted frames, found the implied
distance did not fit the site, and told viewers to discard the muzzle-flash
reading — see
[his breakdown](/Videos/Vid_Ballistics_Gun/Vid_Ballistics_Analysis/Vid_Ballistics_Kirk_KLcwHCd).
Two analysts, arriving separately at "not a muzzle flash," is worth more than
either of them alone.

The other three are characterisations of people, not of objects, and they are
not adopted here. "Pretending to FaceTime" asserts an intention behind a man's
handling of his own phone; "WTF did we do face" and "casual walk" assign meaning
to a stranger's facial expression and walking pace in the seconds after a
shooting. Mr. Turek is a living person, as are the unidentified men in the other
two overlays. People behave in every imaginable way in the minute after a
gunshot — some run, some freeze, some walk — and a slowed-down frame of somebody
looking confused is not evidence of anything. Nothing on this page asserts that
any of them did anything improper.

## What The Footage Shows

* **0:00 - 0:08** — Split screen, both cameras static. Left: wide on the stage
  and audience. Right: close on the stage corner and the front rows.
* **0:08 - 0:10** — The report. The right-hand camera tilts down sharply.
* **0:10 - 0:35** — Both angles run through the reaction: people ducking,
  security staff moving.
* **0:36 - 1:10** — A "Slowed Down" card, then the slow-motion pass carrying the
  reflection and white-dot overlays and the zoom on the phone.
* **1:11 - 2:39** — Slow-motion of the aftermath, carrying the expression and
  walking-pace overlays.
* **2:40 - 3:17** — A different angle: a security detail moving people toward a
  black SUV with a door open. A staff member approaches the camera and raises a
  hand to block the shot.
* **3:18 - 3:56** — Brief chaotic crowd and vehicle shots, then a return to the
  slowed Q and A breakdown.

The stretch from 2:40 to 3:17 is worth flagging separately from the overlay
argument. It is footage of the evacuation to the SUV, including a staffer
physically blocking a bystander camera. That is documentation of what was and
was not allowed to be recorded in the minutes afterward, and it stands on its
own regardless of what the overlays claim.

Note one error carried in the stored description of this file: it labels the man
on stage as "Frank" and describes him in terms that do not match Mr. Turek, who
is the man holding the phone in the audience area. Do not rely on the stored
description for who is who.
