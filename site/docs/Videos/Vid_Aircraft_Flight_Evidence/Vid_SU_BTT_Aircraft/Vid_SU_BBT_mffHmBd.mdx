## What This Video Is

There is no narrator, no music, no caption and no annotation of any kind. Nobody argues anything. It is a record of a lookup — someone typed a registration into a tracker and filmed themselves scrolling the result.

## What The Footage Shows

For the first twenty-five seconds the flight list fills the whole frame and scrolls smoothly upward from late 2024 into October 2025. Each row carries a date, a callsign field — variously "N/A", "239", "SUBTT" or "EGY239" — and the origin and destination airport codes with departure and arrival icons.

The routes visible in the scroll cluster around Cairo (CAI) and the region: Muscat (MCT), Hurghada (HRG), Jeddah (JED), Medina (MED), El Arish (AAC). Interleaved with those are the legs that make this aircraft of interest to the investigation: Paris Le Bourget (LBG), and then a run of United States airports — Omaha (OMA), Lincoln (LNK), Minot (MOT), Provo (PVU), and Wilmington (ILG).

At 0:25 the scroll stops and the record card expands at the top of the screen. It identifies the aircraft as a Dassault Falcon 7X, type code FA7X, serial number 239, then eleven years old, Mode S code 0101D3, registered in Egypt, with the airline field reading "Egypt Government" and the operator field reading "Egypt - Air Force". A photograph credited to Augustin Piffard shows a white trijet with a red and black stripe and the Egyptian flag on the tail.

That card is the substance. It establishes, from a public database and without anyone's commentary, that the registration at the centre of a large part of this investigation belongs to a government-operated executive jet flown by the Egyptian Air Force — not a charter, not a private owner.

What the scroll does not do is establish anything about why any of those flights were made or who was aboard. Flight-tracking databases record transponder returns and filed movements. They do not carry passenger manifests, and for state aircraft they frequently do not carry the full picture at all: the site's own material records claims that some legs involving this registration were not visible to public trackers at departure. A route list is a route list.

The specific legs the investigation has focused on — the first-ever United States arrival in July 2025, the early-September presence at Provo, and the departure to Wilmington on September 10 — are the ones the flight rows in this scroll would have to be read against carefully, frame by frame, at a resolution this clip may not support.
