## What The Footage Shows

The clip opens on three text cards giving the aircraft registration N1098L, the
date September 10, 2025, and the airframe type, a Bombardier Global 6500. It
then cuts into a Flightradar24 browser session and plays the track of callsign
AXLE10 back over a map of Utah County.

The heart of it is a tight circling pattern flown directly over Utah Lake beside
Orem and Provo. An overlay states the aircraft descended to 4,975 feet indicated,
which the caption converts to roughly 600 feet above the lake surface, and slowed
to 118 knots. The map then zooms out to show the full leg, Kalispell in Montana
down to El Paso in Texas, before zooming back in to the loop. At about 0:55 the
Speed and Altitude Graph panel is opened at the bottom of the interface and the
telemetry dip is scrubbed through, which is the single most useful part of the
clip because it lets a viewer see the descent as a curve rather than as a claim.

From 1:50 the video leaves the map entirely. Three static screenshots of defense
trade coverage are shown in sequence, from The Defense Post, The Aviationist and
Breaking Defense, describing the US Army's acquisition of Bombardier Global 6500
jets fitted out for the High Accuracy Detection and Exploitation System, or HADES.

It is worth being precise about what those two halves establish and what they do
not. The first half shows a tracked aircraft flying low and slow over a lake near
a campus. The second half shows that the Army operates this airframe type in an
intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance role. The video places them next to
each other and lets the viewer close the gap. The captions do not show a HADES
mission tasking, an operator, a payload, or any connection between the aircraft
and the events at UVU that afternoon. Nothing on screen names a person.
