## What This Video Is

A 2 minute 40 second desktop screen recording, narrated throughout by a male voice, that
functions as a do-it-yourself tutorial rather than as an argument. The presenter
walks the viewer through pulling N1098L's archived track off Flightradar24,
exporting it as a KML file, and reopening it in Google Earth so the flight path
can be examined in three dimensions over the actual terrain. This copy is filed in the investigation archive under a generic name with no author credit. The same recording also circulates as a Blake Bednarz post dated to his original September 14, 2025 thread, and the transcript of the two files is word for word identical, so they should be understood as one video held twice rather than as two independent sources.

## What Is Said

The narration is instructional and step by step. It opens with the lookup:
"Go to Fly Radar 24 ... you're gonna want to type in November 1098 Lima. That's
a Bombardier Global 6500. Go to the archived flight section, scroll down to
September the 10th, and you're gonna want to download El Paso to Montana.
Download the KML file, okay? Do it for both of these."

At roughly 0:30 the Google Earth import lands and the tone shifts to
presentation: the export "brings up this really neat graphic that really shows
where this ISR spy jet was located." The presenter then makes the timing claim
that the whole exercise is built around. Of the outbound El Paso to Montana leg
he says, "This is 3 hours and 8 minutes before the assassination."

He does not ask the viewer to take the arithmetic on trust. He hands over the
raw timestamps and tells the audience to convert them independently: "Go ahead
and double check the timestamps on it ... ask ChatGPT, or you might be smart
enough to figure it out on your own. What time is 16:01 UTC in Mountain Daylight
Savings Time?" He repeats the instruction for the return leg with 18:48:28 UTC,
and supplies the anchor point: "Keep in mind that Charlie Kirk was assassinated
at 12:23." He also notes that clicking an individual timestamp in the track
yields heading, knots, altitude in feet and exact GPS coordinates, which is the
practical point of the whole walkthrough.

The last thirty seconds change subject. The presenter turns to two
Egyptian-registered aircraft. Of the first he says, "S-U-B-N-D. This Egyptian jet
flashed its ADS-B transponder on and off on the 10th, if someone could see what
that's about." Of the second: "this Egyptian jet, SU-BTT, flew out on the morning
of the 10th from Provo." Both are framed as open questions handed to the
audience, not as findings.

## What The Footage Shows

The first half minute is the Flightradar24 flight history table for N1098L, with
the cursor scrolling to the September 10 rows and the HADES branding overlay on
the opening frame. From about 0:30 to 2:17 the video is inside Google Earth. The
imported path renders as a dense chain of blue aircraft icons circling over Utah
Lake beside Provo and Orem, and the virtual camera orbits, tilts and pans so the
loop can be read against the Wasatch range, the lake shoreline and identifiable
ground features. The closing segment returns to Flightradar24 for the playback
pages of SU-BND, listed as a Gulfstream, and SU-BTT.

No people appear on camera and there is no music. What is actually demonstrated
is a method: how to get from a tail number to a verifiable three-dimensional
track. The narration's assassination framing rides on top of that method rather
than emerging from it, and the video shows no payload, no tasking and no
personnel.
