## What This Video Is

A 2 minute 6 second edited screen recording, circulated by John Cullen as a reply
in a thread with Sharyl Attkisson under a line about four UAVs on site "plus a
CIA spyplane." The clip is an assembled piece rather than a raw capture: title
cards, timed overlays, digital zooms and a scored soundtrack. It carries no
production credit in the copy held here, so it should be read as an anonymous
edit passed hand to hand, with the caveat that the tracking data underneath it is
independently checkable and the press screenshots at the end are real
publications.

There is no speech. The only audio is a low electronic synth drone running the
whole length, and the whisper transcript for the file returned nothing usable,
which is consistent with a video that has no voice on it. Every assertion in this
clip is a caption.

## What The Footage Shows

A title card opens on the registration and date, N1098L, September 10, 2025. The
video then enters Flightradar24 and stays there for roughly ninety seconds. It
first shows the track zoomed in on the Provo area, then pulls out to display the
whole leg from Kalispell, Montana to El Paso, Texas, then returns to the loop.

The load-bearing overlay reads, in the video's own words: "DESCENDING TO 600'
ABOVE LAKE UTAH, REDUCES SPEED TO JUST 118 KNOTS, ADJACENT TO UVU, 3 HOURS PRIOR
TO THE SHOOTING." From 0:54 the Speed and Altitude Graph panel is opened at the
bottom of the interface and the cursor scrubs across the low points, so the
descent and the speed reduction can be seen as a plotted curve rather than taken
on the caption's word. That is the most substantial thirty seconds in the clip.
From 1:21 the aircraft climbs back toward 37,000 feet and continues south.

From 1:49 the video leaves the map. A card states that the aircraft is one of two
HADES jets, and three static screenshots follow: The Defense Post on the second
Bombardier delivery for the Army's HADES program, The Aviationist on the program
generally, and The Defense Post again on the Bombardier spy plane conversion.

Set the argument against the frames. The frames establish a low, slow pass by a
tracked aircraft over a lake near a university campus, and separately establish
that the airframe type is used by the Army in an intelligence and reconnaissance
role. The captioned phrase "adjacent to UVU" is doing real work and deserves
scrutiny, because the loop is over water roughly two miles from the campus rather
than over it. The thread this clip was posted into also references drones on
site; the video itself shows no drones, no payload, no tasking order and no
people. The word "CIA" appears in the post's text, not anywhere in the footage.
