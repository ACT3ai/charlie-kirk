## What This Video Is

A five minute produced segment, the longest and most complete piece in this
cluster. It alternates between desktop screen recordings of Flightradar24 and
Google Earth, narrated by one voice, and short studio inserts in which a bearded
presenter in a grey suit delivers dramatic linking passages in front of a virtual
backdrop carrying a Charlie Kirk portrait. Two distinct narrators are audible,
the analytical one on the maps and a more theatrical one in the studio, which is
a signal that this is an assembled broadcast-style package rather than one
person's screen capture.

The opening frames are a housekeeping note aimed at the researcher audience:
large white text reading "I AM NOT TALKING ABOUT N888KG!", disambiguating this
aircraft from another registration circulating in the same conversation.

## What Is Said

The narrator states the subject immediately: "a particular jet and what it was
doing on September the 10th ... its registration number is November 1098 Lima. A
Bombardier Global 6500 that goes by callsign in the United States Army, and
publicly within air traffic communication, is AXLE10."

He then gives the backstory as he understands it, saying the airframe has been
fitted out for an Army program he names as Athena and HADES, and showing an Army
article on HADES modernising aerial military intelligence and a blog post on ISR
pilot training. His characterisation of the type: aircraft "that look like
private jets, but are far from private jets."

The substantive claims concern two passes. On the first, he says the flight
departed Biggs Army Airfield at El Paso for a Montana destination at 7:47 local,
and that on reaching the Provo area it lost significant altitude and speed and
spoke to the tower. He states he obtained the audio: "I've downloaded that
communication, where the pilot identifies himself as AXLE10 and requests a flyby
of the Provo airport, to then continue on to its destination in Montana. The
tower grants the request." He gives the indicated altitude as 5,425 feet and
converts it against Orem's ground elevation of 4,767 feet to arrive at "actually
600 feet", at 118 knots, which he characterises as near stall speed. He locates
this over the lake at Orem with UVU nearby.

On the second pass, after what he describes as fifty minutes on the ground in
Montana, he says the aircraft "returns to the scene of the crime, just in time,"
requests another Provo flyby, and at a timestamp of 12:48 Mountain is about two
miles from UVU at roughly 335 feet and about 130 knots. He then makes his central
inference explicitly: "This is not where they dispatched something with an
emergency order because Charlie was shot and they're trying to get assets in the
area quickly. This was set. This was pre-done."

He closes with an admission that is easy to miss and important to keep: "there's
really no way to get around the fact that this jet was flying in the area 3 hours
and 8 minutes before. I really don't have any explanation for this." He advances
no named culprit and no mechanism. He says the pattern is unexplained.

## What The Footage Shows

Flightradar24 flight history for N1098L; the Army and Bizjets of War articles
scrolled on screen; map playback of the El Paso to Kalispell leg with the cursor
zooming into Orem and Provo for speed and altitude readouts; the return leg
zoomed to the coordinates of the university; and from 4:30 a Google Earth session
with plotted markers and measurement lines drawn over 3D satellite imagery of
Orem. Studio inserts at 1:06 and 3:13.

Two things are shown and one is only asserted. The tracked altitudes, speeds and
times are visible on screen and can be re-derived by anyone who follows the same
steps. The tower communication is described but never played, so the pilot's
flyby request and the tower's grant rest entirely on the narrator's word here.
That distinction matters, because the tower exchange is what would turn a low
pass into a deliberate, cleared, repeated maneuver, and it is the one element the
video does not put in front of the viewer.
