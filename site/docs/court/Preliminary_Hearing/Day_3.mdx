# Preliminary Hearing — Day 3

*Verbatim record of public judicial proceedings in State of Utah v. [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) (Case No. 251403576). Robinson is charged, not convicted; testimony below is quoted, not adopted as fact.*

Day 3 fell at the midpoint of the week-long [preliminary hearing](/court/preliminary-hearing-motions) before [Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf) in the [Fourth District Court in Provo](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo). The State called its case agent, SBI **Agent [Brian Davis](/People/brian-davis)**, examined by prosecutor [Lauren Hunt](/People/lauren-hunt), who walked through [Robinson's](/People/tyler-robinson) September 11 surrender at the [Washington County Sheriff's Office](/Locations/Washington_County_Sheriff), his booking into the [Utah County Jail](/Locations/Utah_County_Jail), the search warrants, a live [.223 round found on a rooftop](/court/ballistics-atf-cbla), and the interviews of roommate [Lance Twiggs](/People/lance-twiggs) and family friend Mike Mitchell. The afternoon then turned into an extended legal fight — led by defense counsel Richard Novak against prosecutor Hunt — over whether the State could publicly broadcast [Twiggs' recorded 1102 video interview](/court/discord-evidence-court) and the [Discord and text messages](/Tyler_Robinson/Discord_Messages) inside it, invoking *Estes v. Texas* and Robinson's right to a fair trial.

**Speakers:** <span style={{color:'#1a1a1a', fontWeight:700}}>Judge Tony Graf</span> · <span style={{color:'#1b7a3d', fontWeight:600}}>Agent Brian Davis (witness, SBI)</span> · <span style={{color:'#17458f', fontWeight:600}}>Lauren Hunt (prosecution)</span> · <span style={{color:'#6a1b9a', fontWeight:600}}>Chad Grunander (prosecution)</span> · <span style={{color:'#c05a12', fontWeight:600}}>Richard Novak (defense)</span> · <span style={{color:'#555'}}>Ryan McBride, Michael Judd (media counsel), Jeffrey Neiman (Kirk family), and others (gray)</span>

## Rule 104 Foundations & the Gatekeeper Debate

<a href="/People/ryan-mcbride" style={{color:'#555', fontWeight:600}}>Ryan McBride</a> <span style={{color:'#777', fontSize:'0.8em'}}>(14:52)</span>: Ryan McBride for the state, Your Honor. Under rule 104(a), the court must decide preliminary questions about whether the witness is qualified or a privilege exists or evidence is admissible. In so deciding, the court is not bound by the rules of evidence except those on privilege. And I'm bringing this up again because it's likely to come up and it is going to affect the procedures we use as we go forward. The court holds two roles really at this stage.

<span style={{color:'#555', fontWeight:600}}>Ryan McBride</span> <span style={{color:'#777', fontSize:'0.8em'}}>(15:31)</span>: One is a gatekeeper role on admissibility and the second is the fact finder role as the magistrate.
