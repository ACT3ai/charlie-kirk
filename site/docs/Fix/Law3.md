# Law 3: Mandate the Investigation

## Overview

This proposed federal law requires the FBI and U.S. Intelligence Community to conduct a thorough, good-faith investigation into the death of Charlie Kirk — not merely release existing records, but actively pursue every lead.

## Key Provisions

- **Dedicated FBI team**: At least 20 agents with a $10 million independent budget, led by a senior agent with no prior case involvement.
- **175 Schedule A items**: Every item in Schedule A must be specifically investigated and reported on.
- **Independent external monitor**: Appointed by Congress with authority to direct investigators to pursue neglected leads.
- **Conflict-of-interest transfer**: If evidence emerges that a covered agency was itself involved in the death, investigation authority transfers to an independent congressional commission.
- **No interference**: Government officials are prohibited from directing, limiting, or terminating the investigation. Violations carry up to 15 years imprisonment.
- **Public reporting**: The investigation team must produce public progress reports every 90 days.

## Background

Disclosure laws alone are insufficient if agencies can comply by releasing documents while conducting no meaningful investigation. This law closes that gap by mandating investigative action, not just record production.
