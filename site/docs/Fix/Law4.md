# Law 4: Trusted Investigators

## Overview

This proposed federal law establishes independent investigation teams led by three Designated Trusted Investigators — Candace Owens, Dave Smith, and Tucker Carlson — to conduct civilian-led oversight of the Charlie Kirk investigation with full access to government files.

## Key Provisions

- **Six independent teams**: Each trusted investigator receives one FBI team and one Intelligence Community team.
- **Team selection authority**: Trusted investigators have sole authority to select all team members, bypassing normal government hiring and chain of command.
- **Full access**: Teams have access to all agency files, systems, and personnel. They may compel document production and conduct forensic audits.
- **Public release authority**: Trusted investigators have full authority to publicly release findings — no government official may block or delay publication.
- **Volunteer protections**: Government employees may volunteer for these teams confidentially, bypassing their chain of command, with full whistleblower protections.
- **Minimum 24 months**: Teams operate for at least 24 months and cannot be terminated by any government official.
- **90-day public reports**: Teams must publish reports every 90 days addressing all 175 Schedule A items.
- **Non-interference**: Interfering with these teams carries up to 15 years imprisonment.

## Background

Independent civilian oversight is the final safeguard when government agencies investigate themselves. By embedding trusted civilian investigators with direct access to agency resources and records, this law ensures that even if agencies comply technically with disclosure mandates, the actual findings reach the public.
