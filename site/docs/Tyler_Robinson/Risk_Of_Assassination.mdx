Attorney and radio commentator **Baron Coleman** has advanced one of the more alarming claims in the [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) narrative: that Robinson himself is in physical danger. In a clip shared July 13, 2026 by journalist Grace Ashford ([@IGraceAshford](/Influencers/podcasts-baron)), Coleman states that Robinson "is in grave danger because he was never supposed to survive this long," and that if the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) case ever reaches trial, the evidence will not hold.

These are Coleman's stated opinions and reported claims — presented here with attribution and not asserted by this site as fact. Tyler Robinson has been **charged but not convicted**, and no court has adjudicated whether he was framed, whether he acted alone, or whether he is in any danger.

## What Baron Coleman Said

According to the post by [@IGraceAshford](/Influencers/podcasts-baron):

> 🚨Baron Coleman: Tyler Robinson is in grave danger because he was never supposed to survive this long.
>
> If Charlie Kirk's murder case reaches trial, everything will fall apart because the evidence will not hold.

Coleman's argument, as stated in the video, is a chain of reasoning: because (in his view) the state cannot actually prove Robinson pulled the trigger, a full adversarial trial would expose the weakness of the case — and that exposure, not the verdict, is the real risk to whoever built the official story. In Coleman's framing, that makes Robinson's continued survival a problem for the narrative rather than an asset to it.

## Full Transcript

From video testimony by Baron Coleman, shared by [@IGraceAshford](/Influencers/podcasts-baron) on July 13, 2026. The 41-second clip is reproduced in full below:

> I think that there are two people who are in grave danger right now, unbelievably grave danger, and by grave I mean a danger of of being taken out physically from this earth. The first may be obvious. It's Tyler Robinson. Tyler Robinson's in grave danger. Tyler Robinson was not supposed to survive to turn himself in. I'm now convinced of that. Tyler Robinson was framed. I am now 100 convinced of that. But Tyler Robinson cannot go to trial. He cannot because they cannot prove he did it. Not only that, if he goes to trial, it's going to just blow the whole thing apart.

The clip cuts off immediately after Coleman identifies Robinson as the **first** of two people he says are in grave danger. He does not name the second person in this excerpt.

## How This Connects to the Broader Case

Coleman's "grave danger" claim does not stand alone — it rests on the timeline and evidentiary disputes he has raised elsewhere and that other investigators have echoed:

- **The custody-timeline conflict.** Coleman has repeatedly argued that Robinson was Mirandized at **6:25 PM on September 11** — roughly 90 minutes before the **~7:57 PM** Discord messages attributed to him — which he says means someone else may have typed the "confession." See the [Custody-vs-Confession Timeline Conflict](/CoverUp/Custody_Confession_Timeline).

- **The withheld surrender video.** A news organization reportedly requested footage of Robinson's surrender, which Washington County declined to provide and later said had been deleted under a retention policy. See [Surrender Video Not Publicly Released](/CoverUp/Sheriff_Video_Withheld).

- **The "cannot prove he did it" claim.** Coleman ties his danger warning to his view that the forensic case is weak — ballistics described as inconclusive and wound-profile questions that would be tested for the first time at trial. The official case against the charged suspect is laid out, with its documented challenges, on the [Theory: Tyler Robinson — Official Suspect](/Killer/tyler-robinson) page.

Whether any of this reflects a genuine threat to Robinson's safety, or is rhetorical framing by a commentator, is not something the public record resolves. What is documented is that Coleman — an attorney who has followed the case closely — has publicly and repeatedly stated the position, and that his audience of hundreds of thousands has amplified it.

**Status:** Tyler Robinson — Alive; in custody, awaiting trial (charged, not convicted).
