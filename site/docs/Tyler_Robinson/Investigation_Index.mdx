# Tyler Robinson Investigation Index

This is the working memo for the **official suspect** in the Charlie Kirk assassination.
The project's core claim is not that Tyler Robinson is innocent as a matter of law — he
has pleaded not guilty and no trial verdict exists — but that the **physical, timeline,
and forensic record** citizen investigators have assembled does not support the rooftop
.30-06 lone-shooter narrative the government has promoted. Nothing here claims that any
living person committed a crime or knew in advance that Charlie Kirk would be killed.

## Why this section leads with mismatch, not biography

Three investigative layers recur across [Charlie_Kirk.txt](/) source material and X research:

1. **Identity layer** — Campus camera subjects, the [Stairs Guy](./Stairs_Guy), and the
   all-black rooftop figure do not consistently match Tyler's described clothing, backpack
   fill level, or weapon-carry feasibility. See [Black Clothing Suspect](/Suspects/Black_Clothing_Suspect).
2. **Timeline layer** — The [25-second egress window](./Tyler_9_10_2025), [Dairy Queen sighting](./Travel),
   and [September 11 surrender](./Surrender) collide with the reported 6:25 PM Miranda time
   and the 7:57 PM [Discord messages](./Discord_Messages).
3. **Forensics layer** — The ATF called the bullet fragment **inconclusive** relative to the
   attributed [Mauser rifle](./Weapon_Rifle); the [shaped-charge](/Gun_Bullet/overview) and
   [mic theories](/Mic/overview) offer an alternative cause of death that would exonerate
   Robinson on ballistics grounds if confirmed.

## The "two versions of the text messages" theory (claim)

One of the more detailed reconstructions preserved in Charlie_Kirk.txt argues the released text messages exist in **two conflicting versions**, attributed to online investigators (Baron Coleman and others):

- **Version 1.0** — texts reportedly divulged to the FBI by **Lance Twiggs at ~1 a.m. September 12** (the night Robinson turned himself in), in which Tyler allegedly says he will call police to "**go out on my own terms**" — framed as the paper trail for a planned shootout.
- **Version 2.0** — texts in the **September 16 indictment** in which Tyler allegedly says "**I'm gonna turn myself in willingly.**"

The theory holds that the shift between the two reflects a plan that was disrupted when Robinson surrendered rather than dying in a shootout. This is a **reported interpretation**, not an established fact, and is included as one of the alternative-narrative threads the section tracks.

## The "already in custody" official-knowledge claim

The [Impossible Timeline](./Tyler_9_10_2025) and [Surrender](./Surrender) threads rest on a specific conflict recorded in the master file: court documents reportedly show Robinson was **Mirandized at 6:25 p.m. on September 11**, yet the **7:57 p.m. Discord "confession"** and the **7:58–8:00 p.m. press conference** (which released "enhanced" stairwell photos and rooftop video) both occurred **after** he was reportedly already in custody. The file lists roughly a **dozen officials** it says would have known this, including **FBI Director Kash Patel, Deputy Director Dan Bongino, SAIC Robert Bohls, Governor Spencer Cox, Lt. Gov. Deidre Henderson, Utah County Sheriff Mike Smith, Washington County Sheriff Nate Brooksby, and retired detective Scott Messinger.** The file also notes **Sheriff Brooksby resigned on March 27, 2026**. All of this is presented as a reported timeline discrepancy and a disclosure demand, not as an accusation that any named person committed a crime.

## Open disclosure targets

Investigators continue to call for:

- Full UVU, Ring, WYZE, and TPUSA camera archives with chain of custody
- Washington County lobby CCTV and deputy bodycam from the surrender intake
- Discord forensic extractions with authentication metadata (not screenshot photos)
- ATF/FBI VCM examination with defense observer or videotape
- Cell-site location records for the Orem–St. George route on September 10–11

The [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) are framed as one path toward that disclosure.
