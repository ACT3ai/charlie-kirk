# Israel Google Searches for Medical People

This page documents an **unverified investigative claim** advanced by citizen investigators: that the medical examiner, hospital, and surgeons later connected to Charlie Kirk's case were reportedly searched online from Israeli IP addresses in the weeks **before September 10, 2025**. The significance, if the claim holds, would rest entirely on the *pattern* — not on the conduct of any medical person named below.

## The Claim

Citizen investigators studying Google Trends-type search data claim they observed a sudden, geographically anomalous spike in searches for the medical figures tied to Charlie Kirk's case. According to these investigators, the searches reportedly originated from Israeli IP addresses rather than from Washington, DC or Utah — the two places where ordinary interest in these people would be expected.

The broader assertion is that foreign IPs allegedly "scouted EVERY angle pre-assassination," with the medical staff said to be just one cluster among many that were reportedly searched in advance.

Much of this search-data analysis is attributed to **Baron Coleman** (via YouTube/podcast), amplified on X by accounts including **@TheSCIF**, **@BasedSamParker**, **@Queenbee_12_**, **@Mronlinereality**, **@janlove35**, **@LikeThinkKills**, **@katrichski**, **@PainDogbrillo**, **@MZakkiel**, **@malteselove67**, and **@johnmcvicar16**. Importantly, the same investigators say the searches reportedly came from **both Israeli and Washington, DC IP ranges** — the DC angle is part of the claim, not just the Israeli one. They also place the medical searches inside a larger reported set that allegedly included non-medical figures (the Losee Center, Tyler Robinson, the suspect's public defender, judge **Tony F. Graf**, Mikey McCoy, Phil Lyman, Erika Kirk, and event questioners). Those non-medical search claims are covered in the site's broader Israel and Google-searches sections; this page stays on the **medical** people.

## Medical Figures Reportedly Searched

**Dr. Deirdre Amaro**, the chief medical examiner, was reportedly "barely searched in DC or Utah... but ISRAEL? Sudden interest," according to citizen investigators.

A group of **seven Timpanogos surgeons** were allegedly searched in what investigators describe as a roughly two-week frenzy spanning **mid-to-late July 2025**:

Separately, **Andrew Zenger** — who reportedly joined the surgical team on **August 28, 2025** — is claimed to have had his name searched from an Israeli IP on **August 27, 2025**, the day before.

Investigators also report that institutional searches for **"Timpanogos Regional Hospital"** (variously spelled "Timpanogas") and **"Intermountain Health"** appeared from both DC and Israel in the same window. One post additionally claimed a link between **Miriam Adelson's private family jets** and the timing of "17 instances" of Israeli-origin search spikes on Kirk-related hospital/doctor queries — an especially speculative assertion that this site cannot verify and presents only as a reported claim.

## Methodology Caveat — Read This First

This claim has **not been independently verified or established in court**, and the methodology behind it is unproven. Read these limitations before drawing any conclusion:

- The attributions come from **citizen analysis of Google Trends-type data**, not from official records.
- **Google Trends does not publicly expose per-search IP addresses.** The platform reports relative search interest by region, not the IP address behind any individual query. The leap from "search interest" to a specific **"Israeli IP address"** is a methodology that has not been documented, peer-reviewed, or validated.
- No primary data, raw dataset, or reproducible procedure for the "Israeli IP" attribution has been published for independent review.
- Dates and spikes listed above are **as reported by the investigators making the claim** and have not been confirmed by Google, the hospital, or any court.

Treat everything on this page as an **alleged, unverified pattern claim** — interesting if true, but currently unproven.

## Why Investigators Find It Significant

Investigators argue that the significance, if the data were real, would lie in the **pattern** rather than any single search:

- Many distinct hospital figures — the medical examiner, seven surgeons, and a late-addition surgeon — were reportedly searched within the same narrow window.
- The alleged search for Andrew Zenger **the day before** he reportedly joined the surgical team is cited as the most striking item, because it would suggest foreknowledge of a staffing change.
- The institutional searches for the hospital and health system are said to fit a broader picture in which foreign IPs allegedly "scouted EVERY angle" of the case in advance.

This page presents that reasoning as the investigators' argument — not as an established finding. See the cross-investigation context at [Israeli Search Patterns Claims](/Israel/Israeli_Search_Patterns_Claims).

## No Accusation Against the Medical Staff

To be explicit: **none of the medical people named on this page are accused of any wrongdoing.** The claim concerns *who was allegedly searching for them*, not anything they themselves did. Dr. Deirdre Amaro, the Timpanogos surgeons, Andrew Zenger, and every other named individual are presumed innocent of any involvement and are referenced here solely as the *subjects* of the alleged searches — that is, as potential targets of others' interest, not as actors. Nothing on this page should be read as suggesting any of them acted improperly.
