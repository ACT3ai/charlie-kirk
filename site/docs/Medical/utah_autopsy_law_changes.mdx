# Utah Autopsy Law Changes

In 2025 Utah enacted **SB0082**, the **"Autopsy Photo Amendments"** bill, which restricts how autopsy photographs in a medical examiner's record may be shared. Because the law took effect months before Charlie Kirk's death, citizen investigators have raised questions about its timing — while acknowledging that restricting graphic autopsy imagery is a common and defensible policy goal.

## SB0082 — What the Law Does

SB0082 criminalizes the **knowing sharing, publication, or distribution of autopsy photographs** that are part of a medical examiner's record. The offense is a **class B misdemeanor**, unless the photograph is already in the public domain.

The bill also tightens the rules governing the release of medical examiner records more broadly — both autopsy **reports** and **photographs** — so that access is limited to defined categories of people under specific conditions.

## Timeline of the Bill

* **January 9, 2025** — SB0082 introduced into the Utah legislature.
* **May 7, 2025** — The law became **effective**.
* **September 10, 2025** — Charlie Kirk killed at Utah Valley University, roughly **four months after** the law took effect.

The bill predates the case by months, and legislation of this kind has been pursued in other contexts as well.

## Who Can Access ME Records

The amendments establish strict rules for releasing medical examiner records (autopsy reports and photographs), generally limiting access to:

* **Immediate relatives** of the deceased
* **Legal representatives**
* **Physicians**
* **Law enforcement**
* **Researchers** meeting specific conditions

## The Drafting Attorney Search Claim

The bill's drafting attorney was **Greg Gunn**. To be clear: drafting legislation is **lawful, ordinary professional work**, and Greg Gunn is **not accused of any wrongdoing** here. His role is noted only because it became the subject of a citizen-investigator claim.

According to that claim, **unique Google searches for Greg Gunn reportedly originated from Israeli IP addresses** on two occasions: once in **August 2024** — months before the bill was introduced — and again in **May 2025**, reportedly the same week the law took effect. Investigators framed the timing as suspicious, summarizing it as "Google Trends strikes again."

This Israeli-IP search claim is an **unverified citizen-investigator assertion**. The underlying methodology for attributing search activity to specific national IP ranges has not been independently confirmed, and the claim should be treated as reported allegation rather than established fact.

## Caveats

* Restricting the public sharing of **graphic autopsy photographs** is a common, defensible policy goal and has precedent independent of this case.
* The law was introduced and took effect **before** September 10, 2025, so its existence is not itself evidence of foreknowledge.
* The Israeli-IP search claim relies on **unverified methodology** and is attributed to citizen investigators, not to any confirmed forensic source.
* Greg Gunn and any legislators involved are presumed alive and are **not accused of wrongdoing**; describing their work is not an allegation against them.
