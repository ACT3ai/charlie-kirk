Medical and forensic questions are central to this investigation because they can confirm or challenge the official account of how Charlie Kirk died. The direction, distance, and mechanism of the fatal wound; the timing and adequacy of emergency care; and whether the physical evidence matches a rifle shot from the east are all medical questions. Because no autopsy report has been publicly released, these threads are debated through video, photographs, and unofficial descriptions rather than a settled medical record.

The direct pages above break the area into focused questions. Several concern **people**: the doctors and surgeons, the hospital's newly appointed leader, the state medical examiner, the broader pattern of staff changes, and the claim that these medical figures were searched from foreign IP addresses before the event. Others concern **what happened**: Charlie's trip to the hospital, the events once he arrived, and questions about hospital surveillance footage. A third group concerns the **autopsy and the law**: Utah's 2025 autopsy-photo law, the fact that no autopsy report has been released, how the recovered fragments intersect with the Tyler Robinson trial, and the carefully labeled bullet claims attributed to hospital personnel. The grouped **Autopsy** and **Hospital** sections, plus the ballistics, lapel-mic, construction, and digital-forensics pages, round out the area.

Readers new to this area should begin with **Autopsy**, since the absence of a public autopsy report is the gap that drives nearly every other forensic dispute. From there, **Ballistics & Wound Analysis** and **Lapel Mic & Audio Theories** lay out the competing mechanism-of-injury arguments, while **Hospital** addresses what happened to Charlie after he was shot. Throughout, claims are attributed to their sources and labeled as documented, interpreted, or unverified.
