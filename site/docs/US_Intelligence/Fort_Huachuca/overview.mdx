# Fort Huachuca

**Fort Huachuca** is a U.S. Army installation at **Sierra Vista, Cochise County, Arizona**, and it is
the single most-cited military location in the citizen investigation into the September 10, 2025
killing of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) at Utah Valley University. The reason is a compressed
36-hour window: a reported closed-door meeting of roughly a dozen lieutenant colonels on the base,
a U.S. Air Force VIP jet that appeared there and left, and a walk-in witness who says he was detained
and questioned — all in the two days before the shooting. This page is the site's hub for that
material. Everything below is presented as **attributed claims, flight-tracking readings, and
open records questions** — not as findings. No court has found that Fort Huachuca, the U.S. Army,
or any person named here planned, ordered, or assisted an assassination.

## What Fort Huachuca actually is

Fort Huachuca was founded in **1877** as a cavalry post during the Apache campaigns and later became a
long-term home of the **Buffalo Soldiers**. Its modern role is what matters here. The installation
hosts the **U.S. Army Intelligence Center of Excellence (USAICoE)** — the Army's schoolhouse for
military intelligence — and is the home of the **Military Intelligence Corps**. It also hosts the
Army's **counterintelligence** training pipeline, **NETCOM** (Network Enterprise Technology Command),
the **Joint Interoperability Test Command**, and a large restricted electromagnetic test range used
for electronic-warfare and antenna work. Its airfield is **Libby Army Airfield (ICAO KFHU)**.

Two facts about the base drive nearly every claim on this page. First, it is where the Army teaches
**intelligence and counterintelligence tradecraft** — investigation notes circulated on X describe
roughly **12,000 counterintelligence personnel** trained there per year, a figure this site has not
independently verified. Second, Sierra Vista is a small town of roughly **45,000 people** that exists
largely to serve the post, which is why researchers treat geographic search-trend data from that ZIP
code as a proxy for on-base interest.

None of that is secret or sinister on its own. An Army intelligence school trains intelligence
soldiers; general officers and civilian defense executives visit it; VIP aircraft carry them. The
investigation's question is narrower: **why this base, in this window, with these people** — and why
the records that would settle it have not been released.

## The SAM 99-0404 flight

The aircraft at the center of the window is a **U.S. Air Force C-37A Gulfstream V** VIP transport,
tail **99-0404**, flown by the 89th Airlift Wing out of
**[Joint Base Andrews](/Planes/Airport-Joint-Base-Andrews)**. Different trackers logged its callsign
variously as **[SAM-702](/Planes/SAM-702)**, **[SAM-000](/Planes/SAM-000)**, and
**[SAM-112](/Planes/SAM-112)** — "SAM" being Special Air Mission, the prefix used for senior
executive-branch and congressional travel.

The most-cited primary document behind this lane is a **tipster email dated December 10, 2025** to
[Candace Owens](/Influencers/podcasts-candace-owens)' tip line, subject line "Flights - Fort Huachuca
Sept 9, 2025." The writer, who describes themselves as a military tipster, traces the Andrews →
Colorado Springs → Tucson → dark → Fort Huachuca → El Paso → Andrews routing and adds the line
investigators quote most often: **"This is the ONLY time I've noticed this plane be in Fort
Huachuca."** The same email notes the aircraft appeared to leave at about **11:43 AM on the 9th** and
observes that a cabinet official had flown on the same tail roughly five months earlier — offered as
evidence of the aircraft's VIP tasking, not as a claim about that official.

A single tipster's screenshots are not an authenticated flight record. ADS-B gaps are common and
routine for military aircraft; callsign changes between RCH and SAM reflect mission type, not
concealment; and a jet that seats twelve carries passengers whose identities are simply not public.
The full aircraft dossier is at [SAM 99-0404](/Planes/SAM-99-0404) and the airfield page at
[Libby Army Airfield](/Planes/Airport-Fort-Huachuca).

## Bradley Hansell and the July 2025 personnel chain

[Candace Owens](/Influencers/podcasts-candace-owens) alleges that one of the **four passengers** on the VIP
flight that left Fort Huachuca on the morning of September 9, 2025 was
**[Bradley D. Hansell](/People/bradley-hansell)** — the Trump administration's **Under Secretary of Defense (War)
for Intelligence and Security**. That passenger identification is Owens's allegation; no released manifest
confirms it, and it is not asserted here as fact. Hansell's publicly reported background is Army Special Forces, **Boston Consulting Group**, the
National Security Council, a return to BCG, and then Senate confirmation to the Pentagon's top
intelligence post.

The investigation notes place that appointment inside a specific July 2025 sequence:

* **July 11, 2025** — **Dustin Gard-Weiss**, who had been performing the duties of Under Secretary of
  Defense for Intelligence and Security, is announced as **executive director of the CIA**.
* **July 22, 2025** — the Senate confirms **Bradley Hansell** into the office Gard-Weiss vacated.
* Owens' account states that it was **Hansell's office that booked** the Andrews → Colorado Springs →
  Tucson itinerary, before the callsign changed to SAM on the Fort Huachuca leg.
* An article cited in the same thread reports Hansell **visited Joint Task Force South Border
  leadership in Colorado Springs** — described as the same unit the walk-in witness says he
  encountered at Huachuca the following day.

Investigation notes further state that Hansell's portfolio includes the Army's **[HADES](/US_Intelligence/US_Army_HADES)**
ISR program, "run out of Biggs" — the same [Biggs Army Airfield](/Planes/Airport-Biggs-Army-Airfield)
from which [N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview) departed on September 10. That is the link investigators
use to connect the Arizona meeting to the jet over Utah. It is an **organizational** link — a senior
official oversees a program — not evidence that any tasking existed.

Hansell is a living public official. Nothing on this page asserts that he did anything wrong,
knew anything in advance, or had any involvement in the assassination. His name appears because a
publicly identified cabinet-level intelligence official's travel is a matter of legitimate public
interest and because the manifest that would confirm or dispose of the claim has not been released.

## The walk-in witness and the military-police incident report

The account that turned Fort Huachuca from a flight-tracking curiosity into a central thread comes
from a military witness identified publicly as **[Mitch Snow](/People/mitch-snow)** (also rendered
Michael / "Harry Myers" in earlier retellings). As summarized on
[Candace Owens](/Influencers/podcasts-candace-owens)' program and in compiled notes, Snow says he
walked into a closed, high-clearance meeting at Fort Huachuca on the morning of **September 9, 2025**
containing **12 or more lieutenant colonels**, was taken outside and questioned at length by an
officer he identifies as a **"Captain Neff,"** and was then reported to the military police as a
possible bomb threat.

On **December 23, 2025**, Owens presented what she described as the **military-police incident
report** for that morning, arguing it corroborated Snow's presence on base against a coordinated
effort to discredit him. As summarized by [@TheSCIF](/Influencers/x/thescif), the document allegedly
records: an **11:17 AM** tip from a warrant officer about a suspicious individual at **Southwest
Border Mission Headquarters, Building #70525**; a gray Chevrolet Equinox with a California plate;
named responding officers and a sergeant; a partial building evacuation; a **negative** K-9 explosive
sniff on the bag, vehicle, and building; and investigators taking Snow to the MP office.

Owens' own framing is important and is repeated here because it is the honest limit of the claim:
she says the report supports **that he was there and was treated as a bomb-threat problem**, while
stating that **who he believes he saw leaving the meeting remains unconfirmed**. This site has not
seen an authenticated, chain-of-custody copy of the report and does not certify it. Names, plate
numbers, building numbers, and the document's authenticity should all be treated as claims pending
primary records. Full treatment: [The Fort Huachuca JTF Meeting](/Suspicious/US_Intel/fort-huachuca-jtf-meeting).

## The "Captain Neff" question

Snow's account names the interrogating officer as **Captain Neff**, elsewhere given as
**[Adam Neff](/People/adam-neff)** of Cookeville, Tennessee. Commentary then connects that name to
**[Blake Neff](/People/blake-neff)** — described in the notes as a longtime Charlie Kirk producer and
a former **wargames specialist for the U.S. Marine Corps War College (reportedly 2016–2020)**, a role
that involved running simulations with colonels and lieutenant colonels — and to a
**[Lt. Col. Daniel Neff](/People/daniel-neff)** described as a relative.

This is the weakest-supported and most-repeated part of the cluster, and it deserves to be labeled
as such. **Neff is a common surname.** A shared last name between an officer a witness recalls and a
media staffer with a war-college background is a coincidence unless a personnel record links them.
No such record has been produced. Every person named in this section is alive and none has been
charged with or found to have committed any offense connected to the assassination.

## The Cabot Phillips carpet comparison

In January 2026 the X account **[@AMeadowInquiry](/Influencers/x/ameadowinquiry)** posted a
side-by-side visual argument that the **carpet pattern, dark base trim, and yellow wall** visible in
an alibi video posted by **[Cabot Phillips](/People/cabot-phillips)** matched interior
photographs the poster identified as Fort Huachuca rooms.

The site's own caveat is the one that matters: **government-contract interior finishes repeat across
an enormous number of federal buildings**, and no metadata or independent geolocation has been
published for either set of images. It is a documented public claim, logged as a claim. Cabot
Phillips is alive, has [publicly denied the claim](/People/cabot-phillips), and this site makes no assertion that he was at Fort Huachuca or has any
relationship with any intelligence organization. The full write-up, the mirrored video, and the

## The "signals shop" and shape-charge lead

On her program, [Candace Owens](/Influencers/podcasts-candace-owens) said she had begun **receiving
emails** claiming that Fort Huachuca **"has a department that works in signals"** and that such a
shop **"could have put together this rigged mic."** She framed it explicitly as a lead rather than a
finding — *"if I find out they assemble things like this at Fort Huachuca … things are going to get a
lot more interesting."*

[Stew Peters](/Influencers/podcasts-stew-peters) extended the lane in a June 2026 segment, arguing
that an Israeli-owned defense contractor with a research and manufacturing facility in **Chandler,
Arizona** contracts with the U.S. government **on Fort Huachuca** and works on **shaped-charge**
explosives, and connecting that to the **[AES factory explosion](/US_Intelligence_Assisted/aes-factory-explosion)**
in McEwen, Tennessee and a reported low-and-slow overflight of the smoldering site by the same tail,
99-0404. Those are Peters' allegations, presented here as his framing.

The step from **"an intelligence school teaches signals and electronic warfare"** to **"a device was
built there"** is a leap nobody has documented. Signals intelligence, electronic warfare, and antenna
test ranges are the base's advertised mission; institutional **capability is not involvement**. The
same caution governs the electromagnetic thread at
[Fort Huachuca EM Expertise](/Electrocution/fort-huachuca-em-expertise) and the device question at
[the microphone analysis](/Mic/overview).

## Personnel and photo claims

A separate class of claims puts specific individuals on or near the base. All are unverified and are
recorded to show what is circulating, not adopted:

* A **February 2022** screenshot exchange in which **[Jack Posobiec](/People/jack-posobiec)** mentions
  Army training trips to Fort Huachuca and **[Tyler Bowyer](/People/tyler-bowyer)** replies "jack stop
  going to huachuca." Posobiec's Navy intelligence background is publicly documented; the exchange
  predates the assassination by more than three years and establishes nothing about it.
* An open question — explicitly **unresolved** — about whether a man identified online as
  **[Cooper Brown](/People/cooper-brown)** is the same person as **[Jeb Jacobi](/People/jeb-jacobi)** in
  a Fort Huachuca **MWR** (Morale, Welfare & Recreation) community photo. The counterpoint is on the
  record: the photo shows young men in automotive-dealership shirts at what looks like a base
  recreation-center giveaway, which does not establish any Army or intelligence affiliation.
* A note, flagged in the source file as needing verification, that a brother of
  **[Zach Qureshi](/People/zane-qureshi)** "specialized in drones at Fort Huachuca."
* A claim that a man recorded on camera at a witness's residence, **[Kyle Saltz](/People/kyle-saltz)**,
  is a Fort Huachuca-trained counterintelligence operative — an allegation about a living person
  that this site does not adopt.

Every individual named in this section is alive. None has been charged with or convicted of anything
connected to the assassination, and the presence of a person in a photograph on a public Army post
is not evidence of wrongdoing.

## Search-trend claims

Researcher **Ana Escobar** ([@AnaEscobarShow](/Influencers/x/anaescobarshow)), presenting on
[Stew Peters](/Influencers/podcasts-stew-peters)' program in July 2026, walked through Google image
search-trend data geolocated to **Sierra Vista, Arizona**, arguing that searches for named DIA,
CENTCOM, weapons-program, and Kirk-security figures spiked repeatedly in the weeks and days before
September 9, 2025. Peters then claimed that **the day after** the segment aired, results for every
person the show named were **wiped**, while unnamed people still returned results.

The named-vs-unnamed contrast is the only control offered, and it is a weak one: Google Trends
suppresses low-volume queries by design, and a segment that drives traffic to a term changes that
term's own data. Without an archived before-state the claim is effectively unfalsifiable. Both sides
are laid out at [Pre-Sept 9 Search Spikes](/GoogleSearches/fort-huachuca-sept-9-searches) and
[Image Results Reportedly Wiped](/GoogleSearches/fort-huachuca-image-searches).

## Counterarguments

The strongest case against the Fort Huachuca cluster is not that any single item is fabricated — it
is that every item has an ordinary explanation that has never been ruled out:

* **Lieutenant colonels meet at the Army's intelligence school.** That is what a schoolhouse and a
  headquarters do. A dozen O-5s in a room in Arizona is a Tuesday, not an anomaly.
* **VIP jets carry VIPs.** A confirmed Under Secretary touring commands in his first weeks in office
  is a routine familiarization itinerary, and it was reported publicly at the Colorado Springs stop.
* **ADS-B gaps are normal** for military aircraft and are not evidence of concealment.
* **A walk-in on a secure installation triggering an MP response** is exactly what should happen. The
  bomb-dog sweep came back **negative**, which is consistent with a misunderstanding, not an operation.
* **Interior-finish matching is weak identification science.**
* **The cluster is contested inside alternative media itself.**
  [Alex Jones](/Influencers/podcasts-alex-jones) has publicly dismissed the Fort Huachuca thread, and
  several other investigators list it among theories they consider dead ends. That internal split is
  a real data point.
* **No court, inspector general, or congressional finding** connects Fort Huachuca to the killing.
  The prosecution's case remains a lone-suspect case, and the charged suspect has not been convicted.

## Open questions

1. Full **passenger manifests** for tail 99-0404 on September 7–9, 2025, with callsign assignments.
2. **Visitor logs and conference-room bookings** for Fort Huachuca on September 8–9, 2025.
3. An **authenticated copy** of the September 9, 2025 military-police incident report, with chain of
   custody, rather than a summarized screenshot.
4. The **official DoD public-affairs itinerary** for the Under Secretary that week.
5. Whether any **Joint Task Force** unit was in fact reassigned or stood down afterward, on what
   orders, and with what personnel actions.
6. Whether the officer the witness recalls as "Captain Neff" exists in the **Army personnel record**
   for that date and unit, and whether any documented relationship to other named individuals exists.
7. Independent **geolocation** — metadata, not carpet — for the alibi-video footage.
8. Which contractors hold **research and test contracts on the installation**, and whether any touch
   the device questions raised elsewhere in this investigation.

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

Every open question above is a records question, and every one of them is answerable by a document
that already exists inside the government. Flight manifests, base visitor logs, military-police
reports, unit personnel actions, and installation contract records are precisely the categories the
[Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) are drafted to force into public view — in
particular the [FBI & DOJ disclosure law](/laws/DoJ_FBI/Law_1_DoJ_FBI) and the
[intelligence disclosure law](/laws/US_Intel/Law_2_US_Intel). Until they are released, Fort Huachuca stays exactly where
it is: a **timing and institutional lead**, not a proven staging base.
