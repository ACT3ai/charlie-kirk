# US Army HADES (Drone Launching Planes)

<img src="/img/evidence/65e041584366dd6d921c9a23f35edb56a3efcfbd20b5a24399e081ed7f8e11d9.jpg"
  style={{float:'right', width:'48%', marginLeft:'1.5rem', marginBottom:'1rem'}}
  alt="US Army and Bombardier Defense rendering of the future HADES ISR aircraft based on the Global 6500 business jet" />

**HADES** — **High Accuracy Detection and Exploitation System** — is the U.S. Army's
program-of-record for a new fleet of intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance (ISR)
jets built on the **Bombardier Global 6500** airframe. It is a real, publicly announced,
publicly contracted program. That matters here for one reason: the aircraft at the center
of this investigation's flight thread, **[N1098L](/Planes/N1098L/overview)**, is a Global 6500,
and public defense-trade reporting has separately described Army interest in **launching
small drones from that same airframe**.

Everything downstream of those two facts is contested. This page separates what is
documented about the program from what is only alleged about September 10, 2025.

## What HADES is

HADES is intended to replace and consolidate the Army's earlier prototype ISR jets —
**ARTEMIS**, **ARES** and **ATHENA** — with a single higher, faster, longer-legged platform.
Per the case notes, the program is run out of **Biggs Army Airfield (KBIF)** at Fort Bliss,
El Paso, Texas, and is associated with **[Bradley Hansell](/People/bradley-hansell)**, described
in reporting as Under Secretary of War for Intelligence. Contractors named across the
program's reporting include **Bombardier** (airframe), **Sierra Nevada Corporation** (mission
conversion), **Leidos** and **SAIC**.

The mission set for this class of aircraft is described on the
[ISR Operations](/Planes/ISR-Operations) page: signals intelligence (SIGINT), imagery
intelligence (IMINT), synthetic aperture radar (SAR), communications relay, and — the
contested item — drone deployment and recovery.

Two dated milestones anchor the program, both preserved as screenshots of *The Defense Post*
in the [Aircraft Articles photo cluster](/Photos/Aircraft/Articles/overview):

| Date | Reported | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **November 27, 2024** | "Bombardier has delivered the first Global 6500 aircraft" to the US Army HADES program | *The Defense Post*, Christine Casimiro |
| **September 10, 2025** | "SNC Accepts Second Bombardier Jet for US Army HADES Spy Plane Program" — Sierra Nevada Corporation receives airframe #2 for conversion | *The Defense Post*, Rojoef Manuel |

The second date is the day of the shooting. That is a coincidence of publishing schedules
unless something connects them, and nothing on this site connects them. It is recorded
because it is the kind of detail that gets discovered later and treated as concealed.

The first HADES prototype, tail **N808LP**, was photographed at the Bombardier Defense facility
in Wichita, Kansas on **November 25, 2024**. Its FAA registration shows a 2023 **BD-700-1A10**,
serial **60116**, in the **Experimental / Normal** airworthiness class — a detail that becomes
important in the rebuttal section below, because N1098L is serial **60098** in the standard class.

## The air-launched drone reporting — and its deletion

<img src="/img/evidence/1f0ebf9d7cca6d6cb6eb8a5360f8a0bc7eee225f610efb7c12e64413feca7ed0.jpg"
  style={{float:'right', width:'48%', marginLeft:'1.5rem', marginBottom:'1rem'}}
  alt="The War Zone headline about Army air-launched drone trials over a photo of a modified Global Express with a red circle drawn on the ventral fairing" />

On **January 9, 2025**, *The War Zone* (TWZ) published an article by Thomas Newdick headlined
**"Army Wants To Test Air-Launched Drones From A Global Express Business Jet."** Its standfirst
read: *"The planned trials tally with previous comments about launching the drones from the new
Global 6500-based HADES surveillance jet."*

That is the origin of the phrase "drone launching planes" in this investigation. It is not a
citizen-investigator invention — it is defense-trade reporting on a stated Army intention.

After the story began circulating in connection with N1098L and September 10, 2025, the URL
`twz.com/air/army-wants-to-test-air-launched-drones-from-a-global-express-business-jet`
**stopped resolving**. The [Cover Up photo cluster](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/overview) preserves
both states: a full-page capture of the live article, and a full-page capture of the 404 that
replaced it. Thirteen [before/after slides](/Photos/Aircraft/Cover_Up/Cover_Versions/overview)
circulating on X pair the two side by side under the banner "Cover Up in Charlie Kirk."

The site does not adopt the slides' wording. Those slides assert the removal *is* proof. The
careful reading is narrower and is the one used here: **the article existed, it now returns a
404, and no explanation for the change has been published.** Ordinary explanations — a URL
change, a CMS migration, an editorial retraction — have not been ruled out. What defeats the
strongest version of the suppression claim is that a **second outlet, Aeronaut Media,
independently reported the same Army drone-launch tests**, and that capture is still on this
site. The capability reporting survived the deletion.

**N1098L** is a Bombardier Global 6500 (ICAO hex **A0299E**, serial 60098, previously registered
N911PV), operated by **[LASAI Aviation II LLC](/TPUSA/LLCs/lasai-aviation-ii-llc)**, a contractor

(rendered **AXEL10** in some captures), departing **[Biggs Army Airfield](/Planes/Airport-Biggs-Army-Airfield)**
at roughly **07:48 MDT**.

Two low-altitude passes near Orem and Utah Lake are the entire basis of the Utah claim. The

## The rebuttal case

This section is not a formality. The strongest arguments against the HADES drone theory are
technical, and several of them are hard to answer.

**The airframe was not built out for it.** [@tjphager](/Influencers/x/tjphager), the most-cited
skeptic in this thread, argues N1098L carried **interior equipment only, with no visible exterior
modifications** — no radomes, antennas or sensor blisters matching the full HADES **ME-11B**
contracted specification that a real drone-launch or collection platform would show. On this
reading the aircraft is a trainer or survey configuration, not a mission jet.

**Renderings are being mistaken for the aircraft.** The comparison image above makes the point
directly: the sleek grey jet circulating in the slide decks is an **artist's rendering of a future
fully-equipped HADES**, not a photograph of N1098L, which is plain and white.

**The "stall speed" framing is aerodynamically wrong.** A Global 6500's stall speed at approach
weights is comfortably **above** 116 knots. An aircraft actually at its stall speed is not making a
controlled photographic pass — it is falling.

**The timing cannot work if the flight was reactive.** No fixed-wing aircraft can receive news of a
shooting, descend from altitude, set up an approach and execute a 335-foot pass within thirty
seconds. A pre-planned profile that happens to bracket an event is the null hypothesis; a scheduled
pair of passes at 11:47 and 12:24 is a training sortie, and the event landed between them.

**It was broadcasting.** The aircraft flew with its transponder on, visible to every hobbyist tracker
in the country. That is not how a covert operation is conducted.

**And the geography is ordinary.** Utah Lake sits beneath normal arrival and training corridors for
Provo and Salt Lake. "Within two miles of UVU" describes a two-mile radius drawn around a busy
campus in a busy valley.

An October 1, 2025 defense-oriented analysis went further, arguing the Utah airspace presence was
routine HADES training and that linking the jet to the assassination was an information operation
without credible evidence.

## What is actually established

* HADES is a **real, publicly announced** U.S. Army ISR program on the Global 6500 airframe. **Documented.**
* The Army has **publicly stated an interest in testing air-launched drones** from that airframe. **Documented**, by two outlets.
* N1098L **is** a Global 6500 operated by a Leidos-affiliated contractor and **did** fly two low passes near Orem on September 10, 2025. **Documented in ADS-B data.**
* N1098L was **configured** to launch drones. **Not established** — and actively disputed on airframe-configuration grounds.
* N1098L **launched, recovered or supported anything** connected to the shooting. **Not established.** No tasking record, no manifest, no imagery.

Capability is not proof of a September 10 release. What capability establishes is that
**records may exist** — flight tasking orders, mission logs, sensor product, and Fort Bliss ramp
and handling logs — and that those records have not been produced.

## Open questions

1. What was N1098L's tasking order for September 10, 2025, and who issued it?
2. Was the aircraft carrying mission equipment on that date, or was it in a training or ferry configuration?
3. Why did *The War Zone* article stop resolving, and was the removal editorial or requested?
4. Who searched "hades n1098l" from the District of Columbia on July 27 and August 5, 2025?
5. Do Biggs Army Airfield ramp and handling logs for September 9–11, 2025 exist, and have they been preserved?
6. Has any component of the HADES program been asked, on the record, whether N1098L's Utah passes were scheduled?
