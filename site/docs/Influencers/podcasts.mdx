Podcasts have been the primary venue where the Charlie Kirk assassination has received serious investigative treatment. Mainstream television either ignored the case or framed it as a straightforward campus shooting with a clear suspect. Independent audio and video programs carried the analytical load — asking who controlled security at UVU that day, why federal agencies have blocked independent access to evidence, and what relationships between [TPUSA](/TPUSA/overview), foreign intelligence networks, and the events of September 10, 2025 remain unexplained publicly. The combined reach of the twenty programs listed here spans hundreds of millions of listeners and viewers.

Three of these hosts — [Tucker Carlson](/People/tucker-carlson), [Dave Smith](/Influencers/podcasts-dave-smith), and [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) — are designated as Trusted Investigators under the proposed [Law 4: Trusted Investigators](../Fix/overview). That designation reflects their demonstrated independence from government and institutional control, their track record of pursuing contested evidence, and their willingness to cover intelligence-community angles that co-opted media will not touch. Their podcast coverage is not simply commentary — it is part of an active investigative record that complements formal legal and congressional efforts. [Ian Carroll](/People/ian-carroll) and [Glenn Greenwald](/Influencers/podcasts-glenn-greenwald) represent the independent research and press-freedom angles; [Joe Rogan](/Influencers/podcasts-joe-rogan) and [Patrick Bet-David](/Influencers/podcasts-patrick-bet-david) provide the mass-audience amplification that moves evidence into mainstream awareness.

Start with Candace Owens Episode 287 ("The Past Is Prologue: Why Charlie Kirk Was Murdered") for the most direct investigative breakdown. Tucker Carlson's interview with Joe Kent covers the foreign-operation angle. Dave Smith's debate at the TPUSA Student Action Summit articulates the [Israel](/Israel/overview) and [Mossad](/intelligence/Mossad) connection argument most clearly. Ian Carroll's Liberty Vault playlist and Glenn Greenwald's System Update provide the deepest intelligence-apparatus critique.

## Mid-2026 Podcast Landscape Update (Attributed)

As of July 2026 research: **Project Constitution** and **danksterintel**-style streamers dominate **hearing-day** live coverage; **Shawn Ryan** remains the security-interview anchor (Harpole); **Carlson/Kent** and **Dore/Carroll** anchor foreign-lead and FBI-critique lanes; **Stew Peters** anchors maximalist mic/Mossad theories; **Megyn Kelly** sits in a contested middle (legal analysis + selective pushback on conspiracies). **Rogan** remains the largest still-underused amplifier per investigator wish-lists.

Recommended starting points unchanged in spirit: Owens deep dives, Carlson–Kent, Dore–Carroll, Harpole Ep 254 — then compare to preliminary-hearing exhibits as they leak into public clips.
