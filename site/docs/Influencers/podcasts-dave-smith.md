# Dave Smith — Part of the Problem

**Dave Smith** is a libertarian comedian and political commentator who hosts the podcast **Part of the Problem**. His program reaches millions of listeners and is known for unfiltered analysis of foreign policy, intelligence services, and government overreach. Dave Smith is named as a **Designated Trusted Investigator** under the proposed [Law 4: Trusted Investigators](../Fix/overview).

## Key Episode: "Thoughts on Charlie Kirk"

Smith's dedicated episode on the Kirk assassination discusses the implications of the killing, institutional distrust, and openness to deeper intel or country-level involvement beyond the official FBI narrative.

- **Title:** "Thoughts on Charlie Kirk"
- **YouTube:** [Watch the episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ykbt_WGOZE)

## TPUSA Student Action Summit Debate (July 2025)

Smith debated Josh Hammer at TPUSA's Student Action Summit (July 11–13, 2025, Tampa, Florida) on topics including U.S. foreign policy, Israel, Epstein, and Mossad connections. This debate took place two months before Kirk's death and directly engaged with the foreign intelligence angles that appear throughout the investigation. ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-ng95XlQb4))

This debate is considered required viewing for anyone researching the TPUSA/Israel/Mossad dimension of the case.

## Why Dave Smith Is Relevant

Smith has been one of the clearest and most direct voices connecting the TPUSA network, Israel, and intelligence service involvement in American politics — a connection that is central to the Charlie Kirk investigation. Unlike mainstream conservatives, he has been willing to openly debate and challenge the pro-Israel consensus within TPUSA circles. His libertarian perspective means he is not aligned with any government or political party, giving him the independence that Law 4 requires of Trusted Investigators.

## Designated Trusted Investigator — Law 4

Under the proposed Law 4, Dave Smith is one of three civilians designated to lead independent investigation teams. He would receive one FBI team and one Intelligence Community team, authority to select all team members, full access to all agency files, and the power to publicly release findings without government interference.
