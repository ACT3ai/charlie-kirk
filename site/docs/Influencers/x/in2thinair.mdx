# In2ThinAir — X Posts

[@In2ThinAir](https://x.com/In2ThinAir) is the account behind the most-cited post in the
**remote-video-deletion** thread — the claim that witness footage of the shooting disappeared or
changed on phones without the owners acting. That phenomenon has not been demonstrated; this page
records the attributed claims and their reach.

## Claims from X posts

- **October 13, 2025 — remote-deletion post** — the earliest prominent post raising the exact
  claim that witness videos were **deleted or altered remotely**. Reported engagement was roughly
  2,554 likes, 726 reposts, and **270,001 views**, making it the reference point for the whole
  thread — documented with competing explanations (and the caution that reach is not confirmation)
  on [Witness Videos Deleted Remotely](/CoverUp/Videos_Deleted_Remotely).
  ([Primary post](https://x.com/In2ThinAir/status/1977780524006400195))
- **Timeline placement** — the post is logged as an **alleged**, non-court-authenticated event on
  the [October 15, 2025 timeline](/Timeline/october-15-2025), beside the related
  [FBI Asked a Witness to Delete Their Video](/CoverUp/FBI_Asked_Delete_Video) claim.
- **Stage-side clip** — a watermarked @IN2THINAIR video frame covering the working area next to the
  stage (Yrefy / Turning Point signage visible) is preserved in the
  [photo backlog](/Photos/Unfiled_Backlog/Img_Photo_74e7fc), relevant to equipment and access
  questions around the stage.

## Investigative value

The account's deletion post defines a testable claim: device forensics on any witness phone that
allegedly lost footage would either substantiate or collapse it. Until such an examination is
public, the thread remains engagement-heavy and evidence-light — which is exactly how this site
labels it.
