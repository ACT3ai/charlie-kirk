# Cashloren — X Posts

[@Cashloren](https://x.com/Cashloren) contributed one of the key artifacts of the **first-hour
broadcast confusion**: a September 10, 2025 clip of **Glenn Beck** on **Megyn Kelly's** program
relaying that a well-placed source said [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) "has a pulse." The clip
is preserved on this site as documentation of how fast — and how uncertainly — early wound and
status claims spread on live television.

## Claims from X posts

- **Sept 10, 2025** ([post](https://x.com/Cashloren/status/1965869689755812061), ~461 likes,
  ~80k views) — Glenn Beck, visibly emotional on Megyn Kelly's show, says: *"I spoke to somebody in
  a position who knows who said he has a pulse. So that's good news."* Kirk's death was announced
  shortly after — the statement did not hold.
- The clip is filed in the wound-location cluster on
  [Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart) as part of the immediate on-air confusion window
  in which "heart" and "chest" claims also spread.
- The site's photo-cluster analysis
  ([exhibit page](/Photos/Official_Narrative/Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart/Img_Photo_Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart_5))
  treats it as a caution cutting both ways: if a well-sourced commentator can relay wrong
  information within the hour, early "heart" uniformity may be ordinary live-coverage chaos rather
  than anything else. No criticism of Beck or Kelly is intended or implied.

## Investigative value

A single but load-bearing artifact: it anchors the **early broadcast chaos** context against which
the chest-versus-neck witness discrepancies are measured. Pair with
