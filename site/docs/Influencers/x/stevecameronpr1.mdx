# Steve Cameron — X Posts

[@SteveCameronPr1](https://x.com/SteveCameronPr1) (**Steve Cameron**) is the online investigator who
has driven the most sustained scrutiny of the [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)
surrender-and-custody timeline. His person profile is at
[Steve Cameron](/People/steve-cameron); this page compiles the X-post claims themselves — all
attributed commentary, not court findings.

## Claims from X posts

- **April 17, 2026** — shared the video of Sheriff [Nate Brooksby](/People/nate-brooksby)
  recounting the **8:02 PM** call from a retired friend who knew the Robinson family "through
  religious association" and helped arrange a voluntary surrender.
  ([Source](https://x.com/SteveCameronPr1/status/2045116771431321829)) See
  [Washington County Sheriff](/Law_Enforcement/washington-county-sheriff).
- **April 8, 2026** — summarized the defense claim that Robinson was **Mirandized on camera at
  6:25 PM on September 11**, that prosecutors had not disputed the Bates exhibit's clock time, and
  that the **7:57 PM Discord "confession"** therefore lands **after custody**. See
  [Faked Confession (claims)](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/faked-confession).
- **April 22, 2026** — questioned how there could be "no bodycam footage" of the highest-profile
  political-assassination surrender in 50 years, and argued (tagging reporters @EPCoan, @ljgliha,
  @brian_schnee) that "Brooksby's 8:02 PM timeline doesn't add up" against the court documents.
  See [Sheriff Video Withheld](/CoverUp/Sheriff_Video_Withheld).
- **June 1, 2026** (high engagement) — wrote that Robinson "should be released immediately" because,
  in Cameron's reading, WCSO claimed a post-confession surrender when he was "already in custody and
  mirandized at 6:25 PM," citing **"Bates video stamp 3996 WCSO."**
- Also cited on the site's [custody-surrender-mirandizing
  timeline](/Timeline/custody-surrender-mirandizing-timeline), the
  [custody-vs-Discord-confession timeline](/Timeline/custody-vs-discord-confession-timeline), and
  the pages on [official resignations](/Law_Enforcement/official-resignations) following Sheriff
  Brooksby's announced departure.

## Investigative value

Cameron's lane is narrow and testable: **clock times against documents**. If the 6:25 PM on-camera
Miranda and the 7:57 PM Discord messages are both real, someone must reconcile who typed the
confession — which is why the withheld surrender/booking video matters. The state treats the gap as
reconcilable paperwork; commentators like Cameron treat it as decisive. Robinson is charged, not
convicted; no official named here is accused of a crime by this site.
