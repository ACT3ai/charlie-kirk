# ZachCostello_ — X Posts

[@ZachCostello_](https://x.com/ZachCostello_) is one of the named **pro-pattern voices** in the
Google Trends dispute — the most contested OSINT lane in the case — and a secondary amplifier in the
custody-clock and scene-handling threads. The site's standing rule applies to everything below:
Trends anomalies are **leads**, never identification of a living searcher, and never proof of
foreign-state involvement.

## Claims from X posts

- Argues (with the Baron Coleman ecosystem and @denton_doyle) that **pre-event Google Trends
  activity** on victim/witness names, Timpanogos hospital, surgeons, rifle-drop locations, and
  Israel/DC/Alabama geographies is irregular enough to investigate — the pro-pattern position
  indexed across the [Google Searches Investigation Index](/GoogleSearches/investigation-index),
  [Israel IP patterns](/GoogleSearches/israel-ip-search-patterns),
  [July 20, 2025 anchor](/GoogleSearches/july-20-2025-anchor), and
  [Trends deletion claims](/GoogleSearches/trends-deletion-suppression). Skeptics
  (@paramounttactcl, @MaxNordau) counter that Trends is sampled, unreproducible noise.
- Shared screenshots showing **UVU student alerts** announced a suspect "in custody" **~12:42 PM** —
  under 20 minutes after the shot — with a later alert reversing that the shooter had **not** been
  found; see [12:23 Shooting Time](/Timeline/12-23-shooting-time).
- **May 13, 2026** — Posted walkthroughs agreeing with Baron Coleman's **custody clock** analysis —
  recorded on the [Baron Coleman timeline](/Timeline/baron-coleman-timeline) with the caution that
  amplification is not corroboration.
- Amplified the **Dan Merrell / Hardscape Utah** courtyard-paving interview ("paver emergency" four
  days after the shooting) — see [Dirt Removed](/CoverUp/Dirt_Removed).
- Cited as a defender of the Trends work on the [Baron Coleman profile](/Influencers/podcasts-baron):
  pre-event hospital/surgeon/drop-house spikes "remain irregular even if not definitive."

## Investigative value

A bridging account between the Trends-forensics camp and the custody-timeline camp. Its most
concrete contribution is the **campus-alert screenshots** — a dated, checkable record of the false
custody message that kept roughly ten thousand students in place.
