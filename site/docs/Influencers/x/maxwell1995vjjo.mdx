# Maxwell1995Vjjo — X Posts

[@Maxwell1995Vjjo](https://x.com/Maxwell1995Vjjo) posted one of the pattern-defining items in the
"shot in the heart" eyewitness controversy: a **September 11, 2025** repost of a TV witness clip,
framed with the question of why "every eyewitness" from the UVU shooting described a heart or chest
wound when the released footage shows a neck injury. Everything below is an attributed claim from
their X posts, not a confirmed finding.

## Claims from X posts

- Shared a **TV witness clip** in which the witness says: *"It was one shot, right, directly to the
  heart"* — and calls the shooter "a good shooter, a sharp shooter."
  ([Source](https://x.com/Maxwell1995Vjjo/status/1966235480732873149))
- The same witness states the man detained, **[George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn)**, was
  **"the wrong guy"** — consistent with the public record that Zinn was charged with unrelated counts.
  Mr. Zinn is a living person and nothing here asserts he committed any crime.
- Framed the pattern question that carried the claim across X: *"Why did every eye witness from the
  Kirk shooting say he got shot in the heart/chest when he clearly got shot in the neck?"* — an
  argument about the **uniformity** of early accounts rather than any single statement.
- The site notes the framing overstates its own evidence: not every eyewitness said heart, and
  television interviewees repeating each other has an ordinary explanation. See
  [Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart) for both readings.

## Investigative value

The post is the moment the heart/chest thread stopped being a collection of individual accounts and
became an argument about the pattern itself. It anchors the "every eyewitness" cluster on
[Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart) and appears in the
[Photos — Official Narrative](/Photos/Official_Narrative/Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart/Img_Photo_Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart_4)
image analysis. Pair it with the neck-wound accounts from the same afternoon before drawing conclusions.
