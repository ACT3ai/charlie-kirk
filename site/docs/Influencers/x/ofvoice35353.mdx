# OfVoice35353 — X Posts

[@OfVoice35353](https://x.com/OfVoice35353) appears across the site's
[Gun & Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/Gun_Bullet_Investigation_Index) cluster as a **testimony-citing
counter-voice** in the fragment debate — arguing from hearing evidence rather than from skeptic
charts. As with every account on these pages, the items below are attributed claims, not forensic
findings.

## Claims from X posts

- Cites **hearing testimony** that the recovered bullet fragments measure approximately
  **.286–.301 inches** and therefore **fit the .30-caliber family** — a rebuttal to claims that the
  ATF's inconclusive comparison means the bullet "wasn't .30-06." Recorded on
  [ATF Fragment Inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive),
  [Thirty-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch),
  [Rifle Fingerprints Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Rifle_Fingerprints_Mismatch), and
  [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle).
- The citation pairs with [@misfitpatriot_](./misfitpatriot)'s argument that soft-point .30-06
  **should** fragment — together forming the recurring counter-position to non-match overclaims on
  [Alternative Calibers and Weapons](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons),
  [Bullet in Stage Wood](/Gun_Bullet/Bullet_Stage_Wood),
  [Gun Dropped Location](/Gun_Bullet/Gun_Dropped_Location), and
  [Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle).

## Investigative value

The account keeps the fragment-measurement testimony in circulation — the single strongest
data point for the official caliber narrative while the full lab reports remain unpublished. The
site preserves it beside the skeptic energy and trajectory arguments so readers see the dispute
whole.
