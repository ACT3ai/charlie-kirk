# Russell Brand — X Posts

[@rustyrockets](https://x.com/rustyrockets) (**Russell Brand**) is a commentator whose **May 14,
2026** video became one of the most-cited items in the cover-up-of-foreign-involvement narrative.
His claims rest on **unnamed secondhand sources** and are recorded here as attributed allegations,
not confirmed cabinet minutes.

## Claims from X posts

- In a **4:45 video** posted May 14, 2026 (in apparent conversation with Alex Jones), Brand claimed
  **two sources who were in a cabinet meeting six days after** the killing told him that
  [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel), **Tulsi Gabbard**, and the **Vice President** said *"we're not
  looking at foreign involvement"* and *"we're not looking at any accomplices domestically"* —
  which Brand framed as a **cover-up**.
  ([Source](https://x.com/rustyrockets/status/2055025567255191600))
- The video (pinned to IPFS and embedded on the [Israel overview](/Israel/overview)) discusses
  [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s Israel tensions as motive context; the site isolates each
  claim on [Russell Brand Cabinet Claims](/Israel/Russell_Brand_Cabinet_Claims).
- The site's [Patel / Kent probe page](/Suspicious/FBI/patel-shut-down-kent-probe) grades Brand's
  strand as **uncorroborated secondhand hearsay** — the weakest of three strands, and one that also
  cuts against the "Patel acted alone" framing since it places Gabbard in the same decision.
- Brand's account is paired with [Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent)'s on-the-record statement
  that NCTC's parallel foreign-nexus review was ordered to stop — the two anchors of the
  [suppression narrative](/Israel_Main_Suspect/coverup-foreign-involvement).

## Investigative value

Brand supplies the only claimed **inside-the-room** account of the foreign-lane shutdown, which is
exactly why it must be handled carefully: anonymous, secondhand, uncorroborated, and garbled in
parts of the transcript. The site does not assert that Patel, Gabbard, JD Vance, or any official
committed a crime; the claim defines what records (minutes, orders, directives) oversight should
demand.
