# Stellar Man — X Posts

[@stellarman22](https://x.com/stellarman22) (**Stellar Man**) originated the **N888KG** flight-track
claim within hours of the shooting — one of the earliest aircraft threads in the case. The bullets
below are **claims attributed to their X posts**; the tracking gap is checkable, the
characterizations are the poster's.

## Claims from X posts

- Posted on the evening of September 10 that private jet **N888KG** "left [Provo
  airport](/Locations/Provo_Airport) an hour after the shooting," that 30 minutes into the flight
  it "drops off of ADS-B **illegally**," and reappears headed back to Provo about an hour later —
  closing with *"Why did this plane turn off its radar? Where is the shooter?"*
  ([Capture](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_c132eb))
- The attached UTC timeline: assassination **18:10**, takeoff **19:12**, ADS-B loss **19:44**,
  reappearance **20:30**; registry **N888KG LLC (Lehi, UT)**, with the tail number "not available
  for public tracking per request."
- A capture taken ~15 hours after posting (morning of September 11) fixes the claim **before** the
  owner's public statement of a pre-scheduled round trip to Page, AZ — letting readers see the
  sequence: observation first, explanation after. Whether the posted 19:12 UTC departure and the
  owner's ~1:20 PM MT account reconcile remains publicly unresolved.
  ([Timestamped capture](/Photos/Aircraft/Img_Photo_c84196))
- The site's caution is recorded alongside: ADS-B dropouts also occur through terrain shadowing and
  receiver coverage, and blocked tail numbers can vanish from public displays without anything
  happening in the cockpit. The closing question about the shooter is rhetorical and connects
  nothing.
