# Smartski — X Posts

[@2Smartski](https://x.com/2Smartski) (**Smartski**) posted an audio-forensics analysis of the UVU shooting recordings, using waveform and spectrogram panels from audio-editing software on a track labeled rear-right of the tent. Their conclusion — that the shooter was near ground level and close — directly contradicts the official rooftop narrative. These are **the poster's attributed claims**, not verified findings.

## Claims from X posts

- The post states acoustic analysis "confirms" the shooter was **near ground level, about 70 to 90 feet away, not a rooftop**. The word "confirms" is the poster's, not a finding.
- Working shown: **crack-bang intervals of 0.08 to 0.14 seconds** across all recordings equal roughly 70–90 feet of distance, using the speed of sound at 82°F (1,141 ft/s).
- The closest audio clip is said to show total crack and bang overlap with the impact clear, putting the source **under 100 feet**.
- The crack-bang method is legitimate physics — a supersonic round's sonic crack arrives before the muzzle blast — but other analysts dispute whether the two transients identified are genuinely crack and bang rather than direct sound and echo, and whether recordings were properly time-aligned.

The analysis sits inside the site's acoustic-dispute lane: if the crack-bang gap is real and correctly measured, it conflicts with a ~430-foot rooftop shot from the [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center).
