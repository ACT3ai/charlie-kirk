# Ariana_J_21 — X Posts

[@Ariana_J_21](https://x.com/Ariana_J_21) is a citizen ballistics analyst working the **fragment-measurement lane** — the argument that the recovered jacket fragments do not fit the rifle the State recovered. These are **online technical arguments**, not published peer-reviewed ballistics reports.

## Claims from X posts

- Recovered jacket-fragment measurements (**~0.286–0.301"** in their posts) are argued to be **too small** for a full **.30-06 (0.308")** bullet profile. Recorded on [rifle chain of custody](/Law_Enforcement/rifle-chain-of-custody).
- Heavy fragmentation is argued to be **inconsistent** with common non-frangible .30-06 loads — feeding the fragment-versus-caliber debate at [wound analysis theories](/Medical/Autopsy/wound_analysis_theories).
- Amplified July 2026 threads on the **sealed ATF ballistics** and commentary alleging the FBI may revive **Comparative Bullet Lead Analysis (CBLA)** — a technique the FBI abandoned in 2005 after National Academy of Sciences criticism. The site's counterpoint is noted in the same entry: "inconclusive" does not equal exoneration, and observation limits on Virtual Comparison Microscopy are standard policy. See [Ballistics Sealed and CBLA](/CoverUp/Ballistics_Sealed_CBLA).

## Investigative value

The fragment-dimension argument is the most concrete of the citizen ballistics claims because it is falsifiable — released measurements and lab documentation would settle it. Until the ATF/FBI raw files are public, the site holds the claim beside the official "could not be identified or excluded" language from the preliminary hearing.
