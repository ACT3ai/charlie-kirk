# fratercrc — X Posts

[@fratercrc](https://x.com/fratercrc) is cited in the investigation record for one pointed
observation in the **autopsy-transparency** lane: the person publicly explaining
[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s wound was not the person who examined the body. Items
below are attributed claims from X posts, not findings of this site.

## Claims from X posts

- **Dr. Kendall Crowns**, chief medical examiner of **Tarrant County, Texas**, reportedly became
  the **media face** of the wound explanation (appearing, for example, on Nancy Grace) despite
  **not** having performed the autopsy — an attribution recorded on
  [the medical examiner page](/Medical/medical_examiner) and
  [Autopsy Sealed / Examiner](/CoverUp/Autopsy_Sealed_Examiner).
- The point raised is about **who spoke publicly** — why a Texas official offered the public
  explanation while the actual Utah examiner's role and report stayed out of view. Dr. Crowns is
  **not accused of any wrongdoing**; general forensic commentary by out-of-state experts is
  routine.
- Critics extend the observation into the wider pattern of the **sealed autopsy report**: the
  office head can testify to a colleague's report, so the hands-on examiner's identity has stayed
  effectively out of public reach.

## Investigative value

The Crowns observation is a compact example of the site's autopsy-transparency question: the
full report — wound trajectory, toxicology, any residue testing — is not public, and the public
narrative has been carried by people other than the examiner of record. The
[Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview) would compel release of the underlying report.
