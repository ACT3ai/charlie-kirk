# Zeee Media — X Posts

[@zeeemedia](https://x.com/zeeemedia) (**Zeee Media**) supplied the most-cited **defense-side hearing
reports** on the surveillance-identification question during the July 2026 preliminary hearing.
These are claims about testimony as reported on X — not judicial findings that the rooftop figure is
or is not [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson).

## Claims from X posts

- **July 7, 2026** — Reported that defense counsel **Kathryn Nester** elicited testimony from DPS
  Agent **David Hull** that the rooftop footage of the individual **lacks identifiable facial
  features or distinctive clothing detail** sufficient for standalone identification — the single
  most-cited support for the [Tyler as Placeholder](/cameras/Tyler_Placeholder) reading; also
  recorded on [Tyler on Cameras](/cameras/Tyler_On_Cameras) and
  [UVU Surveillance](/cameras/UVU_Surveillance).
- The same thread reported FBI Director **[Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel)** described the released
  stairwell images as **"enhanced"** despite high-quality campus cameras existing — the heart of the
  grainy-releases-versus-institutional-HD complaint at
  [UVU HD Footage Withheld](/CoverUp/UVU_HD_Footage_Withheld).
- Reported the affidavit's own concession that campus cameras "do not capture the act of firing" —
  a correction to viral "trigger-pull video" overclaims.

## Investigative value

Zeee Media's reporting turned the identification debate from speculation into a claim about **sworn
testimony**: if Hull's concession is accurately reported, the state's ID rests on gait and clothing
captions rather than a recognizable face — which makes the withheld higher-resolution angles more
important, not less.
