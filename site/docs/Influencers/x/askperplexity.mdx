# AskPerplexity — X Posts

[@AskPerplexity](https://x.com/AskPerplexity) is the automated question-answering account run by
the Perplexity AI search service. In the Kirk case it appears on this site as the most-cited
example of the **fact-check layer** — automated replies pushing back on citizen-investigator
claims inside the same threads where those claims spread. Its output is recorded here as one
side of the information battlefield, not as adjudication.

## Claims from X posts

- Pushed **lone-gunman confirmation** in replies to investigation threads — the post cited on
  [X Deboosting of Investigators](/Censorship/X_Deboosting_Investigators) as the canonical
  fact-check-layer example. ([Source](https://x.com/AskPerplexity/status/1976908319504699489))
- Summarized that **no public evidence links [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) to foreign
  intelligence** — cited in the counterpoint section of
  [Mossad Involvement Claims](/Theories/Mossad_Involvement_Claims) and in
  [Israeli-Agenda Criticism](/Media/Israeli-Agenda-Criticism), where accounts used its automated
  summaries to dispute foreign-entity ties.
- Its fact-check posts are noted on [Public Response](/Other/Public_Response) as part of the
  online pushback against alternative theories while mainstream outlets carried the prosecution
  narrative.

## Investigative value

This account matters for a different reason than most entries in this section: it shows how
**automated fact-checking** interacts with citizen investigation. Site pages record that
investigation threads and AskPerplexity debunk replies run side by side; the presence of a
rebuttal is not proof of coordination in either direction. Readers should weigh its summaries
as machine-generated aggregation of mainstream sourcing, checked against primary documents.
