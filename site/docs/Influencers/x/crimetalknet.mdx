# CrimeTalkNet — X Posts

[@CrimeTalkNet](https://x.com/CrimeTalkNet) is a court-reporting account cited on this site for its
coverage of the **July 2026 Tyler Robinson preliminary hearing** in Provo. Its posts document how
courtroom secrecy decisions shaped what the public could see. These are hearing reports and
commentary, not court conclusions.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 2026** — Reported (alongside @TPostMillennial and @SarahisCensored) that **Judge Tony
  Graf** limited broadcast of the rooftop video shown to the Kirk family in court, and that
  bind-over was **delayed for written briefs**, with counsel for **Erika Kirk** seeking a firm
  ruling timeline. Logged on [FBI Investigation](/FBI/FBI_Investigation).
- Reported **adjournment without immediate bind-over** — written briefs first, oral argument later,
  and a motion from Erika Kirk's counsel for a firm ruling date. The site notes process delay is
  not proof of impropriety, but it extends the period without full public primary video. See
  [FBI Gag Orders](/FBI/Gag_Orders).
- Their reporting on the family-counsel motion is cited in the site's
  [Victim Family Rights](/Fix/Victim_Family_Rights) law proposal as a documented transparency gap:
  the family viewing restricted hearing video while the public remains locked out.

## Investigative value

A process-transparency source: the account's value is documenting **what was withheld and when** in
the court sequence — the raw material for the site's argument that non-broadcast rulings plus
sealed filings recreate gag effects even when formal gag text is narrower.
