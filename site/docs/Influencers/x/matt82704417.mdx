# Matthew St Baker — X Posts

[@matt82704417](https://x.com/matt82704417) (**Matthew St Baker** — see his
[People profile](/People/matthew-st-baker)) is a citizen investigator whose name appears on this
site because he **reshared and amplified** device-theory analyses — not because he is a subject
of suspicion. The items below are **reported social-media interpretations of footage**, not
authenticated forensic findings.

## Claims from X posts

- **June 21, 2026 — the 4K shirt-tracking post** — Crediting Jon Bray's optical-flow work,
  claimed a 4K close-up follows an alleged device frame-by-frame: starting at the mic receiver
  pack on Kirk's left belt, driven **up under the shirt**, "pinched and yanked violently over his
  left shoulder" toward the neck, then falling diagonally to the lower right. He asserted the
  charge was PETN and framed it as visual proof of "an INTERNAL EXPLOSION," writing "It's over.
  The 30.06 gunshot narrative is dead."
  ([Source](https://x.com/matt82704417/status/2068506485420892470)) — see
  [Shirt Pull at the Shot](/Suspicious/Five_Minutes/shirt-pull-at-the-shot) (site rating:
  SPECULATIVE) and [Mic Explosive](/CoverUp/Mic_Explosive).
- **Shaped-charge pendant reshare (June 21, 2026)** — Reshared a December 2025 thread by a user
  identifying himself as a former USMC explosive breacher (@cletus_jethro), arguing a microscopic
  cone-shaped charge sat in the top of Kirk's **crucifix pendant**, anchored by the chain and
  detonated by a third party ([Exploding Pendant](/Mic/exploding-pendant-shaped-charge)).
- **USB-logo shard claim (June 2026)** — Claimed a USB logo is visible on shards in the
  RODE/receiver explosion video analysis
  ([Mic Explosion Video Analysis](/Mic/mic-explosion-video-analysis)).
- His posts also feed the **shirt-before-blood sequencing argument** — shirt deformation, clasp
  lift, and necklace snap before visible blood
  ([Shirt Before Blood](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/shirt-before-blood)).

## Investigative value

St Baker is the principal June 2026 **amplifier** of the Bray-derived device analyses — the posts
that pushed the exploding-mic/pendant theories back into high circulation. The site pairs each
claim with the ordinary explanations (hydrostatic shock, compression artifacts) and the skeptic
demonstrations arguing a real shaped charge leaves a large smoke signature.
