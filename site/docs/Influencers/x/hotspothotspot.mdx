# HotSpotHotSpot — X Posts

[@HotSpotHotSpot](https://x.com/HotSpotHotSpot) is a citizen-investigator account cited in the
ballistics and crime-scene coverage, mostly for translating hearing developments into plain-language
posts. Every bullet below is an **attributed claim from X posts**, not a court finding; Tyler
Robinson remains charged, not convicted.

## Claims from X posts

- **Fragment-match restatement (July 12, 2026)** — restated for a general audience that prosecutors
  are **unable to conclusively match autopsy bullet fragments** to the suspected murder weapon —
  see [More Reasons](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/more-reasons) and
  [.30-06 Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie).
  The standard counterpoint is preserved alongside: "inconclusive" does not equal exoneration.
- **Hull paving cross-examination clips (July 2026)** — amplified widely shared clips of lead
  investigator [David Hull](/People/david-hull) testifying he **did not approve tent removal**, was
  not involved in the decision to pave the dirt under the tent, and **learned of the paving from
  the news** — see the [scene changes / paving timeline](/Timeline/scene-changes-paving-timeline).
- **Sealed CBLA amplification (July 2026)** — among the accounts (with @KellyOMeara5556,
  @JGlasseir, @Ariana_J_21) amplifying the sealed comparative-bullet-lead-analysis dispute on
  [Ballistics Sealed CBLA](/CoverUp/Ballistics_Sealed_CBLA).

## Investigative value

The account is a **distribution layer** for the two disputes most central to the not-the-assassin
argument: inconclusive fragment ballistics and crime-scene alteration. Its clips give researchers
timestamps into hearing video; the underlying exhibits still need primary release.
