# Ali Zreik — X Posts

[@ZREIKMIESTER](https://x.com/ZREIKMIESTER) (**Ali Zreik**) authored the **LiPO battery / PCB
neck-path** variant of the exploding-mic theory — a set of annotated diagrams and a slow-motion clip
arguing device components, not a rifle round, caused the fatal neck injury. The official finding is
a rifle gunshot; everything below is an attributed, unverified proponent-side illustration.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 17, 2026** — Published three diagrams and a silent slow-motion clip laying out a claimed
  sequence in which a "molten hot LiPO battery pierced into" Kirk's neck "and the PCB followed into
  his neck sleeve." ([Post](https://x.com/ZREIKMIESTER/status/2077921727888138329)) — presented on
  the [Mic theory overview](/Mic/overview) and preserved as primary-source material in the
  [Mic photo cluster](/Photos/Mic/Img_Photo_c92a55).
- Companion claim: "melted into the polyester shirt are battery fragments and Rode microphone clip
  parts" — the most checkable element, since fused fabric with device components either exists or
  does not; see the [diagram page](/Photos/Mic/Img_Photo_05bd8d) and
  [Burnt Object in the SUV](/Mic/burnt-object-rode-transmitter).
- Asserted "they cleaned up the body before delivering it to the hospital" — the author's own
  assertion, naming no individual, and not independently verified.
- The neck-sleeve path framing is an attempt to answer a known objection: the published wound account
  describes a recovered projectile with no exit wound, which an external blast alone would not
  obviously produce.

## Investigative value

Zreik's diagrams give the mic theory its most specific physical claims — named components, a
retained-object mechanism, and testable fabric evidence. No laboratory analysis of any such object
has been published, and photo-based identification attempts are argued to fail on resolution
grounds. The ballistic account it competes with is at [Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview).
