# Brian Harpole (BrianHarpole) — X Posts

[@BrianHarpole](https://x.com/BrianHarpole) is the X presence of **Brian Harpole**, the
Integrity Security / Rockhouse Integrity chief who led Charlie Kirk's private detail at UVU and
who has his own [People profile](/People/brian-harpole). Unlike most accounts in this section he
is a **participant, not an observer** — his public statements (X, the Shawn Ryan Show, Salt Lake
Tribune coverage) are primary material in the security-failure record. **Status: Alive.** No
court has found negligence or wrongdoing by Harpole; the items below are his attributed public
statements and the commentary around them.

## Claims from X posts

- Per threads compiling his statements: he was security chief of the detail, an SUV occupant who
  **shielded Kirk with [Dan Flood](/People/dan-flood)**, reported hearing **two distinct
  sounds**, criticized UVU's **rooftop failure**, and said he **texted Chief
  [Jeff Long](/People/jeff-long)** pre-event about student-accessible roofs — receiving a reply
  to the effect of "I got you covered." Recorded on
  [his profile](/People/brian-harpole) and [Jeff Long's](/People/jeff-long).
- His attributed line that rooftops were supposed to be a **police responsibility** and were not
  covered as promised anchors the promise-vs-delivery dispute on
  [No Counter-Snipers](/Security_Team/no-counter-snipers) and
  [Perimeter and Rooftop Failures](/Security_Team/perimeter-rooftop-failures).
- He has publicly **refuted conspiracy theories** about his own role; separately, legal-watch
  accounts track a reported **Harpole v. Owens** defamation action arising from commentary about
  him.
- Citizen researchers tie his "two distinct sounds" remark to [Mic](/Mic/overview) dual-event
  hypotheses — that linkage is their speculation, not his accusation.

## Investigative value

Harpole's statements are the closest public account from **inside the security bubble**: the
pre-event text chain, the rooftop understanding, and the acoustic detail all come from him. The
open questions are documentary — the full text-message record with Long, Integrity's security
plan submissions, and any after-action report.
