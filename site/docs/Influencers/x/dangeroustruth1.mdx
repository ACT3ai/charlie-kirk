# DangerousTruth1 — X Posts

[@DangerousTruth1](https://x.com/DangerousTruth1) is one of the accounts carrying the
**custody-before-Discord** core claim — the argument that [Tyler
Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) was already in custody when the "confession" messages were sent.
These are attributed social claims, not court findings; Robinson is presumed innocent.

## Claims from X posts

- **April 8, 2026** — Posted that Robinson was *"ALREADY in custody when the infamous Discord
  confession happened,"* citing Utah police-records framing. The claim recirculated into the July
  2026 hearing week and is logged on
  [faked confession](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/faked-confession).
- The site groups this with parallel posts (@SteveCameronPr1, @DiligentDenizen, the James Li /
  Baron Coleman "after arrest" package) restating the **6:25 PM Miranda vs ~7:57 PM Discord**
  authorship logic — see
  [US Intelligence Assisted — faked confession](/US_Intelligence_Assisted/faked-confession).
- The rebuttal side is also on the record: July 2026 hearing watchers summarize sworn testimony
  that the surrender came just before 9:00 p.m. and that the messages were not post-custody — see
  [Mirandizing](/Timeline/Tyler_Robinson/Mirandizing).

## Investigative value

An amplification node for the strongest **procedural** argument in the Proof Not Tyler lane. The
custody-gap question is checkable against primary records (Miranda paperwork, Discord server
metadata, hold-room video) — precisely the records the [Fix Laws](/Fix/overview) aim to force out.
