# The National Desk — X Posts

[@TND](https://x.com/TND) (**The National Desk**) is a broadcast news account, not a citizen investigator. It appears in this investigation for one same-day artifact: an **on-scene eyewitness interview** posted September 10, 2025, that became a fixture of the wound-location debate.

## Claims from X posts

- **September 10, 2025** — An eyewitness at the [UVU](/UVU/overview) event told the outlet: *"It was like a direct shot to the heart. It looked like right where his heart is, it started going red. He fell back and went to his side."* — recorded on this site as the **witness's account, not a medical finding** ([Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart)).  ([Source](https://x.com/TND/status/1965890646746513821))
- The clip is preserved in the [Shot in the Heart photo cluster](/Photos/Official_Narrative/Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart/Img_Photo_Narrative_Shot_in_the_Heart_2) because the account is **specific about location** (the heart, a spreading red patch) and was given within hours, before footage circulated widely.

## Investigative value

Later footage showed visible bleeding **at the neck**, no bullet hole in the shirt front, and no chest blood — and reporting settled on a neck wound. Researchers ask why so many early on-camera speakers converged on the same wrong location; the ordinary answers (chaotic scenes, repetition, distance) are strong ones, and **nothing here accuses the witness or the outlet of anything**. The clip is a primary data point in the [witness-reversal](/Witnesses/Shot_in_Chest_Reversal) record.
