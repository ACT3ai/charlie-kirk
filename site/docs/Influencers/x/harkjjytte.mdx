# HarkjJytte — X Posts

[@HarkjJytte](https://x.com/HarkjJytte) is a citizen-investigator account concentrated on the
**medical and ballistics** side of the case. Its July 2026 posts are cited across the Medical
section as attributed opinions about what the fragment evidence does and does not prove — never as
lab findings.

## Claims from X posts

- **Fragments don't settle mechanism (July 2026)** — argues the courtroom "bullet finding" may
  confirm a projectile **without proving a Mauser match**, and continues to favor a **RØDE wireless
  mic explosion** framing — see [Ballistics and Wound Analysis](/Medical/ballistics_and_wound_analysis)
  and the [Mic overview](/Mic/overview).
- **Narrative-incentive claim** — argues recovered fragments can still be used to prop a
  lone-gunman frame even if they do not match the Mauser — a claim about incentives, not a lab
  result; recorded on [Hospital People and Bullet Claims](/Medical/hospital_people_and_bullet_claims)
  and [Trial and Autopsy Report](/Medical/trial_and_autopsy_report).
- **Medical-examiner OSINT** — connects **Dr. Deirdre Amaro** (state ME office) and hospital
  staffing timing to alleged pre-event **Israeli IP-search** narratives. These are **unverified
  OSINT claims**, easy to misread (VPNs, ad networks, geolocation false positives), and are not
  evidence of malpractice or conspiracy by any named professional — see
  [Israel Google Searches — Medical People](/Medical/israel_google_searches_medical_people) and
  [Medical Examiner](/Medical/medical_examiner).

## Investigative value

The account keeps the sharpest version of the **fragment-vs-rifle** question in circulation: what
exactly did the ATF match, and what would a mic-detonation mechanism require? Its value is in
framing testable questions; its IP-search material should be treated as the weakest tier of claim
until primary logs exist.
