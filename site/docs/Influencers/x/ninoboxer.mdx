# ninoboxer — X Posts

[@ninoboxer](https://x.com/ninoboxer) is the amplification point for one of the most consequential
witness accounts in the case: **Ryne Simmons**'s claim that the [FBI](/FBI/overview) asked him to
**delete** his close-up footage of the assassination. The thread is cited on more than a dozen site
pages. Everything below is an attributed witness account and amplification, not an adjudicated fact.

## Claims from X posts

- Amplified witness **Ryne Simmons**'s account (circulating from ~October 25–29, 2025): he recorded
  a **close-up 4K, 60fps video** of the shooting, **sent it to the FBI** to help investigators, was
  **instructed to delete** his copy — with follow-up calls to confirm the deletion — and says he
  **secretly preserved a copy** anyway.
  ([Source](https://x.com/ninoboxer/status/1982164944914162103))
- The site rates this **EMERGING — stronger than an anonymous claim** because it is a named witness
  describing his own experience. Full treatment:
  [FBI Asked Witness to Delete Video](/CoverUp/FBI_Asked_Delete_Video),
  [Ryne Simmons — FBI Video](/Censorship/Ryne_Simmons_FBI_Video), and
  [Simmons Video Deletion](/Suspicious/FBI/simmons-video-deletion).
- If true, commentators argue, this is direct **federal pressure on a citizen witness** — distinct
  from platform moderation — and an indicator of evidence suppression rather than preservation. See
  [Censorship of Investigators](/Influencers/Censorship_of_Investigators) and
  [Eyewitness Mobile Video](/cameras/Eyewitness_Mobile_Video).
- The amplification wave included @ShadowofEzra, @BasedSamParker, @JustTheTweets17, and @AdameMedia;
  official public statements have **not** fully confirmed or denied the allegations.

## Investigative value

The Simmons thread is the site's most-documented **government-adjacent censorship** episode and the
anchor of the witness-video preservation fight. Its value rides on Simmons's own credibility and on
whether the preserved copy ever enters the public record — both open questions.
