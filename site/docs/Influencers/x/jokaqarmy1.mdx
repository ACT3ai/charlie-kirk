# JOKAQARMY1 — X Posts

[@JOKAQARMY1](https://x.com/JOKAQARMY1) is a court-watch account whose **July 6, 2026**
preliminary-hearing narration became the site's primary source clip for the **Officer Bagley
body-cam gap**. Everything below is reported courtroom narration and framed as open questions —
none of it establishes wrongdoing by Officer Bagley or anyone else.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 6, 2026** — Posted a clip narrating testimony in which Officer **[Chris Bagley](/People/chris-bagley)**
  was asked about footage that "appears to end while you're still on the roof," and reportedly
  explained there is no roof footage "because his battery must have died" — right at that moment
  ([primary post](https://x.com/JOKAQARMY1/status/2074257725697970542),
  [Officer Bagley Body Cam](/Law_Enforcement/officer-bagley-bodycam)).
- The single recording was described as **27:35** long, starting at the flag-holding area and
  ending on the roof **before crime-scene tape was up** — reportedly the only body-cam footage he
  had that day ([Bagley Bodycam Gap](/CoverUp/Bagley_Bodycam_Gap)).
- Bagley reportedly heard the shot at roughly **12:23 p.m.** and reached the Losee Center roof
  about **12:44 p.m.**; he reportedly did not recharge or swap the battery because it was "too
  chaotic."
- The account's hearing coverage (with **@realtoriabrooke** and **@TPostMillennial**) is a cited
  source for the site's [testimony](/court/testimony) and
  [hearing witnesses](/court/hearing-witnesses) pages, including the screwdriver and DNA testimony.

## Investigative value

The clip is the **public entry point** for the body-cam blackout question — paired on the site with
the Candace Owens podcast claim that other police officers called the sudden blackout highly
implausible (anonymous, uncorroborated). It made Bagley's testimony reviewable outside the
courtroom while broadcast of key video was limited.
