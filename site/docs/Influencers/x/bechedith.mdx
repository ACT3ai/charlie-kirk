# Bechedith — X Posts

[@Bechedith](https://x.com/Bechedith) is a small account whose single most consequential
contribution is a **clip share**: the ~7.3-minute video of attorney and radio host
**Baron Coleman** reading the **Bates 003996-R2** Miranda transcript from a defense filing.
Posted July 12, 2026 — replying "He already covered this" to @baroncoleman — the clip became a
primary exhibit in the custody-timeline dispute.

## Claims from X posts

- Shared the Coleman video in which he reads a **defense motion** quoting the Bates-stamped
  Washington County video exhibit **003996-R2**: at **6:25 PM** an officer reads
  [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) his rights, and at **6:26 PM** Robinson asks for
  counsel ("a fellow in Utah named Doug Terry") — the reading embedded on the
  [Mirandize overview](/court/mirandize/mirandize-overview).
  ([Source](https://x.com/Bechedith/status/2076295071326929264))
- The clip underpins the argument that the September 11 Miranda timing sits **before** the
  Discord confession messages — the timeline tension examined on
  [Miranda Defense Filing](/Suspicious/Defense_Attorneys/miranda-defense-filing), where the
  claim is rated MODERATE and notably rests on **a document**, not just commentary.
- The same custody-versus-confession logic feeds
  [Proof Not Tyler — Discord Custody Timeline](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Discord_Custody_Timeline).

## Investigative value

This account shows how a low-follower clip share can move a case debate when the underlying
material is a **court filing** rather than speculation. The strength here is Coleman's sourcing
("This is from the defense. This was a motion they filed"); the open work is docket-checking
whether a suppression motion exists under seal. Robinson is charged, not convicted.
