# RickyTheGuido — X Posts

[@RickyTheGuido](https://x.com/RickyTheGuido) appears in the site's distraction-people record as one
of the commentary accounts framing the on-scene arrests as coordinated misdirection. The bullets
below are **claims attributed to their X posts** — poster framing, not adjudicated findings.

## Claims from X posts

- The account posted: *"What do [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) and [George Zinn](/distraction_people/George_Zinn)
  both have in common? They were both patsies in the assassination of Charlie Kirk."* — an
  allegation of a designed operation, quoted on the George Zinn page as skeptic framing.
- The framing is used by skeptics to argue that Zinn's admitted obstruction (his false "I shot him"
  claim at the scene) undermines a clean **"lone wolf" capture story**; official sources treat
  Zinn's obstruction as real but uncoordinated.
- The handle is cited among the independent X/YouTube commentary sources behind
  [The List of Distraction People (Claims)](/CoverUp/Distraction_People), alongside @TheCKFiles,
  @DiligentDenizen, @sonnydayzzzz, and others.

## Investigative value

The account documents how the **"two patsies" reading** — Robinson and Zinn as expendable figures —
circulated during trial discussion. It is a map of the skeptic narrative, not evidence. Robinson is
charged and not convicted; Zinn's court record covers obstruction, not participation in the killing;
no living, uncharged person is found here to have participated in any crime.
