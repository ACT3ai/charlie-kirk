# blesamerica — X Posts

[@blesamerica](https://x.com/blesamerica) originated one of the most-cited **camera-handling
claims** in the case: that the recording chip closest to the wound angle was pulled within
minutes of the shot. The claim is attributed video interpretation; TPUSA has reportedly said any
media handling was at police instruction or theft prevention, and no court has found intentional
evidence destruction by any named staffer.

## Claims from X posts

- Posted (**September 17, 2025**, widely reshared) that a camera covering the area was **taken
  down and its chip removed within about five minutes** of the shot — before the zone was secured
  as a crime scene — pointing to video of "2 guys" allegedly tampering with equipment. Cited on
  [Signs of Cover-Up](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/signs-of-cover-up).
- Named (with @Uncommonsince76 and @sj27426) as a source for the claim that the **rear
  "behind-the-head" camera's** chip was reportedly removed within ~5 minutes — the camera facing
  the wound and rooftop line — on
  [SD Cards and Cameras Removed](/CoverUp/SD_Cards_And_Cameras_Removed) and, as context, on
  [Hospital Security Cameras Removed](/Medical/hospital_security_cameras_removed).
- Grouped with @DiligentDenizen and @stratagemmer compilations alleging AV-staff camera actions
  and chain-of-custody breaks on the
  [social media analysis overview](/social_media_analysis/overview) — attributed anomaly lists,
  not adjudicated guilt.

## Investigative value

The five-minute chip claim is narrow and testable: raw bystander video of the minutes after the
shot either shows the camera coming down or it does not, and evidence logs either record original
cards or they do not. That specificity is why the claim recurs across the cover-up pages. The
innocent explanations (police instruction, theft prevention, chaos) are documented alongside it.
