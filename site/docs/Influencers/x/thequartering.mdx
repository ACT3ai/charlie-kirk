# TheQuartering — X Posts

[@TheQuartering](https://x.com/TheQuartering) is a large commentary account cited across this site's [Gun & Bullet section](/Gun_Bullet/Gun_Bullet_Investigation_Index) for a **precision point about the ATF ballistics filings** that both camps tend to flatten. The bullets below are **claims attributed to their X posts**, not lab findings.

## Claims from X posts

- Defense filings cite ATF experts **"unable to identify"** the fragmented bullet to the **specific Mauser** — which is **not** a finding that the bullet "wasn't .30-06" ([ATF Fragment Inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive)).
- That framing is quoted alongside @ShekandaPeoples' technical summary (jacket suitable for comparison, lead unsuitable, insufficient individual characteristics) and counters from @misfitpatriot_ and @OfVoice35353 arguing fragments measuring ~.286–.301" fit the .30-caliber family ([Mauser .30-06 Rifle](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle)).
- The point recurs on the sibling ballistics pages — [Alternative Calibers](/Gun_Bullet/Alternative_Calibers_Weapons), [Thirty-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch), [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle), [Rifle Fingerprints Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Rifle_Fingerprints_Mismatch), [Gun Dropped Location](/Gun_Bullet/Gun_Dropped_Location), and [Bullet Stage Wood](/Gun_Bullet/Bullet_Stage_Wood) — as the anti-overclaim caveat.

## Investigative value

Ballistics is one of the most active Kirk threads on X, and headline shorthand routinely converts "inconclusive" into "no match." @TheQuartering's contribution is the **discipline check**: "cannot identify or exclude" keeps the rifle question open in both directions. All ballistics claims await full public exhibits and independent review.
