# cletus_jethro — X Posts

[@cletus_jethro](https://x.com/cletus_jethro) is a self-described **former USMC explosive breacher**
(credentials self-reported and unverified) whose December 2025 thread proposed the
**shaped-charge crucifix-pendant** variant of the concealed-explosive theory. The thread was
reshared June 21, 2026 by @matt82704417
([post](https://x.com/matt82704417/status/2068506485420892470)) and its screenshots are archived on
this site. Everything below is the author's stated analysis and opinion, not established fact — no
explosive device has been recovered, and the official finding remains a rifle shot.

## Claims from X posts

- **Skeptic of the lapel-mic version** — argues an explosive in an *unanchored* lapel microphone
  "makes no sense" because the blast would push the mic away rather than drive a penetrator inward.
  See [Mic Explosive](/CoverUp/Mic_Explosive).
- **Proponent of the pendant version** — proposes a cone-shaped **PETN** micro-charge in the center
  column of [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s crucifix, anchored by the heavy silver box
  chain, using the **Munroe effect** to focus an upward blast toward the neck. Full write-up:
  [The Exploding Pendant](/Mic/exploding-pendant-shaped-charge).
- **PETN math** — cites PETN's relative effectiveness (1.66 vs TNT) and 8,400 m/s detonation
  velocity; argues if a bullet had struck, neck damage would appear before the strange shirt
  movement, not after. Thread screenshots:
  [Mic Exploding Pendant cluster](/Photos/Mic/Mic_Exploding_Pendant/overview).
- **Electrocution trigger** — proposes detonation made dependent on **electrocution through the
  target's own body**, arguing this builds in redundancy and synchronizes the blast with a cover
  sound. Rated **SPECULATIVE** on
  [electrocution trigger](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/electrocution-trigger).
- Linked the reported federal search for the necklace to a hunt for **explosive residue** — see
  [necklace and residue](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/necklace-and-residue).

## Investigative value

The most technically detailed proponent of any concealed-charge mechanism — and simultaneously a
rebuttal source against the rival mic-pack version. That proponents place the alleged device in
three different locations (mic, pendant, ear) is itself documented as a weakness of the theory.
