# STARFORCEHH — X Posts

[@STARFORCEHH](https://x.com/STARFORCEHH) (posting as **Freemason**) authored one of the most-shared
geometric arguments against the rooftop-shot account, preserved in the site's ballistics photo
cluster. The bullets below are **claims attributed to their X posts** — reasoning from second-hand
medical descriptions, since the autopsy is not public.

## Claims from X posts

- Citing "new medical data," the post states the bullet entered [Charlie
  Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s neck at **C2**, obliterated **C3 through C7**, and lodged in **T1**;
  that Kirk was leaning slightly forward, struck slightly left of the Adam's apple.
- Argues that with a gradual **ten-degree downward angle** from the
  [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center) roof at the range attributed to
  [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson), entry should have been around **C7 or T1** — concluding
  a C2 entry is "mathematically impossible" and that the official story "has become nothing more
  than a tall tale."
  ([Full-screen capture](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Photo_a540d0))
- A visible reply from a nurse account argues the opposite — that the bullet **hit the edge of body
  armor and ricocheted upward** — and the site preserved the composite precisely because it captures
  both sides in one frame.
  ([Thread capture](/Photos/Ballistics_Gun/Img_Photo_23d662))

## Investigative value

The argument is purely geometric and can only be tested against actual autopsy measurements, which
remain sealed — the recurring problem with every trajectory claim in the case. The structured
version of this dispute lives at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle) and
[Trajectory and Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch).
