# DaniFesto — X Posts

[@_DaniFesto](https://x.com/_DaniFesto) is the analyst most cited across the site's ballistics pages for the **entry-angle argument** — that the geometry of a rooftop shot does not fit the reported wound location. The bullets below are **claims attributed to their X posts**, not official findings.

## Claims from X posts

- A shot from the [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center) roof (~120 m east, roughly **5–9° downward**) would likely strike the **face or jaw first** — not the throat. ([Primary post](https://x.com/_DaniFesto/status/2039372441068155219))
- The reported entry is instead **between the larynx and trachea, left of the cervical spine** — a more **straight-on** path.
- Quoting [@AndrewKolvet](./andrewkolvet)-sourced wound descriptions, the thread argues prosecutors ask the public to accept a bullet entering at **C2** from ~5–9° down that "magically" missed the face and jaw before traveling to C6/C7 and the thoracic cavity — summarized at [Wound Trajectory Angle](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle).
- A .30-06 fired from the east would be expected to produce a catastrophic neck exit and blood on the white tarp — disputed against scene descriptions. See [Ballistics and Wound Analysis](/Medical/ballistics_and_wound_analysis).

## Investigative value

The angle-versus-entry argument is one of the pillars of [Trajectory–Wound Mismatch](/Proof_Not_Tyler/Trajectory_Wound_Mismatch) and appears in the [Tyler Robinson shooting overview](/Tyler_Robinson/Shooting/overview) and [Weapon &amp; Rifle](/Tyler_Robinson/Weapon_Rifle) pages. It is an independent analyst claim built on reported wound locations that remain sealed — the autopsy report is not public, which is why the argument cannot yet be closed either way.
