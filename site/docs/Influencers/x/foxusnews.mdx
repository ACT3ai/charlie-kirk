# Fox News US — X Posts

[@FoxUSNews](https://x.com/FoxUSNews) is a Fox News US-desk account whose July 2026 courtroom
reporting entered the investigation record as documentation of the **court's media control**
during the [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) preliminary hearing. Items below are
reported claims from the account's X posts.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 10, 2026 (Day 5)** — Reported Judge **Tony Graf**'s **"Effective immediately"** media
  **sanction**: an order to stop capturing or broadcasting **exhibits** after a prior-order
  violation. ([Primary post](https://x.com/FoxUSNews/status/2075622132952777080)) — logged in the
  claims table on [Legal Process, Gag Orders, and Secrecy](/After/Legal_Process_Gag_Orders_And_Secrecy).
- The same report is read two ways in the discourse: citizen accounts frame the sanction as
  **narrative control**, while mainstream accounts frame it as ordinary **courtroom order** —
  the divergence tracked on [Media Narratives and Censorship After](/After/Media_Narratives_And_Censorship_After).
- The sanction followed a **leaked exhibit** (reported as Robinson's note to Lance Twiggs), after
  which the court moved to shield remaining exhibits — see the [2026 timeline](/Timeline/2026).

## Investigative value

A mainstream wire-adjacent account documenting an exhibit-broadcast ban is useful precisely
because it is not a citizen-investigator claim: the sanction itself is a court action reported
by press in the room. What the sanction **means** — process housekeeping or information
lockdown — remains commentary on both sides, and this site records both readings.
