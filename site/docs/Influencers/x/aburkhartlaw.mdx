# Andrea Burkhart — X Posts

[@aburkhartlaw](https://x.com/aburkhartlaw) (**Andrea Burkhart**, an attorney and legal commentator — see her [People page](/People/andrea-burkhart)) publicly itemized the State's evidence package around the **July 2026 preliminary hearing** of Tyler Robinson. Her inventories are the site's most-cited source for what the prosecution actually presented. Robinson is charged, not convicted; the items below are **attributed public inventories and commentary**, not court findings of guilt.

## Claims from X posts

- **Day-of items in the State's package** — a **screwdriver** (DNA mixture discussed in court), a **sniper pad** on the [Losee Center](/UVU/Losee_Center) roof, and **shoe impressions/scuffs** at the northeast drop corner, alongside the Mauser recovery. Logged on [September 10, 2025](/Timeline/Sept_10_2025).
- **Texts beginning ~11 PM** attributed to Robinson and roommate [Lance Twiggs](/People/lance-twiggs), later extracted from [Twiggs' phone](/court/Twiggs_Phone) — allegedly admitting the shooting and describing a towel-wrapped rifle in a bush; authorship remains contested by custody-first researchers. See [Sept 8–13 timeline](/Timeline/Sept_8_to_13).
- The prelim inventory names **[Mike Mitchell](/People/mike-mitchell)** as the family friend who helped coordinate surrender with Washington County; WCSO **interview-room video** of Robinson's entry was described as existing.
- The State's package, per her inventories, treats the Discord messages as **pre-surrender, subpoena-authenticated, and roommate-identified**, and treats **~9 PM** arrival as the sworn surrender time — the counter-reading to the 6:25 PM custody-first argument on [Mirandizing](/Timeline/Tyler_Robinson/Mirandizing).

## Investigative value

Burkhart's lane is unusual on this site: rather than advancing a theory, her inventories describe **what the State says it has** — which both prosecution-side and skeptic-side pages cite. Her material anchors the [Tyler Robinson September timeline](/Timeline/Tyler_Robinson/2025_Sept) and gives the custody-versus-Discord debate its clearest statement of the official position.
