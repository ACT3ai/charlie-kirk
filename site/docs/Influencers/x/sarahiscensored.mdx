# SarahisCensored — X Posts

[@SarahisCensored](https://x.com/SarahisCensored) is cited across the site's FBI section as a
**July 2026 preliminary-hearing reporter** — one of the accounts whose courtroom posts documented
what was shown in court but withheld from the public feed. The bullets below are **claims attributed
to their X posts**, not official transcripts.

## Claims from X posts

- Reported (alongside @TPostMillennial) that **rooftop / surveillance video** was shown to the
  [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) family in court but **not broadcast**, and that **Judge Tony
  Graf** reportedly limited enhanced playback. See [Gag Orders](/FBI/Gag_Orders).
- Reported (with @FrontlinesTPUSA) that **enhanced rooftop footage** was shown in court — while
  skeptic accounts allege start-time gaps and vehicle mismatches in garage footage offered as
  [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson); OSINT disputes, not dispositions. See
  [Official Narrative](/FBI/Official_Narrative).
- Part of the hearing-watch cluster (with @CrimeTalkNet) reporting that Judge Graf **delayed
  bind-over** for written briefs, with counsel for Erika Kirk seeking a firm ruling timeline. See
  [FBI Investigation](/FBI/FBI_Investigation).

## Investigative value

The account matters for a narrow, checkable reason: it documents the gap between what the courtroom
saw and what the public record contains. Investigators argue non-broadcast video plus sealed filings
recreate gag-order effects even where formal gag text is narrower. Process limits are not proof of
impropriety; they extend the period without primary public video.
