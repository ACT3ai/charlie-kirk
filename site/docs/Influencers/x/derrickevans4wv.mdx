# Derrick Evans — X Posts

[@DerrickEvans4WV](https://x.com/DerrickEvans4WV) (**Derrick Evans**) is one of the high-engagement
accounts covering the **public-access fight** in the [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) court
proceedings. His posts frame courtroom transparency rulings as wins against a "hide everything"
strategy. All items below are attributed claims from X posts, not findings of this site.

## Claims from X posts

- **March 14, 2026** — Celebrated a Utah judge **rejecting** efforts to keep records sealed and
  **allowing cameras** at a Robinson hearing, framing the ruling (alongside **@GuntherEagleman**)
  as slamming the door on sealing and granting **public access to key documents** — tracked on
  [Evidence Sealing 2026](/Legal/Evidence-Sealing-2026).
- The same ruling wave is logged as a **transparency milestone** on the
  [post-assassination investigations timeline](/Timeline/post-assassination-investigations),
  reportedly showing Judge **Tony Graf** keeping most filings public.
- His posts sit within the [Fourth District Court in Provo](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo)
  coverage — the physical hub of the **gag-order vs cameras** tension, where counter-narratives
  (e.g. @xRevelationOne) argue the gag order silences parties and thousands of potential witnesses.

## Investigative value

Evans' posts anchor one side of the 2026 **transparency vs secrecy** ledger the site tracks:
open-courtroom optics coexisting with **still-sealed** forensics (autopsy, full ballistics
appendices, unredacted warrants). His March 2026 amplification is cited on the
[Legal section overview](/Legal/overview) as part of what citizen investigators watch.
