# JGlasseir — X Posts

[@JGlasseir](https://x.com/JGlasseir) is a citizen-investigator account focused on the
**ballistics-match question**: whether the bullet recovered from [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)
can be tied to the rifle recovered near UVU. The bullets below are attributed X claims — contested
forensics summaries, not verdicts.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 10, 2026** — Stated that Utah State ballistic experts **"CANNOT confirm"** the bullet found
  in Charlie was shot by the **Mauser Model 98** recovered in the woods on September 11, 2025,
  linking mainstream coverage of the vintage, hard-to-trace rifle
  ([Rifle Chain of Custody](/Law_Enforcement/rifle-chain-of-custody)).
- Listed among the **July 2026 amplifying accounts** (with @HotSpotHotSpot, @KellyOMeara5556,
  @Ariana_J_21) on the **sealed four-page ATF report** described in a news-media filing as
  **inconclusive**, and on commentary alleging the FBI might revive discredited **Comparative
  Bullet Lead Analysis** ([Ballistics Sealed / CBLA](/CoverUp/Ballistics_Sealed_CBLA)).
- The non-match theme aligns with hearing summaries that fragments **"could not be identified or
  excluded"** from the recovered rifle — while prosecutors still cite DNA, a spent casing, and a
  towel as physical links.
- **Counterpoint widely noted:** "inconclusive" does not equal exoneration, and observation limits
  on comparison microscopy are standard lab policy.

## Investigative value

The account keeps the **contested-forensics record** visible: the gap between the charging
narrative's rifle and what public filings say the lab could actually confirm. Read with
[Gun & Bullet analysis](/Gun_Bullet/overview) for the full mechanism debate.
