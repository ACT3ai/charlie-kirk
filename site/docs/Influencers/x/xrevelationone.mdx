# xRevelationOne — X Posts

[@xRevelationOne](https://x.com/xRevelationOne) is the account most cited on this site for the
**gag-order and court-secrecy** lane of *State v. Tyler Robinson*. Their posts frame Judge **Tony
Graf**'s speech restrictions as locking in the early official narrative; supporters counter that
these are ordinary, lawful tools for protecting a fair capital trial. Both readings are recorded;
neither is a site finding about judicial motive.

## Claims from X posts

- Described the **December 16, 2025 gag order** as "unusually expansive" and issued *sua sponte* —
  on the judge's own motion, with neither side requesting it — reportedly binding parties, counsel,
  witnesses, staff, and even non-case attorneys inside the involved firms. Developed at
  [Gag Order and Sealed Court](/CoverUp/Gag_Order_Sealed_Court).
- **July 6, 2026** — Stated the gag reaches **prosecution, defense, and potentially thousands of
  witnesses**, framing it as freezing an early FBI narrative — a scope claim the site marks as
  unverified against the full order text; see
  [Gag Orders and Sealing](/court/gag-orders-sealing) and
  [DOJ Prosecution and Secrecy](/Legal/DOJ_Prosecution_And_Secrecy).
- Their commentary anchors the secrecy ledger on
  [Legal Process, Gag Orders and Secrecy](/After/Legal_Process_Gag_Orders_And_Secrecy),
  [Evidence Sealing 2026](/Legal/Evidence-Sealing-2026),
  [Official Statements](/Legal/Official-Statements), and the
  [Fourth District Court Provo](/Locations/Fourth_District_Court_Provo) venue page.

## Investigative value

This account defines one side of the case's central transparency tension: open-courtroom optics
(cameras allowed, sealing motions denied) coexisting with broad speech locks and sealed forensics.
The disclosure gaps it catalogs are exactly what the [Fix Laws](/Fix/overview) target.
