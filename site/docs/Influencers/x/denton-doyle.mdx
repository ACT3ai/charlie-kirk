# denton_doyle — X Posts

[@denton_doyle](https://x.com/denton_doyle) is one of the **pro-pattern voices** in the Google
Trends / search-pattern debate — the most contested OSINT thread in the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)
investigation. The account is cited across the site's [Google Searches section](/GoogleSearches/investigation-index)
as arguing that pre-event search activity deserves formal investigation. All items below are
attributed claims from X posts, not findings of this site.

## Claims from X posts

- Pre-event **Google Trends activity** on the names of case figures, the hospital, the surgeon,
  and **rifle-drop locations** is irregular enough to warrant investigation.
- Search interest allegedly clustered in **Israel, Washington DC, and Alabama geographies**
  before September 10, 2025 — see [Israel IP search patterns](/GoogleSearches/israel-ip-search-patterns)
  and [DC metro search patterns](/GoogleSearches/dc-metro-search-patterns).
- The account's arguments feed the **[July 20, 2025 anchor-window](/GoogleSearches/july-20-2025-anchor)**
  reading, which treats a midsummer spike cluster as possible planning-phase signal.
- Related threads touch [Trends deletion and suppression claims](/GoogleSearches/trends-deletion-suppression) —
  allegations of post-hoc scrubbing that are hard to falsify without third-party archives.

Skeptic voices (including [@paramounttactcl](./paramounttactcl) and Tim Pool summaries) counter
that Trends is **sampled noise** and not a historical record, especially at single-digit volumes.

## Investigative value

@denton_doyle is a named proponent in the site's rule-framed treatment of Trends evidence:
anomalies are **leads, not identification of a living searcher**, and never proof of
foreign-state involvement. The account appears alongside @ZachCostello_ and the
[Baron Coleman](../podcasts-baron) ecosystem across fourteen Google Searches pages, including
[judges](/GoogleSearches/judges-graf-lunnen-searches), [Kathryn Nester](/GoogleSearches/kathryn-nester-searches),
[Timpanogos medical](/GoogleSearches/timpanogos-medical-searches), and
[Tyler Robinson / Lance Twiggs](/GoogleSearches/tyler-robinson-lance-twiggs-searches) queries.
