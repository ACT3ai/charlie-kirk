# GigaBeers — X Posts

[@GigaBeers](https://x.com/GigaBeers) is a high-engagement account in the **court-transparency**
lane of the investigation record, cited for its June 2026 coverage of access rulings in the
[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) case. Items below are attributed claims from X posts,
not findings of this site.

## Claims from X posts

- **June 1, 2026** — Reported and praised Judge **Tony Graf** **denying** a defense bid to close
  the preliminary hearing and block cameras — public-access framing.
  ([Primary post](https://x.com/GigaBeers/status/2061541956724076640)) — logged in the claims
  table on [Legal Process, Gag Orders, and Secrecy](/After/Legal_Process_Gag_Orders_And_Secrecy).
- With [@EricLDaugh](./ericldaugh), celebrated Graf denying efforts to **close/seal** substantial
  preliminary-hearing material and **allowing cameras** — the "keep it all public" framing after
  Charlie was killed in public, tracked on [gag orders and sealing](/court/gag-orders-sealing).
- The account's posts sit in the site's balanced ledger on [Judge Tony Graf](/court/judge-tony-graf):
  transparency praise on one side, gag-breadth concern (@xRevelationOne and others) on the other.

## Investigative value

GigaBeers documents the **public-access side** of the 2026 secrecy fight. The site's framing:
more public video at the prelim can coexist with still-sealed digital forensics, medical files,
and warrants — so camera wins are tracked alongside what remains locked. Neither reading of
Graf's motives is a site finding; no court has found judicial misconduct in this matter.
