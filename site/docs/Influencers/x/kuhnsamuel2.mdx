# kuhnsamuel2 — X Posts

[@kuhnsamuel2](https://x.com/kuhnsamuel2) posts in the **faked-confession lane** of the case —
the argument that the Discord messages attributed to Tyler Robinson post-date his custody. The
items below are **attributed personal beliefs from X posts**, not court findings. No person is
found by this site to have fabricated any message.

## Claims from X posts

- **July 11, 2026** — Placed the Discord confession at around **8:57 PM on September 11** and
  stated the belief that the messages "were faked to frame him while he was in custody" — an
  explicit authorship allegation the site records as an attributed claim
  ([Faked Confession](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/faked-confession)).
- The **8:57 PM timestamp is an outlier**: it sits roughly an hour later than the ~7:57 PM figure
  used in most defense summaries. The site notes both cannot be canonical without primary server
  metadata — but either way, both sit **after** the reported 6:25 PM Miranda window
  ([US Intelligence — Faked Confession](/US_Intelligence_Assisted/faked-confession)).
- The claim circulates alongside the wider mid-2026 custody-gap package — the "33-hour cover-up"
  slogan, platform-denial claims, and handwriting questions — documented across
  [Custody vs Discord Confession](/CoverUp/Custody_Confession_Timeline).

## Investigative value

The account's contribution is a **timestamp variant** that sharpened the demand for primary
Discord server metadata. Whether the confession preceded or followed custody is the central
sequencing dispute in the Proof Not Tyler section; competing Version B framings place physical
surrender near 9 PM, making the message pre-surrender.
