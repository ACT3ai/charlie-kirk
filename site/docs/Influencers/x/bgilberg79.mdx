# bgilberg79 — X Posts

[@bgilberg79](https://x.com/bgilberg79) is a frame-analysis account whose **July 12, 2026**
posts feed both the ballistics-doubt and device-theory pages on this site. Its single core claim
is a visual-interpretation argument about the order of events at the moment of the shot. That
claim is attributed here as citizen video analysis — not forensic examination.

## Claims from X posts

- Argued from **frame-by-frame analysis** that Charlie Kirk's shirt moved from **something
  internal** before the wound appeared — cited on
  [A .30-06 Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie)
  as a claim overlapping the device theory rather than pure external ballistics.
- The same internal-motion reading is used by exploding-device proponents as
  **order-of-events support** on
  [Explosive Mic Killed Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/explosive-mic-killed-charlie),
  independent of Jon Aaron Bray's longer write-ups.
- The claim circulates alongside — and is contested by — detailed technical debunks (e.g.
  @alexboge's shaped-charge and residue arguments), which the site preserves on the same pages.

## Investigative value

One narrow, checkable claim: does high-frame-rate footage show fabric motion preceding the
visible wound, and if so what causes it? That is answerable with original-resolution video and
imaging expertise, which is why the site logs the claim despite its contested status. Autopsy
findings entered at the July 2026 preliminary hearing state cause of death as gunshot wound to
the neck; the device theory remains a minority interpretation.
