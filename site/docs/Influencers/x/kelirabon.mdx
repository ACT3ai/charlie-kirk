# KeliRabon — X Posts

[@KeliRabon](https://x.com/KeliRabon) contributed widely viewed **video analysis of the seconds
after the shot**, focused on the behavior of Charlie Kirk's staff and security. The items below
are **claims attributed to their X posts**, framed by the poster as questions — not accusations
of wrongdoing against any living person.

## Claims from X posts

- **October 24, 2025** — Circulated a widely viewed analysis clip arguing that Chief of Staff
  [Mikey McCoy](/People/mikey-mccoy), standing near Kirk, **moved away with a phone to his ear
  within a split second** of the shot, without appearing to render aid. Rabon framed this as a
  question about **who was called and why**, not as courtroom proof of conspiracy.
- Security-protocol critiques attributed to @KeliRabon-style frame analyses raise questions about
  **medical handling by the team generally** — the site notes on the
  [Rick Cutler](/People/rick-cutler) page that these critiques are about **process**, not proven
  individual guilt, and that no official ballistic finding links Cutler to the shot.

## Investigative value

The McCoy departure clip is one of the core exhibits in the staff-behavior debate that spans the
[Security Team](/Security_Team/overview) and the "seconds vs minutes" phone-call dispute involving
Erika Kirk's public interview language. It documents observable video behavior while the
interpretation — routine crisis response versus something needing explanation — remains contested.
