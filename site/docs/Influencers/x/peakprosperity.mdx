# Chris Martenson (Peak Prosperity) — X Posts

[@peakprosperity](https://x.com/peakprosperity) (**Dr. Chris Martenson**, Peak Prosperity) is cited
across the site's ballistics and wound-analysis pages for a video-based breakdown of the injury to
[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk). His analysis is mostly cited **secondhand** in the
investigation file — via amplifiers such as @ProjectConstitu and @stevendenoon — and is his
reported analytical opinion, not an official finding.

## Claims from X posts

- The neck injury reflects a **"high-speed cavitation event"** — overpressure expanding the neck,
  **snapping the necklace**, and producing a clenched-fist **"pugilist pose"** attributed to
  midbrain trauma. See [Necklace, Cavitation & Residue](/Suspicious/Medical/necklace-cavitation-residue).
- The round was **not a .30-06**: that cartridge carried **"too much energy, would've knocked him
  flying."** This claim anchors the [.30-06 Energy Mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch)
  and [.30-06 Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie) pages.
- He reportedly favors a **lower-velocity fragmenting round** — a **9mm +P at 1,330 fps** that
  "fragments like hell, dumps energy inside" — a profile commentators tie to
  [Close-Range Theories](/Killer/Close_Range_Theories).
- His cavitation reading is weighed in the [entrance-or-exit](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/entrance-or-exit)
  and [energy-mismatch](/Suspicious/Cause_of_Death/energy-mismatch) analyses, alongside Candace
  Owens's rigged-microphone argument.
- Other posters cite a claimed trajectory match between his wound analysis and a possible
  Sorensen Center origin, discussed in [Tent Shooter Theories](/Tent/Tent_Shooter_Theories).

## Investigative value

Martenson supplies the most-cited **medical-physics framing** of the wounding pattern. The site
flags that **no direct URL to his own analysis is recorded** in the investigation file — his
conclusions are relayed through other accounts, so verification against his primary posts remains
an open task. His profile page: [Chris Martenson](/People/chris-martenson).
