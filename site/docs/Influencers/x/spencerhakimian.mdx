# SpencerHakimian — X Posts

[@SpencerHakimian](https://x.com/SpencerHakimian) enters the record through a single high-reach
episode: viral posts (**tens of millions of views**) about the Dairy Queen sighting still released
by [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens). The site documents the posts as a **timeline-mutation
case study** — attributed claims, later corrected.

## Claims from X posts

- Viral posts read Owens' still of [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) at the **Dairy Queen at
  813 E 800 N in Orem** as roughly **17 minutes after the shot** (~12:38 PM MDT) — implying an
  impossibly calm suspect minutes after the killing. See
  [Tyler Robinson's Post-Shot Escape](/Timeline/tyler-robinson-post-shot-escape).
- **Community Notes and Owens' own presentation** place the still at **6:38 PM MDT** — about a
  17-minute *drive* from campus, six hours later. The 12:38-vs-6:38 spread on one image has never
  been publicly adjudicated frame-by-frame; the site records both readings. See
  [Witness Testimony Discrepancies](/Timeline/witness-testimony-discrepancies).
- The episode sits alongside other escape-window claims — the hat-continuity inference, the
  Panguitch Cowboy's Smokehouse receipt — as examples of how **public clocks mutate** in secondary
  retellings.

## Investigative value

The account's reach, not its analysis, is what mattered: one misread timestamp shaped how millions
first understood the escape timeline. The site keeps the episode because timeline hygiene — which
clock, whose annotation, what the primary source actually says — is a recurring failure mode in
this case. Robinson is charged, not convicted.
