# ShekandaPeoples — X Posts

[@ShekandaPeoples](https://x.com/ShekandaPeoples) is cited across the site's
[Gun and Bullet](/Gun_Bullet/overview) section as the account that gave the clearest technical
explainer of what the ATF's **"inconclusive"** finding actually means. The bullets below are
**claims attributed to their X posts** — analyst commentary, not lab documents.

## Claims from X posts

- Summarized the ATF examination of the autopsy fragments: a damaged **.30-caliber jacket fragment**
  plus lead pieces — **lead unsuitable for comparison; jacket suitable but inconclusive** —
  meaning the examination **neither identified nor excluded** the recovered
  [Mauser .30-06](/Gun_Bullet/Mauser_30_06_Rifle). See
  [ATF Fragment Inconclusive](/Gun_Bullet/ATF_Fragment_Inconclusive).
- The explainer is quoted as the technical middle ground between tabloid framing ("the bullet did
  not match") and the counter-camp (e.g. @misfitpatriot_) arguing soft-point .30-06 rounds should
  fragment and that "non-match" is being overclaimed. See
  [.30-06 Did Not Kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie).
- The framing — **insufficient individual characteristics** to match the fragment to Exhibit 1 —
  recurs on the site's pages for the [energy mismatch](/Gun_Bullet/Thirty_06_Energy_Mismatch),
  [wound trajectory](/Gun_Bullet/Wound_Trajectory_Angle),
  [fingerprints](/Gun_Bullet/Rifle_Fingerprints_Mismatch), and
  [drop-location](/Gun_Bullet/Gun_Dropped_Location) debates.

## Investigative value

"Inconclusive" is the single most over-read word in the case, and this account's contribution is
precision: it keeps skeptics from claiming exclusion and prosecutors from claiming identification.
Citizen investigators still treat the result as a hole in a capital case built around a specific
rifle; prosecutors treat it as an expected fragmentation artifact. Both readings stay open.
