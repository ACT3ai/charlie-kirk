# sj27426 — X Posts

[@sj27426](https://x.com/sj27426) is one of the circulating accounts behind the **rear-camera SD
card** claim — a core item in the footage-handling questions the site tracks. The bullets below are
**claims attributed to their X posts** (alongside @blesamerica and @Uncommonsince76), not verified
findings.

## Claims from X posts

- The camera positioned **behind [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s head** — facing the wound
  and the rooftop line — reportedly had its **SD card/chip removed within about five minutes** of
  the shot. See [SD Cards and Cameras Removed](/CoverUp/SD_Cards_And_Cameras_Removed).
- TPUSA has stated, according to reports, that the card was removed **at police instruction or to
  prevent theft** of the equipment — the innocent explanation is recorded alongside the claim.
- The claim is cited for context on the
  [hospital security cameras](/Medical/hospital_security_cameras_removed) page as part of a wider
  pattern of footage reportedly deleted, edited, or withheld elsewhere in the case — not a claim
  about the hospital itself.
- Related commentary (attributed to others on the same pages) names AV personnel and describes a
  staged "mechanical issue" before the shot — unverified social-media identifications of living
  people, presented as allegation only.

## Investigative value

The rear camera would have held the most direct footage of the wound moment and the rooftop line;
whether its recording exists, who took the card, and where it went are precise, answerable
chain-of-custody questions. The point of preserving the claim is not to assign guilt but to
document that footage-handling questions arose immediately and repeatedly.
