# themarketswork — X Posts

[@themarketswork](https://x.com/themarketswork) is cited on this site for one widely recirculated early post: a shared **USMC sniper-style ballistics analysis** of the shot that killed Charlie Kirk. It is treated here as **competing citizen analysis**, not settled metrology.

## Claims from X posts

- **September 16, 2025** (~598 likes, ~276k views; still recirculated) — A **USMC sniper analysis** arguing the shot came from **slightly behind Kirk's right side**, was **not a .30-06**, and that an **exit wound was visible** — a reading that contradicts both the Losee-east rooftop geometry and the "no exit wound" strand at once ([.30-06 did not kill Charlie](/Tyler_Robinson_Not_Assassin/thirty-06-did-not-kill-charlie)).  ([Source](https://x.com/themarketswork/status/1968024348050198752))
- The same share appears in the site's wound-location catalog as part of the **trajectory / exit-wound debate** that followed the early "shot in the heart" eyewitness confusion ([Shot in the Heart](/Narrative/Shot_in_the_heart)).

## Investigative value

The post matters because it is **internally inconsistent with both major camps** — it disputes the official caliber and geometry while also disputing the no-exit claim most skeptics rely on. That makes it a useful reminder that early video-based ballistics readings diverge sharply and none substitutes for the sealed medical examiner file.
