# Trusted Investigators

The proposed **[Law 4: Trusted Investigators](/Fix/overview)** would name private citizens with
demonstrated independence from government and institutional control to lead oversight teams with
law-enforcement and intelligence-community access. The research corpus most often discusses
**three podcast hosts** in that role.

## Why independence matters here

When a case touches **foreign intelligence**, **federal evidence control**, and **rapid narrative
formation**, public confidence depends on investigators the audience already trusts to pursue
contested facts. Mainstream outlets largely deferred to the FBI frame; these hosts did not.

## Defamation note

Listing someone as a "trusted investigator" describes a **proposed legislative role**, not a claim
that they were victimized by any specific living person's illegal act. We do **not** assert that
government officials targeted these hosts criminally unless citing their own reported allegations with
attribution.
