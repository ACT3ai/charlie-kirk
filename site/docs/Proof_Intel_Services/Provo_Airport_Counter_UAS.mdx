# Provo Airport Counter-UAS

CK strange-event **#31** alleges that around the Kirk assassination week, **Egyptian-government-linked
contractors** operated at **Provo Municipal Airport (PVU)** — testing **Counter-UAS** (anti-drone)
equipment with **U.S. Department of Defense Liaison** badges and **government clearance to skip**
normal security. If true, **foreign and U.S. defense-intel personnel** were active **~10 minutes**
from UVU during the event window.

## Reported details (master file)

- **4–6 contractors** associated with the **Egyptian flight** (see [Egyptian Jets](./Egyptian_Jets_Provo))
- Contractors **did not fly away** with the jet — **stayed on ground**
- Activity framed as **not maintenance** — other SU-BTT trips allegedly went to **army bases**; Provo
  described as unusual **non-military-airport** U.S. stop
- **Counter-UAS gear** tested **September 4–10, 2025**

## Why Counter-UAS matters to intel theories

Counter-UAS capability intersects:

- [N1098L drone-deployment theories](/Planes/N1098L/overview) (John Cullen),
- [Drones](/Drones/overview) section generally,
- **Airspace control** during a possible **UAS-assisted** kill (speculative)

Testing the week of a high-profile political shooting is either **scheduled exercise** or **operational
staging** — distinction requires **contractor rosters and FAA/DoD authorizations**.

## Badge list updated Sept 11

Strange event **#8**: **Provo airport badge access list updated 9/11/25** — day after shooting.
Commentators tie this to **contractor cleanup** or **access revocation**. **Unconfirmed** causation.

## Disclosure targets

- PVU security waiver logs Sept 4–11
- Contractor names, employers, and DOD liaison authorizations
- Counter-UAS test orders and participating agencies

## Defamation note

Named contractors unknown publicly. Do **not** assert they participated in the killing.
