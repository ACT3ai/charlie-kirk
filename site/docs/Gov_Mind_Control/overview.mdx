The Kirk investigation keeps returning to one question: could the accused have been **guided, conditioned, or
framed** by something other than spontaneous intent? That question only makes sense against **real programs** —
**MKUltra** and **Project Artichoke** are declassified; the **Frey Effect** is lab-proven; **digital funneling** is
observable at scale on social platforms. This area lays out that record so readers judge case theories on **facts
first**, speculation second.

**Case-specific threads** split three ways. **Historical assassination feasibility** — the **1954 Artichoke memo**
on involuntary shooters — gives a documented CIA interest in exactly this problem. **Tyler-focused frames** —
[custody vs Discord timing](./discord-confession-timing), [patsy scaffolding](./tyler-robinson-patsy-framework),
[handler names in citizen research](./handler-network-claims) — test whether the **narrative** was engineered even if
the **person** was not hypnotized. **Modern “MKUltra 2.0”** — algorithmic radicalization and private-group triggers
— maps how intel *could* manufacture conviction without a lab.

## What Citizen Investigators Emphasize on X

This section exists because citizen investigators refuse to treat "lone radicalized youth" as a complete causal story without testing **influence, coercion, and information operations** hypotheses. X discourse ranges from careful Discord-timing analysis to extreme V2K claims. The overview's job is to **label tiers**: historical CIA programs (documented), theoretical playbooks, and Robinson-specific applications (**unverified**). No page here finds that Robinson was mind-controlled.
