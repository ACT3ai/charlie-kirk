## What the Frey Effect is

According to a June 28, 2026 post by [@UAPLuigi](https://x.com/UAPLuigi/status/2071074071404798401), pulsed microwaves can create audible sensations inside a person's head — clicks, buzzes, chirps, or muffled sounds — **without any speaker or involvement of the ear**. This is the **Frey Effect** (the microwave auditory effect), first noticed near radar installations in World War II and studied in detail by neuroscientist **Allan Frey** in 1961–62. The post explains the mechanism as tiny, rapid heating of brain tissue that creates a pressure wave the cochlea then picks up.

The post notes this much is **real and lab-proven**. It is the established, peer-reviewed core of the topic, distinct from the applications described below.

## From clicks to words

The post states that experiments in the 1970s showed **voice-modulated microwaves could transmit recognizable words**, and that a 1989 U.S. patent — **US4877027A** — describes a system to do exactly this using specific pulsed waveforms. These references are cited as evidence that the concept of beaming intelligible audio (not just buzzes) has been formally documented.

## The V2K claim

"**Voice to Skull**" (V2K) is, in the words of the post, the term used in conspiracy circles for directed-energy weapons that beam clear voices or commands into people's heads for harassment or mind control. The post also notes that official documents reference conceptual non-lethal "voice to skull devices" using pulsed microwaves — including an "electronic scarecrow" concept for birds — and that programs like **MEDUSA** explored crowd-control uses.

The post is careful about the limits of the claim. It states that turning the basic Frey Effect into **practical, long-range, intelligible voice projection** for covert mind control faces huge technical barriers — power, targeting, safety, and consistency — and presents this as the honest gap between proven science and the full V2K allegation.

## The video demonstration

The attached video, which the post bills as a demonstration of **CIA / DARPA's Project Pandora** microwave voice-to-skull technology, shows a live audience demonstration of a directional sound device. From the transcript, the presenter describes the device as emitting a tightly focused, non-spreading **modulated beam** — comparing it to a laser — and then makes it audible by placing a modulation on top of the carrier. Key narrated lines:

> "Now, the sound that you're hearing is being created in this beam. It almost sounds like it's coming from your head. So let me try to scan the whole audience, so everybody gets the experience. Absolutely remarkable."

The presenter scans the beam across the audience so each person individually perceives the sound as originating inside their own head. (The clip is largely the demo audio itself; the transcript above is partial.)
