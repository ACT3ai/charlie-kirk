# Beepers & Walkie-Talkies as Weapons (Claims)

The everyday **pager** — a "beeper" — became the signature weapon of [Operation Grim Beeper](./Operation_Grim_Beeper), the September 2024 attack on [Hezbollah](./Hezbollah). This page covers how ordinary communication devices were reportedly turned into synchronized bombs, and why citizen investigators cite the method when discussing Israeli operational capability in the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) case.

## The Supply-Chain Method

According to reporting on the 2024 attack:

* Hezbollah had reportedly moved to **low-tech pagers and walkie-talkies** specifically to avoid smartphone-based tracking and interception.
* That decision was allegedly anticipated. The devices — reportedly tied to Taiwanese manufacturer **Gold Apollo** and its licensing chain — were said to have been **tampered with during manufacture or transit**, with small charges of [PETN](./petn) and detonators concealed inside the battery compartments.
* On **September 17-18, 2024**, the devices were triggered, reportedly by a signal, causing thousands of near-simultaneous explosions.

The takeaway commentators draw is that the attack required **infiltration of a legitimate commercial supply chain** months in advance — a hallmark of a state intelligence service rather than an individual actor.

## Why It Is Cited in the Kirk Case

According to **[@SwordTruth](/Influencers/x/swordtruth)** on X (July 10, 2026), the pager operation demonstrates that [Mossad](./Mossad) "infiltrated the supply chain of Taiwanese manufacturer Gold Apollo" — an example of reach and patience used to argue that a conventional killing would be "child's play" by comparison.

On this site the point is a **capability reference**, not evidence. No booby-trapped device, tampered supply chain, or remote detonation has been alleged in Kirk's death; the connection is strictly the argument that the same service could act by simpler means. Readers weighing the physical evidence in Kirk's case should turn to the [ballistics analysis](/Gun_Bullet/overview), which examines the actual wound and weapon claims rather than analogies to foreign operations.
