# Israel Hamptons

:::caution Legal Disclaimer
Nothing on this page is a finding of wrongdoing, criminal conduct, or ethical violation by any living person. No attendee is **accused** of involvement in Charlie Kirk's death. What happened at this gathering — its tone, specific exchanges, and outcome — is **disputed along partisan lines**. Both the pressure allegations and the attendee denials are **reported claims**, not court findings.
:::

The **Bridgehampton gathering of August 4-5, 2025** — widely called the **"Hamptons intervention"** after Charlie Kirk's assassination — is one of the most-cited **motive-layer** data points. Hedge-fund manager [Bill Ackman](/People/bill-ackman) convened Charlie and a group of conservative influencers and pro-Israel advocates. [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) and *The Grayzone* later framed it as a pro-Israel **pressure session**; Ackman and several named attendees **strongly deny** that characterization. All accounts remain contested unless corroborated by attendee records or on-record testimony.

## The event itself (as described by Ackman)

- **Not** held at Ackman's personal house. It took place at a hotel in **Bridgehampton — the Topping Rose House**, per Ackman's public statements.
- Ackman **covered costs** (travel, food, lodging) for participants. Charlie Kirk and members of Ackman's team **put together the invitation list**.
- Sessions ran from the **afternoon of August 4 to the afternoon of August 5**, 2025.
- Topics reportedly included politics (e.g., the [Zohran Mamdani](/People/overview) NYC mayoral race), America's economic future, cultural shifts, and foreign policy.
- Ackman states his interactions with Kirk were **"extremely cordial, albeit limited"** and that he offered no money to change Kirk's views.

This gathering is roughly four days after a separate **late-July 2025 off-the-record White House meeting** on countering "woke-right defamation" of Israel — a distinct event Charlie and TPUSA were reportedly **absent** from (see [Before: 2025 meetings](/Before/Political_Pressure_And_Meetings_2025)).

## What Candace Owens and critics allege

According to [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) and commentary threads (often citing *The Grayzone*):

- An **"intense intervention"** with **threats**, a reported **Netanyahu call/offer** of massive funding (~**$150 million** in some accounts), and pressure to change Kirk's Israel stance or cancel [Tucker Carlson](/People/tucker-carlson) at AmericaFest.
- Kirk reportedly felt **"hammered"** and complained privately about **"moral blackmail"** and being made to feel his loyalty was questioned.
- The next day (**Aug 6**), Kirk appeared on [Megyn Kelly](/Influencers/overview)'s show and complained about donor pressure and texts questioning his loyalty.

These are **reported allegations from commentary**, not adjudicated facts.

## The Grayzone report (Sept 15, 2025)

Max Blumenthal's *Grayzone* piece cited **five sources with "intimate knowledge"** plus attendees. Per that reporting: the meeting began on Mamdani but **"went off the rails"** when Ackman confronted Kirk over Israel criticism and platforming critics like Tucker Carlson. One attendee described Kirk as **"hammered," "horrified,"** and afterward worried about Israeli **"blackmail."** An **"unknown British woman"** — later identified as [Natasha Hausdorff](/People/natasha-hausdorff) — allegedly **"screamed"** at Kirk. Hausdorff **categorically denies** screaming at Kirk or any such intervention, calling the accusation "categorically untrue."

## Ackman's denial

[Bill Ackman](/People/bill-ackman) **strongly denied** any "intervention," threats, or blackmail, calling the claims "totally false" and Owens' account **"slander."** To rebut the narrative he:

- Posted **WhatsApp/text exchanges** with Kirk showing friendly post-event interactions (casual dinner and NYC-visit invitations).
- Shared and **endorsed an attendee's summary** as "accurate."
- Stated **no money was offered** to influence Kirk's views. Ackman noted Kirk had previously told him young conservatives were "getting tired of defending Israel."

Ackman later offered a **$1M reward** related to Kirk's death, which he says is unrelated to the gathering.

## Attendee pushback

Several attendees dispute the "intervention" framing:

- [Josh Hammer](/People/josh-hammer): "numerous open-ended conversations, usually moderated by Kirk… only one of which was actually about Israel." Kirk was critical of the **way** some advocates argued, "not critical of the underlying substance," and "only doubled down on his opposition to Islam and suggested that Israel is a natural ally."
- [Xaviaer DuRousseau](/People/xaviaer-durousseau) and others emphasized **normal, robust discussion** and Kirk's continued Israel support.
- [Natasha Hausdorff](/People/natasha-hausdorff) called the "screaming" claim "categorically untrue."

## Seth Dillon deposition shift

A significant update in the research record: **[Carrie Prejean Boller](/People/carrie-prejean-boller)** reportedly questioned [Seth Dillon](/People/seth-dillon) under oath about the gathering. Dillon's account **shifted**:

| When | Dillon's account (as reported) |
|------|------------------------------|
| Sep 16, 2025 (X post) | No "angry confrontation"; enjoyable summer retreat |
| Later, under oath | **"Heated and at times spirited debate"** admitted |

Commentary alleges Dillon and [Josh Hammer](/People/josh-hammer) initially **downplayed** the pressure Charlie faced. Both are **living persons**; this page attributes the contrast to **reported deposition testimony and public posts**, not a court finding of dishonesty or wrongdoing.

## Pro-Israel exit posts (September 2025)

Commentators tie Charlie's **early-September posts** about leaving the pro-Israel movement to escalation after the summer pressure. The posts are a **public breaking point** in motive timelines — not proof of who ordered violence. See [Charlie Quotes](./Charlie_Quotes_Israel).

## Sourcing caution

Corroboration requires: attendee lists, venue records, sworn testimony transcripts, and direct communications. Until then, treat both the pressure allegations **and** the denials as **reported claims**, supporting motive theory only when paired with independent execution evidence.
