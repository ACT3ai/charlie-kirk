A short video circulating on X in **July 2026** replays an older television interview in which a pro-Israel speaker
says that Western politicians who obstruct the destruction of Iran's nuclear program "are going to fall." The person
who reposted it, **[@Wendy7839570959](/Influencers/x/wendy7839570959)**, wrote only: *"I recorded this off Tik tok in November 2025z interesting 🧐"*.

The clip is presented here as **attributed public commentary**, not as evidence of anything. The speaker in the
archival footage is not identified on screen in the copy that circulated, no target is named, and the words
"are going to fall" are not defined by the speaker. The commentator narrating the clip supplies the alarming reading;
the speaker, when pressed, gives a **conventional electoral** answer — that supporters will simply stop voting for
those politicians.

## What Is Said in the Clip

The narrator opens by framing the footage as something viewers have forgotten: *"I bet you don't remember this clip
from a year ago. Take a listen."* The archival audio then runs:

> "The Iranian nuclear program is going to be destroyed. And anyone who gets in the way — it doesn't matter where
> they are, even if they are politicians in the West. They are going to fall."

The narrator stops the tape: *"Wait, what?… Hold on, did you hear what he just said? He threatened politicians in the
West that if they don't agree with Israel's war against Iran, that they're going to fall."*

## The Interviewer Presses for a Definition

The most important part of the clip is the exchange that follows, because it is where the speaker is given the chance
to say what "fall" means. According to the transcript:

> **Interviewer:** "Are you saying Israel will murder politicians in the West, or get rid of them politically through
> bribery or whatever else? What do you mean the politicians in the West will fall if they don't agree to back
> Israel's war against Iran?"
>
> **Speaker:** "Yeah, we will not vote for them anymore."
>
> **Interviewer:** "Oh, well, you don't vote for them now. You're not in our country. What do you mean the politicians
> will fall?"
>
> **Speaker:** "We live in democracy. And we have the right to bring them up and to bring them down. Those who agree
> with [Iran remain] in office — and all the sympathizers with them…"
>
> **Interviewer:** "How are you bringing them down? Who is 'we,' and how are you bringing down politicians in the West
> if they dare to defy Israel? Tell me how you're bringing them down."
>
> **Speaker:** "I already answered you that."
>
> **Interviewer:** "Are you going to murder them, or are you going to bribe them? Are you going to bribe someone else?"

The clip ends without the speaker answering the last question. The narrator closes: *"Is it just me, or does that
admission feel a little different after the Charlie Kirk assassination? I'm just asking questions."*

## Why Citizen Investigators Cite It

This clip is circulated as **motive-adjacent atmosphere**, not as evidence tied to Utah Valley University. It is
grouped with the material on this site covering [Israel's alleged motivation](./motivation), the
[Charlie–Israel friction checklist](./Charlie_Israel_Friction), and the reported

## Counterarguments

Several caveats apply and should be read alongside the clip:

* **The speaker's own answer is electoral.** Asked directly whether he meant murder or bribery, he said voting. The
  narrator's framing — that this is a threat of assassination — is the narrator's inference, not the speaker's
  statement.
* **No identification, no date, no source chain.** The reposter says the clip was recorded off TikTok in
  November 2025; the underlying interview is older still. Neither the broadcaster nor the full unedited exchange is
  provided, so the words could be missing surrounding context.
* **No link to the Kirk case.** Nothing in the clip references Charlie Kirk, UVU, or September 10, 2025. The
  connection is made entirely by the narrator's closing question.
* **The official case names a domestic suspect.** [Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson) is charged — and
  **charged is not convicted**. No court has found that any foreign government, service, or private person ordered
  Charlie Kirk's death.
