According to UVU GRAMA records obtained by the **Daily Caller News Foundation** (50 documents, 112 pages), two separate individuals or groups attempted to flag concerns about the Charlie Kirk event the day before the assassination on **September 10, 2025**. Both were either dismissed or buried in the record.

## Two Unidentified Men at 10:35 PM on September 9

According to document **UVU 25-330:0023**, at **10:35 PM on September 9, 2025**, a UVU staff member reported that someone from Event Services came to their office stating that **two people had showed up at UVU offices asking to talk to police about "a plan."** The staff member wrote that the two individuals were reportedly directed to police.

The message was considered serious enough that it was sent to the **university cabinet** — the university president's office.

Forty-four minutes later, at **11:19 PM**, according to the same GRAMA records, someone responded in the communication chain stating that the two gentlemen "are NOT from a senator's office. They are **GOP reps who played like they knew more than they did!**"

According to @danksterintel, who reportedly reviewed all 112 pages:
- Nobody in the chat asked what "the plan" was
- Nobody documented what the two men told police
- The names of the two men are **REDACTED** in the released records

**14 hours later, Charlie Kirk was shot from a rooftop and killed.**

## Separate Warning Email at 2:23 PM on September 9

According to document **UVU 25-330:0079/0080**, a separate email was sent at **2:23 PM on September 9** through UVU Police Chief **Jeff Long's** communication chain. The subject line read simply: **"FYI."**

The email reportedly stated: *"He wants us to get a message to Charlie Kirk. His phone number is [REDACTED]."*

According to the released records:
- The name of the person wanting to reach Kirk is **REDACTED**
- The phone number is **REDACTED**
- Whether the message was ever delivered is **not documented** anywhere in the 112 pages

## Orem Police Department Involvement

According to the GRAMA records, the **Orem Police Department** sent UVU the Charlie Kirk ticket link on **September 3**, with the subject line: "Charlie Kirk Tickets." After the shooting, Orem PD reportedly stated they were "not involved."

According to @danksterintel, UVU pays Orem PD annually for a **joint Public Order Unit** designed for exactly this type of large public event. It was reportedly never activated for the Charlie Kirk event.

## Emergency Alert Failures

According to the GRAMA records:

- The **first emergency alert**, sent **19 minutes** after the shooting, reportedly falsely stated "suspect in custody." The suspect was not in custody. An estimated 10,000 students remained on campus believing it was safe.
- The **Run-Hide-Fight alert**, sent **88 minutes** after the shooting, reportedly still contained **placeholder text**: *"Shooter in [INSERT location]."* The location was never filled in.

## Security Gaps (As Reported)

According to @danksterintel's review of the 112 pages:

- Faculty had reportedly never been trained on active shooter protocols
- No ambulance was on site
- No outside law enforcement was coordinated
- **6 campus police officers** for a crowd the chief later called **3,000 people**

## Ongoing Redactions

According to @danksterintel, every name in the September 9 communications remains redacted under Utah state law exemptions for "security of public property" and "privacy." The suspect has been in custody for 7 months, the event is over, and there is no ongoing security threat to the campus.

UVU has reportedly acknowledged that **additional documents exist** and has refused to release them.
