# Ring Doorbell Cameras

**Ring** is a residential doorbell-camera brand (owned by Amazon). The released September 10 timeline relies on Ring footage from homes on the streets south of the [UVU](/UVU/overview) campus for three of its morning sightings. Because these are **private neighborhood cameras**, the name attached to the moving figure comes from how investigators and commentators read the footage, not from the cameras themselves.

## Ring sightings on September 10, 2025

Per the doorbell footage described as released by law enforcement, a figure in a **maroon shirt and light-colored shorts** — and later in **jeans and a long-sleeve shirt** after a reported outfit change — was captured by Ring cameras at these times (source: investigation notes, [Charlie_Kirk.txt]):

| Time (MDT) | Location | Action |
|------------|----------|--------|
| **8:07 a.m.** | **S 800 W** | Walking toward campus |
| **11:44 a.m.** | **W 800 S** | Walking (after outfit change) |
| **11:49 a.m.** | **S 800 W** | Walking toward campus again |

These three Ring frames **bookend the morning**: an early approach toward campus, then a return shortly before the late-morning [UVU surveillance](./UVU_Surveillance) rooftop sequence. Intervening sightings come from [WYZE](./WYZE) (8:23, 9:57) and UVU (8:29).

## What Ring footage can and cannot establish

A doorbell camera records whoever passes the front of a single house. It can place a person on a particular street at a particular minute, but it cannot, on its own, confirm identity, intent, or destination. The captions naming the figure "[Tyler Robinson](/People/tyler-robinson)" are interpretations layered onto the footage. For the full chain see [Tyler on Surveillance Cameras](./Tyler_On_Cameras).

## The 9:00 a.m. doorbell-timeline dispute (claim)

Commentators have challenged the doorbell-camera timeline directly. Investigator **Baron Coleman** is credited in citizen-research notes with a **"shadow analysis"** arguing that the released **9:00 a.m. doorbell camera timeline** does not line up with the **sun-shadow angles** visible in the footage (source: [Charlie_Kirk.txt], defense-motion commentary).

This is a **reported analytical claim**, not an official finding. If shadows and timestamps do not match, the entire morning chain that links neighborhood cameras to [UVU](./UVU_Surveillance) weakens — a thread developed on [Tyler as a Placeholder Figure](./Tyler_Placeholder).

## Female-accomplice Ring claim

Separate online commentary raises whether a **Ring camera captured a female figure** alongside the person identified as Robinson. The only person named in connection with that claim is **[Olivia Bishop](/People/olivia-bishop)**, described in notes as an **Orem Police Department records specialist** who reportedly said she had contact with Robinson before the event (source: citizen-research commentary, [Charlie_Kirk.txt]).

This is an **unverified allegation**. Bishop has not been charged with anything. The claim is included because it is part of public discussion of Ring footage — not because this site endorses it.

## Enhanced photos and Ring timeline

Posts tie Baron Coleman's shadow work to broader claims that investigators **passed off AI-enhanced stairwell photos** and released **low-quality** suspect video while HD campus cameras existed (see [Merchant & Travel CCTV](./Merchant_And_Travel_CCTV) for evening frames and [UVU Surveillance](./UVU_Surveillance) for campus HD). Kash Patel reportedly admitted on FOX that stairwell images were **"enhanced"** — commentary argues that undermines fair representation of original scenes.
