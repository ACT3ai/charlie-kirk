# Surveillance Cameras

Nearly the entire public case for Tyler Robinson's movements on the morning of September 10, 2025 is built on camera footage — neighborhood doorbell cameras, a WYZE camera, and UVU's own campus surveillance. This section breaks those sources apart camera by camera so a reader can see exactly which device made each sighting, what it claims to show, and where the gaps and contradictions are.

## What this area covers

The morning timeline released by law enforcement is a chain of camera sightings, not eyewitness identifications. Each link in that chain belongs to a specific device: a **Ring** doorbell on a residential street, a **WYZE** camera on a neighboring property, and **UVU surveillance** on campus. Because most of these are private neighborhood cameras rather than official feeds, the captions attaching the name "Tyler Robinson" to a moving figure come from how investigators and commentators read the footage — not from anything the cameras themselves can prove. This section keeps those distinctions visible.

The substance is in the timestamps and what they do and do not establish. Doorbell and WYZE cameras place a person in maroon and light shorts on the streets south of campus between **8:07 and 9:57 a.m.**, after which an **outfit change** to jeans and a long sleeve shirt is reported; UVU surveillance then picks up a figure in a black American-flag shirt with a distinctive stiff-right-leg gait starting around **11:50 a.m.**, tracking him toward the Losee Center roof. The clothing the suspect wore on camera does not match what Tyler Robinson was wearing when he surrendered that night — a mismatch that sits at the center of the placeholder and decoy questions covered here.

Readers tracing the official narrative should start with [UVU Campus Surveillance](./UVU_Surveillance), the backbone of the affidavit. Readers focused on the neighborhood sightings should read [Ring](./Ring) and [WYZE](./WYZE) together, since those two camera types alternate across the morning. Anyone evaluating identification — whether the figure on camera is actually Tyler — should read [Tyler on Surveillance Cameras](./Tyler_On_Cameras) for the full sighting list and [Tyler as a Placeholder Figure](./Tyler_Placeholder) for the clothing-mismatch and decoy arguments.
