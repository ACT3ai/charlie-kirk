The Other section holds leads and methods that cut across FBI, UVU, Planes, and Media. The pages above focus on claims backed by named sources or documented records.

This website does not assert as fact that any living person named in this section committed a crime, did anything wrong or illegal, or had any knowledge of or involvement in the assassination. Names appear here only in the context of public discussion and reporting. Nothing here is a finding by this website.

Investigation methodology explains how this site labels facts vs. allegations and why March 2026 seal dates and gag orders matter. Evidence contradictions and forensic gaps catalog timeline and ballistics conflicts that sealed discovery would adjudicate. Investigation challenges and witness reliability frame what citizen researchers can and cannot verify today.

The [Investigation Methodology](./Investigation_Methodology) page explains how this site distinguishes facts from allegations. [Evidence Contradictions](./Evidence-Contradictions) catalogs where timelines disagree, and [Public Response](./Public_Response) — together with [Political Aftermath](/Topic-Analyses/Political_Aftermath) — traces the societal impact.

## Public commentary

The **Other** hub collects cross-cutting problems that do not fit a single ballistics or person page: evidence contradictions, forensic gaps, investigation process obstacles, methodology standards, miscellaneous leads, psychological-profiling caution, public-response landscape, and witness reliability.

In public discussion, the Charlie Kirk case is still widely treated as open investigative terrain. Frequently raised cross-cutting themes include the sealed autopsy versus hearing cause-of-death accounts, second-vehicle claims involving an SUV and hospital, the microphone-versus-rifle mechanism debate, and reported resistance to FOIA requests on flight records. The child pages above organize these threads for structured reading.
