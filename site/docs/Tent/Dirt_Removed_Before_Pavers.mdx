# 10 Inches of Dirt Removed Before the Pavers

**This page is about what happened to the ground *before* the landscaping contractor arrived.**
It is a separate event from the paver installation covered on the
[Daniel Merrell — Hardscape Utah](./Daniel_Merrell_Hardscape_Utah) and
[Hardscape Specialties LLC](./Hardscape_Specialties) pages. By the time that hardscape crew reached
the [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) tent courtyard at Utah Valley University on **Sunday,
September 14, 2025**, roughly **ten inches of soil was already gone** — excavated and hauled away by
a different, still-unidentified crew described in the public record only as **men working for the
state of Utah**.

Who that first crew was, which agency directed it, when exactly it dug, and where the soil went are
all **unresolved**. That is the subject of this page.

## The anchor claim

The most reliable public marker for when the dirt was already gone is the contractor's own arrival:

> **First report that the dirt had been removed: Dan Merrell's arrival and observations on Sunday,
> September 14, 2025 — he found roughly ten inches already gone and photographed the excavated state.**

In his circulating on-camera account (amplified by
[@ShadowofEzra](/Influencers/x/shadowofezra), May 21, 2026, and by
[@MJTruthUltra](/Influencers/x/mjtruthultra), May 2026), Merrell is quoted as saying:

> *"When I showed up I took pictures of what it looked like, they had taken out about ten [inches]
> of it. It didn't cross my mind."*

Secondary accounts repeating the figure — [Stew Peters](/Influencers/podcasts-stew-peters),
[Candace Owens](/Influencers/podcasts-candace-owens) discussing a longer Merrell interview with Jimmy
Rex, and numerous X threads — describe **8 to 10 inches or more** of earth already stripped and
hauled off by a **prior team**. Everything on this page therefore rests on **one contractor's
account of what he saw and was told**, plus what public records do and do not corroborate.

## Who the first crew reportedly was

Merrell's description of the people already on site when he arrived:

- He was called by **"men who work for the state of Utah."**
- One managed a **state-owned facility in American Fork**.
- The other was a **maintenance groundskeeper** at a **facility in Provo**.
- On arrival he found a **"ragtag crew"** with those two men **in charge** — not a named landscaping
  or excavation company.
- One coordinator told him: *"We're not talking to anybody. We're not saying anything to the media."*
  and *"This is above our pay grade. The governor and the FBI said they want this done by Monday."*

Those quotes are **Merrell's memory of a conversation**. Neither the office of Governor
[Spencer Cox](/People/spencer-cox) nor [Kash Patel](/People/kash-patel)'s FBI has publicly confirmed
issuing any such directive in the materials reviewed here. The two coordinators have never been
publicly named, and **no company, agency, or crew roster has been identified** for the excavation
itself. See [State Workers & Groundskeepers](./State_Workers_Groundskeepers) for the fuller record on
those two men.

**The identity of the crew that removed the dirt remains one of the hardest open questions in this
investigation.** It has resisted identification for the simple reason that no invoice, work order,
crew list, or disposal manifest naming them has entered the public record.

## Timing window — as tight as the record allows

| Marker | Date | What it establishes |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| Shooting | **Wed, Sept 10, 2025** | Courtyard becomes homicide scene |
| Emergency payment request to a redacted vendor, $6,090.52, "Clean up for shooting incident" | **Thu, Sept 11** | Judicial Watch GRAMA release — cleanup money moving one day after |
| UVU text messages: *"We're not gonna need to cut out any sod… I bet we can just wash it all down"*; *"There's still a[n] eight inch puddle ish"*; *"The FBI is still there. I guess."* | **Fri, Sept 12** | Ground still **intact**; plan at this point is **hosing**, not digging |
| Scene reportedly **released** back to the university | **Sat, Sept 13** | Per accounts relayed by Gary Melton and amplified by Liz Wheeler |
| Merrell arrives — **~10 inches already excavated**, photographs it, installs pavers | **Sun, Sept 14** | First observation that the dirt is **gone** |
| Reuters (Jim Urquhart) photos of paver installation; KUTV's Jim Spiewak reports crews removing the ground area; campus staff return to a paved courtyard | **Mon, Sept 15** | Alteration becomes publicly visible and photographed |

**Best available window for the excavation: after the scene release on September 13 and before
Merrell's Sunday arrival on September 14.** No source in the public record gives an hour. No
timestamped video, security log, or work ticket for the dig itself has been released. Overnight or
early-morning work on the 13th–14th is consistent with the "emergency" framing Merrell describes but
is **not confirmed** by any document.

## What the Judicial Watch records show — and don't

Judicial Watch filed a GRAMA request with UVU on **November 20, 2025**; the records were released
publicly around **July 2026**. What they contain:

- The **September 11** emergency payment request form to a **redacted vendor** for **$6,090.52**,
  described as **"Clean up for shooting incident."**
- The **September 12** text thread about washing blood down with irrigation and hoses —
  *"The solution to pollution is dilution"* — explicitly saying sod would **not** need to be cut out.
- Follow-on texts into **September 13** about scheduling the cleanup and needing gloves.

What they **do not** contain:

- Any description of a **ten-inch excavation**.
- Any **name** for the crew, company, or agency that dug and hauled.
- Any **soil-disposal manifest** or record of where the material went.
- Any **contract, change order, or authorization** for earth removal.

UVU Police indicated they did **not** control the scene or participate in evidence or materials
removal, so their records were not searched on that point. The gap between the September 12 plan
(**wash it down, don't cut sod**) and the September 14 reality (**ten inches gone**) is the exact
interval no public document currently covers.

## Why the depth matters

The courtyard under the tent is the **primary blood and trace surface** of the homicide. Removing the
top ten inches of it removes, at minimum:

1. **Blood-soaked soil** usable for spatter-pattern and volume analysis.
2. **Bullet or fragment media** — anything that passed through and buried itself.
3. **Residue** relevant to the [shaped-charge and exploding-mic hypotheses](/Mic/Canon_XA55_Audio).
   [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) commentary specifically raises **PETN**, arguing that an
   explosive residue absorbing into soil *"would have to be dug out."* That is an **allegation**, not
   a lab finding — no public soil test result exists either way.
4. **Small debris** — casings, housing fragments, plastic, glass.

Ten inches is also **deeper than blood soaks**. That is the observation citizen investigators keep
returning to: an eight-inch surface puddle described on September 12 does not, on its face, require a
ten-inch dig two days later.

## Competing explanations

| Framing | Argument |
|---------|----------|
| Routine biohazard remediation | Blood-contaminated sod and soil are removed and disposed of under standard regulations after a scene is released |
| Standard paver prep | Merrell himself describes over-excavation as normal: *"To do pavers correctly you over-excavate, you get rid of the top… you want to get a good base"* — though he attributes the ten inches to the crew **before** him |
| Restoration and reopening | UVU framed courtyard work as restoration ahead of the campus reopening |
| Citizen-investigator concern | The dig went deeper and faster than remediation requires, under stated urgency, by a crew nobody can name, with no disposal record |

Lead investigator **[David Hull](/People/david-hull)** later testified during Tyler Robinson
preliminary-hearing proceedings that he had **no role** in removing the tent or paving the area, was
**not consulted**, and learned of the paving **from the news**. That testimony does not identify the
excavation crew, but it does mean the case's lead investigator was not directing the site work.

## Open questions

1. Which agency employed the American Fork facility manager and the Provo groundskeeper?
2. Who signed the authorization for **earth removal**, as distinct from the paving contract?
3. What equipment was used, and where did the excavated soil go — landfill, storage, or testing?
4. Was any of the removed material **retained** for forensic examination?
5. Were ballistics, blood-pattern, and explosive-residue collection **completed and documented**
   before the topsoil left the site?
6. Do Merrell's photographs of the pre-paver excavated state exist with **intact timestamps**, and
   will he release them?
7. Does any UVU or state security camera cover the courtyard between the evening of September 12 and
   the morning of September 14?

## Defamation note

The coordinators, groundskeepers, and crew described here are **unnamed living persons**. This page
records **Merrell's reported account** and the documented gaps in the public record. It does **not**
state that any state employee, official, or agency destroyed evidence, obstructed justice, or had
foreknowledge of the killing. No court has made a spoliation finding in this matter.

September 13–14, 2025, and pre-excavation forensic clearance records are exactly the category of

## What happened next — the landscaping contractor

Once the ground was stripped, the **hardscape phase** began. That is a separate crew, a separate set
of records, and a separate page:

- **[Daniel Merrell — Hardscape Utah](./Daniel_Merrell_Hardscape_Utah)** — the contractor who arrived
  Sunday **September 14** to a courtyard already excavated, installed the pavers, and later described
  on camera what he found and what the coordinators told him.
- **[Hardscape Specialties LLC](./Hardscape_Specialties)** — the separate corporate entity flagged in
  research as a possible authorizing or contracting party for the hardscape work.
- **[Paving Timeline — September 14](./Paving_Timeline_Sept_14)** — how the excavation and the paver
  install fit into the day-by-day chronology.
- **[Site Alteration Evidence](./Site_Alteration_Evidence)** — the full catalog of physical changes to
  the tent zone and the forensic categories each one affects.

Keep the two phases distinct. The soil was removed by **one crew**; the pavers were laid by
**another**. Conflating them is how the "next day" and "four days later" narratives ended up
contradicting each other.
