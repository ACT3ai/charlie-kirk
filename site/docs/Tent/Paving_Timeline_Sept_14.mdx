# Paving Timeline — September 14

The UVU **tent courtyard** was reportedly **stripped of grass and paved within days** of
Charlie Kirk's death on **September 10, 2025**. This page assembles **date claims** from the
research record and Merrell's on-camera account. Timeline disputes ("next day" vs "four days")
should be resolved against work orders and security video when released.

## Anchor dates

| Date | Event (reported) |
|------|------------------|
| **Sept 10, 2025** | Charlie Kirk shot at UVU courtyard tent/stage ([Timeline](/Timeline/overview)) |
| **~Sept 12, 2025** | Work order issued **~two days** post-shooting ([Hardscape Specialties](./Hardscape_Specialties) notes) |
| **Sept 14, 2025 (Sunday)** | **Dan Merrell** says he **installed pavers**; paving reportedly **started ~four days** after killing |
| **By Monday** | On-site coordinator told Merrell **governor and FBI** wanted completion **by Monday** (per Merrell) |

Sunday hardscape on a university courtyard is widely described in citizen research as **unusual**
for Utah County — most campus/state maintenance runs weekdays or Saturday at latest.

## Competing "speed" narratives

Two framings appear in CK_FILE and X threads:

1. **"Four days later"** — aligns with Merrell's **9/14/25** paver date and CK landscaping notes.
2. **"The next day"** — CK line: *"They covered up the evidence THE NEXT DAY, demolishing and repaving the crime scene."*

These may reflect **multiple work phases** (early excavation vs Sunday pavers) rather than a single
error. [State Workers](./State_Workers_Groundskeepers) commentary describes a **first government
team** removing soil before Merrell arrived. Disclosure of **dated photos, invoices, and delivery
logs** should reconcile the sequence.

## UVU "pre-scheduled" claim vs emergency framing

Research notes state UVU characterized work as **pre-scheduled** in some postures, while citizen
compilations argue **evidence contradicts** routine scheduling given the homicide timing. UVU's
president reportedly cited **pending external review** when declining specifics (AP reporting in
CK notes). KUTV/KATV noted courtyard changes without always naming contractors.

## Strange-events list

CK strange event **#15** asks plainly: **"Why paved over Tent area?"** That question assumes the
pave-over is **anomalous** relative to normal post-event preservation — not that any official
admitted wrongdoing.

## Forensic window

Whatever the exact hour-by-hour log, the critical investigative issue is whether **ballistics,
blood-pattern, and explosive-residue collection** finished **before** topsoil removal and paver
base installation. [Site Alteration Evidence](./Site_Alteration_Evidence) lists trace categories
at risk. [FBI Crime Scene Paving](/FBI/Crime_Scene_Paving) frames federal authorization questions.

## Laws (Charlie Kirk)

Dated work orders, emergency authorizations, and pre-paving forensic clearance records are among
targets of the [Charlie Kirk Investigation Laws](/Fix/overview).
