# No Security Plan for the September 11, 2025 Event (Alleged)

:::caution This is alleged by others. We don't allege it.

Everything on this page is an **allegation made by other people** — principally
[Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens), relaying an **anonymous tipster** on her podcast.
This site does **not** allege that any security plan was missing, that anyone was
negligent, or that anyone had foreknowledge of the assassination. We document what was
said publicly, who said it, who disputes it, and what remains unverified. The claim is
**unproven** and is the subject of **pending litigation**.

:::

## The Allegation in One Sentence

Candace Owens alleged that TPUSA security — specifically contractor **Brian Harpole** of
Integrity Security Solutions and TPUSA security director **[Dan Flood](/People/dan-flood)** —
had **no pre-existing security plan and no police coordination** prepared for
[Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)'s scheduled appearance on **September 11, 2025**, the
day after his assassination. Again: **that is her allegation, not this site's.**

## Background: The Event That Never Happened

Charlie Kirk was fatally shot on **September 10, 2025** at a TPUSA "American Comeback Tour"
event at [Utah Valley University](/UVU/overview) in Orem, Utah. He was scheduled to speak the
**next day** as a featured speaker at the **Truth & Liberty Conference** at **Charis Bible
College** in **Woodland Park, Colorado** (part of Andrew Wommack Ministries).

After the assassination, organizers revised that schedule. Kirk's slot became a
**tribute, vigil, and panel** honoring his life and legacy, with prayer, video tributes, and
discussion. Security was **heightened** for the revised event, and hundreds attended. The
Colorado stop was **not canceled outright** — it was significantly altered.

## What Owens Said

Owens — a former TPUSA employee who has extensively questioned the assassination, TPUSA's
handling of it, and its security posture — said on her podcast (reported around **early
January 2026**) that she received a tip from someone **"directly involved"** in planning the
Colorado event.

Reported details from her statements, compiled from news coverage and circulated podcast
excerpts:

- After the assassination, **"there was a bit of a panicked meeting that took place which was
  headed by the chief of police."**
- Organizers decided to transform the event into a **vigil**.
- **"They were panicked because, believe it or not, they had absolutely no security plan in
  place. They thought how odd we don't have a security plan in place."**
- **"No police officer involvement scheduled. Nothing. Suddenly they're panicked because they
  realize they don't have anything scheduled."**
- She questioned why TPUSA security had **not coordinated with Colorado police departments in
  advance**, given that Harpole had publicly described the team's normal process — advance
  work, plans, questionnaires, permitting, and coordination with local police.
- The tipster reportedly found it odd that nothing was prepared for the next day's
  high-profile stop, especially since Harpole later described **rigorous planning** for
  events, such as the UVU stop being planned weeks in advance.

Owens framed this as another **"glaring oddity in this security protocol"** and suggested it
raised questions about foreknowledge or negligence — at one point putting it as **"as if they
knew [Kirk] wouldn't make it."** She contrasted the tip with Harpole's remarks on
*The Shawn Ryan Show* about advance security work, rooftop concerns, drone issues,
jurisdictional limits, and coordination with local and campus police.

**She has not publicly identified the tipster.** No independent verification of the tipster or
of the "no plan" claim appears in reporting.

## Responses and Denials

This section matters as much as the allegation. The people named **dispute** it.

**TPUSA** addressed Owens' wider conspiracy claims as **"utterly vile,"** as
misrepresentations, and as unfounded. The organization invited her to a livestream Q&A to
discuss her questions about the assassination while criticizing her for naming staff and
pushing unproven theories. TPUSA has emphasized its audits, threat awareness, and a
prioritization of open outreach over maximum-security posture.

**Brian Harpole sued Owens** — and others, including a podcast guest — for **defamation, false
light, and conspiracy**, in a suit reported as filed around **April 2026** in federal court in
Tennessee. The suit targets claims that he conspired in the assassination (including an
alleged secret planning meeting at [Fort Huachuca](/US_Intelligence/Fort_Huachuca/overview) with Erika Kirk and military and intelligence
figures) **and** statements portraying him and his team as incompetent or professionally
negligent in security planning. Harpole **denies** the claims, says they are contradicted by
records and travel logs, and alleges reputational and business harm. He has rejected the
broader conspiracy narratives. Owens has responded to the suit by standing by her criticisms.

**On this specific tip**, available reporting contains **no prominent public denial or
confirmation** from TPUSA or Harpole addressing the "no plan for September 11" claim on its
own — it is subsumed under their general pushback against Owens' theories.

## Broader Security Context

The allegation sits inside wider scrutiny of TPUSA and Kirk's security at UVU and across the
tour. These are context, not corroboration:

- TPUSA security had **limited jurisdiction** — focused on Kirk's immediate vicinity, relying
  on local and campus police for perimeter and rooftops. See
  [Day-of Security](/Charlie/Day_Of_Security).
- Reports of **no counter-snipers**, drone restrictions under FAA and campus rules, **varying
  security levels across venues**, and no re-entry policies or metal detectors at some stops.
- Harpole has stated his team **raised rooftop and staffing concerns beforehand** but was
  limited by jurisdiction and by what approvals allowed.
- A parallel, separately sourced claim about a missing next-day plan is documented at
  [The Next Event Had No Security Plan](/Security_Team/next-event-no-plan) — note that page
  names the venue as "Therese Bible College," while the Owens account describes **Charis Bible
  College**. Whether these are the same claim garbled in transmission is itself unresolved.

The foreknowledge framing around this allegation echoes former NCTC director
[Joe Kent](/US_Intelligence/joe-kent), who has said on the record that online pre-knowledge of
the assassination suggests "more than likely there's something else at play" — and that his
center's parallel foreign-nexus review was reportedly halted before those leads were exhausted.

## How It Spread on X

The claim went **viral in early January 2026** (roughly January 8–10) after Owens' podcast
episode, with video clips widely shared. Engagement concentrated in conservative,
conspiracy-oriented, and TPUSA-critical circles.

## Sourcing

The allegation stems primarily from **Owens' podcast**, with video clips and transcripts
referenced in news coverage by outlets including *Hindustan Times* and *Times of India*, which
reported her words directly. **No independent verification** of the tipster or the "no plan"
claim appears in reporting. It remains **Owens' allegation, based on an anonymous source.**
