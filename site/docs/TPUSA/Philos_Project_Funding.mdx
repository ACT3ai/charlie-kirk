# Philos Project and Pro-Israel Advocacy Funding

The **Philos Project** is a pro-Israel Christian-conservative advocacy organization frequently cited in timelines about **donor pressure on [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk)** in 2025. Public reporting describes Philos as part of the funding and organizing ecosystem around the **July 31 White House meeting** — not as a party to the assassination.

## Organization Profile (Public Materials)

According to reporting compiled in this project:

- Philos promotes **U.S.–Israel alliance** within conservative Christian networks
- It is publicly described as receiving support from major donors including networks associated with **Paul Singer** ([Paul Singer Funding](./Paul_Singer_Funding))
- It co-organized the **July 31, 2025** off-the-record White House session with the Faith & Freedom Coalition and Latino Coalition for Israel

## July 31 Meeting — Philos Role

Documented meeting themes:

- Countering what attendees called **"woke-right defamation"** of Israel
- Addressing perceived **antisemitism inside the MAGA movement**
- Strategic alignment among pro-Israel advocacy groups **without TPUSA at the table**

Reported attendees tied to Philos networks include **[Yehuda Kaploun](/People/yehuda-kaploun)** (as named in [Law 1 people definitions](/laws/other/people.md) and timeline pages).

## Relevance to Kirk / TPUSA

Investigators note:

1. Kirk was historically **pro-Israel** and TPUSA was a flagship youth vehicle for that alignment
2. By **September 2025**, Kirk was publicly **breaking from** that consensus
3. Philos-linked meetings occurred **without Kirk** while parallel **intervention** meetings targeted him directly

This pattern is cited in **motive theories** — it does not prove Philos or any employee acted unlawfully.

## Post-Assassination Organizational Questions

Researchers compare Philos-era donor priorities to **post-death TPUSA policy reversals** documented in [TPUSA Opposite Charlie](/TPUSA/TPUSA_Opposite_Charlie) — e.g., hawkish Iran rhetoric, attacks on Thomas Massie, reduced Epstein scrutiny.

Whether those reversals reflect **donor realignment** is debated; Philos is discussed as one node in a larger network (AIPAC, ADL, Heritage, Faith & Freedom — see law definitions).
