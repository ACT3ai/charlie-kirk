## Connection to the Charlie Kirk case

Investigation notes and a query raised by **Blake** ask which insurer underwrote **the policy on [Charlie Kirk](/People/charlie-kirk) that GGLF 2023 LLC owned**. Per the notes, the LLC was **created May 15, 2023**, registered in Wyoming, and the policy was taken out in 2023 — with commentary connecting the timing to the period after Kirk began questioning aspects of Israel policy following October 7. TPUSA reportedly paid a **~$350,000 loan** connected to the arrangement, and circulating estimates put a potential payout in the **$20M–$50M** range. **Patrick Bet-David** is mentioned in the notes as possibly having drafted terms.

Every figure here is **unverified**. Key-person policies are routine; the investigative interest is in **who owned the policy, who benefits, and the timing** relative to Kirk's death.

## Why investigators track it

A large policy owned through an out-of-state LLC, with TPUSA money attached, is a natural audit target and intersects the [motive](/Motive/overview) analysis: who stood to gain financially. It is one reason the abandoned [DOGE audit](../doge-audit-results) draws scrutiny.

## Open questions

1. Which insurer issued the policy, and who is the named beneficiary?
2. Why was the policy held in a Wyoming LLC rather than by TPUSA directly?
3. What was the $350,000 loan for, and was it repaid?
