# Does TPUSA Support the Iran War After Charlie?

**People are curious if there has been a change after Charlie Kirk died — if TPUSA changed to support the bombing of Iran.**

This page answers that one question, and it is narrowly about **bombing and air strikes**. It is not
about ground troops. Nobody at TPUSA is quoted here calling for boots on the ground, and this site
does not claim they did. The question on the table is whether the organization Charlie Kirk built —
and the show that still carries his name — moved from his **restraint** on Iran toward **supporting,
accepting, and celebrating U.S. air strikes** after September 10, 2025.

Everything below is **attributed**. Public commentary about a political organization's messaging is
not an accusation of criminal conduct against anyone.

## The short answer

Based on the public record of statements by TPUSA-affiliated voices after Kirk's death: **yes,
in the sense of supporting, accepting, and positively framing bombing** — and **no, in the sense of
preemptively demanding new bombing campaigns**. Those are two different things and the distinction
matters.

What is documented in public commentary is that hosts of *The Charlie Kirk Show* and TPUSA's
Frontlines reporting arm:

- **Rooted for the success** of U.S. air strikes on Iran once they were underway.
- **Accepted the strikes as potentially the right call**, framing them as the president's decision
  to make.
- **Highlighted the results favorably** — degraded infrastructure, eliminated regime officials, air
  dominance.
- **Asked the Pentagon publicly** about Iran's rebuilt nuclear capability, which critics read as
  pressure for renewed strikes.

What is **not** documented is TPUSA demanding a bombing campaign before one existed, or calling for
a ground invasion. The hosts have said explicitly that they never "beat the drums of war."

## December 2025 — the Pentagon question

The earliest concrete artifact critics point to is a **December 2025** Pentagon briefing.

TPUSA's Frontlines account (**@FrontlinesTPUSA**) and reporter **Monica Paige** posted video from
the briefing. The context: Iran's president had vowed to rebuild the country's nuclear facilities
"with greater strength," roughly six months after the earlier **[Operation Midnight
Hammer](/Iran/midnight-hammer-12-day-war)** strikes. Paige asked the Department of War for an update
on the status of Iran's nuclear capabilities.

Critics — including **Max Blumenthal** and a large volume of X commentary — characterized this as
TPUSA "lobbying the Pentagon to bomb Iran again," or pushing for renewed strikes.

Paige has publicly disputed that reading. According to her response, she was **simply asking about
the status of rebuilding efforts** given Trump's stated red line on an Iranian nuclear weapon. That
denial belongs in the record alongside the accusation. A reporter asking a briefing question is not,
by itself, advocacy — but the choice of question is what critics say revealed the organization's
posture.

## Late February–March 2026 — the air campaign

The clearer body of material comes during **Operation Epic Fury** and the air campaigns that
followed it in 2026. Here the hosts spoke in their own voices, on the record.

### Blake Neff's framing

**[Blake Neff](/People/blake-neff)**, a producer of *The Charlie Kirk Show*, stated that Charlie —
and he himself — had **opposed a regime-change war**, and then said:

> "President Trump has elected for regime change in Iran. As an American patriot I must hope for the
> best… Ideally, this will be our last MidEast War: Toppling the Islamic Republic would remove the
> last large hostile power in the region… If the President can deliver that, this will be a success
> for America."

Read plainly, that is **not** a call for war. It is an acknowledgment that the decision was made by
someone else, followed by a hope that it succeeds. Critics read the second half as the pivot: the
show is now describing regime change in Iran as something that could be "a success for America" —
an outcome Kirk had spent years arguing against.

### Andrew Kolvet's framing

**[Andrew Kolvet](/People/andrew-kolvet)**, TPUSA's Chief Communications Officer and executive
producer of the show, along with Neff, reportedly described Trump's interventions as **"limited and
successful"** in some contexts. On the decision to strike, the framing was that it was not something
they had "frothed over," but — as quoted in circulating commentary — **"Doesn't mean that this
wasn't the best national security decision that the president could have made."**

Kolvet also posted updates framing ongoing and upcoming strikes in favorable terms, including that
**"the hardest hits are yet to come"** and that coming action would be **"even more PUNISHING on
Iran,"** alongside reporting on terminated military leaders in Tehran.

These are the specific lines critics cite. They are quoted here as **reported statements** about
military operations — political speech, not evidence of any crime.

### The Kirk precedent they invoke

The hosts have pointed out that **Charlie himself ultimately endorsed the earlier 2025 surgical
strikes after the fact**, and argue they are treating the later campaign the same way: skeptical
beforehand, supportive of success once the decision was made. That is a real argument and it is not
easily dismissed — Kirk's June 2025 posture is documented on
[Kirk's Iran Warnings: The Record](/Iran/kirk-warnings-quotes) and
[Charlie's Stand Against the Iran War](/Iran/charlie-anti-iran-war).

Whether that continuity claim holds is the actual dispute. Kirk's documented objection was to
**getting into** an Iran war and to **regime change**; the post-death show is describing regime
change as a potentially good outcome.

## What the hosts say in their own defense

Fairness requires stating their position clearly. According to their own commentary, the hosts:

- **Never demanded a war** and reject the claim that they "beat the drums."
- Remained **skeptical of prolongation** — of a campaign dragging on into a quagmire.
- Warned about the **political cost** of a long Middle East engagement.
- Framed their support as **"the decision is made, now hope it works"** patriotism rather than
  advocacy for the decision itself.
- Draw on **Kirk's own after-the-fact endorsement** of the June 2025 strikes as precedent.

Nothing in the public record shows TPUSA calling for a ground invasion of Iran. Claims that they did
should be treated as unsupported.

## Why critics still call it a shift

The counterargument, made across X and by former TPUSA-aligned commentators, runs like this:

1. Kirk's value to the America First coalition was that he was **the brake** — the one large youth
   organization arguing against another Middle East war.
2. Within months of his death, the same platform was **rooting for strikes**, publicizing their
   destructive results, and asking the Pentagon about Iran's nuclear rebuild.
3. "Support the president once he decides" is a posture that, applied consistently, means the
   organization **can never again function as the brake** Kirk was.

That is a critique of political direction, not proof of anything about how or why Kirk died. It sits
alongside the broader reversal catalog on
[TPUSA Contradicts Kirk's Legacy](./TPUSA_Opposite_Charlie) and the attributed claims on
[TPUSA's Post-Death Pivot on Iran](/Iran/post-death-pivot).

## Defamation caution

The people named here — Andrew Kolvet, Blake Neff, and Monica Paige — are **living private
individuals engaged in political commentary and journalism**. Nothing on this page alleges that any
of them committed a crime, and none has been charged with one. Supporting, opposing, or changing a
position on U.S. military policy is lawful political speech. Their own explanations and denials are
included above and should be read as part of the record.

This page also does **not** claim that TPUSA's Iran posture is evidence of involvement in Charlie
Kirk's death. It documents a **messaging change that readers ask about** — nothing more.
