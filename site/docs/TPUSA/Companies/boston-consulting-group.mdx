# Boston Consulting Group (BCG) — Context (Claims)

:::caution Context only — attributed claims
BCG is a large, lawful consulting firm. It appears here only where its name intersects the case via one official's resume and reported contracts. Nothing below implies the firm had any role in the assassination.
:::

## Where it intersects the case

BCG surfaces at the edges of the investigation through **attributed claims**:

- **The SAM-702 flight:** [Candace Owens](/People/candace-owens) alleges that one of the passengers was **Bradley D. Hansell** — described as an Under Secretary of War for Intelligence with a resume moving between **Army Special Forces, BCG, the National Security Council, and back to BCG** — naming him as one of four passengers on the **SAM-702** VIP flight the day before the assassination. That passenger identification is Owens's allegation and has not been confirmed against any released manifest. The flight analysis lives on the [Planes](/Planes/overview) pages.
- **Gaza-related work:** Notes allege BCG ran a **"Project Aurora"** consulting engagement modeling payments for Palestinians to leave Gaza and helped stand up the **Gaza Humanitarian Foundation** before withdrawing — after which **Johnny Moore** (reportedly [Andrew Kolvet](/People/andrew-kolvet)'s former boss) became chairman.

**These are attributed claims tying BCG to the [Israel](/Israel/overview) and intelligence threads by association**, not evidence of any role in Kirk's death.

## Why investigators track it

BCG functions as a **connective node** between an intelligence official on a scrutinized flight and the broader [Israel](/Israel/overview)/Gaza policy backdrop. It is mapped here for completeness, with a clear line that adjacency is not participation.
