# Turning Point PAC

:::caution Attributed claims only
A political action committee doing political work is exactly what a PAC is for. Nothing here is a finding of a crime; the PAC is included for structural completeness.
:::

## Entity snapshot

| Role in the debate | The entity where partisan spending is supposed to sit |
| Evidence rating | Contextual |

## Why it matters to the analysis

Turning Point PAC is the one entity in the family that is **fully allowed** to do political activity. Its existence is central to the reporting argument made by Zach of [Wolves And Finance](https://youtu.be/_QwGo0LyZ3I): his claim is not that political activity is wrong, but that partisan spending allegedly done through the **non-political 501(c)(3)s** — [Turning Point USA](./turning-point-usa) and [America's Turning Point](./americas-turning-point) — should instead have run through the PAC (or the c4, [Turning Point Action](./turning-point-action)).

In other words, the PAC is the "correct" home for political money; the dispute is over whether some political money went elsewhere and was under-reported. **That remains an attributed allegation, disputed by TPUSA's attorney [Robert Barnes](./turning-point-usa).**

## Why investigators track it

Mapping which entity is allowed to do what — c3 vs c4 vs PAC — is the framework the whole [DOGE audit results](../doge-audit-results) discussion rests on. This page fixes the PAC's place in that map.
