# SMBB Break Man

A separate unidentified figure surfaced in police-radio discussion well north of
Provo: a man who reportedly **stated there was an APB out on him for the shooting
at UVU**. He is referenced in connection with "SMBB Break" — the **Sorenson
Molecular Biotechnology Building (SMBB)** at the University of Utah in Salt Lake
City. This man has **not been publicly identified**, and no public evidence ties
him to the shooting.

## What is reported

- **His claim.** Per circulated police-radio audio, the man said an **APB** was out
  on him "for the shooting at UVU."
- **Where.** The reports place the encounter at the **Urban Pioneer Cafe** referenced
  alongside "SMBB Break." Notes flag an ambiguity: Urban Pioneer Cafe reportedly has
  **both a UVU-campus location and a Salt Lake City location**, so the exact site is
  unsettled. The Sorenson Molecular Biotechnology Building is at **35 S Wasatch Dr,
  Salt Lake City** (University of Utah) — roughly a **43-minute drive north** of Provo.
- **The reported "stand by" burden.** Per the notes, the FBI reportedly wanted local
  officers to **stand by with the man**, which commentators estimate would tie up a
  patrol for roughly **two hours** without producing an interview.
- **The "don't interview" report.** The most significant procedural claim is that
  officers were reportedly told **not to interview** the man. Audio circulated by
  citizen researchers includes the line, attributed to the radio traffic: *"As per
  SBI and FBI, we've been given clear direction NOT to interview."*
- **Possibly distinct from Pellet Gun Man.** Sources note this man "may be
  different than [Pellet Gun Man](./Pellet_Gun_Man)."

## Why it draws questions

Two things make this report stand out: a man volunteering that he is wanted for the
UVU shooting, and a reported directive that local police **stand by but not
interview** — which, if accurate, would mean tying up a patrol for hours without
generating a witness statement. The open question is whether the encounter was a
diversion, a confused bystander, or a misread of routine radio traffic. None of
that is resolved publicly.

## Open questions

1. Who was the man, and was he ever formally interviewed by any agency?
2. Was there in fact an instruction not to interview, and who issued it?
3. How long were local officers held at the location, and what else was happening
   during that window?

no individual is accused of any crime.
