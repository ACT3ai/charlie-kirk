# Jaxson Thomas Fox — Jailhouse Informant

**Jaxson Thomas Fox** is not an on-scene UVU figure — he enters the story **after** arrest as a reported
**jailhouse informant** in the Tyler Robinson prosecution. CK strange-event **#25** flags his **October 2025**
court appearance. Some commentators treat informant-driven evidence as a **distraction layer** that steers
the trial away from ballistics, audio, and intel threads. We document **process facts** only.

## What is reported

- **Jaxson Thomas Fox** referenced in Tyler Robinson **trial/court** coverage as informant-related (Oct 2025)
- [Tyler Robinson — Trial](/Tyler_Robinson/Trial/overview) and [Legal](/Legal/overview) pages cover gag orders and closed hearings
- Transport/order paperwork for informant witnesses cited in media summaries (verify against docket)

## Why informants appear in "distraction" framing

Citizen researchers argue:

- Informant testimony can **replace** physical evidence gaps (e.g. [ATF inconclusive](/Proof_Not_Tyler/ATF_Inconclusive_Ballistics))
- High-profile cases sometimes add **jailhouse witnesses** after narratives harden
- Fox's role keeps public attention on **Tyler's intent** instead of **third-party operatives**

That is **commentary**, not proof Fox was planted or knew anything in advance.
