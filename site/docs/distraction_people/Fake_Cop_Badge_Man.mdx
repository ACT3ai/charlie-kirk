# Badge Man — the "Fake Cop" Claim

This is the **thinnest entry** in the distraction-people discussion, included only
for completeness. According to a small number of image-analysis posts, an
unidentified figure at or near the Utah Valley University scene on **September 10,
2025** appeared to wear a **clip-on badge and then remove it** — prompting
speculation that he may have been posing as an officer. No identity, agency, or
record supports the claim, and an entirely innocent explanation is at least as
likely.

## What is claimed

- **The badge detail.** Posters analyzing scene photos and video say the
  individual "had a clip-on badge, then removed it," and suggest he may have
  appeared to be law enforcement before shedding the badge — adding to confusion
  about who at the scene was an officer and who was not.
- **The distraction framing.** On the alleged distraction roster, the claim is
  that a figure who looks like an officer and then is not could muddy the early
  picture of who was responding. This is the entire basis of the claim.

## Why this entry is weak

- It rests on a **single, uncorroborated line** drawn from image analysis.
- A **plainclothes officer, a security contractor, or an ordinary bystander**
  with a lanyard or event credential would produce the same appearance.
- No name, agency, badge number, or official record has ever been attached to it.

This page exists to document that the claim appears in the roster discussion —
not to suggest the person did anything wrong, and not to assert that any "fake
officer" was present.

## Open questions

1. Is there any clear footage that actually shows a badge being removed, or is
   this an interpretation of a low-resolution frame?
2. Could the figure simply be a plainclothes officer or credentialed staffer?
3. Has any witness or official ever referenced an unaccounted-for "officer" at
   the scene?

## Status

**Status:** Unknown (unidentified). No identity is offered and none is implied.
Treat this as an open question raised online, not a finding.
